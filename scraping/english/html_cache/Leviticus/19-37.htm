Leviticus 19
1. A repetition of various laws














Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
You must keep all My statutes and all My ordinances and follow them.
This command emphasizes the comprehensive nature of God's law given to the Israelites. The statutes and ordinances refer to the various laws, decrees, and commandments that God provided through Moses. These laws covered moral, ceremonial, and civil aspects of life, reflecting God's holiness and His desire for His people to be set apart. The call to keep and follow these laws highlights the importance of obedience and faithfulness in the covenant relationship between God and Israel. This is echoed in Deuteronomy 6:5-6, where love for God is expressed through obedience to His commands. The laws served as a guide for righteous living and were intended to distinguish Israel from surrounding nations, as seen in Leviticus 20:26.

I am the LORD.
This phrase underscores the authority and identity of God as the covenant-keeping LORD, Yahweh. It serves as a reminder of His sovereignty and the reason for obedience. The declaration "I am the LORD" is a recurring affirmation throughout Leviticus, emphasizing God's unchanging nature and His role as the ultimate lawgiver. It also points to the relational aspect of the covenant, where God is not only a lawgiver but also a personal deity who desires a relationship with His people. This phrase is a reminder of God's faithfulness and His deliverance of Israel from Egypt, as seen in Exodus 20:2. It also foreshadows the coming of Jesus Christ, who fulfills the law and establishes a new covenant, as mentioned in Matthew 5:17 and Hebrews 8:6.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Moses
The prophet and leader of the Israelites who received the laws from God on Mount Sinai. He is the central human figure in the delivery of the Levitical laws.

2. Israelites
The chosen people of God, who were given the laws to set them apart as a holy nation. They are the direct recipients of the commandments in Leviticus.

3. Mount Sinai
The place where God delivered the Ten Commandments and other laws to Moses. It is a significant location for the giving of the law.

4. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant-keeping God of Israel, who establishes His laws and ordinances for His people to follow.

5. Levitical Law
A collection of laws given to the Israelites, covering moral, ceremonial, and civil aspects of life, intended to guide them in holiness and righteousness.
Teaching Points
Obedience to God's Commandments
The call to keep God's statutes and ordinances is a timeless principle that underscores the importance of obedience in the life of a believer. Obedience is an expression of our love and reverence for God.

Holiness and Distinctiveness
The laws given to the Israelites were meant to set them apart as a holy nation. As Christians, we are called to live distinctively, reflecting God's holiness in our conduct.

The Authority of God
The phrase "I am the LORD" emphasizes God's authority and sovereignty. Recognizing His lordship should inspire us to submit to His will and commands.

Continuity of God's Law
While the ceremonial aspects of the law were fulfilled in Christ, the moral principles remain relevant. We are called to uphold these principles in our daily lives.

Practical Application of Scripture
Engaging with God's word requires action. We are encouraged to apply biblical principles in practical ways, impacting our relationships, decisions, and community involvement.

What Does Leviticus 19:37 Mean
You must keep all My statutes

- The word “keep” carries the idea of guarding or treasuring. God’s statutes are not suggestions; they are safeguards for holy living (see Deuteronomy 6:17: “You must diligently keep the commandments of the LORD your God”).

- “All” leaves no room for selective obedience. James 2:10 echoes this: “Whoever keeps the whole law yet stumbles at just one point is guilty of breaking all of it.”

- By commanding Israel—and by extension all who belong to Him—to keep every statute, God reveals His expectation of wholehearted devotion (compare Psalm 119:4).

and all My ordinances

- “Ordinances” refers to God-given decisions that govern community life and personal conduct. They cover everything from justice in the courts (Exodus 23:6) to compassion for the poor (Leviticus 19:9-10).

- The pairing of “statutes” and “ordinances” shows that God cares about both worship (vertical) and relationships (horizontal); Jesus captures this balance in Matthew 22:37-40.

- Obedience in public and private spheres demonstrates allegiance to the One who authored every command (Psalm 19:7-9).

and follow them

- “Follow” moves obedience from the head to the feet. It is continuous action—daily steps of surrender (Deuteronomy 30:16).

- Jesus reiterates this heart-level obedience: “If you love Me, you will keep My commandments” (John 14:15).

- Genuine faith produces a lifestyle aligned with God’s standards (1 John 2:3-6).

I am the LORD

- The command rests on God’s character. He alone has the authority to legislate morality because He is the covenant-keeping LORD (Exodus 20:2).

- His name guarantees the reliability of every promise and warning (Numbers 23:19).

- Obedience, then, is an act of worship, acknowledging His sovereignty and goodness (Psalm 100:3).

summary

Leviticus 19:37 calls God’s people to guard every statute, honor every ordinance, live them out daily, and do so because the covenant LORD—holy, faithful, and sovereign—has spoken. Keeping His commands is not legalistic drudgery; it is joyful allegiance to the God who redeemed us and knows what leads to our flourishing.
