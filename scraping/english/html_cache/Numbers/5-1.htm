Then the LORD said to Moses,
This phrase indicates direct communication from God to Moses, a common occurrence in the Pentateuch, highlighting Moses' unique role as a prophet and leader of Israel. The phrase underscores the divine authority behind the instructions that follow, emphasizing that they are not merely human decisions but commands from God Himself. This communication method is consistent with other instances in the Torah where God provides guidance, laws, and instructions to Moses, such as in Exodus 3:4 at the burning bush and throughout the giving of the Law at Sinai. The use of "LORD" in all capitals signifies the translation of the Tetragrammaton, YHWH, the covenant name of God, which emphasizes His eternal and unchanging nature. This divine interaction sets the stage for the instructions that follow, which are part of maintaining the holiness and purity of the Israelite camp, reflecting God's holiness. Moses, as the mediator of the Old Covenant, prefigures Christ, the mediator of the New Covenant, who also communicated God's will to His people.

Teaching Points
Holiness and Separation
God’s instructions to Moses highlight the importance of holiness and separation from impurity. As believers, we are called to live lives that are set apart and reflect God’s holiness.

Obedience to God’s Commands
The Israelites were required to follow God’s commands precisely. This teaches us the importance of obedience in our walk with God, trusting that His instructions are for our good.

Community Responsibility
The instructions given to Moses were for the entire community, emphasizing that maintaining purity is a collective responsibility. In the church, we are called to support one another in living holy lives.

God’s Presence Among His People
The need for purity in the camp underscores the reality of God’s presence among His people. We are reminded that God dwells within us through the Holy Spirit, calling us to live in a way that honors Him.

What Does Numbers 5:1 Mean
Then

• The opening adverb links this command to the flow of the narrative. It follows the careful census and ordering of the Levites in Numbers 4, showing that God’s instructions are given in a deliberate sequence (Numbers 4:49).

• Scripture often notes precise timing to underline that God’s commands are neither random nor delayed (Genesis 22:20; Joshua 1:1).

• “Then” reminds us that obedience to previous revelation prepares the way for the next step (Psalm 119:60; James 1:22).

The LORD

• The covenant name, “YHWH,” stresses God’s unchanging faithfulness (Exodus 3:14; Malachi 3:6).

• He alone defines holiness for His people, as seen throughout the wilderness journey (Leviticus 11:45; 1 Peter 1:16).

• The same LORD who redeemed Israel from Egypt continues to direct their daily life, affirming His ongoing, personal involvement (Deuteronomy 4:7; Hebrews 13:8).

Said

• God speaks with absolute authority; His words carry creative and moral power (Genesis 1:3; Isaiah 55:11).

• Revelation is verbal and clear, not vague impressions—“All Scripture is God-breathed” (2 Timothy 3:16).

• Because God speaks, His people are expected to listen and respond (Deuteronomy 6:4-5; John 10:27).

To Moses

• Moses serves as the appointed mediator, receiving and relaying God’s will (Exodus 19:3-7; Numbers 12:7-8).

• His leadership underscores the principle of divinely established authority within the covenant community (Hebrews 3:5).

• The pattern anticipates the greater Mediator, Jesus Christ, who perfectly reveals the Father (Deuteronomy 18:18; John 1:17-18).

summary

Numbers 5:1, “Then the LORD said to Moses,” signals another precise, authoritative command from the covenant-keeping God, delivered through His chosen mediator. The verse anchors the subsequent instructions in God’s unbroken sequence of revelation, highlighting His faithfulness, the power of His spoken word, and the necessity of obedient response from His people.THE UNCLEAN TO BE REMOVED (verses 1-4).
