We also asked for their names
In the context of Ezra 5, the Jewish exiles had returned to Jerusalem and were rebuilding the temple. The local authorities, including Tattenai, the governor of the region, questioned their authority to rebuild. Asking for names was a common practice in ancient times to establish accountability and ensure that those involved had legitimate authority. This reflects the bureaucratic nature of the Persian Empire, which required detailed records and reports.

so that we could write down the names of their leaders
The emphasis on leaders highlights the importance of leadership in the restoration of the temple. The leaders of the Jewish community, such as Zerubbabel and Jeshua, were pivotal in guiding the people and ensuring the work continued despite opposition. This mirrors the biblical theme of God raising leaders to fulfill His purposes, as seen with Moses, Joshua, and David. The act of writing down names also signifies the importance of record-keeping in maintaining order and accountability.

for your information
This phrase indicates that the inquiry was intended to be reported back to higher authorities, likely King Darius. It underscores the hierarchical structure of the Persian administration, where local governors were responsible for reporting activities within their regions. This reflects the broader biblical theme of submission to governing authorities, as seen in Romans 13:1-7, where believers are encouraged to respect and obey governmental structures.

The central place of worship for the Jewish people, which was being rebuilt after the Babylonian exile.

5. Darius the King
The Persian king to whom Tattenai and his companions reported the activities of the Jews.
Teaching Points
Authority and Obedience
The Jews were questioned about their authority to rebuild the temple. This highlights the importance of understanding and respecting authority while also being obedient to God's commands.

Courage in the Face of Opposition
The Jews continued their work despite the questioning, demonstrating courage and faithfulness. Believers today are called to stand firm in their faith even when faced with opposition.

Documentation and Accountability
The request for names and leaders shows the importance of accountability and transparency in leadership. In our lives, we should strive for integrity and accountability in our actions.

God's Sovereignty
Despite the questioning and potential for conflict, God's plan for the temple's reconstruction was not thwarted. This reminds us of God's sovereignty and faithfulness to His promises.

What Does Ezra 5:10 Mean
We also asked for their names

The Persian officials who visited the rebuilt temple wanted to know exactly who was in charge. By requesting the builders’ names, they signaled that the investigation was personal and specific, not a vague inquiry.

• Earlier enemies had written a slanderous letter to Artaxerxes (Ezra 4:12-15), so these officials needed accurate data, not rumors.

• Taking names was common in royal investigations (Esther 6:1-2); it made each person answerable for his actions.

• Scripture often shows God’s servants willingly identifying themselves (Nehemiah 1:6-11) because transparent obedience to the Lord fears no scrutiny.

so that we could write down

The officials recorded what they learned, underscoring how God’s work stands up to documentation.

• Careful record-keeping echoes Jeremiah’s deed of purchase (Jeremiah 32:10-12) and Luke’s “orderly account” (Luke 1:3).

• Written reports preserve truth against distortion (Proverbs 22:20-21).

• This moment reminds us that our own lives are “living letters” (2 Corinthians 3:2-3), meant to be open records of faithfulness.

the names of their leaders

Leadership carries responsibility before both God and man.

• Israel had long listed tribal heads (Numbers 1:4-16), projecting accountability.

• Spiritual leaders today must be “above reproach” (1 Timothy 3:2), ready for examination.

• By providing their leaders’ names, the Jews were saying, in effect, “We stand behind this work because the Lord has commissioned it” (Haggai 1:13-14).

for your information

Everything gathered would reach King Darius, demonstrating that civil authority and divine purpose need not clash.

• Romans 13:1-4 teaches believers to respect governing powers unless commanded to disobey God (Acts 5:29).

• Mordecai’s disclosure of a plot “for the king’s information” (Esther 2:22) saved lives; here, truthful information protected God’s project.

• The Jews trusted the king would act justly once he knew the facts (Ezra 6:1-12).

summary

Ezra 5:10 shows Persian officials conducting a thorough, name-specific inquiry so they could report accurately to Darius. The verse highlights transparency, the legitimacy of godly leadership, and confidence that truth recorded and reviewed will ultimately vindicate God’s people.
