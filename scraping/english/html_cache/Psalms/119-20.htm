Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
My soul is consumed with longing
This phrase reflects a deep, intense desire for God's Word and His righteous decrees. The psalmist's use of "soul" indicates that this longing is not superficial but deeply rooted in his innermost being. This mirrors the biblical theme of yearning for God, as seen in Psalm 42:1, "As the deer pants for streams of water, so my soul pants for you, my God." The intensity of this longing suggests a spiritual hunger that can only be satisfied by divine truth.

for Your judgments
"Judgments" here refers to God's laws, decrees, and decisions. In the context of the Old Testament, these judgments are part of the Torah, the first five books of the Bible, which contain God's instructions for living a life pleasing to Him. The psalmist's desire for God's judgments indicates a commitment to understanding and living by God's standards. This aligns with the wisdom literature's emphasis on the value of God's commandments, as seen in Proverbs 2:1-5, which encourages seeking wisdom and understanding as one would search for hidden treasures.

at all times
The phrase "at all times" underscores the constancy and persistence of the psalmist's longing. It suggests a continuous, unwavering desire to align with God's will, regardless of circumstances. This reflects the biblical call to meditate on God's law day and night, as instructed in Joshua 1:8. The psalmist's commitment to seeking God's judgments at all times serves as a model for believers to maintain a consistent and enduring pursuit of God's truth throughout their lives.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Psalmist
The author of Psalm 119, traditionally believed to be King David, who expresses a deep longing for God's laws and judgments.

2. God
The divine author of the laws and judgments that the psalmist longs for.

3. Judgments
Refers to God's decrees, laws, and commandments, which are central to the psalmist's desire and spiritual life.
Teaching Points
Longing for God's Word
The psalmist's soul is consumed with a desire for God's judgments, illustrating the importance of cultivating a deep yearning for Scripture in our own lives.

Continuous Desire
The phrase "at all times" emphasizes the need for a constant and unwavering desire for God's guidance and wisdom, not just in times of trouble or need.

Spiritual Nourishment
Just as physical hunger drives us to seek food, spiritual longing should drive us to seek God's word for sustenance and growth.

Alignment with God's Will
By longing for God's judgments, we align our desires with His will, seeking to understand and live according to His divine plan.

Transformation through Scripture
A deep longing for God's word leads to transformation, as we allow His judgments to shape our thoughts, actions, and character.

What Does Psalm 119:20 Mean
My soul is consumed

• The psalmist speaks of a wholehearted, all-absorbing experience; his very inner being is “eaten up” with desire.

• Similar language appears in Psalm 42:1-2, “As the deer pants for streams of water, so my soul longs after You, O God”, and in Psalm 84:2, where the heart and flesh “cry out” for the living God.

• Such intensity underscores that a relationship with God is not a casual hobby but the central passion of life.

• Isaiah 26:9 echoes this: “My soul longs for You in the night; yes, my spirit within me seeks You early”.

with longing

• “Longing” conveys persistent yearning, not a passing whim.

• Psalm 63:1 illustrates it: “My soul thirsts for You; my body yearns for You in a dry and weary land without water”.

• Romans 8:23 speaks of believers “groaning inwardly” for full redemption—another picture of holy ache.

• Philippians 1:23 shows Paul “torn between the two” desires, demonstrating that longing for God reshapes every other priority.

for Your judgments

• “Judgments” refers to God’s decisions, decrees, and standards revealed in His Word—true and right in every case (Psalm 19:9; Revelation 15:3).

• The psalmist is not fearing condemnation but craving clear guidance and righteous verdicts.

• Psalm 119 repeatedly highlights this craving: verses 7, 30, 75 celebrate trusting and learning God’s judgments.

• Romans 11:33 exclaims, “Oh, the depth of the riches of the wisdom and knowledge of God! How unsearchable are His judgments”—fueling the desire to know them more.

at all times

• The longing is continual, not seasonal. True devotion presses on “day and night” (Joshua 1:8).

• Psalm 34:1 models this constancy: “I will bless the LORD at all times; His praise will always be on my lips”.

• 1 Thessalonians 5:17 mirrors the idea with “pray without ceasing”—a life rhythm centered on God’s presence and precepts.

• Such constancy guards against spiritual complacency and keeps the heart aligned with divine truth.

summary

Psalm 119:20 pictures a believer whose entire being is captivated by an ongoing, intense hunger for God’s righteous decisions. This longing is not fleeting but continuous, reaching for the clarity, wisdom, and purity that only God’s Word supplies. Living in that posture reorients every moment around His perfect, trustworthy judgments, anchoring the soul in joy and steadfast devotion.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(20) Breaketh.--The Hebrew is peculiar to this place and Lamentations 3:16. The LXX., Vulg., and Aquila have "greatly desired;" Symmachus, "was perfect;" Theodotion, "had confidence;" Jerome, "longed," all which point either to a different reading or to a different sense from that which is given in the lexicons to the word.
