I will crush his foes before him
This phrase reflects God's promise of protection and victory for His anointed, often understood as King David and his descendants. The imagery of crushing foes is consistent with the biblical theme of God as a divine warrior who fights on behalf of His people (Exodus 15:3). Historically, this can be seen in the numerous battles where Israel triumphed over their enemies, such as David's victories over the Philistines (2 Samuel 5:17-25). Theologically, this points to God's sovereignty and His commitment to uphold His covenant with David (2 Samuel 7:12-16). In a broader sense, this can be seen as a type of Christ, who ultimately defeats all spiritual enemies (1 Corinthians 15:25).

and strike down those who hate him
This phrase emphasizes God's active role in defending His chosen leader against adversaries. The act of striking down is a demonstration of divine justice against those who oppose God's anointed. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, kings were often seen as representatives of God on earth, and opposition to them was tantamount to opposition to God Himself. This is echoed in the New Testament, where Jesus, the ultimate anointed one, faces opposition but is ultimately vindicated by God (Acts 2:23-24). The phrase also serves as a reminder of the ultimate judgment that awaits those who reject God's authority (Revelation 19:11-21).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Ethan the Ezrahite
The author of Psalm 89, who is traditionally believed to be a wise man during the time of King Solomon. He is credited with writing this psalm, which is a contemplation on God's promises to David.

2. David
The psalm is a reflection on God's covenant with David, promising him protection and the establishment of his throne. David is the central figure in this promise, representing God's chosen king.

3. Enemies/Foes
The adversaries of David, who are mentioned as those whom God will crush and strike down. These foes symbolize opposition to God's anointed and, by extension, opposition to God's plans.

4. God
The ultimate protector and deliverer, who promises to act on behalf of His anointed, ensuring victory over enemies.

5. Covenant
The Davidic Covenant is the backdrop of this psalm, highlighting God's promise to establish David's lineage and protect his kingdom.
Teaching Points
God's Faithfulness to His Promises
God is unwavering in His commitments. Just as He promised to protect David, He remains faithful to His promises to us today.

Divine Protection
Believers can trust in God's protection against spiritual and physical adversaries. This assurance allows us to live boldly in faith.

The Power of God's Anointed
The anointing of God carries with it divine authority and protection. As Christians, we are anointed through Christ and can rely on His power in our lives.

Victory Over Opposition
God ensures victory for His people. This victory may not always be immediate or visible, but it is assured in His timing and way.

Trust in God's Sovereignty
Understanding that God is in control allows us to rest in His plans, even when faced with challenges or opposition.

What Does Psalm 89:23 Mean
I will crush

• God Himself speaks, revealing that the victory is His work, not merely the king’s military skill.

• The verb is decisive—complete destruction, not a temporary setback (Genesis 3:15; Romans 16:20; Psalm 68:21).

• Because Scripture is true in every detail, we can rest in the certainty that what God says He will do, He will do—no enemy can withstand His resolve (Numbers 23:19).

his foes

• “Foes” are the personal enemies of the Davidic king, yet they are also God’s enemies because they oppose His chosen servant (Psalm 110:1; 1 Samuel 2:10).

• By extension, all who resist the Messiah fit this category (Acts 4:25-28).

• The verse underscores God’s covenant loyalty: He defends those He has anointed (2 Samuel 7:9).

before him

• The crushing happens “before him,” meaning right in the king’s presence. The anointed witness­es God’s power firsthand (Exodus 14:13-14; Deuteronomy 31:3).

• This visibility boosts faith, reminding the king—and us—that deliverance is not abstract but observable (Joshua 10:10).

• It also warns opponents: defiance will be publicly overturned.

and strike down

• “Strike down” intensifies the promise—God not only disables, He eliminates resistance (Isaiah 11:4; Revelation 19:15).

• The phrase conveys swift, targeted action, leaving no doubt about the outcome (1 Samuel 17:50-51).

• For believers today, it assures us that evil will not merely be restrained; it will be decisively judged.

those who hate him

• Hatred of God’s anointed springs from hostility toward God Himself (John 15:23-25).

• Psalm 81:15 notes, “Those who hate the LORD would pretend submission to Him, but their doom would last forever”, echoing the fate described here.

• Opposition is ultimately spiritual (Ephesians 6:12), yet God pledges to deal with every manifestation, whether human or demonic (Luke 19:27).

summary

Psalm 89:23 delivers a triple assurance: God personally acts, He targets every adversary of His anointed, and He does so openly and conclusively. Because the promise rests on God’s unchanging character and covenant faithfulness, we can face opposition with confidence, knowing the Lord Himself will crush, strike down, and forever silence all who oppose His chosen King—and by extension, all who belong to Him.
