The descendants of His servants will inherit it
This phrase emphasizes the promise of inheritance for the faithful, echoing the covenantal language found throughout the Old Testament. The "descendants" refer to the spiritual lineage of those who serve God, reminiscent of the promises made to Abraham in Genesis 12:7, where God assures him that his offspring will inherit the land. This inheritance is not merely physical but also spiritual, pointing to the eternal promises fulfilled in Christ. The concept of inheritance is central to the biblical narrative, symbolizing the blessings and responsibilities bestowed upon God's people. In the New Testament, this is expanded to include all believers as heirs with Christ (Romans 8:17).

and those who love His name will settle in it
Loving God's name implies a deep, personal relationship with Him, characterized by reverence and obedience. This love is not just emotional but is demonstrated through faithful living. The promise of settling in the land is a metaphor for peace and security, reflecting the ultimate rest found in God's presence. This phrase connects to the broader biblical theme of God preparing a place for His people, as seen in John 14:2-3, where Jesus speaks of preparing a place for His followers. The idea of "settling" also points to the eschatological hope of the new heavens and new earth, where God's people will dwell with Him eternally (Revelation 21:1-3).

Persons / Places / Events

2. Servants of God

3. Descendants
The future generations of God's servants, highlighting the promise of inheritance and continuity of God's blessings.

4. Inheritance
The land or blessings promised by God, often symbolizing spiritual inheritance and eternal life.

5. Those who love His name
Individuals who have a deep reverence and love for God, emphasizing a personal and relational aspect of faith.
Teaching Points
The Promise of Inheritance
God's promises extend beyond the immediate to future generations, assuring us of His faithfulness and the enduring nature of His covenant.

The Importance of Loving God's Name
Loving God's name is not just about reverence but involves a deep, personal relationship with Him, which leads to spiritual blessings and inheritance.

Faithfulness Across Generations
Our faithfulness impacts not only our lives but also the lives of our descendants, encouraging us to live in a way that honors God and sets a foundation for future generations.

Spiritual Inheritance Over Material
While the verse speaks of inheritance, the focus is on spiritual blessings and eternal life, reminding us to prioritize spiritual over material wealth.

Living as God's Servants
Being a servant of God involves obedience, devotion, and a commitment to His will, which leads to the fulfillment of His promises in our lives.

What Does Psalm 69:36 Mean
The descendants of His servants will inherit it

“The descendants of His servants will inherit it” (Psalm 69:36a)

• God pledges a literal future for the faithful line of His people. This echoes Genesis 17:7-8, where the Lord promises Abraham’s offspring an everlasting possession.

• The “it” refers to Zion mentioned in verse 35 (“For God will save Zion and rebuild the cities of Judah”). The rebuilding is not merely metaphorical; Scripture repeatedly presents Jerusalem’s restoration as a concrete event (Isaiah 62:1-7; Jeremiah 33:10-11).

• Inheritance language underscores permanence. Just as Israel received Canaan by covenant (Deuteronomy 4:37-38), believers in Christ are promised an incorruptible inheritance kept in heaven (1 Peter 1:3-4), to be fully realized when Christ reigns on earth (Revelation 5:10; 20:6).

• The verse affirms God’s faithfulness to successive generations. Psalm 102:28 states, “The children of Your servants will dwell securely, and their descendants will be established before You,” reinforcing that covenant blessings outlast individual lifetimes.

• Practically, this assures us that present obedience influences future blessing. Our legacy in Christ matters (Proverbs 20:7; 2 Timothy 1:5).

Those who love His name will settle in it

“and those who love His name will settle in it” (Psalm 69:36b)

• Loving God’s name—His revealed character—marks true believers (Psalm 5:11; Malachi 3:16-18). Genuine affection leads to abiding, not just visiting.

• “Settle” conveys peace and security, pointing to the millennial and eternal dwelling of God’s people (Isaiah 60:21; Ezekiel 37:25). Revelation 21:3-4 pictures this final settlement where “God Himself will be with them.”

• The clause widens the promise beyond ethnic Israel to all who revere the Lord. Romans 10:12-13 affirms, “For everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved,” aligning Gentile believers with Israel’s hope (Ephesians 2:19-22).

• The verse anticipates New-Covenant fulfillment: Hebrews 12:22-24 presents believers already spiritually come to “Mount Zion,” yet awaiting full manifestation.

• On a daily level, loving His name anchors us amid upheaval. Psalm 91:14 assures, “Because he loves Me, I will deliver him,” tying affection for God to present protection and future habitation.

summary

Psalm 69:36 promises that God’s loyal servants and all who cherish His name will receive and inhabit the restored Zion. The pledge is literal, rooted in God’s unbreakable covenant, and foreshadows the secure, everlasting home Christ secures for His people. Our present love and obedience link us to that future inheritance, assuring us that God preserves both His promises and His people through every generation.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 36. - The seed also of his servants shall inherit it. Nor shall the city alone - the mere walls and buildings - continue to exist. "The seed of God's servants" - his people Israel - shall continue to inhabit it. And they that love his Name shall dwell therein. When the earthly Zion fell away and forfeited the promises, they passed to the heavenly Zion (Hebrews 12:22) - the Church of God, the true Israel.
