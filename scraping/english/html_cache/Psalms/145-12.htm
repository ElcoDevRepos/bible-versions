Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
to make known to men
This phrase emphasizes the importance of declaring God's works to all humanity. The act of making known suggests a deliberate and active effort to communicate. In biblical context, this aligns with the Great Commission (Matthew 28:19-20), where Jesus instructs His followers to spread the gospel to all nations. Historically, the Israelites were chosen to be a light to the Gentiles (Isaiah 49:6), tasked with revealing God's character and deeds to the world. This phrase underscores the responsibility of believers to share their faith and testify to God's power and goodness.

Your mighty acts
The mighty acts refer to the powerful deeds and miracles performed by God throughout history. In the Old Testament, these include the creation of the world, the deliverance of Israel from Egypt, and the numerous victories in battles. These acts demonstrate God's sovereignty and power over creation and history. In the New Testament, the ultimate mighty act is the resurrection of Jesus Christ, which signifies victory over sin and death. This phrase invites reflection on God's intervention in both personal and communal history, encouraging believers to trust in His continued power and presence.

and the glorious splendor of Your kingdom
The kingdom of God is a central theme in Scripture, representing God's rule and reign over all creation. The glorious splendor suggests beauty, majesty, and divine perfection. In the historical context of Israel, the kingdom was often associated with the Davidic monarchy, which foreshadowed the eternal kingdom established through Christ. Jesus frequently spoke of the kingdom of God, describing it as both a present reality and a future hope (Luke 17:20-21, Revelation 21:1-4). This phrase calls believers to recognize and proclaim the beauty and majesty of God's kingdom, which is characterized by righteousness, peace, and joy in the Holy Spirit (Romans 14:17).

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of Psalm 145, King David, is known for his deep relationship with God and his role as a leader of Israel. This psalm is a hymn of praise, reflecting his heart for worship and his desire to declare God's greatness.

2. God's Kingdom
The psalm speaks of God's kingdom, which is characterized by His mighty acts and glorious splendor. It is a kingdom that transcends earthly realms and is eternal in nature.

3. Israel
As the chosen people of God, Israel is often the recipient of God's mighty acts, which David seeks to proclaim to all men.
Teaching Points
Proclaiming God's Works
As believers, we are called to actively share the accounts of God's mighty acts in our lives and throughout history. This is a form of worship and witness.

Understanding God's Kingdom
Reflect on the nature of God's kingdom, which is not just a future hope but a present reality that influences how we live and interact with the world.

Living in Awe of God's Splendor
Cultivate a heart of gratitude and awe for the splendor of God's kingdom, allowing it to transform our perspective and priorities.

Engaging in Evangelism
Use opportunities in daily life to share the gospel, making known the glorious splendor of God's kingdom to those around us.

What Does Psalm 145:12 Mean
To make known

The verse begins with a clear purpose: “to make known.” God’s people are not to keep His greatness private. They are commissioned to publicize it.

• Psalm 78:4 declares, “We will not hide them from their children, but will declare to the next generation the praises of the LORD and His power.”

• Isaiah 12:4 echoes, “Give thanks to the LORD, proclaim His name; make His works known among the nations.”

Sharing is not optional; it is obedience. Every testimony, every retelling of God’s rescue, magnifies Him and invites others to trust Him.

To men

The audience is broad—“men” in the generic sense of all people.

• Psalm 22:27 foresees, “All the ends of the earth will remember and turn to the LORD.”

• Acts 1:8 shows the pattern: Jerusalem, Judea, Samaria, “to the ends of the earth.”

God’s deeds are too glorious to be limited to one group. The reach of our witness must match the reach of His mercy.

Your mighty acts

The content of the message starts with power in action.

• Exodus 15:11 marvels, “Who is like You—majestic in holiness, awesome in glory, working wonders?”

• Psalm 106:8–10 recounts the Red Sea deliverance; Luke 1:51 praises God who “has performed mighty deeds with His arm.”

Bullet-point reminders of those mighty acts:

– Creation itself (Genesis 1)

– The flood and rescue of Noah (Genesis 7–8)

– Israel’s exodus and wilderness provision (Psalm 105:37–45)

– The cross and resurrection (1 Corinthians 15:3–4)

Each showcases God’s unmatched strength and faithfulness.

And the glorious splendor

“Mighty acts” point to works; “glorious splendor” highlights beauty and majesty.

• Psalm 96:6 tells us, “Splendor and majesty are before Him.”

• Psalm 104:1 exclaims, “O LORD my God, You are very great; You are clothed with splendor and majesty.”

His glory is not abstract; it captivates hearts, stirs worship, and silences pride (Habakkuk 2:14).

Of Your kingdom

All the power and beauty culminate in a realm—God’s kingdom.

• Psalm 103:19 affirms, “The LORD has established His throne in heaven, and His kingdom rules over all.”

• Daniel 4:3 declares, “His kingdom is an everlasting kingdom.”

• Matthew 6:10 teaches us to pray, “Your kingdom come,” showing present longing and future fulfillment.

Revelation 11:15 looks ahead: “The kingdom of the world has become the kingdom of our Lord and of His Christ, and He will reign forever and ever.”

Proclaiming the kingdom points people to the rightful King, invites allegiance, and offers hope that history is moving toward His perfect rule.

summary

Psalm 145:12 captures a mission statement: publicly announce God’s unmatched deeds and His breathtaking majesty so every person can recognize and submit to His eternal kingdom. Our role is to broadcast His power, beauty, and reign until the whole earth is filled with His glory.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 12. - To make known to the sons of men his mighty acts. It is a part of the duty of "saints" (ver. 10) to make known as widely as possible - if possible, to all men - the "mighty acts" and glory of God; primarily, for God's glory; and secondarily, to bring about their conversion to God's service. And the glorious majesty of his kingdom (comp. vers. 5, 11).
