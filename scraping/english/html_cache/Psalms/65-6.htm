You formed the mountains by Your power
This phrase emphasizes God's role as the Creator, highlighting His omnipotence. The formation of mountains is a testament to His creative power, as seen in Genesis 1:9-10, where God commands the dry land to appear. Mountains often symbolize stability and permanence in Scripture, reflecting God's unchanging nature (Psalm 90:2). The imagery of mountains also points to God's sovereignty over creation, as seen in Job 38:4-6, where God questions Job about the foundations of the earth. The mountains serve as a reminder of God's majesty and the awe-inspiring nature of His works.

having girded Yourself with might
This phrase portrays God as a warrior prepared for action, a common biblical motif. The imagery of girding oneself is associated with readiness and strength, as seen in Ephesians 6:14, where believers are instructed to gird themselves with truth. God's might is a recurring theme throughout the Psalms, emphasizing His ability to protect and deliver His people (Psalm 24:8). The concept of divine might is also linked to God's role as the ultimate judge and ruler, as seen in Isaiah 40:10, where God comes with power to rule and reward. This phrase underscores God's active involvement in sustaining and governing the world, reflecting His omnipotence and authority.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty in Creation
Recognize that God is the ultimate Creator and Sustainer of all things. The mountains, with their grandeur and stability, are a testament to His sovereign power.

The Power of God
Reflect on the might of God, who not only formed the mountains but also sustains them. This same power is at work in our lives, providing strength and stability.

Trust in God's Strength
Just as the mountains stand firm, we can trust in God's strength and might to uphold us in times of trouble. His power is a source of comfort and assurance.

Worship the Creator
Let the majesty of creation lead us to worship the Creator. The beauty and strength of the mountains should inspire awe and reverence for God.

God's Might in Our Lives
Consider how God's might is evident in our personal lives. Just as He formed the mountains, He is actively shaping and guiding our paths.

What Does Psalm 65:6 Mean
You formed the mountains

• David begins by spotlighting God as the direct, hands-on Maker of the mountains. This is no poetic exaggeration; Scripture consistently teaches that the same Lord “laid the foundations of the earth” (Job 38:4-6) and that “the mountain peaks belong to Him” (Psalm 95:4-5).

• The mountains stand as silent testimonies to His craftsmanship—majestic, immovable, and ancient. When Genesis 1:9-10 records the emergence of dry land, it is describing the literal moment God shaped the very landmasses that would rise into ranges and peaks.

• Psalm 65, as a whole, praises God for His providential care over creation (vv. 9-13). Verse 6 zooms in on the mountains as Exhibit A of that care. Their sheer size and permanence shout that the universe is not random; it is the deliberate work of a personal Creator.

by Your power

• The verse grounds the formation of mountains in divine power, not natural forces acting on their own. Jeremiah 10:12 echoes the thought: “The LORD made the earth by His power.”

• Power here is more than raw force; it is the sovereign ability to bring matter, order, and beauty into existence. As Psalm 33:9 notes, “For He spoke, and it came to be; He commanded, and it stood firm.”

• This same power sustains creation moment by moment (Colossians 1:16-17). When we see a mountain range, we are looking at an ongoing display of God’s might, a daily reminder that “nothing will be impossible with God” (Luke 1:37).

• Because that power is personal, believers can trust Him for both cosmic and everyday needs. Romans 1:20 affirms that creation reveals “His eternal power and divine nature,” leaving humanity without excuse.

having girded Yourself with might

• The picture is of God arming or clothing Himself as a warrior. Psalm 93:1 says, “The LORD reigns! He is robed in majesty; the LORD has clothed and armed Himself with strength.”

• Girding highlights readiness and intentionality. God does not merely possess strength; He actively prepares and applies it for His purposes—whether shaping continents or upholding His people.

• Isaiah 40:10 links this readiness to His reign: “Behold, the Lord GOD comes with power, and His arm rules for Him.” The same arm that carved mountains is stretched out to protect, guide, and redeem.

• For the worshiper, the line encourages reverent confidence. The One who belts Himself with might is never caught off guard. Life’s upheavals may feel like shifting ground, but the God who established mountains cannot be shaken.

summary

Psalm 65:6 celebrates the Lord as the intentional Creator who physically formed the mountains, did so by His unmatched power, and continues to rule with self-girded strength. The verse calls us to marvel at the grandeur of His handiwork and to rest in the assurance that the God who shaped the world’s highest peaks is both able and willing to sustain those who trust Him.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(6) Girded.--We see the Divine Architect of the world, girt for his labours in the Oriental fashion (see Note, Psalm 18:32), setting the mountains firm on their bases (comp. Psalm 75:3), the poet evidently thinking at the same time how empires, as well as mountains, owe their stability to God.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 6-8. - God having been praised for his moral qualities, is now further eulogized in respect of his doings in nature. The mountains set forth his majesty and permanence (ver. 6); the seas and waves, his power to control and subdue (ver. 7); the outgoings of the morning and evening - sunrise and sunset - his gracious loving kindness (ver. 8). Verse 6. - Which by his strength setteth fast the mountains (comp. Psalm 36:6; Psalm 95:4; Amos 4:13). The mountains are an emblem of God's strength and firmness and fixedness. They stand up in still and silent majesty; they seem as if they could never be moved. He who created them must be girded with power (camp. Psalm 93:1).
