Ascribe the power to God
This phrase calls believers to recognize and declare God's supreme power. In biblical context, "ascribe" means to attribute or give credit. This is a call to worship, acknowledging God's sovereignty and omnipotence. Throughout Scripture, God's power is evident in creation (Genesis 1), in His deliverance of Israel from Egypt (Exodus 14), and in His ongoing sustenance of the world (Colossians 1:17). The act of ascribing power to God is an acknowledgment of His rightful place as the ultimate authority over all things.

Whose majesty is over Israel
This phrase highlights God's special relationship with Israel, His chosen people. Historically, Israel was set apart by God to be a nation through which He would reveal Himself to the world (Deuteronomy 7:6-8). God's majesty over Israel is seen in His covenant with Abraham (Genesis 12:1-3), His guidance through the wilderness (Exodus 13:21-22), and His establishment of the Davidic kingdom (2 Samuel 7:12-16). This majesty is also prophetic, pointing to the coming of Jesus Christ, the Messiah, who fulfills God's promises to Israel (Matthew 1:1-17).

Whose strength is in the skies
This phrase emphasizes God's omnipotence and His dominion over the heavens. In ancient Near Eastern culture, the skies were often associated with the divine realm. God's strength in the skies is a reminder of His control over the natural world, as seen in the creation account (Genesis 1:6-8) and in His ability to command the weather (Job 37:5-6). This imagery also connects to the New Testament, where Jesus' ascension into heaven (Acts 1:9-11) and His promised return from the skies (1 Thessalonians 4:16-17) affirm His divine authority and power.

Persons / Places / Events
1. God
The central figure in this verse, God is depicted as powerful and majestic, deserving of praise and recognition for His strength and authority.

2. Israel
The nation over which God's majesty is declared. Israel is often seen as God's chosen people, through whom He reveals His power and glory.

3. The Skies
Symbolic of the heavens, representing the vastness and omnipresence of God's strength and authority.
Teaching Points
Recognizing God's Sovereignty
Acknowledge that God's power and majesty are unparalleled. This recognition should lead to a life of worship and reverence.

God's Presence with His People
Just as God's majesty is over Israel, His presence is with His people today. This should encourage believers to trust in His guidance and protection.

The Vastness of God's Strength
The imagery of God's strength in the skies serves as a reminder of His omnipotence. Believers can find comfort in knowing that God's power is limitless and available to them.

Ascribing Glory to God
Actively ascribe glory and power to God in daily life through prayer, worship, and obedience. This is a practical way to honor Him.

Living in Awe of God's Majesty
Cultivate a sense of awe and wonder at God's creation and His works, which can deepen faith and inspire worship.

What Does Psalm 68:34 Mean
Ascribe power to God

“ “Ascribe the power to God ” calls us to deliberately recognize and declare that all might belongs to Him alone.

• This is not a polite suggestion; it’s a joyful duty. Just as Psalm 29:1-2 proclaims, “Ascribe to the LORD glory and strength,” the worshiper here joins heaven’s chorus, crediting every victory and blessing to God.

• When David offered the throne to Solomon, he prayed, “Yours, O LORD, is the greatness and the power” (1 Chronicles 29:11). Whether in royal courts or private devotion, believers echo that same confession.

• This verse invites hearts to shift from self-reliance to God-dependence. James 1:17 reminds us that every good gift “comes down from the Father of lights.” We therefore ascribe what is already His.

Whose majesty is over Israel

God’s royal splendor rests “over Israel,” affirming His covenant faithfulness.

• Deuteronomy 33:26-29 pictures Him “riding the heavens” in majestic rescue of His people, a direct link to the imagery of Psalm 68, where He leads captive captors home (v. 18).

• Psalm 33:12 states, “Blessed is the nation whose God is the LORD, the people He has chosen.” Israel’s history—Exodus, conquest, exile, return—shows the King consistently ruling for their good and His glory.

• The phrase isn’t restrictive; it sets a precedent. Romans 15:8-9 explains that Christ confirmed God’s promises to Israel so “the Gentiles might glorify God for His mercy.” His majesty extends worldwide, but Israel remains the living proof of His throne.

Whose strength is in the skies

“Strength in the skies” points to the sheer scope of God’s power.

• Creation itself testifies: “The heavens declare the glory of God” (Psalm 19:1). Every sunrise, thunderclap, and constellation is a billboard of His might.

• Psalm 104:3 portrays Him “making the clouds His chariot,” while Job 38:4-11 recounts Him laying earth’s foundations and commanding the seas. The skies are His arena, yet He stoops to strengthen His people on earth (Isaiah 40:26-31).

• By highlighting the heavens, the psalmist assures us that no force—cosmic or earthly—outranks our God. When life feels unstable, we remember the One who holds galaxies in place is the same Lord who promises, “I am with you always” (Matthew 28:20).

summary

Psalm 68:34 calls believers to:

• Acknowledge God as the sole source of all power.

• Rest in His covenant rule that proves faithful over Israel and, by extension, over us.

• Marvel at His limitless strength displayed in the very skies above.

Responding with worship and trust, we join the psalmist in exalting the God whose power, majesty, and strength never fail.
