Look upon my affliction and rescue me
This phrase is a plea for divine intervention and deliverance. The psalmist is asking God to notice his suffering and act to save him. In the biblical context, affliction often refers to persecution or hardship faced by the faithful. This echoes the cries of the Israelites in Egypt (Exodus 3:7-8) and the prayers of the prophets. The request for rescue is a common theme in the Psalms, reflecting a deep trust in God's ability to save. It also foreshadows the ultimate deliverance through Jesus Christ, who is seen as the rescuer of humanity from sin and death (Colossians 1:13-14).

for I have not forgotten Your law
The psalmist emphasizes his faithfulness to God's commandments despite his suffering. This reflects the covenant relationship between God and His people, where obedience to the law is a sign of loyalty and love (Deuteronomy 6:6-9). The law here is not just a set of rules but a guide for living in righteousness. The psalmist's commitment to the law is reminiscent of the righteous man in Psalm 1, who delights in the law of the Lord. This phrase also highlights the importance of remembering and meditating on God's word, a theme that runs throughout Psalm 119. In the New Testament, Jesus fulfills the law (Matthew 5:17), and believers are called to uphold its spirit through love and obedience (Romans 13:8-10).

Teaching Points
God's Awareness of Our Suffering
The psalmist's plea for God to "look upon" his affliction reminds us that God is aware of our struggles and is compassionate towards us.

The Importance of Remembering God's Law
Despite his affliction, the psalmist emphasizes that he has not forgotten God's law, highlighting the importance of staying grounded in Scripture during difficult times.

Trust in God's Deliverance
The request for rescue demonstrates a trust in God's ability and willingness to deliver us from our troubles, encouraging believers to rely on God's faithfulness.

Prayer as a Response to Affliction
The psalmist models turning to God in prayer when faced with suffering, teaching us to seek divine help and comfort through prayer.

Endurance Through Faithfulness
By not forgetting God's law, the psalmist shows that endurance through trials is possible by remaining faithful to God's teachings.

What Does Psalm 119:153 Mean
Look upon my affliction

- The psalmist opens with a direct plea for God’s personal attention. This is not merely a passing glance—it is an appeal for the Lord to gaze intently, to notice every detail of his hardship.

- Similar cries appear throughout the Psalms, such as “Turn to me and show me grace” (Psalm 25:16) and “See my misery and suffering” (Psalm 25:18). Each reflects confidence that the righteous Judge sees and cares.

- The believer today can echo this assurance. Romans 8:18 reminds us that present sufferings are “not comparable to the glory that will be revealed,” confirming that God’s observing eye always carries redemptive purpose.

and rescue me

- Observation alone is not enough; the psalmist seeks decisive intervention. He trusts that the God who sees also saves.

- Psalm 34:17 affirms, “The righteous cry out, and the LORD hears; He delivers them from all their troubles.” The action requested is immediate and complete—deliverance, not mere consolation.

- Notice the personal nature: “rescue me.” Salvation is not abstract. Just as the LORD redeemed Israel from Egypt (Exodus 14:30-31) and Daniel from the lions (Daniel 6:22-23), He still steps into individual stories.

- For believers, Christ’s finished work provides the ultimate rescue (Colossians 1:13-14). Every plea for deliverance stands on that secure foundation.

for I have not forgotten Your law

- The psalmist anchors his request in covenant fidelity. He obeys; therefore, he calls on God’s covenant promises.

- Proverbs 3:1-2 urges, “My son, do not forget My teaching,” linking remembrance to life and peace. Similarly, Joshua 1:8 connects meditation on the law with success.

- By claiming that he has not forgotten, the writer emphasizes ongoing meditation and obedience, not flawless perfection. Genuine loyalty to God’s Word fuels confident prayer (1 John 3:21-22).

- This line exposes a simple but profound dynamic: clinging to Scripture in hardship invites God’s active aid. Faithfulness to the Word and experience of deliverance go hand-in-hand (Psalm 119:92).

summary

Psalm 119:153 is a threefold petition: “Look, rescue, because.” The psalmist trusts the Lord’s attentive care, seeks immediate deliverance, and grounds his request in faithful obedience to God’s Word. For every believer who treasures Scripture, the verse offers a model: lean on God’s watchful eye, ask boldly for His saving action, and stand firmly on the promises made sure in His unchanging law.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 153. - Consider mine affliction (see above, vers. 50, 67, 71, 75, 92, etc.). And deliver me. The "affliction" intended is that which the psalmist suffers at the hands of his enemies, who "reproach" him (ver. 22), "deride" him (ver. 51), and otherwise "persecute" him (ver. 161). For I do not forget thy Law (comp. vers. 16, 83, 93, 109, 141, 176).
