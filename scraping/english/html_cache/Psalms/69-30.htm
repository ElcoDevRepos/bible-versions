I will praise God’s name in song
This phrase emphasizes the importance of worship through music, a practice deeply rooted in biblical tradition. The Psalms themselves are a collection of songs and prayers used in ancient Israelite worship. Music was a central part of temple worship, as seen in 1 Chronicles 15:16, where David appoints Levites to sing and play instruments. The act of praising God’s name signifies acknowledging His character and deeds, as His name represents His essence and attributes. This practice is seen throughout Scripture, such as in Exodus 15:1-2, where Moses and the Israelites sing a song of praise after crossing the Red Sea. The use of song in worship is a timeless expression of devotion and reverence, transcending cultural and historical boundaries.

and exalt Him with thanksgiving.
Exalting God with thanksgiving highlights the attitude of gratitude that believers are called to maintain. Thanksgiving is a recurring theme in the Psalms and throughout the Bible, as seen in passages like 1 Thessalonians 5:18, which instructs believers to give thanks in all circumstances. This phrase underscores the importance of recognizing God’s provision and blessings, fostering a heart of humility and dependence on Him. In the context of Psalm 69, which is a lament, the call to thanksgiving amidst suffering reflects a deep trust in God’s sovereignty and goodness. This attitude is exemplified by Jesus Christ, who gave thanks even before His crucifixion (Luke 22:19). Thanksgiving is not only an expression of worship but also a means of drawing closer to God, acknowledging His lordship and faithfulness.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
Traditionally attributed as the author of Psalm 69, David is expressing his deep distress and his reliance on God through praise and thanksgiving.

2. God
The central figure in the Psalm, God is the one whom David seeks to honor and magnify through his worship.

3. Jerusalem
While not directly mentioned in this verse, Jerusalem is often the backdrop for David's psalms, representing the center of worship and God's presence among His people.
Teaching Points
The Power of Praise
Praise is a powerful tool for believers, shifting focus from personal struggles to God's greatness. It is an act of faith that acknowledges God's sovereignty and goodness.

Thanksgiving as Worship
Thanksgiving is not just a response to blessings but a form of worship that magnifies God. It is a deliberate act of recognizing God's hand in every situation.

Consistency in Worship
David's commitment to praise God with song and thanksgiving, even in distress, teaches us the importance of consistent worship regardless of our circumstances.

The Name of God
Praising the name of God signifies acknowledging His character and attributes. It is a reminder to meditate on who God is and His faithfulness.

Magnifying God
To magnify God is to make His presence and power more evident in our lives. It involves focusing on His greatness rather than our problems.

What Does Psalm 69:30 Mean
I will praise

The psalmist begins with a personal, intentional choice: “I will.” Praise is not passive; it is an act of the will.

• Psalm 34:1 affirms the same resolve: “I will bless the LORD at all times; His praise will always be on my lips.”

• Hebrews 13:15 encourages believers to “continually offer to God a sacrifice of praise.”

Our worship is a decisive response to God’s character, whatever our circumstances (Psalm 69 itself comes from deep distress), showing that praise is rooted in faith, not feelings.

God’s name

To “praise God’s name” centers on His revealed character and reputation.

• Proverbs 18:10 reminds us, “The name of the LORD is a strong tower; the righteous run to it and are safe.”

• Exodus 34:5-7 shows God proclaiming His own name, revealing mercy, justice, and steadfast love.

When we lift up His name, we affirm every attribute He has made known—His holiness (Isaiah 6:3), His faithfulness (Lamentations 3:23), and His sovereignty (Psalm 99:1-3).

in song

Music gives voice to truth and stirs the heart.

• Psalm 33:2-3 urges, “Sing to Him a new song; play skillfully with a shout of joy.”

• Ephesians 5:19 calls us to “speak to one another with psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs, singing and making melody to the Lord.”

Singing unites head and heart, teaching doctrine while engaging emotion. Even when words fail, melody carries praise upward (Acts 16:25).

and exalt Him

To exalt is to lift high, placing God in the position He already holds.

• Psalm 34:3 invites, “Magnify the LORD with me; let us exalt His name together.”

• Psalm 145:1 declares, “I will exalt You, my God and King.”

Exalting shifts our focus from self to the supremacy of God, enlarging our vision of His greatness and aligning our lives under His rule (Philippians 2:9-11).

with thanksgiving

Thanksgiving keeps praise grounded in gratitude for God’s works.

• Colossians 3:17 instructs, “Whatever you do…do it all in the name of the Lord Jesus, giving thanks to God the Father through Him.”

• 1 Thessalonians 5:18 commands, “Give thanks in every circumstance, for this is God’s will for you in Christ Jesus.”

Gratitude fuels joy, combats anxiety (Philippians 4:6-7), and testifies to God’s goodness before a watching world (Psalm 107:1-2).

summary

Psalm 69:30 calls us to a deliberate lifestyle of worship: choosing to praise, focusing on God’s revealed name, expressing adoration through song, lifting Him high above all else, and doing it all with a grateful heart. Such praise glorifies God and strengthens believers, turning even seasons of hardship into platforms for His honor.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 30-36. - In conclusion, the psalmist bursts out into praise. Confident of receiving the deliverance for which he has prayed, he anticipates it by at once offering thanksgiving (ver. 30). He then calls on others to rejoice with him, first on the poor and humble (vers. 32, 33), then on heaven and earth and their inhabitants generally (ver. 34). Finally, he delivers a confident prophecy of the continued prosperity of Judah and Jerusalem (vers. 35, 36). Verse 30. - I will praise the Name of God with a song. (For praise of the Name of God, see Psalm 7:17; Psalm 9:2; Psalm 29:2; Psalm 34:3; Psalm 66:1; Psalm 68:4, etc.) And will magnify him with thanksgiving. Thanksgiving had already, in Psalm 50:13, 14, been set above sacrifice.
