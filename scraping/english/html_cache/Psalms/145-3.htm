Great is the LORD
This phrase emphasizes the supreme and unparalleled nature of God. In the biblical context, the greatness of the LORD is a recurring theme, highlighting His sovereignty and majesty. The Hebrew word for "great" (gadol) conveys a sense of magnitude and importance. Throughout the Old Testament, God's greatness is demonstrated through His creation, His acts of deliverance, and His covenant faithfulness. In Exodus 15:11, the greatness of God is celebrated in the song of Moses after the crossing of the Red Sea, illustrating His power over creation and His enemies.

and greatly to be praised;
The call to praise God greatly underscores the appropriate response to His greatness. Praise in the biblical sense involves both verbal expression and a lifestyle that honors God. The Psalms frequently call for exuberant and wholehearted worship, as seen in Psalm 150, which lists various instruments and actions in worship. The cultural context of ancient Israel included communal worship at the temple, where God's people gathered to offer sacrifices and sing praises. This phrase also connects to the New Testament, where believers are encouraged to offer a sacrifice of praise (Hebrews 13:15).

His greatness is unsearchable.
This statement acknowledges the infinite and incomprehensible nature of God's greatness. The term "unsearchable" suggests that human understanding is limited and cannot fully grasp the extent of God's majesty. Job 11:7-9 echoes this sentiment, questioning whether humans can fathom the mysteries of God. Theologically, this points to the doctrine of God's transcendence, which asserts that while God is knowable, He is beyond complete human comprehension. This concept is also reflected in Romans 11:33, where Paul marvels at the depth of God's wisdom and knowledge.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of Psalm 145, King David, is known for his deep relationship with God and his role as a leader of Israel. This psalm is a hymn of praise, reflecting David's heart for worship.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The central figure in this verse, Yahweh is the covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal and unchanging nature. The verse highlights His greatness and worthiness of praise.

3. Israel
The nation of Israel, to whom the psalm was originally addressed, is called to recognize and declare the greatness of God.
Teaching Points
The Incomparable Greatness of God
God's greatness is beyond human comprehension. We are called to acknowledge that His ways and thoughts are higher than ours.

A Call to Worship
Our response to God's greatness should be one of praise and worship. This is not just a duty but a natural response to recognizing who God is.

The Limitlessness of God's Nature
The term "unsearchable" reminds us that there is always more to learn and discover about God. Our journey with Him is one of continual growth and revelation.

Encouragement in Times of Doubt
When faced with uncertainty or challenges, remembering God's unsearchable greatness can provide comfort and assurance that He is in control.

Living a Life of Praise
As believers, our lives should reflect a continual attitude of praise, recognizing God's greatness in all circumstances.

What Does Psalm 145:3 Mean
Great is the LORD

– Scripture begins with a declaration, not a suggestion. “Great is the LORD” (Psalm 145:3) sets the tone for the whole psalm.

• His greatness is inherent; it is who He is, not what He does (Psalm 48:1; Jeremiah 10:6).

• Creation itself bears witness to His immeasurable greatness—consider the heavens in Psalm 19:1 and the sustaining power seen in Isaiah 40:12-26.

• Because His greatness is constant, our confidence in every promise is secure (Numbers 23:19; James 1:17).

and greatly to be praised

– The logical response to recognized greatness is significant, wholehearted praise.

• Praise is not optional or occasional; it is “greatly” due to Him (Psalm 96:4).

• Praise should be proportionate to His worth. David models continual praise in Psalm 34:1 and spontaneous praise in 2 Samuel 6:14-15.

• Corporate worship (Psalm 95:1-3) and personal devotion (Psalm 103:1-5) both flow from this command.

• In eternity the anthem continues: Revelation 4:11 shows heavenly beings reflecting the same truth—God’s greatness demands great praise.

His greatness is unsearchable

– Human limits meet divine infinity here.

• “Unsearchable” doesn’t mean unknowable; it means inexhaustible. We can probe, but never exhaust (Job 5:9; Job 9:10).

• Even God’s judgments and ways are “unsearchable” and “inscrutable” (Romans 11:33). This humbles intellect and fuels worship.

• It assures us He will always exceed our expectations—Ephesians 3:20 calls Him “able to do infinitely more than all we ask or imagine.”

• Mystery invites trust. When we cannot trace His hand, we still trust His heart, as Job did (Job 42:2-6).

summary

Psalm 145:3 stacks truth upon truth: who God is, how we respond, and why we will never reach the end of His magnificence. His inherent greatness calls for abundant praise, and the endless depths of His character keep our worship fresh forever.
