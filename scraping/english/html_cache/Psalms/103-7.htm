He made known His ways to Moses
This phrase highlights God's intimate communication with Moses, who is a central figure in the Old Testament. Moses is often seen as a type of Christ, serving as a mediator between God and His people. The "ways" of God refer to His character, laws, and plans, which were revealed to Moses on Mount Sinai (Exodus 19-20). This revelation included the Ten Commandments and the detailed instructions for the tabernacle, which symbolized God's presence among His people. Moses' unique relationship with God is emphasized in Numbers 12:6-8, where God speaks to him "face to face," unlike any other prophet. This direct communication underscores the special role Moses played in leading Israel and establishing the covenant relationship between God and His people.

His deeds to the people of Israel
The "deeds" refer to the mighty acts and miracles God performed on behalf of the Israelites, particularly during the Exodus from Egypt. These include the plagues, the parting of the Red Sea, and the provision of manna and water in the wilderness (Exodus 7-16). These acts demonstrated God's power, faithfulness, and commitment to His covenant promises. The historical context of these events is crucial, as they occurred during a time when Israel was transitioning from slavery to becoming a nation under God's rule. The deeds of God served as a testimony to His sovereignty and were meant to instill faith and obedience in the Israelites. This phrase also connects to the broader biblical narrative, where God's mighty works are continually recounted to remind His people of His faithfulness and to encourage trust in His future promises.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Moses
A central figure in the Old Testament, Moses was chosen by God to lead the Israelites out of Egyptian bondage. He is known for receiving the Ten Commandments and for his close relationship with God, often speaking to Him directly.

2. The People of Israel
The descendants of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, who were chosen by God to be His people. They experienced God's deliverance from Egypt and His provision and guidance in the wilderness.

3. God's Ways and Deeds
Refers to the manner in which God interacts with His people, including His laws, commandments, and miraculous acts. His "ways" imply His character and intentions, while His "deeds" are the actions He performs.
Teaching Points
Understanding God's Ways
Seek to know God's character and intentions through prayer and study of His Word, as Moses did. This deepens our relationship with Him and aligns our lives with His will.

Remembering God's Deeds
Regularly recall and give thanks for the ways God has worked in your life and in the lives of others. This strengthens faith and encourages trust in His future provision.

The Importance of Obedience
Just as the Israelites were called to follow God's commandments, we are called to live in obedience to His Word. This obedience is a response to His love and faithfulness.

God's Faithfulness to His People
Reflect on how God has been faithful to His promises throughout history and in your personal life. This assurance of His faithfulness provides hope and confidence in times of uncertainty.

The Role of Leaders in God's Plan
Recognize the importance of godly leadership, as exemplified by Moses, in guiding and teaching others about God's ways and deeds. Pray for and support leaders in your community and church.

What Does Psalm 103:7 Mean
He made known His ways to Moses

• This first line highlights that God did not keep His character hidden; He chose to reveal His “ways”—His principles, character, and intentions—to one man, Moses. Exodus 33:13 records Moses’ plea, “Now if I have indeed found favor in Your sight, please let me know Your ways,” and the Lord granted that request (Exodus 34:5-7).

• God’s “ways” include His mercy, justice, holiness, and covenant faithfulness. Deuteronomy 34:10-12 emphasizes that no prophet knew the LORD “face to face” like Moses, who witnessed both God’s glory and His gracious compassion.

• By revealing His ways, God invited Moses into intimate fellowship. Numbers 12:7-8 underscores that God spoke with Moses “clearly and not in riddles,” a privilege rooted in relationship rather than mere information.

• Practical takeaway: Knowing God’s ways equips believers to trust His heart even when circumstances confuse us (Psalm 25:4-10).

His deeds to the people of Israel

• While Moses experienced God’s ways, the nation saw God’s “deeds”—His mighty acts. The plagues in Egypt (Exodus 7-12), the Red Sea crossing (Exodus 14), water from the rock (Exodus 17:1-7), manna from heaven (Exodus 16:4-15), and victories over enemies (Joshua 24:5-13) showed the Lord’s power to save, protect, and provide.

• Psalm 78:12-16 recounts these deeds so future generations would remember. Deuteronomy 11:1-7 charges parents who personally witnessed God’s deeds to teach their children, linking national memory to obedience.

• Unlike the personal revelation given to Moses, the deeds were public and undeniable, intended to inspire faith and obedience in the whole community (Psalm 105:1-5).

• Practical takeaway: God’s visible works confirm the truth of His character. Reflecting on His past deeds fortifies present trust (Psalm 143:5).

summary

Psalm 103:7 shows God’s gracious pattern: He reveals His heart (“ways”) to those who seek Him personally, and He displays His hand (“deeds”) so that entire communities may know and trust Him. Moses enjoyed intimate knowledge of God’s character; Israel experienced unmistakable demonstrations of God’s power. Together, they testify that the LORD is both knowable and faithful, inviting every believer to seek His face and remember His works.

Verse 7. - He made known his ways unto Moses. God's ways are "past finding out" by man (Romans 11:33); they must be "made known" to him. God made them known to Moses by the revelations which he gave him, especially those of Sinai. His acts unto the children of Israel.
