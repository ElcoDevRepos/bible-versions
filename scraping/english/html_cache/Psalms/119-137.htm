Righteous are You, O LORD
This phrase acknowledges the inherent righteousness of God, a central theme throughout the Bible. The Hebrew word for "righteous" (צַדִּיק, tzaddik) conveys the idea of being just, morally right, and virtuous. God's righteousness is a foundational attribute, often contrasted with human unrighteousness. In the Old Testament, God's righteousness is demonstrated through His covenant faithfulness, as seen in His dealings with Israel. The righteousness of God is also a key theme in the New Testament, where it is fulfilled in Jesus Christ, who embodies God's perfect righteousness (2 Corinthians 5:21). This phrase reflects the psalmist's deep reverence and trust in God's moral perfection.

and upright are Your judgments
The term "judgments" (מִשְׁפָּטִים, mishpatim) refers to God's decrees, laws, and decisions. The word "upright" (יָשָׁר, yashar) suggests fairness, integrity, and straightness. God's judgments are not arbitrary but are based on His righteous character. In the context of ancient Israel, God's laws were given to guide the people in living a life that reflects His holiness (Leviticus 19:2). The psalmist's affirmation of God's upright judgments underscores the belief that God's laws are just and beneficial, providing a moral compass for His people. This aligns with the broader biblical narrative that God's Word is a lamp to our feet and a light to our path (Psalm 119:105). The ultimate expression of God's upright judgments is seen in the person of Jesus Christ, who perfectly fulfilled the law and the prophets (Matthew 5:17).

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Psalmist
The author of Psalm 119, traditionally believed to be King David, who expresses deep devotion to God's law and righteousness.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal, self-existent nature and His relationship with His people.

3. Judgments
Refers to God's decrees, laws, and decisions, which are described as upright and just.
Teaching Points
Understanding God's Righteousness
God's righteousness is intrinsic to His character. It is not merely an attribute but the essence of who He is. As believers, we are called to trust in His righteous nature, knowing that His ways are perfect and just.

Upright Judgments
God's judgments are upright, meaning they are fair, equitable, and true. In a world where human judgments can be flawed, we can rely on God's perfect justice. This should encourage us to seek His guidance in our decisions and to align our lives with His Word.

Living in Light of God's Righteousness
As followers of Christ, we are called to reflect God's righteousness in our lives. This involves living according to His commandments and striving to be upright in our dealings with others. Our lives should be a testimony to God's righteous character.

Trusting in God's Justice
In times of injustice or when we face trials, we can find comfort in knowing that God is just. He sees all and will ultimately bring about justice. This assurance should lead us to patience and perseverance, trusting in His perfect timing.

The Role of Scripture in Understanding Righteousness
The Bible is our guide to understanding God's righteousness. Regular study and meditation on Scripture help us to know God's character and His will for our lives. It equips us to live righteously and to discern His judgments.

What Does Psalm 119:137 Mean
Righteous are You

Psalm 119:137 opens with a straight-to-the-point declaration: “Righteous are You.”

• The psalmist is not discovering something new but affirming what is already revealed throughout Scripture—God’s moral perfection.

• Psalm 11:7 says, “For the LORD is righteous; He loves justice.” That same truth is echoed here.

• Deuteronomy 32:4 celebrates that “all His ways are just,” reminding us that God’s righteousness is woven into every act He performs.

• Because He is righteous, God is the unchanging standard for right and wrong (James 1:17). Our confidence doesn’t rest on shifting cultural norms; it rests on His flawless character.

O LORD

The personal, covenant name “LORD” (Yahweh) is intentionally placed in the middle of the verse.

• Using the covenant name anchors righteousness in relationship. The God who is righteous is also the God who draws near to His people (Exodus 3:14–15).

• This isn’t an abstract, distant deity. “The LORD is my shepherd” (Psalm 23:1) shows the same personal commitment.

• Calling Him “LORD” in prayer or song invites us to trust His character, knowing He keeps every promise (Lamentations 3:22-23).

and upright are Your judgments

The verse concludes by extending God’s righteousness to His “judgments.”

• “Judgments” points to every decision, decree, and written command that comes from God.

• Psalm 19:9 agrees: “The judgments of the LORD are true, altogether righteous.”

• Because His judgments are upright, Scripture is the final authority for faith and practice (2 Timothy 3:16-17).

• We can obey with confidence, knowing His commands are never arbitrary but always for our good (Psalm 119:68; Romans 8:28).

• God’s verdicts are never overturned or revised; they stand forever (Isaiah 40:8).

summary

Psalm 119:137 celebrates the seamless unity between who God is and what God says. He is inherently righteous; He relates to us personally as the LORD; and every judgment He renders is upright. Because His character and His Word are perfectly aligned, we can trust, obey, and rest in Him without hesitation.
