They confronted me in my day of calamity
This phrase reflects a time of intense distress and adversity faced by the psalmist, traditionally identified as David. The "day of calamity" can be understood as a period of severe trial or danger, possibly referring to the numerous challenges David faced, such as Saul's pursuit or battles with surrounding nations. Biblically, calamity often signifies divine testing or the presence of evil forces. The confrontation suggests an aggressive opposition, which can be seen in the broader context of spiritual warfare, as described in Ephesians 6:12, where believers are reminded of the struggle against spiritual forces of evil. Historically, David's life was marked by numerous confrontations, both physical and spiritual, which are documented in the books of Samuel and Chronicles.

but the LORD was my support
This phrase highlights the divine intervention and sustenance provided by God during times of trouble. The term "support" implies a foundation or a source of strength, akin to a rock or fortress, which is a recurring theme in the Psalms (e.g., Psalm 18:2). This reliance on God as a support is a central tenet of faith, emphasizing trust in God's sovereignty and protection. Theologically, it points to the covenant relationship between God and His people, where God promises to be their protector and deliverer. This concept is echoed in the New Testament, where Jesus Christ is seen as the ultimate support and cornerstone for believers (1 Peter 2:6). The assurance of God's support is a source of comfort and hope, encouraging believers to trust in God's unfailing presence and aid during their own times of calamity.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of Psalm 18, David is reflecting on his experiences of being pursued by his enemies and how God delivered him. This psalm is a song of gratitude for God's protection and support.

2. Enemies
The adversaries who confronted David during his times of distress. These could be literal enemies like King Saul or metaphorical ones representing trials and tribulations.

3. Day of Calamity
A period of great distress or trouble in David's life. This could refer to specific events such as Saul's pursuit of David or other times of personal crisis.

4. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His faithfulness and personal relationship with His people. In this verse, God is depicted as David's support and deliverer.

5. Support
The Hebrew word used here is "mishen," which means a support or stay, indicating God's role as a stabilizing force in times of trouble.
Teaching Points
God as Our Support
Just as God was David's support during his trials, He remains our steadfast support today. We can rely on Him in our own "days of calamity."

Facing Adversity with Faith
David's example teaches us to face our adversities with faith, trusting that God will uphold us even when others confront us.

The Reality of Trials
The presence of enemies and calamities is a reality for believers. However, these challenges are opportunities to experience God's faithfulness and deliverance.

Personal Relationship with God
The use of "the LORD" emphasizes a personal relationship. We are encouraged to cultivate a deep, personal connection with God, who is our support.

Gratitude for Deliverance
Like David, we should express gratitude for God's deliverance and support, recognizing His hand in our lives during difficult times.

What Does Psalm 18:18 Mean
They confronted me

• David records a real moment when hostile forces literally closed in on him; 2 Samuel 22:18–19 repeats the same words, tying them to specific battles during Saul’s pursuit.

• “They” were numerous, determined, and personal. Psalm 3:6 says, “I will not fear the thousands arrayed against me on every side,” and Psalm 27:2 adds, “When the wicked came upon me to devour my flesh, my enemies and foes stumbled and fell.”

• The clash was not only physical but moral and spiritual. Psalm 118:12 pictures enemies “swarming like bees,” showing how opposition can feel relentless.

• For believers today, confrontation can come through people, circumstances, or unseen spiritual forces (Ephesians 6:12). The verse reminds us that hostility is not an imaginary threat; it is expected for those who stand with God (John 15:18–19).

in my day of calamity

• The “day” is a specific season when everything seems to collapse at once—David’s most desperate hour. Proverbs 24:10 warns, “If you falter in a time of trouble, how small is your strength!”—but David did not falter.

• Scripture repeatedly acknowledges that God’s people face genuine calamity. Psalm 34:19 affirms, “Many are the afflictions of the righteous, but the LORD delivers him from them all.”

• This phrase reassures us that crises do not surprise God. Ephesians 6:13 speaks of “the day of evil,” urging believers to be prepared, not paralyzed.

• Calamity does not nullify God’s promises; it provides the backdrop against which His rescue shines brightest (Psalm 23:4).

but the LORD was my support

• The turning point comes with the word “but.” Human help failed, yet the covenant God stepped in. Psalm 54:4 declares, “Surely God is my helper; the Lord is the sustainer of my soul.”

• “Support” pictures steady, under-girding strength. Psalm 94:18 echoes, “If I say, ‘My foot is slipping,’ Your loving devotion, O LORD, supports me.”

• God’s support is personal—“my” support—showing intimate involvement. Isaiah 41:10 promises, “I will strengthen you; I will surely help you; I will uphold you with My righteous right hand.”

• The verse invites believers to shift their weight from self-reliance to divine reliance. Like David, we can testify that when everything else gives way, the LORD holds us up (2 Corinthians 12:9).

summary

Psalm 18:18 describes a real confrontation, a real calamity, and a real rescue. Enemies pressed hard, the crisis peaked, yet the LORD personally sustained His servant. Whatever form our “day of calamity” takes, the same faithful God stands ready to be the unshakable support of all who trust Him.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 18. - They prevented me in the day of my calamity (comp. 1 Samuel 23:13-15; 1 Samuel 24:1-3; 1 Samuel 26:1-4, etc.). But the Lord was my Stay. God frustrated all the designs of David's foes, and prevented him from falling into their hands.
