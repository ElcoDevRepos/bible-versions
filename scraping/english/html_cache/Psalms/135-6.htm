The LORD does all that pleases Him
This phrase emphasizes the sovereignty and omnipotence of God. Throughout Scripture, God's will is portrayed as supreme and unchallenged (Isaiah 46:10). The idea that God acts according to His pleasure underscores His freedom and authority over creation. This aligns with the biblical theme that God's purposes cannot be thwarted (Job 42:2). Theologically, this reflects the doctrine of divine providence, where God governs all things according to His will. It also highlights God's benevolent nature, as His pleasure is inherently good and just.

in the heavens
The heavens often symbolize God's dwelling place and His supreme authority over the universe (Psalm 115:3). In biblical cosmology, the heavens are not just the sky but the entire spiritual realm where God reigns. This phrase indicates that God's will is executed perfectly in the spiritual realm, aligning with the Lord's Prayer, "Your will be done, on earth as it is in heaven" (Matthew 6:10). The heavens also declare God's glory and handiwork (Psalm 19:1), reinforcing His creative power and majesty.

and on the earth
This phrase asserts God's dominion over the physical world. The earth is the realm of human activity, and God's involvement in earthly affairs is a recurring biblical theme. From the creation narrative in Genesis to the prophetic visions of a new earth in Revelation, God's interaction with the earth is central to His redemptive plan. This also connects to the idea of God's immanence, where He is actively present and working within His creation (Acts 17:24-28).

in the seas
The seas in ancient Near Eastern culture often represented chaos and danger. By stating that God does as He pleases in the seas, the psalmist affirms God's control over chaos and the natural order. This is reminiscent of God's power displayed in the parting of the Red Sea (Exodus 14) and Jesus calming the storm (Mark 4:39), both demonstrating divine authority over the waters. The seas also symbolize the vastness of God's creation and His ability to govern even the most unpredictable elements.

and in all their depths
The depths refer to the deepest parts of the seas, often associated with mystery and the unknown. This phrase highlights God's omnipresence and omniscience, as there is no part of creation beyond His reach or understanding (Psalm 139:7-12). In biblical literature, the depths can also symbolize the grave or Sheol, suggesting that God's sovereignty extends even over life and death. This comprehensive dominion assures believers of God's ultimate control and care over all aspects of existence.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty
Recognize that God is in control of all things, from the vast heavens to the deepest seas. His will is supreme, and nothing happens outside of His divine plan.

Trust in God's Plan
In times of uncertainty, remember that God does what pleases Him, and His plans are for our ultimate good, even when we cannot see it.

Worship the Creator
Acknowledge God's power and authority in your worship. He is worthy of praise because He governs all creation with wisdom and might.

Embrace Humility
Understanding God's sovereignty should lead us to humility, knowing that our lives are in the hands of a powerful and loving God.

Seek Alignment with God's Will
Strive to align your desires and actions with God's will, seeking His guidance in all areas of life.

What Does Psalm 135:6 Mean
The LORD

- The verse begins by naming the covenant God, “Yahweh,” reminding us immediately of His unique identity revealed to Israel (Exodus 3:14-15).

- This is the same LORD who declared in Isaiah 45:5, “I am the LORD, and there is no other; apart from Me there is no God,” grounding our confidence that we are dealing with the one true, living God.

- Because He is LORD, all that follows in the verse rests on His unchallenged authority (Psalm 115:3).

does all

- Notice the totality: not “some,” but “all.” He actually accomplishes what He wills, never thwarted (Job 42:2; Isaiah 46:10).

- This reinforces that His power is not theoretical; it is active and effective every moment.

that pleases Him

- God acts according to His own good pleasure, which flows from His holy and righteous nature (Ephesians 1:11; Romans 9:15-16).

- Because His character is perfect, what pleases Him is also what is ultimately good, wise, and just (Deuteronomy 32:4).

- For believers, this provides assurance that divine sovereignty is never arbitrary but always consistent with His loving purposes (Romans 8:28).

in the heavens

- The celestial realm, angels, cosmic forces—all operate under His command (Psalm 103:20-21; Daniel 4:35).

- Stars, planets, seasons move because He wills it (Genesis 1:14-18; Jeremiah 31:35-36).

- Even spiritual warfare is bounded by His authority (Job 1:12; Luke 22:31-32).

and on the earth

- History, nations, rulers rise and fall at His direction (Acts 17:26; Proverbs 21:1).

- Everyday details—from sparrows falling (Matthew 10:29-31) to our own steps (Proverbs 16:9)—unfold within His plan.

- Natural processes, harvests, weather submit to Him (Psalm 104:14; Amos 4:7).

in the seas

- Oceans symbolize chaos to ancient readers, yet God rules them effortlessly (Psalm 89:9; Jonah 1:4).

- Marine life, currents, and boundaries obey His voice (Genesis 1:21; Job 38:8-11).

- Jesus later demonstrates this dominion by calming the storm and walking on water (Mark 4:39; Matthew 14:25-27).

and in all their depths

- Nothing is hidden from Him, even in the most remote trenches (Psalm 139:7-12).

- The mysterious deeps—unknown to human exploration—are fully known and governed by God (Job 38:16).

- Evil forces sometimes depicted as lurking in deep waters are also under His leash (Revelation 20:1-3).

summary

Psalm 135:6 declares that the LORD’s sovereign will prevails everywhere—heaven, earth, seas, and the deepest realms. He does everything that pleases Him, and because His nature is holy, what pleases Him is always wise, good, and just. This truth anchors our trust, silences fear, and stirs worship, for the God who saved us in Christ rules all creation without limit or rival.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 6. - Whatsoever the Lord pleased, that did he (comp. Psalm 115:3). God's power is only limited by his own attributes of truth and goodness. He cannot contradict his own reason, or his own moral qualities. Otherwise he can do anything and everything. In heaven, and in earth, in the seas, and all deep places. This is intended as a complete division of space:

(1) the heavens above the earth;

(2) the earth and seas, in the middle sphere; and

(3) the abysses, or depths below the earth, as far as they can be conceived of as extending.
