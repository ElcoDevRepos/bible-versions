You who fear the LORD, praise Him!
This phrase calls upon those who have a reverent awe and respect for God to offer Him praise. The "fear of the LORD" is a recurring theme in the Bible, often associated with wisdom and obedience (Proverbs 9:10). It signifies a deep respect and acknowledgment of God's power and holiness. In the context of Psalm 22, which begins with a cry of anguish and ends in praise, this call to praise reflects a shift from suffering to trust in God's deliverance. The exhortation to praise is a common biblical response to recognizing God's sovereignty and faithfulness (Psalm 33:8).

All descendants of Jacob, honor Him!
The "descendants of Jacob" refers to the Israelites, the chosen people of God, tracing their lineage back to Jacob, also known as Israel. This phrase emphasizes the covenant relationship between God and the people of Israel. Honoring God involves recognizing His authority and living in a way that reflects His character and commandments. Historically, the Israelites were called to be a light to the nations, demonstrating God's holiness and justice (Isaiah 49:6). This call to honor God is a reminder of their identity and purpose as His people.

All offspring of Israel, revere Him!
The "offspring of Israel" reiterates the call to the nation of Israel, emphasizing their collective identity and responsibility to revere God. To "revere" means to hold in deep respect and veneration. This reverence is not just an emotional response but involves a commitment to worship and obedience. The use of both "descendants of Jacob" and "offspring of Israel" highlights the continuity of God's promises from the patriarchs to the present generation. This reverence is also prophetic, pointing to the ultimate fulfillment in Jesus Christ, who is the true Israel and through whom all nations are blessed (Galatians 3:16).

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal existence and faithfulness to His promises.

2. Descendants of Jacob
Refers to the Israelites, the chosen people of God, who are called to honor and praise Him.

3. Offspring of Israel
Another term for the Israelites, highlighting their identity as God's covenant people.

4. Psalmist (David)
Traditionally attributed to King David, who often expressed deep trust and reliance on God through his psalms.

5. Fear of the LORD
A reverential awe and respect for God, recognizing His holiness and authority.
Teaching Points
Reverence for God
The call to "fear the LORD" is a call to live in awe of His majesty and holiness. This reverence should permeate every aspect of our lives, influencing our decisions and actions.

Identity and Responsibility
As spiritual descendants of Jacob through faith in Christ, Christians are called to honor and revere God. Our identity in Christ comes with the responsibility to live in a way that reflects His glory.

Praise as a Community
The collective call to praise God emphasizes the importance of communal worship. Gathering with other believers to honor God strengthens our faith and witness to the world.

Consistency in Worship
Our praise and reverence for God should not be limited to specific times or places but should be a consistent part of our daily lives, reflecting our ongoing relationship with Him.

What Does Psalm 22:23 Mean
You who fear the LORD, praise Him!

Psalm 22 has just moved from the psalmist’s cry of abandonment (vv.1-21) to a declaration of God’s deliverance (v.22). Verse 23 is the first ripple of that good news, calling everyone who “fears the LORD” to respond with praise.

• “Fear” here speaks of reverent awe and wholehearted trust (Psalm 33:8; 111:10; Proverbs 9:10). It’s the posture of hearts that understand both God’s holiness and His mercy shown in the cross-shaped prophecy of this psalm (cf. Hebrews 2:11-12 quoting Psalm 22:22).

• The only fitting answer to rescue is worship: “Fear God and give Him glory” (Revelation 14:7).

• This praise is not passive. It’s voiced, public, and contagious (Psalm 34:2-3). True reverence always overflows in audible gratitude.

All descendants of Jacob, honor Him!

The summons narrows to the covenant family—“Jacob” being Israel’s patriarchal name (Genesis 32:28).

• “Honor” means giving weight to God, placing Him at the center of national and personal life (Deuteronomy 10:12, 20).

• Israel’s history is laced with God’s faithfulness; honoring Him is covenant loyalty expressed in obedience and celebration (Psalm 135:19; Romans 9:4).

• Because this psalm ultimately points to the Messiah who comes from Jacob’s line (Acts 3:25-26), honoring God includes recognizing and exalting His Anointed One (John 5:23).

All offspring of Israel, revere Him!

The call widens again, stressing every generation. “Offspring” reminds us that worship is to be handed down.

• Reverence involves deep respect shown through holy living (Leviticus 19:30; 1 Samuel 12:24).

• Parents and grandparents are charged to pass on God’s mighty acts so children too will “set their hope in God” (Psalm 78:4-7; Luke 1:50).

• The psalm anticipates a worldwide family who will join Israel’s offspring in reverence (Psalm 22:27; Galatians 3:29).

summary

Psalm 22:23 layers three concentric circles of invitation: everyone who fears God, the physical descendants of Jacob, and every generation of Israel’s offspring. All are urged to praise, honor, and revere the LORD because He hears, saves, and reigns—proved supremely at the cross foreshadowed in this very psalm. Our response is wholehearted worship that proclaims His deliverance to the ends of the earth and to the end of time.
