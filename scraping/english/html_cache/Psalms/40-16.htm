Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
May all who seek You rejoice and be glad in You;
This phrase emphasizes the joy and fulfillment found in seeking God. In biblical context, seeking God is often associated with a sincere pursuit of His presence and will, as seen in passages like Jeremiah 29:13, "You will seek me and find me when you seek me with all your heart." The call to rejoice and be glad suggests a deep, spiritual satisfaction that transcends circumstances, aligning with the New Testament teaching in Philippians 4:4, "Rejoice in the Lord always." Historically, the Israelites expressed joy in God's deliverance and presence, as seen in their worship practices and festivals. This joy is not merely emotional but rooted in the assurance of God's faithfulness and salvation.

may those who love Your salvation
This phrase highlights the affection and gratitude of believers for God's saving acts. Salvation in the Old Testament often referred to deliverance from physical enemies or peril, as seen in the Exodus narrative. However, it also points to the ultimate spiritual salvation through the Messiah, a theme that is fulfilled in the New Testament through Jesus Christ. The love for God's salvation is a response to His grace and mercy, as reflected in Ephesians 2:8-9, which speaks of salvation as a gift from God. This love is a defining characteristic of the faithful, who recognize their dependence on God's redemptive work.

always say, “The LORD be magnified!”
This phrase is a call to glorify God continually. The act of magnifying the Lord is to acknowledge His greatness and sovereignty, a theme prevalent throughout the Psalms. In cultural context, magnifying God was a communal activity, often expressed in public worship and song, as seen in the temple practices of ancient Israel. This declaration is not only an expression of praise but also a testimony to others of God's power and goodness. It connects to the New Testament exhortation in 1 Corinthians 10:31, "So whether you eat or drink or whatever you do, do it all for the glory of God." The phrase also reflects the ultimate purpose of creation, to bring glory to the Creator, as seen in Revelation 4:11.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
Traditionally attributed as the author of Psalm 40, David is expressing a prayerful desire for those who seek God.

2. Seekers of God
Individuals who actively pursue a relationship with God and desire to know Him more deeply.

3. Lovers of Salvation
Those who have experienced God's deliverance and hold His salvation in high regard.

4. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal and unchanging nature.

5. Jerusalem
While not directly mentioned in this verse, it is often the central place of worship and praise in the Psalms.
Teaching Points
Joy in Seeking God
Seeking God should be a source of joy and gladness. As believers, we are encouraged to find delight in our pursuit of knowing Him more.

Love for Salvation
Our love for God's salvation should be evident in our lives. This love compels us to continually praise and magnify the Lord.

Magnifying the Lord
To magnify the Lord means to make His name great in our lives and communities. This involves both our words and actions reflecting His glory.

Community of Believers
The verse highlights the importance of community among those who seek God and love His salvation. Together, we can encourage one another in our faith journey.

Consistent Praise
The call to always say, "The LORD be magnified," suggests a lifestyle of consistent praise and acknowledgment of God's greatness.

What Does Psalm 40:16 Mean
May all who seek You

• The verse begins with an invitation that reaches every heart genuinely pursuing God. “Seek” is active, intentional, and continual (Deuteronomy 4:29; Jeremiah 29:13).

• Scripture promises that such seekers will find Him (Matthew 7:7–8; Hebrews 11:6).

• In this psalm David prays that these seekers be gathered into a blessed community, united by their common pursuit of the Lord.

rejoice and be glad in You

• Joy is more than an emotion—it is the God-given response to His presence (Psalm 16:11; John 15:11).

• Gladness “in You” shifts the focus from circumstances to the Person of God. Even trials cannot rob this gladness (Habakkuk 3:17–18; Philippians 4:4).

• Notice the progression: seeking leads to finding, and finding leads to rejoicing. In God’s design, pursuit and praise belong together (Psalm 5:11).

may those who love Your salvation

• “Salvation” is God’s rescuing work, ultimately fulfilled in Christ (Isaiah 12:2; Acts 4:12).

• Loving that salvation means treasuring both the Deliverer and His deliverance (Psalm 35:27; 1 Peter 1:8–9).

• Such love isn’t passive; it stirs affection, loyalty, and proclamation (Psalm 119:166).

always say

• Praise is to be habitual, not occasional (Psalm 34:1; 71:8).

• “Always” reminds us that worship is a lifestyle, extending beyond gathered services into every ordinary moment (Colossians 3:17; 1 Thessalonians 5:16–18).

• Our words reveal what fills our hearts; continual praise reflects continual trust.

"The LORD be magnified!"

• To magnify the LORD is to exalt, honor, and make His greatness obvious (Psalm 69:30; Luke 1:46).

• This declaration turns attention away from self to God’s supremacy (Psalm 145:3; 34:3).

• Corporate praise amplifies the witness: as believers join voices, the world hears the anthem of God’s glory (Psalm 22:22; Hebrews 13:15).

summary

Psalm 40:16 paints a vivid picture of God-seekers who discover joy in His presence, cherish His salvation, and live in continual, vocal praise. Seeking leads to rejoicing, loving His rescue fuels worship, and unified voices magnify the LORD for all to hear.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 16. - Let all those that seek thee rejoice and be glad in thee. The psalmist cannot long be satisfied with mere prayer for self. He must extend his supplication, and make it cover the whole body of the faithful, "all those that seek God" (comp. Psalm 25:2, 3, 20-22; Psalm 28:1-9, etc.). Let such as love thy salvation say continually, The Lord be magnified; ie. "Give them constant occasion to say, and give them the grateful heart to say, The Lord be praised for his mercies" (comp. Psalm 35:27).
