Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Your way, O God, is holy.
This phrase emphasizes the distinctiveness and purity of God's actions and character. In biblical context, "holy" signifies being set apart and morally perfect. God's ways are often contrasted with human ways, which are flawed and sinful (Isaiah 55:8-9). The holiness of God is a central theme throughout Scripture, seen in His laws, His interactions with Israel, and His ultimate plan of redemption through Jesus Christ. The holiness of God is also reflected in the tabernacle and temple practices, where only the consecrated could approach the Holy of Holies, symbolizing the separation between God's purity and human sinfulness.

What god is so great as our God?
This rhetorical question underscores the supremacy and uniqueness of the God of Israel. In the ancient Near Eastern context, many nations worshiped a pantheon of gods, each with limited power and domain. The psalmist declares the incomparable greatness of the one true God, who is sovereign over all creation. This echoes the declaration in Exodus 15:11, "Who among the gods is like you, LORD?" and is a theme reiterated in the prophetic writings, such as Isaiah 40:18, where God is depicted as unparalleled. The greatness of God is ultimately revealed in the person of Jesus Christ, who embodies the fullness of God (Colossians 2:9) and whose works and teachings demonstrate divine authority and power.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Asaph
The author of Psalm 77, Asaph was a prominent Levite singer and seer in David's court. He is credited with writing several psalms and was known for his deep spiritual insight and musical talent.

2. God
The central figure in this verse, God is depicted as holy and incomparable. The psalmist reflects on God's greatness and holiness, emphasizing His unique nature.

3. Israel
While not directly mentioned in this verse, the context of the psalm reflects the experiences and history of the nation of Israel, particularly their reliance on God's guidance and deliverance.
Teaching Points
Understanding God's Holiness
God's holiness is central to His character. It sets Him apart from all other beings and underscores His purity, righteousness, and perfection.

Recognizing God's Uniqueness
The rhetorical question "What god is so great as our God?" invites believers to reflect on the uniqueness of God compared to any other entity or idol.

Trusting in God's Ways
Even when circumstances are challenging, believers can trust that God's ways are holy and perfect, leading to ultimate good.

Worshiping God for His Greatness
Worship should be a response to recognizing God's greatness and holiness, acknowledging His supreme authority and power.

Reflecting on God's Past Faithfulness
Remembering how God has acted in history, particularly in the lives of His people, can strengthen faith and trust in His ongoing work.

What Does Psalm 77:13 Mean
Your way, O God, is holy

Psalm 77 opens with honest anguish, but verse 13 pivots to worship by focusing on God’s path. “Your way” points to everything God does—His plans, decisions, and interventions in history.

• Set apart and pure. Psalm 18:30 declares, “As for God, His way is perfect,” and Isaiah 6:3 pictures seraphim crying, “Holy, holy, holy.” God’s actions can never be tainted or mistaken.

• Consistent with His Word. Numbers 23:19 reminds us He does not lie. What He promises, He performs.

• Evident in redemption. Earlier in Psalm 77 the writer recalls the Red Sea miracle; holiness led Israel out of bondage (Exodus 14, 15:11).

• Comfort in crisis. Because His way is holy, hardship cannot cancel His purpose (Romans 8:28). When life feels chaotic, the psalm directs our eyes to the unchanging purity of God’s path.

What god is so great as our God?

The second half of the verse invites comparison and finds none equal.

• Unique in existence. Deuteronomy 4:35 affirms, “There is no other besides Him,” while 1 Timothy 2:5 states, “There is one God.” All other so-called gods are human constructs (Isaiah 44:9–20).

• Supreme in power. Creation itself testifies: “His eternal power and divine nature have been clearly seen” (Romans 1:20). Job 38–41 highlights forces of nature under His command.

• Matchless in grace. John 3:16 shows the lengths He goes to redeem; no other deity offers substitutionary love.

• Proven in history. From the plagues of Egypt (Exodus 7–12) to the resurrection of Christ (1 Corinthians 15:3–4), His greatness is not theory but record.

• Answer to idolatry. Psalm 95:3 calls Him “the great God, the great King above all gods.” Knowing His greatness exposes the emptiness of lesser loyalties—possessions, status, even self.

summary

Psalm 77:13 shifts the psalmist—and us—from despair to confident praise. Because God’s way is holy, every act He takes is utterly pure and purposeful. Because no other god is remotely as great, worship and trust belong to Him alone. Remembering these twin truths lifts hearts, steadies faith, and fuels obedience, no matter the storm.

(13) In the sanctuary.--Rather, with the holy, i.e., with "Israel," the "saint" of God.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 13. - Thy way, O God, is in the sanctuary; rather, in holiness. God's "way" - his conduct, his proceedings - however strange and mysterious it may seem to us, is always holy, i.e. just and right (comp. Genesis 18:25; Job 8:3). Who is so great a God as our God? God is both good and great; just in himself, and able to execute justice.
