Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
I wait for You, O LORD;
The act of waiting on the LORD is a recurring theme throughout the Psalms and the broader biblical narrative. It signifies a posture of trust and patience, acknowledging God's sovereignty and timing. In the ancient Near Eastern context, waiting was often associated with hope and expectation, not passive resignation. This phrase reflects a deep reliance on God, reminiscent of other scriptures such as Isaiah 40:31, which speaks of those who wait on the LORD renewing their strength. The psalmist's use of "LORD" (YHWH) emphasizes the covenant relationship between God and His people, highlighting trust in God's faithfulness and promises.

You will answer,
This expression of confidence in God's response is rooted in the understanding of God's character as attentive and responsive to His people's cries. The assurance of divine intervention is a common theme in the Psalms, as seen in Psalm 34:17, where the righteous cry out, and the LORD hears them. Historically, the Israelites experienced God's deliverance repeatedly, reinforcing their belief in His willingness to answer. This phrase also points to the prophetic nature of God's promises, where His word does not return void (Isaiah 55:11).

O Lord my God.
The personal nature of this address underscores a relationship that is both intimate and reverent. "Lord" (Adonai) conveys authority and mastery, while "my God" indicates personal possession and devotion. This duality reflects the balance of fear and love in the believer's relationship with God. The phrase echoes the covenantal language found throughout the Old Testament, where God declares, "I will be their God, and they will be my people" (Jeremiah 31:33). It also foreshadows the New Testament revelation of Jesus Christ as both Lord and God, as seen in Thomas's declaration in John 20:28.

Teaching Points
The Importance of Waiting on God
Waiting on God is an act of faith and trust, acknowledging His sovereignty and perfect timing.

Confidence in God's Response
Believers can have confidence that God will answer, as He is faithful to His promises and attentive to the cries of His people.

The Role of Prayer in Distress
In times of trouble, turning to God in prayer is crucial, as it aligns our hearts with His will and opens us to His guidance and comfort.

Developing Patience and Endurance
Patience is a fruit of the Spirit that is cultivated through trials and waiting, leading to spiritual maturity and deeper reliance on God.

Personal Relationship with God
Addressing God as "my God" signifies a personal and intimate relationship, encouraging believers to cultivate a close walk with the Lord.

What Does Psalm 38:15 Mean
I wait for You

- David speaks in the present tense, showing active, deliberate patience. This is not passive resignation but a steady posture of expectation (Psalm 27:14; Isaiah 40:31).

- Waiting acknowledges God’s timing as perfect, trusting His sovereignty over every circumstance (Psalm 130:5–6).

- While waiting, faith is strengthened; the soul leans on promises already given (Lamentations 3:25).

O LORD

- By addressing God as “LORD,” David invokes the covenant name that stresses God’s unchanging character and faithful love (Exodus 34:6).

- Calling on this name reminds believers that the One they wait for has proven His reliability throughout redemptive history (Psalm 9:10; Psalm 33:18).

- The title also underscores God’s absolute authority: He is not merely an option among many helpers; He is the unrivaled Ruler to whom all petitions must rise (Isaiah 45:5).

You will answer

- Confidence replaces uncertainty: David declares, not wonders, that God’s reply is certain (Psalm 34:4).

- This assurance rests on God’s promises that He hears His people’s cries (Jeremiah 33:3) and on Christ’s later affirmation, “Ask, and it will be given to you” (Matthew 7:7).

- Such certainty fuels perseverance; believers continue in prayer precisely because they anticipate a response (Luke 18:1).

O Lord my God

- Adding “my” shifts from general truth to personal relationship, claiming intimate belonging (Psalm 23:1; John 20:28).

- The double title “Lord … God” unites submission (“Lord”—Master) with worship (“God”—Creator), expressing both reverence and closeness (Psalm 42:6; Romans 8:15).

- Personal allegiance makes the waiting and the confidence credible: one can only expect an answer from the One who has already become “my God” through covenant grace.

summary

Psalm 38:15 captures the believer’s posture in trials: patient expectation toward the covenant-keeping LORD, certainty of divine response, and personal communion with the One who rules and redeems. Waiting is active trust; calling on His covenant name affirms His faithfulness; declaring His assured answer strengthens hope; owning Him as “my God” seals the relationship. Together these truths invite every follower of Christ to rest confidently, pray persistently, and rely wholly on the God who always hears and answers.
