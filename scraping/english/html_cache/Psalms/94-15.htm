Surely judgment will again be righteous
This phrase reflects a confidence in the restoration of justice, suggesting a time when God's righteous judgment will prevail. In the context of the Psalms, this is a recurring theme where the psalmist often contrasts the current state of injustice with the anticipated divine intervention. Historically, Israel experienced periods of corruption and injustice, often under unrighteous rulers or foreign oppression. This phrase reassures the faithful that God's justice will ultimately be restored. Biblically, this aligns with the prophetic visions found in books like Isaiah and Jeremiah, where the promise of a righteous reign is a central theme. It also echoes the eschatological hope found in Revelation, where God's final judgment establishes eternal righteousness.

and all the upright in heart will follow it
The "upright in heart" refers to those who are sincere and faithful in their devotion to God. This phrase implies that true followers of God will naturally align themselves with His righteous judgment. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, the heart was seen as the center of moral and spiritual life, so being "upright in heart" signifies integrity and purity. This concept is echoed in other scriptures, such as Psalm 24:4, which speaks of those with clean hands and pure hearts. Theologically, this can be seen as a type of Christ, who embodies perfect righteousness and calls His followers to walk in His ways. The phrase also suggests a future time when God's people will fully embrace His righteous rule, a theme that resonates with the New Testament teachings on the Kingdom of God.

Teaching Points
The Assurance of Divine Justice
Believers can trust that God's judgment is always righteous and will ultimately prevail, even when human justice systems fail.

The Call to Righteous Living
The "upright in heart" are those who align their lives with God's standards. We are called to live righteously, trusting that God sees and rewards our faithfulness.

The Role of Patience and Faith
In times of injustice, patience and faith are crucial. We must wait on the Lord, knowing that His timing and justice are perfect.

The Importance of Aligning with God's Will
Following God's righteous judgment requires a heart that seeks His will above all else. This involves daily surrender and obedience to His Word.

Encouragement in Times of Oppression
For those facing oppression or injustice, Psalm 94:15 offers hope and encouragement that God is aware and will act in His perfect time.

What Does Psalm 94:15 Mean
Surely judgment

• The opening word “Surely” signals absolute certainty; God’s verdicts are never in doubt (Deuteronomy 32:4; James 1:17).

• “Judgment” points to God’s governance over nations and individuals. He is the ultimate Judge who “has prepared His throne for judgment” (Psalm 9:7–8).

• When culture seems to mock justice, this line anchors the believer’s confidence: God is still on the bench, and His court is never adjourned (Isaiah 33:22).

Will again be righteous

• “Again” implies a restoration. Seasons of corruption or delay in justice do not nullify God’s plan; He will set things right in His timing (Isaiah 2:4; Acts 17:31).

• “Righteous” assures that His future judgments will mirror His holy character—pure, impartial, and perfectly moral (Psalm 97:2; Revelation 19:11).

• This promise sustains the faithful during times when evil seems to prosper, reminding us that God’s righteousness is not merely theoretical but will be manifested in history (Habakkuk 2:3).

And all the upright in heart

• “Upright in heart” describes those whose inner life aligns with God’s standards, not just external behavior (Psalm 24:3–4; Matthew 5:8).

• Their uprightness is a fruit of grace—God works righteousness in His people so they can walk blamelessly (Psalm 15:2; Philippians 2:13).

• The verse distinguishes the faithful remnant from the wicked, assuring believers that their integrity is seen and valued by God (Malachi 3:16–18).

Will follow it

• “Follow” pictures active pursuit. The righteous don’t merely applaud God’s judgments; they pattern their lives after them (Psalm 119:33–34; John 14:15).

• Alignment with God’s ruling means standing for truth, refusing compromise, and trusting His timetable for ultimate vindication (1 Peter 2:21–23).

• As God’s justice emerges, the upright gladly submit, finding security and freedom in His righteous order (Psalm 119:45; John 8:31–32).

summary

Psalm 94:15 promises that God’s justice will unquestionably prevail, it will be perfectly righteous, and those whose hearts are aligned with Him will willingly march in its wake. This assurance fuels perseverance: evil’s triumph is temporary, God’s verdict is certain, and the faithful will both witness and share in the vindication of His righteousness.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(15) But.--Better, For; literally, for to righteousness judgment shall turn, and after it all upright in heart--i.e., there shall no longer be the seeming contradiction in things. God's righteousness will triumph over the injustice under which Israel groans; His ways will be vindicated, so that all the upright in heart will acknowledge that "there is a reward for the righteous, a God who judges in the earth" (Psalm 58:11). Luther's fine paraphrase, "For Right must, whatever happens, remain Right," expresses the feeling; but, better still, the question, "Shall not the Lord of all the earth do right?" The phrase, "shall after it," is a common one for expressing attachment and adherence to a party or cause (Exodus 23:2; 2Samuel 2:10; Psalm 49:13), and specially of adherence to Jehovah (1Samuel 12:14; 1Kings 14:8). . . .
Pulpit Commentary
Verse 15. - But judgment shall return unto righteousness. "Judgment," i.e. God's actual award of good and evil upon the earth, which has seemed to be divorced from justice, while the ungodly have prospered and the pious been afflicted (vers. 3-6), shall in the end "return unto righteousness," i.e. once more, evidently, conform to it and coincide with it. And all the upright in heart shall follow it; i.e. "and then all honest hearted men shall recognize the fact, see it, and rejoice in it."
