Blessed be the LORD
This phrase is a declaration of praise and gratitude towards God. It reflects a common biblical theme where individuals acknowledge God's sovereignty and goodness. The act of blessing the LORD is seen throughout the Psalms and other scriptures, such as Psalm 103:1-2, where David calls on his soul to bless the LORD for His benefits. This expression of worship is rooted in the understanding of God's covenantal relationship with His people, where He is both the Creator and Sustainer.

for He has shown me His loving devotion
The term "loving devotion" is often translated from the Hebrew word "chesed," which encompasses God's steadfast love, mercy, and faithfulness. This concept is central to the Old Testament, highlighting God's unwavering commitment to His covenant with Israel. In the context of Psalm 31, David acknowledges God's personal intervention and care in his life, which is a recurring theme in his psalms. This loving devotion is also a foreshadowing of the New Testament revelation of God's love through Jesus Christ, as seen in John 3:16.

in a city under siege
The imagery of a city under siege evokes a situation of extreme distress and danger. Historically, cities in ancient Israel, such as Jerusalem, faced sieges from various enemies, including the Assyrians and Babylonians. This phrase may reflect a specific historical event in David's life or serve as a metaphor for personal trials and adversities. Theologically, it underscores God's protection and deliverance in times of crisis, as seen in other biblical accounts like the siege of Jerusalem in 2 Kings 19, where God miraculously delivered the city from the Assyrian army. This also points to the ultimate deliverance found in Christ, who provides refuge and salvation for believers amidst spiritual battles.

Teaching Points
God's Faithfulness in Trials
God's loving devotion is evident even in the most challenging circumstances. Believers can trust in His faithfulness when they feel besieged by life's difficulties.

The Power of Praise
Like David, we should bless the Lord and express gratitude for His protection and love, recognizing His hand in our deliverance.

Spiritual Warfare
The imagery of a city under siege can be likened to spiritual battles. Christians are called to rely on God's strength and protection in spiritual warfare.

Trust in God's Sovereignty
Understanding that God is in control, even when situations seem dire, encourages believers to place their trust in His sovereign plan.

Community Support
Just as a city under siege requires unity and support, believers should seek and offer support within the Christian community during times of trial.

What Does Psalm 31:21 Mean
Blessed be the LORD

• David opens with praise. He is not waiting for circumstances to improve; he blesses the LORD right in the middle of crisis (Psalm 34:1; 1 Thessalonians 5:18).

• The focus is on the covenant name “LORD,” reminding us that the God who spoke to Moses at the burning bush is personally involved (Exodus 3:14).

• Praise anchors the heart when everything else shakes (Psalm 18:46; Habakkuk 3:17-18).

for He has shown me

• David looks back at concrete evidence of God’s help, not vague optimism (Psalm 66:16).

• Testimony fuels trust: yesterday’s deliverance gives courage for today’s battle (2 Corinthians 1:10; Revelation 12:11).

• By using “me,” David personalizes God’s intervention; the Almighty cares for individuals (Psalm 40:1-3; Luke 12:6-7).

His loving devotion

• The phrase points to God’s steadfast, covenant love—unyielding, undefeatable (Psalm 136; Lamentations 3:22-23).

• Loving devotion combines affection and loyalty: He feels for His people and acts for them (Deuteronomy 7:9; Romans 8:38-39).

• In Christ we see this devotion perfected, as He lays down His life for us (John 15:13; 1 John 4:10).

in a city under siege

• The setting is extreme pressure: walls surrounded, supplies cut off, hope fading (2 Kings 6:24-27).

• God’s love is not hindered by locked gates or enemy armies (Psalm 46:4-7).

• When believers feel hemmed in—financially, relationally, culturally—this verse assures us that divine help reaches through every blockade (2 Corinthians 4:8-9; Hebrews 13:5-6).

summary

Psalm 31:21 celebrates a God who deserves praise because He steps into the tightest corners of life with steadfast, personal love. David’s experience becomes ours: even when besieged, we bless the LORD, recount His past faithfulness, rely on His unwavering devotion, and rest in the certainty that no siege can keep Him out.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(21) Shewed me his marvellous kindness . . .--Better, made his kindness distinguished or manifest, referring to Psalm 31:19.

In a strong city.--Some see a reference to David's adventures at Ziklag or Keilah; others to Jeremiah's in Jerusalem (Jeremiah 38). It is, however, better to regard it merely as a general image of the Divine protection.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 21. - Blessed be the Lord: for he hath showed me his marvellous kindness in a strong city. The "strong city" has been explained as Ziklag (Delitzsch), or Maha-naim (2 Samuel 17:24), but is probably as much a figure of speech as the "pavilion" of ver. 20. God has showed David his marvellous loving-kindness by giving him an assurance of absolute security.
