Hallelujah!
This term is a call to praise, derived from the Hebrew words "halal," meaning "to praise," and "Yah," a shortened form of "Yahweh," the name of God. It is an exclamation of worship and joy, often used in the Psalms to invite the congregation to join in praising God. The use of "Hallelujah" sets the tone for the psalm, emphasizing the importance of worship and reverence for God.

Blessed is the man
The concept of being "blessed" in the biblical sense refers to a state of happiness and well-being that comes from a right relationship with God. This echoes the Beatitudes in the New Testament, where Jesus describes the blessedness of those who live according to God's will. The "man" here is representative of any person who aligns their life with God's principles.

who fears the LORD
The "fear of the LORD" is a central theme in wisdom literature, such as Proverbs and Ecclesiastes. It denotes a deep respect, reverence, and awe for God, recognizing His power, authority, and holiness. This fear is not about being afraid but about acknowledging God's rightful place in one's life, leading to obedience and worship. It is the beginning of wisdom and understanding.

who greatly delights in His commandments
Delighting in God's commandments implies a love and appreciation for His laws and teachings. This is not mere duty but a genuine joy in following God's ways, as seen in Psalm 1, where the righteous man meditates on the law day and night. It reflects a heart transformed by God's grace, finding pleasure in living according to His will. This delight is a mark of true devotion and commitment to God.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Psalmist
The author of Psalm 112, traditionally attributed to King David, who expresses themes of wisdom and righteousness.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, central to the worship and reverence described in the Psalm.

3. The Blessed Man
Represents the individual who fears the LORD and delights in His commandments, embodying the ideal of a righteous life.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Reverence
Fearing the LORD is foundational to a blessed life. It involves a deep respect and awe for God's holiness and authority.

Delighting in God's Commandments
True joy and fulfillment come from aligning our desires with God's will as revealed in His Word. This delight is not burdensome but a source of joy.

The Path to Blessing
The Psalm highlights that blessings are not merely material but are deeply spiritual, rooted in a life that honors God.

Living Out Our Faith
The blessed man is an example of active faith, where reverence for God translates into practical obedience and joy in His commandments.

Cultivating a Heart for God
Encourage a personal relationship with God through regular study and meditation on His Word, fostering a heart that delights in His ways.

What Does Psalm 112:1 Mean
Hallelujah!

• The psalm opens with a shout of praise that lifts our eyes from ourselves to the Lord.

• Similar calls erupt throughout Scripture—“Hallelujah! Praise the LORD, O my soul” (Psalm 146:1)—showing that worship is the natural response of a heart captivated by God’s greatness (Psalm 106:1; Revelation 19:1–6).

• By beginning with praise, the psalm reminds us that every blessing described in the verses that follow is rooted in God’s character, not human merit.

Blessed is the man

• “Blessed” speaks of a settled, God-given happiness that cannot be shaken by circumstances (Psalm 1:1–3; Jeremiah 17:7–8).

• This blessing is personal—“the man”—yet open to all who embrace the same posture of faith (Luke 11:28).

• True prosperity, then, is defined not by material gain but by God’s favor resting on a life aligned with His ways.

Who fears the LORD

• The fear of the LORD is reverent awe, not crippling dread (Proverbs 9:10). It is the recognition that He is Creator, Judge, and Redeemer.

• Such fear produces:

– Wisdom (Proverbs 1:7)

– Confidence (Proverbs 14:26)

– Comfort for the church (Acts 9:31)

– Enduring love from generation to generation (Psalm 103:17)

• When we rightly fear God, lesser fears lose their grip.

Who greatly delights in His commandments

• The blessed person doesn’t obey grudgingly; he “greatly delights” in God’s directives, echoing “Oh, how I love Your law!” (Psalm 119:97).

• Delight leads to:

– Consistent obedience—“If you love Me, you will keep My commandments” (John 14:15).

– Freedom—“The one who looks intently into the perfect law of freedom…will be blessed in what he does” (James 1:25).

– Light burdens—“His commandments are not burdensome” (1 John 5:3).

• Such joy-filled obedience flows from gratitude for salvation, not an attempt to earn it.

summary

Psalm 112:1 paints a vivid portrait of the truly blessed life: it begins with wholehearted praise, is marked by God-centered happiness, rooted in reverent awe, and expressed through joyful obedience. This verse assures us that when we fear the LORD and delight in His commands, we step into a life of unshakable, God-given blessing.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 1. - Praise ye the Lord. Again a "heading," or "introduction" (see the comment on Psalm 111:1). Blessed is the man that feareth the Lord. Connect this with Psalm 111:10. The closing thought of Psalm 111. is taken up and expanded in Psalm 112. That delighteth greatly in his commandments (comp. Psalm 1:2; Psalm 119:16, 17, 24, 70, 77, etc.). "True obedience can only come from pleasure in the commandments of God" (Hengstenberg).
