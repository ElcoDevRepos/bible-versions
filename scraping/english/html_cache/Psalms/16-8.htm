I have set the LORD always before me.
This phrase reflects a conscious decision to prioritize God in one's life. The psalmist, traditionally understood to be David, emphasizes a continual awareness and acknowledgment of God's presence. In biblical context, setting the LORD before oneself implies a life of devotion and obedience, aligning with the first commandment to have no other gods (Exodus 20:3). This practice of keeping God at the forefront is echoed in Proverbs 3:6, which encourages acknowledging Him in all ways. The phrase also suggests a personal relationship with God, akin to the intimacy seen in the life of Enoch, who "walked with God" (Genesis 5:24).

Because He is at my right hand,
The right hand is a position of honor and strength in ancient Near Eastern culture. To have God at one's right hand signifies divine support and protection. This imagery is consistent with other biblical passages where God's presence provides strength and security, such as in Psalm 121:5, where the LORD is described as a shade at the right hand. Theologically, this can be seen as a type of Christ, who is seated at the right hand of the Father (Hebrews 1:3), symbolizing authority and power. The right hand also denotes a place of advocacy, suggesting that God is an ever-present help in times of trouble (Psalm 46:1).

I will not be shaken.
This declaration of confidence is rooted in the assurance of God's steadfast presence. The term "shaken" implies instability or fear, which is countered by the security found in God. This assurance is a recurring theme in the Psalms, as seen in Psalm 62:6, where the psalmist declares that he will not be moved. Historically, David faced numerous adversities, yet his trust in God provided stability. This phrase also connects to the New Testament, where believers are encouraged to build their lives on the solid foundation of Christ (Matthew 7:24-25). The unshakeable nature of faith in God is a promise of spiritual security and peace, transcending circumstances.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
Traditionally attributed as the author of Psalm 16, David was the second king of Israel and a man after God's own heart. His life was marked by a deep trust in God, despite facing numerous challenges and adversities.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal presence and faithfulness. In this Psalm, David expresses his reliance on Yahweh as his protector and guide.

3. Right Hand
In ancient Near Eastern culture, the right hand symbolized power, authority, and protection. David's reference to God being at his right hand signifies God's role as his defender and source of strength.
Teaching Points
Constant Awareness of God's Presence
Believers are encouraged to cultivate a continual awareness of God's presence in their lives, just as David set the LORD always before him.

God as Our Protector
Understanding that God is at our right hand provides assurance and confidence, knowing that He is our defender and source of strength.

Unshakeable Faith
With God as our foundation, we can face life's challenges without being shaken, trusting in His steadfastness and support.

Christ as the Fulfillment
Recognize that Jesus embodies the ultimate fulfillment of this Psalm, as He is the one who conquered death and provides eternal security for believers.

What Does Psalm 16:8 Mean
I have set the LORD always before me

The psalmist speaks of a conscious, ongoing choice: “I have set the LORD always before me.” This is more than a passing thought; it is a settled posture of life.

• Priority: Every decision and desire is filtered through the reality of God’s presence (Proverbs 3:6, “In all your ways acknowledge Him, and He will make your paths straight,”).

• Focus: Eyes fixed on the Lord keep distractions and anxieties from ruling the heart (Hebrews 12:2, “Let us fix our eyes on Jesus, the author and perfecter of our faith,”).

• Fellowship: Continual nearness breeds confidence and joy (Psalm 25:15, “My eyes are ever on the LORD, for He will free my feet from the net,”).

Practical ways to “set” Him before us:

– Regular meditation on Scripture (Psalm 1:2)

– Ongoing conversation in prayer (1 Thessalonians 5:17)

– Quick obedience to His promptings (John 14:21)

Because He is at my right hand, I will not be shaken

“Right hand” imagery communicates strength, protection, and honor. To have the LORD there is to enjoy His personal guardianship.

• Protector: “The LORD is your keeper; the LORD is the shade on your right hand” (Psalm 121:5).

• Champion: “But the Lord stood by me and strengthened me” (2 Timothy 4:17).

• Rock‐solid security: “He alone is my rock and my salvation; He is my fortress, I will never be shaken” (Psalm 62:2).

Because of this unbreakable support:

– External turmoil cannot uproot inner stability (Isaiah 26:3).

– Spiritual opposition meets divine defense (Isaiah 54:17).

– Future uncertainties lose their power to intimidate (Romans 8:31).

summary

Psalm 16:8 draws a straight line from deliberate Godward focus to unshakable stability. By continually placing the Lord in the forefront of heart and mind, and resting in His steadfast presence at our right hand, we stand firm no matter the circumstance.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(8) At my right hand.--Comp. Psalm 109:31; Psalm 110:5; Psalm 121:5. The image seems to be a military one: the shield of the right-hand comrade is a protection to the man beside him.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 8. - I have set the Lord always before me. I have brought myself, that is, to realize the continual presence of God, alike in happiness and in trouble. I feel him to be ever with me. Because he is at my right hand (i.e. close to me, ready to protect and save), therefore I shall not be moved. Nothing will shake me or disturb me from my trust and confidence.
