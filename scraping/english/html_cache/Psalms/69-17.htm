Hide not Your face from Your servant
In biblical times, the concept of God hiding His face was often associated with divine displeasure or judgment. When God "hides His face," it signifies a withdrawal of His favor and presence, which was deeply feared by the faithful. This plea reflects a desire for restored fellowship and assurance of God's attention and care. The term "servant" indicates a relationship of submission and loyalty, often used by David to express his devotion to God. This phrase echoes the sentiments found in other Psalms, such as Psalm 27:9 and Psalm 143:7, where the psalmist seeks God's presence and favor.

for I am in distress
The psalmist's distress is a common theme in the Psalms, where personal anguish and external persecution are frequently expressed. This distress could be due to personal sin, enemies, or overwhelming circumstances. Historically, David faced numerous trials, including King Saul's pursuit and internal family strife, which could be the backdrop for this plea. The expression of distress is a call for divine intervention, recognizing human limitations and the need for God's deliverance.

Answer me quickly!
The urgency in this request highlights the psalmist's desperation and the immediacy of his need. In the ancient Near Eastern context, quick responses were often associated with the reliability and power of a deity. The plea for a swift answer underscores faith in God's ability to act promptly and decisively. This urgency is mirrored in other scriptures, such as Psalm 102:2 and Isaiah 58:9, where the faithful seek God's rapid intervention. The cry for a quick response also foreshadows the New Testament's emphasis on God's readiness to hear and respond to His people, as seen in passages like Luke 18:7-8.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
Traditionally attributed as the author of Psalm 69, David is expressing a deep personal plea for God's intervention during a time of distress.

2. God
The central figure to whom the plea is directed. David seeks God's presence and swift response to his urgent need.

3. Distress
The emotional and spiritual state of David, characterized by a sense of urgency and desperation for divine help.
Teaching Points
The Urgency of Prayer
David's plea for a quick response highlights the importance of bringing our urgent needs to God without delay.

God's Presence in Distress
The request for God not to hide His face underscores the comfort and strength found in God's presence during difficult times.

Faith in God's Response
Trusting that God hears and will answer our prayers, even when we are in distress, is a key aspect of faith.

The Role of Servanthood
Identifying as God's servant, as David does, reflects a posture of humility and submission, which is crucial in our relationship with God.

The Importance of Quick Divine Intervention
Recognizing that there are times when we need God to act swiftly and believing that He is capable of doing so.

What Does Psalm 69:17 Mean
Hide not Your face

• In Scripture, God’s “face” represents His favor and nearness (Numbers 6:24-26; Psalm 27:9).

• David fears separation from that favor; he prays that God will not turn away as a judge hides from the guilty (Isaiah 59:2).

• Because God’s character is unchanging mercy (Exodus 34:6 - 7), David appeals confidently, expecting real, literal intervention.

from Your servant

• By calling himself “Your servant,” David confesses submission and loyalty (Psalm 86:2; 2 Samuel 7:25).

• This title reminds us that God hears those who belong to Him (John 10:27).

• The relationship is covenantal: a master protects and provides for a servant (Psalm 123:2).

for I am in distress

• The plea is grounded in present, crushing trouble—physical, emotional, perhaps both (Psalm 31:7-9).

• Scripture shows God’s heart moved by His people’s affliction (Isaiah 63:9; Exodus 3:7-8).

• Naming the distress is an act of faith, bringing the burden into God’s light (1 Peter 5:7).

Answer me quickly!

• Urgency colors the final cry; prolonged silence would deepen despair (Psalm 143:7).

• God’s Word often couples quick deliverance with His righteousness (Isaiah 65:24; Luke 18:7-8).

• The psalmist expects God to act in real time, revealing that prompt divine help is consistent with His nature (Psalm 40:1-3).

summary

Psalm 69:17 captures a servant’s urgent, confident request for God’s immediate presence and aid. David longs for God’s favor (Hide not Your face), appeals from a humble relationship (from Your servant), admits overwhelming trouble (for I am in distress), and seeks swift intervention (Answer me quickly!). The verse teaches that believers may approach the Lord openly and expectantly, trusting His faithful character to respond when life presses hard.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 17. - And hide not thy face from thy servant (comp. Psalm 10:1; Psalm 13:1; Psalm 22:24; Psalm 27:9, etc.). For I am in trouble; literally, for there is trouble to me. On the probable nature of the "trouble," see the introductory paragraph. Hear me speedily (comp. Psalm 22:19; Psalm 31:2; Psalm 38:22; Psalm 70:1, etc.).
