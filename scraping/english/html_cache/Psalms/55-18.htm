He redeems my soul in peace
This phrase emphasizes God's role as a redeemer, a theme prevalent throughout the Bible. Redemption in the Old Testament often refers to God's deliverance of His people from physical danger or bondage, as seen in the Exodus narrative. The concept of peace here is not merely the absence of conflict but a holistic sense of well-being and restoration. This peace is reminiscent of the Hebrew word "shalom," which encompasses completeness and harmony. Theologically, this redemption foreshadows the ultimate redemption through Jesus Christ, who is referred to as the Prince of Peace in Isaiah 9:6. The peace that God provides is not just external but internal, affecting the soul, which aligns with the New Testament teaching in Philippians 4:7 about the peace of God guarding hearts and minds.

from the battle waged against me
The imagery of battle is a common metaphor in the Psalms, reflecting the struggles and adversities faced by the psalmist. Historically, David, the attributed author of this psalm, faced numerous battles, both literal and figurative, including conflicts with Saul, his own son Absalom, and various external enemies. This phrase can also be understood spiritually, representing the internal and external struggles believers face, such as temptation, persecution, and spiritual warfare. Ephesians 6:12 speaks of the spiritual battle against "the rulers, the authorities, the powers of this dark world," highlighting the ongoing nature of such conflicts. The assurance of God's deliverance from these battles is a recurring theme, providing comfort and hope.

even though many oppose me
This part of the verse acknowledges the reality of opposition and adversity. In David's life, opposition came from various sources, including political rivals, personal betrayals, and foreign enemies. The mention of "many" underscores the overwhelming nature of the opposition, yet it also highlights God's power to save despite the odds. This is echoed in Romans 8:31, which states, "If God is for us, who can be against us?" Theologically, this opposition can be seen as a type of the opposition Jesus faced during His earthly ministry, culminating in His crucifixion. Despite the multitude of adversaries, God's plan prevails, offering a message of hope and perseverance for believers facing their own trials.

What Does Psalm 55:18 Mean
He redeems

• “He redeems” signals God’s personal intervention, not a distant help but an active rescue (Psalm 34:22; Isaiah 43:1).

• Redemption here is literal deliverance; David is confident that God “will deliver you from every trap” (Psalm 91:3).

• The verb’s present tense underscores ongoing care—what God has done, He continues to do (2 Timothy 4:18).

my soul

• The focus is the whole person—mind, will, emotions—not just physical safety (Psalm 16:10; Matthew 10:28).

• God values and preserves the inner life of His people when circumstances threaten to tear it apart (Psalm 23:3).

• This personal note invites every believer to trust Him for their deepest needs, not merely outward relief (Philippians 4:7).

in peace

• Peace is more than calm feelings; it is God-given wholeness while conflict still rages (Isaiah 26:3; John 14:27).

• David’s peace stems from faith, not from the disappearance of trouble (Psalm 4:8).

• The promise anticipates Christ, our “Prince of Peace,” who gives rest even in turmoil (Ephesians 2:14).

from the battle

• David faced literal conspiracies (2 Samuel 15), yet he sees God as the One who lifts him out of the fight (Psalm 18:16–17).

• Believers today confront spiritual warfare (Ephesians 6:12), and God’s deliverance remains just as real.

• The phrase confirms that conflict is expected for the righteous, but so is rescue (John 16:33).

waged against me

• Hostility is intentional and ongoing; enemies strategize, but God counters every scheme (Psalm 140:1–4).

• The Lord’s deliverance highlights His sovereignty over every plot (Proverbs 21:30–31).

• Personal opposition strengthens dependence on God rather than self (2 Corinthians 12:9–10).

even though many oppose me

• Numbers don’t intimidate God; “Many are the afflictions of the righteous, but the LORD delivers him out of them all” (Psalm 34:19).

• The verse reassures believers in seasons when resistance feels overwhelming (Romans 8:31).

• God’s track record with Israel and the church shows His ability to protect a minority against a majority (2 Kings 6:16).

summary

Psalm 55:18 celebrates God’s continual, personal, and comprehensive rescue. David declares that the Lord reaches into the thick of conflict, pulls him out, and grants inward peace even while opposition swirls. The same God still redeems every believer who trusts Him, proving that no enemy force—large or small—can overrule His saving power.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(18) He hath delivered.--The Targum rightly makes this the petition just mentioned, "Deliver," &c

(18) From the battle.--The reading of the LXX. is preferable, "from these drawing near to me."

For there were many with me.--This is only intelligible if we insert the word fighting. "For there were many fighting with me," i.e., "against me." But the text seems corrupt.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 18. - He hath delivered my soul in peace from the battle that was against me. Once mere "the preterite of prophetic certainty." David sees his deliverance effected. He beholds the coming battle (2 Samuel 17:11; 2 Samuel 18:6-8). He sees that there are many with him; i.e. "many that contend with him;" but his courage does not fail - he is assured of being "delivered" and re-established in his kingdom "in peace."
