How many are Your works, O LORD!
This phrase acknowledges the vastness and diversity of God's creation, reflecting the biblical theme of God's omnipotence and creativity. The psalmist marvels at the multitude of God's works, which can be seen in the variety of life forms and natural phenomena. This echoes Genesis 1, where God creates the heavens and the earth, emphasizing His role as the ultimate Creator. The exclamation "O LORD" uses the covenant name of God, Yahweh, highlighting a personal relationship with the Creator. This phrase invites believers to recognize and praise God's handiwork in the world around them.

In wisdom You have made them all;
Here, the psalmist attributes the creation of the world to God's wisdom, a concept that is central to biblical theology. Wisdom in the Bible often refers to God's perfect knowledge and understanding, as seen in Proverbs 3:19, "By wisdom the LORD laid the earth’s foundations." This wisdom is not just intellectual but also practical, as it is applied in the creation and sustaining of the universe. The phrase suggests that every aspect of creation is intentional and purposeful, reflecting God's wise design. This wisdom is also personified in Jesus Christ, who is described as the wisdom of God in 1 Corinthians 1:24.

the earth is full of Your creatures.
This statement highlights the abundance and diversity of life on earth, all of which are sustained by God. The term "creatures" encompasses all living beings, from the smallest insects to the largest mammals, each playing a role in the ecosystem. This reflects the biblical view of God's providence and care for all His creation, as seen in Matthew 6:26, where Jesus speaks of God's provision for the birds of the air. The phrase also implies stewardship, as humans are called to care for God's creation, a mandate given in Genesis 1:28. The fullness of the earth with God's creatures points to His generosity and the interconnectedness of all life.

Teaching Points
God's Wisdom in Creation
The intricate design and order of the universe reflect God's infinite wisdom. As believers, we are called to marvel at His creation and recognize His hand in every detail.

Stewardship of Creation
Understanding that the earth is full of God's creatures should inspire us to care for and steward the environment responsibly, honoring the Creator by preserving His works.

Worship through Observation
Observing the natural world can lead us to worship. The beauty and complexity of creation should draw us closer to God, prompting praise and adoration.

Trust in God's Sovereignty
Recognizing God's wisdom in creation encourages us to trust His sovereignty in our lives. If He can manage the universe, He can certainly guide and sustain us.

Diversity Reflects God's Creativity
The manifold works of God highlight the diversity of life, reminding us that diversity is a reflection of God's creative nature and should be celebrated within the body of Christ.

What Does Psalm 104:24 Mean
How many are Your works, O LORD!

- The psalmist bursts with wonder at the sheer number and variety of God’s acts. Think of standing on a shoreline, unable to count the waves—yet every wave testifies, “God did this.”

- Scripture paints the same scene elsewhere:

• “Many, O LORD my God, are the wonders You have done” (Psalm 40:5).

• Job marvels that God “performs wonders that cannot be fathomed, miracles that cannot be counted” (Job 5:9).

- From galaxies to ladybugs, every detail reminds us that the Creator never runs out of ideas. No wonder Paul cries, “Oh, the depth of the riches of the wisdom and knowledge of God!” (Romans 11:33).

In wisdom You have made them all

- Creation is not haphazard; it is the handiwork of divine intelligence. Proverbs 3:19 echoes, “By wisdom the LORD founded the earth.”

- Notice the balance:

• The sun’s distance from earth—close enough to warm, far enough to avoid scorching (Jeremiah 10:12).

• Ecosystems where predator and prey keep populations in check (Colossians 1:16–17 shows Christ holding all things together).

- Each detail shouts that God’s wisdom is practical, purposeful, and perfect. Revelation 4:11 sums it up: “You created all things, and by Your will they exist and came to be.”

the earth is full of Your creatures

- Look around: forests teeming with birds, oceans alive with whales, deserts dotted with resilient plants. Genesis 1 records God’s repeated declaration that what He made was “good,” and here the psalmist celebrates that goodness on full display.

- Psalm 24:1 reminds us, “The earth is the LORD’s, and the fullness thereof,” so every life-form belongs to Him.

- Even the unnoticed critters matter: “Ask the animals, and they will instruct you” (Job 12:7–10). The vast biodiversity is a living museum of God’s glory.

summary

Psalm 104:24 invites us to pause and gasp at creation’s scope, its wise design, and its living abundance. Countless works, flawless wisdom, everywhere life—each element calls us to worship the Lord who crafted it all.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(24) Riches.--LXX., "creation;" Aquila, Symmachus, and the Vulg., "possession." The MSS. vary between singular and plural. Creatures will perhaps. best express the sense here.

There is something as fine in art as true in religion in this sudden burst of praise--the "evening voluntary" of grateful adoration--into which the poet bursts at the mention of the day's close. Weariness leaves the soul, as it is lifted from contemplation of man's toil to that of God. Athanasius remarked on the sense of rest and refreshment produced by this change of strain.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 24. - O Lord, how manifold are thy works! This is a parenthetic ejaculation, from which the psalmist cannot refrain, as he contemplates creation so far. It breaks the continuity of his description (vers. 2-32), but not unpleasingly. In wisdom hast thou made them all (comp. Proverbs 3:19, "The Lord by wisdom hath founded the earth; by understanding hath he established the heavens"). (On the "wisdom" of God, as shown in creation, see the whole series of 'Bridgewater Treatises.') The earth is full of thy riches; or possessions (comp. Psalm 105:21). "The earth is the Lord's, and the fulness thereof" (Psalm 24:1). Creation gives the right of ownership.
