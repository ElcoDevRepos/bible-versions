You rebuke the arrogant
In biblical context, arrogance is often associated with pride and self-reliance, which are contrary to the humility God desires. Throughout Scripture, God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble (James 4:6, Proverbs 3:34). The rebuke of the arrogant is a theme seen in the lives of figures like Pharaoh in Exodus, who resisted God's commands and faced divine judgment. This phrase underscores God's active role in opposing those who elevate themselves above His authority.

the cursed
The concept of being cursed in the Bible often relates to being under God's judgment or outside His blessing. In Deuteronomy 28, blessings and curses are outlined based on obedience or disobedience to God's law. The cursed are those who have turned away from God's covenant, similar to the warnings given to Israel about the consequences of forsaking God's commandments. This reflects the seriousness of straying from God's path and the spiritual consequences that follow.

who stray from Your commandments
Straying from God's commandments is a recurring issue in the history of Israel, as seen in the cycles of sin and repentance in the Book of Judges. The commandments refer to God's law given to Moses, which was meant to guide the Israelites in righteous living. Straying implies a deliberate choice to deviate from God's instructions, leading to spiritual and often physical consequences. This phrase highlights the importance of adherence to God's word and the dangers of neglecting it, as seen in the lives of kings like Saul, who lost God's favor due to disobedience.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Psalmist
The author of Psalm 119, traditionally believed to be King David, who expresses a deep love for God's law and a desire to live according to His commandments.

2. The Arrogant
Individuals who are prideful and self-reliant, often rejecting God's authority and commandments.

3. God
The divine being who gives the law and rebukes those who stray from it.

4. The Cursed
Those who are under God's judgment due to their disobedience and arrogance.

5. God's Commandments
The laws and decrees given by God, which are meant to guide His people in righteous living.
Teaching Points
The Danger of Arrogance
Arrogance leads to a rejection of God's authority and results in divine rebuke. Believers are called to humility and submission to God's will.

The Importance of Obedience
Straying from God's commandments brings about curses and negative consequences. Obedience to God's law is essential for a blessed life.

God's Righteous Judgment
God's rebuke of the arrogant is a demonstration of His justice. Understanding God's righteous judgment should lead us to repentance and a desire to align with His commandments.

The Blessing of Humility
Humility is the antidote to arrogance and opens the door to God's grace and favor. Cultivating a humble heart is crucial for spiritual growth.

The Role of Scripture in Guiding Life
God's commandments, as revealed in Scripture, are the ultimate guide for righteous living. Regular engagement with the Bible is necessary to stay aligned with God's will.

What Does Psalm 119:21 Mean
You rebuke

God is the One taking action: “You rebuke.” His correction is not passive; He steps in and confronts sin.

• Proverbs 3:11-12, quoted in Hebrews 12:5-6, reminds us that “the LORD disciplines the one He loves,” showing rebuke is a facet of covenant love.

• Revelation 3:19 echoes the same heart: “Those I love, I rebuke and discipline.”

• The Psalmist therefore rests in a God who actively preserves holiness, refusing to let rebellion go unchecked.

the arrogant

Arrogance is self-exaltation that refuses submission to God’s ways.

• Proverbs 16:5 states, “Everyone with a proud heart is detestable to the LORD.”

• James 4:6 repeats, “God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble.”

When Psalm 119:21 singles out “the arrogant,” it underscores that pride blinds people to correction and keeps them outside the shelter of God’s grace.

the cursed

To be “cursed” is to stand under divine judgment rather than blessing.

• Deuteronomy 27:26 says, “Cursed is he who does not uphold the words of this law by carrying them out.”

• Galatians 3:10 cites the same verdict against all who rely on law-keeping yet break it.

The verse ties arrogance to this curse: prideful hearts reap the penalty prescribed for disobedience.

who stray from Your commandments

The root issue is departure from God’s revealed will.

• Joshua 1:7-8 urges, “Do not turn from it to the right or to the left.”

• Psalm 1:1-2 contrasts the blessed man who “delights in the law of the LORD” with those who wander.

Straying is not accidental in this context; it is willful neglect born of arrogance, placing the sinner under God’s rebuke and curse.

summary

Psalm 119:21 paints a sober picture: the Lord personally rebukes proud hearts that wander from His commands, and such people live under a curse decreed by the very law they despise. The verse urges every reader to embrace humility, cherish God’s commandments, and welcome His loving correction that leads to life and blessing.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 21. - Thou hast rebuked the proud. It is difficult to connect this with the preceding. But perhaps the link may be found in the double sense of mishpatim, "judgments," which includes verbal sentences against sinners delivered in the Law, and also actual sentences upon them in deed and fact. These last are in the writer's mind in the present verse - such judgments as those upon Pharaoh (Exodus 14:23-31), Zerah (2 Chronicles 14:9-15), and Sennacherib (2 Kings 19:32-37). That are cursed. This clause is questioned, as metrically redundant. But Hebrew metrology is scarcely as yet an exact science. And the clause finds its justification in Deuteronomy 27:26. Which do err from thy commandments. Such error brings under a curse those who commit it. If it be a blessed thing to walk in God's Law (ver. 1), it must be a cursed thing to transgress against it.
