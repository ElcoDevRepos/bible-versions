Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
For all His ordinances are before me;
This phrase emphasizes the psalmist's commitment to keeping God's laws constantly in view. The term "ordinances" refers to God's decrees or commands, which are part of the Torah given to Moses. In the historical context, the Israelites were expected to meditate on the law day and night (Joshua 1:8). The psalmist's declaration reflects a heart aligned with God's will, similar to the description of the righteous man in Psalm 1:2. This commitment to God's ordinances can be seen as a type of Christ, who perfectly fulfilled the law (Matthew 5:17).

I have not disregarded His statutes.
The word "statutes" refers to specific laws or decrees that require obedience. The psalmist's assertion of not disregarding them indicates a life of faithfulness and integrity. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, adherence to God's statutes was a sign of covenant faithfulness. This phrase connects to the broader biblical theme of obedience leading to blessing, as seen in Deuteronomy 28. The psalmist's faithfulness prefigures Christ's perfect obedience to the Father, as seen in John 8:29, where Jesus states that He always does what pleases God.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of Psalm 18, David is expressing his devotion and obedience to God's laws. This psalm is a song of gratitude for God's deliverance and faithfulness.

2. God
The central figure in the psalm, God is portrayed as a deliverer and protector who rewards those who follow His commandments.

3. Ordinances and Statutes
These refer to God's laws and commandments, which David commits to keeping before him, signifying his dedication to living a life in accordance with God's will.
Teaching Points
Commitment to God's Word
As believers, we are called to keep God's ordinances before us, just as David did. This involves regular reading, meditation, and application of Scripture in our daily lives.

Obedience Brings Blessing
David's life demonstrates that obedience to God's statutes leads to divine protection and blessing. We should strive to live in obedience, trusting that God will honor our faithfulness.

The Importance of God's Law
God's statutes are not just rules but are life-giving principles meant to guide us into a deeper relationship with Him. We should view them as a source of wisdom and guidance.

Consistency in Faith
David's unwavering commitment to God's ordinances serves as a model for us to remain steadfast in our faith, even when faced with challenges.

What Does Psalm 18:22 Mean
All His ordinances are before me

• David says, “For all His ordinances are before me”, picturing God’s commands held constantly in his view. He is choosing to live with the Word front-and-center, not tucked away for emergencies.

• This echoes Joshua 1:8, where the Lord tells Joshua to keep the Book of the Law “always on your lips.” It also parallels Psalm 119:30, “I have set Your ordinances before me,” showing a deliberate, ongoing focus.

• The phrase “all His ordinances” reminds us that the believer isn’t free to pick and choose which parts of God’s instruction to follow. Proverbs 3:1–2 urges, “My son, do not forget My teaching, but let your heart keep My commandments.”

• By keeping every ordinance “before” him, David demonstrates a heart that values God’s wisdom above his own impulses (Psalm 19:7–11).

I have not disregarded His statutes

• The second line, “I have not disregarded His statutes,” highlights active obedience. Unlike Saul, who blatantly ignored God’s clear commands (1 Samuel 15:22–23), David can sincerely say he does not shove God’s statutes aside.

• Psalm 119:102 offers a similar testimony: “I have not departed from Your judgments, for You Yourself have instructed me.” This isn’t a claim to sinless perfection; it is a declaration of consistent loyalty. When David failed, he repented swiftly (Psalm 51).

• God later sums up David’s life this way: “My servant David kept My commandments and followed Me with all his heart, doing only what was right in My eyes” (1 Kings 14:8). The overall pattern of obedience matters, and God notices.

• Jesus echoes the same principle: “If you love Me, you will keep My commandments” (John 14:15). True devotion is inseparable from submission to divine authority.

summary

Psalm 18:22 shows David holding God’s Word in constant view and refusing to brush it aside. He loves, trusts, and obeys every statute the Lord has revealed. For believers today, the verse is an invitation to keep Scripture continually before us and to demonstrate genuine love for God through steadfast obedience, knowing He still blesses those who trust and follow His unchanging Word.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 22. - For all his judgments were before me; i.e. "all his commandments" (compare the use of the same word (מִשׁפַט throughout the hundred and nineteenth psalm). And I did not put away his statutes from me. The wicked are said to "cast God's commandments behind their back" (1 Kings 14:9; Nehemiah 9:26; Psalm 50:17; Ezekiel 23:35). David declares that he had never so acted; he had kept God's statutes always well before him, had borne them in mind, and given heed to them.
