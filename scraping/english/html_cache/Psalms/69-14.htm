Rescue me from the mire and do not let me sink;
This phrase reflects a plea for divine intervention and deliverance from overwhelming circumstances. The "mire" symbolizes a state of despair or entrapment, often used metaphorically in the Bible to describe sin or distress (Psalm 40:2). The imagery of sinking suggests a loss of control and impending doom, akin to Peter's experience when he began to sink in the water and cried out to Jesus for help (Matthew 14:30). The psalmist's cry for rescue underscores the human need for God's saving power, a theme prevalent throughout the Psalms and the broader biblical narrative.

deliver me from my foes
The request for deliverance from enemies is a common theme in the Psalms, reflecting the historical context of Israel's frequent conflicts with surrounding nations. The psalmist's foes could be literal adversaries or symbolic of spiritual and personal struggles. This plea for deliverance echoes the broader biblical promise of God's protection and victory over evil (Psalm 18:17). It also foreshadows the ultimate deliverance through Jesus Christ, who conquers sin and death (1 Corinthians 15:57).

and out of the deep waters.
The "deep waters" symbolize chaos and danger, often associated with the forces of evil and disorder in ancient Near Eastern thought. In biblical literature, water can represent both life and destruction, as seen in the flood narrative (Genesis 7) and the parting of the Red Sea (Exodus 14). The psalmist's cry to be saved from the deep waters highlights the need for God's intervention in times of overwhelming crisis. This imagery is also prophetic, pointing to Jesus calming the storm (Mark 4:39) and His power over the chaotic forces of nature and evil.

1. David
Traditionally attributed as the author of Psalm 69, David is expressing a deep sense of distress and seeking God's deliverance.

The adversaries that David refers to, representing those who oppose him and cause him distress.

Metaphorical imagery used to describe overwhelming troubles and the feeling of being trapped or sinking.
Teaching Points
Cry for Deliverance
Just as David cried out to God in his distress, we too can call upon the Lord in our times of trouble, trusting in His power to save.

Metaphors of Distress
The imagery of mire and deep waters can represent any overwhelming situation in our lives. Recognizing these metaphors helps us relate to the psalmist's experience and apply it to our own struggles.

God as Rescuer
This verse highlights God's role as a deliverer. We are reminded to rely on His strength rather than our own when facing adversities.

Faith in Action
Like David, we should actively seek God's intervention through prayer and faith, believing that He will not let us sink.

Spiritual Warfare
Understanding that our foes can be both physical and spiritual, we are encouraged to put on the full armor of God as described in Ephesians 6.

What Does Psalm 69:14 Mean
Rescue me from the mire

• David pictures himself stuck in thick, filthy mud—unable to move or free himself. This is no mere poetic exaggeration; it is as real to him as the literal mire Jeremiah sank into (Jeremiah 38:6).

• By crying, “Rescue me,” he trusts the Lord’s active intervention, just as Psalm 40:2 celebrates: “He lifted me up from the pit of destruction, out of the miry clay; He set my feet upon a rock.”

• The verse assures us that God does, in fact, reach down and pull His children out of desperate, immobilizing situations—whether physical, emotional, or spiritual—because He is a God who acts in history.

Do not let me sink

• David is already in danger; now he dreads being dragged under completely. Psalm 69:2 voices the same fear: “I have sunk into the miry depths, where there is no footing.”

• The request echoes Peter’s plea when the waves swallowed his courage: “Lord, save me!” (Matthew 14:30). As Jesus literally kept Peter from going under, so the Father literally keeps His saints from ultimate collapse.

• Notice the humble dependence: David knows he cannot keep himself afloat. Only God’s sustaining hand can hold him up when the bottom drops out.

Deliver me from my foes

• The mud and rising water are not David’s only threats; human enemies close in as well. He asks for rescue from those “too mighty” for him (Psalm 18:17).

• Scripture records God repeatedly silencing or scattering hostile forces: “In Your loving devotion, silence my enemies; destroy all my foes” (Psalm 143:12).

• The verse reminds us that danger often comes in layers—circumstances, inner turmoil, opponents—yet the Lord’s deliverance covers every angle.

Out of the deep waters

• “Deep waters” pictures overwhelming chaos, a flood designed to swallow him whole. Psalm 124:4-5 recalls how only God kept Israel from being swept away.

• Isaiah 43:2 extends the same promise to every believer: “When you pass through the waters, I will be with you; and when you go through the rivers, they will not overwhelm you.”

• From Noah to Jonah, Scripture shows God literally ruling flood and sea; therefore David prays with confidence that the waters threatening to drown him will instead showcase the Lord’s power to save.

summary

Psalm 69:14 portrays a believer pinned in mud, sinking fast, hemmed in by enemies, and engulfed by floods—yet appealing to the Lord who literally rescues, steadies, and delivers. Each phrase underscores God’s readiness to reach into the most hopeless circumstances and pull His child to safety. Trust Him to do the same today.
