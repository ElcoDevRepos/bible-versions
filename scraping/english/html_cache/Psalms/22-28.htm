For dominion belongs to the LORD
This phrase emphasizes God's ultimate authority and sovereignty over all creation. The concept of dominion is rooted in the biblical understanding of God's kingship, as seen in Genesis 1:26-28, where humanity is given dominion over the earth as stewards under God's supreme rule. The idea of God's dominion is a recurring theme throughout the Psalms (e.g., Psalm 47:2, Psalm 103:19) and is central to the biblical narrative, affirming that all power and authority originate from Him. This reflects the ancient Near Eastern context where kingship was often associated with divine mandate, yet the Bible uniquely attributes ultimate dominion to the LORD alone, distinguishing Him from other deities of the time.

and He rules over the nations.
This phrase highlights God's governance over all peoples and nations, not just Israel. It underscores the universal scope of God's reign, as seen in passages like Daniel 4:17, where God's sovereignty over earthly kingdoms is declared. This is significant in the historical context of Israel, a small nation surrounded by powerful empires, affirming that despite appearances, God is in control of world events. Theologically, this points to the eschatological hope found in prophecies such as Isaiah 2:2-4 and Revelation 11:15, where God's rule is fully realized on earth. It also prefigures the messianic reign of Jesus Christ, who is described as the King of Kings and Lord of Lords in Revelation 19:16, fulfilling the promise of God's rule over all nations.

The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal existence and sovereign authority over all creation.

2. Nations
Refers to all the peoples and kingdoms of the earth, highlighting the universal scope of God's dominion.

3. David
Traditionally attributed as the author of Psalm 22, David was the second king of Israel and a man after God's own heart.

4. Israel
The chosen people of God, through whom the message of God's sovereignty and salvation is revealed.

5. Messianic Prophecy
Psalm 22 is often seen as a prophetic psalm pointing to the suffering and ultimate victory of the Messiah, Jesus Christ.
Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty
Recognize that God is the ultimate ruler over all nations and authorities. His dominion is not limited by human boundaries or political powers.

Trust in God's Plan
In times of global uncertainty or personal turmoil, trust that God's sovereign plan is unfolding. He is in control, even when circumstances seem chaotic.

Prayer for Nations
As believers, we are called to pray for our leaders and nations, acknowledging God's authority and seeking His guidance and wisdom for those in power.

Living Under God's Rule
Live in a manner that reflects God's rule in your life. Submit to His authority in all areas, allowing His principles to guide your decisions and actions.

Hope in Christ's Return
Look forward to the fulfillment of God's kingdom on earth, when Christ will return to establish His eternal reign over all nations.

What Does Psalm 22:28 Mean
For

The verse opens with the small word “For,” signaling that what follows explains why the whole earth will “remember and turn to the LORD” (Psalm 22:27).

• “For” grounds the worldwide worship described in the previous line.

• Scripture often uses this hinge to connect human response to God’s prior action or character (Romans 11:36; Psalm 100:3).

• Here it declares that global repentance is reasonable because God already possesses absolute authority.

Dominion belongs to the LORD

“Dominion belongs to the LORD” (Psalm 22:28a).

• Dominion is not something God will one day acquire; it is His present possession (Psalm 103:19; 1 Chronicles 29:11-12).

• The verse affirms that all political power is, in the final sense, on loan from Him (Daniel 2:21; John 19:11).

• Because dominion is His, we can rest in His unshakable rule when earthly leaders falter (Psalm 46:1-2).

• This line also underlines the confidence of the entire psalm, which moves from agonized lament (vv.1-21) to triumphant praise (vv.22-31), mirroring Christ’s suffering and victory (Matthew 27:46; Hebrews 2:12).

He rules over the nations

“And He rules over the nations” (Psalm 22:28b).

• The reach of His reign is universal—every tribe, tongue, and people (Psalm 47:8; Revelation 11:15).

• God’s rule is active now, not merely future, though it will be openly displayed when Christ returns (Matthew 28:18; Revelation 19:15-16).

• His governance is righteous, guaranteeing that justice will ultimately prevail (Psalm 9:7-8; Isaiah 33:22).

• Practical implications:

– We honor Him above national loyalties (Acts 5:29).

– We intercede for leaders, knowing their hearts are in His hand (1 Timothy 2:1-2; Proverbs 21:1).

– We proclaim the gospel confidently, certain that every nation is within His saving plan (Matthew 24:14).

summary

Psalm 22:28 anchors worldwide worship in God’s present, universal sovereignty. Because dominion already belongs to Him and He actively rules over every nation, the psalmist can foresee all peoples turning to the LORD. This truth steadies believers today: no matter the turmoil, God’s throne is secure, His justice certain, and His kingdom advancing until every knee bows and every tongue confesses that Jesus Christ is Lord.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 28. - For the kingdom is the Lord's (comp. Psalm 96:10; Psalm 97:1). Christ has taken the kingdom, and even now rules on the earth - not yet wholly over willing subjects, but over a Church that is ever expanding more and more, and tending to become universal. And he is the Governor among the nations. Not the Governor of one nation only, but of all.
