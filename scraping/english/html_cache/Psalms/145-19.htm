Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
He fulfills the desires of those who fear Him;
This phrase emphasizes the relationship between God and those who revere Him. The concept of "fear" in this context is not about being afraid but rather having a deep respect and reverence for God. This reverence leads to a life aligned with His will, which in turn results in God fulfilling their desires. This is consistent with the biblical principle found in Matthew 6:33, where seeking God's kingdom first leads to the fulfillment of needs. Historically, the Israelites experienced this during their journey to the Promised Land, where their needs were met as they followed God's commands. Theologically, this reflects the covenant relationship where God promises blessings to those who are faithful.

He hears their cry and saves them.
This part of the verse highlights God's responsiveness and deliverance. The act of crying out to God is a recurring theme in the Psalms, symbolizing a plea for help and trust in God's power to save. This is seen in the Exodus narrative, where God hears the cries of the Israelites in bondage and delivers them (Exodus 3:7-8). Theologically, this points to God's omnipresence and omnipotence, assuring believers that He is always attentive and capable of providing salvation. In the New Testament, this is fulfilled in Jesus Christ, who offers ultimate salvation to those who call upon His name (Romans 10:13). The phrase underscores the personal nature of God's salvation, emphasizing His willingness to intervene in the lives of those who seek Him.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
- The author of Psalm 145, King David, is known for his deep relationship with God and his role as a leader of Israel. This psalm is a hymn of praise, reflecting his understanding of God's character and faithfulness.

2. God
- The central figure in this verse, God is portrayed as a responsive and compassionate deity who listens to and fulfills the desires of those who revere Him.

3. Those who fear Him
- This refers to the faithful believers who hold God in reverence and awe, acknowledging His power and authority in their lives.
Teaching Points
Reverence Leads to Fulfillment
Fearing God is not about being afraid but about having a deep respect and awe for His majesty and authority. This reverence aligns our desires with His will, leading to their fulfillment.

God's Attentive Ear
God is attentive to the cries of His people. This assurance encourages believers to bring their needs and desires before Him in prayer, trusting in His willingness to listen and respond.

Salvation and Deliverance
The promise of salvation in this verse is both immediate and eternal. God saves His people from their troubles and ultimately offers eternal salvation through faith in Jesus Christ.

Aligning Desires with God's Will
As believers grow in their relationship with God, their desires begin to reflect His will. This alignment is key to experiencing the fulfillment of desires as promised in this verse.

What Does Psalm 145:19 Mean
He fulfills the desires

“​He fulfills the desires…” (Psalm 145:19a)

• God’s generosity is not abstract; He tangibly meets longings that are in line with His will. Psalm 37:4 reminds us that as we delight in Him, “He will give you the desires of your heart.”

• David had experienced this firsthand: “You have granted him his heart’s desire” (Psalm 21:2).

• Jesus echoed the same principle: “Seek first the kingdom of God and His righteousness, and all these things will be added to you” (Matthew 6:33).

• The scope is lavish—Paul says God “is able to do immeasurably more than all we ask or imagine” (Ephesians 3:20).

When our requests spring from hearts aligned with His purposes, we can trust Him to supply in ways that perfectly match what is best for us and glorifies Him.

of those who fear Him

“…of those who fear Him…” (Psalm 145:19b)

• “Fear” here is reverent awe, not terror. Proverbs 9:10 calls it “the beginning of wisdom.”

• Such reverence shows up in obedience (Acts 10:2), humility (Psalm 25:14), and joyful worship (Psalm 33:8).

• The Lord “encamps around those who fear Him, and He delivers them” (Psalm 34:7).

• Malachi 3:16-17 pictures God writing a “book of remembrance” for those who fear Him, promising they will be His treasured possession.

God’s promise in Psalm 145:19 is therefore not blanket indulgence; it is a covenant benefit reserved for those who honor Him as Lord.

He hears their cry

“…He hears their cry…” (Psalm 145:19c)

• Prayer is never a one-way monologue. “The eyes of the LORD are on the righteous, and His ears are open to their cry” (Psalm 34:15).

• From Israel’s groans in Egypt (Exodus 2:23-25) to believers today, God listens attentively.

• Because He hears, we can “approach the throne of grace with confidence” (Hebrews 4:16) and know “whatever we ask according to His will, He hears us” (1 John 5:14).

• Even feeble, whispered petitions reach Him: “Before they call, I will answer” (Isaiah 65:24).

Our cries matter because they rise to a Father who attends to every detail of His children’s lives.

and saves them

“…and saves them.” (Psalm 145:19d)

• Salvation includes immediate rescues—“Many are the afflictions of the righteous, but the LORD delivers him from them all” (Psalm 34:19).

• It reaches its climax in eternal redemption: “Everyone who calls on the name of the Lord will be saved” (Romans 10:13).

• God’s saving work spans body and soul. He can shut lions’ mouths (Daniel 6:22), calm storms (Mark 4:39), and, supremely, forgive sin through the cross (John 3:16).

• Future hope is guaranteed: “He will wipe away every tear from their eyes” (Revelation 21:4).

When He saves, He does so fully, bringing deliverance that starts now and consummates in eternity.

summary

Psalm 145:19 assures believers that the Lord personally satisfies their God-shaped desires, listens intently to their prayers, and acts powerfully to deliver them. These blessings flow to those who live in reverent awe of Him, demonstrating that holy fear and intimate fellowship walk hand in hand.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 19. - He will fulfill the desire of them that fear him (comp. ver. 16). What he does for "every living thing," he will do more especially for men, if they truly" fear" him, and love him (ver. 20), and draw near to him in sincerity and truth. He also will hear their cry, and will save them; i.e. deliver them out of their troubles.
