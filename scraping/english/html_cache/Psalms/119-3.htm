Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
They do no iniquity;
This phrase emphasizes the moral purity and righteousness expected of those who follow God's commandments. In the biblical context, "iniquity" refers to sin or moral wrongdoing. The Hebrew word for iniquity, "avon," often implies a deviation from what is right. The psalmist highlights the ideal state of those who are committed to God's law, suggesting a life free from habitual sin. This aligns with the broader biblical narrative that calls for holiness, as seen in Leviticus 19:2, "Be holy because I, the LORD your God, am holy." The New Testament echoes this call to righteousness in 1 John 3:6, which states, "No one who abides in Him keeps on sinning."

they walk in His ways.
"Walking" in biblical terms often symbolizes one's conduct or lifestyle. To "walk in His ways" means to live according to God's commandments and statutes. This phrase suggests a continuous, active pursuit of God's will, reflecting a life of obedience and devotion. The concept of walking in God's ways is deeply rooted in the covenant relationship between God and His people, as seen in Deuteronomy 10:12-13, where God commands Israel to walk in all His ways and to love Him. This imagery is also prophetic, pointing to the ultimate fulfillment in Jesus Christ, who perfectly walked in God's ways and invites believers to follow Him, as seen in John 14:6, where Jesus declares, "I am the way, the truth, and the life."

Teaching Points
Walking in God's Ways
To walk in God's ways means to live a life that aligns with His commandments and teachings. This requires a daily commitment to understanding and applying Scripture.

Avoiding Iniquity
The phrase "do no iniquity" suggests a conscious effort to avoid sin. This involves both the avoidance of sinful actions and the cultivation of a heart that desires righteousness.

The Role of Scripture
Immersing oneself in God's Word is essential for understanding His ways. Regular Bible study and meditation on Scripture help believers discern right from wrong.

Dependence on the Holy Spirit
Walking in God's ways is not possible through human effort alone. Believers must rely on the Holy Spirit for guidance and strength to live righteously.

Community and Accountability
Being part of a faith community provides support and accountability, helping believers stay on the path of righteousness and avoid iniquity.

What Does Psalm 119:3 Mean
They do no iniquity

To “do no iniquity” points to a life that refuses to practice sin as a settled pattern. It is not claiming absolute, flawless perfection this side of heaven, but it is describing hearts so aligned with God that sin is neither cherished nor excused.

• Psalm 119:11 says, “I have hidden Your word in my heart that I might not sin against You,” showing the connection between Scripture stored up and sin driven out.

• 1 John 3:9 adds, “No one born of God practices sin, because God’s seed remains in him,” reminding us that new birth produces new appetites.

• Proverbs 4:23 urges, “Guard your heart with all diligence, for from it flow springs of life,” highlighting vigilance against anything that would draw us toward iniquity.

When Scripture speaks this way, it calls believers to an integrity that reaches motives, words, and deeds. The verse therefore comforts and challenges: comfort, because God promises power over sin; challenge, because excuse-making is stripped away.

they walk in His ways

The phrase shifts from what the righteous avoid to how they actively live. “Walk” is the Bible’s everyday verb for a lifestyle, suggesting steady, deliberate steps under divine direction.

• Deuteronomy 10:12 asks, “What does the LORD your God require of you but to fear the LORD your God, to walk in all His ways…?” Obedience has always been relational, not mechanical.

• John 14:15 records Jesus saying, “If you love Me, you will keep My commandments,” tying walking in His ways to love rather than legalism.

• 1 John 2:6 clarifies, “Whoever claims to abide in Him must walk as Jesus walked,” fixing the pattern squarely on Christ.

• Psalm 1:1-3 contrasts the blessed man who “walks not in the counsel of the wicked” but instead delights in God’s law, picturing stability and fruitfulness that come from consistent steps of obedience.

Practical markers of such a walk include:

– A mind continually renewed by Scripture (Romans 12:2).

– Choices filtered through the question, “Does this please the Lord?”

– Readiness to repent quickly when stumbling occurs, keeping short accounts with God (1 John 1:9).

summary

Psalm 119:3 celebrates believers who, by God’s grace, refuse to let sin dominate and instead make obedience their daily path. The verse pairs the negative (“do no iniquity”) with the positive (“walk in His ways”), portraying a wholehearted pursuit of righteousness. It assures us that Scripture-shaped hearts can turn from sin and stride after God with steady, purposeful steps.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 3. - They also do no iniquity: they walk in his ways. Positive righteousness includes negative. Those who walk always in God's Law (ver. 1) will, a fortiori, keep clear of all iniquity.
