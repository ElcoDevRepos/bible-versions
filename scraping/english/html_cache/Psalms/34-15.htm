The eyes of the LORD are on the righteous
This phrase emphasizes God's attentive care and watchfulness over those who live in accordance with His will. In biblical context, "the righteous" refers to individuals who seek to live in obedience to God's commandments and maintain a relationship with Him. This concept is echoed in other scriptures, such as Proverbs 15:3, which states, "The eyes of the LORD are in every place, keeping watch on the evil and the good." The imagery of God's eyes suggests His omniscience and personal involvement in the lives of His people. Historically, the idea of divine watchfulness was comforting to the Israelites, who often faced external threats and needed assurance of God's protection. This phrase also foreshadows the New Testament teaching of Jesus as the Good Shepherd, who knows and cares for His sheep (John 10:14).

and His ears are inclined to their cry
This part of the verse highlights God's responsiveness to the prayers and pleas of the righteous. The imagery of God's ears being inclined suggests a posture of attentiveness and readiness to listen. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, crying out to God was a common practice in times of distress, as seen in the Psalms and other Old Testament narratives. This assurance of God's listening ear is paralleled in 1 Peter 3:12, which reiterates that "the eyes of the Lord are on the righteous, and His ears are open to their prayer." Theologically, this reflects the covenant relationship between God and His people, where He promises to hear and respond to their needs. This phrase also points to the intercessory role of Jesus Christ, who, as our High Priest, mediates on behalf of believers, ensuring that their prayers are heard by the Father (Hebrews 7:25).

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of Psalm 34, who wrote this psalm after pretending to be insane before Abimelech, who drove him away, and he left. This context is crucial as it reflects David's reliance on God during times of distress.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His personal relationship with His people. In this verse, His attentiveness to the righteous is highlighted.

3. The Righteous
Those who are in right standing with God, living according to His commandments and seeking His will.

4. The Cry of the Righteous
Represents the prayers and supplications of those who are faithful to God, seeking His intervention and guidance.

5. Abimelech
The Philistine king before whom David feigned madness, leading to his escape. This event underscores the theme of divine deliverance and protection.
Teaching Points
God's Attentiveness
God is not distant or indifferent; He is actively watching over those who are righteous. This should encourage believers to live in a way that aligns with His will.

The Power of Prayer
The Lord's ears are inclined to the cries of the righteous, underscoring the importance of prayer as a means of communication with God. Believers should be diligent in prayer, trusting that God hears and responds.

Righteous Living
The promise of God's attention is directed towards the righteous. This calls for a life of integrity, obedience, and faithfulness to God's commands.

Divine Protection and Deliverance
Just as David experienced God's deliverance, believers can trust in God's protection and intervention in times of trouble.

Encouragement in Trials
In moments of distress, believers can find comfort in knowing that God is aware of their situation and is attentive to their cries for help.

What Does Psalm 34:15 Mean
The eyes of the LORD

• “The eyes of the LORD are in every place, observing the evil and the good” (Proverbs 15:3).

• Unlike human eyes that tire or miss details, His vision is constant and comprehensive (Psalm 121:4).

• This watchfulness is personal, not detached; “The LORD watches over you” (Psalm 121:5).

• Because God sees perfectly, nothing about our lives—joys, struggles, temptations—goes unnoticed (Job 34:21).

Are on the righteous

• “The LORD’s unfailing love surrounds the one who trusts in Him” (Psalm 32:10). That trust is the essence of righteousness—right standing through faith (Genesis 15:6; Romans 4:3).

• God’s gaze is affectionate, protective, and approving toward those covered by His grace (Psalm 33:18).

• This does not promise a pain-free life, but it assures divine oversight in every circumstance (Psalm 37:23-24).

His ears

• The same God who sees also “bends down to listen” (Psalm 116:2).

• “Surely the arm of the LORD is not too short to save, nor His ear too dull to hear” (Isaiah 59:1).

• His listening is active: He discerns motives, unspoken fears, and broken whispers (1 Samuel 1:13-17).

Are inclined to their cry

• “I have indeed seen the affliction of My people… and I have heard them crying out” (Exodus 3:7).

• God leans in, ready to act—rescuing, guiding, comforting (Psalm 18:6; 2 Corinthians 1:3-4).

• Even when answers delay, His posture is toward us, not away from us (Habakkuk 2:3; Luke 18:7-8).

• New-covenant believers have this assurance amplified in Christ, our Advocate who “always lives to intercede” (Hebrews 7:25; 1 John 2:1).

summary

Psalm 34:15 declares a two-fold promise: God continually watches over those who belong to Him and attentively listens when they call. His vigilant eyes guarantee that no trial escapes His notice, and His receptive ears assure us that every prayer reaches His heart. Together these truths invite steady confidence and heartfelt communion with the Lord who sees, hears, and lovingly responds.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(15) The eyes.--A verse quoted in 1Peter 3:12. (See New Testament Commentary). This psalm had a deep hold on the national mind. With the expression, "his ears to their cry," we may compare the phrase, "to have a person's ear."

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 15. - The eyes of the Lord are upon the righteous (comp. Job 36:7; Psalm 33:18; 1 Peter 3:12; and see the comment on Psalm 33:18). And his ears are open unto their cry. The specific statement of ver. 6 is now generalized. What God had done in the case of the psalmist, he will do in all other similar cases. His eyes will be open to his people's needs, and his ears attent unto their prayers (2 Chronicles 6:40).
