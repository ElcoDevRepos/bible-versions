The LORD knows the days of the blameless
This phrase emphasizes God's omniscience and intimate involvement in the lives of the righteous. The term "blameless" refers to those who live in integrity and righteousness, aligning with God's will. In biblical context, being blameless does not imply sinlessness but rather a sincere devotion to God, as seen in figures like Noah (Genesis 6:9) and Job (Job 1:1). The assurance that God "knows" their days suggests His providential care and guidance, echoing the sentiment found in Psalm 139:1-4, where God's knowledge of our lives is comprehensive and personal. This divine awareness assures believers that their lives are under God's sovereign control, providing comfort and security.

and their inheritance will last forever
The concept of inheritance in the Bible often refers to the blessings and promises God bestows upon His people. For the Israelites, this included the Promised Land, a tangible representation of God's covenant (Deuteronomy 4:21). In a broader spiritual sense, inheritance signifies eternal life and the kingdom of God, as seen in the New Testament (1 Peter 1:4). The promise that this inheritance "will last forever" underscores its eternal nature, contrasting with the temporary and fleeting nature of earthly possessions. This eternal perspective is a recurring theme in Scripture, encouraging believers to focus on heavenly treasures (Matthew 6:19-20). The assurance of an everlasting inheritance is ultimately fulfilled in Jesus Christ, who secures this promise through His death and resurrection, offering believers a share in His eternal kingdom (Ephesians 1:11-14).

Teaching Points
God's Omniscience and Care
God is fully aware of the lives and struggles of the righteous. His knowledge is not passive but active, involving care and provision.

The Security of the Righteous
The inheritance of the blameless is secure and eternal. Unlike earthly possessions, which can be lost or destroyed, the inheritance from God is everlasting.

Living with Integrity
The call to be blameless is a call to live with integrity and righteousness. This involves daily choices to follow God's ways and trust in His promises.

Eternal Perspective
Believers are encouraged to focus on eternal rewards rather than temporary, worldly gains. This perspective helps in enduring trials and maintaining faithfulness.

What Does Psalm 37:18 Mean
the LORD knows

“The LORD knows the days of the blameless…” (Psalm 37:18)

• God’s knowledge is personal and protective, echoing Psalm 1:6: “For the LORD knows the way of the righteous.”

• This isn’t mere awareness; it is covenant loyalty—2 Timothy 2:19 reminds us, “The Lord knows those who are His,” and Jesus adds in Matthew 10:30 that even our hairs are numbered.

• Because He knows, He governs; nothing in the believer’s timeline is random (Romans 8:28).

the days of the blameless

• “Blameless” speaks of those walking with integrity, like Noah in Genesis 6:9 or Job in Job 1:1, yet ultimately declared righteous by faith (Romans 4:5).

• Every “day” is divinely measured (Psalm 139:16). Whether many or few, each is infused with purpose (Ephesians 2:10).

• Psalm 34:15 assures, “The eyes of the LORD are on the righteous,” so our seasons—joyful or trying—are under His watchful care.

their inheritance will last forever

• The promise stretches beyond earthly real estate; it reaches into eternity. Psalm 37:29 already hinted, “The righteous will inherit the land and dwell in it forever.”

• Peter clarifies the scope: an “inheritance imperishable, undefiled, and unfading, reserved in heaven for you” (1 Peter 1:4).

• As co-heirs with Christ (Romans 8:17), believers await a kingdom that “cannot be shaken” (Hebrews 12:28).

• Unlike fleeting worldly wealth (Proverbs 23:5), this inheritance is guaranteed by the resurrected Christ (1 Peter 1:3) and sealed by the Spirit (Ephesians 1:13-14).

living in the reality of this promise

• Rest—Psalm 37:7 urges, “Be still before the LORD and wait patiently for Him.”

• Resist envy—since our future is secure, we need not fret over the temporary success of the wicked (Psalm 37:1-2).

• Invest eternally—1 Timothy 6:18-19 calls us to store up “treasure as a good foundation for the future.”

• Hope in trials—suffering is light and momentary compared to “an eternal weight of glory” (2 Corinthians 4:17).

summary

The verse assures that God intimately oversees every moment of those who walk with Him, and He has secured for them an everlasting inheritance. Because He knows our days, we can face each one with calm confidence. Because He guarantees our forever, we can live generously, fearlessly, and hopefully right now.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 18. - The Lord knoweth the days of the upright; literally, of the perfect - those who yield him a complete obedience. God takes loving note of their days, knows their number, and the events which each day will bring. He will cause all things to "work together for their good." And their inheritance shall be for ever (comp. vers. 27, 29, and 37; which all, like this verse, point, albeit vaguely, to a future life). The mere continuance of a man's posterity in a prosperous condition cannot exhaust the meaning of such phrases as, "Their inheritance shall be for ever;" "Dwell for evermore;" "The righteous shall inherit the land, and dwell therein for ever." If David himself meant no more than this, yet the Holy Spirit which inspired him may have meant more. At any rate, to the Christian the words will always bring up the thought of that "inheritance incorruptible, and undefiled, and that fadeth not away, which is reserved for us in heaven" (1 Peter 1:4).
