Your righteousness is everlasting
This phrase emphasizes the eternal nature of God's righteousness. In the biblical context, righteousness refers to God's moral perfection and justice. The Hebrew word for "righteousness" (צְדָקָה, tzedakah) conveys the idea of being right or just. God's righteousness is not subject to change or decay, unlike human standards of morality which can shift over time. This concept is echoed in other scriptures such as Isaiah 51:6, which states that God's righteousness will never be dismayed. The everlasting nature of God's righteousness assures believers of His unchanging character and faithfulness throughout all generations. This eternal aspect is also a type of Christ, who is described as the same yesterday, today, and forever (Hebrews 13:8).

and Your law is true.
The law referred to here is the Torah, the first five books of the Bible, which contain God's instructions and commandments. The Hebrew word for "law" (תּוֹרָה, torah) can also mean "instruction" or "teaching." The psalmist declares the truthfulness of God's law, affirming its reliability and trustworthiness. In a historical and cultural context, the law was central to the life and identity of the Israelites, guiding their worship, ethics, and community life. The truth of God's law is further supported by Jesus in the New Testament, who stated that He came not to abolish the law but to fulfill it (Matthew 5:17). This fulfillment is seen in Christ's life and teachings, which perfectly embody the truth and intent of the law. The law's truth is also prophetic, pointing to the ultimate revelation of God's will through Jesus, who is described as the Word made flesh (John 1:14).

What Does Psalm 119:142 Mean
Your righteousness is everlasting

- The psalmist affirms that God’s righteousness is not bound by time; it is permanent, unchanging, and dependable. “Before the mountains were born… from everlasting to everlasting You are God” (Psalm 90:2) echoes this same confidence.

- Isaiah 51:8 adds, “My righteousness will last forever,” underscoring that all God’s actions are aligned with His perfect moral character—yesterday, today, and forever (Hebrews 13:8).

- Because God’s righteousness is everlasting, it gives believers an anchor in every season. Whether in exile like Daniel, who prayed about “everlasting righteousness” (Daniel 9:24), or amid trials today, we rest knowing the standard of right never shifts.

- Practical implication: instead of chasing cultural trends, we cling to the One whose character remains steady. That certainty steadies our hearts (James 1:17).

and Your law is true

- The same verse pivots from God’s character to His Word, celebrating the trustworthiness of His commands. Psalm 19:7 affirms, “The Law of the LORD is perfect, reviving the soul,” while Psalm 33:4 declares, “For the word of the LORD is upright.”

- Jesus endorsed this confidence: “Your word is truth” (John 17:17) and “Until heaven and earth pass away, not a single jot or stroke shall pass from the Law” (Matthew 5:18).

- Paul concurs: “The law is holy, and the commandment is holy, righteous, and good” (Romans 7:12).

- Because God’s law is true, it exposes falsehood, guides decisions, and offers freedom (John 8:32). The same voice that framed creation now frames our daily choices, making Scripture the believer’s most reliable compass.

summary

Psalm 119:142 invites us to rejoice that the God we serve is eternally righteous and that His Word is unfailingly true. His unchanging character secures our future; His trustworthy law directs our steps today.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 142. - Thy righteousness is an everlasting righteousness. With God there is "no variableness, neither shadow of turning" (James 1:27). His righteousness (צדקה) is pure abstract right (צדק), one and the same always - not shifting, like human ideas of justice. And thy Law is the truth. An embodiment of that immutable morality which rests upon a basis of absolute eternal truth.
