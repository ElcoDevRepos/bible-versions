The high mountains are for the wild goats
The phrase highlights the natural habitat of wild goats, which are known to inhabit the rugged and elevated terrains of the Middle East. In biblical times, these animals were often seen in the mountainous regions of Israel, such as the Judean wilderness and the hills of Gilead. The high mountains symbolize places of strength and refuge, reflecting God's provision for all creatures. This imagery can be connected to the idea of God as a protector and provider, as seen in other scriptures like Psalm 18:2, where God is described as a "rock" and "fortress."

the cliffs a refuge for the rock badgers
Rock badgers, also known as hyraxes, are small, herbivorous mammals that dwell in rocky terrains. The cliffs provide them with safety from predators, illustrating God's design in creation, where each creature has a suitable habitat. This imagery of refuge can be paralleled with the spiritual refuge God offers to His people, as seen in Psalm 46:1, where God is described as "our refuge and strength." The mention of cliffs as a refuge also evokes the idea of God as a sanctuary, a theme prevalent throughout the Psalms.

Teaching Points
God's Provision in Creation
Just as God provides specific habitats for wild goats and rock badgers, He provides for all His creation, including us. We can trust in His provision for our needs.

Finding Refuge in God
The cliffs and high mountains symbolize the safety and refuge we find in God. In times of trouble, we can seek shelter in His presence.

Adaptation and Resilience
The wild goats and rock badgers are examples of creatures that thrive in their environments. We, too, can adapt and find strength in God to navigate life's challenges.

Wisdom in Creation
Observing how God has intricately designed habitats for different creatures can deepen our appreciation for His wisdom and creativity.

What Does Psalm 104:18 Mean
The high mountains are for the wild goats

• The psalmist points to a literal feature of creation: rugged peaks that God has intentionally assigned to agile mountain goats. Job 39:1–4 reminds us that the Lord personally oversees the birth and life of these creatures in such remote regions.

• By singling out “high mountains,” the verse celebrates God’s sovereign ordering of space—He designates specific habitats for specific beings. Psalm 18:33 echoes this, describing how the Lord “makes my feet like those of a deer and stations me upon the heights”, underscoring His provision of sure footing amid dangerous terrain.

• This arrangement reveals the Creator’s generosity: even the most inhospitable places are filled with life that flourishes under His care. It invites us to marvel at His wisdom and trust Him with the details of our own circumstances (Matthew 6:26).

• Spiritually, the scene pictures God’s ability to equip His people for challenging assignments. Habakkuk 3:19 uses the same imagery—“The Lord GOD is my strength; He makes my feet like those of a deer; He makes me walk upon the heights”—to convey confidence that the Lord enables believers to navigate lofty, demanding paths.

the cliffs a refuge for the rock badgers

• Cliffs, crags, and crevices become safe houses for “rock badgers” (hyraxes). Proverbs 30:26 observes, “rock badgers are creatures of little power, yet they make their homes in the rocks”, highlighting both their vulnerability and the security God provides.

• The term “refuge” signals protection. Psalm 94:22 applies the same word to the Lord Himself: “the LORD has been my stronghold, my God is my rock of refuge”. The physical safety rock badgers find in stone shelters points to the spiritual safety believers find in God.

• While the animal is small and seemingly defenseless, the environment God assigns it neutralizes its weaknesses. This showcases the Creator’s compassion for even the least conspicuous members of His world and assures us that He custom-tailors places of protection for His people as well (Psalm 91:1–2).

• The cliffs also symbolize stability and permanence. Just as the hyrax depends on unmovable rock, we are called to rest on the unchanging character of God (Isaiah 26:4).

summary

Psalm 104:18 spotlights God’s meticulous provision: He plants nimble goats on lofty mountains and shelters fragile rock badgers in craggy cliffs. These literal scenes of creation demonstrate the Lord’s wise allocation of habitats, His compassion for every creature, and His offer of surefooted guidance and secure refuge to all who trust Him.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(18) Wild goats.--Heb., climbers, and so at home on the "high hills." (See 1Samuel 24:2, "the rocks of the wild goats.") "This animal, which is a relation of the Swiss ibex or steinbock, is now called the beden or jaela "(Bible Educator, II., 104).

Conies.--Heb., sh?phan, i.e., "hider." (Comp. Leviticus 11:5, and Bible Educator, II., 201.) Naturalists know it as the hyrax Syriacus. The LXX., Vulg., and Aquila have "hedgehogs."

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 18. - The high hills are a refuge for the wild goats. Even the desolate ranges of the higher mountains are designed by God for the good of his creatures. They furnish a refuge for the ibex, or wild goat, when the hunter presses ca him; and, if they cannot give him food, give him safety. And the rocks for the conies; rather, for the marmots. Marmots still inhabit Palestine, though they are rarely seen; "conies," i.e. rabbits do not. The marmots are "a feeble folk, that make their houses in the rocks" (Proverbs 30:26).
