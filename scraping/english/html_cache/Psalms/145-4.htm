One generation will commend Your works to the next
This phrase emphasizes the importance of passing down the knowledge and experience of God's works from one generation to another. In the biblical context, this reflects the tradition of oral history in ancient Israel, where stories of God's deliverance, such as the Exodus, were recounted to ensure that future generations understood their heritage and God's faithfulness. This practice is seen in Deuteronomy 6:6-7, where parents are instructed to teach God's commandments to their children. The concept of generational teaching is also evident in the New Testament, as seen in 2 Timothy 2:2, where Paul encourages Timothy to entrust teachings to reliable people who can teach others.

and will proclaim Your mighty acts—
The proclamation of God's mighty acts refers to the recounting of His powerful interventions in history, such as creation, the flood, the plagues of Egypt, and the conquest of Canaan. These acts demonstrate God's sovereignty and power over creation and history. In the cultural context of the ancient Near East, where many gods were worshiped, the proclamation of Yahweh's mighty acts served to affirm His supremacy over all other deities. This theme is echoed in the New Testament, where the ultimate mighty act is the resurrection of Jesus Christ, as seen in Acts 2:24, which is proclaimed as the definitive demonstration of God's power and the fulfillment of His promises.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of Psalm 145, King David, is known for his deep relationship with God and his role as a leader who sought to honor God through his life and reign.

2. Generations
The psalm speaks to the continuity of faith and the responsibility of each generation to pass on the knowledge and praise of God's works to the next.

3. God's Mighty Acts
Refers to the powerful deeds and miracles performed by God throughout history, which are to be remembered and proclaimed.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Generational Faithfulness
Each generation has a responsibility to share the knowledge of God and His works with the next. This ensures the continuity of faith and the preservation of God's truth.

Proclaiming God's Mighty Acts
Believers are called to actively recount and celebrate God's deeds, both in personal testimonies and in communal worship, to inspire faith in others.

Role of Parents and Mentors
Parents and spiritual mentors play a crucial role in teaching and modeling faith to the younger generation, emphasizing the importance of intentional discipleship.

The Power of Testimony
Sharing personal experiences of God's faithfulness can be a powerful tool in encouraging and strengthening the faith of others.

Living a Life Worthy of Imitation
Believers should strive to live lives that reflect God's character and works, serving as examples for others to follow.

What Does Psalm 145:4 Mean
One generation

- Picture the Psalmist scanning the crowd: fathers, mothers, teens, toddlers. He doesn’t begin with abstractions; he starts with real people living in real time.

- Scripture often connects God’s faithfulness with specific generations (Exodus 3:15; Psalm 100:5). Each group receives its own “chapter” in the unfolding story of redemption.

- Psalm 71:18 captures the heartbeat: “Even when I am old and gray, do not forsake me, O God, until I proclaim Your power to the next generation.”

- Implication: no generation is a spiritual cul-de-sac. What we know of God must never stop with us.

will commend Your works

- “Commend” means enthusiastically celebrate and hand over something precious. Think of a grandparent passing down a family heirloom—but the heirloom is every work God has ever done.

- Deuteronomy 32:3-4 models this: “For I will proclaim the name of the LORD… He is the Rock; His work is perfect.”

- Psalm 105:1-2 urges, “Give thanks to the LORD… make His deeds known among the nations… tell of all His wonders.”

- Practical shape:

• retelling creation, the flood, the exodus, the cross, the resurrection

• testimonies of answered prayer and daily providences

• weaving God’s deeds into ordinary conversation

to the next

- The direction is forward-looking. Our gaze shifts from what God has done to whom we are handing it.

- Moses pressed this into Israel’s rhythm: “…teach them to your children, speaking of them when you sit in your house and when you walk along the road” (Deuteronomy 11:19).

- Proverbs 22:6 and 2 Timothy 1:5 show that early deposits of truth take root for life.

- A few on-ramps:

• family worship around the table

• mentoring younger believers at church

• writing a personal faith story for grandchildren

• serving in children’s ministries so doctrine is not outsourced to a screen

and will proclaim Your mighty acts—

- The verse crescendos: it is not only gentle commendation but bold proclamation. God’s “mighty acts” include the plagues, the Red Sea, Jericho’s walls, Elijah’s fire, the virgin birth, Calvary, the empty tomb, Pentecost, and every miracle of new birth today.

- Psalm 89:1 echoes, “I will sing of the loving devotion of the LORD forever; with my mouth I will proclaim Your faithfulness to all generations.”

- Acts 4:20 shows the same unstoppable impulse: “For we cannot stop speaking about what we have seen and heard.”

- Proclaiming means:

• declaring the gospel publicly and privately

• highlighting God’s power in testimonies (Luke 8:39)

• refusing to allow secular narratives to eclipse divine intervention

summary

Psalm 145:4 sketches a relay race of praise: each generation grips the baton of God’s works and, without breaking stride, places it firmly in the hands of the next. Our role is clear—know His deeds, celebrate them, speak them, and keep them moving forward until every generation under the sun has heard of His mighty acts.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(4) Shall praise.--Or, praises, with idea of indefinite continuance; and so in the following verses.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 4. - One generation shall praise thy works to another, and shall declare thy mighty acts. The handing down of God's mercies and deliverances from age to age is always regarded in Scripture as the principal mode whereby they are kept in remembrance (Exodus 12:26, 27; Exodus 13:8-10, 14; Deuteronomy 32:7; Psalm 44:1; Psalm 78:3-7, etc.).
