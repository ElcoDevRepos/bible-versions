When he calls out to Me
This phrase emphasizes the importance of prayer and communication with God. Throughout the Bible, calling out to God is a recurring theme, seen in the lives of figures like David, Daniel, and Elijah. It reflects a relationship where the believer actively seeks God's presence and intervention. The act of calling out signifies faith and dependence on God, acknowledging His sovereignty and willingness to listen. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, calling upon the name of the Lord was a common practice during times of distress, as seen in Psalms and the prophetic books.

I will answer him
God's promise to answer is a reassurance of His attentiveness and responsiveness to the prayers of the faithful. This reflects the covenant relationship between God and His people, where He commits to being their protector and guide. The assurance of an answer is echoed in other scriptures, such as Jeremiah 33:3, where God invites His people to call upon Him with the promise of revealing great and unsearchable things. This promise is not just about receiving what is asked for but about God’s presence and wisdom being imparted.

I will be with him in trouble
This phrase highlights God's promise of presence during difficult times. It is reminiscent of God's assurance to Joshua in Joshua 1:9, where He promises to be with him wherever he goes. The presence of God in times of trouble is a central theme in the Bible, providing comfort and strength to believers. Historically, this reflects the understanding that God is not distant but actively involved in the lives of His people, offering support and guidance through trials.

I will deliver him
Deliverance is a key theme in the Bible, often associated with salvation and rescue from danger or oppression. This promise of deliverance can be seen in the Exodus narrative, where God delivers the Israelites from slavery in Egypt. It also points to the ultimate deliverance through Jesus Christ, who delivers believers from sin and death. The concept of deliverance is both physical and spiritual, encompassing God's intervention in various aspects of life.

and honor him
The promise of honor signifies God's recognition and reward for faithfulness. In biblical times, honor was a significant aspect of social and religious life, often associated with reputation, respect, and divine favor. This promise can be connected to the Beatitudes in Matthew 5, where Jesus speaks of the blessings and honor bestowed upon those who are faithful and righteous. It reflects the biblical principle that God exalts the humble and honors those who trust in Him.

What Does Psalm 91:15 Mean
When he calls out to Me

• The verse assumes a living, verbal relationship between the believer and the LORD. He expects us to speak up.

• Calling out is an act of faith, acknowledging God as the only sufficient Helper (Psalm 50:15; Romans 10:13).

• Scripture encourages this bold approach: “Call to Me, and I will answer you and show you great and mighty things you do not know” (Jeremiah 33:3). The promise of Psalm 91:15 rests on that same certainty.

I will answer him

• God’s response is not tentative; He pledges to answer.

• His answers may come as a clear yes, a protective no, or a wise delay, yet they always flow from perfect love (Isaiah 65:24; 1 John 5:14-15).

• “For the eyes of the Lord are on the righteous and His ears are inclined to their prayer” (1 Peter 3:12). This is a literal promise, not merely a comforting idea.

I will be with him in trouble

• God does not merely eliminate difficulty; He enters it with us.

• “When you pass through the waters, I will be with you … the rivers will not sweep over you” (Isaiah 43:2). The pattern recurs from Joseph’s prison to Paul’s shipwreck—divine presence in real‐world hardship (Genesis 39:20-21; Acts 27:23-24).

• His nearness is the believer’s unshakable security: “I will never leave you nor forsake you” (Deuteronomy 31:6; echoed in Hebrews 13:5).

I will deliver him and honor him

• Deliverance is rescue—sometimes immediate, sometimes through perseverance, always certain in its ultimate outcome (Psalm 34:17-19; 2 Timothy 4:18).

• God goes further: He honors those who trust Him. “Those who honor Me I will honor” (1 Samuel 2:30).

• Honor may appear as vindication before enemies (Psalm 23:5), fruitful service now (John 12:26), and eternal glory later (Romans 2:7).

summary

Psalm 91:15 unfolds a fourfold pledge from the LORD to every believer: when we cry out, He answers; when trouble comes, He stays; when danger threatens, He rescues; and beyond rescue, He exalts. Each clause is a literal promise, rooted in God’s faithful character and repeatedly confirmed throughout Scripture. Trusting these words invites us to pray boldly, rest securely, persevere confidently, and anticipate honor that only God can bestow.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 15. - He shall call upon me, and I will answer him. This is equivalent to, "Whenever he calls upon me, I will answer him," or "I will grant all his prayers." I will be with him in trouble (comp. Psalm 46:1). I will deliver him (see above, ver. 14). And honour him; or, "bring him to honour" (compare "I will set him on high," in the preceding verse).
