My lips pour forth praise
This phrase emphasizes the psalmist's response to God's teachings. In biblical context, praise is a common expression of gratitude and reverence towards God, seen throughout the Psalms. The act of lips pouring forth suggests an abundant and overflowing expression, indicating that the praise is not forced but a natural outpouring. This aligns with the biblical principle that out of the abundance of the heart, the mouth speaks (Luke 6:45). The psalmist's praise is a direct result of their relationship with God and understanding of His word.

for You teach me Your statutes
The psalmist acknowledges God as the ultimate teacher, highlighting a personal and direct relationship with the divine. In the historical and cultural context of ancient Israel, statutes refer to the laws and decrees given by God, particularly those found in the Torah. This teaching is not merely academic but transformative, guiding the believer in righteous living. The connection to other scripture is evident in passages like Psalm 25:4-5, where the psalmist seeks God's guidance and truth. This teaching is also prophetic, pointing to the New Covenant where God's laws are written on the hearts of believers (Jeremiah 31:33). In a typological sense, Jesus Christ is the ultimate teacher, fulfilling the law and embodying God's statutes, as seen in His Sermon on the Mount (Matthew 5-7).

Teaching Points
The Overflow of Praise
When we truly understand and internalize God's teachings, our natural response is to praise Him. This praise is not forced but flows naturally from a heart transformed by His word.

The Role of Divine Instruction
God's statutes are not just rules but are teachings that guide us to live a life that honors Him. Understanding this helps us see His laws as a source of wisdom and joy.

The Importance of Learning
Continual learning and meditation on God's word are crucial for spiritual growth. As we learn, we are equipped to live out our faith more fully.

The Connection Between Knowledge and Worship
True worship stems from a deep knowledge of God and His ways. As we grow in understanding, our worship becomes more profound and sincere.

Living Out the Statutes
Application of God's statutes in daily life is essential. It's not enough to know them; we must live them out, reflecting God's character in our actions.

What Does Psalm 119:171 Mean
My lips

- The psalmist speaks personally—“My lips”—showing that praise is not delegated but voiced firsthand (Psalm 34:1).

- Lips represent the gateway of the heart (Matthew 12:34); what fills the inner life must come out.

- Notice the readiness: no hesitation, no condition, just an open mouth prepared to honor God (Psalm 51:15).

Pour forth praise

- “Pour forth” pictures a steady, overflowing stream, not a trickle (Psalm 71:8).

- Praise is more than polite acknowledgment; it is abundant celebration of God’s character (Revelation 5:13).

- Such effusive worship refreshes both speaker and hearer, echoing throughout community life (Colossians 3:16).

For You teach me

- The reason for praise is clear: divine instruction. God is both Author and Teacher (Isaiah 54:13).

- Spirit-led teaching penetrates deeper than human insight, shaping motives, choices, and affections (John 14:26).

- Gratitude naturally rises when we see that every lesson comes from a loving Father intent on our good (Psalm 25:4-5).

Your statutes

- “Statutes” are fixed, trustworthy boundaries that safeguard flourishing (Psalm 19:7-8).

- They reveal God’s moral beauty and invite willing obedience (James 1:25).

- Embracing these statutes leads to stability amid cultural change (Matthew 7:24-25) and fuels ongoing praise (Psalm 119:7).

summary

- Praise begins with a personal, willing mouth.

- Overflowing worship springs from a heart saturated with God’s teaching.

- The Lord Himself instructs, and His unchanging statutes anchor our lives.

- When we cherish His word, our lips cannot help but pour forth unending praise.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
TAU.

(171) Shall utter.--Better, preserving the metaphor of the Hebrew, pour forth a stream of praise.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 171. - My lips shall utter praise, when thou hast taught me thy statutes; rather, let my lips pour forth praise, for thou teachest me thy statutes. The psalmist feels that his prayer for enlightenment (ver. 169b) is answered, or just about to be answered, and that therefore it behooves him to gush forth with praise like a fountain (comp. ver. 7).
