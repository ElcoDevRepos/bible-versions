Man ate the bread of angels;
This phrase refers to the manna provided by God to the Israelites during their wilderness journey after the Exodus from Egypt. Manna is described in Exodus 16 and Numbers 11 as a miraculous food that appeared each morning, sustaining the Israelites for 40 years. The term "bread of angels" suggests a heavenly origin, emphasizing the divine provision and the supernatural nature of this sustenance. In Jewish tradition, manna is often associated with spiritual nourishment, symbolizing God's care and provision. The New Testament draws a parallel between manna and Jesus Christ, the "bread of life" (John 6:31-35), highlighting the spiritual sustenance He provides to believers.

He sent them food in abundance.
This part of the verse underscores the generosity and sufficiency of God's provision. Despite the Israelites' complaints and lack of faith, God provided not just enough, but an abundance of food. This reflects God's grace and mercy, even in the face of human disobedience. The abundance of manna can be seen as a foreshadowing of the spiritual abundance found in Christ, who offers life and sustenance to all who believe in Him. The concept of divine abundance is echoed in other scriptures, such as Philippians 4:19, where God is described as supplying all needs according to His riches in glory.

Teaching Points
God's Provision
God provides for our needs abundantly, just as He did for the Israelites with manna. We can trust Him to meet our needs today.

Spiritual Sustenance
Just as manna sustained the Israelites physically, Jesus, the "bread of life," sustains us spiritually. We should seek Him daily for our spiritual nourishment.

Gratitude and Contentment
The Israelites often grumbled despite God's provision. We are called to cultivate gratitude and contentment, recognizing God's blessings in our lives.

Faith in God's Promises
The provision of manna was a test of faith for the Israelites. We are encouraged to trust in God's promises and His timing, even when circumstances seem challenging.

What Does Psalm 78:25 Mean
Man ate the bread of angels

Psalm 78:25 declares, “Man ate the bread of angels,” pointing to the miraculous manna that appeared each morning in the wilderness.

• Exodus 16:4 shows the LORD promising to “rain down bread from heaven,” underscoring that this food was not ordinary but heavenly in origin.

• Psalm 105:40 calls the manna “bread of heaven,” emphasizing that angels—God’s heavenly servants—are accustomed to such perfect sustenance. Israel, though fallen and earth-bound, was welcomed to the same table.

• 1 Corinthians 10:3 labels manna “spiritual food,” reminding believers that God’s provision reaches past physical needs to nurture faith itself.

• John 6:31 records the crowd quoting this very event to Jesus, proving its lasting significance in Israel’s collective memory.

Taken literally, the verse teaches:

– The source: God sent a substance unknown on earth.

– The status: What nourishes angels is sufficient for people; God gives His very best.

– The symbolism: Just as manna came daily, so Christ, the true “bread of life” (John 6:35), must be received continually.

He sent them food in abundance

The second half of the verse affirms the quantity and faithfulness of God’s supply.

• Exodus 16:17-18 records that whether one gathered much or little, “each one gathered as much as he needed,” spotlighting both abundance and equity.

• Nehemiah 9:20 praises God for not withholding manna, reinforcing the idea that the supply never ran out during forty wilderness years.

• Deuteronomy 8:3 connects manna with a lesson in dependence: God provided abundantly so Israel would learn that “man does not live on bread alone.”

• Jesus applies that lesson in John 6:35—abundant manna foreshadows His all-sufficient life; whoever comes to Him “will never hunger.”

Key takeaways:

– Abundance reveals God’s character: He is generous, not stingy.

– The steady flow of manna teaches trust; Israel never needed a backup plan.

– For Christians, the passage assures that God still supplies richly “according to His glorious riches in Christ Jesus” (Philippians 4:19).

summary

Psalm 78:25 celebrates a literal historical miracle in which God fed His people with heavenly bread and did so lavishly. The verse reminds us that:

1. God’s provision originates in heaven and meets both physical and spiritual hunger.

2. He gives His very best—what angels enjoy—to undeserving people.

3. His supply is unceasing and more than sufficient, foreshadowing the fullness found in Christ, the true Bread from heaven.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(25) Angels' food.--See margin, and comp. Wisdom Of Solomon 16:20. LXX. and Vulgate, "angels' bread." Some explain, after Job 24:22; Job 34:30, lordly food, such as nobles eat--here, quails. But in connection with "food from heaven," the popular idea of angels' food which poetry reluctantly gives up may be retained.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 25. - Man did eat angels' food; literally, bread of the mighty ones, by which the LXX. and most commentators understand "angels" to be meant. "Angels' food" may mean either the actual food on which angels subsist, or food supplied by the ministration of angels, and derived from their dwelling place. It cannot be laid down dogmatically that angels require no food. He sent them meat to the full (comp. Exodus 16:3, where the Israelites contrast with their wretched life in the wilderness their life in Egypt, where they "did eat bread to the full").
