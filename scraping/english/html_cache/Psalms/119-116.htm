Sustain me as You promised
This phrase reflects a plea for divine support and is rooted in the covenantal promises God made to His people. The psalmist is invoking the faithfulness of God, which is a recurring theme throughout the Scriptures. In the Old Testament, God's promises to Israel included protection, provision, and presence (Deuteronomy 31:6). The request for sustenance is not merely physical but spiritual, aligning with the understanding that God's Word is life-giving (Deuteronomy 8:3). This plea can also be seen as a type of Christ, who is the ultimate fulfillment of God's promises, providing eternal life and sustenance to believers (John 6:35).

that I may live
The desire for life here is both physical and spiritual. In the biblical context, life is often associated with obedience to God's commandments and living in His presence (Deuteronomy 30:19-20). The psalmist's request underscores the belief that true life is found in relationship with God. This is echoed in the New Testament, where Jesus declares that He came to give life abundantly (John 10:10). The concept of life in the Bible is holistic, encompassing well-being, peace, and fulfillment in God's purposes.

let me not be ashamed of my hope
Hope in the biblical sense is a confident expectation based on God's promises. The psalmist's concern about being ashamed reflects the cultural context where honor and shame were significant social values. In the ancient Near East, being put to shame was a serious matter, often associated with defeat or failure. The psalmist's hope is rooted in God's faithfulness, and he seeks assurance that this hope will not lead to disappointment. This connects to the New Testament teaching that hope in Christ does not disappoint because of God's love poured into our hearts (Romans 5:5). The ultimate hope for believers is the resurrection and eternal life through Jesus Christ, who is the anchor of our hope (Hebrews 6:19).

Teaching Points
Reliance on God's Promises
The psalmist's request for sustenance is rooted in the promises of God. Believers are encouraged to rely on God's Word as the foundation of their hope and life.

The Nature of True Hope
True hope in God is not wishful thinking but a confident expectation based on His faithfulness. This hope will not lead to shame or disappointment.

Living by Faith
The psalmist's desire to "live" is not just physical survival but a life enriched by faith and obedience to God's commands. Christians are called to live by faith, trusting in God's provision and guidance.

Avoiding Shame through Trust
The plea to not be ashamed underscores the importance of trusting in God rather than in human strength or wisdom. Believers are reminded that their hope in God will be vindicated.

Sustenance through Scripture
Just as the psalmist seeks sustenance from God's promises, Christians are encouraged to find spiritual nourishment and strength in the study and application of Scripture.

What Does Psalm 119:116 Mean
Sustain me as You promised

God’s support is not a vague wish; it rests on definite promises recorded in His Word. The psalmist knows those promises are true and calls on the Lord to act on them.

• Psalm 54:4: “Surely God is my helper; the Lord is the sustainer of my soul.”

• Isaiah 41:10: “Do not fear, for I am with you… I will uphold you with My righteous right hand.”

• Hebrews 13:5–6 reminds believers, “He Himself has said, ‘Never will I leave you, never will I forsake you.’ So we say with confidence, ‘The Lord is my helper; I will not be afraid.’”

The verse invites us to lean wholly on God’s unbreakable word, trusting Him to hold us steady when our own strength ends.

That I may live

The desired outcome of God’s sustaining power is life—full, vibrant, God–centered life.

• Deuteronomy 8:3 (quoted by Jesus in Matthew 4:4) states, “Man does not live on bread alone but on every word that comes from the mouth of the LORD.”

• John 10:10: “I have come that they may have life, and have it in all its fullness.”

• Psalm 119:25 echoes the same need: “My soul clings to the dust; revive me according to Your word.”

Life here encompasses physical preservation, daily strength, and the spiritual vitality that flows from obedience to Scripture. The psalmist knows that without God’s ongoing help, genuine life slips away.

Let me not be ashamed of my hope

Hope fixed on God’s promises will never embarrass the believer. The psalmist asks to be spared any situation where trust in the Lord might seem misplaced.

• Romans 5:5: “Hope does not disappoint, because God’s love has been poured into our hearts through the Holy Spirit.”

• 1 Peter 2:6, quoting Isaiah 28:16: “Whoever believes in Him will never be put to shame.”

• Psalm 25:3: “Surely none who wait for You will be put to shame.”

This line is both a confession of confident expectation and a plea for God to vindicate that expectation publicly. When the Lord upholds His people, He guards them from disgrace and proves that their reliance on Him is well founded.

summary

Psalm 119:116 is a compact prayer of dependence: the psalmist asks the Lord to uphold him according to the sure promises of Scripture, so that he may continue to experience true life and never face the humiliation of a hope that fails. Each phrase points us back to God’s faithfulness—He sustains, He grants life, and He confirms the hope placed in Him. Those who cling to His Word can rest assured that He will keep them steady, vibrant, and unashamed.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 116. - Uphold me according unto thy Word, that I may live; or, "according to thy promise." God's sustaining grace, always needed to "uphold" the righteous, is graciously "promised" them. Their "life" depends upon God's faithfulness to his promise. And let me not be ashamed of my hope. As he would be, if God's promise were not kept.
