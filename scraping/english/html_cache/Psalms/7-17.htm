I will thank the LORD for His righteousness
This phrase emphasizes the psalmist's gratitude towards God, specifically for His righteousness. In the biblical context, righteousness refers to God's moral perfection and justice. The psalmist acknowledges that God's actions are always just and fair, which is a recurring theme throughout the Psalms. This gratitude is not just for personal deliverance but for the assurance that God governs the world with justice. The concept of God's righteousness is also seen in Genesis 18:25, where Abraham appeals to God's justice. In the New Testament, this righteousness is fulfilled in Jesus Christ, who embodies God's perfect justice and offers it to believers (Romans 3:22).

and sing praise to the name of the LORD Most High.
Singing praise is a common expression of worship in the Bible, reflecting joy and reverence. The "name of the LORD" signifies God's character and reputation, which are worthy of praise. The title "Most High" (El Elyon) is used to denote God's supreme authority and sovereignty over all creation. This title is first seen in Genesis 14:18-20, where Melchizedek blesses Abram in the name of God Most High. It underscores God's ultimate power and dominion, which is a source of comfort and assurance for the faithful. In the New Testament, Jesus is recognized as the fulfillment of this divine authority, as seen in Philippians 2:9-11, where every knee will bow to His name.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of this psalm, David is expressing his gratitude and praise to God. He often wrote psalms during times of distress, seeking God's justice and deliverance.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal existence and faithfulness to His people. In this verse, David acknowledges God's righteousness.

3. The LORD Most High (El Elyon)
A title for God that emphasizes His supreme authority and sovereignty over all creation. It reflects God's exalted position and power.
Teaching Points
Gratitude for Righteousness
Recognize that God's righteousness is a reason for thanksgiving. His just nature ensures that He will always act in accordance with His perfect character.

Praise as a Response
Our response to God's attributes, such as His righteousness, should be one of praise. Singing and worship are appropriate expressions of our gratitude.

Acknowledging God's Sovereignty
By referring to God as the "LORD Most High," we acknowledge His ultimate authority and control over all circumstances in our lives.

Consistency in Worship
Like David, we should cultivate a habit of praising God in all situations, not just when things are going well.

Reflecting on God's Character
Regularly meditate on the attributes of God, such as His righteousness, to deepen your understanding and appreciation of who He is.

What Does Psalm 7:17 Mean
I will thank the LORD

David’s statement is personal, deliberate, and ongoing. Gratitude is not reactive; it is an act of the will.

• Psalm 9:1 affirms the same resolve: “I will praise You, O LORD, with my whole heart.”

• 1 Thessalonians 5:18 calls every believer to “give thanks in all circumstances,” underscoring that thanksgiving is a standing order, not an occasional suggestion.

• Choosing to thank God shifts our focus from circumstances to His character, allowing faith to rise above fear or frustration.

for His righteousness

The reason for praise is specific: God’s flawless righteousness.

• Psalm 11:7 declares, “For the LORD is righteous; He loves justice.”

• Romans 3:25-26 shows that God’s righteousness is fully displayed in Christ, who satisfied justice so He could “be just and the justifier of the one who has faith in Jesus.”

• Because the Lord never deviates from what is right, believers can trust His judgments, timing, and promises without reservation.

and sing praise

Thanksgiving naturally overflows into worshipful song.

• Psalm 13:6: “I will sing to the LORD, for He has been good to me.”

• Ephesians 5:19 urges us to speak “to one another with psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs,” making melody in our hearts to the Lord.

• Vocal praise unites heart, mind, and body, turning theology into doxology and reinforcing truth through repetition and melody.

to the name of the LORD Most High

David honors God’s revealed name, exalting Him above every other claim to authority.

• Genesis 14:19 identifies Him as “God Most High, Creator of heaven and earth,” a title declaring absolute sovereignty.

• Psalm 91:1 gives assurance to those who “dwell in the shelter of the Most High,” linking His name to protection and peace.

• Philippians 2:9-11 promises that every knee will bow at the name of Jesus, confirming that the “Most High” title culminates in Christ’s exaltation.

summary

Psalm 7:17 models a believer’s response to God’s unchanging righteousness: a conscious decision to give thanks, a heartfelt song of praise, and an unwavering recognition of the LORD’s supreme authority. Such worship reorients our hearts, strengthens our faith, and proclaims to the world that the Most High is both righteous and worthy of all honor.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 17. - I will praise the Lord according to his righteousness. Another abrupt transition - a song of thankfulness to Jehovah for giving the deliverance which the psalmist foresees, and considers as good as accomplished. And will sing praise to the Name of the Lord most high (comp. Psalm 8:1, 9, "How excellent is thy Name in all the earth!"). God is identified with his Name very commonly in Scripture, or, perhaps we should say, the Name of God is used as a periphrasis for God himself. Where God puts his special presence, he is said to "put his Name" (Deuteronomy 12:5, 21: 1 Kings 14:21; 2 Chronicles 12:13). His Name is "holy and reverend" (Psalm 111:19); "incense is offered unto it" (Malachi 1:11); it is "magnified for ever" (1 Chronicles 17:24); for it the temple is built (1 Kings 8:44); through it the godly "tread down their enemies" (Psalm 44:5); the "desire of men's souls is to it" (Isaiah 26:8). (See also Psalm 92:1; Psalm 96:8; Psalm 99:3; Psalm 103:1; Psalm 105:1; Psalm 113:1; Psalm 115:1; Psalm 119:55; Psalm 145:1, 2, 21; Psalm 148:13; Psalm 149:3.)
