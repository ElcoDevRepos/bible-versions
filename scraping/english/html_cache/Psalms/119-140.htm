Your promise
The term "promise" in this context refers to the Word of God, which encompasses His laws, decrees, and assurances given to His people. Throughout the Bible, God's promises are seen as reliable and trustworthy, forming the foundation of faith for believers. This phrase highlights the covenantal nature of God's relationship with His people, as seen in His promises to Abraham (Genesis 12:1-3), Moses (Exodus 6:6-8), and David (2 Samuel 7:12-16). These promises are fulfilled in the New Testament through Jesus Christ, who is the ultimate expression of God's faithfulness (2 Corinthians 1:20).

is completely pure;
The purity of God's promise signifies its perfection and freedom from any deceit or error. In biblical terms, purity often relates to holiness and righteousness, attributes of God Himself. The psalmist emphasizes that God's Word is refined and tested, akin to precious metals purified by fire (Psalm 12:6). This purity assures believers that God's promises are untainted and reliable, contrasting with the flawed and often deceptive words of humans (Numbers 23:19).

therefore Your servant
The term "servant" reflects a position of humility and dedication to God. In the biblical context, being a servant of God is an honorable role, as seen in figures like Moses (Deuteronomy 34:5) and David (Psalm 89:20). This phrase indicates a personal relationship with God, where the psalmist acknowledges their role in serving and obeying God's commands. It also reflects the broader biblical theme of servanthood, which is exemplified by Jesus Christ, who came not to be served but to serve (Mark 10:45).

loves it.
The love for God's promise is a recurring theme in Psalm 119, where the psalmist expresses deep affection and reverence for God's Word. This love is not merely emotional but is demonstrated through obedience and meditation on the Scriptures (Psalm 119:97). The psalmist's love for God's promise is rooted in the understanding of its truth, reliability, and life-giving power. This love is a response to the transformative impact of God's Word, which guides, comforts, and sustains believers (Psalm 119:105).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Psalmist
The author of Psalm 119, traditionally believed to be King David, who expresses deep love and reverence for God's Word.

2. God
The divine author of the promises and laws that the psalmist cherishes.

3. Servant
Refers to the psalmist himself, who identifies as a devoted follower of God, committed to His Word.
Teaching Points
The Purity of God's Word
God's promises are described as "completely pure," indicating their perfection and reliability. Believers can trust in the absolute truth and integrity of Scripture.

Love for God's Word
The psalmist's love for God's promises is a model for believers. This love stems from recognizing the purity and truth of God's Word, which should inspire devotion and obedience.

The Role of the Servant
Identifying as God's servant implies a relationship of humility and submission. Believers are called to serve God by adhering to His Word and living out its truths.

The Transformative Power of Scripture
The purity of God's Word has the power to transform lives. As believers meditate on and apply Scripture, they are sanctified and equipped for righteous living.

What Does Psalm 119:140 Mean
Your promise

- The psalmist singles out God’s “promise” (or “word,” v. 103, v. 105) as the focal point of trust.

- Scripture consistently portrays every divine declaration as certain: “The word of the LORD is upright, and all His work is done in faithfulness” (Psalm 33:4).

- Because God’s character is unchanging (Malachi 3:6), every promise He makes stands firm (Numbers 23:19).

- Jesus mirrors this confidence when He prays, “Your word is truth” (John 17:17).

is completely pure

- “Completely pure” pictures metal refined until every impurity is removed (Psalm 12:6, “words… like silver refined seven times”; Proverbs 30:5).

- No mixture of error, exaggeration, or half-truth exists in Scripture: “The sum of Your word is truth, and every one of Your righteous judgments endures forever” (Psalm 119:160).

- Such purity flows from God’s holy nature (Isaiah 6:3); He cannot lie (Titus 1:2), so His word cannot deceive.

- This absolute reliability invites our wholehearted confidence, echoing David’s testimony: “As for God, His way is perfect; the word of the LORD is flawless” (2 Samuel 22:31).

therefore Your servant loves it

- Love here is more than emotion; it is devotion expressed in obedience (John 14:21).

- The psalmist identifies as “Your servant,” gladly submitting to the Word that has proven flawless.

- Love grows with exposure: “Oh, how I love Your law! All day long it is my meditation” (Psalm 119:97).

- Because the Word is life-giving (Deuteronomy 32:47) and illuminating (Psalm 119:105), affection rises naturally: “I have treasured Your word in my heart” (Psalm 119:11).

- Practical responses:

• Read it daily (Joshua 1:8).

• Memorize and meditate on it (Psalm 1:2).

• Align choices with it (James 1:22-25).

summary

Psalm 119:140 celebrates the flawless integrity of God’s Word and the fitting response of devoted love. Since every promise God speaks is utterly pure, His servants delight to receive, trust, and obey it, confident that it will never fail.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(140) Pure.--More literally, purged by trial. LXX. and Vulg., "fired." It is not only the excellence, but the proved excellence, of the Divine Word, which is the object of love and adoration here.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 140. - Thy Word is very pure; or, "is purged, assayed, tried in the fire." God's "promises" are especially meant, which the course of events tests. Therefore thy servant loveth it (comp. vers. 97, 113, 119, 127, 159, 163).
