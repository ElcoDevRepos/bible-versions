My tongue sings of Your word
This phrase emphasizes the psalmist's deep devotion and joy in God's word. Singing is a form of worship and expression of joy, often used in the Psalms to convey praise and adoration. The act of singing about God's word suggests a heart overflowing with gratitude and reverence. In biblical times, singing was a common way to memorize and meditate on scripture, as seen in Deuteronomy 31:19-22, where Moses instructs the Israelites to learn a song as a testimony. This practice underscores the importance of internalizing God's word. The psalmist's use of "tongue" highlights the personal and vocal nature of this praise, aligning with James 3:9-10, which speaks about the power of the tongue to bless God.

for all Your commandments are righteous
This phrase affirms the inherent righteousness of God's commandments. The psalmist acknowledges that God's laws are not arbitrary but are rooted in His holy and just nature. This reflects the biblical understanding that God's commandments are a reflection of His character, as seen in Leviticus 19:2, where God calls His people to be holy because He is holy. The term "righteous" indicates moral perfection and justice, qualities that are consistently attributed to God's laws throughout scripture. In Romans 7:12, Paul echoes this sentiment by declaring the law holy, righteous, and good. The psalmist's declaration serves as a reminder that adherence to God's commandments leads to a life aligned with His will, providing a standard for ethical and moral living.

Teaching Points
The Power of the Tongue
Our speech should reflect our reverence for God's word. Just as the psalmist sings of God's commandments, we should use our words to glorify God and edify others.

Righteousness of God's Commandments
Recognize that God's laws are inherently righteous and just. This understanding should guide our moral and ethical decisions.

Joy in God's Word
The psalmist finds joy in singing about God's word. We should cultivate a similar joy and enthusiasm for studying and sharing Scripture.

Living Out the Word
It's not enough to know God's commandments; we must live them out. Our actions should align with the righteousness of God's word.

Consistency in Worship
Regularly engage in worship that includes the proclamation of God's word, whether through song, prayer, or study.

What Does Psalm 119:172 Mean
My tongue sings

• King David moves beyond silent meditation to vocal, public praise, showing that true delight in God overflows audibly (Psalm 34:1; Psalm 71:8; Hebrews 13:15).

• “My tongue” underscores personal responsibility; no proxy worship suffices. The believer’s own mouth is meant to echo God’s greatness (Psalm 63:3–5).

• Singing is not reserved for ideal circumstances; it is a discipline of faith even in affliction, as demonstrated earlier in the psalm (Psalm 119:61, 62).

of Your word

• The content of the song is Scripture itself—God’s revealed truth, promises, and testimonies (Psalm 119:54, 111).

• When God’s word fills the heart, it naturally shapes the lyrics that pour from the lips (Colossians 3:16).

• Praising “Your word” reinforces confidence that every line of Scripture is worthy of celebration and proclamation (Psalm 19:7–10).

for all Your commandments

• “For” provides the reason: praise is grounded in the character of God’s commands, not fleeting emotion.

• “All” leaves nothing out; every statute, precept, and ordinance is included (Psalm 119:6, 160). Selective obedience or selective admiration has no place.

• The verse ties worship to obedience; delight in God inevitably honors His directives (John 14:15; 1 John 5:3).

are righteous

• God’s commandments mirror His own perfect nature; therefore, they are inherently righteous (Psalm 119:142; Romans 7:12).

• Because His laws are righteous, they can be trusted to guide, correct, and bless (Psalm 19:11; 2 Timothy 3:16–17).

• Declaring their righteousness in song testifies to the world that God’s standards are good and just, countering any culture that challenges His moral authority (Isaiah 5:20).

summary

Psalm 119:172 portrays a believer whose mouth cannot stay silent: God’s word fills his heart, so praise fills his tongue. Every command, without exception, is righteous, giving solid ground for continual, vocal worship and wholehearted obedience.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(172) My tongue shall speak of Thy word.--Rather, My tongue shall make response to Thy word, that all Thy commandments are true.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 172. - My tongue shall speak of thy Word; or, "let my tongue respond to thy promise " - return praise, i.e., for the fulfillment of thy promise. For all thy commandments are righteousness. And therefore are worthy objects of praise.
