Great is our Lord
This phrase emphasizes the supremacy and majesty of God, a central theme throughout the Psalms. The greatness of God is a recurring motif in the Old Testament, highlighting His sovereignty over creation and His unparalleled authority. In the context of ancient Israel, acknowledging God's greatness was a declaration of faith in His ability to protect and provide for His people, contrasting with the limited power of human rulers and pagan deities. This greatness is also reflected in the New Testament, where Jesus is recognized as Lord, affirming His divine nature and authority (Philippians 2:9-11).

and mighty in power
The power of God is a testament to His ability to act in the world and in the lives of individuals. This power is evident in the creation narrative (Genesis 1), where God speaks the universe into existence. It is also demonstrated in the deliverance of Israel from Egypt, showcasing His ability to intervene in history. The power of God is not just physical but also spiritual, as seen in the resurrection of Jesus, which is the ultimate display of divine power over sin and death (Romans 1:4). This power assures believers of God's capability to fulfill His promises and sustain His creation.

His understanding has no limit
God's understanding is infinite, surpassing human comprehension. This attribute is closely related to His omniscience, the ability to know all things past, present, and future. In the biblical context, God's understanding is often contrasted with human wisdom, which is limited and flawed (Isaiah 55:8-9). The limitless understanding of God assures believers that He is aware of all circumstances and can provide perfect guidance and judgment. This is further illustrated in the New Testament, where Jesus, as the embodiment of divine wisdom, discerns the thoughts and intentions of the heart (Hebrews 4:12-13).

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Psalmist
The author of Psalm 147, traditionally attributed to King David, who is expressing praise and adoration for God's greatness and power.

2. The Lord (Yahweh)
The central figure in this verse, whose greatness, power, and limitless understanding are being extolled.

3. Israel
The nation often addressed in the Psalms, representing God's chosen people who are recipients of His mighty acts and wisdom.

4. Creation
While not directly mentioned in this verse, the context of Psalm 147 includes references to God's creation, highlighting His power and understanding.

5. The Exiles
The historical context of the Psalm may relate to the return of the Jewish exiles from Babylon, a time when God's power and understanding were crucial for restoration.
Teaching Points
God's Infinite Power
Recognize that God's power is beyond human comprehension and is evident in both creation and His providential care for His people.

Limitless Understanding
Trust in God's perfect wisdom, especially in times of uncertainty, knowing that His understanding is without limit.

Praise and Worship
Cultivate a heart of worship by acknowledging God's greatness and mighty power in your daily life.

Dependence on God
In practical matters, rely on God's wisdom rather than solely on human understanding, seeking His guidance in decision-making.

Encouragement in Trials
Find comfort in God's omnipotence and omniscience, knowing that He is fully aware of your circumstances and has the power to sustain you.

What Does Psalm 147:5 Mean
Great is our Lord

“Great is our Lord…” (Psalm 147:5) invites us to pause and recognize the unmatched majesty of God.

• This greatness is absolute, not relative; there is no higher standard against which He is measured (Psalm 145:3; 1 Chronicles 29:11).

• Creation itself testifies—He “determines the number of the stars and calls them each by name” (Psalm 147:4). Only a truly great Lord governs galaxies and guides sparrows (Matthew 10:29–31).

• Because His greatness is personal (“our Lord”), the psalmist moves worship from theory to relationship. Like David, we can say, “The LORD is my shepherd” (Psalm 23:1).

and mighty in power

God’s greatness flows into limitless power that acts in history and in our lives.

• He speaks worlds into existence (Genesis 1:1–3) and parts seas for His people (Exodus 14:21–22).

• Jeremiah marvels, “Ah, Lord GOD, You made the heavens and the earth by Your great power… nothing is too difficult for You” (Jeremiah 32:17).

• The cross and empty tomb reveal this power most clearly: “the immeasurable greatness of His power toward us… when He raised Christ from the dead” (Ephesians 1:19–20).

• Such might guarantees His promises; when He says He will “heal the brokenhearted” (Psalm 147:3), He possesses the strength to do exactly that.

His understanding has no limit

Unlike human insight, God’s knowledge is infinite and flawless.

• Isaiah echoes, “His understanding is unfathomable” (Isaiah 40:28).

• He knows every thought before it forms (Psalm 139:1–4) and perceives the end from the beginning (Isaiah 46:10).

• Romans 11:33 exclaims, “Oh, the depth of the riches of the wisdom and knowledge of God!”—a truth that brings comfort when life confounds us.

• Because His understanding is limitless, His plans are never frustrated (Proverbs 19:21), and His guidance for us is always perfect (James 1:5).

summary

Psalm 147:5 pulls back the curtain on God’s character: unrivaled greatness, unstoppable power, and infinite understanding. Together these truths assure us that the Lord who calls the stars by name also upholds, rescues, and guides His people with perfect wisdom and boundless strength.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(5) Of great power.--Literally, abounding in power.

Infinite.--Literally, without number. (See Note, Psalm 145:3, and Isaiah 40:28; that prophetic passage being still in the poet's mind, though the expression is changed.)

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 5. - Great is our Lord, and of great power; or, "mighty in strength" (comp. Nahum 1:3). His understanding is infinite. He is at once omnipotent and omniscient.
