Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Let me live to praise You;
This phrase expresses a deep desire for life with the purpose of glorifying God. The psalmist's plea to "live" is not merely about physical existence but about living a life that honors and exalts God. This reflects the biblical theme that true life is found in relationship with God (John 10:10). The act of praising God is central to the life of a believer, as seen throughout the Psalms (Psalm 150:6). The psalmist's request aligns with the biblical understanding that our ultimate purpose is to glorify God (Isaiah 43:7). This desire to live for praise can be seen as a type of Christ, who lived a life fully devoted to glorifying the Father (John 17:4).

may Your judgments sustain me.
The term "judgments" refers to God's decrees, laws, and decisions. In the context of the Old Testament, this would include the Torah, which provided guidance and structure for the Israelites' lives. The psalmist seeks sustenance not from material means but from God's righteous judgments, indicating a reliance on divine wisdom and justice (Psalm 19:9). This reflects a trust in God's sovereignty and His ability to uphold and support His people through His word. The sustaining power of God's judgments can be connected to the New Testament, where Jesus is described as the Word made flesh (John 1:14), who provides spiritual nourishment and sustenance to believers (John 6:35).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Psalmist
The author of Psalm 119, traditionally attributed to King David, who expresses a deep longing for God's word and guidance.

2. God
The central figure whom the psalmist seeks to praise and whose judgments are desired for sustenance.

3. Judgments
Refers to God's decrees, laws, and decisions, which the psalmist seeks for guidance and support.
Teaching Points
Living with Purpose
The psalmist's request to live in order to praise God challenges us to evaluate our life's purpose. Are we living to glorify God in all we do?

Dependence on God's Word
Just as the psalmist seeks sustenance from God's judgments, we should rely on Scripture for guidance and strength in our daily lives.

Praise as a Lifestyle
The desire to praise God continually suggests that worship should be an integral part of our lives, not just reserved for specific times or places.

Sustained by Divine Wisdom
The psalmist's reliance on God's judgments highlights the importance of seeking divine wisdom in decision-making and life's challenges.

Eternal Perspective
The psalmist's focus on living to praise God encourages us to maintain an eternal perspective, prioritizing spiritual growth and God's glory over temporal concerns.

What Does Psalm 119:175 Mean
Let me live to praise You

The psalmist asks for life with a single purpose—praise. He is not bargaining for comfort or longevity for its own sake; he longs for breath so that every breath can exalt the LORD. Psalm 115:17-18 reminds us that “It is not the dead who praise the LORD… But we will bless the LORD, both now and forevermore.” Scripture repeatedly ties life and praise together:

• Life is God-given (Psalm 31:15, “My times are in Your hands”).

• Our created purpose is worship (Psalm 150:6, “Let everything that has breath praise the LORD”).

• Even in hardship praise remains the goal (Philippians 1:20-21, Paul’s “to live is Christ”).

By praying “Let me live,” the writer confesses that ongoing existence itself depends on God’s sustaining mercy. By adding “to praise You,” he declares that every heartbeat should echo Psalm 34:1, “I will bless the LORD at all times.”

may Your judgments sustain me

Here the psalmist identifies the means by which life is preserved: God’s judgments—His righteous decisions, commands, and decrees. Psalm 19:9 calls those judgments “true… altogether righteous,” and Psalm 119:93 testifies, “I will never forget Your precepts, for by them You have given me life.”

How do His judgments sustain?

• They feed the soul (Matthew 4:4, “Man shall not live on bread alone, but on every word that comes from the mouth of God”).

• They steady the heart (Psalm 119:116, “Sustain me as You promised, and I will live”).

• They correct and guard us from ruin (2 Timothy 3:16, God-breathed Scripture “is useful for teaching, rebuking, correcting, and training in righteousness”).

The psalmist trusts that obedience to God’s revealed will is not a burden but a lifeline. Divine judgments are literally life-support, maintaining spiritual vitality as surely as God maintains physical breath.

summary

Psalm 119:175 pairs two inseparable realities: life and the Word. The writer seeks continued existence not for self-indulgence but for nonstop praise, and he knows that such life is upheld only by God’s righteous judgments. Living to glorify God and leaning on God’s Word form a single prayer—one that every believer can echo with confidence that “He who promised is faithful” (Hebrews 10:23).

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 175. - Let my soul live, and it shall praise thee; i.e. "quicken me; give me true life in my soul and spirit - my real self - and then my praise shall be ever of thee." And let thy judgments help me. Let the course of thy providence be such as to help me to praise thee.
