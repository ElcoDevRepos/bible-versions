I recounted my ways
The psalmist begins by reflecting on his life and actions, suggesting a moment of introspection and confession. This act of recounting is akin to a personal inventory, where one acknowledges their past deeds and thoughts. In biblical context, confession is a key aspect of repentance, as seen in 1 John 1:9, where believers are encouraged to confess their sins. The act of recounting one's ways is also reminiscent of the Israelites' practice of remembering their history and God's faithfulness, as seen throughout the Old Testament.

and You answered me
This phrase highlights the responsiveness of God to the psalmist's confession and prayer. It underscores the personal relationship between the believer and God, where communication is two-way. The assurance of God's answer is a recurring theme in the Psalms, such as in Psalm 34:4, where the psalmist declares that God answered him and delivered him from all his fears. This reflects the covenant relationship God has with His people, promising to hear and respond to their cries.

teach me Your statutes
The psalmist expresses a desire for divine instruction, seeking to understand and live by God's laws. This request for teaching indicates humility and a willingness to be guided by God's wisdom. The statutes refer to God's decrees and commandments, which are seen as a source of life and guidance, as emphasized in Psalm 19:7-11. The desire to learn God's statutes is a theme that runs throughout Psalm 119, highlighting the importance of God's Word in the believer's life. This request also points to the role of the Holy Spirit, who teaches and reminds believers of God's truth, as promised in John 14:26.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Psalmist
The author of Psalm 119, traditionally believed to be King David, who is expressing his devotion and desire for God's guidance.

2. God
The divine listener and responder to the Psalmist's recounting of his ways, who is asked to teach His statutes.

3. The Ways
The life paths, actions, or decisions of the Psalmist that he recounts before God.

4. Statutes
The laws, decrees, or teachings of God that the Psalmist desires to learn and follow.
Teaching Points
Confession and Communication with God
The Psalmist's recounting of his ways is an act of confession and open communication with God. Believers are encouraged to bring their lives before God honestly.

God's Response to Our Prayers
The verse assures us that God listens and responds to our prayers. This encourages believers to have faith in God's attentiveness and willingness to guide.

Desire for Divine Instruction
The Psalmist's request for God to teach him His statutes reflects a deep desire for spiritual growth and understanding. Believers should cultivate a similar hunger for God's Word.

The Role of God's Statutes
God's statutes are not just rules but are life-giving instructions that lead to a fulfilling and righteous life. Believers should view them as a source of wisdom and guidance.

Reflecting on Our Ways
Regular self-examination and reflection on our life paths are crucial for spiritual growth. Believers should make it a practice to recount their ways before God.

What Does Psalm 119:26 Mean
I recounted my ways

• The psalmist begins with honest self-disclosure: “I recounted my ways”.

– This is more than casual reflection; it’s a full, candid laying out of one’s life before God, like Psalm 32:5 where David says, “Then I acknowledged my sin to You and did not hide my iniquity.”

– Such recounting includes victories and failures, echoing 1 John 1:9 where confessing sins leads to forgiveness and cleansing.

– By telling God everything, the writer models a life open to divine scrutiny—Psalm 139:23-24 asks, “Search me, O God…see if there is any offensive way in me.”

• Practical takeaway: We’re invited to keep nothing hidden. Confession is not informing God but aligning ourselves with His truth.

and You answered me

• God’s response is immediate and personal: “and You answered me.”

– Psalm 34:4 illustrates this pattern: “I sought the LORD, and He answered me; He delivered me from all my fears.”

– Jeremiah 33:3 promises, “Call to Me and I will answer you and show you great and unsearchable things.”

• The answer may come as:

– Assurance of forgiveness (Isaiah 1:18).

– Fresh direction (Proverbs 3:5-6).

– Peace that guards heart and mind (Philippians 4:6-7).

• Key idea: God is not distant. Confession opens the channel; God’s reply follows.

teach me Your statutes

• Having experienced God’s response, the psalmist hungers for instruction: “teach me Your statutes.”

– Psalm 25:4-5 echoes this plea: “Show me Your ways, O LORD, teach me Your paths.”

– It’s a shift from simply being heard to being shaped. Forgiven people become teachable disciples.

• Statutes are fixed truths, not suggestions (Psalm 19:7-8). Seeking them shows commitment to obedience, aligning life with God’s unchanging Word (2 Timothy 3:16-17).

• Steps for embracing divine teaching:

– Regular Scripture intake (Joshua 1:8).

– Willingness to act on what’s learned (James 1:22-25).

– Dependence on the Spirit’s illumination (John 14:26).

summary

Psalm 119:26 traces a progression: honest confession, a gracious divine answer, and a longing to be schooled in God’s unchanging statutes. The verse assures us that when we lay our lives bare before the Lord, He responds, forgives, and then guides us into deeper obedience so that our ways increasingly mirror His ways.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(26) I have declared.--Or, recounted.

My ways.--Or, as we should say, my courses, my past life, including, as the context shows, confession of sins and prayer for pardon.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 26. - I have declared thy ways, and thou heardest me; i.e. "I have opened my griefs to thee, O God, and poured forth my heart before thee. I am confident that thou didst listen to me." Teach me thy statutes. That I may show my gratitude by keeping them (comp. ver. 33).
