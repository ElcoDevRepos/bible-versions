Turn my heart to Your testimonies
This phrase is a plea for divine intervention in aligning one's desires with God's Word. The heart, in biblical terms, often represents the center of one's will and emotions. The psalmist seeks a transformation that only God can provide, indicating a dependence on divine grace. The "testimonies" refer to God's laws and decrees, which are seen as a reflection of His character and will. This request echoes the Shema in Deuteronomy 6:4-9, where the Israelites are commanded to love the Lord with all their heart, soul, and strength, and to keep His commandments close. The desire to turn one's heart to God's testimonies is a call to internalize and live out the truths of Scripture, much like the New Covenant promise in Jeremiah 31:33, where God writes His law on the hearts of His people.

and not to covetous gain
This part of the verse contrasts the desire for God's Word with the temptation of materialism and greed. Covetousness is condemned throughout Scripture, as seen in the Ten Commandments (Exodus 20:17) and in the teachings of Jesus, who warned against storing up treasures on earth (Matthew 6:19-21). The psalmist's prayer reflects an understanding that the pursuit of wealth can lead one away from God, as illustrated in the parable of the rich fool (Luke 12:16-21). The request to avoid covetous gain aligns with the wisdom literature, such as Proverbs 30:8-9, which asks for neither poverty nor riches but daily sustenance. This phrase underscores the biblical principle that true contentment and fulfillment are found in God alone, not in material possessions.

Teaching Points
Heart Orientation
The psalmist's prayer emphasizes the importance of having a heart oriented towards God's Word rather than worldly desires. Believers should regularly examine their hearts to ensure they are aligned with God's will.

Desire for God's Word
Cultivating a love for God's testimonies requires intentional effort and prayer. Engaging with Scripture daily can help believers grow in their understanding and love for God's commands.

Avoiding Materialism
The contrast between God's testimonies and covetous gain serves as a reminder to prioritize spiritual wealth over material possessions. Christians are called to live counter-culturally, valuing eternal treasures over temporary gains.

Prayer for Transformation
Like the psalmist, believers should actively seek God's help in transforming their hearts and minds. Prayer is a powerful tool for inviting God's work in our lives.

What Does Psalm 119:36 Mean
Turn my heart

The psalmist begins with an honest plea: “Turn my heart.” He recognizes that real change starts inside, and only the Lord can redirect a heart that naturally drifts.

• Psalm 51:10 echoes this same dependence: “Create in me a clean heart, O God.”

• Ezekiel 36:26 promises God’s enabling: “I will give you a new heart and put a new spirit within you.”

• Philippians 2:13 affirms the divine initiative even under the new covenant: “For it is God who works in you to will and to act on behalf of His good purpose.”

To Your testimonies

The request has a clear goal—alignment with God’s written Word. “Testimonies” speaks of every declaration God has made, calling for more than casual reading; it invites delight and obedience.

• Psalm 19:7: “The testimony of the LORD is trustworthy, making wise the simple,” underscoring Scripture’s reliability.

• Joshua 1:8 illustrates the blessing of a heart fixed on the Word: “This Book of the Law shall not depart from your mouth; meditate on it day and night… then you will prosper.”

• James 1:22 urges active response: “Be doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving yourselves.”

And not

The phrase sets up a decisive contrast. Biblical devotion is exclusive; a heart cannot chase conflicting loves (Matthew 6:24).

• Romans 12:2 directs believers to “be transformed by the renewing of your mind” and refuse conformity to the age.

• Psalm 1:1–2 pictures the blessed man who avoids ungodly influences while delighting in God’s law.

• Galatians 5:16–17 shows the Spirit-flesh conflict, reminding us that we choose daily which desire gains traction.

To covetous gain

Here the psalmist identifies the rival attraction: greedy profit. Scripture often pairs love for wealth with spiritual peril.

• 1 Timothy 6:10: “For the love of money is the root of all kinds of evil.”

• Proverbs 28:22 warns, “A stingy man hastens after wealth and does not know that poverty will come upon him.”

• Luke 12:15 cautions, “Watch out and guard yourselves against every form of greed, for one’s life does not consist in the abundance of his possessions.”

The heart that longs for God’s testimonies cannot simultaneously crave dishonest gain; the two appetites are mutually exclusive.

summary

Psalm 119:36 is a concise, two-part prayer: “Lord, bend my inner desires toward Your reliable Word and away from the lure of selfish profit.” It reminds us that true obedience flows from a divinely redirected heart, saturated with Scripture, and guarded against the consuming pull of greed.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(36) Covetousness.--Literally, rapine, prey. In Psalm 30:9 simply, "gain."

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 36. - Incline my heart unto thy testimonies. The writer recognizes that the right inclination of the heart, which he claims to have (vers. 7, 10, 32, 34, etc.), is itself the gift of God. And not to covetousness; or, "to gain" (comp. ver. 72).
