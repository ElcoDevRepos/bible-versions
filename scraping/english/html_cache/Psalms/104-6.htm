You covered it with the deep like a garment;
This phrase refers to the initial covering of the earth with water, reminiscent of the creation narrative in Genesis 1:2, where the Spirit of God hovers over the waters. The "deep" signifies the primordial waters, a common motif in ancient Near Eastern creation myths, symbolizing chaos before divine order. The imagery of a garment suggests God's sovereign control and intentional design, as garments are crafted and fitted. This covering can also be seen as a type of baptism, symbolizing cleansing and renewal, prefiguring the waters of baptism in the New Testament.

the waters stood above the mountains.
This part of the verse evokes the imagery of the Flood narrative in Genesis 7:19-20, where the waters rose and covered the highest mountains. It underscores God's power over creation, as even the mighty mountains are submerged under His command. Theologically, it reflects God's judgment and mercy, as the Flood was both a means of purging wickedness and preserving righteousness through Noah. The waters standing above the mountains can also be seen as a type of Christ's burial and resurrection, where the old is submerged and the new emerges, pointing to the transformative power of God's salvation plan.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Deep
Refers to the primordial waters, often associated with chaos in ancient Near Eastern cosmology. In the biblical context, it signifies the vast, unformed waters present at creation.

2. Mountains
Symbolize stability and permanence in the biblical account. Here, they are covered by the waters, indicating the overwhelming power and presence of God's creation.

3. Creation
The event of God forming the earth and its features, including the separation of land and water, as described in Genesis.
Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty Over Creation
The imagery of waters covering the mountains highlights God's ultimate authority over creation. He commands the elements and sets their boundaries.

The Power of God's Word
Just as God spoke creation into existence, His Word continues to hold power and authority in our lives. We are called to trust in His Word and its promises.

The Symbolism of Water
Water in the Bible often symbolizes both life and judgment. It is a reminder of God's provision and His righteous judgment, as seen in the flood account.

Creation as a Reflection of God's Glory
The natural world, with its vast oceans and towering mountains, reflects the majesty and glory of God. We are encouraged to worship Him as the Creator.

Trust in God's Control
In times of chaos or uncertainty, we can find peace in knowing that God is in control, just as He was at the beginning of creation.

What Does Psalm 104:6 Mean
You covered it

Psalm 104:6 begins, “You covered it,” pointing back to verse 5 where God “set the earth on its foundations.”

• The focus is God as the active subject—He alone shrouds His creation (Job 38:8–9; Isaiah 40:22).

• The verse underscores divine initiative; the earth’s stability is entirely in His hands (Colossians 1:16–17).

With the deep

The “deep” refers to the primeval waters God gathered on Day 3 of creation (Genesis 1:9–10).

• Scripture often uses “the deep” to describe vast, untamed waters emphasizing God’s mastery (Psalm 33:7; Proverbs 8:27–29).

• These waters once engulfed the earth, a reminder that creation originally emerged out of watery chaos wholly subject to God.

Like a garment

Covering the earth “like a garment” pictures an all-enveloping cloak.

• Garments protect and conceal; similarly, the waters completely wrapped the planet (Habakkuk 3:10).

• The metaphor highlights both magnitude and intimacy—God clothes His world, showing care and control (Matthew 6:30).

The waters stood

“They stood” speaks of a deliberate, sustained condition, not a momentary splash.

• This steady posture echoes the flood account when waters “prevailed” (Genesis 7:17–20).

• God’s sovereignty keeps even chaotic waters in place until He commands otherwise (Psalm 93:3–4).

Above the mountains

The phrase stresses depth: the highest peaks lay submerged.

• Genesis 7:19 confirms “all the high mountains…were covered,” linking Psalm 104:6 to the global Flood.

• Such imagery magnifies God’s power—He elevates or restrains waters at will (Psalm 46:2–3; Nahum 1:5).

summary

Psalm 104:6 recalls a time when God wrapped the whole earth in deep waters, likely alluding to the Flood, demonstrating His absolute rule over creation. Every clause underscores that the same Lord who founded the earth can also drape it in oceans and lift those waters above mountains. The verse invites confidence that the God who once controlled the deep continues to sustain and govern all things today.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(6) The deep.--The water-world is first considered as a vast garment wrapped round the earth, so that the mountain-tops are covered. But here it is beyond its right, and the Divine rebuke forces it to retire within narrower limits. It is noticeable that the idea of a chaos finds no place in the poetic conception of the world's genesis. The primitive world is not formless, but has its mountains and valleys already existing, though merged beneath the sea.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 6. - Thou coveredst it with the deep, as with a garment (see Genesis 1:9). A watery covering was spread at first over the whole earth, and enveloped it like a garment. The waters stood above the mountains. The highest inequalities of the land were concealed under the watery integument.
