Give thanks to the LORD
This phrase is a call to worship and gratitude, emphasizing the importance of acknowledging God's sovereignty and benevolence. In the Hebrew tradition, giving thanks was an integral part of worship, often expressed through psalms and hymns. The act of thanksgiving is not just a response to God's blessings but a recognition of His character and deeds. This call to give thanks is echoed throughout the Psalms (e.g., Psalm 100:4) and is a central theme in the life of Israel, especially during festivals like Passover and the Feast of Tabernacles.

for He is good
The goodness of the LORD is a foundational attribute of God in the Hebrew Scriptures. This goodness is not merely moral perfection but encompasses His kindness, mercy, and provision for His people. The declaration of God's goodness is a recurring theme in the Psalms (e.g., Psalm 34:8) and serves as a reminder of His faithfulness and trustworthiness. In the context of Israel's history, God's goodness is seen in His deliverance from Egypt, provision in the wilderness, and the establishment of the covenant.

His loving devotion endures forever
The phrase "loving devotion" translates the Hebrew word "chesed," which conveys God's steadfast love, mercy, and covenant faithfulness. This enduring love is a central theme in the Old Testament, reflecting God's unchanging commitment to His people despite their unfaithfulness. The phrase "endures forever" underscores the eternal nature of God's love, a concept that is celebrated in the refrain of Psalm 136. This enduring love is ultimately fulfilled in the New Testament through Jesus Christ, whose sacrificial love provides eternal redemption for humanity.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal existence and faithfulness to His promises.

2. The Psalmist
The author of Psalm 107, traditionally attributed to King David, who calls the people to worship and gratitude.

3. Israelites
The original audience of the Psalm, who experienced God's deliverance and are called to remember His goodness.

4. The Exodus
A historical event where God delivered the Israelites from slavery in Egypt, often recalled as a demonstration of His enduring love.

5. The Promised Land
The land of Canaan, a symbol of God's faithfulness and the fulfillment of His promises to His people.
Teaching Points
The Call to Gratitude
Recognize that gratitude is a fundamental response to God's character and actions. It is not dependent on circumstances but on the unchanging nature of God.

Understanding God's Goodness
God's goodness is intrinsic to His nature. Reflect on how His goodness has been evident in your life and the lives of others.

Enduring Love
God's loving devotion (Hebrew: "chesed") is steadfast and eternal. Consider how this love has been demonstrated throughout biblical history and in your personal experiences.

Worship as a Response
Worship is a natural response to recognizing God's goodness and love. Engage in regular worship, both personally and corporately, as an expression of gratitude.

Living Out Thanksgiving
Let gratitude shape your daily interactions and decisions. Consider how you can express thankfulness to God and others in practical ways.

What Does Psalm 107:1 Mean
Give thanks to the LORD

– The verse opens with an action: “Give thanks.” Gratitude is not optional for God’s people; it is commanded.

• Psalm 100:4 reminds us to “Enter His gates with thanksgiving and His courts with praise.”

• In 1 Thessalonians 5:18 we are told, “Give thanks in every circumstance, for this is God’s will for you in Christ Jesus.”

• Thanksgiving directs our focus away from self and circumstances and back to the Sovereign Lord, acknowledging His hand in all things.

for He is good

– The motivation for thanksgiving follows immediately: God’s inherent goodness.

• Psalm 34:8 invites, “Taste and see that the LORD is good; blessed is the man who takes refuge in Him.”

• Nahum 1:7 affirms, “The LORD is good, a stronghold in the day of distress; He cares for those who trust in Him.”

• James 1:17 echoes this truth: “Every good and perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of the heavenly lights.”

– God’s goodness is not dependent on our situation; it is an unchanging attribute that undergirds all His actions toward His people.

His loving devotion endures forever

– The verse concludes with the reason we can trust His goodness: His “loving devotion” is eternal.

• Lamentations 3:22–23 declares, “Because of the LORD’s loving devotion we are not consumed, for His compassions never fail. They are new every morning.”

• Psalm 103:17 comforts, “But from everlasting to everlasting the loving devotion of the LORD is with those who fear Him.”

• Exodus 34:6 records God’s own proclamation: “The LORD, the LORD God, compassionate and gracious, slow to anger, abounding in loving devotion and faithfulness.”

– “Endures forever” means His covenant love has no expiration date. Circumstances may shift, but His commitment to His people remains constant.

summary

Psalm 107:1 calls us to active, continual gratitude rooted in two unshakable truths: the LORD is good, and His loving devotion never ends. When we remember these realities, thanksgiving becomes our natural response, and confidence in His faithfulness becomes our daily strength.
