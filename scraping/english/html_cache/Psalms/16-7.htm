I will bless the LORD who counsels me;
This phrase emphasizes the psalmist's intention to praise and thank God for His guidance. The act of blessing the LORD is a form of worship and acknowledgment of His sovereignty and wisdom. In the biblical context, seeking God's counsel was crucial for making decisions, as seen in the lives of David and other leaders (1 Samuel 23:2, 2 Samuel 5:19). The LORD's counsel is often associated with His Word, which provides wisdom and direction (Psalm 119:105). This phrase also reflects a personal relationship with God, where the psalmist recognizes God's active role in his life, similar to how Jesus sought the Father's guidance during His earthly ministry (John 5:19-20).

even at night my conscience instructs me.
The mention of "night" suggests a time of reflection and meditation, when distractions are minimal, and one can focus on spiritual matters. In ancient Israel, night was often a time for prayer and contemplation (Psalm 63:6). The "conscience" here can be understood as the inner voice or spirit that aligns with God's will, indicating that the psalmist is attuned to divine instruction even during rest. This aligns with the biblical theme of God speaking to His people in dreams or through the inner prompting of the Holy Spirit (Job 33:14-16, Acts 2:17). The phrase underscores the continuous nature of God's guidance, available at all times, and foreshadows the New Testament teaching of the Holy Spirit as the Counselor who instructs believers (John 14:26).

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of Psalm 16, David is expressing his trust and reliance on God. He is known as a man after God's own heart and often sought God's guidance in his life.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal presence and faithfulness. David blesses the LORD for His guidance and counsel.

3. Night
Symbolic of times of rest, reflection, or even distress. It is during these times that David finds instruction from God, indicating a deep, personal relationship.
Teaching Points
God as Our Counselor
God provides guidance and wisdom to those who seek Him. Just as David blesses the LORD for His counsel, we too should seek God's guidance in our decisions and daily lives.

The Role of Conscience
The Hebrew word for "conscience" can also be understood as "inner self" or "heart." This suggests that God communicates His wisdom to us internally, often through our conscience, which aligns with His Spirit.

Nighttime Reflection
Nighttime can be a powerful time for reflection and receiving God's instruction. We should cultivate a habit of meditating on God's word and listening for His voice, even in the quiet moments of the night.

Blessing God for His Guidance
Like David, we should actively bless and thank God for His guidance and instruction in our lives. Recognizing His hand in our decisions fosters a heart of gratitude and dependence on Him.

What Does Psalm 16:7 Mean
I will bless the LORD

– David begins with an intentional act of praise.

• “I will bless” shows a conscious decision, not a passing feeling (cf. Psalm 34:1, “I will bless the LORD at all times; His praise will always be on my lips,”).

• The focus is Yahweh, the covenant-keeping God whose character merits continual adoration (cf. Revelation 4:11).

• Praise sets the tone for trust; acknowledging who God is prepares the heart to receive His guidance (cf. 1 Thessalonians 5:16-18).

who counsels me

– The LORD is portrayed as a personal advisor.

• Psalm 32:8 promises, “I will instruct you and teach you the way you should go,”.

• Proverbs 3:5-6 calls the believer to trust Him for straight paths.

• Isaiah 9:6 names Messiah “Wonderful Counselor,” fulfilled in Christ (cf. John 14:26 where the Spirit “will teach you all things,”).

• God’s counsel is reliable, authoritative, and rooted in His unchanging Word (Psalm 119:24).

even at night

– His guidance is constant, not limited to daylight or public worship.

• Psalm 42:8 notes, “the LORD will send His loving devotion by day, and His song will be with me in the night,”.

• Night often symbolizes isolation or trial; God’s presence pierces both (cf. Acts 16:25, Paul and Silas singing hymns at midnight).

• Resting believers can expect His peace to guard them (Philippians 4:7).

my conscience instructs me

– The inner man, shaped by God’s truth, becomes an instrument of His counsel.

• Romans 8:16 affirms, “The Spirit Himself testifies with our spirit,”.

• Hebrews 8:10 speaks of God’s laws written on hearts.

• An instructed conscience warns, corrects, and directs (cf. Psalm 119:11, “I have hidden Your word in my heart that I might not sin against You,”).

• This inner instruction is not independent of Scripture but harmonizes with it (John 17:17).

summary

Psalm 16:7 celebrates an unbroken fellowship with the LORD: deliberate praise, reliable divine counsel, round-the-clock care, and an enlightened conscience working together. David models a life anchored in God’s Word and presence, confident that the Lord’s guidance is constant and personal for every believer who trusts Him.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(7) Given me counsel . . .--i.e., led me to a right and happy choice of the way of life.

My reins--i.e., my heart.

Instruct me.--Better, warn me. Conscience echoes the voice of God. The Hebrew word, from a root meaning bind, includes the sense of obligation. Once heard, the Divine monition becomes a law to the good man, and his own heart warns him of the slightest danger of deviation from it.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 7. - I will bless the Lord, who hath given his counsel. God has become David's "Counsellor" (see Psalm 32:8), makes suggestions to him which he follows, and so guides his life that he feels bound to praise and bless him for it. My reins also instruct me in the night seasons. The reins, according to Hebrew ideas, are the seat of feeling and emotion. David is "instructed" or "stimulated" (Hengstenberg) to bless God by the feelings which stir within him as he lies awake at night - feelings, we must suppose, of affection and gratitude.
