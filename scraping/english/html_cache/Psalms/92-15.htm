Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
to proclaim
This phrase emphasizes the act of declaring or announcing something publicly. In biblical times, proclamation was often done by prophets, priests, or kings to convey important messages from God. The act of proclaiming is a form of worship and testimony, reflecting the believer's duty to share God's truth with others. It aligns with the Great Commission in Matthew 28:19-20, where believers are called to spread the gospel.

“The LORD is upright;
This statement affirms the moral integrity and righteousness of God. The term "upright" suggests that God is just, fair, and consistent in His character and actions. This is a recurring theme throughout the Psalms and the entire Bible, as seen in Deuteronomy 32:4, which describes God as a "God of truth and without iniquity, just and right is He." It reassures believers of God's unchanging nature and His commitment to justice.

He is my Rock,
The metaphor of God as a "Rock" signifies strength, stability, and protection. In the ancient Near East, rocks and mountains were often seen as symbols of refuge and safety. This imagery is prevalent in the Psalms, such as Psalm 18:2, where God is described as a "rock, fortress, and deliverer." It highlights God's reliability and the security He provides to those who trust in Him.

and in Him there is no unrighteousness.”
This phrase underscores the absolute purity and holiness of God. Unlike humans, who are prone to sin and moral failure, God is entirely free from unrighteousness. This concept is echoed in 1 John 1:5, which states that "God is light, and in Him is no darkness at all." It assures believers that God's judgments and actions are always just and perfect, providing a foundation for trust and faith.

Teaching Points
God's Uprightness
God's nature is inherently righteous and just. Believers can trust in His moral perfection and fairness in all circumstances.

The Rock of Stability
In a world of uncertainty, God is the unchanging Rock. Believers can find security and refuge in His steadfastness.

Living in Righteousness
As God is righteous, believers are called to reflect His character in their lives, pursuing holiness and integrity.

Trust in God's Justice
Even when faced with injustice, believers can rest in the assurance that God will ultimately bring about justice and righteousness.

Worship and Declaration
Worship is not just personal but a public declaration of God's attributes. Believers are encouraged to proclaim God's righteousness and faithfulness to others.

What Does Psalm 92:15 Mean
The LORD is upright

Psalm 92:15 opens by celebrating that “The LORD is upright.” This isn’t merely an abstract statement; it is a concrete affirmation of God’s flawless moral character. Throughout Scripture the same truth is echoed:

• Deuteronomy 32:4 calls Him “the Rock; His work is perfect, for all His ways are just.”

• Psalm 25:8 reminds us, “Good and upright is the LORD; therefore He shows sinners the way.”

• Exodus 34:6 – 7 portrays Him as “abounding in loving devotion and truth” while maintaining perfect justice.

Because He is upright:

– His judgments are never mistaken.

– His promises are never broken.

– His ways are straight when the world’s ways twist.

When believers rehearse this fact, confidence grows; we can align our lives to His unerring standard, knowing that He is always right and never capricious.

He is my Rock

Calling God “my Rock” moves from the objective (who God is) to the personal (who He is for me). A rock is stable, immovable, and protective:

• David sang, “The LORD is my Rock, my fortress, and my deliverer” (2 Samuel 22:2–3).

• Psalm 18:2 repeats, “My God is my Rock, in whom I take refuge.”

• Isaiah 26:4 urges, “Trust in the LORD forever, for the LORD God is an everlasting Rock.”

Key implications:

– Security: A rock withstands storms; so does God shield His people.

– Permanence: Trends change, cultures shift, but the Rock remains (Malachi 3:6).

– Identity: Saying “my” Rock testifies to a personal, covenant bond, not a distant concept.

Standing on this Rock means refusing to build on the sand of human opinion (Matthew 7:24–25). It invites calm assurance even when circumstances shake.

In Him there is no unrighteousness

The verse concludes by clearing away any doubt: God contains zero unrighteousness. Light mingles with no darkness (1 John 1:5). He is “of purer eyes than to behold evil” (Habakkuk 1:13) and remains “the Father of lights, with whom there is no variation or shadow of turning” (James 1:17).

Consider the comfort this provides:

– His guidance is never tinged with hidden motives.

– His discipline is always fair, never cruel.

– His salvation is entirely trustworthy; if no unrighteousness resides in Him, then none will surface later to invalidate His promises.

Believers can therefore rest, worship, and obey without fear of divine inconsistency.

summary

Psalm 92:15 invites us to proclaim three linked truths: the LORD’s character is perfectly upright, His relationship to us is that of a steadfast Rock, and His nature is so pure that no unrighteousness can be found in Him. Together these truths inspire worship, foster unwavering trust, and call us to reflect His integrity in daily life.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 15. - To show that the Lord is upright. The happy and flourishing old age of the righteous (ver. 14; comp. Psalm 91:16) is a strong indication of God's faithfulness and truth, showing, as its does, that he keeps his promises, and never forsakes those that put their trust in him (comp. Psalm 27:10; Psalm 37:25; Isaiah 41:17, etc.). He is my Rock - rather, that he is my Rock - and that there is no unrighteousness in him. Both clauses depend on the "show" of the preceding hemistich.
