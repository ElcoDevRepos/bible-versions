I will set his hand over the sea
This phrase signifies dominion and authority. In biblical times, the sea often represented chaos and the unknown. By setting his hand over the sea, it implies control over these elements. This can be seen as a reference to the Davidic covenant, where God promises to establish David's lineage and kingdom. The sea could also symbolize the Gentile nations, indicating a future reign over all peoples. This foreshadows the messianic rule of Jesus Christ, who calms the stormy seas in the Gospels, demonstrating His authority over creation (Mark 4:39).

and his right hand upon the rivers
The right hand is traditionally a symbol of power and strength. Placing it upon the rivers suggests control over the life-giving and sustaining forces of the earth. Rivers were crucial in ancient times for agriculture, trade, and sustenance. This imagery can be linked to the promise of a prosperous and expansive kingdom under David's line. It also connects to the prophecy of the Messiah's reign, where Jesus is seen as the source of living water (John 4:14), providing spiritual sustenance and life. The rivers may also represent boundaries of the promised land, indicating the extent of the kingdom's reach.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Ethan the Ezrahite
The author of Psalm 89, who is traditionally understood to be a wise man during the time of King Solomon. He is credited with writing this psalm, which reflects on God's covenant with David.

2. David
The psalm is a reflection on God's covenant with David, promising him an enduring dynasty. David is a central figure in the Old Testament, known for his kingship and his heart for God.

3. The Sea and Rivers
Symbolic elements in this verse representing vast dominion and control. In the ancient Near Eastern context, the sea often symbolized chaos, and rivers were sources of life and prosperity.

4. God's Covenant
The overarching theme of Psalm 89 is God's covenant with David, promising that his descendants would rule forever. This covenant is a key element in understanding the messianic hope in the Old Testament.

5. Messianic Fulfillment
The ultimate fulfillment of God's promise to David is seen in Jesus Christ, who is often referred to as the Son of David in the New Testament.
Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty
God's promise to set David's hand over the sea and rivers signifies His ultimate control over creation. Believers can trust in God's sovereignty over the chaos and challenges in their lives.

Covenant Faithfulness
Just as God was faithful to His covenant with David, He remains faithful to His promises to us. This encourages believers to rely on God's unchanging nature.

Messianic Hope
The promise of dominion is ultimately fulfilled in Jesus Christ. Believers are called to recognize Jesus as the King who reigns over all and to live under His lordship.

Spiritual Authority
As followers of Christ, we are given authority to overcome spiritual challenges. This verse reminds us of the power we have in Christ to influence the world for His kingdom.

Trust in God's Plan
Even when circumstances seem chaotic, believers can trust that God is working out His plan, just as He did through David and ultimately through Christ.

What Does Psalm 89:25 Mean
I will set

• The promise comes straight from God, underscoring that the coming rule is His doing, not human achievement (Psalm 89:3-4).

• This personal pledge reflects the same covenant language God used with David in 2 Samuel 7:12-16 and again applied to Christ in Luke 1:32-33.

• God’s initiative guarantees the certainty of fulfillment; He alone “declares the end from the beginning” (Isaiah 46:9-10).

His hand over the sea

• “Hand” speaks of active control. By placing it “over the sea,” the Lord grants His chosen king universal authority—no boundary can hem him in (Psalm 72:8, Revelation 11:15).

• Jesus demonstrated this authority literally when He calmed the storm—“Silence! Be still!” (Mark 4:39)—and when He walked on the water (Matthew 14:25-27).

• The sea often symbolizes chaos and hostile nations; Christ’s reign subdues both outward turmoil and inner unrest (Isaiah 17:12-13, John 14:27).

His right hand upon the rivers

• The “right hand” implies strength, favor, and victorious power (Psalm 118:15-16).

• “Rivers” in Israel’s geography (like the Euphrates) mark far-flung borders; placing His right hand there pictures dominion reaching every frontier (Genesis 15:18, Psalm 72:8).

• Christ’s authority over rivers mirrors Old Testament miracles that foreshadow His sovereignty—Elijah striking the Jordan (2 Kings 2:8) and Israel crossing the Red Sea (Exodus 14:21-22).

• The Messiah not only masters waters; He also supplies living water to His people (John 7:37-38), proving He governs creation and meets spiritual need.

summary

Psalm 89:25 affirms that God Himself installs the Davidic King—ultimately Jesus—with complete, unhindered, and victorious authority. Whether over the restless sea or the distant rivers, nothing escapes His sovereign reach, assuring believers that the same powerful hand that rules the world also keeps and sustains them.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(25) In the sea.--A reference, as in Psalm 72:8; Psalm 80:11, to the limits of the Solomonic kingdom, the Mediterranean and the Euphrates. For the figure we may compare a saying attributed by Curtius to some Scythian ambassadors, who addressed Alexander in these terms: "If the gods had given thee a body as great as thy mind, the whole world would not be able to contain thee. Thou wouldst reach with one hand to the east, and with the other to the west."

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 25. - I will set his hand also in the sea, and his right hand in the rivers (comp. Psalm 72:8). "The sea" is probably the Mediterranean, and "the rivers" the Euphrates, with its canals and affluents (see 1 Kings 4:21, 24; Psalm 137:1). The promise of an extended dominion is implied in 2 Samuel 7:9.
