I rejoice in Your promise
The psalmist expresses a deep sense of joy and satisfaction in God's promises. This joy is not merely emotional but is rooted in the assurance and reliability of God's word. In the biblical context, God's promises often refer to His covenants with His people, such as the Abrahamic, Mosaic, and Davidic covenants. These promises are seen as unbreakable and are a source of hope and encouragement. The joy in God's promise can be compared to the joy of salvation, as seen in other scriptures like Isaiah 12:3, where believers draw water from the wells of salvation with joy. This phrase also reflects the psalmist's personal relationship with God, where His promises are not just abstract concepts but personal assurances.

like one who finds great spoil
The imagery of finding great spoil suggests a victorious outcome after a battle, where the spoils of war are collected. In ancient times, spoils were the goods and treasures taken from a defeated enemy, symbolizing triumph and reward. This metaphor indicates the immense value and satisfaction the psalmist finds in God's promises, equating them to the tangible and substantial rewards of victory. Historically, spoils were a significant part of warfare in the ancient Near East, often determining the wealth and power of a nation. Spiritually, this can be connected to the believer's victory over sin and the world through Christ, as seen in passages like Romans 8:37, where believers are described as "more than conquerors." The psalmist's comparison underscores the incomparable worth of divine promises, which provide spiritual riches and eternal security.

4. Spoil

Teaching Points
The Value of God's Promises
Just as spoil is a reward after a battle, God's promises are treasures that provide spiritual wealth and victory in life's challenges.

Joy in the Word
The psalmist's joy is not in material wealth but in the spiritual riches found in God's promises. Believers are encouraged to find their joy in Scripture.

Seeking and Finding
Like a treasure hunter, believers should diligently seek God's promises, knowing that they hold the key to true joy and fulfillment.

Faith in God's Faithfulness
Trusting in God's promises requires faith, as they are often fulfilled in His timing. This faith brings joy and peace, even in waiting.

Rejoicing in All Circumstances
The psalmist's example teaches us to rejoice in God's promises regardless of our current circumstances, trusting in His ultimate plan and provision.

What Does Psalm 119:162 Mean
I rejoice

Joy is not a shallow emotion but a deliberate celebration of who God is and what He has said. David’s exclamation mirrors Psalm 5:11, “But let all who take refuge in You rejoice; let them ever shout for joy.” Paul echoes the same heartbeat in Philippians 4:4, “Rejoice in the Lord always. I will say it again: Rejoice!” When God speaks, His people respond with praise—because His words are life, certainty, and hope (Psalm 119:111).

in Your promise

The focal point of the joy is the Lord’s promise—His revealed word. Psalm 119:140 says, “Your word is wholly pure; therefore Your servant loves it.” Every declaration God makes is utterly reliable; 2 Corinthians 1:20 affirms, “For all the promises of God are ‘Yes’ in Christ.” Because Scripture is true in every detail, the believer’s confidence rests on an unshakable foundation (Psalm 12:6).

like one who finds great spoil

Picture a soldier stumbling upon an overflowing treasure chest after victory: surprise, delight, abundance. That is the Psalmist’s reaction each time he opens God’s word. Proverbs 2:4–5 describes seeking wisdom “as for hidden treasure,” and Jesus likens the kingdom to “a treasure hidden in a field” (Matthew 13:44). The image underscores:

• Unexpected richness—truths that exceed expectations

• Lasting value—wealth that never diminishes (Isaiah 55:11)

• Personal ownership—the spoil becomes the finder’s possession, just as God’s promises belong to every believer (Colossians 3:16)

summary

Psalm 119:162 celebrates wholehearted, overflowing joy in the flawless promises of God. Each verse discovered is a victory prize, a treasure chest opened, a reason to rejoice anew. The believer who treasures Scripture this way lives in continual triumph, certain that every word from the Lord is priceless, proven, and personally theirs.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 162. - I rejoice at thy Word, as one that findeth great spoil. We must not limit this to the promises contained in God's Law. The psalmist views the precepts of the Law as a real treasure (vers. 14, 72, 127), since they make him wise unto salvation.
