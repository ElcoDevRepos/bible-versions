So David shepherded them with integrity of heart
David, the youngest son of Jesse, was chosen by God to be king over Israel, a role that began with his experience as a shepherd. This phrase highlights David's leadership qualities, emphasizing his integrity. Integrity of heart suggests a sincere and honest commitment to God and His people, reflecting the biblical ideal of a leader who is after God's own heart (1 Samuel 13:14). David's shepherding role is a type of Christ, the Good Shepherd (John 10:11), who leads His flock with purity and righteousness. The shepherd imagery is deeply rooted in the cultural and historical context of ancient Israel, where shepherding was a common occupation and a metaphor for leadership (Psalm 23).

and guided them with skillful hands
David's guidance of Israel is marked by his skillful leadership, which includes military prowess, administrative abilities, and spiritual guidance. The phrase "skillful hands" indicates not only physical ability but also wisdom and discernment in governance. David's reign brought unity and prosperity to Israel, establishing Jerusalem as the political and spiritual center. His leadership is a foreshadowing of Christ's perfect rule, as prophesied in Isaiah 9:6-7. The historical context of David's reign includes the consolidation of the tribes of Israel and the establishment of a centralized monarchy, which required both strategic and diplomatic skills. David's ability to lead effectively is seen as a gift from God, aligning with the biblical theme that true wisdom and skill come from the Lord (Proverbs 2:6).

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The second king of Israel, known for his heart after God and his leadership qualities. He is a central figure in the Old Testament, representing a model of godly leadership.

2. Israel
The nation that David shepherded, representing God's chosen people. Israel's history is marked by periods of faithfulness and rebellion, and David's reign is often seen as a high point in their history.

3. Shepherding
A metaphor for leadership used throughout the Bible. It implies care, guidance, and protection, reflecting how David led Israel.
Teaching Points
Integrity in Leadership
True leadership is rooted in integrity. David's leadership was effective because it was grounded in a sincere and upright heart. As Christians, we are called to lead with integrity in our families, workplaces, and communities.

Skillful Guidance
Leadership also requires skill. David's ability to guide Israel with "skillful hands" suggests that we should seek to develop our abilities and talents to serve others effectively.

The Heart of a Shepherd
The metaphor of shepherding reminds us of the importance of caring for those we lead. Like David, we should be attentive to the needs of others, providing guidance and protection.

God's Choice and Anointing
Just as God chose David for his heart, He looks at our hearts. We should strive to cultivate a heart that is pleasing to God, knowing that He equips those He calls.

Christ as the Ultimate Shepherd
David's role as a shepherd points to Jesus, the ultimate Shepherd. We can find comfort and guidance in Christ, who leads us with perfect integrity and skill.

What Does Psalm 78:72 Mean
So David

- The verse opens by naming David, reminding us that God’s choice of leaders is intentional (1 Samuel 16:1, 13; Acts 13:22).

- Psalm 78 has just recounted God’s mighty works and Israel’s failures; now the focus turns to the man God raised up “from tending the ewes” (Psalm 78:70).

- By highlighting David, God showcases a tangible example of covenant faithfulness for the nation to follow (2 Samuel 5:2).

Shepherded them

- “Shepherd” is more than a job description; it is a God-given picture of tender yet vigilant care (Psalm 23:1; Ezekiel 34:23; John 10:11-14).

- David’s leadership flowed naturally from his earlier life watching over sheep—he protected, fed, and led them (1 Samuel 17:34-35).

- Israel needed this same careful oversight after the chaotic era of the judges (Judges 21:25).

With integrity of heart

- Integrity speaks of moral wholeness—being the same in private and public (Psalm 15:2; Proverbs 4:23).

- Though imperfect, David’s pattern was to seek God sincerely (1 Samuel 24:5-7; 26:9-11) and to repent when confronted (2 Samuel 12:13).

- God’s promise to Solomon underscores the standard: “walk before Me with integrity of heart” (1 Kings 9:4). David largely modeled that benchmark.

And guided them

- Leadership involves direction, not merely presence. David “guided” by establishing just laws, organizing worship, and unifying the tribes (2 Samuel 8:15; 1 Chronicles 15:1-3).

- The word pictures a steady hand at the helm, like God who “led Your people” through the wilderness (Exodus 15:13).

- Wise guidance brings stability: “Where there is no guidance, a people falls” (Proverbs 11:14).

With skillful hands

- Skill points to competency, planning, and excellence (Ecclesiastes 9:10). David was a strategist in battle (2 Samuel 5:17-25), a gifted musician (1 Samuel 16:18), and an able administrator (2 Samuel 8:16-18).

- God equips leaders not only with pure motives but with practical abilities so the work succeeds (Exodus 31:3-5 for Bezalel; parallel principle).

- When heart-integrity and hand-skill converge, God’s people flourish.

summary

Psalm 78:72 presents the ideal blend for godly leadership: a shepherd’s heart and a craftsman’s hand. David shows that sincere devotion to God (“integrity of heart”) must partner with competent action (“skillful hands”). When both qualities align under God’s calling, His people are protected, directed, and blessed.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 72. - So he fed them according to the integrity of his heart. On the whole, David performed his task of governing Israel faithfully. He had the direct testimony of God to that effect (see 1 Kings 9:4). And he guided them by the skilfulness of his hands. David was not only an upright and faithful king, but also a "skilful" or prudent one. He built up his kingdom into an empire without suffering any serious disasters. Israel reached its acme of glory and prosperity under him, decline setting in under Solomon.
