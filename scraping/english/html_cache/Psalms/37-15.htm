But their swords will pierce their own hearts
This phrase reflects the principle of divine justice, where the wicked's own schemes lead to their downfall. The imagery of a sword piercing one's own heart suggests self-destruction, a common theme in biblical literature. In the historical context, swords were primary weapons of war, symbolizing power and aggression. The Bible often uses the sword metaphorically to represent the Word of God (Hebrews 4:12), but here it signifies the destructive plans of the wicked. This concept is echoed in Proverbs 26:27, which states that those who dig a pit will fall into it. Theologically, this can be seen as a type of Christ's victory over evil, where the plans of the wicked ultimately lead to their own ruin, much like how the crucifixion, intended to destroy Jesus, resulted in the salvation of mankind.

and their bows will be broken
Bows, like swords, were essential tools of warfare in ancient times, representing strength and the ability to attack from a distance. The breaking of bows signifies the nullification of the wicked's power and intentions. This imagery is consistent with other biblical passages, such as Psalm 46:9, where God is described as breaking the bow and shattering the spear, symbolizing the end of conflict and the establishment of peace. The breaking of bows can also be seen as a prophetic assurance of God's ultimate justice and the futility of opposing His will. In a broader theological sense, this reflects the ultimate triumph of God's kingdom, where all opposition is rendered powerless, aligning with the eschatological vision in Revelation where Christ reigns supreme.

Teaching Points
The Self-Destructive Nature of Wickedness
Wicked actions often lead to self-inflicted harm. Just as the wicked's swords pierce their own hearts, sin ultimately leads to one's downfall.

Trust in Divine Justice
Believers are encouraged to trust in God's justice rather than seeking revenge. God will ensure that the wicked face the consequences of their actions.

The Futility of Opposing God
The breaking of the bows symbolizes the futility of opposing God's will. No weapon formed against God's people will ultimately prosper.

Encouragement for the Righteous
The righteous can find peace and assurance in knowing that God is their protector and avenger.

Living a Life of Integrity
Believers are called to live with integrity, trusting that God sees their faithfulness and will reward them in due time.

What Does Psalm 37:15 Mean
Their swords will pierce their own hearts

“But their swords will pierce their own hearts” (Psalm 37:15a)

• A vivid promise of divine retribution. What the wicked intend for others rebounds upon themselves, showing the built-in justice of God’s moral order (Psalm 7:14-16; Esther 7:10).

• The sword represents aggressive, open hostility. When the Psalm says it will pierce their own hearts, it points to a literal end that fully satisfies justice—evil is not merely restrained; it is fatally turned inward (Proverbs 26:27; Matthew 26:52).

• God’s people can therefore rest from anxiety and refrain from retaliation, knowing the Lord will make sure every hostile plan self-destructs (Romans 12:19; Psalm 94:23).

• Practical takeaway:

– Trust that God sees every injustice.

– Expect Him to act in His timing, even if the turnaround is invisible at first.

– Refuse the temptation to pick up the enemy’s weapons; they come pre-loaded with self-inflicted harm.

Their bows will be broken

“…and their bows will be broken” (Psalm 37:15b)

• The bow symbolizes calculated, distant, and sometimes hidden aggression (Psalm 11:2). God guarantees that every device designed for long-range harm will be snapped and rendered useless (Psalm 46:9; Job 20:24).

• “Broken” implies permanent disarming, not a temporary setback. Once God shatters the bow, the threat is gone for good (Psalm 37:17; Isaiah 54:17).

• The picture also underscores God’s complete control over outcomes. Even when wicked plans appear strong and well-aimed, they cannot bypass His sovereign intervention (Proverbs 21:30-31).

• Practical takeaway:

– Recognize that no strategy against the righteous can ultimately stand.

– Live boldly, not fearfully, because the Lord Himself dismantles what you cannot.

– Praise Him in advance for victories you have not yet seen.

summary

Psalm 37:15 assures believers that every weapon formed against them carries a built-in expiration date: swords turn inward, bows snap in God’s hands. The verse calls us to patience, confidence, and refusal to retaliate, knowing the Lord’s perfect justice will unfailingly overturn the violence of the wicked.

(15) Their sword.--The lex talionis. (Comp. Psalm 7:15-16.)

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 15. - Their sword shall enter into their own heart. Such as "take the sword" often "perish by the sword" (Matthew 26:52). Absalom's rebellion cost him his life. Marauders would sometimes meet with a stout resistance, and be slain by those whom they had intended to plunder. And their bows shall be broken; i.e. they shall meet with failure.
