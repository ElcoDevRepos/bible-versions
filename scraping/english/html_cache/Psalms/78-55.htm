Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
He drove out nations before them
This phrase refers to God's actions in the conquest of Canaan, where He enabled the Israelites to defeat and displace the existing inhabitants of the land. This event is a fulfillment of the promises made to Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob regarding the land of Canaan (Genesis 15:18-21). The driving out of nations is also a demonstration of God's sovereignty and judgment against the Canaanites' idolatry and immorality (Deuteronomy 9:4-5). Archaeological evidence supports the existence of various Canaanite city-states during this period, which were eventually overtaken by the Israelites.

and apportioned their inheritance
This phrase highlights God's role in dividing the land among the tribes of Israel, as described in the book of Joshua (Joshua 13-21). The land was distributed by lot, which was seen as a means of divine decision-making (Proverbs 16:33). This apportionment was not just a physical allocation but also a spiritual inheritance, as the land was considered a gift from God, fulfilling His covenant promises. The concept of inheritance is significant in biblical theology, symbolizing the blessings and responsibilities of being God's chosen people.

He settled the tribes of Israel in their tents
This phrase indicates the establishment of the Israelites in the Promised Land, where each tribe received its designated territory. The reference to "tents" may evoke the imagery of the Israelites' nomadic past during the wilderness wanderings, contrasting with their new settled life in Canaan. This settlement was a crucial step in the formation of Israel as a nation, allowing them to develop socially, economically, and religiously. Theologically, it points to God's faithfulness in providing rest and security for His people, a theme echoed in the New Testament with the spiritual rest found in Christ (Hebrews 4:8-10).

Persons / Places / Events
1. God
The central figure in this verse, God is depicted as the one who actively drives out nations and apportions the land to Israel.

2. Nations
These are the pagan nations that occupied the Promised Land before the Israelites. God drove them out to fulfill His promise to Israel.

3. Israel
The chosen people of God, who receive the inheritance of the land as promised to their forefathers.

4. Tribes of Israel
The twelve tribes that descended from the sons of Jacob, each receiving a portion of the land.

5. Promised Land
The land of Canaan, which God promised to Abraham and his descendants.
Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty and Faithfulness
God is sovereign over nations and history. He fulfills His promises, as seen in the inheritance given to Israel.

The Importance of Obedience
Israel's inheritance was contingent upon their obedience to God's commands. We, too, must live in obedience to receive God's blessings.

Spiritual Inheritance
Just as Israel received a physical inheritance, believers receive a spiritual inheritance in Christ. We are called to live in the fullness of this inheritance.

God's Provision and Guidance
God not only provides but also guides His people to their rightful place. Trust in His provision and guidance in your life.

The Role of Faith in God's Promises
Faith is essential in receiving God's promises. Israel had to trust God to drive out the nations and give them the land.

What Does Psalm 78:55 Mean
He drove out nations before them

• “He drove out nations before them” (Psalm 78:55a) recalls the Lord’s direct intervention in clearing the land of Canaan.

– Exodus 23:27-28 describes how God would “send My terror ahead of you and throw into confusion every nation.”

– Joshua 24:12 records God’s words: “It was not by your sword or bow that I gave you the land.”

• The action is deliberate: God, not Israel’s military skill, displaced the Amorites, Hittites, Perizzites, and others (Deuteronomy 7:1-2).

• Spiritual takeaway: the same God who removed obstacles for Israel still overrules opposition to fulfill His promises (Romans 8:31).

and apportioned their inheritance

• After the nations were expelled, the Lord “apportioned their inheritance” (Psalm 78:55b).

– Numbers 26:53 shows God ordering Moses, “The land must be divided among the tribes as an inheritance.”

– Joshua 13-19 details the lot-casting that assigned territory tribe by tribe, fulfilling God’s covenant pledge to Abraham (Genesis 15:18-21).

• Key truths:

– The portion each tribe received was neither random nor earned; it was allotted by divine sovereignty (Joshua 14:1-2).

– God’s faithfulness is concrete: promises made centuries earlier become visible borders, cities, and fields (Joshua 21:43-45).

He settled the tribes of Israel in their tents

• The verse concludes, “He settled the tribes of Israel in their tents” (Psalm 78:55c).

– Deuteronomy 12:10 looked forward to a time when Israel would “live in safety” in the land the Lord gives.

– Judges 2:6 highlights Joshua dismissing the people, “each to his inheritance to possess the land.”

• Settling implies rest after wandering:

– Forty years of desert tents give way to permanent homes and farms (Deuteronomy 6:10-11).

– God’s purpose was not only conquest but peaceful habitation—communities rooted in covenant obedience (Leviticus 26:5-6).

• Applied today: the Lord still provides places of rest and security for His people (Psalm 91:1-2), ultimately fulfilled in the eternal dwelling He prepares (John 14:2-3).

summary

Psalm 78:55 celebrates three linked acts of God: He removed hostile nations, allotted the land by divine decree, and settled Israel securely. The verse underscores His sovereign power, covenant faithfulness, and desire to give His people rest. Trust the same Lord to clear obstacles, provide inheritance, and establish lasting peace for all who rely on Him.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 55. - He cast out the heathen also before them (comp. Exodus 34:24; Deuteronomy 7:1; 1 Kings 21:26: Psalm 44:2, etc.). "They get not the land in possession by their own sword, neither did their own arm save them; but God's right hand, and his arm, and the light of his countenance" (Psalm 44:3). And divided them an inheritance by line. The measuring line, which was employed in parcelling out territory, is intended (comp. Jeremiah 31:39; Amos 7:17). Joshua's division of the land (Joshua 15-19.) among the tribes is specially pointed at. And made the tribes of Israel to dwell in their tents; i.e. in the tents of the heathen - the abodes of the Hivites, Hittites, Amorites, Porizzites, Girgashites, and Jebusites.
