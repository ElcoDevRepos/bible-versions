Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
The channels of the sea appeared
This phrase evokes imagery of the parting of the Red Sea, as described in Exodus 14:21-22, where God miraculously divided the waters for the Israelites to escape from Egypt. It signifies God's power over creation and His ability to reveal hidden things. The "channels" suggest pathways or routes that were previously unseen, emphasizing God's sovereignty in making a way where there seems to be none.

and the foundations of the world were exposed
This imagery suggests a cataclysmic event where the very structure of the earth is laid bare. It can be seen as a metaphor for God's judgment and His ability to uncover the deepest truths. The "foundations" symbolize stability and order, and their exposure indicates a divine intervention that disrupts the natural order. This can be connected to the creation narrative in Genesis, where God establishes the earth, and to prophetic literature like Isaiah 24:18-20, which speaks of the earth reeling under God's judgment.

at the rebuke of the LORD
The term "rebuke" indicates a strong expression of disapproval or correction from God. Throughout scripture, God's rebuke is often associated with His judgment against sin and injustice, as seen in passages like Psalm 18:15 and Isaiah 50:2. It underscores God's authority and His role as the righteous judge who holds creation accountable.

at the blast of the breath of His nostrils
This phrase uses anthropomorphic language to describe God's power and wrath. The "breath of His nostrils" is a vivid depiction of God's life-giving and life-taking force, reminiscent of Genesis 2:7, where God breathes life into Adam. It also parallels the imagery in Job 4:9 and Isaiah 11:4, where God's breath is a means of executing judgment. This phrase highlights the immediacy and potency of God's actions, reinforcing His omnipotence and the awe-inspiring nature of His presence.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty Over Creation
God's power is unmatched, and He controls the very elements of the earth. This should lead us to trust in His ability to handle the chaos in our lives.

The Power of God's Word
Just as God rebukes the sea and exposes the foundations of the world, His Word has the power to transform and reveal truth in our lives.

Reverence for God's Majesty
Recognizing God's authority should lead us to worship and revere Him, acknowledging His greatness and our dependence on Him.

God's Deliverance
Just as God delivered David, He is able to deliver us from our trials. We should seek His intervention and trust in His timing.

The Breath of God
The imagery of God's breath signifies life and power. We should seek the Holy Spirit's presence in our lives to empower and guide us.

What Does 2 Samuel 22:16 Mean
The channels of the sea appeared

- David draws on the dramatic picture of a seabed laid bare. Think of Exodus 14:21-22, where “the waters were divided” and Israel walked “through the sea on dry ground.” The same thought re-emerges in Psalm 18:15, a parallel song: “The channels of the sea appeared…”

- This line proclaims that when God decides to rescue, every barrier— even an ocean— simply moves aside. Joshua 3:16 shows the Jordan River piling up in a heap; Psalm 77:16-19 recalls waters trembling at God’s presence. All these scenes echo the idea that creation itself is no match for the Creator.

- The picture stirs confidence: if the Lord can make the ocean floor visible, He can certainly open a path for us through any crisis.

and the foundations of the world were exposed

- “Foundations” suggests the deepest, firmest parts of the earth. Job 38:4-6 asks where those foundations were laid; Psalm 104:5 says God “set the earth on its foundations.” When He intervenes, even those hidden supports shake (Nahum 1:5-6; Revelation 6:14).

- The verse tells us that no realm— physical or spiritual— is outside God’s reach. He can peel back the layers of the world as easily as we lift a stone.

- Practical takeaway: because He sees what is normally unseen, He also exposes motives, plots, and dangers we cannot detect. Nothing stays buried when He acts.

at the rebuke of the LORD

- A “rebuke” is more than a verbal correction; it carries force. Psalm 106:9 says, “He rebuked the Red Sea, and it dried up.” Isaiah 50:2 reminds us that at His rebuke, seas turn to deserts.

- David credits God’s spoken authority for his deliverance, not military skill or circumstance. Creation obeys God’s voice, so foes must also bow.

- For us, this means every enemy— visible or invisible— ultimately answers to that same rebuke. When the Lord speaks against something, it cannot stand.

at the blast of the breath of His nostrils

- The picture intensifies: not only God’s word, but even the breath from His nostrils unleashes might. Exodus 15:8 celebrates that “At the blast of Your nostrils the waters piled up.” Job 4:9 adds, “By the breath of God they perish.”

- Breath suggests both life-giving and life-ending power. Genesis 2:7 shows God breathing life into Adam, while Psalm 18:8 describes smoke and fire issuing from Him in judgment.

- David’s point: what terrifies enemies is effortless for God— it is no more difficult than breathing. That is the measureless gap between Creator and creation.

summary

2 Samuel 22:16 paints a cascading picture: God breathes, rebukes, lays bare seas and earth, and rescues His servant. Every phrase shouts that the Lord’s power is absolute, immediate, and personal. If He can expose ocean channels and earth’s foundations, He can certainly reach into our circumstances, overturn obstacles, and defend His people.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(16) Of the sea.--Psalms 18, "of waters." There are several such slight differences between 2Samuel 22:15-16, and the parallel verses in the psalm, which mark the two as distinctly different recensions. The most striking change is that of the last pronoun from "his" to "thy in the psalm, as appropriate to its use in public worship.
