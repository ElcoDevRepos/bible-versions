Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Indeed, their two sons, Ahimaaz son of Zadok and Jonathan son of Abiathar, are there with them.
Ahimaaz and Jonathan were the sons of the priests Zadok and Abiathar, respectively. Zadok and Abiathar were loyal to King David during Absalom's rebellion. The mention of their sons highlights the importance of family loyalty and the role of the priesthood in supporting the king. Ahimaaz and Jonathan served as messengers, a critical role in the communication network during the rebellion. This reflects the trust David placed in the priestly families, as well as the strategic use of familial connections in political and military affairs. The presence of these young men also underscores the continuity of the priestly line and their involvement in the nation's leadership.

Send them to me with everything you hear.
David instructs that Ahimaaz and Jonathan be sent to him with information, indicating the urgency and importance of intelligence during the conflict with Absalom. This phrase underscores the necessity of reliable communication in times of crisis. The use of messengers was a common practice in ancient times, as it was the primary means of relaying information over distances. This directive also highlights David's strategic acumen and his reliance on trusted individuals to maintain his position. The phrase can be seen as a precursor to the role of the Holy Spirit in the New Testament, who conveys God's messages to believers. Additionally, it reflects the theme of divine guidance and protection, as David seeks to stay informed and make wise decisions amidst turmoil.

Teaching Points
The Importance of Loyal Allies
In times of crisis, having trustworthy and loyal allies is crucial. Ahimaaz and Jonathan exemplify the kind of faithfulness that supports and sustains leadership.

God's Sovereignty in Crisis
Despite the rebellion, God orchestrates events and people to protect His anointed. Trust in God's sovereignty is essential during tumultuous times.

The Role of Communication
Effective communication is vital in overcoming challenges. The network established by David through Zadok and Abiathar highlights the importance of clear and reliable communication.

Courage in Adversity
Ahimaaz and Jonathan's willingness to risk their lives to support David teaches us about the courage required to stand for righteousness.

Faithfulness to God's Anointed
The loyalty of Zadok and Abiathar to David, God's chosen king, reminds us of the importance of supporting and praying for our leaders.

What Does 2 Samuel 15:36 Mean
Indeed

David’s opening word expresses settled conviction. He is not guessing about the situation; he knows it “indeed.” When God’s anointed king speaks with this confidence, it reflects his assurance that the Lord is still guiding events (2 Samuel 15:25; Psalm 37:23). Just as Samuel once said, “The LORD will do what He has spoken” (1 Samuel 3:12), David is staking his next steps on something certain, not speculative.

their two sons

By highlighting “their two sons,” David underscores that the next generation of priestly families is ready to serve. Scripture often notes the faithfulness of sons who walk in their fathers’ callings (1 Chronicles 6:8–9; 2 Timothy 1:5). Here, God’s covenant plan moves forward through family lines that remain loyal in a national crisis.

Ahimaaz son of Zadok and Jonathan son of Abiathar

Naming the messengers adds personal weight.

• Ahimaaz had already proven quick and reliable (2 Samuel 18:19–27).

• Jonathan’s presence fulfills his father Abiathar’s shared priestly role (1 Samuel 22:20).

Together they represent both priestly houses, echoing how “every matter is established by two or three witnesses” (Deuteronomy 19:15; Matthew 18:16).

are there with them

The sons are stationed inside Jerusalem with their fathers while David retreats beyond the city (2 Samuel 15:29). God strategically places His servants where they are most useful—think of Esther in the palace (Esther 4:14) or the unnamed servant girl in Naaman’s house (2 Kings 5:2–3). Location is part of divine provision.

Send them to me

David sets up a communication network. Although he trusts the Lord, he still exercises prudent planning (Proverbs 21:31; Nehemiah 4:9). Obedience involves both faith and action—David will wait on God, yet he also directs practical steps to secure information.

with everything you hear

David wants the full report, nothing held back, because accurate intelligence will guide his next move (2 Samuel 17:20–22). Jesus likewise told His disciples to “make known everything I have commanded you” (Matthew 28:20). Complete truth, not selective updates, protects God’s people.

summary

2 Samuel 15:36 records David’s clear, confident plan to receive timely news through the faithful sons of the priests. It showcases:

• Certainty that God is still in control.

• Generational faithfulness ready for service.

• Two reliable witnesses anchoring truth.

• Strategic placement of God’s servants.

• Wise stewardship that pairs trust in God with practical action.

• A call for full, honest communication.

The verse assures us that, even in upheaval, the Lord positions His people and their next steps are secure in His sovereign hands.
