Helez the Paltite
Helez is listed among David's mighty warriors, a group of elite soldiers who played a crucial role in establishing and defending David's kingdom. The term "Paltite" likely refers to his origin, possibly from a place called Palti. This highlights the diverse backgrounds of David's warriors, emphasizing the unity and strength found in their shared loyalty to David. The inclusion of Helez in this list underscores the importance of each warrior's contribution to the success of David's reign, reflecting the biblical theme of God using individuals from various backgrounds to fulfill His purposes.

Ira son of Ikkesh the Tekoite
Ira is identified as the son of Ikkesh and hails from Tekoa, a town in Judah known for its strategic location and later as the home of the prophet Amos. Tekoa's mention indicates the geographical spread of David's supporters, showing that his influence extended throughout Judah. The name "Ira" means "watchful" or "vigilant," which may reflect his character or role. The reference to his father, Ikkesh, suggests a family of some standing, possibly indicating a tradition of service or valor. This connection to Tekoa and the mention of his lineage highlight the importance of heritage and legacy in biblical narratives, as well as the role of families in God's unfolding plan.

Helez is one of David's mighty warriors, known as the "Paltite," which likely indicates his origin from a place called Palti. He is listed among the valiant men who served King David, demonstrating loyalty and bravery.

2. Ira son of Ikkesh the Tekoite
Ira is another of David's mighty warriors. He is identified as the son of Ikkesh and hails from Tekoa, a town in Judah. Tekoa is also known as the hometown of the prophet Amos.

3. David's Mighty Warriors
This group of elite soldiers served King David with distinction. They are celebrated for their extraordinary feats and unwavering loyalty to David, playing a crucial role in establishing and securing his kingdom.
Teaching Points
Loyalty and Service
The mention of Helez and Ira among David's mighty warriors underscores the importance of loyalty and dedicated service. As Christians, we are called to serve God and others with the same commitment and faithfulness.

God Uses Ordinary People
Both Helez and Ira were ordinary men from specific towns, yet they were used by God in significant ways. This reminds us that God can use anyone, regardless of their background, to accomplish His purposes.

Community and Fellowship
The list of David's mighty warriors highlights the importance of community and fellowship among believers. Just as these men supported David, we are called to support and encourage one another in our spiritual journeys.

Courage in Faith
The bravery of David's warriors serves as an example of courage in faith. We are encouraged to stand firm in our beliefs and face challenges with the strength that comes from God.

What Does 2 Samuel 23:26 Mean
Helez the Paltite

– “Helez the Paltite” appears again in the parallel list of David’s mighty men in 1 Chronicles 11:27, and he later commands the seventh monthly division of the army in 1 Chronicles 27:10.

– These references show that his courage was not a one-time act; he maintained steady faithfulness over many years, rising to trusted leadership.

– As an Ephraimite (1 Chronicles 27:10), Helez reminds us that God knit the kingdom together by drawing brave servants from every tribe, fulfilling the ideal of Psalm 133:1—“How good and pleasant it is when brothers live together in harmony!”

– The simple listing of his name in 2 Samuel 23:26 (“Helez the Paltite,”) assures believers that the Lord notices every act done for His anointed king (cf. Hebrews 6:10).

– His inclusion among “the Thirty” (2 Samuel 23:24–39) underscores that greatness in God’s eyes flows from loyal obedience more than public fame (compare Matthew 25:21).

Ira son of Ikkesh the Tekoite

– Tekoa, Ira’s hometown, sits in Judah’s hill country (cf. 2 Chronicles 11:6) and later produced the prophet Amos (Amos 1:1). The area was known for rugged terrain and hardy people, fitting for a mighty warrior.

– Ira’s father is named—“Ira son of Ikkesh the Tekoite”—highlighting a heritage of faithfulness. Scripture often links spiritual legacy with future usefulness (e.g., 2 Timothy 1:5).

– Parallel passages (1 Chronicles 11:28) list Ira immediately after Helez, showing that men from different tribes and regions stood shoulder to shoulder in David’s service, foreshadowing the unity Christ would establish among believers from every nation (Ephesians 2:13–16).

– By nesting Ira’s name in the same verse with Helez, the Spirit teaches that every believer, whether well known or obscure, has a part in God’s unfolding plan (1 Corinthians 12:18).

– Tekoa’s later role as a place of wise counsel (2 Samuel 14:2–3) hints that Ira grew up where godly insight was valued, preparing him for courageous, discerning service.

summary

2 Samuel 23:26 catalogs two of David’s elite warriors—Helez and Ira—not merely to fill out a roster but to affirm that the Lord records and rewards every steadfast deed done for His kingdom. Their diverse tribal backgrounds display the unity God forges around His anointed king, their lasting service models perseverance, and their remembered names encourage believers today that humble loyalty will never be overlooked by the King of kings.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(26) Helez.--He was general of the seventh army division (1Chronicles 27:10). There, and also in 1Chronicles 11:27, he is called a Pelonite.

Ira was general for the sixth month (1Chronicles 27:9). His home, Tekoah, was about six miles south of Bethlehem.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 26. - Helez. He is twice called a Pelonite in Chronicles, and was general of the seventh brigade (1 Chronicles 27:10), where he is said to have belonged to the tribe of Ephraim. Whether Paltite or Pelon-ite is right, no one knows; but Beth-Palet was a town in the tribe of Judah, and not in Ephraim. Ira. Ira had the command of the sixth brigade (1 Chronicles 27:9). Tekoah (see note on 2 Samuel 14:2). This Ira is a distinct person from his namesake, David's confidential minister (2 Samuel 20:26).
