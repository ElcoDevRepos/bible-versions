Seven steps led up to it
The number seven often symbolizes completeness or perfection in the Bible, as seen in the seven days of creation (Genesis 1). The steps leading up to the gate may signify a progression towards holiness or divine presence, as the temple represents God's dwelling place. The ascent could also symbolize the spiritual journey of drawing closer to God, reminiscent of the Psalms of Ascent (Psalms 120-134), which were sung by pilgrims traveling to Jerusalem.

and its portico was opposite them
The portico, or porch, served as an entryway to the temple, providing a transition from the outer court to the inner sanctum. This architectural feature is consistent with other ancient Near Eastern temples, emphasizing the sacredness of the space. The positioning of the portico opposite the steps suggests a direct and intentional approach to worship, highlighting the importance of entering God's presence with reverence and preparation.

it had palm trees on its side pillars
Palm trees are a recurring motif in temple imagery, symbolizing victory, peace, and eternal life. They were used in the decoration of Solomon's temple (1 Kings 6:29) and are associated with the Feast of Tabernacles, a time of celebration and remembrance of God's provision (Leviticus 23:40). The presence of palm trees on the pillars may also foreshadow the triumphal entry of Jesus into Jerusalem, where palm branches were laid before Him (John 12:13), symbolizing His kingship and victory over sin.

one on each side
The symmetry of the palm trees on each side of the portico's pillars underscores the balance and order inherent in God's design. This balance reflects the harmony and peace found in God's presence, as well as the equal access to His grace for all who enter. The dual placement may also represent the unity of Jew and Gentile in Christ, as both are brought together in the new covenant (Ephesians 2:14-16).

What Does Ezekiel 40:26 Mean
Seven steps led up to it

• In every gate of Ezekiel’s visionary temple we meet an ascent (Ezekiel 40:6, 31, 34). The literal seven steps here lead worshipers from the outer court toward the inner court, portraying an ordered, progressive approach to God.

• Seven in Scripture often highlights completeness—think of the seven days of creation (Genesis 2:2-3) and the seven lamps on the lampstand (Exodus 25:37). These steps therefore speak of a full, sanctioned way into God’s presence.

• The upward movement echoes Psalm 24:3, “Who may ascend the hill of the LORD?”; the answer is found in a holy pathway God Himself provides.

• For the believer today, Hebrews 10:19-22 points to Christ as the greater, perfect means of approach. As the worshiper in Ezekiel’s vision literally climbs higher, so we spiritually draw near through Jesus.

its portico was opposite them

• The portico (entrance hall) faces directly toward the steps, forming a straight, unhindered line of sight from the courtyard into the gatehouse. Ezekiel 40:15 notes that the distance from the front of a gate to the rear is fifty cubits, emphasizing symmetry and order.

• This architectural precision—seen again in Ezekiel 40:48 with the temple porch—underscores God’s desire for clarity, not confusion (1 Corinthians 14:33).

• The orientation also ensures each worshiper, upon reaching the top step, immediately sees the doorway ahead. John 10:9 records Jesus saying, “I am the gate; if anyone enters through Me, he will be saved.” The portico’s placement illustrates that salvation is found by moving straight to the God-appointed entrance.

it had palm trees on its side pillars, one on each side

• Literal palm carvings adorned Solomon’s temple as well (1 Kings 6:29, 35). Palms symbolize righteous flourishing—“The righteous will flourish like a palm tree” (Psalm 92:12).

• Palms also mark joyous victory, foreshadowing Revelation 7:9 where the redeemed wave palm branches before the throne.

• By stationing palms on both sides, God frames the gate with life, triumph, and worship. Every entrant walks between reminders that life with God is abundant and everlasting (John 10:10).

• The symmetry—one on each side—reinforces that this victory and flourishing are not random decorations but divinely balanced, intentional features of His dwelling.

summary

Ezekiel 40:26 presents a literal gate of God’s future temple with seven ascending steps, a directly aligned portico, and palm-carved pillars. Together they picture the complete, orderly, victorious approach God provides for His people: we rise toward Him on a path He has designed, enter through a clearly defined doorway, and are greeted by symbols of everlasting life and triumph.
