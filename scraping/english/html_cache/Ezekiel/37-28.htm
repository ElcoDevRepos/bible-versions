Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Then the nations will know
This phrase indicates a future revelation to the Gentile nations. It suggests a time when God's actions will be so evident that all peoples will recognize His sovereignty. This is reminiscent of other prophetic passages where God's power and holiness are demonstrated to the world, such as in Isaiah 52:10 and Psalm 98:2-3.

that I the LORD
The use of "I the LORD" emphasizes God's personal involvement and authority. It is a declaration of His identity as Yahweh, the covenant-keeping God of Israel. This phrase is a reminder of God's unique relationship with Israel and His ultimate authority over all creation, as seen throughout the Old Testament.

sanctify Israel
To sanctify means to set apart as holy. This indicates a divine action where God purifies and consecrates Israel, fulfilling His promises of restoration and holiness. This sanctification is a recurring theme in Ezekiel, where God promises to cleanse and renew His people (Ezekiel 36:25-27).

when My sanctuary is among them
The sanctuary refers to the dwelling place of God, symbolizing His presence among His people. Historically, this was the tabernacle and later the temple in Jerusalem. The prophecy points to a future time when God's presence will be permanently established among His people, echoing the promise of a new covenant in Jeremiah 31:33.

forever.’”
The term "forever" signifies an eternal promise, indicating the everlasting nature of God's covenant with Israel. This eternal aspect is seen in the prophetic vision of a new heaven and new earth in Revelation 21:3, where God's dwelling is with humanity forever. It also points to the ultimate fulfillment in Jesus Christ, who is the true temple and through whom God's presence is eternally with His people (John 1:14, Revelation 21:22).

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant-keeping God of Israel, who reveals His holiness and sovereignty through His actions and presence.

2. Israel
The chosen people of God, whom He has set apart for His purposes and through whom He reveals His glory to the nations.

3. The Nations
Refers to the Gentile nations surrounding Israel, who will witness God's sanctification of Israel and His eternal presence among them.

4. Sanctuary
Represents God's dwelling place among His people, symbolizing His presence, holiness, and the restoration of His relationship with Israel.

5. Ezekiel
A prophet called by God to deliver messages of judgment and hope to the exiled Israelites during the Babylonian captivity.
Teaching Points
God's Presence as a Sign of Sanctification
God's presence among His people is a powerful testament to His sanctifying work. As believers, we are called to live in a way that reflects His holiness, knowing that His Spirit dwells within us.

The Witness to the Nations
The transformation and sanctification of God's people serve as a witness to the world. Our lives should demonstrate God's power and love, drawing others to Him.

The Eternal Nature of God's Promises
God's promise to dwell with His people forever assures us of His faithfulness. We can trust in His eternal promises and find hope in His unchanging nature.

The Role of the Sanctuary
The sanctuary symbolizes God's presence and His desire for a relationship with His people. In Christ, we become the temple of the Holy Spirit, called to live in communion with God.

What Does Ezekiel 37:28 Mean
Then the nations will know

God promises a day when every people group will unmistakably recognize His work in Israel.

• Earlier in Ezekiel, the Lord repeats this purpose: “Then they will know that I am the LORD” (Ezekiel 36:23; 38:23).

• Isaiah foresees the same worldwide awakening: “All the ends of the earth will see the salvation of our God” (Isaiah 52:10).

• Romans 11:15 points to Israel’s restoration triggering “life from the dead” for the world.

The verse anticipates a public, historical event—not a hidden, spiritualized idea—showcasing God’s faithfulness on a global stage.

that I the LORD

The focus is not Israel’s glory but the revealing of the covenant-keeping LORD Himself.

• In Exodus 3:15 God ties His name to His eternal faithfulness; Ezekiel echoes that theme.

• Isaiah 45:5–6 underscores there is no other God; the coming revelation leaves no room for rivals (Philippians 2:10–11).

When God acts, He leaves no doubt about His identity or exclusivity.

sanctify Israel

“Sanctify” means to set apart as holy. God Himself does the sanctifying, fulfilling promises such as Leviticus 22:32—“I am the LORD who sanctifies you.”

• Ezekiel 20:12 links Israel’s distinctiveness to God’s own character.

• Jeremiah 31:33 shows the internal aspect—God writes His law on their hearts.

• Romans 11:26–27 affirms this future national cleansing through the New Covenant.

The verse assures that Israel’s holiness is God’s doing, not human achievement.

when My sanctuary is among them

The key signal of Israel’s sanctification is God’s physical dwelling in their midst.

• The previous verse vows, “I will set My sanctuary among them forever” (Ezekiel 37:26–27).

• This mirrors Exodus 25:8, where God first asked Israel to build a tabernacle “so that I may dwell among them.”

• Zechariah 2:10–11 and Revelation 21:3 point forward to the Lord living with His people in a renewed earth.

The promise looks to a literal temple presence—ultimately fulfilled when God Himself abides with redeemed humanity.

forever

The duration seals the promise.

• “Forever” repeats the eternal language of the Davidic covenant (2 Samuel 7:13) and the New Covenant (Jeremiah 32:40).

• Ezekiel 37:25 just declared that Israel will “dwell there forever.”

• Revelation 21:22–27 pictures an unending, secure dwelling with God.

This everlasting aspect guarantees the promise cannot fail or fade; it stands beyond any temporary restoration of the past.

summary

Ezekiel 37:28 assures that God will visibly, eternally dwell with a sanctified Israel, and when He does, every nation will recognize His sovereign hand. The verse ties together God’s global glory, His unique identity, Israel’s holiness, the reality of His dwelling place, and the permanence of His covenant—all certain because the LORD Himself has declared it.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 28 describes the effect which such a glorious transformation of Israel's character and condition, should produce upon the heathen world. They should recognize from his presence amongst his people, symbolized by the establishment in their midst of his sanctuary, that he had both the power and the will to sanctify them, by making them inwardly as well as outwardly holy; and, recognizing this, they would seek admittance to the congregation and fellowship of God's spiritual Israel.
