Then the righteous will shine like the sun
This phrase draws from the imagery found in Daniel 12:3, where the wise are said to shine like the brightness of the heavens. It signifies the glorification and vindication of the righteous at the end of the age. The sun, a symbol of purity and brilliance, represents the transformation and eternal reward of those who have followed God's ways. This transformation is not only physical but also spiritual, reflecting the holiness and righteousness of God Himself. The righteous are those who have been justified by faith, living in accordance with God's will, and their shining is a testament to their faithfulness and God's glory.

in the kingdom of their Father.
The kingdom of their Father refers to the ultimate reign of God, where His sovereignty is fully realized. This kingdom is both a present reality and a future hope, as Jesus inaugurated the kingdom through His ministry, death, and resurrection. The phrase emphasizes the familial relationship between God and believers, highlighting the intimacy and inheritance they share as children of God. This kingdom is characterized by peace, justice, and the presence of God, contrasting with the kingdoms of this world. It is the fulfillment of God's promises throughout Scripture, including the covenants with Abraham, David, and the New Covenant through Christ.

He who has ears, let him hear.
This call to listen is a frequent exhortation in Jesus' teachings, emphasizing the importance of spiritual discernment and understanding. It is a call to attentiveness and responsiveness to the truths being revealed. In the cultural context, hearing implies obedience and action, not just passive listening. This phrase challenges the audience to reflect deeply on the parables and teachings of Jesus, urging them to seek the wisdom and insight necessary to grasp the mysteries of the kingdom. It echoes the prophetic tradition, where hearing God's word is essential for faith and obedience, as seen in passages like Isaiah 6:9-10 and Revelation 2-3.

What Does Matthew 13:43 Mean
Then

- “Then” links directly to the end-time scene Jesus has just described, where “the Son of Man will send out His angels, and they will weed out of His kingdom every cause of sin” (Matthew 13:41).

- The word fixes our attention on a definite, literal moment after judgment, echoing promises such as Matthew 25:34 (“Come, you who are blessed by My Father; inherit the kingdom”) and Revelation 21:1-4 (the new heaven and earth).

- Because Scripture’s timeline is precise, believers can be certain that God keeps His schedule: first the removal of evil (2 Thessalonians 1:7-9), and then the unveiling of glory for His people.

the righteous

- “The righteous” are those declared right with God through faith (Romans 3:22) and made new to live in practical holiness (1 John 3:7).

- Their righteousness is Christ’s own righteousness credited to them (2 Corinthians 5:21) and displayed in obedient lives (Matthew 5:20).

- This group includes “all who have loved His appearing” (2 Timothy 4:8), showing that righteous standing and righteous living stand together.

will shine like the sun

- The promise is literal: God’s people will radiate brilliant, unfading glory. Daniel 12:3 foretold, “Those who are wise will shine like the brightness of the expanse.”

- Paul confirms the transformation: “our lowly bodies will be like His glorious body” (Philippians 3:21), and the resurrection body is “raised in glory” (1 Corinthians 15:42-43).

- “Like the sun” underscores intensity and purity (Revelation 1:16). No trace of sin will dull this brightness.

in the kingdom of their Father

- The setting is the Father’s kingdom, a real realm where His will is perfectly done (Colossians 1:12-13).

- “Their Father” highlights adoption: believers are “heirs of God and co-heirs with Christ” (Romans 8:17).

- Jesus prepares a place there (John 14:2-3). In that kingdom, “the Lord God Almighty and the Lamb are its temple” and “the glory of God illuminates it” (Revelation 21:22-23).

He who has ears, let him hear

- This familiar charge calls for more than casual listening; it demands wholehearted acceptance (Matthew 13:9).

- Hearing must lead to obeying (James 1:22), lest dull hearts miss the warning (Hebrews 3:7-8).

- The Spirit repeats the same plea to the churches (Revelation 2:7), pressing every generation to respond while there is still time.

summary

Jesus guarantees that after evil is removed, every believer—clothed in Christ’s righteousness—will blaze with sun-like splendor in the Father’s kingdom. This is a concrete future, secured by God’s timetable. The closing call urges every listener to embrace and live out this truth today.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(43) Then shall the righteous shine forth as the sun.--The imagery is so natural that we hardly need to look for any reference to older teaching, yet we can hardly help remembering the path of the just that "shineth more and more unto the perfect day" (Proverbs 4:18), and yet more, as connected more closely with the judgment to come, those "that shall shine as the brightness of the firmament and as the stars for ever and ever" (Daniel 12:3). Yet the promise here has one crowning and supreme blessing: the kingdom in which the righteous shall thus shine forth is the kingdom of their Father.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 43. - Then shall the righteous. For with these also their character is seen in their lives (Matthew 5:45, note). Shine forth as the sun. An undoubted reference to the substance of Daniel 12:3. Observe that according to the thought of the parable, it is suggested that the likeness consists not only in the brightness of the sun in itself, but also in its being alone in the sky, with nothing round it to prevent its full glory being seen. Then. The chief lesson of the parable; not before, but at, that time. In the kingdom of their Father. In ver. 38 they were spoken of as "the sons of the kingdom;" here their Father is expressly mentioned, not "the Son of man" (vers. 37, 41). The same reference to his Father rather than to himself is found in Matthew 26:29. Did our Lord wish already to hint that "then cometh the end, when he shall deliver up the kingdom to God, even the Father" (1 Corinthians 15:24)? Had St. Paul's teaching also here a direct connexion with that of our Lord (ver. 41, note)? Who hath ears to hear, let him hear (ver. 9, note).
