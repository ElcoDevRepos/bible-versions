Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
For the Son of Man will come
This phrase refers to the anticipated return of Jesus Christ, often called the Second Coming. The title "Son of Man" is a messianic term rooted in Daniel 7:13-14, where it describes a divine figure given authority and an everlasting kingdom. This return is a central tenet of Christian eschatology, emphasizing Jesus' role as both judge and redeemer.

in His Father’s glory
The glory of the Father signifies divine majesty and holiness. In biblical terms, "glory" often denotes the visible manifestation of God's presence. This phrase underscores the divine authority and splendor with which Jesus will return, reflecting the unity and shared essence between the Father and the Son, as seen in John 17:5.

with His angels
Angels are often depicted as God's messengers and servants, playing significant roles in both Old and New Testament narratives. Their presence at the Second Coming highlights the cosmic significance of the event and the execution of divine judgment, as seen in passages like Matthew 24:31 and 1 Thessalonians 4:16.

and then He will repay each one
This phrase indicates a time of judgment where individuals are held accountable for their actions. The concept of divine retribution is consistent with biblical teachings on justice and righteousness, as seen in Romans 2:6-8. It reflects the moral order established by God, where deeds are evaluated.

according to what he has done
This emphasizes personal responsibility and the moral implications of one's actions. It aligns with the biblical principle of sowing and reaping, as found in Galatians 6:7-8. The evaluation is not merely about external actions but also the intentions and faith behind them, as seen in 1 Corinthians 4:5.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Son of Man
A title Jesus frequently uses for Himself, emphasizing both His humanity and His divine authority. It is rooted in the prophetic vision of Daniel 7:13-14, where the "Son of Man" is given dominion and glory.

2. His Father’s Glory
Refers to the divine majesty and splendor of God the Father. This phrase underscores the divine authority and power with which Jesus will return.

3. His Angels
The heavenly beings who serve God and will accompany Jesus at His second coming, signifying the divine nature of the event.

4. Repayment
The concept of divine justice where each person is rewarded or judged based on their deeds. This reflects the biblical principle of sowing and reaping.

5. What He Has Done
Refers to the actions and deeds of individuals, highlighting the importance of living a life in accordance with God’s will.
Teaching Points
The Certainty of Christ’s Return
Jesus’ promise of His return is a central tenet of Christian faith. Believers are called to live in anticipation of this event, maintaining faithfulness and readiness.

Divine Justice and Accountability
The passage emphasizes that our actions have eternal significance. Christians are encouraged to live righteously, knowing that God’s justice is perfect and impartial.

Living in Light of Eternity
Understanding that Jesus will repay each according to their deeds should motivate believers to prioritize eternal values over temporal gains.

The Role of Angels in God’s Plan
The mention of angels highlights their role in God’s redemptive plan and serves as a reminder of the spiritual realities that accompany Christ’s return.

The Glory of God as Our Ultimate Goal
The reference to the Father’s glory calls believers to live for God’s glory, aligning their lives with His purposes and reflecting His character.

What Does Matthew 16:27 Mean
For the Son of Man

• Jesus often calls Himself “the Son of Man,” a title that ties Him to Daniel’s vision of a divine, everlasting ruler (Daniel 7:13-14).

• It reminds us Christ is fully human and fully divine. He walked among us (John 1:14) yet possesses authority to judge all humanity (John 5:27; Matthew 26:64).

• We meet a Savior who understands our weakness (Hebrews 4:15) yet stands over history as its rightful King.

will come

• This points to a future, personal, visible return—just as the angels promised in Acts 1:11 and as Jesus declares in Revelation 22:12.

• His coming is certain; the timing is the Father’s secret (Matthew 24:36).

• Believers live expectantly, “looking for the blessed hope and glorious appearing” (Titus 2:13).

in His Father’s glory

• Jesus won’t return in the humility of Bethlehem but clothed with the same glory He shared with the Father “before the world existed” (John 17:5).

• The radiance of God’s presence will accompany Him (Hebrews 1:3; Colossians 1:19).

• His glory will silence every rival claim to power (Philippians 2:9-11).

with His angels

• Heaven’s armies attend Him (Matthew 25:31), emphasizing His royal authority.

• Angels serve as gatherers of the righteous and executors of judgment (Matthew 13:41; 2 Thessalonians 1:7).

• Their presence underscores the universal scope of the event—no corner of creation is overlooked.

and then He will repay

• Judgment follows Christ’s arrival; reward and recompense are not abstract ideas but guaranteed realities (Romans 14:10-12; 2 Corinthians 5:10).

• “Repay” shows perfect justice: mercy for those in Christ, wrath for persistent unbelief (John 3:36; Revelation 20:11-15).

• Nothing done for Him is forgotten (Mark 9:41; Revelation 22:12).

each one according to what he has done

• God’s assessment is individual—no one hides in a crowd (Psalm 62:12; Proverbs 24:12).

• Works reveal the authenticity of faith (James 2:17). Salvation is by grace through faith (Ephesians 2:8-9), yet true faith produces fruit God will acknowledge (Ephesians 2:10; Galatians 6:7-8).

• This reality encourages perseverance, holiness, and wholehearted service (1 Corinthians 15:58).

summary

Matthew 16:27 anchors hope and accountability in one verse. Jesus, the divine Son of Man, will personally return in unparalleled splendor, attended by heavenly hosts, to execute perfect justice. For believers, that day brings vindication and reward; for the unrepentant, righteous judgment. Knowing He will repay each of us according to our deeds stirs us to live faithfully, confidently, and expectantly until we see His glory unveiled.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(27) For the Son of man shall come.--The fact stands in a logical relation to the preceding verse. The fact that the Son of Man is about to come to execute judgment, clothes its abstract statement with an awful certainty. No bribe can be offered to the Eternal Judge to change the sentence of forfeiture if that forfeiture has been rightfully incurred. From first to last in our Lord's teaching (e.g., for its earlier stages, Matthew 7:23-24; John 5:26-27) this claim to be the future Judge of all men is never absent. It is asserted in every great discourse, implied in almost every parable.

With his angels.--We are justified by Matthew 25:31 in referring the possessive pronoun to Christ rather than the Father. "All things that the Father hath are Mine" (John 16:15), and among these the angels that do His pleasure.

His works.--The better MSS. give a word in the singular, his doing or conduct. The sentence is made to depend on the collective character of what has been done rather than on the multitude of individual acts.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 27. - For the Son of man shall come. The final judgment would put things in their true light - would show the value of self-sacrifice, would reveal the punishment of self-pleasing. Our Lord seems to refer to Daniel 7:13, as it were, in testimony to the truth of what he had just said. Shall come; μέλλει ἔρχεσθαι: venturus est (Vulgate), is more than the bare announcement, and implies that it is in accordance with the eternal counsels of God that he should appear this second time. In the glory of his Father. As one with the Father, and his Representative. So he speaks of "the glory which thou hast given me" (John 17:22). Reward; ἀποδώσει: render, reddet (Vulgate). The term includes punishment as well as recompense. Works (πρᾶξιν); doing, work. The word does not signify isolated acts, but general course of conduct, practice as a whole.
