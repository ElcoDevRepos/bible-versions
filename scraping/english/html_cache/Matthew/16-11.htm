How do you not understand that I was not telling you about bread?
In this phrase, Jesus is addressing His disciples' misunderstanding of His earlier warning. The context is crucial: Jesus had just performed the miracles of feeding the 5,000 and the 4,000, demonstrating His power over physical needs. The disciples' concern about physical bread shows their lack of spiritual insight. This reflects a common theme in the Gospels where Jesus' teachings are often misunderstood by His followers, who focus on the literal rather than the spiritual. This phrase challenges believers to seek deeper spiritual truths beyond the surface level.

But beware of the leaven of the Pharisees and Sadducees.”
Leaven, or yeast, is used metaphorically here. In Jewish culture, leaven often symbolizes corruption or sin, as seen in the Passover tradition where unleavened bread is used to signify purity. The Pharisees and Sadducees were influential Jewish sects during Jesus' time, known for their strict adherence to the law and their political power, respectively. Jesus warns against their teachings, which were marked by hypocrisy, legalism, and a lack of true understanding of God's will. This warning is a call to discernment, urging believers to be cautious of doctrines that deviate from the truth of the Gospel. The reference to leaven connects to other scriptures, such as 1 Corinthians 5:6-8, where Paul warns about the corrupting influence of sin. This phrase emphasizes the importance of maintaining purity in doctrine and practice, aligning with the broader biblical narrative of holiness and truth.

3. Pharisees
A Jewish religious group known for strict adherence to the Law and traditions. They often opposed Jesus and His teachings.

4. Sadducees
Another Jewish sect, often in conflict with the Pharisees, known for their denial of the resurrection and emphasis on the written Law.

5. Yeast (Leaven)
Used metaphorically by Jesus to represent the corrupting influence of false teachings and hypocrisy.
Teaching Points
Discernment in Teaching
Jesus emphasizes the importance of understanding and discerning spiritual truths beyond the literal interpretation. Believers should seek wisdom and insight from the Holy Spirit to recognize false teachings.

Beware of Hypocrisy
The "yeast of the Pharisees and Sadducees" symbolizes hypocrisy and false doctrine. Christians are called to live authentically and align their actions with their beliefs.

Influence of False Doctrine
Just as yeast affects the whole dough, false teachings can infiltrate and corrupt a community. Vigilance and adherence to biblical truth are essential.

Spiritual Growth and Maturity
Understanding Jesus' teachings requires spiritual maturity. Believers should strive to grow in their faith and knowledge of God's Word.

Community Accountability
The church community should hold each other accountable to prevent the spread of false teachings and encourage sound doctrine.

What Does Matthew 16:11 Mean
How do you not understand

• Jesus speaks with gentle rebuke, expecting His disciples to connect recent experiences with spiritual truth (Mark 8:17-18).

• Their physical focus on forgotten loaves (Matthew 16:5) shows how easily worry over daily needs dulls spiritual perception, despite His miraculous feedings (Matthew 14:20-21; 15:37).

• Scripture consistently calls God’s people to grow in discernment, moving from milk to solid food (Hebrews 5:12-14).

That I was not telling you about bread

• Jesus states plainly that His warning was never about baked loaves. He often uses tangible images to teach unseen realities, just as He did with living water (John 4:10-14) and true manna (John 6:32-35).

• Physical provision is real and important (Matthew 6:11), yet it always points beyond itself to the One who “does not live on bread alone, but on every word that comes from the mouth of God” (Deuteronomy 8:3; Matthew 4:4).

But beware of the leaven

• Leaven starts small, permeates silently, and alters an entire batch—an everyday picture of how ideas and attitudes spread (1 Corinthians 5:6-8).

• Jesus’ use of “beware” signals danger: disciples must stay alert, refusing to let even a small amount of error or hypocrisy gain influence (Galatians 5:9).

• Vigilance requires measuring every teaching against God’s unchanging Word (Acts 17:11).

Of the Pharisees and Sadducees

• Pharisees added human tradition to Scripture, creating burdensome rules and masking hearts far from God (Matthew 23:13, 23-28).

• Sadducees denied key doctrines—resurrection, angels, and the supernatural (Acts 23:8)—stripping Scripture of its full authority.

• Both groups, though different, shared a common leaven: man-centered religion that resists Jesus’ lordship. Their influence can still seep into churches today through legalism, skepticism, or any doctrine that distracts from Christ’s gospel (Colossians 2:8).

summary

Matthew 16:11 records Jesus correcting His disciples’ fixation on physical bread and redirecting them to the spiritual threat of corrupt teaching. He calls believers to sharpen spiritual understanding, recognize subtle influences, and guard unwaveringly against doctrines—like those of the Pharisees and Sadducees—that distort or dilute the truth of God’s Word.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(11) How is it that ye do not understand?--True to His method of education our Lord does not Himself interpret the parable, but is, as it were, content to suggest the train of thought which led to the interpretation. And the disciples, slow of heart as they were, followed the clue thus given. "Then understood they." Memory did at last quicken faith, and faith imparted the imaginative insight which sees its way through parables and dark sayings.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 11. - That I spake it not to you, etc. The Revised Version, following many modern editors, divides the clause into two, thus: that I spake not to you concerning bread? But beware of the leaven, etc. This is the second ground for the Lord's reproof administered to the apostles. They had taken in a carnal, literal sense a word which he had used in a symbolical or mystical meaning. It is the want of spiritual discernment which he censures. They had had frequent opportunities of hearing and appreciating his mode of teaching: miracles, parables, discourses, had an inner signification, which it was their duty to apprehend. The want of understanding was a moral fault for which they were answerable. We may say it would have been easier for our Lord to have spoken of doctrine without using the misunderstood figure of leaven. But it is in the way of his providence to speak words which need thought and grace to make them fully comprehended. They are thus more impressed upon the heart and memory, and bring forth better fruit. A well instructed Hebrew ought to have no difficulty in understanding metaphorical allusions. His Scriptures were full of them, and could not be intelligently read without the light thus cast upon them.
