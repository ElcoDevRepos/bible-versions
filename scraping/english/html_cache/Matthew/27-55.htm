Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
And many women were there,
This phrase highlights the presence of women at the crucifixion, which is significant given the cultural context of the time. Women were often marginalized in ancient Jewish society, yet they played a crucial role in Jesus' ministry. Their presence at such a critical moment underscores their faithfulness and courage. This also contrasts with the absence of most of the male disciples, who had fled.

watching from a distance.
The women observed from afar, possibly due to the danger and fear associated with being too close to the crucifixion site. This distance could also reflect the Roman practice of keeping onlookers at bay during executions. Despite the physical distance, their emotional and spiritual proximity to Jesus is evident, showcasing their unwavering support.

They had followed Jesus from Galilee
This indicates the long-term commitment and dedication of these women to Jesus' ministry. Galilee was the region where Jesus began His public ministry, and these women had been with Him since then, suggesting they were eyewitnesses to His teachings and miracles. Their journey from Galilee to Jerusalem reflects their loyalty and the significant role they played in supporting Jesus.

to minister to Him.
The term "minister" implies service and care, which these women provided throughout Jesus' ministry. This service could have included financial support, preparing meals, and other acts of hospitality. Their ministry to Jesus fulfills the biblical principle of serving others, as seen in passages like Matthew 20:28, where Jesus speaks of serving rather than being served. Their actions exemplify true discipleship and devotion.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Women Followers
These women had been following Jesus from Galilee, demonstrating their commitment and dedication to His ministry. They were present at the crucifixion, showing their loyalty even in the face of danger and despair.

2. Galilee
This region is significant as the starting point of Jesus' ministry and the place where these women began their journey with Him. It represents the beginning of their discipleship and service.

3. Crucifixion
The event of Jesus' crucifixion is central to the Christian faith, marking the moment of His sacrificial death for humanity's sins. The presence of these women highlights their courage and faithfulness during this pivotal moment.
Teaching Points
Faithful Service
The women’s dedication to Jesus, even in His darkest hour, serves as a powerful example of faithful service. Believers are encouraged to remain steadfast in their commitment to Christ, regardless of circumstances.

Role of Women in Ministry
This passage highlights the significant role women played in Jesus' ministry. It challenges modern believers to recognize and value the contributions of women in the church today.

Courage in Adversity
The courage of these women to be present at the crucifixion, despite the potential danger, inspires believers to stand firm in their faith, even when it is unpopular or risky.

Witnessing from a Distance
The women watched from a distance, yet their presence was impactful. This teaches that one can be a witness for Christ in various ways, sometimes quietly and from afar, yet still effectively.

What Does Matthew 27:55 Mean
And many women

- Scripture highlights the presence of “many women,” underscoring that faithful female disciples were numerous, not rare (cf. Luke 8:2–3; Acts 1:14).

- Their inclusion in the Passion narrative demonstrates God’s valuing of both men and women in His redemptive story (Genesis 1:27; Galatians 3:28).

- While the male disciples largely fled (Matthew 26:56), these women remained, exemplifying courage and steadfast love (Proverbs 17:17).

were there

- “There” is Calvary, the place of Jesus’ crucifixion (John 19:17–18).

- Their physical presence testifies as eyewitnesses to the historical reality of the event, later contributing to the credibility of the resurrection accounts (Matthew 28:1–10; 1 Corinthians 15:3–8).

- God often plants faithful witnesses right where His greatest works unfold (Ruth 4:11; Acts 2:32).

watching from a distance

- Roman execution sites were guarded; a respectful distance kept them safe yet present (Luke 23:35, 49).

- Distance did not equal detachment; their hearts were near, mirroring Psalm 34:18—“The LORD is near to the brokenhearted.”

- This posture recalls Psalm 22:17, a messianic prophecy: “I can count all my bones; they look and stare at me”—even in pain, Jesus is observed and honored.

They had followed Jesus from Galilee

- Their discipleship was not sudden; it began in Galilee, the region of Jesus’ public ministry (Matthew 4:23; Mark 1:39).

- Following implies intentional, ongoing commitment—echoing Jesus’ call in Matthew 16:24.

- The journey from Galilee to Jerusalem spans roughly 80–90 miles, illustrating perseverance (Hebrews 10:36).

to minister to Him

- “Minister” translates practical service: funding, food, care (Luke 8:3).

- Their service mirrors the actions of angels who “ministered to Him” after the wilderness temptation (Matthew 4:11), showing continuity of divine provision.

- Serving Christ personally fulfills Deuteronomy 10:12: “What does the LORD your God require of you but to fear the LORD…to love Him, to serve the LORD your God with all your heart.”

summary

Matthew 27:55 spotlights a company of devoted women who remained near the cross, eyewitnesses to Jesus’ suffering. Their longstanding discipleship from Galilee and practical ministry to the Lord demonstrate steadfast faith, courage, and love in action. Their example encourages modern believers to stay close to Christ, serve Him wholeheartedly, and testify to His saving work, even when circumstances press us to stand “at a distance.”

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(55) Many women were there beholding.--The group was obviously distinct from that of "the daughters of Jerusalem," of Luke 23:28, but was probably identical with that mentioned in Luke 8:2-3, as accompanying our Lord in many of His journeyings.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 55. - Many women. These are mentioned as witnesses of all these events which the apostles are not recorded to have seen. Courageous and loving, they had followed the procession to Calvary, and at a distance watched the woeful proceedings there. Some, we know, had ventured to come closer to their dying Lord (see John 19:25). Which followed (equivalent to had followed) Jesus from Galilee, ministering unto him. They had accompanied Jesus on his last journey to the Passover at Jerusalem, tending him during all the time, and of their substance ministering to his wants (Luke 8:3).
