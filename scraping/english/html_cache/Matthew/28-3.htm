His appearance was like lightning
The description of the angel's appearance as "like lightning" emphasizes the divine and supernatural nature of the being. Lightning is often associated with God's power and presence in the Bible, as seen in Exodus 19:16 during the giving of the Law at Mount Sinai. This imagery conveys a sense of awe and fear, highlighting the angel's role as a messenger of God. The brightness and suddenness of lightning also symbolize the revelation of God's truth and the breaking in of the divine into the earthly realm. This description aligns with other biblical accounts of angelic appearances, such as in Daniel 10:6, where an angel is described with a face like lightning.

and his clothes were white as snow
The whiteness of the angel's clothes signifies purity, holiness, and righteousness. In biblical symbolism, white garments often represent the righteousness of God and those who are in His presence. This imagery is consistent with other scriptural references, such as in Revelation 3:5, where the faithful are promised to be clothed in white garments. The comparison to snow, which is pure and unstained, further emphasizes the angel's divine origin and the message of resurrection and new life. This description also connects to the transfiguration of Jesus in Matthew 17:2, where His clothes became as white as light, indicating His divine glory. The use of snow, a natural element, underscores the purity and perfection of the heavenly realm.

Teaching Points
Divine Presence and Power
The angel's appearance as "like lightning" signifies the power and majesty of God. Believers are reminded of the awe-inspiring nature of God's presence and His ability to intervene powerfully in human history.

Purity and Holiness
The description of the angel's clothes as "white as snow" symbolizes purity and holiness. Christians are called to pursue holiness in their own lives, reflecting the purity of Christ.

Assurance of the Resurrection
The angel's presence at the tomb serves as a divine confirmation of Jesus' resurrection. This event assures believers of the truth of the resurrection and the hope of eternal life.

Fear and Reverence
The reaction to the angel's appearance often includes fear and reverence. Believers are encouraged to approach God with a sense of reverence and awe, recognizing His holiness and power.

What Does Matthew 28:3 Mean
His appearance was like lightning

Matthew reports, “His appearance was like lightning”. The description is straightforward: the angel literally blazed with sudden, overwhelming brilliance.

• Lightning is a visible sign of God’s power and presence. When the Lord descended on Sinai, “there were thunder and lightning flashes” (Exodus 19:16). The same dazzling quality now surrounds the heavenly messenger who announces the risen Christ.

• Daniel saw a similar figure: “his face like the brilliance of lightning” (Daniel 10:6), underscoring continuity between Old Testament visions and this New Testament event.

• Lightning is fast, uncontrollable, and awe-inspiring; its very nature provokes reverent fear. The guards respond exactly that way in the next verse, trembling and becoming “like dead men.”

• The image also anticipates the glorified Christ, whose return will be “like lightning that flashes and lights up the sky” (Luke 17:24). The resurrection morning therefore foreshadows the final victory still to come.

and his clothes were white as snow

The sentence continues, “and his clothes were white as snow.” Again, the language is literal: pure whiteness that no earthly laundering could achieve.

• White garments signal holiness and absolute purity. Daniel saw the Ancient of Days: “His clothing was white as snow” (Daniel 7:9). The angel shares the spotless character of the God he serves.

• At Jesus’ transfiguration “His clothes became radiant, exceedingly white” (Mark 9:3). The same dazzling purity that once surrounded the Son now surrounds the messenger who proclaims the Son’s victory over death.

• Revelation pictures the redeemed “clothed in white” (Revelation 3:5), linking spotless garments with victory and righteousness. The angel’s attire announces that Christ’s triumph has secured true purity for His people.

• Snow suggests freshness and cleansing; Isaiah declares, “Though your sins are scarlet, they shall be as white as snow” (Isaiah 1:18). The resurrection signals that such cleansing is now accomplished in Christ.

summary

Matthew 28:3 presents a literal, eye-witness glimpse of a heavenly messenger whose blazing face and snow-white robe testify to God’s power, purity, and victory. The lightning-like appearance mirrors the awesome presence of God at Sinai and prefigures Christ’s glorious return, while the spotless clothing proclaims the holiness and cleansing made certain by the resurrection. Together these details assure us that God has decisively acted, the tomb is empty, and the risen Savior reigns in unmatched splendor.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(3) Like lightning.--The word employed by St. Luke to describe the "raiment has the same force. The "white as snow" has its counterpart in the record of the Transfiguration (Mark 9:3) and the vision of the Ancient of Days in Daniel 7:9.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 3. - His countenance (ἰδέα, appearance) was like lightning. The angel's aspect was as bright and startling as the flash of lightning (comp. Ezekiel 1:14; Daniel 10:6). His raiment white as snow. Pure and glistening, like the effect of the Transfiguration on the Lord (Matthew 18:2; comp. Acts 1:10; Revelation 10:1).
