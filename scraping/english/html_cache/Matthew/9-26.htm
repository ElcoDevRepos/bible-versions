And the news about this
This phrase refers to the miraculous event of Jesus raising the synagogue leader's daughter from the dead, as described earlier in Matthew 9. Such miracles were unprecedented and served as a testament to Jesus' divine authority and power. In the context of first-century Judea, news of miraculous events would spread rapidly by word of mouth, as there were no modern means of communication. This phrase highlights the impact of Jesus' ministry and the growing awareness of His works among the people.

spread throughout
The rapid dissemination of news in ancient times relied heavily on oral communication. The phrase indicates the widespread nature of the news, suggesting that it reached a broad audience. This reflects the communal nature of society at the time, where news would be shared in marketplaces, synagogues, and homes. The spreading of such news would have contributed to the growing crowds that followed Jesus, eager to witness His teachings and miracles.

that region
The region referred to here is likely Galilee, where much of Jesus' early ministry took place. Galilee was a densely populated area with a mix of Jewish and Gentile communities. The spread of news throughout this region underscores the geographical reach of Jesus' influence. Galilee's location, with its proximity to major trade routes, would facilitate the rapid spread of information. This also sets the stage for the broader mission of Jesus, as His fame begins to extend beyond local boundaries, fulfilling prophecies about the Messiah's impact on both Jews and Gentiles.

What Does Matthew 9:26 Mean
And

This simple connector tells us the raising of the synagogue ruler’s daughter (Matthew 9:25) did not end in silence—it led straight into public reaction. Whenever God moves, events do not sit in isolation. See how the same word keeps the action flowing in John 2:11 where “the first of His signs… and His disciples believed in Him,” as well as in Acts 3:10 when healing the lame man sparked immediate recognition.

the news

What circulated was not rumor but a clear report of a literal miracle:

• “He took the girl by the hand, and she got up” (Matthew 9:25).

• People carried firsthand testimony—like the shepherds in Luke 2:17 who “spread the word” after seeing the newborn Messiah.

• Mark 1:28 echoes the pattern: “The news about Jesus spread quickly through the whole region of Galilee.”

about this

The “this” centers on a young girl restored from death, paired with the woman healed of a twelve-year hemorrhage (Matthew 9:18-22). Such layered wonders magnify Christ’s authority:

• Power over disease (Matthew 8:16-17; Isaiah 53:4).

• Power over death (John 11:43-44; 1 Kings 17:22).

• Power expressed in compassion (Luke 7:13–15).

spread

The verb paints movement—unstoppable, organic, fueled by eyewitnesses:

• “The word of the Lord continued to spread and multiply” (Acts 12:24).

• Like seed scattered (Matthew 13:3-9), the report found soil in open hearts.

• Attempts to contain it—such as the leper told to stay quiet in Mark 1:44-45—only accelerated the flow.

throughout

No corner was untouched. The idea mirrors Matthew 4:24: “News about Him spread all over Syria.” God’s acts are never intended for a private inner circle; they radiate outward:

• Psalm 145:6-7 praises declaring His “awesome works.”

• Revelation 5:12-13 envisions global, even cosmic, proclamation of the Lamb’s worth.

that region

Galilee, dotted with fishing villages and trade routes, functioned as a cultural crossroads. Spreading “throughout that region” positioned multitudes to hear, believe, and seek Jesus:

• Matthew 14:35 records all Galilee recognizing Him and bringing their sick.

• Isaiah 9:1 foretold light dawning “in Galilee of the nations,” now fulfilled in these cascading reports.

summary

Matthew 9:26 shows the inevitable ripple effect of Christ’s miraculous work: eyewitnesses carry the story; the report races unhindered; whole territories awaken to the presence and power of Jesus. The verse invites confidence that what God literally does cannot be contained—good news is meant to travel, stir faith, and ready hearts for the Savior who still reigns today.
