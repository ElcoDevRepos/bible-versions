When Arphaxad was 35 years old
Arphaxad is a descendant of Noah, specifically the son of Shem, who is one of Noah's three sons. This genealogy is significant as it traces the lineage from Noah to Abraham, establishing the Semitic line. The age of 35 is noteworthy as it reflects the post-flood decrease in human lifespan, which is a theme seen throughout the genealogies in Genesis. This decrease in lifespan is often interpreted as a result of the changing conditions on Earth after the flood.

he became the father of Shelah
Shelah is an important figure in the genealogical line leading to Abraham, and subsequently to the nation of Israel. The name Shelah means "request" or "petition," which may reflect cultural or familial expectations at the time. This lineage is also mentioned in the genealogy of Jesus Christ in the Gospel of Luke (Luke 3:35-36), highlighting its significance in the messianic line. The continuity of this line emphasizes God's faithfulness in preserving the lineage through which He would fulfill His promises to Abraham and ultimately bring about redemption through Christ.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Arphaxad
A descendant of Noah, specifically the son of Shem. He is part of the genealogical line leading to Abraham, which is significant in the biblical account as it traces the lineage of the Israelites and ultimately Jesus Christ.

2. Shelah
The son of Arphaxad. His mention in the genealogy highlights the continuation of the line from Shem, emphasizing the fulfillment of God's promises through generations.

3. Genealogy
This event is part of the genealogical record in Genesis, which serves to connect the post-flood world to the patriarchs, particularly Abraham, and to demonstrate God's ongoing plan for humanity.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Genealogy in Scripture
Genealogies in the Bible are not mere historical records; they demonstrate God's faithfulness in fulfilling His promises through specific family lines.

God's Sovereignty in History
The continuation of the line from Shem to Abraham shows God's sovereign hand in guiding history according to His divine plan.

Faithfulness Across Generations
The mention of Arphaxad and Shelah reminds us of the importance of faithfulness in our own families, encouraging us to pass down our faith to future generations.

The Connection to Christ
Understanding the genealogical line leading to Jesus helps us appreciate the continuity of God's salvation plan from the Old Testament to the New Testament.

Trust in God's Timing
The genealogies remind us that God's promises may take time to unfold, but they are always fulfilled in His perfect timing.

What Does Genesis 11:12 Mean
When

Genesis situates this event in the unfolding years after the Flood (cf. Genesis 11:10). The narrative’s time-marker reminds us that God’s plan moves forward in real history, one generation after another—never late, never early (cf. Psalm 145:13).

Arphaxad

• Grandson of Noah through Shem (Genesis 10:22).

• Part of the righteous line through which God will eventually send the Messiah (Luke 3:35-36).

• His inclusion shows God preserving a faithful remnant despite scattering at Babel (Genesis 11:9).

Was 35 years old

The specific age underscores literal chronology, anchoring the genealogy in measurable years (cf. Genesis 5:32). God values details; He records them to strengthen our confidence that these are real people in real time (cf. Numbers 23:19).

He became the father

• Parenthood is God’s chosen means to extend the covenant line (Genesis 1:28; Psalm 127:3-5).

• Each birth testifies that God’s promise to crush the serpent’s head (Genesis 3:15) is still advancing through earthly families.

• The phrase marks a new link in the unbroken chain leading to Abraham (Genesis 12:1-3).

Shelah

• His name reappears when the inspired writers trace Jesus’ ancestry (Luke 3:35).

• Through Shelah’s line, God will bring forth Eber, Peleg, and ultimately Abram, demonstrating divine faithfulness across centuries (Genesis 11:14-26).

• Though otherwise unremarkable, Shelah’s mention shows that every life in God’s story matters (cf. 1 Corinthians 1:27-29).

summary

Genesis 11:12 is far more than a genealogical footnote. It testifies that God works through ordinary people, precise times, and family lines to fulfill extraordinary promises, moving one step closer to the redemption accomplished in Christ.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 12, 13. - And Arphaxad lived five and thirty years (the first indication of a change having transpired upon human life after the Flood, the average age of paternity prior to that event being 117, the earliest 65, and the latest 187), and begat Salah. Shalach, literally, emission, or the sending forth, of water, a memorial of the Flood (Bochart); or of an arrow or dart (vide Genesis 10:24). And Arphaxad lived after he begat Salah four hundred and three years (making a total of 438, i.e. 339 years less than the youngest complete life in the prediluvian table, - Enoch's, of course, being excepted, and 162 less than Shem's: a second indication of the shortening of the period of existence), and begat sons and daughters.
