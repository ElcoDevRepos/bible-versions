Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
What if there are fifty righteous ones in the city?
This phrase is part of Abraham's intercession for Sodom, demonstrating his role as a mediator. The number fifty is significant, representing a substantial minority that could influence the moral standing of the city. The concept of "righteous ones" is central to biblical theology, emphasizing the importance of a faithful remnant. This echoes the idea found in other scriptures, such as Noah's family being spared during the flood (Genesis 6-9) and the remnant of Israel (Isaiah 10:20-22).

Will You really sweep it away
The phrase "sweep it away" suggests total destruction, akin to the flood narrative. It reflects the severity of divine judgment against sin, a theme consistent throughout the Bible. This anticipates the eventual destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah, serving as a warning of the consequences of widespread unrighteousness.

and not spare the place
The concept of sparing is linked to God's mercy and justice. Abraham appeals to God's character, suggesting that the presence of the righteous should lead to mercy. This reflects the biblical tension between justice and mercy, seen in passages like Exodus 34:6-7, where God is described as both just and merciful.

for the sake of the fifty righteous ones who are there?
This phrase highlights the potential influence of the righteous within a community. It suggests that the presence of the righteous can bring blessings or preservation to others, a principle seen in Joseph's story in Egypt (Genesis 39:5) and the New Testament teaching of believers as "salt and light" (Matthew 5:13-16). The intercession here can be seen as a type of Christ, who intercedes on behalf of humanity (Hebrews 7:25).

Teaching Points
The Power of Intercession
Abraham's dialogue with God highlights the importance of interceding for others. Believers are called to pray for their communities and nations, trusting in God's mercy and justice.

God's Justice and Mercy
This passage reveals God's willingness to spare the wicked for the sake of the righteous. It encourages believers to trust in God's perfect balance of justice and mercy.

The Influence of the Righteous
The presence of righteous individuals can have a profound impact on their surroundings. Christians are encouraged to live righteously and influence their communities positively.

The Seriousness of Sin
Sodom's impending judgment serves as a warning about the consequences of sin. Believers are reminded to pursue holiness and avoid complacency in a sinful world.

Faith in God's Character
Abraham's boldness in negotiating with God is rooted in his understanding of God's character. Christians are encouraged to deepen their knowledge of God to strengthen their faith and prayer life.

What Does Genesis 18:24 Mean
What if there are fifty righteous ones in the city?

• Abraham, standing before the LORD, dares to suggest a very specific number—“fifty”—showing that righteousness is not theoretical but countable and observable (Genesis 18:22–23).

• By framing the request as “What if…?” he is probing God’s revealed character, convinced that the Judge of all the earth acts consistently (Genesis 18:25).

• The “righteous” are those who live in reverent faith (Hebrews 11:7; James 2:23). Their presence matters. Jeremiah heard a similar challenge—if even one man sought justice, Jerusalem could be spared (Jeremiah 5:1).

• This opening question sets a benchmark that will soon descend to ten, emphasizing both God’s readiness to save and the tragic scarcity of the faithful (Ezekiel 22:30).

Will You really sweep it away

• “Sweep it away” echoes God’s earlier judgment language at the flood (“I will wipe out mankind,” Genesis 6:7), underscoring the seriousness of sin in Sodom and Gomorrah (Genesis 18:20–21).

• Abraham recognizes that divine wrath is not hasty or capricious; he still asks, because he trusts God’s justice yet pleads for mercy (Psalm 89:14).

• In asking, he illustrates the believer’s role as intercessor (1 Timothy 2:1), standing in the gap much like Moses later would for Israel (Exodus 32:11–14).

and not spare the place

• “Spare” points to God’s willingness to show covenant mercy even to the undeserving when righteousness is present (Proverbs 11:10).

• God’s readiness to spare an entire region for a remnant shows that the righteous are a preservative force—what Jesus later calls “the salt of the earth” (Matthew 5:13).

• This element anticipates prophetic promises that cities can be delivered through repentance and the presence of godly people, as seen in Nineveh’s reprieve (Jonah 3:10).

for the sake of the fifty righteous ones who are there?

• Abraham hinges his plea on substitutionary blessing: judgment withheld “for the sake of” others. This principle culminates in Christ, whose righteousness saves all who trust Him (Isaiah 53:11; Romans 5:19).

• The verse teaches corporate accountability: the unrighteous survive because of the righteous, but if the righteous are absent, judgment falls (2 Peter 2:6–9).

• It also underscores God’s meticulous fairness: He distinguishes individuals within communities, never condemning the righteous with the wicked (Genesis 18:25; Psalm 34:15).

summary

Genesis 18:24 reveals Abraham’s bold yet humble intercession, rooted in confidence that God’s justice and mercy coexist perfectly. By testing whether fifty righteous people could shield an entire city, the text teaches that righteous presence restrains judgment, highlights God’s eagerness to spare, and foreshadows the ultimate righteousness of Christ who secures salvation “for the sake of” all who believe.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 24. - Peradventure there be fifty righteous within the city. A charitable supposition, as the event showed, though at first sight it might not appear so to Abraham; and the bare Possibility of Sodom's - not Sodom alone (Kalisch), but the Pentapolis - containing so many good men was enough to afford a basis for the argument which followed. Wilt thou also destroy and not spare - literally, take away (sc. the iniquity) i.e. remove the punishment from - the place (not the godly portion of the city merely, but the entire population; a complete discovery of Abraham s design) for the fifty righteous that are therein?
