Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Then Abraham said, “May the Lord not be angry, but let me speak further.
Abraham's approach to God is marked by humility and reverence, acknowledging God's sovereignty and righteousness. This reflects the cultural context of the ancient Near East, where approaching a deity required respect and acknowledgment of one's own unworthiness. Abraham's intercession is a type of Christ, who intercedes on behalf of humanity (Hebrews 7:25).

Suppose thirty are found there?”
Abraham continues his negotiation with God, demonstrating his deep concern for the righteous in Sodom. This reflects the biblical principle of God's justice and mercy, as seen in Ezekiel 18:23, where God expresses no pleasure in the death of the wicked. The number thirty, while arbitrary, signifies Abraham's hope that a small remnant could avert judgment, similar to the remnant theology found throughout Scripture (Isaiah 10:20-22).

He replied, “If I find thirty there, I will not do it.”
God's response highlights His willingness to spare the city for the sake of the righteous, underscoring His mercy and justice. This is consistent with God's character as revealed throughout the Bible, where He is patient and desires repentance (2 Peter 3:9). The dialogue between God and Abraham foreshadows the ultimate intercession of Christ, who secures salvation for believers through His righteousness (Romans 5:9-10).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Abraham
The patriarch of the Israelites, known for his faith and obedience to God. In this passage, he is interceding for the city of Sodom.

2. The Lord
God, who is engaging in a dialogue with Abraham regarding the fate of Sodom and Gomorrah.

3. Sodom
A city known for its wickedness, which God plans to destroy due to its sinfulness.

4. Intercession
The act of Abraham pleading with God to spare the city if righteous people can be found there.

5. Divine Mercy
The theme of God's willingness to spare the city for the sake of the righteous, highlighting His mercy and justice.
Teaching Points
The Power of Intercession
Abraham's dialogue with God shows the importance and power of intercessory prayer. Believers are encouraged to pray for others, trusting in God's mercy and justice.

God's Justice and Mercy
This passage illustrates the balance between God's justice in dealing with sin and His mercy in sparing the righteous. Christians are reminded of the importance of living righteously.

Boldness in Prayer
Abraham's respectful yet bold approach to God encourages believers to approach God with confidence, knowing that He listens to our prayers.

The Value of Righteousness
The potential sparing of Sodom for the sake of a few righteous individuals underscores the impact that a righteous life can have on a community.

God's Patience
God's willingness to engage in dialogue with Abraham shows His patience and desire for relationship with His people.

What Does Genesis 18:30 Mean
Then Abraham said

• Abraham continues the conversation begun in Genesis 18:23, where he first interceded for Sodom.

• His willingness to press on shows both bold faith and a shepherd’s heart (compare Exodus 32:11-14).

• Scripture often highlights the power of one righteous person’s plea (James 5:16; 1 Timothy 2:1).

May the Lord not be angry, but let me speak further

• Abraham’s humility is unmistakable—he acknowledges God’s absolute right to judge, yet he appeals to grace.

• Similar reverent boldness appears in Moses’ petitions (Numbers 14:18-19) and in David’s cries for mercy (Psalm 51:1-4).

• The phrase reminds us that respectful, persistent prayer delights God (Luke 11:8-10).

Suppose thirty are found there?

• Abraham lowers the number from forty to thirty (Genesis 18:29-30), inching toward the reality of Sodom’s wickedness.

• Each reduction underlines the scarcity of righteousness in the city and the depth of God’s patience (2 Peter 3:9).

• The pattern anticipates Jesus’ parable of the persistent widow, encouraging believers never to give up in intercession (Luke 18:1-8).

He replied, “If I find thirty there, I will not do it.”

• God’s answer confirms His willingness to spare the many for the sake of the few, reflecting His just yet merciful nature (Jeremiah 5:1; Ezekiel 22:30).

• The conditional “if” underscores personal responsibility: genuine righteousness has communal impact (Proverbs 11:10-11).

• Ultimately, the verse foreshadows the gospel—judgment withheld because of the presence of the righteous One, Christ (Romans 5:9-10).

summary

Genesis 18:30 reveals a gracious God who invites bold, humble intercession. Abraham models reverence without resignation, steadily appealing to the Lord’s mercy. God, in turn, shows that even a small remnant of righteousness can stay His hand of judgment, a truth fulfilled perfectly in Jesus, whose righteousness secures deliverance for all who believe.
