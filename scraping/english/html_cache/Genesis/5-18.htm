When Jared was 162 years old
The genealogies in Genesis 5 provide a timeline from Adam to Noah, emphasizing the longevity of early humans. Jared's age at the time of Enoch's birth reflects the extended lifespans before the Flood, which some interpret as a sign of a different pre-Flood environment. This longevity is often seen as a testament to the vitality of early humanity and the effects of sin on creation over time. Jared's age also fits into the broader genealogical framework that connects Adam to Noah, underscoring the continuity of God's plan through generations.

he became the father of Enoch
Enoch is a significant figure in biblical history, known for his close relationship with God. Unlike others in the genealogy, Enoch's life is marked by his "walk with God," and he is one of only two people in the Bible who did not experience death, the other being Elijah. Enoch's life prefigures the concept of eternal life and the hope of resurrection. His story is further expanded in the New Testament, where he is mentioned in Hebrews 11:5 as an example of faith. Enoch's prophetic role is also highlighted in Jude 1:14-15, where he is said to have prophesied about the coming judgment, linking him to themes of righteousness and divine justice.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jared
A descendant of Seth, Jared is part of the genealogy that leads to Noah. His name in Hebrew, (Yered), is often associated with the idea of descent or coming down, which may symbolize the continuation of the human lineage.

2. Enoch
The son of Jared, Enoch is a significant figure in biblical history known for his close relationship with God. He is one of the few people in the Bible who did not experience death in the traditional sense, as God took him away.

3. Genealogy
This verse is part of the genealogical record in Genesis 5, which traces the lineage from Adam to Noah. It highlights the continuity of God's creation and the unfolding of His plan through generations.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Lineage
The genealogies in Genesis remind us of the importance of family and heritage in God's plan. Each generation plays a role in the unfolding of His purposes.

Walking with God
Enoch's life, as introduced in this genealogy, sets a precedent for living a life that is pleasing to God. It challenges us to consider our own walk with the Lord.

Faith and Legacy
Jared's role as the father of Enoch highlights the impact of faith and obedience on future generations. Our spiritual legacy can influence those who come after us.

God’s Sovereignty in History
The genealogies demonstrate God's sovereign hand in history, orchestrating events and lives according to His divine plan.

What Does Genesis 5:18 Mean
When Jared was

- We step into the ongoing genealogy of Genesis 5, where each entry begins with a time marker that firmly roots the narrative in real history (see Genesis 5:3, 6, 9).

- This continues the line of Seth, the “other seed” given after Abel’s death (Genesis 4:25–26), underscoring God’s faithful preservation of a righteous lineage that would ultimately lead to the Messiah (Luke 3:38).

- The wording signals a normal pattern of life unfolding under God’s providence, echoing the blessing to “be fruitful and multiply” first given in Genesis 1:28.

162 years old

- The long lifespans in Genesis 5 are recorded as literal years, showing humanity’s vigor before the flood (Genesis 6:3).

- Such longevity magnifies God’s original design for a lengthy, fruitful existence and highlights how sin, though already present, had not yet produced the sharply shortened lifespans seen after the flood (Genesis 11:10–25; Psalm 90:10).

- The precise number reminds us that God tracks every season of life. Scripture often links righteousness with prolonged days (Exodus 20:12; Proverbs 3:1–2), and Jared’s length of life fits that pattern.

He became the father of

- Parenthood in Genesis 5 is more than biology; it is covenant succession. Each generation receives, preserves, and passes along the knowledge of the Lord (Deuteronomy 6:6–7).

- The phrase emphasizes God’s ongoing creative activity, still at work through ordinary family life even after the fall (Psalm 127:3).

- It also marks a new phase: with each birth the hope of the promised Deliverer (Genesis 3:15) is kept alive.

Enoch

- Enoch immediately stands out in the genealogy because “Enoch walked with God” and “God took him” (Genesis 5:22–24).

- His later translation to heaven without seeing death prefigures the believer’s ultimate victory over death (Hebrews 11:5; 1 Corinthians 15:51–52).

- Jude 14–15 records Enoch’s prophetic witness against ungodliness, showing that the godly line was not silent but testified to the truth in a corrupt world.

- By naming Enoch here, the verse sets the stage for a striking contrast: long-lived Jared fathers a son whose life, though shorter, is marked by intimate fellowship with God—reminding us that quality of walk outweighs quantity of years.

summary

Genesis 5:18 grounds us in real time, real ages, and real people, spotlighting God’s faithfulness to extend the godly line through Jared to the remarkable Enoch. The verse quietly but powerfully affirms that each generation is part of God’s unfolding plan, longevity is a gift in His hands, and every birth carries forward the promise of redemption until Christ comes.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(18) Jared.--Heb., Yered. This name is supposed to mean the descent, especially of water. Hence many have endeavoured to show that he is the Indian water-god Varuna; but competent modern commentators regard all such Aryan expositions as exploded. Mr. Sayce tells us that the word in Assyrian means servant (Chald. Gen. 311), but this is not quite satisfactory. Until, however, this very ancient Semitic dialect is thoroughly explored, we are scarcely in a position to speak with certainty as to these old names.

Further, he was 162 years of age when he begat Enoch. It is probable from this that Enoch was not the eldest son, but that the birthright became his because of his special excellencies. It is also to be observed that Enoch holds the seventh place from Adam, seven being the number of perfection; that he attains to the highest rank among the patriarchs; and that he passes over into immortality without death.
