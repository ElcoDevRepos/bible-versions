Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
And Noah and his wife, with his sons and their wives
This phrase highlights the family unit that God chose to preserve through the flood. Noah, a righteous man in a corrupt generation, is accompanied by his immediate family. This reflects the biblical principle of household salvation seen in Acts 16:31, where the faith of one can lead to the salvation of the family. The inclusion of Noah's wife, sons, and their wives emphasizes the importance of family in God's plan and the continuation of humanity post-flood. It also underscores the concept of covenant, as God made a covenant with Noah and his descendants (Genesis 9:9).

entered the ark
The act of entering the ark signifies obedience to God's command. The ark itself is a type of Christ, providing salvation and refuge from judgment. Just as Noah and his family were saved by entering the ark, believers are saved by being "in Christ" (2 Corinthians 5:17). The ark, made of gopher wood and sealed with pitch, symbolizes the atoning work of Christ, who covers and protects believers from the wrath of God. The entry into the ark marks a transition from the old world to a new beginning, paralleling the believer's transition from the old life to new life in Christ.

to escape the waters of the flood
The floodwaters represent God's judgment on a sinful world, as described in Genesis 6:5-7. The flood is both a literal historical event and a foreshadowing of future judgment, as seen in 2 Peter 3:6-7, where the world is reserved for fire. The escape from the floodwaters is a picture of salvation, where God provides a way of escape from His righteous judgment. This also connects to the theme of baptism in 1 Peter 3:20-21, where the floodwaters symbolize the cleansing and new life that comes through faith in Christ. The flood narrative serves as a reminder of God's justice and mercy, offering salvation to those who trust and obey Him.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Noah
A righteous man chosen by God to survive the flood and preserve human and animal life. His obedience and faith are central to this account.

2. Noah's Sons
Shem, Ham, and Japheth, who, along with their wives, were part of God's plan to repopulate the earth after the flood.

3. Noah's Wife and Sons' Wives
These women are part of the family unit that God preserved, highlighting the importance of family in God's plan.

4. The Ark
A massive vessel built according to God's specifications, serving as a means of salvation from the floodwaters.

5. The Flood
A cataclysmic event sent by God to cleanse the earth of its widespread wickedness, sparing only Noah and his family.
Teaching Points
Obedience to God's Command
Noah's entry into the ark demonstrates his obedience to God's command. Believers are called to trust and obey God's instructions, even when they may not fully understand His plans.

Family as a Unit of Salvation
The inclusion of Noah's family in the ark underscores the importance of family in God's redemptive plan. Christians are encouraged to lead their families in faith and righteousness.

God's Provision and Protection
The ark symbolizes God's provision and protection for those who are faithful. Believers can trust that God will provide a way through life's challenges.

Judgment and Mercy
The flood represents God's judgment on sin, but also His mercy in providing a means of salvation. This duality is a reminder of the seriousness of sin and the grace available through Christ.

What Does Genesis 7:7 Mean
And Noah and his wife

- Scripture first makes mention of Noah together with his wife, underscoring that obedience to God begins at home.

- The covenant promise given in Genesis 6:18 is being fulfilled: “But I will establish My covenant with you, and you will enter the ark—you and your sons and your wife and your sons’ wives with you.”

- Their unity models the one-flesh design of Genesis 2:24 and reminds us that faithfulness to God is lived out in marriage.

- Hebrews 11:7 adds heaven’s commentary: “By faith Noah, when warned about things not yet seen, in holy fear built an ark to save his family.” Their entry is an act of faith, not mere survival instinct.

with his sons and their wives

- The inclusion of Shem, Ham, Japheth, and their wives shows God’s concern for entire households (compare Genesis 9:1; Acts 16:31).

- Eight people (1 Peter 3:20) preserve the human line; judgment is real, yet grace provides a remnant.

- Family lines matter to God: from this preserved family will flow the nations (Genesis 10) and, ultimately, the Redeemer (Luke 3:36).

- This detail also highlights parental influence: Noah’s faith shaped the next generation, fulfilling the principle that “if anyone does not provide for his own… he has denied the faith” (1 Timothy 5:8).

entered the ark

- Faith moves from belief to action. God had already given the invitation—“Then the LORD said to Noah, ‘Come into the ark, you and all your household’” (Genesis 7:1).

- The ark is a physical picture of salvation: a single door (Genesis 6:16) foreshadows Jesus’ claim, “I am the door; if anyone enters through Me, he will be saved” (John 10:9).

- Stepping inside separates Noah’s family from a perishing world, illustrating 2 Corinthians 6:17, “Come out from among them and be separate.”

- The timing matters: once God shut the door (Genesis 7:16), opportunity outside was over. Obedience is urgent, not optional.

to escape the waters of the flood

- The flood is literal judgment on pervasive sin (Genesis 6:5–7). Deliverance comes only God’s way.

- 2 Peter 2:5 calls Noah a “preacher of righteousness,” showing that while judgment fell, mercy had been proclaimed.

- The water both destroys and saves—a paradox explained in 1 Peter 3:20–21. Just as the ark lifted believers above wrath, Christ now saves through His finished work symbolized in baptism.

- Psalm 32:6 echoes the principle: “Therefore let everyone who is godly pray to You while You may be found; surely when great waters rise, they will not reach him”.

summary

Genesis 7:7 records a decisive moment of faith. Noah, responding to God’s covenant promise, brings his entire family into the refuge God provided. Their united obedience, entering the ark before judgment falls, displays the pattern of salvation: God warns, provides a way, and calls for immediate, family-wide trust.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 7. - And Noah went in. I.e. began to go in a full week before the waters came (vide ver. 10). "A proof of faith and a warning to the world." And his sons, and his wife, and his sons' wives with him. In all eight persons (1 Peter 3:20); whence it is obvious that "each had but one wife, and that polygamy, as it began among the Cainites, was most probably confined to them" (Poole). Into the ark, because of the waters of the flood. Literally, from the face of the waters, being moved with fear and impelled by faith (Hebrews 11:7).
