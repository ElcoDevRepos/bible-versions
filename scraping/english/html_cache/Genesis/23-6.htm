Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Listen to us, sir.
This phrase indicates a respectful address to Abraham by the Hittites, acknowledging his status and the importance of the negotiation. The use of "sir" shows deference, which is significant given Abraham's status as a foreigner in the land. This respect reflects the fulfillment of God's promise to make Abraham's name great (Genesis 12:2).

You are God’s chosen one among us.
The Hittites recognize Abraham as a man blessed by God, which aligns with God's covenant with Abraham, promising to make him a great nation and bless him (Genesis 12:2-3). This acknowledgment by the local people highlights Abraham's reputation and the visible evidence of God's favor upon him. It also foreshadows the future recognition of Israel as God's chosen people.

Bury your dead in the finest of our tombs.
The offer to bury Sarah in the finest tombs indicates the high regard the Hittites have for Abraham. It also reflects the cultural practice of honoring the dead with proper burial rites. This gesture of generosity underscores the peaceful coexistence and mutual respect between Abraham and the Hittites, despite his status as a sojourner.

None of us will withhold his tomb for burying your dead.
This statement emphasizes the willingness of the Hittites to accommodate Abraham's needs, showing a communal respect and generosity. It also highlights the cultural importance of burial practices in ancient times, where securing a burial site was a significant concern. This act of kindness can be seen as a fulfillment of God's promise to give Abraham and his descendants the land, as it begins with the acquisition of a burial site for Sarah.

Despite being a foreigner, Abraham's status as a "mighty prince" shows God's faithfulness to His promises. Trust in God's timing and faithfulness in your life.

What Does Genesis 23:6 Mean
Listen to us, sir.

• The Hittites address Abraham respectfully, acknowledging his voice is worth hearing. Compare this courtesy with Proverbs 18:13, which praises listening before responding, and with Romans 12:10, “Outdo one another in showing honor”.

• Their invitation to “listen” signals a public, legal negotiation at the city gate (Genesis 23:10), underscoring that burial arrangements are a community matter, not a private favor.

• By pausing to hear them out, Abraham models the humility of James 1:19—“Everyone should be quick to listen, slow to speak”.

You are God’s chosen one among us.

• The Hittites recognize Abraham’s unique relationship with the LORD, echoing God’s earlier promise: “I will make you into a great nation” (Genesis 12:2–3).

• Their words fulfill Genesis 21:22, where Abimelech acknowledged, “God is with you in all that you do”.

• This unwitting testimony from pagans prefigures 1 Peter 2:12, which urges believers to live so honorably that even unbelievers glorify God.

• The title “chosen one” reminds us that God’s covenant people stand out, just as Israel later was called “a people holy to the LORD” (Deuteronomy 7:6).

Bury your dead in the finest of our tombs.

• Offering prime burial sites shows heartfelt generosity, similar to Joseph securing the choicest land of Goshen for his family (Genesis 47:6).

• Burial signified permanence; by laying Sarah to rest in Canaan, Abraham expresses faith that this territory will indeed belong to his descendants (Hebrews 11:13).

• Respect for the dead resonates with Ecclesiastes 7:2, which says visiting a house of mourning teaches wisdom.

None of us will withhold his tomb for burying your dead.

• The Hittites promise unanimous cooperation, reflecting a community pledge akin to Ruth 1:16—“Where you die, I will die, and there I will be buried”.

• Yet Abraham refuses a gift and insists on paying full price (Genesis 23:9, 13). Like David who declined to offer God “burnt offerings that cost me nothing” (2 Samuel 24:24), Abraham wants a clear, uncontested title to the land.

• Their openhandedness fulfills God’s assurance in Proverbs 16:7: “When a man’s ways please the LORD, He makes even his enemies to be at peace with him”.

summary

Genesis 23:6 shows pagan neighbors publicly honoring Abraham, testifying that God is with him, and offering their best resources for Sarah’s burial. Their respect validates God’s covenant promises, reveals Abraham’s reputation for righteousness, and foreshadows the permanent inheritance of the land. The episode teaches the value of listening, honoring God’s people, generous hospitality, and acting with integrity in all dealings.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(6) A mighty prince.--Heb., a prince of God. Comp. "wind of God" (Genesis 1:2); "wrestlings of God" (Genesis 30:8); "mountains of God" (Psalm 36:6); "cedars of God" (Psalm 80:10). So also "a sleep of Jehovah" for a deep sleep (1Samuel 26:12).

In the choice of our sepulchres.--The interview between Abraham and the Hittites is marked by the utmost courtesy on both sides, but it is a mistake to suppose that this acceptance of the patriarch's proposal contained the idea that he might select a sepulchre without paying for it. The payment, in true Oriental fashion, is kept in the background, but is pre-supposed on both sides. After the acceptance of his proposal, it was Abraham's turn to name the burying-place he wished, and the owner next consents, but while treating the purchase-money as a matter of small importance, he nevertheless asks a very high price, to which Abraham at once consents. . . .
