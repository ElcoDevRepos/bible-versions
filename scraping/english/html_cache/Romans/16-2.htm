Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Welcome her in the Lord
This phrase emphasizes the Christian practice of hospitality, rooted in the teachings of Jesus and the early church. Welcoming someone "in the Lord" suggests a spiritual kinship and unity among believers. It reflects the idea that all Christians are part of the body of Christ (1 Corinthians 12:27) and should treat each other with love and respect. The early church often met in homes, and hospitality was crucial for the spread of the Gospel (Acts 2:46-47).

in a manner worthy of the saints
The term "saints" refers to all believers, set apart for God’s purposes. This phrase calls for a reception that reflects the dignity and honor due to those who are part of God's family. It echoes the call to live a life worthy of the calling received (Ephesians 4:1). The early church valued mutual respect and support, as seen in the communal living described in Acts 4:32-35.

and assist her with anything she may need from you
This instruction highlights the importance of practical support within the Christian community. The early church was known for its generosity and care for one another (Galatians 6:10). The phrase suggests a readiness to provide for physical, emotional, and spiritual needs, reflecting the teachings of Jesus to love one another (John 13:34-35).

For she has been a great help to many people
This acknowledges the significant contributions of the individual being referred to, likely Phoebe, mentioned in Romans 16:1. Her role as a helper or benefactor indicates she may have been a person of means or influence, using her resources to support the church. This aligns with the biblical principle of stewardship and using one's gifts for the kingdom (1 Peter 4:10).

including me
Paul's personal acknowledgment of her help underscores the interconnectedness of the early Christian community. It reflects the apostle's gratitude and recognition of the vital role that women played in the ministry. This personal note adds authenticity and warmth to the letter, showing that the work of the Gospel was a collaborative effort (Philippians 4:3).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Phoebe
A servant of the church in Cenchreae, Phoebe is commended by Paul for her service and assistance to many, including himself. She is likely the bearer of the letter to the Romans.

2. Cenchreae
A port city near Corinth, Cenchreae was an important location for trade and travel in the ancient world. It was home to a Christian community where Phoebe served.

3. Paul
The apostle who wrote the letter to the Romans, commending Phoebe to the church in Rome and urging them to support her.
Teaching Points
Welcoming Fellow Believers
As Paul instructs the Romans to welcome Phoebe, we are reminded of the importance of hospitality and support within the Christian community. This includes recognizing and honoring the contributions of others.

Service and Support
Phoebe's example as a helper to many, including Paul, underscores the value of service in the church. We are called to assist and support those who labor for the gospel.

Recognition of Women's Ministry
Phoebe's role as a servant and helper highlights the significant contributions of women in the early church. This encourages us to acknowledge and support the diverse roles women play in ministry today.

Living Worthy of the Saints
The phrase "in a manner worthy of the saints" challenges us to live in a way that reflects our calling as believers, showing love, respect, and support to one another.

What Does Romans 16:2 Mean
Welcome her in the Lord

Paul’s opening charge is personal and warm. “Welcome” is more than a polite hello; it calls for embracing Phoebe as family because she is “in the Lord.”

• Acts 18:27 shows believers in Achaia welcoming Apollos and encouraging him.

• Philippians 2:29 urges, “Welcome him in the Lord with great joy.”

• When we recognize another believer’s union with Christ (Galatians 3:28), we respond with love that supersedes social, ethnic, or gender distinctions.

in a manner worthy of the saints

The welcome is to reflect the high calling of God’s people.

• Ephesians 4:1 exhorts us to “walk in a manner worthy of the calling.”

• Colossians 1:10 speaks of “walking worthy of the Lord.”

Practical outworking:

- Treat her with honor, not token courtesy.

- Offer hospitality without hesitation (Hebrews 13:2).

- Guard her reputation and well-being as you would your own (1 Peter 1:22).

and assist her with anything she may need from you

Phoebe’s mission required tangible support. Paul calls for proactive help, not passive good intentions.

• Galatians 6:10: “As we have opportunity, let us do good to everyone, and especially to those who are of the household of faith.”

• James 2:15-16 warns against empty words when a brother or sister lacks daily needs.

Ways the Roman believers could assist:

- Provide lodging, food, and safe guidance through the city.

- Introduce her to key leaders and house churches.

- Supply funds or letters of recommendation (3 John 5-8).

For she has been a great help to many people, including me

Paul highlights Phoebe’s proven record of service as motivation to honor her now.

• Proverbs 11:25: “Whoever refreshes others will be refreshed.”

• 1 Corinthians 16:15-18 commends the household of Stephanas for similar service.

Noteworthy observations:

- Phoebe’s past faithfulness models servant leadership.

- Paul publicly acknowledges her, setting a pattern for encouraging those who labor quietly (1 Thessalonians 5:12-13).

- Gratitude fuels generosity; remembering how others have blessed us spurs us to reciprocate (Philemon 17-20).

summary

Romans 16:2 calls believers to recognize, honor, and actively support fellow servants of Christ. Welcoming “in the Lord” means embracing one another as family. Doing so “in a manner worthy of the saints” elevates our hospitality to reflect God’s holiness. Offering practical assistance meets real needs and enables ministry to flourish. Finally, gratitude for past service compels current generosity. By following this pattern, the church today extends Christ’s love and advances His work just as the Romans were urged to do for Phoebe.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(2) In the Lord.--With the consciousness that you are performing a Christian act, subject to all those serious obligations implied in the name.

As becometh saints.--As Christians ought to receive a fellow-Christian.

Succourer.--Patroness or protectress, in the exercise of her office as deaconess.

Of myself also.--Perhaps in illness.
