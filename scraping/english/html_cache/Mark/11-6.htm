The disciples answered as Jesus had instructed them
This phrase highlights the obedience of the disciples to Jesus' specific instructions. In the context of Mark 11, Jesus had sent two of His disciples to fetch a colt for His entry into Jerusalem, a fulfillment of the prophecy in Zechariah 9:9. The disciples' compliance underscores the importance of following divine guidance, a theme prevalent throughout Scripture. Their obedience also reflects the trust and faith they had in Jesus, knowing that His instructions were purposeful and aligned with God's plan.

and the people gave them permission
This part of the verse demonstrates the divine orchestration of events. The people’s willingness to allow the disciples to take the colt suggests a recognition of Jesus' authority or possibly a divine influence on their hearts. In the cultural context of the time, animals were valuable property, and such a request would not typically be granted without question. This incident also parallels the concept of God preparing the way for His purposes, as seen in other biblical narratives where God moves the hearts of people to fulfill His plans, such as in Exodus 12:36 when the Israelites found favor in the eyes of the Egyptians.

Teaching Points
Obedience to Christ's Instructions
The disciples' actions demonstrate the importance of following Jesus' commands, even when the reasons are not immediately clear.

Trust in Divine Provision
The willingness of the people to let the disciples take the colt shows how God provides what is needed to fulfill His purposes.

Fulfillment of Prophecy
Jesus' entry into Jerusalem on a colt is a direct fulfillment of prophecy, reinforcing the reliability of Scripture and God's sovereign plan.

Humility in Leadership
Jesus' choice of a donkey over a horse symbolizes His peaceful and humble approach to kingship, contrasting with worldly expectations of power.

Faith in Action
The disciples' faith is demonstrated through their actions, reminding believers that faith often requires stepping out in obedience.

What Does Mark 11:6 Mean
The disciples answered

• Picture the scene in Mark 11:5-6: some bystanders see two strangers untying a colt and immediately ask, “Why are you doing that?”. The disciples do not freeze or improvise.

• They simply speak the words Jesus had given them (v. 3). This echoes earlier moments when followers repeated His message verbatim—see Luke 10:16 and John 7:46.

• Their calm, clear reply models:

– Confidence that Jesus’ word is sufficient.

– Readiness to give an answer when questioned (1 Peter 3:15 finds a living illustration here).

As Jesus had instructed them

• The phrase underlines exact obedience. Nothing is added, nothing withheld. Compare the same pattern in Mark 14:16 where the disciples “found everything as Jesus had told them.”

• Throughout Scripture, blessing follows precise compliance with God’s directives—Exodus 12:28, Joshua 6:2-5, John 2:5-10.

• For us, it reinforces:

– Trusting Jesus’ foresight even when details seem ordinary.

– The link between love and obedience—John 14:15, 15:14.

And the people gave them permission

• The bystanders’ sudden cooperation shows Christ’s quiet authority over circumstances. In Luke’s parallel account (Luke 19:32-34) the owners themselves release the animal.

• God often moves human hearts to favor His purposes—Nehemiah 2:4-8; Proverbs 21:1.

• The incident also previews Jesus’ rightful claim as King: even the colt’s owners must yield, just as creation must yield when He calms storms (Mark 4:39) or multiplies bread (Mark 6:41-42).

summary

Mark 11:6 illustrates uncomplicated obedience producing uncomplicated results. The disciples repeat Jesus’ words; strangers grant what is needed; God’s plan for the triumphal entry advances unhindered. When we respond exactly as He directs, He works out the permissions, provisions, and timing that glorify His Son.
