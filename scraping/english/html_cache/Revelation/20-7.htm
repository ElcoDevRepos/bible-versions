When the thousand years are complete
This phrase refers to the end of the millennial reign of Christ, a period described earlier in Revelation 20:1-6. The "thousand years" is often interpreted as a literal or symbolic period of peace and righteousness where Christ reigns with His saints. This concept is rooted in Old Testament prophecies such as Isaiah 11:6-9, which describe a time of peace and harmony. The completion of this period signifies a transition in God's redemptive plan.

Satan will be released
The release of Satan indicates a temporary allowance for evil to once again test humanity. This release serves as a final demonstration of God's justice and the persistent nature of sin in the human heart. It echoes the testing of humanity seen in the Garden of Eden (Genesis 3) and throughout biblical history. The release is part of God's sovereign plan, allowing for the ultimate defeat of evil.

from his prison
Satan's "prison" refers to the abyss where he was bound during the millennium (Revelation 20:1-3). This binding signifies God's control over evil and His ability to restrain it according to His purposes. The imagery of a prison underscores the temporary nature of Satan's confinement and the ultimate authority of God over all spiritual forces. This release sets the stage for the final confrontation between good and evil, leading to Satan's ultimate defeat and the establishment of God's eternal kingdom.

Teaching Points
Understanding the Millennium
The thousand-year reign is a time of Christ's dominion and peace, a foretaste of the eternal kingdom. Believers should live in anticipation of this future hope.

The Reality of Spiritual Warfare
Even after a period of peace, Satan's release reminds us that spiritual warfare is ongoing. Christians must remain vigilant and prepared.

God's Sovereignty and Timing
God's plan unfolds according to His perfect timing. Trusting in His sovereignty helps believers remain steadfast in faith.

The Deceptive Nature of Satan
Satan's release highlights his persistent nature as a deceiver. Believers must be discerning and grounded in truth to resist deception.

The Ultimate Victory of Christ
Despite Satan's temporary release, the ultimate victory belongs to Christ. This assurance should embolden believers to live courageously and faithfully.

What Does Revelation 20:7 Mean
When the thousand years are complete

Revelation 20:7 opens with, “When the thousand years are complete”.

• Scripture treats the thousand-year reign (Revelation 20:4-6) as a literal, defined span in history.

• During this Millennium, Christ rules on earth with perfect justice (Isaiah 2:2-4; Zechariah 14:9).

• Satan is bound throughout those years (Revelation 20:2-3), ensuring a time of unparalleled peace (Isaiah 11:6-9).

• The phrase “are complete” underscores that God has set a fixed, unalterable timetable (Acts 1:7; Galatians 4:4). When God’s calendar reaches the exact end of that thousand-year mark, the next event unfolds precisely as foretold.

Satan will be released

The text continues, “Satan will be released.”

• The devil is a real, personal being (Job 1:6-7; 1 Peter 5:8). His release is not symbolic; it is an actual event in time and space.

• God alone authorizes the release, proving His absolute sovereignty even over evil (Romans 9:17-18; Revelation 17:17).

• Purpose:

– To expose the hearts of those living under Christ’s perfect reign who have never faced external temptation (Revelation 20:8-9).

– To demonstrate the unchanging wickedness of Satan (John 8:44) and the final need for his eternal judgment (Revelation 20:10).

• This moment reminds believers that genuine faith must flow from the heart, not merely from ideal surroundings (Matthew 7:21-23).

from his prison

Revelation 20:7 concludes, “from his prison.”

• His “prison” is the abyss referenced earlier (Revelation 20:1-3), a real place of confinement for fallen angels (Luke 8:31; 2 Peter 2:4).

• The fact that Satan can be locked up and then let out shows he is a creature, not an equal rival to God (Isaiah 14:12-15).

• Even hell’s gates cannot move without divine permission (Revelation 1:18).

• Knowing that Satan’s activity is limited by God encourages believers today to resist him confidently (James 4:7), trusting the Lord who holds the keys.

summary

Revelation 20:7 teaches that after a literal thousand-year reign of Christ, God will purposely release Satan from the abyss to carry out His final plan. The verse highlights God’s precise control over history, the unchangeable evil of the devil, and the necessity of personal faith even in ideal conditions. Ultimately, it sets the stage for Satan’s permanent defeat and the ushering in of the eternal state (Revelation 20:10-15; 21:1-4).

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
THE LOOSING OF SATAN AND HIS FINAL OVERTHROW.

(7) And when the thousand years are expired.--Rather, And when the thousand years have been finished. The binding of Satan implied restraint put upon his power and freedom of action; the loosing means the removing of these restraints. The reign of Christ with His saints has been a witness to the power of our Master over the wicked one. This witness has been an opportunity also to the world. It was the earthly approximation to the ideal picture. It testified how completely "all power in heaven and in earth was given to Christ," and how there lay, therefore, within the reach of men the power of Him who would tread down their true enemies, and turn His hand against their adversaries (Psalm 81:13-14). But the time of opportunity must end. "O that Israel would have hearkened," is a cry that might have its counterpart over the history of earth's lost opportunities. Christendom is planted in the world to be a framework of regenerating power to mankind, just as the Law and its adjuncts were designed to be in Israel. But, as there the old idolatrous influences broke in upon the rule of God's covenant, so here do we find the vision picturing to us how Christendom will be invaded by the influences of the evil one, when mankind has let slip this splendid opportunity of a really golden age.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 7. - And when the thousand years are expired, Satan shall be loosed out of his prison; are finished; that is, the power of the devil having been in principle completely overthrown by our Lord (see on preceding verses), Satan is still permitted to wage war and exercise sway on the earth. "His prison" is the "abyss" of vers. 1, 3 (cf. also ver. 3).
