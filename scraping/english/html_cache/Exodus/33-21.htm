Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
The LORD continued
This phrase indicates an ongoing conversation between God and Moses. It follows a dialogue where Moses seeks assurance of God's presence with Israel. The continuity suggests a deep, personal relationship between God and Moses, highlighting Moses' unique role as a mediator for the Israelites. This ongoing communication is a testament to the intimate relationship God desires with His chosen leaders.

There is a place near Me
This suggests a specific location where God intends to reveal His presence to Moses. The idea of proximity to God is significant, as it implies a place of safety and divine revelation. In biblical terms, being "near" God often symbolizes favor and protection. This also foreshadows the New Testament concept of drawing near to God through Christ (Hebrews 10:22).

where you are to stand
Standing is a posture of readiness and respect. In biblical times, standing before someone, especially a figure of authority, was a sign of attentiveness and reverence. This instruction to stand indicates that Moses is to be prepared for a significant encounter with God, emphasizing the seriousness and sacredness of the moment.

upon a rock
The rock is a symbol of stability and strength throughout Scripture. In this context, it serves as a literal and figurative foundation for Moses as he prepares to witness God's glory. The rock can also be seen as a type of Christ, who is referred to as the spiritual rock in 1 Corinthians 10:4. This connection underscores the idea of Christ as the foundation of faith and the source of spiritual sustenance.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant-keeping God of Israel, who is speaking directly to Moses, offering guidance and assurance of His presence.

2. Moses
The leader of the Israelites, chosen by God to lead His people out of Egypt. In this passage, Moses is seeking assurance of God's presence as he leads the people.

3. The Rock
A specific location designated by God where Moses is to stand. It symbolizes stability, safety, and the presence of God.

4. Mount Sinai
The broader setting of this encounter, where Moses frequently meets with God and receives the Law.

5. The Israelites
The people of God, who are in the wilderness journeying towards the Promised Land, relying on Moses' leadership and God's guidance.
Teaching Points
God's Presence Provides Assurance
Just as God provided a specific place for Moses to stand, He offers us assurance of His presence in our lives. We can find stability and peace in His promises.

The Rock as a Symbol of Christ
The rock in Exodus can be seen as a foreshadowing of Christ, our ultimate foundation. Standing on Christ means relying on His strength and salvation.

Seeking God's Guidance
Moses' interaction with God highlights the importance of seeking divine guidance in our decisions and leadership roles. We should continually seek God's direction in prayer and through His Word.

The Importance of Obedience
Moses' obedience to God's instruction to stand on the rock is a reminder of the blessings that come from following God's commands. Obedience leads to experiencing His presence more fully.

Finding Refuge in God
Just as the rock provided a place of safety for Moses, God offers us refuge in times of trouble. We can trust Him to be our protector and deliverer.

What Does Exodus 33:21 Mean
The LORD continued

“The LORD continued” (Exodus 33:21) reminds us that God is personally carrying on a conversation with Moses. He is not an impersonal force but a covenant-keeping God who speaks, guides, and reveals Himself (cf. Exodus 33:19; Hebrews 1:1-2). His words flow out of the gracious offer He has just made: “My presence will go with you” (Exodus 33:14). This continuing dialogue underscores how God initiates and sustains fellowship:

• The same pattern appeared when “the LORD spoke to Moses face to face, as a man speaks with his friend” (Exodus 33:11).

• Jesus later assures His disciples, “I am with you always” (Matthew 28:20), showing that divine companionship is a constant theme from Sinai to the Great Commission.

“There is a place near Me”

God pinpoints an exact location “near Me.” Closeness to the Almighty is always by invitation, never by presumption:

• Only Moses is summoned to that nearness (Exodus 24:2), yet the principle extends: “Draw near to God, and He will draw near to you” (James 4:8).

• Psalm 73:28 celebrates, “But as for me, it is good to draw near to God,” confirming that proximity to the LORD is both privilege and protection (see also Psalm 91:1).

The phrase makes clear that intimacy with God is spatially real and spiritually vital. He is not distant; He invites His servant into His immediate presence so that revelation can occur.

“where you are to stand”

Moses is commanded to “stand,” not sit or kneel. The posture signals readiness and firmness:

• “Stand firm and you will see the salvation of the LORD” (Exodus 14:13) shows how standing often precedes divine intervention.

• Later, believers are urged, “Having done everything, to stand” (Ephesians 6:13), highlighting active faith, grounded yet alert.

• Standing also implies obedience: Moses must occupy the exact spot God assigns, illustrating that revelation follows submission (cf. 2 Chronicles 20:17).

God’s people do not choose their own footing; they accept the stance He appoints.

“upon a rock”

The rock provides solid footing and safety from the overwhelming glory soon to pass by (Exodus 33:22). All through Scripture, rock imagery points to stability and God Himself:

• “The LORD is my rock, my fortress, and my deliverer” (Psalm 18:2).

• “He is the Rock; His work is perfect” (Deuteronomy 32:4).

• Isaiah 26:4 celebrates the LORD as an “everlasting rock,” while Paul identifies “that rock was Christ” (1 Corinthians 10:4).

• Jesus describes the wise builder who “built his house on the rock” (Matthew 7:24-25), stressing that life founded on God’s word withstands every storm.

By placing Moses on a literal rock, God visually proclaims the spiritual truth that only His provided foundation can support a mortal in the blaze of divine glory.

summary

Exodus 33:21 shows God personally guiding Moses into a secure, intimate, and appointed place so that Moses can safely witness God’s passing glory. Each phrase—God’s continued speech, the nearness to Him, the command to stand, and the firm footing on a rock—reveals that divine revelation is always on God’s terms, in God’s chosen location, and upon God’s unshakable foundation.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(21) There is a place by me.--A place on the summit of Sinai, where God had been manifesting Himself, is clearly intended; but it is impossible to fix the place with any certainty. Speculations like those of Dr. Robinson (Biblical Researches, Vol. 1, p. 153) are of little value.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 21. - Behold, there is a place by me. No sufficient indication is given by these words, or by any other words in Scripture, of the exact locality of the manifestation to Moses. The so-called" traditions "are worthless; and we can only say that the scene was probably some portion of the upper part of the Ras Sufsafeh.
