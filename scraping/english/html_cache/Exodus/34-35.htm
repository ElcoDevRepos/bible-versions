Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
and the Israelites would see that the face of Moses was radiant.
This phrase highlights the visible transformation of Moses after speaking with God. The radiance of Moses' face signifies the glory of God reflected upon him, a result of his intimate communion with the LORD on Mount Sinai. This radiance serves as a testament to the divine authority and authenticity of Moses' leadership and the commandments he received. The Israelites' ability to see this radiance underscores the tangible impact of God's presence. This event parallels the transfiguration of Jesus in the New Testament (Matthew 17:2), where His face shone like the sun, symbolizing divine glory and authority.

So Moses would put the veil back over his face
The use of a veil by Moses indicates a separation between the divine glory and the people, emphasizing the holiness of God and the need for mediation. The veil serves as a protective measure for the Israelites, who were not able to directly behold the glory of God. This act foreshadows the role of Christ as the mediator between God and humanity, as described in 1 Timothy 2:5. The veil also symbolizes the limitations of the Old Covenant, which is later contrasted with the New Covenant in 2 Corinthians 3:13-16, where Paul explains that in Christ, the veil is removed, allowing believers to behold the glory of the Lord with unveiled faces.

until he went in to speak with the LORD.
This phrase indicates the ongoing relationship and communication between Moses and God. It highlights the unique role of Moses as a prophet and leader who had direct access to God, serving as an intermediary for the Israelites. The act of removing the veil when speaking with the LORD signifies the openness and intimacy of Moses' relationship with God, contrasting with the veiled interactions with the people. This direct communication is a precursor to the New Covenant, where believers have direct access to God through Jesus Christ, as seen in Hebrews 4:16, which encourages believers to approach the throne of grace with confidence.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Moses
The central figure in this passage, Moses is the leader of the Israelites and the mediator between God and His people. After spending time in God's presence on Mount Sinai, his face becomes radiant.

2. Israelites
The people of Israel, who are journeying from Egypt to the Promised Land. They witness the radiance of Moses' face and are initially afraid to approach him.

3. Mount Sinai
The mountain where Moses receives the Ten Commandments and where he encounters God, resulting in the transformation of his appearance.

4. The Tent of Meeting
The place where Moses would speak with God and where he would cover his face with a veil after speaking with the Israelites.

5. The Veil
A physical covering that Moses uses to hide the radiance of his face from the Israelites after speaking with them, symbolizing the separation between God's holiness and the people's sinfulness.
Teaching Points
The Transformative Power of God's Presence
Spending time in God's presence can transform us, just as it did for Moses. This transformation should be evident in our lives, reflecting God's glory to others.

The Role of a Mediator
Moses serves as a mediator between God and the Israelites, pointing to the ultimate mediator, Jesus Christ, who bridges the gap between God and humanity.

The Veil and Spiritual Understanding
The veil symbolizes the limited understanding of God's glory under the old covenant. In Christ, the veil is removed, allowing us to see and reflect God's glory more fully.

Fear and Reverence for God's Holiness
The Israelites' fear of Moses' radiant face reminds us of the awe and reverence we should have for God's holiness and glory.

Living as Reflections of God's Glory
As believers, we are called to reflect God's glory in our daily lives, allowing others to see His light through our actions and character.

What Does Exodus 34:35 Mean
the Israelites would see

“the Israelites would see” anchors the verse in corporate eyewitness.

• Every return of Moses from the tent or the mountain provided visible confirmation that he had, in fact, met with God (Exodus 34:29–32).

• The people’s reaction—first fear, then attentive listening—underscored God-given authority (Exodus 19:16–19; 34:30–31).

• Paul later recalls this scene to describe the fading glory of the old covenant versus the surpassing glory of the new (2 Corinthians 3:7–8).

that the face of Moses was radiant

“that the face of Moses was radiant” shows literal, supernatural afterglow.

• Moses reflected God’s glory after intimate communion (Exodus 33:18–23; 34:29).

• Light radiating from a human face anticipates greater revelations—Christ’s transfiguration (Matthew 17:2) and His exalted appearance (Revelation 1:16).

• God is light (1 John 1:5); fellowship with Him changes His servants in ways others can see (Psalm 34:5; Acts 6:15).

So Moses would put the veil back over his face

“Then Moses would put the veil back over his face” highlights practical and symbolic purpose.

• Protection: the intensity of reflected glory unsettled sinful people (Exodus 34:30).

• Preservation: the veil kept their focus on God’s words rather than on the phenomenon (Deuteronomy 5:22–27).

• Prophetic picture: the veil illustrated a barrier later removed in Christ (2 Corinthians 3:13–16; Hebrews 10:19–20).

until he went in to speak with the LORD

“until he went in to speak with the LORD” shows an ongoing rhythm.

• Moses entered God’s presence unveiled—no barrier between mediator and Master (Exodus 33:11; Numbers 12:6–8).

• Each fresh encounter renewed the radiance and the message he brought (Numbers 7:89).

• The pattern foreshadows our open access through the final Mediator, Jesus (Hebrews 4:14–16; John 15:15).

summary

Exodus 34:35 records a literal phenomenon that authenticated Moses’ unique role. Israel regularly saw their leader’s shining face, grasped the holiness it represented, and learned that only God can remove the veil that shields sinful people from His glory. The cycle of unveiled communion and veiled ministry pointed forward to Christ, who both reveals God fully and provides believers with permanent, unveiled access to Him.
