And the tent curtains will be a cubit longer on either side
The tabernacle's design was divinely instructed, emphasizing precision and purpose. A cubit, an ancient measurement roughly equivalent to 18 inches, reflects the meticulous care in God's instructions. The extra length of the curtains signifies God's provision and protection, ensuring complete coverage. This detail underscores the importance of obedience to God's specific commands, as seen throughout the construction of the tabernacle. The tabernacle itself is a type of Christ, symbolizing God's dwelling among His people, and the curtains represent the separation between the holy and the common, akin to the veil in the temple torn at Christ's crucifixion (Matthew 27:51).

and the excess will hang over the sides of the tabernacle to cover it
The excess material hanging over the sides of the tabernacle served a practical purpose of protection from the elements, symbolizing God's encompassing care and shelter over His people. This imagery of covering is echoed in Psalm 91:4, where God covers His people with His feathers, providing refuge. The tabernacle, as a precursor to the temple, foreshadows the ultimate covering and atonement provided by Jesus Christ, who is our refuge and strength (Hebrews 9:11-12). The covering also signifies the completeness and sufficiency of God's provision, ensuring that nothing is left exposed or vulnerable.

The specific measurements and instructions for the tabernacle remind us of the importance of reverence and precision in our worship practices.

The extra cubit of curtain symbolizes God's provision and protection, covering and safeguarding His dwelling place, just as He covers and protects us.

Symbolism of the Tabernacle
The tabernacle serves as a physical representation of God's desire to dwell with His people, pointing to the ultimate fulfillment in Jesus Christ.

What Does Exodus 26:13 Mean
Setting the stage

- God is giving Moses precise blueprints for the wilderness tabernacle—“a sanctuary, that I may dwell among them” (Exodus 25:8).

- Every measurement matters because the structure is a visible witness of God’s holiness and nearness (Hebrews 8:5).

- The verse sits in a paragraph that describes eleven goat-hair curtains laid over the inner linen curtains (Exodus 26:7–12).

One cubit longer on either side

- Exodus 26:13 states, “And the tent curtains will be a cubit longer on either side”.

- A cubit (about 18 in/45 cm) of extra fabric on both the north and south sides ensured the goat-hair covering completely exceeded the dimensions of the inner linen layer (compare Exodus 26:2, 8).

- This literal surplus protects what is beneath and underscores that God provides more than just the minimum (Ephesians 3:20).

The excess will hang over the sides

- “And the excess will hang over the sides of the tabernacle to cover it” (Exodus 26:13).

- Hanging down like protective eaves, the curtain shields the sacred space from sun, wind, and desert grit—practical care for holy things (Numbers 4:5–6).

- The language echoes the Lord’s promise, “He will cover you with His feathers; under His wings you will find refuge” (Psalm 91:4), making the tabernacle a visual sermon of divine shelter.

Purpose fulfilled in Christ

- John 1:14 uses tabernacle imagery: “The Word became flesh and dwelt [tabernacled] among us,” signaling that the ultimate, all-sufficient covering is now the incarnate Son (Colossians 2:9).

- Just as the goat-hair layer concealed and guarded the inner glory, Christ’s humanity veiled His deity while still fully protecting and revealing God’s presence (Matthew 17:2).

- Believers are likewise “clothed with power from on high” (Luke 24:49); the pattern of surplus covering continues in the Spirit’s work.

summary

Exodus 26:13 literally commands an extra cubit of goat-hair curtain on each side so the protective layer hangs down and fully covers the tabernacle. This surplus illustrates God’s thorough provision and foreshadows the complete, gracious covering He supplies in Christ for all who trust Him.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 13. - And a cubit. Rather, "And the cubit." The cubit by which the goats' hair tent-cloth, which was thirty cubits across (ver. 8), would exceed the linen covering, which was twenty-eight cubits (ver. 2), on either side of the tabernacle, was to be allowed to hang down, like a valance, hiding so far the golden boards of the tabernacle. The outer protection (ver. 14).
