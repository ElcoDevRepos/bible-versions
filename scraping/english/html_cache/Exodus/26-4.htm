Make loops of blue material
The use of blue material in the tabernacle's construction is significant, as blue often symbolizes divinity, heaven, and the presence of God. This color was used in various elements of the tabernacle, including the priestly garments (Exodus 28:31). The loops were functional, serving to connect the curtains, but they also carried symbolic meaning, representing the heavenly nature of the tabernacle as a dwelling place for God among His people.

on the edge of the end curtain in the first set
The tabernacle was constructed with ten curtains, divided into two sets of five. The "end curtain in the first set" refers to the outermost curtain of the first group. The precise instructions for the tabernacle's construction highlight the importance of order and obedience in worship. The tabernacle was a portable sanctuary, reflecting the Israelites' nomadic lifestyle during their wilderness journey. The detailed craftsmanship and specific instructions underscore the holiness and sacredness of the space.

and do the same for the end curtain in the second set
This repetition emphasizes the symmetry and unity required in the tabernacle's design. The two sets of curtains were to be joined together, symbolizing the unity of God's people and the completeness of His covenant. The tabernacle served as a precursor to the temple and ultimately pointed to Jesus Christ, who is the true tabernacle (John 1:14), dwelling among us and uniting believers in Him. The meticulous design reflects God's desire for His people to approach Him with reverence and precision, foreshadowing the perfect order found in Christ.

What Does Exodus 26:4 Mean
Make loops of blue material

“Make loops of blue material” (Exodus 26:4a)

•The loops are literal fabric rings that will receive the clasps joining the curtain panels (Exodus 26:5).

•The color blue, repeatedly specified for the Tabernacle (Exodus 25:4; Numbers 15:38), points hearts heavenward, reminding worshipers of God’s holy throne (Ezekiel 1:26) and of the commandments tied to covenant loyalty.

•In Christ the heavenly reality these fabrics foreshadow becomes flesh and dwells among us (John 1:14; Hebrews 8:5).

on the edge

“on the edge” (Exodus 26:4b)

•The loops are placed at the very border—where one curtain ends and connection to another is needed.

•God attends even to edges; nothing is left to chance (Matthew 10:29-31).

•Believers, likened to living stones set precisely in God’s house (1 Peter 2:5), find encouragement that the Lord values every joining place.

of the end curtain

“of the end curtain” (Exodus 26:4c)

•End curtains would normally be vulnerable to fraying or separation. By strengthening them with loops, God ensures durability.

•The detail illustrates His promise that none of His people will be lost at the “edges” (John 6:39).

•It also hints at Christ bearing reproach “outside the camp” (Hebrews 13:11-13), taking the place of the outermost panel.

in the first set

“in the first set” (Exodus 26:4d)

•The ten fine-linen curtains are divided into two equal groups of five (Exodus 26:3). Orderly grouping prevents confusion and models worship “done decently and in order” (1 Corinthians 14:40).

•The first set represents the initial act of divine revelation; later pieces build upon it, just as Old Testament revelation lays a foundation for the New (Ephesians 2:20).

do the same for the end curtain

“and do the same for the end curtain” (Exodus 26:4e)

•Identical loops on both sides guarantee perfect alignment when the clasps are added (Exodus 26:6).

•God shows no partiality (Romans 2:11); He grants equal honor to every segment of His dwelling.

•The mirrored instruction also underscores His unchanging nature (Malachi 3:6).

in the second set

“in the second set” (Exodus 26:4f)

•The first and second sets, once clasped, form “one Tabernacle” (Exodus 26:6). Two become one—anticipating the unity of Jew and Gentile in Christ (Ephesians 2:14-16).

•Only when both sets are joined can the Most Holy Place be covered, foreshadowing the torn veil that opens access to God through Jesus’ flesh (Hebrews 10:19-20; Matthew 27:51).

summary

Every phrase of Exodus 26:4 highlights God’s meticulous care, revealed purpose, and passion for unity. Blue loops fastened to the edges of end curtains tie separate panels into a single sanctuary, pointing to Christ who unites heaven and earth and makes believers one dwelling for God’s Spirit (Ephesians 4:3-4). The verse invites confidence that the Lord secures every connection in His household and finishes what He starts.
