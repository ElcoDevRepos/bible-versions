Behold, I will stand there before you by the rock at Horeb.
This phrase indicates God's direct involvement and presence in the miracle about to occur. Horeb is another name for Mount Sinai, a significant location in Israel's history where Moses would later receive the Ten Commandments. The rock at Horeb symbolizes a place of divine encounter and provision. God's promise to stand before Moses signifies His authority and assurance of His power to provide. This event foreshadows Christ, the Rock, who provides living water (1 Corinthians 10:4).

And when you strike the rock,
Striking the rock is an act of obedience and faith on Moses' part. It demonstrates the necessity of human action in response to divine instruction. The act of striking can be seen as a type of Christ's suffering, where He was "struck" for our transgressions (Isaiah 53:4-5). The rock, representing Christ, being struck to bring forth water, prefigures the crucifixion, where Christ's sacrifice brings forth spiritual life.

water will come out of it for the people to drink.”
Water symbolizes life and sustenance, essential for the survival of the Israelites in the desert. This miraculous provision highlights God's care and ability to meet the needs of His people. It also points to Jesus' offer of living water, which quenches spiritual thirst (John 4:10, 14). The provision of water from the rock is a testament to God's faithfulness and a reminder of His covenant with Israel.

So Moses did this in the sight of the elders of Israel.
Moses' obedience in the presence of the elders serves as a witness to God's power and faithfulness. The elders represent the leadership and authority within the community, and their presence validates the miracle. This public demonstration reinforces the trustworthiness of Moses as God's chosen leader and the reliability of God's promises. It also serves as a teaching moment for the Israelites, emphasizing the importance of faith and obedience.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Moses
The leader of the Israelites, chosen by God to lead His people out of Egypt. In this passage, Moses is instructed by God to strike the rock to provide water for the Israelites.

2. The Israelites
The people of God who were delivered from slavery in Egypt and are now journeying through the wilderness. They are in need of water, which leads to this miraculous event.

3. Horeb
Also known as Mount Sinai, this is a significant location where God reveals His power and provision. It is the place where Moses strikes the rock to bring forth water.

4. The Rock
Symbolic of God's provision and presence. In the New Testament, the rock is often seen as a type of Christ, who provides living water.

5. The Elders of Israel
Leaders among the Israelites who witness the miracle, serving as representatives of the people and validating the event.
Teaching Points
God's Provision
God provides for our needs, often in unexpected ways. Just as He provided water from a rock, He can meet our needs in miraculous ways.

Obedience to God's Instructions
Moses' obedience in striking the rock as God commanded is a reminder of the importance of following God's instructions precisely.

Christ as the Rock
The rock at Horeb is a foreshadowing of Christ, who provides spiritual sustenance and living water to all who believe.

Witnessing God's Miracles
The presence of the elders as witnesses underscores the importance of community in experiencing and affirming God's work.

Faith in Times of Need
The Israelites' need for water and God's provision is a lesson in trusting God during times of scarcity and need.

What Does Exodus 17:6 Mean
Behold

- This opening word calls for alert attention, signaling that God is about to act in an extraordinary way (cf. Exodus 3:14; Isaiah 43:19).

- It reminds Israel—and us—that divine intervention is never random but purposeful and worthy of focused faith.

I will stand there before you

- God Himself promises His manifest presence, emphasizing that He is not distant but involved (Exodus 13:21-22; Deuteronomy 31:8).

- His standing “before” Moses underscores authority and assurance, much like the Angel of the LORD who went before Israel (Exodus 14:19).

by the rock at Horeb

- Horeb is another name for Sinai, the mountain where God would later give the Law (Exodus 19:1-3).

- Positioning at the rock links this miracle to covenant revelation: the same God who provides water will give His Word (Nehemiah 9:13-15).

- Paul later identifies the rock typologically with Christ (1 Corinthians 10:4), showing God’s provision culminates in the Messiah.

And when you strike the rock

- God directs a specific, obedient action from Moses (Exodus 4:2-3; Numbers 20:8 contrasts a later command to “speak” to the rock).

- The striking hints at substitutionary suffering: the rock (representing Christ) is smitten once, resulting in life-giving refreshment (Isaiah 53:4-5).

water will come out of it

- Physical water meets an urgent need, confirming God’s care in the wilderness (Psalm 78:15-16).

- Spiritually, it foreshadows living water offered by Jesus (John 4:14; 7:37-38), linking Old Testament miracle to New Testament fulfillment.

for the people to drink

- The provision is communal, illustrating God’s grace to an often-complaining nation (Exodus 16:2-4).

- Divine supply is not merely sufficient; it is generous and accessible to all, mirroring Christ’s invitation, “Let anyone who is thirsty come to Me” (John 7:37).

So Moses did this

- Immediate obedience highlights Moses’ faith and models leadership that trusts God despite previous pressures from the people (Hebrews 11:24-29).

- Action rooted in God’s word brings tangible results; faith and works operate together (James 2:22).

in the sight of the elders of Israel

- The elders serve as witnesses, ensuring the miracle is publicly confirmed (Exodus 3:16-18).

- Public testimony strengthens communal faith and sets a foundation for future remembrance (Psalm 78:4-7).

summary

Exodus 17:6 reveals God’s personal, powerful presence, His covenant faithfulness, and His compassionate provision. By standing before Moses, directing the striking of the Horeb rock, and bringing forth water, the Lord supplies Israel’s physical need while foreshadowing Christ, the smitten Rock who gives living water. Moses’ obedient act, observed by the elders, turns divine promise into experienced reality, inviting every generation to trust the God who still satisfies the thirsty.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 6. - Behold, I will stand before thee there. A visible Divine appearance seems to be intended, which would guide Moses to the exact place where he should strike. The rock in Horeb must have been a remarkable object, already known to Moses during the time that he dwelt in the Sinai-Horeb region; but its exact locality cannot be pointed out. It cannot, however, have been very far distant from Rephidim. (See ver. 8.)
