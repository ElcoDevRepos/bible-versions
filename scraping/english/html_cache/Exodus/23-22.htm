But if you will listen carefully to his voice
This phrase emphasizes the importance of obedience and attentiveness to divine guidance. In the context of Exodus, God is speaking through His angel, who represents His presence and authority. Listening carefully implies a deep, intentional focus on God's instructions, which is a recurring theme throughout the Bible (e.g., Deuteronomy 6:4-5). The concept of hearing and obeying is central to the covenant relationship between God and Israel, highlighting the necessity of faithfulness to God's commands.

and do everything I say
This part underscores the comprehensive nature of obedience required by God. It is not enough to merely hear; action is required. The Israelites are called to follow all of God's commandments, which are detailed in the Mosaic Law. This reflects the covenantal relationship where blessings are contingent upon obedience (Deuteronomy 28:1-2). The call to do everything God says is echoed in the New Testament, where Jesus emphasizes the importance of obedience to His teachings (John 14:15).

I will be an enemy to your enemies
Here, God promises divine protection and support against adversaries. This assurance is part of the covenant blessings, where God commits to fighting on behalf of Israel. Historically, this was significant as Israel faced numerous hostile nations in their journey to the Promised Land. Theologically, it reflects God's sovereignty and His role as a divine warrior (Exodus 15:3). This promise is seen as a precursor to the ultimate victory over evil through Christ, who defeats sin and death (1 Corinthians 15:57).

and a foe to your foes
This phrase reinforces the previous promise, emphasizing God's active opposition to those who oppose His people. It assures Israel of God's continued presence and intervention in their struggles. The repetition of the concept highlights the certainty and seriousness of God's commitment. In a broader biblical context, this reflects the theme of divine justice and retribution against those who oppose God's purposes (Psalm 37:12-15). It also foreshadows the ultimate triumph of God's kingdom, where all enemies are subdued (Revelation 19:11-16).

3. Enemies/Foes
The adversaries of Israel, representing any opposition or threat to God's people.

4. Moses
The leader of the Israelites, through whom God communicates His laws and promises.

5. Promised Land
The destination of the Israelites, a land flowing with milk and honey, symbolizing God's provision and faithfulness.
Teaching Points
Obedience as a Covenant Requirement
The verse emphasizes the conditional nature of God's protection, which hinges on the Israelites' obedience. This reflects the covenant relationship between God and His people.

Divine Protection
God's promise to be an enemy to our enemies assures us of His active involvement in our battles. This encourages believers to trust in God's sovereignty and protection.

Spiritual Warfare
The concept of God being a foe to our foes can be applied to spiritual battles, reminding us that God fights for us against spiritual adversaries.

Faith and Action
Obedience requires both faith in God's promises and action in following His commands. This balance is crucial for experiencing God's blessings.

Community and Accountability
The collective call to obedience highlights the importance of community in faith, where believers support and hold each other accountable in their walk with God.

What Does Exodus 23:22 Mean
But if you will listen carefully to his voice

• The call is for attentive, deliberate listening, not casual hearing. Similar language appears in Deuteronomy 28:1—“If you will diligently obey the voice of the LORD your God…”.

• God had just promised to send His angel before Israel (Exodus 23:20-21), so “his voice” points to God’s appointed messenger who carries divine authority.

• Jesus later affirms this principle: “My sheep listen to My voice; I know them, and they follow Me” (John 10:27). Consistent, careful listening is a hallmark of genuine relationship with God.

and do everything I say

• Obedience flows from listening. James 1:22 reminds believers, “Be doers of the word, and not hearers only”.

• The scope is “everything,” indicating comprehensive submission, echoed in Joshua 1:7-8 as Joshua is charged to observe “all the law.”

• Blessing and victory are tied to obedience throughout Scripture, from Genesis 22:18 to John 14:15, where Jesus says, “If you love Me, you will keep My commandments”.

I will be an enemy to your enemies

• God personally commits to oppose those who oppose His obedient people. This promise reflects Genesis 12:3—“I will bless those who bless you, and curse those who curse you”.

• In practical terms, Israel saw this in Egypt (Exodus 14:24-25) and later in Canaan (Joshua 10:11).

• For believers today, Romans 8:31 offers the same assurance in Christ: “If God is for us, who can be against us?”.

and a foe to your foes

• The repetition intensifies the promise. God does not merely defend; He actively takes the offensive against the adversaries of His covenant people.

• Psalm 35:1 captures this: “Contend, O LORD, with my contenders; fight against those who fight against me”.

• Revelation 19:11 shows the ultimate fulfillment when Christ rides forth as the Faithful and True warrior on behalf of His saints.

summary

Exodus 23:22 ties attentive hearing to wholehearted obedience, promising that those who submit to God’s voice will experience His active, powerful protection. The verse assures believers that God not only guards but aggressively opposes every force set against His faithful people.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(22) An adversary unto thine adversaries.--Rather, an afflictor of thy afflictors.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 22. - If thou shalt indeed obey his voice, and do all that I speak. The change of persons in the latter clause - "all that I speak," instead of "all that he speaks" - implies the doctrine of the perienchoresis or circuminsessio, that God the Father is in the Son and the Spirit, as they are in him. An adversary to thy adversaries. Rather "an afflictor of thy afflictors."
