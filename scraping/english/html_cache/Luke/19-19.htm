Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
And to this one he said
This phrase indicates a direct address from the master to the servant, highlighting a personal interaction. In the parable of the minas, the master represents Jesus, and the servants symbolize His followers. This interaction underscores the personal relationship between Christ and each believer, emphasizing accountability and reward based on individual faithfulness.

‘You shall have authority
Authority in this context refers to the responsibility and governance granted by the master. Biblically, authority is often associated with stewardship and leadership. This reflects the biblical principle that those who are faithful in small matters will be entrusted with greater responsibilities (Luke 16:10). It also echoes the dominion mandate given to humanity in Genesis 1:28, where humans are called to rule over creation.

over five cities.’
The mention of "five cities" signifies a substantial reward and responsibility. In the historical and cultural context of the time, cities were centers of commerce, governance, and culture. The number five, while not necessarily symbolic, indicates a significant level of trust and reward. This allocation of cities can be seen as a metaphor for the eternal rewards and responsibilities believers will receive in God's kingdom, as seen in Revelation 2:26-27, where faithful believers are promised authority over nations. This also reflects the eschatological hope of reigning with Christ in His kingdom.

Teaching Points
Faithful Stewardship
Believers are called to be faithful with the resources and opportunities God has given them. This includes time, talents, and treasures.

Accountability
Every believer will give an account of their stewardship to God. This should motivate us to live purposefully and diligently.

Eternal Rewards
The parable teaches that our faithfulness in this life will be rewarded in the life to come. This encourages us to focus on eternal rather than temporal gains.

Use of Gifts
God has given each of us unique gifts and abilities. We are to use them for His glory and the advancement of His Kingdom.

Kingdom Responsibility
The reward of authority over cities signifies that faithful service leads to greater responsibilities in God's Kingdom.

What Does Luke 19:19 Mean
And to this one he said

Jesus addresses the second faithful servant personally, highlighting that He knows each worker and his exact service (John 10:27; 2 Corinthians 5:10). The fact that the Master “said” underscores the finality and authority of His judgment—what He decrees stands forever (Psalm 33:9). It also reveals His fairness: every servant receives a verdict directly from the Master, not through intermediaries (Romans 14:12).

You shall have authority

The reward is not leisure but rulership. Faithfulness in small matters brings greater responsibility (Luke 16:10; Matthew 24:47). “Authority” points to real, delegated governance in Christ’s coming kingdom (Revelation 2:26-27; 2 Timothy 2:12). This promise validates that present obedience prepares believers for future leadership and underlines the literal reign of Christ where His servants will share His rule (1 Corinthians 6:2).

over five cities

The number “five” matches the servant’s gain of five minas (Luke 19:18). Rewards are proportionate—God measures stewardship, not comparing us to others but to what He entrusted to each (Matthew 25:21; 1 Peter 4:10). “Cities” signals a tangible, earthly dimension to the coming kingdom (Luke 22:29-30; Revelation 21:24). Real geography awaits real rulers; this isn’t abstract symbolism but a concrete promise of territorial administration under Christ’s lordship (Isaiah 32:1).

summary

Luke 19:19 shows the Master rewarding faithful service with real authority in His future kingdom. Christ personally evaluates each disciple, grants rulership proportionate to their stewardship, and assigns literal cities to govern. Present obedience, therefore, directly shapes our eternal responsibilities under the reign of Jesus.
