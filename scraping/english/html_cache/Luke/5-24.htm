Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
But so that you may know that the Son of Man has authority on the earth to forgive sins…
This phrase highlights Jesus' self-identification as the "Son of Man," a title with deep roots in Daniel 7:13-14, where it describes a divine figure with everlasting dominion. The authority to forgive sins is a divine prerogative, indicating Jesus' divine nature. In Jewish culture, only God could forgive sins, so this statement is a direct claim to divinity. The phrase "on the earth" emphasizes that this authority is not limited to the heavenly realm but is active and present in the world.

He said to the paralytic,
The paralytic represents those who are spiritually and physically in need of healing. In the context of first-century Judea, physical ailments were often seen as a result of sin, making the forgiveness of sins and healing interconnected. Jesus addressing the paralytic directly demonstrates His personal care and attention to individual needs.

I tell you,
This phrase underscores Jesus' authoritative teaching style. Unlike the scribes and Pharisees, who relied on tradition and interpretation, Jesus speaks with inherent authority. This direct command reflects His role as a prophet and teacher, echoing the authority of God speaking through His prophets in the Old Testament.

get up,
The command to "get up" signifies a call to action and transformation. It is a physical manifestation of the spiritual healing that has taken place. This mirrors the resurrection power of Christ, who calls believers to rise from spiritual death to new life.

pick up your mat,
The instruction to pick up the mat serves as a tangible sign of the miracle. It demonstrates the completeness of the healing, as the man is not only able to walk but also to carry his own burden. This act symbolizes the removal of past burdens and the beginning of a new journey in faith.

and go home.
Returning home signifies restoration to community and normalcy. In a broader sense, it represents the reconciliation with God and the return to one's spiritual home. This command also emphasizes the personal and communal aspects of Jesus' miracles, as the healed man would testify to his family and community about the power of Christ.

What Does Luke 5:24 Mean
But so that you may know

• Jesus openly states His purpose: to give clear, undeniable evidence.

• Throughout Scripture God provides signs so His people “may know” (Exodus 10:2; John 20:31).

• The goal is conviction, not curiosity; He wants observers to grasp who He is and respond in faith (John 11:42).

that the Son of Man has authority on the earth

• “Son of Man” links Jesus to Daniel 7:13-14—Messiah, fully human yet ruling with divine authority.

• Authority “on the earth” underscores that His power is active here and now, not only in heaven (Matthew 28:18).

• Unlike religious leaders who spoke about God’s power, Jesus wields it personally (John 10:18).

to forgive sins…

• Forgiveness is God’s exclusive domain (Psalm 103:12; Isaiah 43:25).

• By claiming this right, Jesus equates Himself with God, prompting either worship or accusation of blasphemy (Mark 2:7).

• Physical healing will verify the invisible miracle of pardon (Matthew 9:6).

He said to the paralytic,

• Attention shifts from debate to the needy man lowered through the roof (Luke 5:18-19).

• Jesus addresses him directly, revealing personal compassion as well as sovereign authority (Luke 7:14).

• The man represents every sinner: helpless until Christ speaks life (Ephesians 2:1-5).

I tell you, get up, pick up your mat, and go home.

• A triple command: rise, lift, depart. Each step visibly confirms the unseen forgiveness.

• Immediate obedience displays restored strength (John 5:8-9).

• Carrying the mat turns previous weakness into a testimony, much like the healed beggar walking and leaping in Acts 3:6-8.

• “Go home” reconnects him to community, illustrating reconciliation with God and people (Mark 5:19).

summary

Luke 5:24 showcases Jesus proving His divine right to forgive by coupling an inner miracle with an outer one. He speaks so that onlookers will know, beyond doubt, that the Son of Man possesses earthly authority straight from heaven. The paralytic’s instant, visible healing seals the truth: when Jesus forgives, sins are gone and new life begins—powerfully, publicly, and forever.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 24, 25. - That ye may know that the Son of man hath power upon earth to forgive sins, (he said unto the sick of the palsy,) I say unto thee, Arise, and take up thy couch, and go into thine house. And immediately he rose up before them, and took up that whereon he lay, and departed to his own house, glorifying God. The lookers-on, the curious, the cavillers, the friendly, too, as the unfriendly, who crowded that Capernaum house, could not see with their eyes the Redeemer's remission of the palsied man's sins. The sufferer alone was conscious that the great burden which pressed on his soul was removed at the Master's word. But all could see the miracle which followed. Any one of those present, had he dared, might have uttered the solemn absolution. None but he could surely risk, as he risked, such words which followed, and which challenged an instant and visible fulfilment. It was a strange, great claim the Master made that day, and we may be sure it and the mighty sign which followed sank deep into many a heart. We see why the memory of this day's work was treasured up so faithfully. He took up that whereon he lay. This could easily have been done. The bed or pallet would be nothing but a light portable framework covered with a blanket.
