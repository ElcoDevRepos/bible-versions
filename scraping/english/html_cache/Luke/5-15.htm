Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
But the news about Jesus spread all the more
The rapid spread of news about Jesus highlights the impact of His ministry and the growing interest in His teachings and miracles. This phrase indicates the increasing popularity of Jesus, which is a recurring theme in the Gospels. The spread of news can be seen as a fulfillment of the prophetic anticipation of a coming Messiah who would draw people to Himself. The phrase also reflects the oral culture of the time, where news was primarily spread by word of mouth, emphasizing the authenticity and power of Jesus' works that compelled people to share what they had witnessed.

and great crowds came to hear Him
The gathering of great crowds signifies the deep spiritual hunger and anticipation among the people of Israel for a deliverer. This mirrors the Old Testament prophecies of a shepherd who would gather His flock (Ezekiel 34:23). The crowds' desire to hear Jesus underscores His role as a teacher and prophet, akin to Moses, who delivered God's word to the people. The presence of large crowds also foreshadows the eventual opposition Jesus would face from religious leaders who felt threatened by His influence.

and to be healed of their sicknesses
The healing of sicknesses by Jesus serves as a testament to His divine authority and compassion. It fulfills the messianic prophecies such as those found in Isaiah 53:4, which speak of the Messiah bearing the infirmities of the people. Healing was a sign of the in-breaking of God's kingdom, demonstrating Jesus' power over physical and spiritual ailments. This phrase also highlights the holistic nature of Jesus' ministry, addressing both the spiritual and physical needs of individuals, and prefigures the ultimate healing and restoration promised in the eschatological hope of the new creation.

Teaching Points
The Power of Testimony
The spreading news about Jesus underscores the importance of sharing personal testimonies of His work in our lives. Just as the news of His healing power drew crowds, our testimonies can draw others to Christ.

Jesus as Healer
Jesus' ability to heal physical sicknesses points to His power to heal spiritual ailments. We should seek Him not only for physical healing but also for spiritual restoration.

The Draw of Truth and Compassion
The crowds were drawn to Jesus because of His truth and compassion. As followers of Christ, we should embody these qualities to attract others to the Gospel.

Persistent Faith
The crowds' determination to reach Jesus despite obstacles is a model of persistent faith. We should strive to overcome barriers in our pursuit of Christ.

Community of Believers
The gathering of great crowds around Jesus illustrates the importance of community in the Christian faith. We are called to gather together to learn, grow, and support one another.

What Does Luke 5:15 Mean
But the news about Jesus spread all the more

• Earlier in the chapter Jesus healed a leper and asked him to “tell no one” (Luke 5:14). Instead, the man’s gratitude overflowed, and the report of Jesus’ power raced through Galilee, showing how impossible it is to hide light under a basket (cf. Mark 1:45; Luke 4:37).

• This growing reputation fulfills messianic expectation: Isaiah 9:7 foretells an ever-increasing kingdom, and Luke records it happening in real time.

• The spread underscores that God’s plan advances despite human disobedience or opposition; even unintended testimony magnifies the Son (Philippians 1:18).

and great crowds came to hear Him

• Hearing precedes faith (Romans 10:17). Luke highlights that the people were first drawn to Jesus’ words, not merely His wonders—mirroring later scenes where “all the people were hanging on His words” (Luke 19:48).

• Jesus consistently prioritized teaching: He “went throughout all Galilee, teaching in their synagogues” before He healed (Matthew 4:23). The crowds recognized that authoritative truth met their spiritual hunger (Luke 4:32).

• The scene anticipates the Sermon on the Mount dynamic—listeners gathering for life-giving instruction (Matthew 5:1-2).

and to be healed of their sicknesses

• Physical maladies in Scripture often symbolize deeper spiritual need; Jesus addresses both. His miracles fulfill Isaiah 53:4—“He took on our infirmities and carried our diseases”—validating His messianic identity (Matthew 8:16-17).

• Compassion moves Him: “He had compassion on them and healed their sick” (Matthew 14:14), revealing God’s heart for the broken (Psalm 147:3).

• Each healing is a preview of the ultimate restoration promised in Revelation 21:4, where sickness and sorrow will vanish forever.

summary

Luke 5:15 shows the unstoppable spread of news about Jesus, the eagerness of people to hear His life-changing teaching, and His gracious power to heal. The verse captures the gospel’s magnetic pull: truth proclaimed, faith awakened, and lives restored—clear evidence that the promised Messiah has come and His kingdom is advancing.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(15) So much the more.--The statement agrees with St. Mark, St. Matthew closing his account with the command given to the leper. Both the verbs, "went" and "came together," are in the tense that implies continuous action.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 15. - But so much the more went there a fame abroad of him: and great multitudes came together to hear, and to be healed by him of their infirmities. It is evident that his wishes and commands were neglected, possibly out of a mistaken feeling of gratitude. The result was that his work of teaching was hindered by the crowds who resorted to him at once as a Physician of extraordinary power. But he had graver and much more important work before him than even the blessed task of relieving suffering. So he withdrew himself, says our evangelist, and again spent a short season in solitude and prayer.
