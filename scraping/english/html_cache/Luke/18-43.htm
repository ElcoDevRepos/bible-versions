Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Immediately he received his sight
This phrase highlights the instantaneous nature of the miracle, emphasizing Jesus' divine power and authority. In biblical context, blindness often symbolizes spiritual ignorance or lack of understanding, and receiving sight can represent enlightenment or salvation. The immediacy of the healing underscores the completeness and effectiveness of Jesus' miracles, reflecting prophecies such as Isaiah 35:5, which speaks of the eyes of the blind being opened in the Messianic age.

and followed Jesus
The act of following Jesus signifies discipleship and a commitment to a new way of life. In the cultural context of the time, following a rabbi or teacher was a sign of allegiance and a desire to learn from them. This man's decision to follow Jesus after being healed indicates a transformation not just physically but spiritually, aligning with the call to discipleship seen throughout the Gospels (e.g., Matthew 4:19).

glorifying God
Glorifying God is a response of worship and gratitude, acknowledging God's power and mercy. This reflects the biblical principle that miracles and blessings should lead to worship and recognition of God's sovereignty. The healed man's response is consistent with the purpose of Jesus' miracles, which were to reveal God's glory and lead people to faith (John 11:4).

And all the people, when they saw it, gave praise to God
The public nature of the miracle and the collective response of the people highlight the communal aspect of faith and worship. Witnessing the miracle led the crowd to praise God, showing that Jesus' works were not only for the benefit of individuals but also served to strengthen the faith of the community. This mirrors other instances in the Gospels where Jesus' miracles lead to public acknowledgment of God's power (e.g., Luke 7:16). The reaction of the people fulfills the purpose of miracles as signs pointing to the divine nature of Jesus and the coming of God's kingdom.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jesus
The central figure in this passage, Jesus is the one who performs the miracle of healing the blind man, demonstrating His divine power and compassion.

2. The Blind Man
Although not named in this verse, the blind man is the recipient of Jesus' miraculous healing. His immediate response is to follow Jesus and glorify God.

3. The Crowd
The people who witness the miracle. Their reaction is to praise God, showing the impact of Jesus' miracles on the broader community.

4. Jericho
The location where this event takes place. Jericho is significant as a setting for several biblical events and is a place where Jesus performs this miracle.

5. The Miracle
The event of the blind man receiving his sight. This miracle is a testament to Jesus' authority and the fulfillment of messianic prophecies.
Teaching Points
Faith and Healing
The blind man's faith in Jesus leads to his healing. We are encouraged to have faith in Jesus' power to transform our lives.

Immediate Response to God's Work
The blind man immediately follows Jesus and glorifies God. Our response to God's work in our lives should be immediate and filled with gratitude.

Public Witness
The crowd's reaction to the miracle is to praise God. Our lives should be a testimony to God's power, leading others to glorify Him.

Spiritual Sight
Beyond physical healing, Jesus offers spiritual sight. We should seek to have our spiritual eyes opened to understand and follow God's will.

Community Impact
The miracle not only affects the individual but also the community. Our faith and actions can have a ripple effect, influencing those around us.

What Does Luke 18:43 Mean
Immediately he received his sight

- “Immediately he received his sight …” (Luke 18:43) shows instant, complete restoration—proof of Jesus’ absolute authority (Matthew 20:34; Mark 10:52).

- The suddenness echoes prophecies such as Psalm 146:8 and Isaiah 35:5, where the LORD opens blind eyes.

- As salvation itself is immediate when we believe (John 3:16; Acts 16:31), this miracle pictures the new birth transforming us in a moment.

and followed Jesus

- Sight leads straight to discipleship: “… and followed Jesus …” (Luke 18:43).

- True faith always moves from receiving to following (John 10:27; Matthew 16:24; Luke 5:28; John 12:26).

- Grace precedes action; once our eyes are opened, we can walk in His steps (Ephesians 2:8-10).

glorifying God

- The healed man’s first words are worship: “… glorifying God.”

- Praise is the natural overflow of a grateful heart (Psalm 34:3; Luke 17:15-18; Luke 5:25).

- This response aligns with Jesus’ mission to honor the Father (John 17:4; Philippians 2:11).

And all the people, when they saw it, gave praise to God

- The crowd moves from observation to adoration (Luke 7:16; Luke 13:17; Acts 4:21; Matthew 9:8).

- One transformed life sparks communal worship, illustrating how personal testimony advances the gospel (1 Peter 2:12; Matthew 5:16).

summary

Luke 18:43 traces a four-fold sequence: immediate miracle, committed following, individual worship, and widespread praise. Jesus opens blind eyes, the healed man becomes a disciple who glorifies God, and the watching crowd joins in, all spotlighting the power and glory of Christ.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(43) Glorifying God.--The account of the effect of the miracle on the blind man himself, and on the people, is peculiar to St. Luke, and seems to belong to the class of phenomena which he loved to study (Luke 5:25-26; Luke 7:16; Acts 3:8; Acts 14:10-11).
