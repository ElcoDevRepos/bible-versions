Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
But Jesus declared
In this passage, Jesus is in the midst of a crowd, highlighting His accessibility and the people's eagerness to be near Him. His declaration is significant as it demonstrates His awareness and authority. This moment is a turning point in the narrative, emphasizing Jesus' role as a teacher and healer. The use of "declared" suggests a public announcement, drawing attention to the importance of the event.

Someone touched Me
The phrase indicates Jesus' sensitivity to the faith and needs of individuals, even in a large crowd. This touch was not accidental but intentional, reflecting the woman's faith and desperation. In the cultural context, touching a rabbi or teacher was not common, especially for a woman, which underscores the boldness of her action. This moment also highlights the personal nature of Jesus' ministry, where individual faith is recognized and rewarded.

for I know that power has gone out from Me
This statement reveals Jesus' divine nature and the reality of His healing power. The concept of power going out from Him suggests a tangible transfer of divine energy, which is unique to Jesus as the Son of God. It connects to other instances in the Gospels where Jesus heals through touch or command, demonstrating His authority over physical ailments. This power is not diminished by being shared, indicating the infinite nature of His divine resources. Theologically, it points to Jesus as the source of life and healing, fulfilling Old Testament prophecies about the Messiah bringing restoration and wholeness.

Teaching Points
The Power of Faith
The woman's faith in reaching out to Jesus is a powerful example of how faith can lead to divine intervention.

Jesus' Awareness
Jesus' immediate awareness of the power leaving Him demonstrates His divine omniscience and personal attention to individual needs.

The Nature of Divine Power
The Greek word for "power" (dynamis) indicates a miraculous force, underscoring the supernatural aspect of Jesus' healing ministry.

Personal Relationship with Christ
This passage encourages believers to seek a personal encounter with Jesus, trusting in His power and willingness to respond.

The Importance of Confession
The woman's eventual confession of her act of faith is a reminder of the importance of acknowledging Jesus' work in our lives.

What Does Luke 8:46 Mean
But Jesus declared

— In the jostling crowd, Jesus suddenly stops and speaks, signaling both His authority and His personal awareness of what has just happened (Mark 5:30; Luke 8:45).

— His deliberate declaration shows that He is never overwhelmed by circumstances; He rules over them (Psalm 33:9; Matthew 28:18).

— Rather than letting the miracle pass unnoticed, He brings it into the open so faith can be seen and God glorified (Luke 8:47; John 11:4).

Someone touched Me

— Many hands were brushing against Him, yet He singles out one intentional, faith-filled touch (Matthew 9:20–21; Mark 5:27–28).

— The wording underscores personal relationship: Jesus is not a distant healer but a Savior who knows His sheep individually (John 10:3; Hebrews 4:15).

— This touch illustrates how genuine faith reaches beyond mere proximity to Jesus and actually receives from Him (James 1:6; Hebrews 11:6).

for I know that power has gone out from Me

— The Lord literally sensed healing power flow—an objective reality, not symbolism (Luke 5:17; Luke 6:19).

— His power is limitless; its release to one person does not diminish His capacity for others (Ephesians 3:20; Colossians 1:17).

— The phrase confirms that miracles proceed from His divine nature, affirming both His humanity (He feels) and His deity (He commands power) (Philippians 2:6-7; Acts 10:38).

— By acknowledging the transfer of power, Jesus highlights the connection between faith and divine virtue; faith is the conduit, His power the cure (Mark 9:23; 1 Peter 2:24).

summary

Luke 8:46 records a literal moment when Jesus stopped, identified a faith-driven touch, and confirmed that healing power had flowed from Him. The verse reveals His sovereign awareness, His personal concern for individuals, and His inexhaustible divine power, inviting believers today to approach Him with the same expectant faith that draws on His living, saving strength.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(46) Somebody hath touched me.--What St. Mark gives historically as a fact, St. Luke reports as uttered by our Lord Himself.

That virtue is gone out of me.--See Note on Mark 5:30. To St. Luke the word was probably familiar as a technical term.
