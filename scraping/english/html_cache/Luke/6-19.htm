The entire crowd was trying to touch Him
This phrase highlights the immense popularity and demand for Jesus' presence and healing power. In the context of first-century Judea, crowds often gathered around teachers and miracle workers, but Jesus' ability to heal set Him apart. The act of touching is significant in Jewish culture, as it often conveyed a transfer of blessing or power. This scene echoes the desperation and faith of the woman with the issue of blood in Luke 8:43-48, who believed that touching Jesus' garment would heal her. The crowd's eagerness to touch Jesus underscores their recognition of His divine authority and the hope they placed in Him for physical and spiritual restoration.

because power was coming from Him
This phrase indicates the divine nature of Jesus' ministry. The power emanating from Him is a testament to His identity as the Son of God, fulfilling prophecies such as Isaiah 53:4-5, which speaks of the Messiah bearing our infirmities. The Greek word for power, "dynamis," suggests a miraculous force or ability, emphasizing that Jesus' healings were not mere acts of compassion but demonstrations of divine authority. This power is also a type of the Holy Spirit, which would later be given to the apostles and the early church, as seen in Acts 1:8.

and healing them all
The comprehensive nature of Jesus' healing ministry is highlighted here. Unlike other healers of the time, Jesus healed all who came to Him, regardless of their condition or social status. This universal healing reflects the inclusive nature of the Gospel, as Jesus came to save all of humanity, not just a select few. It also fulfills Old Testament prophecies about the Messiah bringing healing and restoration, such as in Isaiah 35:5-6. The healings serve as a foretaste of the ultimate healing and restoration that will occur in the Kingdom of God, as described in Revelation 21:4.

Teaching Points
The Power of Faith
The crowd's desire to touch Jesus signifies their faith in His ability to heal. Faith is a crucial element in receiving God's blessings and miracles.

Jesus' Compassion
Jesus' willingness to heal all who came to Him reflects His compassion and love for humanity. As followers of Christ, we are called to show compassion to others.

Divine Authority
The passage underscores Jesus' divine authority and power over sickness and disease, affirming His identity as the Son of God.

Seeking Jesus
Just as the crowd sought Jesus for healing, we are encouraged to seek Him in our times of need, trusting in His power and willingness to help.

Community of Believers
The crowd represents a community united in their need for Jesus. As believers, we should support one another in faith and prayer.

What Does Luke 6:19 Mean
The entire crowd

- Luke 6:17 notes that people came “from all Judea, Jerusalem, and the coastal region of Tyre and Sidon.” No one was excluded; the needy pressed in together.

- Similar scenes: “Large crowds followed Him, and He healed them there” (Matthew 19:2); “Great crowds came to Him, bringing the lame, the blind… and He healed them” (Matthew 15:30).

- Every person mattered to Jesus. He welcomed the multitude rather than turning them away (cf. John 6:37).

Was trying to touch Him

- Physical contact expressed faith that merely touching Jesus could change their condition, as later shown by the woman with the hemorrhage: “She came up behind Him and touched the fringe of His cloak… and immediately her bleeding stopped” (Luke 8:44).

- Mark 3:10 records the same urgency: “He had healed many, so that all who had diseases were pressing forward to touch Him.”

- Touch becomes a picture of personal trust—moving from observing Christ to reaching out for Him.

Because power was coming from Him

- Luke repeatedly highlights divine power active in Jesus: “The power of the Lord was present for Him to heal the sick” (Luke 5:17).

- Acts 10:38 later echoes this: “God anointed Jesus of Nazareth with the Holy Spirit and with power, and… He went around doing good and healing.”

- The power was not borrowed or temporary; it flowed continually from the Son of God, confirming His identity (cf. Colossians 1:19).

And healing them all

- No partial or selective mercy: “He healed them all” (Matthew 12:15).

- Acts 5:16 shows the risen Lord continuing His work through apostles: “Crowds gathered… and all of them were healed.”

- Physical healings point to the greater spiritual healing available to everyone who believes (1 Peter 2:24).

summary

Luke 6:19 paints a vivid snapshot of Jesus’ ministry: a diverse crowd gathers, each person desperate enough to reach out and touch Him. Divine power continually flows from Christ, meeting every need without limitation. The verse invites believers today to approach Him with the same expectant faith, confident that His power is still sufficient to heal, restore, and save all who come.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(19) There went virtue out of him.--The use of the term "virtue" (or power) in this technical sense is peculiar to St. Luke, and may be noted as characteristic of the medical Evangelist. (Comp. Introduction.)

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 19. - And the whole multitude sought to touch him: for there went virtue out of him, and healed them all. The words here used are few, and we pass them over often without pausing to think of what they involve. It was, perhaps, the hour in the ministry of Jesus when his miraculous power was most abundantly displayed.
