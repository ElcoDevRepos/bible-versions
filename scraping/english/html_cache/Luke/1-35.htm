Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
The angel replied
This phrase indicates a direct response from the angel Gabriel to Mary, who had questioned how she could conceive a child as a virgin. Gabriel's role as a messenger is significant, as angels often serve as divine intermediaries in Scripture, delivering God's messages to humans. This encounter emphasizes the importance of the message being delivered.

The Holy Spirit will come upon you
The Holy Spirit's involvement in the conception of Jesus highlights the divine nature of His birth. The phrase "come upon you" suggests an action initiated by God, reminiscent of the Spirit's role in creation (Genesis 1:2) and empowerment of individuals for God's purposes (Judges 14:6). This divine intervention ensures that Jesus is both fully God and fully man, fulfilling prophecies of a miraculous birth (Isaiah 7:14).

and the power of the Most High will overshadow you
The term "Most High" is a title for God, emphasizing His supreme authority. The imagery of overshadowing recalls the presence of God in the Old Testament, such as the cloud that covered the Tabernacle (Exodus 40:34-35). This signifies God's protective and creative power at work, ensuring the sanctity and divine origin of Jesus' conception.

So the Holy One to be born
The designation "Holy One" underscores the sinless nature of Jesus, set apart for God's purposes. This title is often associated with God Himself (Psalm 16:10), indicating Jesus' divine identity. His holiness is essential for His role as the perfect sacrifice for sin, fulfilling the requirements of the Law and the prophecies concerning the Messiah.

will be called the Son of God
This title affirms Jesus' divine sonship and unique relationship with God the Father. It fulfills Old Testament prophecies about the Messiah being God's Son (Psalm 2:7) and establishes His authority and identity. The term "Son of God" also connects to the New Testament revelation of the Trinity, highlighting the unity and distinct roles within the Godhead.

Teaching Points

The Holy Spirit is active in the miraculous and the mundane, guiding believers and empowering them for God's purposes.

Divine Sovereignty and Human Obedience
God's plans often involve human cooperation. Mary's willingness to accept God's plan is a model of faith and obedience for believers.

The Mystery of the Incarnation
Jesus' conception by the Holy Spirit underscores the mystery of the incarnation, where God became man to redeem humanity.

The Identity of Jesus
Recognizing Jesus as the Son of God is foundational to Christian faith, affirming His divinity and His role as Savior.

Faith in God's Promises
Believers are called to trust in God's promises, even when they seem impossible, as demonstrated by Mary's faith.

What Does Luke 1:35 Mean
The angel replied

The messenger Gabriel answers Mary’s “How can this be?” (Luke 1:34) with the assurance of God’s direct intervention.

• Similar angelic explanations appear in Genesis 18:13-14 and Judges 13:8-18, reminding us that nothing is impossible for God.

• Angels consistently communicate God’s plan with certainty (Hebrews 1:14), emphasizing that divine revelation, not human reasoning, grounds Mary’s faith.

The Holy Spirit will come upon you

Gabriel identifies the Holy Spirit as the active agent.

• The same Spirit hovered over the waters at creation (Genesis 1:2) and empowers believers for service (Acts 1:8), showing His life-giving power.

• Mary’s conception is entirely the Spirit’s work; no human father is involved (Matthew 1:18), ensuring Jesus’ sinless nature (2 Corinthians 5:21).

• The Spirit’s “coming upon” parallels His filling of prophets (Numbers 11:25-26) and judges (Judges 6:34), yet here He creates new life, surpassing all prior manifestations.

The power of the Most High will overshadow you

The phrase pictures protective, glorious presence.

• “Most High” (Psalm 91:1) underscores God’s supremacy.

• “Overshadow” evokes the cloud over the tabernacle (Exodus 40:34-35) and the disciples at the Transfiguration (Matthew 17:5). God’s glory covers Mary as the true Holy of Holies.

• This divine covering guarantees purity; conception is miraculous, not mythical. It fulfills Isaiah 7:14: “Behold, the virgin will conceive and give birth to a son.”

So the Holy One to be born

• Jesus is “holy” from conception, unlike every descendant of Adam (Romans 5:12).

• Set apart for God’s purposes (Hebrews 7:26), He embodies perfect holiness, qualifying Him as the flawless Lamb (1 Peter 1:19).

• His origin is heavenly, yet He takes true humanity (John 1:14), uniting holiness with flesh.

Will be called the Son of God

• The title affirms both His divine nature and messianic mission (Psalm 2:7; Mark 1:1).

• “Called” indicates public recognition; heaven declares what earth must acknowledge (Matthew 3:17).

• As Son, He inherits David’s throne (2 Samuel 7:12-14; Luke 1:32-33) and shares the Father’s essence (John 5:18).

• Virgin conception proves His sonship, distinguishing Him from adopted or symbolic “sons” (Romans 8:14).

summary

Luke 1:35 reveals the triune God orchestrating the Incarnation. The Spirit miraculously conceives, the Father’s power shelters, and the Son enters humanity holy and sinless. Gabriel’s answer grounds Mary—and us—in the certainty that Jesus is truly God’s Son, divinely conceived to save sinners and reign forever.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(35) The Holy Ghost shall come upon thee.--See Note on Luke 1:15. Here, however, the context would suggest to one familiar with the sacred writings, another aspect of the Spirit's work, as quickening the dead chaos into life (Genesis 1:2), as being the source of life to all creation (Psalm 104:30).

The power of the Highest shall overshadow thee.--The divine name is used in obvious harmony with "the Son of the Highest" in Luke 1:32.

Therefore also . . . shall be called the Son of God.--The words appear to rest the title, "Son of God," rather on the supernatural birth than on the eternal pre-existence of the Son as the Word that was "in the beginning with God and was God" (John 1:1), and we may accept the fact that the message of the angel was so far a partial, not a complete, revelation of the mystery of the Incarnation. It gave a sufficient reason for the name which should be given to the Son of Mary, and more was not then required. . . .

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 35. - The Holy Ghost shall come upon thee, and the power of the Highest shall overshadow thee. Again the angel makes use of the term "Highest" when alluding to the eternal Father. The expression of Gabriel, "the power of the Highest shall overshadow thee," reminds us of the opening words of Genesis, where the writer describes the dawn of life in creation in the words, "The Spirit of God moved [or, 'brooded'] over the face of the deep." "The Word was conceived in the womb of a woman, not after the manner of men, but by the singular, powerful, invisible, immediate operation of the Holy Ghost, whereby a virgin was, beyond the law of nature, enabled to conceive, and that which was conceived in her was originally and cmnpletcly sanctified" (art. 3, Bishop Pearson on the Creed).
