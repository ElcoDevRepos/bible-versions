Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
until I make
This phrase indicates a future action and fulfillment of a promise. It reflects God's sovereign plan and timing. The use of "until" suggests a period of waiting and anticipation, emphasizing God's control over history and His ultimate victory over evil. This aligns with the biblical theme of God's patience and the eventual establishment of His kingdom.

Your enemies
In the biblical context, "enemies" often refers to those who oppose God's will and His anointed one. Historically, this can be seen in the opposition faced by Israel and later by Jesus Christ. Theologically, it represents the spiritual forces of evil and those who reject God's authority. This phrase connects to the broader biblical narrative of the conflict between good and evil, as seen in passages like Ephesians 6:12.

a footstool for
The imagery of a footstool signifies complete subjugation and defeat. In ancient Near Eastern culture, placing one's feet on the necks of conquered foes symbolized total victory. This metaphor is used in several Old Testament passages, such as Joshua 10:24, to depict triumph over adversaries. It underscores the ultimate authority and dominion of the Messiah.

Your feet.”’
The reference to "Your feet" points to the Messiah, Jesus Christ, as the one who will exercise dominion over His enemies. This imagery is consistent with the messianic prophecy found in Psalm 110:1, which is frequently quoted in the New Testament to affirm Christ's exaltation and authority (e.g., Hebrews 1:13). It highlights the fulfillment of God's promise to establish His Son's reign over all creation.

Teaching Points
Christ's Authority
Jesus' use of this scripture underscores His divine authority and fulfillment of prophecy. Believers are called to recognize and submit to His lordship in every area of life.

Victory Over Opposition
The imagery of enemies as a footstool signifies ultimate victory over all opposition. Christians can find hope and assurance in Christ's triumph over sin and death.

Fulfillment of Prophecy
This passage highlights the importance of understanding Old Testament prophecies and their fulfillment in Christ, encouraging deeper study of scripture.

Spiritual Warfare
Believers are reminded of the ongoing spiritual battle and the assurance of victory through Christ. This calls for vigilance and reliance on God's power.

Eternal Perspective
The promise of Christ's enemies being subdued encourages believers to maintain an eternal perspective, focusing on God's ultimate plan and purpose.

What Does Luke 20:43 Mean
until

Jesus cites Psalm 110:1, saying, “Sit at My right hand until I make Your enemies a footstool for Your feet” (Luke 20:42–43).

• “Until” signals a definite, God-appointed period. The risen Son is presently seated at the Father’s right hand (Acts 2:33–35), and this seat is occupied until the Father’s purpose is fulfilled.

• The word carries both patience and certainty. Christ’s current heavenly rule (Colossians 3:1) unfolds over time, but its conclusion is guaranteed (Hebrews 10:12–13).

• Believers live in this “until” era, confident that the next stage—the final subjugation of evil—will arrive right on schedule (2 Peter 3:9–10).

I make

The initiative is entirely God’s.

• The Father promises to act: “I will put My Spirit upon Him… He will proclaim justice” (Matthew 12:18).

• This divine resolve assures victory. Jesus’ followers rest in God’s power, not human schemes (Zechariah 4:6).

• Scripture repeatedly highlights the Father exalting the Son (Philippians 2:9–11; Revelation 5:6–10). The same God who raised Jesus will complete His plan.

Your enemies

The foes in view are real, personal, and hostile to Christ’s reign.

• They include Satan and demonic forces (Ephesians 6:12).

• They encompass rebellious nations and leaders (Revelation 19:19).

• They also represent human sin and death itself (1 Corinthians 15:25–26).

The passage assures that every enemy—spiritual or earthly—will be compelled to bow (Psalm 2:1–12; Isaiah 45:23).

a footstool

Ancient kings placed conquered foes under their feet as a sign of total dominance (Joshua 10:24).

• God pledges this exact outcome for His Messiah. The enemies will not merely be restrained; they will be utterly subjected (Romans 16:20).

• The image communicates finality. Once underfoot, opposition can never rise again (Malachi 4:3).

• It also highlights triumph shared with believers: “The one who conquers, I will grant him to sit with Me on My throne” (Revelation 3:21).

for Your feet

The Father honors the Son personally.

• The victory is tailored “for” Jesus, spotlighting His unique authority (Hebrews 1:13).

• Christ’s feet once pierced (Luke 24:39–40) will rest upon everything that resisted Him.

• Because believers are united with Christ (Ephesians 2:6), His victory secures their future inheritance (Romans 8:17).

summary

Luke 20:43 guarantees that the ascended Jesus will reign “until” the Father completes the decisive act of making every enemy His “footstool.” The promise underscores God’s initiative, the certainty of Christ’s triumph, the total defeat of evil powers, and the shared victory awaiting all who belong to Him.
