By your patient endurance
This phrase emphasizes the necessity of perseverance in the Christian life. The context of Luke 21 is Jesus' discourse on the end times, where He warns of coming trials and tribulations. Patient endurance is a recurring theme in the New Testament, often associated with faithfulness amidst persecution (James 1:3-4, Romans 5:3-4). The Greek word for "endurance" (hypomonē) suggests a steadfastness that is not passive but active, involving a commitment to remain faithful despite challenges. This endurance is not merely a human effort but is empowered by the Holy Spirit (Galatians 5:22-23).

you will gain your souls
The phrase "gain your souls" can be understood as securing one's eternal life. In the biblical context, the soul represents the whole person, not just the immaterial part. Jesus is teaching that through enduring faith, believers will ultimately receive salvation. This echoes the promise of eternal life found in other scriptures (Matthew 24:13, Revelation 2:10). The concept of "gaining" suggests a reward or outcome of faithful living, aligning with the biblical principle that those who remain steadfast in faith will be saved (Hebrews 10:36-39). This assurance of salvation is a central tenet of Christian hope, rooted in the redemptive work of Christ.

Teaching Points
Understanding Endurance
The Greek word for "endurance" (hypomon?) implies steadfastness and perseverance under trial. Believers are called to remain faithful despite challenges.

Spiritual Gain
"Gain your souls" suggests that endurance is not just about survival but about spiritual growth and ultimate salvation. It emphasizes the eternal perspective over temporal struggles.

Faith in Action
Endurance is an active expression of faith. It requires trust in God's promises and His ultimate plan, even when circumstances are difficult.

Preparation for Trials
Jesus' teaching prepares believers for inevitable trials. Understanding that challenges are part of the Christian journey helps believers to face them with courage and hope.

Community Support
Endurance is often strengthened in community. Believers are encouraged to support one another, sharing burdens and encouraging perseverance.

What Does Luke 21:19 Mean
By

- The single word “By” points to the means through which the promised outcome arrives. It is not through chance, talent, or human schemes, but through the avenue God names.

- Jesus often identifies a distinct channel for blessing or rescue—“By this all men will know that you are My disciples, if you love one another” (John 13:35). In Luke 21, the chosen channel is endurance.

- Acts 14:22 echoes this principle: “We must endure many tribulations to enter the kingdom of God”. The path is laid out; the result is certain for those who walk it.

your patient endurance

- “Patient endurance” is steadfast, hopeful perseverance under pressure—refusing to let go of Christ when trials intensify.

- Scripture consistently joins saving faith with endurance:

• Hebrews 10:36—“You need perseverance so that, after you have done the will of God, you will receive what He has promised.”

• James 1:3-4—testing “produces perseverance. Let perseverance finish its work so that you may be mature and complete.”

• Revelation 14:12—“Here is a call for the endurance of the saints who keep the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus.”

- Endurance is possible because the Spirit empowers believers (Romans 15:5). We cling to Christ, and He keeps us (John 10:28).

you will gain

- “Will gain” is a future-tense certainty, not wishful thinking. The Lord promises that perseverance secures something precious.

- Mark 8:35 assures, “Whoever loses his life for My sake and for the gospel will save it.” Losing now, gaining forever.

- Romans 8:37 insists that in tribulation “we are more than conquerors through Him who loved us.” The gain is guaranteed by God’s faithfulness, not our strength.

- Philippians 3:12 reminds us that while we “press on to take hold,” the reason we succeed is that “Christ Jesus took hold of” us first.

your souls

- The prize is “your souls”—your true, eternal life. Jesus is not offering mere survival of the body but the everlasting safety of the person.

- 1 Peter 1:9 celebrates “the outcome of your faith, the salvation of your souls.”

- Hebrews 10:39 contrasts those who “shrink back to destruction” with those “who have faith and preserve their souls.”

- Matthew 16:26 warns, “What will it profit a man if he gains the whole world, yet forfeits his soul?” The stakes could not be higher; endurance guards what matters most.

summary

Luke 21:19 assures believers that clinging to Christ through every hardship results in the ultimate, irreversible gain—the salvation of our souls. Endurance is the God-appointed means; the promise is rock-solid; the reward is eternal life with Him.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(19) In your patience possess ye your souls.--Better, By your endurance gain ye your lives. The verb, unless used in the perfect tense, always involves the idea of "acquiring" rather than "possessing," and the command so understood answers to the promise, "He that endureth to the end, the same shall be saved," in Matthew 23:13, Mark 13:13. Some of the best MSS., indeed, give this also as a promise, "By your endurance ye shall gain."

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 19. - In your patience possess ye your souls. Quiet, brave patience in all difficulty, perplexity, and danger, was the attitude pressed upon the believers of the first days by the inspired teachers. St. Paul constantly strikes this note.
