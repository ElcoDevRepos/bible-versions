Thus says the LORD
This phrase introduces a divine proclamation, emphasizing the authority and sovereignty of God. It is a common introduction in prophetic literature, underscoring that the message is not from the prophet himself but from God. This phrase is used throughout the Old Testament to affirm the authenticity and divine origin of the message.

who gives the sun for light by day
This highlights God's role as the Creator, referencing Genesis 1:16, where God creates the sun to govern the day. It underscores His power and control over creation, providing order and sustenance. The sun is a symbol of God's provision and faithfulness, as it rises daily without fail.

who sets in order the moon and stars for light by night
This phrase points to God's meticulous design and maintenance of the universe. The moon and stars are part of the celestial bodies created by God to illuminate the night, as seen in Genesis 1:16. This order reflects God's wisdom and the consistency of His creation, which is also a testament to His reliability and care for the world.

who stirs up the sea so that its waves roar
This imagery of the sea and its waves is often used in Scripture to symbolize chaos and the unknown, yet God is depicted as having control over it. This echoes passages like Psalm 89:9, where God is described as ruling over the surging sea. It demonstrates His power over nature and His ability to bring order out of chaos.

the LORD of Hosts is His name
This title, "LORD of Hosts," signifies God's supreme command over the heavenly armies and all creation. It is a title that conveys His majesty, authority, and omnipotence. This name is used frequently in the Old Testament to remind the people of Israel of God's unmatched power and His ability to protect and deliver them.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty
Recognize that God is in control of all creation, from the vastness of the universe to the smallest details of our lives.

Faithfulness of God
Just as the sun, moon, and stars are constant, so is God's faithfulness to His promises. Trust in His unchanging nature.

Order in Creation
God's establishment of order in the universe reflects His desire for order in our lives. Seek to align your life with His divine order.

God's Power Over Chaos
The sea, often a symbol of chaos, is under God's command. In times of turmoil, remember that God can bring peace and order.

Worship the Creator
Let the beauty and order of creation lead you to worship and reverence the Creator, acknowledging His greatness and majesty.

What Does Jeremiah 31:35 Mean
Thus says the LORD

- Jeremiah begins with God’s personal declaration, underscoring that the assurance to Israel rests on divine, not human, authority (Isaiah 55:11; Numbers 23:19).

- The phrase reminds the exiles who hear it that the preceding new-covenant promises (Jeremiah 31:31-34) stand immovably on God’s own word.

Who gives the sun for light by day

- God is not only the Creator of the sun (Genesis 1:16-18) but its ongoing Provider, causing it to rise each morning (Psalm 136:7-8).

- Daylight becomes a daily sign that God’s covenant endures just as reliably (Jeremiah 33:20-21).

- Israel’s hope is tied to something they can physically witness every sunrise.

Who sets in order the moon and stars for light by night

- “Sets in order” highlights deliberate precision; the night sky follows His schedule (Psalm 104:19; Job 38:31-33).

- Israel’s feast calendar hinges on lunar cycles (Leviticus 23:4-6), so national worship depends on God’s exact timing.

- Each star is named and numbered by Him (Psalm 147:4), proving that no detail of His promise will ever slip.

Who stirs up the sea so that its waves roar

- The same God who maintains cosmic order can unleash or still the sea’s power (Psalm 93:3-4).

- Israel recalls the Red Sea parted under His command (Exodus 14:21-22), confirming His mastery over chaos.

- Jesus echoes this authority when He calms the storm (Mark 4:39), tying the Lord of Jeremiah 31:35 to the Lord of the Gospels.

The LORD of Hosts is His name

- “LORD of Hosts” (Yahweh Sabaoth) portrays the Commander of heavenly armies (1 Samuel 17:45; 2 Kings 6:17).

- A name reveals character: He is unchanging (Malachi 3:6); therefore His covenant cannot fail.

- Because all hosts obey Him, no empire can overturn His plans for Israel (Psalm 46:7).

summary

Jeremiah 31:35 anchors God’s new-covenant promises in the fixed, observable order of creation. If sun, moon, stars, and sea still move at His command, Israel’s future—and the salvation flowing from it—stands just as secure. As surely as dawn breaks and waves crash, every word God has spoken will come to pass.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(35, 36) Which giveth the sun for a light by day . . .--The leading thought in the lofty language of this passage is that the reign of law which we recognise in God's creative work has its counterpart in His spiritual kingdom. The stability and permanence of natural order is a pledge and earnest of the fulfilment of His promises to Israel as a people. The new Covenant of pardon and illumination is to be, what the first Covenant was not, eternal in its duration. We have learnt, through the teaching of St. Paul, while not excluding Israel according to the flesh from its share in that fulfilment, to extend its range to the children of the faith of Abraham, the true Israel of God (Romans 2:28-29; Romans 4:11-12).

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 35-37. - Guarantee of Israel's national continuance. A marvellous promise, in the face of the Babylonian Captivity. Verse 35. - The ordinances of the moon; i.e. the moon in its appointed changes (comp. Jeremiah 33:23). Which divideth the sea when, etc.; rather, which stirreth up the sea, so that, etc. This is one of the points of content Jeremiah with the latter part of Isaiah (see Isaiah 51:17; and comp. Job 26:12).
