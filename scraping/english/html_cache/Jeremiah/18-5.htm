Then the word of the LORD came to me, saying,
This phrase introduces a divine revelation to the prophet Jeremiah, emphasizing the direct communication between God and His chosen messenger. In the context of the Old Testament, prophets often received God's word to convey His messages to the people of Israel. This underscores the role of prophets as intermediaries between God and humanity. The phrase "the word of the LORD" is a common biblical expression indicating divine authority and truth, as seen in other prophetic books like Isaiah and Ezekiel. The personal nature of "came to me" highlights Jeremiah's unique calling and the intimate relationship he had with God, reminiscent of other prophets such as Moses and Samuel. This communication is part of a larger narrative where God uses the imagery of a potter and clay to illustrate His sovereignty over nations, a theme that resonates with the biblical understanding of God's control and purpose in history.
Persons / Places / Events
1. Jeremiah
A major prophet in the Old Testament, called by God to deliver His messages to the people of Judah. Known for his prophecies of warning and hope.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal existence and faithfulness to His promises. In this context, He is the one communicating with Jeremiah.

3. The Potter's House
Earlier in Jeremiah 18, God instructs Jeremiah to go to the potter's house, where he observes the potter working with clay. This setting serves as a metaphor for God's sovereignty over Israel.

4. Judah
The southern kingdom of Israel, which is the primary audience of Jeremiah's prophecies. They are often warned of impending judgment due to their disobedience.

5. The Word of the LORD
Represents God's direct communication to His prophets, conveying His will and intentions for His people.
Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty
Just as a potter has control over the clay, God has authority over nations and individuals. We must recognize His ultimate control and trust in His plans.

Human Responsibility
While God is sovereign, humans are responsible for their actions. Judah's disobedience led to their judgment, reminding us to align our lives with God's will.

Repentance and Restoration
The potter's ability to reshape the clay symbolizes God's willingness to restore those who repent. We should seek God's forgiveness and allow Him to mold us anew.

Listening to God's Word
Jeremiah's role as a prophet underscores the importance of listening to God's word. We should be attentive to His guidance through Scripture and prayer.

Hope in God's Plan
Despite warnings of judgment, God's messages often include hope for redemption. We can find assurance in His promises and faithfulness.

What Does Jeremiah 18:5 Mean
Then

• A simple time marker, yet it anchors the scene in real history.

• It links what follows to what Jeremiah has just witnessed at the potter’s house (Jeremiah 18:1-4).

• Scripture often uses “then” to show God’s timely intervention (Genesis 22:11; Acts 9:3-6), underscoring that He speaks at precisely the right moment for His purposes.

the word of the LORD

• Not a vague impression but the authoritative, infallible message of God Himself (Jeremiah 1:2; Isaiah 55:11).

• God’s word is living and active, accomplishing what He intends (Hebrews 4:12; 2 Peter 1:21).

• Here it affirms that what follows carries divine weight, just as every prophetic declaration in Scripture does.

came to me

• Highlights the personal nature of revelation: the Almighty addresses an individual servant (Jeremiah 1:4-5).

• Echoes earlier callings—Samuel hearing his name (1 Samuel 3:10), Amos being summoned from shepherding (Amos 7:14-15).

• Shows that God still reaches out personally, speaking through chosen messengers to guide His people.

saying

• Introduces specific content, assuring us that God communicates in understandable words (Jeremiah 7:1-3; Revelation 1:17-19).

• What God “says” is meant to be heard, heeded, and shared (John 12:49-50).

• Sets the stage for the vivid lesson on divine sovereignty that follows in verses 6-10.

summary

Jeremiah 18:5 presents a concise yet weighty transition: at God’s chosen moment (“Then”), His authoritative message (“the word of the LORD”) reaches a willing prophet (“came to me”) in clear terms (“saying”). The verse reminds us that the Lord speaks personally, purposefully, and powerfully, calling His people to listen and respond.
