Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Now the LORD has fulfilled the word that He spoke.
This phrase highlights the faithfulness of God in keeping His promises. The context is Solomon speaking at the dedication of the temple, acknowledging that God has fulfilled His promise to David regarding his lineage and the establishment of the temple. This reflects the broader biblical theme of God's covenant faithfulness, as seen in passages like Genesis 12:1-3 and 2 Samuel 7:12-13, where God promises to establish David's throne forever. It underscores the reliability of God's word and His sovereign control over history.

I have succeeded my father David,
Solomon's succession to the throne was a significant event in Israel's history, marking the continuation of the Davidic dynasty. This transition was not merely a political event but a fulfillment of God's covenant with David. The peaceful transfer of power from David to Solomon contrasts with the often tumultuous successions in other ancient Near Eastern cultures. It also prefigures the ultimate successor of David's line, Jesus Christ, who is referred to as the "Son of David" in the New Testament (Matthew 1:1).

and I sit on the throne of Israel,
Solomon's reign is characterized by peace and prosperity, fulfilling the promise of a stable kingdom. The throne of Israel symbolizes not just political authority but also spiritual leadership, as the king was expected to lead the nation in covenant faithfulness to God. This phrase connects to the messianic expectation of a future king who would reign on David's throne, as prophesied in Isaiah 9:7 and fulfilled in Christ's eternal reign.

as the LORD promised.
This reiterates the theme of divine promise and fulfillment. God's promise to David in 2 Samuel 7:16 that his house and kingdom would endure forever finds its immediate fulfillment in Solomon's reign. It also points forward to the ultimate fulfillment in Jesus Christ, whose kingdom is eternal. This assurance of God's promises provides a foundation for faith and hope for believers.

I have built the house for the Name of the LORD,
The construction of the temple was a monumental achievement and a central aspect of Solomon's reign. The temple served as the dwelling place of God's presence among His people, a concept rooted in the tabernacle of the wilderness (Exodus 25:8). The phrase "for the Name of the LORD" emphasizes the temple's purpose as a place dedicated to God's glory and worship. It foreshadows the New Testament understanding of believers as the temple of the Holy Spirit (1 Corinthians 6:19).

the God of Israel.
This designation affirms the covenant relationship between God and Israel. It highlights God's unique identity and His special relationship with His chosen people. The temple was not just a national symbol but a testament to the living God who had revealed Himself to Israel. This relationship is foundational to the biblical narrative and is fulfilled in the New Covenant through Jesus Christ, who brings salvation to all nations (Galatians 3:28-29).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Solomon
The son of King David, Solomon is the king of Israel who succeeded his father and built the temple in Jerusalem.

2. David
The former king of Israel, David was a man after God's own heart and received the promise from God that his son would build the temple.

3. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant-keeping God of Israel who made promises to David and fulfilled them through Solomon.

4. The Throne of Israel
Represents the Davidic line of kingship, which God promised would be established through David's descendants.

5. The Temple in Jerusalem
The house built by Solomon for the Name of the LORD, serving as a central place of worship for Israel.
Teaching Points
God's Faithfulness
God's promises are sure and steadfast. Just as He fulfilled His promise to David through Solomon, He will fulfill His promises to us.

Generational Legacy
The faithfulness of one generation can impact the next. David's faithfulness set the stage for Solomon's success.

Purpose and Calling
Solomon was called to build the temple. We should seek to understand and fulfill our God-given purposes.

Worship and Dedication
The temple was built for the Name of the LORD, emphasizing the importance of dedicating our lives and works to God.

Leadership and Responsibility
Solomon's role as king and temple builder highlights the responsibility of leaders to follow God's guidance and serve His people.

What Does 2 Chronicles 6:10 Mean
Now the LORD has fulfilled the word that He spoke

• Solomon opens by celebrating God’s absolute reliability. Every promise uttered to David in 2 Samuel 7:12-13 and echoed in 1 Chronicles 22:9-10 is now a visible reality.

• Scripture always ties God’s character to His word—“Not one of all the LORD’s good promises to Israel failed” (Joshua 21:45; see also Numbers 23:19; Hebrews 10:23).

• For believers today, the completed temple stands as a concrete reminder that what God says, God does (Isaiah 55:11; 2 Peter 3:9).

I have succeeded my father David

• The royal succession was not an accident of politics but a direct act of God’s covenant faithfulness (1 Kings 2:12).

• David’s charge to Solomon in 1 Chronicles 28:5-7 underscores that the kingship itself was a sacred trust, tied to obedience.

• The smooth transition highlights God’s ability to use ordinary human events—crowning a new king—to advance His redemptive plan.

and I sit on the throne of Israel, as the LORD promised

• Solomon’s reign fulfills the “everlasting throne” guarantee in Psalm 132:11-12.

• Every day Solomon occupied that throne, Israel saw living proof of 2 Samuel 7:13.

• The promise-keeping God anchors national stability; political legitimacy flows from divine commitment, not human maneuvering (1 Kings 8:20).

I have built the house for the Name of the LORD, the God of Israel

• The temple’s completion brings to fruition David’s long-held desire (1 Kings 8:17-18) and Moses’ earlier directive to seek “the place the LORD will choose for His Name” (Deuteronomy 12:5, 11).

• “For the Name” stresses that the building exists to display God’s character and glory, never to elevate human achievement (2 Chronicles 2:1, 4).

• The structure foreshadows the ultimate dwelling of God with His people, fully realized in Christ (John 1:14; Revelation 21:3).

summary

2 Chronicles 6:10 strings together four linked testimonies: God’s promise kept, David’s heir enthroned, the covenant throne secured, and the temple raised. Each clause showcases the Lord’s unfailing word and invites us to trust every subsequent promise He makes.

(10) The Lord . . . his word.--And Jehovah hath established, or ratified, his word. Literally, caused to rise up.

Spoken--i.e., promised.

Set.--Seated. (No variant from Kings.)

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 10, 11. - The moment that might have witnessed the utmost inflation of spiritual pride, the acme of ambition, the highest point of even moral kind of grandeur, being touched, is saved from the peril. To the "performing of the Lord" the glory is all given (Luke 1:54, 55, 68-72). Probably delivered from earthly feeling, and sheltered just now from self and human ambition, Solomon was in a very high degree "in the spirit" (Revelation 1:10) on this great day. The moment was a proud moment in Solomon's history, as well there may be proud moments in men's lives, but it was divinely shielded, as divinely inspired. Hereafter, for all that, "the thorn in the flesh" might become very necessary, lest Solomon "be exalted above measure" in the memory of all that had transpired.
