And the Levites from the Kohathites and Korahites
The Levites were a tribe set apart for religious duties in Israel, and within them, the Kohathites and Korahites were specific clans. The Kohathites were responsible for the care of the sanctuary's most sacred objects (Numbers 4:4-15). The Korahites, descendants of Korah, were known for their role in temple worship and music (1 Chronicles 6:31-38). This highlights the importance of organized worship and the role of music in praising God, a tradition that continues in Christian worship today.

stood up to praise the LORD,
Standing up signifies readiness and respect, a common posture in worship. This act of standing to praise God reflects a communal and active participation in worship, emphasizing the importance of physical expression in spiritual devotion. It also indicates a moment of collective unity and purpose among the Levites, setting an example for the congregation.

the God of Israel,
This phrase reaffirms the covenant relationship between God and Israel, a central theme throughout the Old Testament. It underscores God's unique identity and His faithfulness to His chosen people. This covenant relationship is foundational to understanding the narrative of the Bible and is fulfilled in the New Testament through Jesus Christ, who establishes a new covenant with all believers.

shouting in a very loud voice.
The act of shouting in worship signifies exuberance and unrestrained joy. It reflects a deep, heartfelt response to God's greatness and deliverance. This expression of worship can be seen in other biblical instances, such as the fall of Jericho (Joshua 6:20) and the triumphal entry of Jesus into Jerusalem (Matthew 21:9). It demonstrates the power of vocal praise in spiritual warfare and victory, encouraging believers to express their faith boldly.

Teaching Points
The Power of Praise
Praise is a powerful spiritual weapon. The Levites' loud praise was an act of faith and trust in God's deliverance.

Role of Worship Leaders
Worship leaders, like the Levites, play a crucial role in guiding the congregation in worship and setting the spiritual atmosphere.

Unity in Worship
The collective praise of the Kohathites and Korahites illustrates the importance of unity in worship, as they came together to glorify God.

Praise in Adversity
Praising God in the face of challenges, as the Levites did, can shift our focus from the problem to the power and faithfulness of God.

What Does 2 Chronicles 20:19 Mean
The Levites

The Levites were the God-appointed worship leaders of Israel. In 2 Chronicles 20, they step naturally into that calling.

• Numbers 18:5 reminds us that the Levites were “responsible for the care of the sanctuary.”

• Deuteronomy 10:8 says they were “to stand before the LORD to minister to Him and to bless in His name to this day.”

Here, their immediate reaction to God’s promise of victory is worship, showing how spiritual leadership sets the tone for the nation.

From the Kohathites and Korahites

These two clans within Levi had distinguished musical and temple‐service histories.

• Numbers 4:4–15 assigns the Kohathites the holy task of carrying the most sacred objects of the tabernacle.

• Psalm 42, written by the “sons of Korah,” displays their heritage of heartfelt praise.

The presence of these families in the front line of worship underscores how God redeems and reuses entire lineages for His glory.

Stood Up

Their posture is intentional. Standing signifies readiness, respect, and public witness.

• Psalm 134:1 calls servants to “stand by night in the house of the LORD.”

• Nehemiah 9:5 shows Levites commanding Israel to “stand up and bless the LORD your God.”

In a moment of national crisis, the Levites physically rise, modeling faith that is active, not passive.

To Praise the LORD, the God of Israel

Praise is the immediate response to God’s Word delivered through Jahaziel (2 Chronicles 20:14-17).

• 2 Chronicles 5:13 records how “the singers raised their voices in praise,” and God’s glory filled the temple.

• Psalm 22:3 reminds us that God “inhabits the praises of Israel,” linking praise with His manifest presence.

Their focus is clear—Yahweh, covenant God of Israel, is worthy of exaltation before any battle is fought.

Shouting in a Very Loud Voice

Volume reflects conviction. Their loud praise reinforces confidence in God’s promise.

• Ezra 3:11-13 speaks of people shouting “with a great shout” as they lay the temple foundation.

• Psalm 47:1 commands, “Shout to God with a voice of triumph.”

Such exuberant worship drowns out fear and unites the community around God’s assured victory (2 Chronicles 20:22).

summary

2 Chronicles 20:19 shows Levites—specifically Kohathites and Korahites—rising to lead Judah in bold, audible praise immediately after God’s promise of deliverance. Their standing posture, their lineage of worship, and their loud shouts all convey unwavering trust. This verse teaches that when God speaks, faith answers first with wholehearted worship, setting the stage for the victory He has already declared.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(19) And the Levites.--The Levites are the first to rise up, in order to break forth into a hymn of thanksgiving.

Of the children of the Kohathites, and of the children of the Korhites.--Sons of the Kohathites specifies the clan, and sons of the Korhites the house of the musicians who rose up on this occasion. The Korhites were the leading division of Kohath (1Chronicles 6:22). And is explanatory; even, namely some of the sons of Korah. The "sons of Korah" were a guild of Levitical minstrels of the first rank. (Comp. the headings of many psalms, e.g., 44-49, connecting them with their authorship.)

To praise the Lord God of Israel.--1Chronicles 16:14 (hallel).

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 19. - Of the children of the Korhites; i.e., with Revised Version, of the Korahites, who were the best of the Kohathite family (1 Chronicles 6:22; also at head of Psalm 42-49, Authorized Version and Revised Version). Keil would translate, "Of the sons of Kohath, yea, of the Korahites."
