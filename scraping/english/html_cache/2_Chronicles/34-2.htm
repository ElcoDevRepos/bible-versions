And he did what was right in the eyes of the LORD
This phrase highlights the moral and spiritual integrity of King Josiah, who reigned over Judah. Doing what is "right in the eyes of the LORD" implies adherence to God's commandments and a heart aligned with divine will. This is a recurring theme in the Old Testament, where the kings of Israel and Judah are often evaluated based on their faithfulness to God. Josiah's actions are contrasted with those of many of his predecessors who led the nation into idolatry and sin. His reign is marked by significant religious reforms, including the restoration of the temple and the re-establishment of the covenant, as seen in 2 Kings 22-23.

and walked in the ways of his father David
David is often considered the ideal king in the biblical narrative, a man after God's own heart (1 Samuel 13:14). By walking in David's ways, Josiah is associated with a legacy of faithfulness and devotion to God. This connection underscores the importance of Davidic lineage and covenant, which is central to the messianic hope in the Old Testament. Josiah's reforms and his commitment to the Law of Moses reflect David's own dedication to God, as seen in the Psalms and his efforts to establish Jerusalem as the spiritual center of Israel.

he did not turn aside to the right or to the left
This phrase signifies unwavering commitment and steadfastness in following God's commandments. It echoes the instructions given to Joshua in Joshua 1:7, where God commands him to be strong and courageous, not deviating from the law. This imagery of a straight path is used throughout scripture to describe a life of righteousness and obedience (Proverbs 4:27). Josiah's consistent adherence to God's ways, without deviation, sets a standard for leadership and personal conduct, emphasizing the importance of faithfulness and integrity in one's spiritual journey.

What Does 2 Chronicles 34:2 Mean
Did what was right in the eyes of the LORD

“​And he did what was right in the eyes of the LORD​” sets the standard: God Himself is the one evaluating Josiah’s reign.

• “Right” means lining up with the LORD’s revealed will (Deuteronomy 12:28).

• Scripture records other kings who measured up to this divine appraisal—Hezekiah (2 Chronicles 29:2) and Asa (2 Chronicles 14:2)—showing a pattern of blessing when leaders heed God’s Word.

• By placing God’s eyes first, the verse reminds us that public opinion, tradition, or convenience never outrank the absolute authority of Scripture (James 1:22).

Outcome: Josiah’s decisions, reforms, and personal conduct were governed by what pleased the LORD rather than by politics or culture.

Walked in the ways of his father David

“​…and walked in the ways of his father David​​” links Josiah to Israel’s model king.

• David’s “ways” are summed up as wholehearted devotion (1 Kings 15:5; Acts 13:22).

• Josiah bypassed the immediate influence of his father Amon (an idolater, 2 Chronicles 33:22) and chose the covenant faithfulness exemplified by David.

• Walking implies steady, daily movement—consistent obedience over time (1 Kings 3:14).

• The connection underscores the power of a godly legacy; centuries later David’s example still guides a young king (2 Kings 22:2).

Takeaway: honoring faithful predecessors encourages us to imitate their commitment, even when the surrounding generation drifts.

He did not turn aside to the right or to the left

“​…he did not turn aside to the right or to the left​​” pictures unwavering focus.

• The phrase echoes Moses’ charge to Israel (Deuteronomy 5:32) and God’s exhortation to Joshua (Joshua 1:7).

• It stresses balanced obedience—no detours into legalism (adding burdens) or laxity (ignoring commands).

• Practical outworking in Josiah’s life:

– Destroyed idolatrous altars (2 Chronicles 34:3-7).

– Repaired the temple (34:8-13).

– Renewed covenant reading and submission to Scripture (34:18-31).

• Proverbs 4:25-27 paints the same straight-path image, while Hebrews 12:2 calls believers to “fix our eyes on Jesus,” avoiding distraction.

Key idea: steadfast allegiance keeps a believer from drifting, whether pressure pulls to compromise or extremism.

summary

2 Chronicles 34:2 celebrates King Josiah’s wholehearted, consistent obedience. He oriented every choice around what God considered right, patterned his life after David’s example of devoted worship, and stayed on course without wavering. The verse urges us to calibrate our lives by Scripture, follow proven models of faithfulness, and maintain steady devotion that neither veers into compromise nor excess.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(2) And declined . . . the left.--So Kings. Josiah is the only king upon whom this encomium is pronounced. It is equivalent to saying that his observance of the law was perfect. Comp. Deuteronomy 5:32; Deuteronomy 17:20 (the law of the king), 28:14.
