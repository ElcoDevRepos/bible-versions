Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Indeed, God is mighty
This phrase emphasizes the omnipotence of God, a central theme throughout the Book of Job. The Hebrew word for "mighty" here underscores God's unparalleled power and authority over creation. This aligns with other scriptural affirmations of God's might, such as in Psalm 147:5, which declares, "Great is our Lord and mighty in power." The context of Job, a book that deals with suffering and divine sovereignty, highlights that God's might is not just in physical power but in His ability to govern the universe with justice and wisdom.

but He despises no one
This phrase reveals God's impartiality and His love for all creation. Despite His might, God does not look down on anyone, reflecting His just and merciful nature. This is consistent with the biblical teaching that God shows no favoritism (Acts 10:34-35). In the cultural context of the ancient Near East, where gods were often seen as capricious and favoring the powerful, this statement is revolutionary, portraying a God who values each individual.

He is mighty in strength of understanding
Here, the focus shifts to God's wisdom and understanding, which are as mighty as His power. This echoes the theme of divine wisdom found in Proverbs 3:19, "By wisdom the LORD laid the earth’s foundations." The phrase suggests that God's understanding is not just intellectual but also practical, guiding His actions and decisions. In the context of Job, this is crucial, as it reassures that God's governance of the world, including human suffering, is based on perfect wisdom. This understanding is ultimately fulfilled in Christ, who is described as the wisdom of God (1 Corinthians 1:24).

Teaching Points
God's Might and Compassion
God is both mighty and compassionate. His power does not lead to despising anyone, showing that true strength is accompanied by love and understanding.

Understanding God's Nature
Recognizing God's attributes helps us trust Him more deeply, especially in times of suffering or confusion.

The Balance of Power and Wisdom
God's might is perfectly balanced with His understanding. This balance is a model for leadership and decision-making in our lives.

Trusting in Divine Wisdom
In moments of doubt or suffering, we can rely on God's infinite understanding, knowing that He sees the bigger picture.

Humility Before God
Acknowledging God's might and wisdom should lead us to humility, recognizing our limitations and the need for divine guidance.

What Does Job 36:5 Mean
Indeed

– The verse opens with a word of certainty. Elihu is not guessing about God’s character; he is declaring what is absolutely true.

– Scripture regularly begins profound statements with a purposeful affirmation. “The word of the LORD is flawless” (2 Samuel 22:31) and “Truly, truly, I tell you…” (John 5:24) both use a similar device to underscore reliability.

– This “indeed” invites us to anchor our thinking in revealed truth rather than speculation.

God is mighty

– God’s might includes His unlimited power over creation (Jeremiah 32:17) and His sovereign rule over nations (Daniel 4:35).

– Job has already heard about God’s power in nature (Job 9:4–10), yet Elihu reminds him again: divine strength is never in question.

– Mighty does not simply mean “stronger than us”; it means omnipotent. Nothing can thwart His purposes (Isaiah 14:27).

– Because God is mighty, He can keep every promise, uphold every righteous judgment, and provide every needed rescue (Psalm 24:8).

but He despises no one

– Power often corrupts human rulers, but God’s omnipotence is joined to perfect love and justice.

– “God does not show favoritism” (Acts 10:34). He values every person He has made (Genesis 1:27), whether rich or poor (Proverbs 22:2).

– Even in discipline He seeks restoration, not destruction (Ezekiel 18:32).

– Job fears he may have been cast off, yet Elihu insists otherwise: the Almighty never dismisses or belittles those who suffer.

– This balance—limitless strength with unfailing compassion—is echoed in Psalm 145:8–9, where His greatness and His goodness stand side by side.

He is mighty in strength of understanding

– God’s power is guided by perfect wisdom. “With Him are wisdom and strength; counsel and understanding are His” (Job 12:13).

– Finite minds often ask why suffering exists; an infinite mind sees every connection and purpose (Romans 11:33).

– Because His understanding is exhaustive, His timing and methods are flawless, even when hidden from us (Isaiah 55:8–9).

– In seasons of confusion, we rest in the truth that “Great is our Lord, and mighty in power; His understanding has no limit” (Psalm 147:5).

summary

Job 36:5 assures us that the God who rules the universe combines unlimited power, impartial love, and perfect wisdom. He is strong enough to act, caring enough to notice every individual, and wise enough to weave all events into His righteous plan. Such a God is worthy of our trust, especially when life’s mysteries press hardest.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 5. - Behold, God is mighty. The preface over, the argument to prove God's justice begins. First, he "is mighty." How unlikely that any one who is mighty - nay, almighty - should be unjust! Next, he despiseth not any. Job has wrongly charged him with "despising the work of his own hands." In truth, he despises nothing that he has made. "Are not two sparrows sold for a farthing? and one of them shall not fall to the ground without your Father. But the very hairs of your head are all numbered" (Matthew 10:29, 30). Much less, then, is any man despised. Moreover, God is mighty in strength and wisdom; or rather, in strength of undertaking and therefore above the weakness of being unjust.
