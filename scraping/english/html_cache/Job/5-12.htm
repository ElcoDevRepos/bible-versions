He thwarts the schemes of the crafty
This phrase highlights God's sovereignty and justice. The term "thwarts" indicates divine intervention, suggesting that God actively opposes the plans of those who are deceitful or cunning. The "crafty" are often associated with those who use their intelligence for manipulation or deceit, reminiscent of the serpent in Genesis 3:1, who is described as "more crafty than any of the wild animals." This reflects a broader biblical theme where God opposes the proud and deceitful, as seen in Proverbs 6:16-19, which lists things the Lord hates, including a "heart that devises wicked schemes."

so that their hands find no success
The imagery of "hands" represents action and the execution of plans. In biblical times, hands were often symbolic of power and ability. The phrase suggests that despite the intentions and efforts of the crafty, their plans will ultimately fail under God's watchful eye. This aligns with the biblical principle found in Proverbs 19:21, "Many are the plans in a person’s heart, but it is the Lord’s purpose that prevails." It underscores the futility of opposing God's will and serves as a reminder of His ultimate control over human affairs. This can also be seen as a type of Christ, who, in His earthly ministry, often thwarted the plans of the Pharisees and other leaders who sought to undermine Him, demonstrating that divine purpose cannot be hindered by human schemes.

Persons / Places / Events

One of Job's three friends who comes to comfort him. Eliphaz is the speaker in this passage, offering his perspective on God's justice and the fate of the wicked.

2. Job
A righteous man who undergoes severe trials and suffering. The book of Job explores his responses and the dialogues with his friends about the nature of God and suffering.

3. Teman
A region in Edom, known for its wise men. Eliphaz, being a Temanite, is considered wise and knowledgeable.
Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty Over Human Schemes
God is ultimately in control, and human plans that oppose His will are destined to fail. This should encourage believers to align their plans with God's purposes.

The Futility of Craftiness Against God
Craftiness and deceit may seem successful temporarily, but God ensures that such schemes do not prosper in the long run. Believers should pursue integrity and righteousness.

Trust in God's Justice
Even when it seems like the wicked prosper, trust that God will bring justice in His timing. This trust can bring peace and patience in the face of injustice.

Wisdom in Aligning with God's Will
True wisdom is found in seeking God's guidance and aligning our actions with His will, rather than relying on our own cunning or understanding.

What Does Job 5:12 Mean
He thwarts

God actively steps in to block evil intentions. We see this pattern throughout Scripture:

• “The LORD nullifies the counsel of the nations” (Psalm 33:10).

• “Many plans are in a man’s heart, but the purpose of the LORD will prevail” (Proverbs 19:21).

These passages underline that the Almighty is no passive observer; He intervenes, overruling what contradicts His righteousness.

the schemes

Schemes are calculated plans designed to secure an advantage, often at someone else’s expense. Scripture shows that while people can be clever, their plotting never catches God off guard. Compare with Psalm 2:1–4, where nations conspire but the Lord “sits in the heavens and laughs.” His foreknowledge and power render every deceitful blueprint ineffective.

of the crafty

“The crafty” are those who rely on manipulation instead of moral integrity—think of Haman in Esther 3–7 or the advisers against Daniel in Daniel 6. Job 12:16 affirms, “With Him are strength and sound wisdom; the deceived and the deceiver are His”. Even the most sophisticated strategists remain under God’s authority.

so that

This phrase shows purpose. God’s thwarting is not random; He restrains evil to protect His people, uphold justice, and display His glory. Romans 8:28 reminds us that “in all things God works for the good of those who love Him.” His deliberate actions steer history toward His redemptive ends.

their hands

“Hands” symbolize action and execution. A plot is merely an idea until hands put it into motion. When God intervenes, He stops the plan at the point of action—illustrated when the Red Sea closed over Pharaoh’s army (Exodus 14) or when Saul’s weapons could not touch David despite multiple attempts (1 Samuel 19–26).

find no success

The outcome is certain failure. Isaiah 54:17 rings true: “No weapon formed against you shall prosper.” God’s nullification may appear delayed, but it is inevitable. He might confuse communication (Genesis 11), turn plans upon the plotters (Esther 7:10), or simply render efforts fruitless (Psalm 146:4).

summary

Job 5:12 reassures us that God sovereignly disrupts wicked plotting. However intricate the plan or skilled the schemer, the Lord’s intervention empties their hands of power and denies them success. His people can rest in the certainty that righteousness, not cunning, ultimately prevails.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(12) So that their hands cannot perform their enterprise.--Or, so that their hands can do-nothing that is sound or of worth, can accomplish nothing effectual.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 12. - He disappointeth the devices of the crafty; or, frustrateth - makes them of no effect (comp. Psalm 33:10; Isaiah 8:10). Some suppose Eliphaz to insinuate here that Job's apparent wisdom has not been true wisdom, but cunning or craft, and that therefore God has brought it to nought. But to us it rather seems that he enunciates a.general sentiment, and a true one. He is giving examples of the "marvellous things" which God does (ver. 9), and naturally enumerates among them his victories over the craft and cunning of his adversaries (comp. Isaiah 44:25). So that their hands cannot perform their enterprise; literally, and their hands accomplish nothing solid. No substantial result is effected by all their scheming.
