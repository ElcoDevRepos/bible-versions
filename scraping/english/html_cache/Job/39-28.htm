He dwells on a cliff
This phrase refers to the eagle, a bird known for its majestic and solitary nature. In biblical times, eagles were seen as symbols of strength and freedom. The imagery of dwelling on a cliff suggests a place of safety and vantage, away from predators and disturbances. This can be seen as a metaphor for God's protection and the security found in Him, as referenced in Psalm 91:4, where God is described as a refuge and fortress.

and lodges there
The idea of lodging implies a sense of permanence and stability. Eagles build their nests, or eyries, in high places, which are difficult to reach. This reflects the biblical theme of God providing a secure dwelling for His people, as seen in Psalm 61:2-3, where God is described as a rock and a strong tower. The eagle's lodging on high places can also symbolize the believer's call to live a life set apart, elevated above worldly concerns.

his stronghold is on a rocky crag
A stronghold on a rocky crag emphasizes the strength and unassailability of the eagle's position. In ancient Near Eastern culture, rocky crags were often associated with fortresses and places of refuge. This imagery is used throughout Scripture to describe God's protection and the believer's security in Him, as in Psalm 18:2, where God is called a rock and fortress. The eagle's stronghold can also be seen as a type of Christ, who is the ultimate refuge and strength for believers, as highlighted in Hebrews 6:18-19, where hope in Christ is described as an anchor for the soul.

The eagle's dwelling on high cliffs reflects God's sovereign design in creation, showcasing His power and wisdom.

Just as the eagle finds safety in the rocky crags, believers can find security and refuge in God, who is our stronghold.

The eagle's high dwelling place symbolizes the spiritual elevation and perspective we gain through faith and trust in God.

The eagle's life and habitat remind us of the majesty and mystery of God's creation, encouraging us to trust in His unseen plans.

What Does Job 39:28 Mean
He dwells on a cliff

• God points Job to the eagle, a bird that literally makes its home hundreds of feet above the earth (Job 39:27).

• That lofty dwelling testifies to the Creator’s wisdom: the eagle is safe from most predators and has a sweeping view of the land below (Proverbs 30:19).

• Deuteronomy 32:11–13 pictures God’s care for Israel in similar terms, comparing Him to an eagle stirring up its nest high above danger. The Lord is illustrating how He provides vantage and protection for His creatures—and, by extension, for His people.

and lodges there

• “Lodges” speaks of resting or spending the night. The eagle’s nightly confidence, perched on an exposed rock face, highlights its God-given security (Psalm 4:8).

• Psalm 91:1 reminds us, “He who dwells in the shelter of the Most High will abide in the shadow of the Almighty”. As surely as the eagle trusts the height, believers can rest in the Lord’s sovereign care.

• The untroubled lodging of the bird also contrasts with Job’s turmoil, underscoring that peace is found by trusting the One who designed both bird and man (Job 39:13–18).

his stronghold is on a rocky crag

• A “stronghold” is a fortress—a place no enemy can easily reach. The eagle’s nest is literally built into the rock, much like a fortress wall (Obadiah 1:3–4).

• Scripture frequently calls God “my rock and my fortress” (Psalm 18:2; 2 Samuel 22:2). By choosing the eagle’s crag as an illustration, the Lord drives home His own unassailable strength.

• Habakkuk 3:19 declares, “The Lord GOD is my strength; He makes my feet like those of a deer; He makes me walk upon the heights”. The believer’s spiritual footing on the Rock is as sure as the eagle’s nest on the cliff.

summary

Job 39:28 depicts the eagle nesting high on an unreachable cliff, sleeping securely, and relying on the rock itself as a fortress. God uses this vivid picture to reveal His unmatched wisdom, the safety He builds into His creation, and the security He offers to those who trust Him. As the eagle confidently rests on the crag, so the child of God can confidently rest in the Lord, our true and eternal stronghold.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 28. - She dwelleth and abideth on the rook, upon the crag of the rook, and the strong place; literally, the tooth of the rock. The craggy summits of rocks bear a resemblance to the fangs of a tooth. Hence we have in France the Dent du Chat, and in Switzerland the Dent de Jaman and the Dent du Midi.
