Have I not stored up these things
This phrase suggests the idea of God keeping a record of actions and events. In biblical context, it reflects God's omniscience and His ability to remember and account for the deeds of humanity. This concept is echoed in other scriptures, such as Job 14:17, where it speaks of God sealing up transgressions in a bag. It emphasizes God's justice and the certainty that He will address sin and righteousness in His timing.

sealed up within My vaults?
The imagery of sealing and vaults conveys security and preservation. In ancient Near Eastern culture, important documents and treasures were often sealed and stored in secure places to protect them from tampering or loss. This metaphor indicates that God's plans and judgments are secure and unchangeable. It also implies that there is a future time when these stored things will be revealed or acted upon, aligning with prophetic themes found in Revelation 20:12, where books are opened for judgment. This phrase can also be seen as a type of Christ, who is the ultimate fulfillment of God's promises and judgments, as He holds the keys to death and Hades (Revelation 1:18).

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty and Justice
God is sovereign and His judgments are certain. He stores up His judgments, ensuring that justice will be served in His perfect timing.

The Certainty of God's Promises
Just as God stores up judgments, He also stores up blessings for those who are faithful. Believers can trust in His promises and His timing.

The Importance of Obedience
The Israelites are reminded of the consequences of disobedience. Believers today should heed this warning and strive to live in obedience to God's commands.

Trust in God's Timing
God's timing is perfect, and His plans are stored up for the right moment. Believers should trust in His timing, even when it seems delayed.

The Role of Remembrance
The Song of Moses serves as a reminder of God's past faithfulness and future promises. Believers should regularly reflect on God's work in their lives.

What Does Deuteronomy 32:34 Mean
Have I not

- The Lord employs a firm rhetorical question, insisting that His people recognize His active oversight (Isaiah 46:9-10; Jeremiah 23:24).

- Nothing escapes His notice or slips from His control (Psalm 33:10-11).

- The phrase anchors the verse in God’s unassailable authority, laying the groundwork for all that follows.

stored up these things

- “These things” points to Israel’s rebellion and the judgments already pronounced in the Song of Moses (Deuteronomy 32:35-36).

- God keeps an exact record, much like a ledger of debt awaiting settlement (Job 14:17; Romans 2:5; Revelation 18:5).

- His perfect memory guarantees justice; no sin is overlooked and no act of righteousness is forgotten (Hebrews 4:13).

sealed up

- Sealing indicates finality and unchangeable security (Daniel 12:4; Revelation 5:1).

- Judgment is not forgotten; it is merely held until God’s appointed moment (Acts 1:7).

- The seal also reassures the faithful that His promises of salvation are equally secure (John 10:28-29).

within My vaults

- A vault is an impenetrable treasury; only the owner controls access (Job 38:22; Psalm 135:7; Deuteronomy 28:12).

- God’s “vaults” symbolize His sovereign timing—He alone decides when to release stored judgment or blessing (Romans 8:31; Revelation 15:1).

- The imagery underscores that nothing can thwart His purposes.

summary

Deuteronomy 32:34 reveals God as the meticulous, sovereign Judge. He personally records wrongdoing, seals it beyond tampering, and stores it in His own heavenly vault until the precise moment He chooses to act. Evil will not remain unanswered, and righteousness will not go unrewarded. Such certainty both comforts believers and calls us to walk in reverent obedience before the One who never forgets and always fulfills His word.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 34-43. - Notwithstanding the iniquity of Israel and the judgments that should come upon them, God would have compassion upon them for his Name's sake, and would appear for their vindication and defense. The "this" in ver. 34 is by some understood of the sinful doings of the Israelites which God should not forget or overlook. So the Targum of Onkelos: "Are not all their works manifest before me, kept against the day of judgment in my treasures?" So also Calvin, "Quanquam de poenis hunc versum quidam exponunt, acsi Deus assereret diversas earum species apud se paratas esse, quas depromat quoties libuerit: rectius tamen est de sceleribus intelligere." But there is a more 'comprehensive reference here. Not only the deeds of the transgressors, but the judgments that should come on Israel, and also God's interposition on their behalf, were laid up in store with him, and sealed up among his treasures. All that had been done had been noted, and all that should happen was decreed, and should certainly come to pass. The "this' has thus both a retrospective and a prospective reference; it includes both the sin of the nation and God's dealing with them afterwards, as well as his judgments on their enemies. Verse 34. - My treasures. God's treasures contain not only a store of blessing, but also instruments of punishment, which as he sees meet, he sends forth on men (cf. Deuteronomy 28:12; Job 38:22, 23; Psalm 135:7).
