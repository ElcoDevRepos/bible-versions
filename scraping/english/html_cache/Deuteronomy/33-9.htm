He said of his father and mother, ‘I do not consider them.’
This phrase highlights the Levites' dedication to God over familial ties. In the context of the golden calf incident (Exodus 32:25-29), the Levites demonstrated their loyalty to God by siding with Moses, even against their own kin. This act of prioritizing divine command over family reflects the call to love God above all else, as seen in Matthew 10:37, where Jesus speaks of the cost of discipleship.

He disregarded his brothers and did not know his own sons,
The Levites' commitment to God required them to set aside personal relationships. This echoes the radical call to discipleship found in Luke 14:26, where Jesus speaks of the necessity to "hate" one's family in comparison to the love for Him. The Levites' actions serve as a type of Christ's own sacrifice and prioritization of God's will over earthly ties.

for he kept Your word and maintained Your covenant.
The Levites' faithfulness to God's word and covenant is central to their role as priests and teachers of the law (Deuteronomy 31:9-13). Their actions during the golden calf incident solidified their position as guardians of the covenant, a role that foreshadows the New Covenant established through Christ, who perfectly kept God's word and fulfilled the law (Matthew 5:17). This phrase underscores the importance of obedience and faithfulness in maintaining a relationship with God.

Teaching Points
Prioritizing God's Word
The Levites exemplified the importance of placing God's word and covenant above all else, even familial relationships. This teaches us to prioritize our spiritual commitments and responsibilities.

Sacrificial Service
The Levites' willingness to forsake personal ties for the sake of their divine duties serves as a model for sacrificial service in our own lives. We are called to serve God wholeheartedly, sometimes at personal cost.

Guardians of the Covenant
Just as the Levites were tasked with guarding the covenant, Christians today are called to uphold and live out the teachings of the New Covenant through Christ.

Faithfulness in Ministry
The Levites' dedication to their priestly roles encourages us to be faithful in whatever ministry or service God has called us to, ensuring that we are diligent and committed.

What Does Deuteronomy 33:9 Mean
He said of his father and mother, “I do not consider them”

Moses praises Levi for siding with the LORD during the golden-calf crisis (Exodus 32:26-28). Their literal refusal to let even parental affection outweigh God’s command previews Christ’s own standard in Matthew 10:37.

• God’s word outranks family expectations (Genesis 2:24; 1 Samuel 2:30).

• When loyalty to relatives conflicts with loyalty to God, obedience to God comes first.

He disregarded his brothers

Levi’s zeal included confronting siblings who persisted in idolatry, just as Deuteronomy 13:6-8 later requires. This illustrates that true love does not shield sin (Leviticus 19:17; 2 Corinthians 6:14-17).

• Spiritual integrity is more vital than social peace.

• Such firmness prepared Levi for priestly authority (Numbers 18:1-7).

And did not know his own sons

Exodus 32:29 notes each Levite “was against his son and his brother.” “Did not know” shows they put covenant purity above paternal instinct. Malachi 2:4-6 recalls this moment as the reason God granted them the priesthood.

• Discipleship entails costly choices (Luke 14:26).

• God rewards those who sacrifice personal comfort for His holiness.

For he kept Your word

Their actions flowed from simple, literal obedience (Psalm 119:97; John 14:15). They prized what God said above all competing voices.

• Obedience guards worship from corruption (1 Timothy 6:3-5).

• Faithfulness to Scripture unlocks divine favor (Revelation 3:8).

And maintained Your covenant

By defending the covenant at Sinai, Levi preserved Israel’s relationship with God, allowing the priestly system that foreshadowed Christ’s atonement (Hebrews 9:22-24) to continue.

• Covenant loyalty brings blessing (Deuteronomy 7:9).

• Their priesthood anticipates Jesus, our eternal High Priest (Hebrews 7:24-27).

summary

Deuteronomy 33:9 honors Levi for placing God above the closest human ties, proving fierce devotion by safeguarding His word and covenant. Their unwavering obedience won them the priestly privilege and models for every believer the call to honor God first, whatever the cost.
