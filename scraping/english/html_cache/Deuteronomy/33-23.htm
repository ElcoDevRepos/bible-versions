Concerning Naphtali he said:
This phrase introduces the blessing given to the tribe of Naphtali by Moses before his death. Naphtali was one of the twelve tribes of Israel, descended from Naphtali, the sixth son of Jacob and the second son of Bilhah, Rachel's maidservant. The blessings pronounced by Moses in Deuteronomy 33 are reminiscent of Jacob's blessings in Genesis 49, where each tribe receives a prophetic word about its future.

Naphtali is abounding with favor,
The term "abounding with favor" suggests a life filled with grace and goodwill. In the context of the Old Testament, favor often implies divine approval and blessing. The tribe of Naphtali was known for its bravery and skill in battle, as seen in the story of Barak and Deborah in Judges 4-5. This favor could also be seen in the fertile land allotted to Naphtali, which was rich in resources and strategically located.

full of the blessing of the LORD;
Being "full of the blessing of the LORD" indicates a complete and overflowing measure of God's provision and protection. This blessing is not just material but also spiritual, encompassing peace, prosperity, and divine presence. The land of Naphtali, located in the northern part of Israel, was known for its beauty and productivity, aligning with this description of fullness. This phrase also connects to the New Testament, where Jesus began His ministry in the region of Galilee, which includes the land of Naphtali, fulfilling Isaiah's prophecy about a great light shining in Galilee (Isaiah 9:1-2; Matthew 4:13-16).

he shall take possession of the sea and the south.
This part of the blessing refers to the geographical inheritance of the tribe of Naphtali. The "sea" likely refers to the Sea of Galilee, which was part of Naphtali's territory. The "south" could indicate the southern part of their land or a broader influence extending beyond their immediate borders. Historically, the tribe of Naphtali played a significant role in the region, and their land was a key area during the time of Jesus' ministry. The mention of the sea also foreshadows the importance of this area in the New Testament, where Jesus called His first disciples and performed many miracles.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Naphtali
One of the twelve tribes of Israel, descended from Naphtali, the sixth son of Jacob and the second son of Bilhah, Rachel's maidservant. The tribe is known for its valor and is often associated with the northern region of Israel.

2. Moses
The leader of the Israelites who delivered them from Egyptian bondage and led them through the wilderness. In Deuteronomy 33, Moses is giving his final blessings to the tribes of Israel before his death.

3. The LORD
The covenant name of God, Yahweh, who is the source of all blessings and favor. His presence and favor are central to the identity and prosperity of the tribes of Israel.

4. The Lake
Likely referring to the Sea of Galilee, a significant body of water in the northern part of Israel, which would be part of the inheritance of the tribe of Naphtali.

5. Inheritance
The allocation of land to the tribes of Israel as promised by God. This inheritance is both a physical possession and a spiritual blessing.
Teaching Points
Abounding with Favor
God's favor is not limited by human circumstances. Naphtali's blessing reminds us that divine favor can lead to abundant life and opportunities, even in unexpected places.

Full of the Blessing of the LORD
True blessing comes from the LORD. It is not merely material wealth but encompasses spiritual richness and fulfillment. We should seek God's blessing in all areas of life.

Inheritance and Identity
Our spiritual inheritance in Christ is secure and significant. Just as Naphtali received a specific inheritance, believers have an eternal inheritance in the Kingdom of God.

Geographical and Spiritual Significance
The physical land of Naphtali holds spiritual significance, as it becomes a place where Jesus ministers. Our own "territories" can be places where God's work is evident.

Living in the Light of Prophecy
The fulfillment of prophecy in Naphtali's land through Jesus encourages us to trust in God's promises and timing. We are called to live in anticipation of His work in our lives.

What Does Deuteronomy 33:23 Mean
Concerning Naphtali he said

Moses is pronouncing inspired, covenant-shaping words over each tribe just before his death (Deuteronomy 33:1). These blessings are not pious wishes; they are sure promises rooted in God’s faithfulness. Naphtali, sixth son of Jacob (Genesis 30:7-8) and later a full tribe in Israel, receives a concise but lavish word. Together with Jacob’s earlier prophecy, “Naphtali is a doe let loose; he delivers beautiful words” (Genesis 49:21), the Spirit paints a consistent picture of a people marked by freedom, beauty, and fruitfulness.

Naphtali is abounding with favor

• “Favor” points to grace freely bestowed, not earned. Psalm 5:12 affirms that the LORD “surrounds the righteous with favor as with a shield.”

• Historically, Naphtali’s territory in upper Galilee enjoyed plentiful water, fertile hills, and strategic trade routes—tangible proofs of that grace (Joshua 19:32-39).

• When Israel faced oppression, God singled out Naphtali to fight alongside Deborah and Barak (Judges 4:6; 5:18). Their willingness to answer the call reflected — and reinforced — divine favor.

• Ultimately, the greatest evidence of God’s favor on this land was Christ’s ministry there: “He left Nazareth and settled in Capernaum…in the region of Zebulun and Naphtali” (Matthew 4:13-16, citing Isaiah 9:1). The gospel dawned first where God had promised delight.

Full of the blessing of the LORD

• God’s blessings always overflow where His favor rests. James 1:17 reminds us “every good and perfect gift is from above.”

• Naphtali’s inheritance included 19 fortified cities (Joshua 19:35-38); security is a blessing.

• The Sea of Galilee provided abundant fish and commerce (John 21:1-6). Provision is a blessing.

• Spiritual blessing also abounded: many of Jesus’ miracles occurred on Naphtali’s soil (Mark 1:16-34; Luke 5:1-11). Physical and spiritual bounty met in one place, displaying the “fullness” Moses foretold.

He shall take possession of the sea

• “Sea” (Hebrew yam) refers here to the Sea of Galilee, the eastern border of Naphtali’s allotment. Joshua lists cities “by the sea” such as Chinnereth (Joshua 19:35), proving the literal fulfillment.

• Possession means stewardship. The tribe was to settle, cultivate, fish, trade, and defend this coastline.

• Centuries later, fishermen from Naphtali—Peter, Andrew, James, and John—left their nets on that very sea to follow Jesus (Mark 1:16-20). God’s blessing on the water became a launching point for the gospel.

And the south

• Moses adds “the south” (darom) to show the territory would extend beyond the lake shore into the fertile valleys and hills lying southward—rich land suited for agriculture and vineyards (Deuteronomy 8:7-10).

• By holding both the shoreline and the southern reaches, Naphtali enjoyed variety and abundance: fisheries, farmland, and highland pastures.

• The phrase underscores God’s generosity: nothing essential was withheld (Psalm 34:10). The tribe lacked no good thing within its borders.

summary

Deuteronomy 33:23 declares that Naphtali would live under an open heaven of grace, enjoy overflowing blessings, and securely possess both the Sea of Galilee and the fertile southlands. History confirms the promise: Naphtali’s rich territory, its courageous participation in Israel’s battles, and above all the Messiah’s light shining first in Galilee all testify to God’s unfailing, literal faithfulness. What He spoke, He did—inviting every believer to trust His word and walk gratefully in the portion He provides.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 23. - In Jacob's blessing, Naphtali appears invested with the attributes of freedom, gracefulness, and graciousness; here Moses assures that tribe of the Divine grace and blessing, and promises to it prosperity and felicity. Possess thou the west and the south. The word rendered "west" here (ָֻם) properly means sea, and came to signify "west" from the fact of the Mediterranean, or Great Sea, lying to the west of Palestine. The proper meaning of the word is to be retained here. As the territory of Naphtali lay in the north of Canaan, and was far from the sea, the blessing here pronounced upon him must be understood generally of prosperity and felicity. He was to possess riches as of the sea, and genial and fructifying warmth as of the south.
