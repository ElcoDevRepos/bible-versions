Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
But the LORD said to my father David
This phrase highlights the direct communication between God and David, emphasizing the personal relationship they shared. In 2 Samuel 7, God makes a covenant with David, promising that his lineage would endure forever. This covenant is foundational for understanding the messianic prophecy, as Jesus Christ is later revealed to be the fulfillment of this promise, being a descendant of David. The phrase also underscores the importance of divine revelation in guiding the actions of Israel's leaders.

‘Since it was in your heart
The heart, in biblical terms, often represents the center of one's will and desires. David's intention to build a temple for God reflects his deep devotion and desire to honor God. This intention is significant because it shows that God values the intentions and desires of the heart, even if the actions are not carried out. This aligns with 1 Samuel 16:7, where God tells Samuel that He looks at the heart rather than outward appearances.

to build a house for My Name
The "house" refers to the temple, a central place of worship for the Israelites. The temple was to be a dwelling place for God's presence among His people, symbolizing His covenant relationship with Israel. The phrase "for My Name" indicates that the temple was to be dedicated to God's glory and honor. This concept is echoed in Deuteronomy 12:5, where God instructs the Israelites to seek the place He chooses for His Name to dwell. The temple serves as a type of Christ, who is the ultimate dwelling place of God's presence (John 1:14).

you have done well to have this in your heart
God acknowledges David's good intentions, even though David was not the one to build the temple. This affirmation highlights the importance of righteous desires and intentions in the eyes of God. It also serves as a reminder that God values the heart's intentions, as seen in Matthew 5:8, where Jesus teaches that the pure in heart will see God. David's desire to build the temple is a reflection of his commitment to God's glory and serves as an example of faithful devotion.

Teaching Points
The Importance of Intentions
God values the intentions of our hearts. David's desire to build the temple was commendable, even though he was not the one to complete it.

God's Sovereign Plan
While David had a good intention, God's plan was for Solomon to build the temple. We must trust God's timing and purposes, even when they differ from our own plans.

Legacy of Faith
David's heart for God set a foundation for Solomon. Our faith and intentions can influence future generations.

Heart for God's Name
David's desire was to honor God's Name. Our actions and desires should aim to glorify God above all else.

Encouragement in Unfulfilled Desires
Even when our desires are not fulfilled as we expect, God acknowledges and values our heart's intentions.

What Does 1 Kings 8:18 Mean
But the LORD said to my father David

– “But the LORD said to my father David”

• Solomon recalls a private word from God, grounding the temple’s story in divine initiative (2 Samuel 7:4–7; 1 Chronicles 17:3–4).

• The phrase highlights personal relationship: God addresses David by name, confirming that He sees and directs individual lives (Psalm 139:1–4).

• By repeating the LORD’s prior word during the temple dedication, Solomon reminds Israel that this project is unfolding exactly as God foretold (1 Kings 8:15).

Since it was in your heart to build a house for My Name

– “Since it was in your heart to build a house for My Name”

• God affirms David’s motive more than the physical structure; the readiness of David’s heart mattered first (Psalm 132:3–5; Acts 13:22).

• A “house for My Name” stresses God’s covenant presence among His people (Deuteronomy 12:5–11). The temple would showcase His character and glory rather than confine Him (Isaiah 66:1).

• David’s desire sprang from gratitude for God’s faithfulness; he sat in a palace while the ark stayed in a tent (2 Samuel 7:2). God honors such reverent concern for His reputation.

you have done well to have this in your heart

– “you have done well to have this in your heart”

• Though David was not permitted to build the temple (1 Chronicles 22:7-8), God still commends the intention. Motive is measured alongside action (Proverbs 21:2; 2 Chronicles 6:8).

• Divine affirmation shows that righteous desires, even unrealized, carry eternal value (Hebrews 6:10).

• God’s encouragement to David foreshadows New Testament teaching: “for it is God who works in you to will and to act” (Philippians 2:13). He births holy aspirations and then provides the means, in this case through Solomon (1 Chronicles 28:6).

summary

1 Kings 8:18 reveals God’s approval of David’s God-honoring desire, even though another would complete the task. The verse underscores that the Lord sees the heart, values motives aligned with His glory, and weaves individual intentions into His sovereign plan.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 18. - And the Lord said unto David my father [Not, perhaps, totidem verbis. The Divine approval was implied in 2 Samuel 7:11-16, and it may have been expressed at the same time. The narratives of Scripture are necessarily greatly condensed], Whereas it was in thine heart to build an house unto my name, thou didst well that it was in thine heart.
