Hear and testify against the house of Jacob
This phrase is a call to attention and action. The "house of Jacob" refers to the nation of Israel, descendants of Jacob, also known as Israel. The call to "hear" is a common prophetic summons, urging the audience to listen to God's message. The command to "testify against" suggests a legal proceeding, where witnesses are called to present evidence of wrongdoing. This reflects the covenantal relationship between God and Israel, where Israel is held accountable to the laws and commandments given at Sinai. The prophets often served as God's messengers, bringing charges against Israel for their covenant violations.

declares the Lord GOD
This phrase emphasizes the authority and sovereignty of the message. "Lord GOD" is a translation of the Hebrew "Adonai Yahweh," underscoring God's supreme authority. The use of "declares" indicates that this is not merely a suggestion or opinion but a divine proclamation. Throughout the Old Testament, God's declarations are binding and carry the weight of divine judgment or promise. This phrase assures the audience that the message is not from Amos himself but from God, lending it ultimate authority.

the God of Hosts
This title, "God of Hosts," or "Yahweh Sabaoth," highlights God's command over the heavenly armies and all creation. It underscores His power and ability to execute judgment. In the context of Amos, this title serves as a reminder of God's might and the seriousness of the impending judgment against Israel. The term "hosts" can refer to angelic beings or the stars, symbolizing God's control over both the spiritual and physical realms. This title is often used in prophetic literature to emphasize God's ability to carry out His plans and enforce His decrees.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Amos
A prophet from Tekoa, called by God to deliver messages of judgment and repentance to Israel.

2. House of Jacob
Refers to the nation of Israel, descendants of Jacob, who are being called to account for their sins.

3. The Lord GOD
The sovereign ruler, Yahweh, who commands respect and obedience.

4. God of Hosts
A title emphasizing God's supreme authority over all heavenly and earthly armies.

5. Testify
The act of bearing witness or giving evidence, often in a legal or formal setting.
Teaching Points
The Seriousness of God's Call
God’s call to "hear and testify" underscores the gravity of His message. Believers should approach God's Word with reverence and readiness to respond.

Accountability Before God
Just as Israel was held accountable, Christians are reminded that they too will give an account of their lives. This should inspire a life of integrity and obedience.

The Role of Witnesses
The call to testify against Israel serves as a reminder of the importance of witnesses in God's justice system. Believers are called to be witnesses of God's truth in the world.

God's Sovereignty and Authority
The title "God of Hosts" emphasizes God's ultimate control over all creation. Trust in His sovereignty should bring comfort and confidence to believers.

Repentance and Restoration
The prophetic call to testify is not just about judgment but also an invitation to repentance. God desires restoration and reconciliation with His people.

What Does Amos 3:13 Mean
hear

• The verse opens with an imperative: “Hear.” God is summoning His people to listen attentively, just as He did in Deuteronomy 6:4—“Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God, the LORD is One”.

• This call echoes throughout Scripture (Isaiah 1:2; Revelation 2:7), underscoring that revelation brings responsibility. When God speaks, His people must tune in with undivided hearts.

and testify

• “Testify” indicates that what follows is courtroom language. Amos is commissioned as a witness who will give sworn testimony.

• Moses performed a similar role when he gathered the elders to “hear these words and call heaven and earth to witness against them” (Deuteronomy 31:28).

• God often appoints prophets as legal witnesses (Isaiah 43:10), ensuring that no one can claim ignorance when judgment falls.

against the house of Jacob

• The target is “the house of Jacob,” a covenant name reminding Israel of its patriarchal roots (Genesis 32:28).

• Despite their privileged relationship, they have violated covenant stipulations (Amos 3:1-2; Jeremiah 2:4-5), so the testimony is “against” them, not for them.

• The phrase also points to the northern kingdom’s accountability—prosperity had not excused their injustice and idolatry.

declares the Lord GOD

• “Declares” carries the weight of an official proclamation. This is not Amos’s opinion but a divine decree.

• The doubled title “Lord GOD” stresses absolute sovereignty (Isaiah 45:18)—He owns the message and guarantees its fulfillment.

• Every prophetic utterance rests on this bedrock of divine authority (Ezekiel 22:31).

the God of Hosts

• “Hosts” refers to angelic armies and all cosmic forces. The One speaking commands them all (1 Samuel 17:45).

• By ending the sentence this way, God reminds Israel that the Judge has limitless resources to carry out His verdict (Isaiah 6:3; Jeremiah 10:16).

• What He promises—whether discipline or deliverance—cannot be thwarted.

summary

Amos 3:13 is a solemn summons. God calls His people to listen, appoints His prophet to testify, singles out covenant-breaking Israel, and seals the message with titles that trumpet His unrivaled authority and power. The verse warns that the Judge of all the earth is ready to act, and His word is final.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 13. - Hear ye; Septuagint, Ἱερεῖς ἀκούσατε, "Hear, O ye priests." The address is to the heathen, already summoned (ver. 9) to witness the sins of Israel, and now called to witness her punishment, In the house; better, against the house of Jacob, the tribes of Israel (ver. 1). God of hosts. God of the powers of heaven and earth, and therefore able to execute his threats. Septuagint, ὁ Παντοκράτωρ, "the Almighty."
