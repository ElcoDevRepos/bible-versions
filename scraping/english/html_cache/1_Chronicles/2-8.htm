The son of Ethan:
Ethan is listed among the descendants of Judah, specifically in the genealogies that trace the lineage of the tribe. Ethan is also mentioned in 1 Kings 4:31 as one of the wise men whom Solomon surpassed in wisdom. This connection highlights the importance of wisdom and understanding in the biblical narrative. Ethan's inclusion in the genealogy underscores the significance of family lineage in Israelite culture, where ancestry was crucial for identity and inheritance. The genealogies in Chronicles serve to establish the continuity of God's covenant with His people, tracing the line through which the promises to Abraham and David would be fulfilled.

Azariah:
Azariah, meaning "Yahweh has helped," is a name that appears frequently in the Old Testament, indicating its popularity and the cultural emphasis on divine assistance. This particular Azariah is not extensively detailed in the biblical text, but the name itself reflects a common theme in Israelite history: reliance on God's help and intervention. The name Azariah is also associated with several other notable biblical figures, such as Azariah the priest in 2 Chronicles 26:17-20, who opposed King Uzziah's attempt to burn incense in the temple. This highlights the role of priests and prophets in maintaining the covenantal relationship between God and Israel. The recurrence of the name Azariah throughout the Bible serves as a reminder of God's faithfulness and the importance of seeking His guidance and support.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Ethan
A descendant of Judah, Ethan is listed in the genealogies of 1 Chronicles. His name means "enduring" or "strong" in Hebrew, and he is noted for his wisdom in other parts of the Bible.

2. Azariah
The son of Ethan, Azariah's name means "Yahweh has helped" in Hebrew. This name is common in the Old Testament and often associated with individuals who have a significant role in Israel's history.

3. Judah
The tribe from which Ethan and Azariah descend. Judah is one of the twelve tribes of Israel, known for its leadership and the lineage of King David and ultimately Jesus Christ.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Genealogies
Genealogies in the Bible serve to connect us to God's unfolding plan through history. They remind us of God's faithfulness across generations.

Names and Their Meanings
Biblical names often carry significant meanings that reflect God's character or actions. Understanding these can deepen our appreciation of the text.

Wisdom and Legacy
Ethan's mention in connection with wisdom challenges us to consider the legacy we leave. Are we known for godly wisdom that points others to Christ?

God's Help in Our Lives
Azariah's name, meaning "Yahweh has helped," is a reminder of God's continual assistance in our lives. Reflect on how God has helped you in your journey.

Lineage of Faith

What Does 1 Chronicles 2:8 Mean
The son

1 Chronicles 2:8 states plainly, “The son of Ethan: Azariah.” The simple word “son” anchors the verse in the flow of a literal genealogy—an unbroken chain stretching from Adam (Genesis 5:1-3) through the patriarchs (Exodus 3:6) and ultimately to Christ (Luke 3:23-38). By recording each generation:

• Scripture safeguards the historical reliability of the covenant line (cf. Numbers 1:18).

• It affirms God’s meticulous care for individual families (Psalm 78:5-7).

• It shows that divine promises are transmitted person-to-person (Galatians 3:16).

The verse may appear small, yet it serves as one indispensable link in that larger, Spirit-breathed chain.

of Ethan

Ethan has already appeared in the genealogy of Zerah (1 Chronicles 2:6). Elsewhere a man named Ethan is noted for wisdom that only Solomon surpassed (1 Kings 4:31) and for composing Psalm 89 (title). By identifying Azariah “of Ethan,” the chronicler:

• Roots Azariah in a distinguished household, reminding us that family legacy matters (Proverbs 17:6).

• Shows God’s faithfulness to every branch of Judah’s line, even the lesser-known ones (1 Chronicles 15:17-19).

• Highlights that wisdom, worship, and service can characterize an entire family tree (cf. Psalm 78:70-72).

Azariah

Though this verse lists only one descendant, Scripture mentions several later men named Azariah who served as priests, prophets, or kings (e.g., 2 Kings 14:21; 2 Chronicles 26:17-20; Nehemiah 8:7). This recurring name signals:

• God’s ongoing provision of leaders in every generation (Jeremiah 1:5).

• The continuity of Judah’s witness, even after exile (Ezra 3:2).

• A reminder that a single faithful life can influence many who follow (2 Timothy 1:5).

Azariah’s placement here assures readers that God did not overlook any part of Judah’s lineage; every life—well-known or obscure—has a place in His redemptive tapestry.

summary

1 Chronicles 2:8 may read like a brief footnote, yet “The son of Ethan: Azariah” underscores God’s precise record-keeping, the value He places on family heritage, and His pattern of raising servants across generations. The verse affirms that no name is accidental, no link is missing, and every believer today stands within that same faithful continuity the Lord has carefully preserved.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(8) The sons of Ethan.--Nothing is known of this Ethanite Azariah. It seems plain that the writer wished to name only the historically famous members of the Zarhite branch of Judah--in 1Chronicles 2:6, the four proverbial sages; in 1Chronicles 2:7, Achar who brought woe upon Israel by taking of the devoted spoils of Jericho.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 8. - The line through Ethan, another of the seven grandsons, seems to stop with Azariah, a name found nowhere else.
