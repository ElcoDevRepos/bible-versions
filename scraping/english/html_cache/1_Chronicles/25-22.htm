Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
the fifteenth to Jeremoth,
Jeremoth is listed as the fifteenth in the order of musicians assigned by David for the service of song in the house of the Lord. This reflects the organized structure David implemented for worship, emphasizing the importance of music in temple services. The name Jeremoth appears several times in the Old Testament, often associated with the Levitical line, indicating a heritage of service to God.

his sons, and his brothers—
This phrase highlights the familial nature of the Levitical service. The inclusion of both sons and brothers suggests a communal and generational involvement in worship, which was a common practice among the Levites. It underscores the importance of family in the transmission of faith and duties, as well as the collective responsibility in worship.

12 in all;
The number twelve is significant throughout the Bible, often symbolizing completeness or divine order, as seen in the twelve tribes of Israel and the twelve apostles. In this context, the number may represent a complete unit of musicians, ensuring that worship was conducted in an orderly and full manner. This reflects the meticulous planning and dedication to worship that characterized David's reign.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jeremoth
A leader of one of the divisions of musicians appointed by King David for the service of the house of the Lord. His name is listed among those who were responsible for prophesying with lyres, harps, and cymbals.

2. Sons and Brothers
This refers to the family members of Jeremoth who were part of the musical division. The inclusion of family highlights the communal and familial aspect of worship in the temple.

3. Twelve in All
This number signifies the total members in Jeremoth's division. The number twelve often symbolizes completeness or divine order in the Bible, reflecting the organized structure of temple worship.
Teaching Points
The Role of Music in Worship
Music is a vital component of worship, serving as a means to prophesy and glorify God. Just as Jeremoth and his family were dedicated to this service, believers today are encouraged to use their musical gifts for God's glory.

Family Involvement in Ministry
The inclusion of Jeremoth's sons and brothers highlights the importance of involving family in ministry. Families can serve together, strengthening their bonds and their collective witness for Christ.

Order and Structure in Worship
The organized division of musicians into groups of twelve reflects the importance of order in worship. Structured worship allows for a more focused and reverent approach to honoring God.

The Significance of Numbers in Scripture
The number twelve, often associated with completeness, reminds us of God's perfect design and order. Recognizing the significance of numbers can deepen our understanding of biblical texts.

What Does 1 Chronicles 25:22 Mean
the fifteenth

• In the casting of lots for the 24 divisions of temple musicians, the fifteenth lot marked a specific place in the roster (1 Chronicles 25:8-9).

• God directed the process, ensuring fairness and divine order—just as He had done when assigning priestly courses in 1 Chronicles 24:7-18.

• The number fifteen reminds us the service was neither random nor optional; every slot mattered in God’s carefully arranged worship plan (Proverbs 16:33).

to Jeremoth

• “Jeremoth” appears earlier in the list of Heman’s descendants (1 Chronicles 25:4), so this lot belongs to that godly musical family dedicated to prophesying with lyres, harps, and cymbals (1 Chronicles 25:1).

• His name being singled out underscores personal accountability in ministry (Colossians 3:23-24).

• Examples of named worship leaders—such as Asaph, Heman, Jeduthun (1 Chronicles 6:33-38)—show how God values individual faithfulness inside corporate worship.

his sons

• Temple music was a family affair: fathers discipled children to carry on praise (Psalm 78:4-6; Deuteronomy 6:6-7).

• Serving together deepened both skill and spiritual legacy, reflecting God’s heart for generational continuity (2 Timothy 1:5).

• Their inclusion testifies that no age group is sidelined in worship; everyone can proclaim God’s glory (Psalm 148:12-13).

and his brothers

• “Brothers” widens the circle to cousins or clan members, spotlighting teamwork (Psalm 133:1).

• Worship is communal, echoing New-Testament body life where each part supplies what the others lack (1 Corinthians 12:12-26).

• Shared service fostered accountability, much as the singers rotated together under royal oversight (1 Chronicles 25:6).

12 in all

• Twelve signifies completeness and governmental order throughout Scripture—12 tribes (Genesis 35:22-26), 12 apostles (Matthew 10:2-4), 12 foundation stones (Revelation 21:14).

• Every group of twelve musicians matched the 24 courses, totaling 288 trained singers (1 Chronicles 25:7). This symmetry shows that God loves both excellence and structure.

• The exact headcount affirms historical accuracy; these were real people filling real slots at real times (Luke 1:5 compares similar priestly scheduling centuries later).

summary

1 Chronicles 25:22 records more than a head-count; it reveals God’s orderly, multi-generational design for worship. The fifteenth lot underscores divine control; Jeremoth’s name highlights personal calling; the mention of sons and brothers shows family and community engagement; the total of twelve illustrates completeness. Together, the verse encourages us to serve in our appointed places, side by side with family and fellow believers, confident that every role matters in God’s perfect orchestration of praise.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(22) The fifteenth to Jeremoth.--Heb., to fifteenth, to Jeremoth; i.e., as regards the fifteenth lot, it was for Jeremoth. The construction is the same to the end of the chapter.

Spelling, and probably pronunciation, fluctuated between Jeremoth and Jerimoth (1Chronicles 25:4). (Comp. 1Chronicles 23:23; 1Chronicles 24:30.) The LXX. and Vulg. spell "Jerimoth" in both places here; Syriac, "Jarm-th"; Arabic, "J?r?m?th" and "Jarm-th."
