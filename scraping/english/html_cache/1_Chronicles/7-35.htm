The sons of his brother Helem:
Helem is mentioned as a brother, indicating a familial connection within the tribe of Asher, one of the twelve tribes of Israel. This highlights the importance of family lineage and tribal affiliation in ancient Israelite society. The genealogies in 1 Chronicles serve to establish the continuity of God's people and their inheritance in the Promised Land. The mention of Helem and his sons underscores the detailed record-keeping and the value placed on heritage and identity.

Names in the Bible often carry significant meanings and can reflect the hopes or experiences of the family. Zophah's inclusion in the genealogy emphasizes the continuation of the family line and the fulfillment of God's promises to the tribes of Israel.

Imna,
Imna, another son of Helem, is part of the genealogical record that traces the lineage of the tribe of Asher. The name Imna could be related to the Hebrew word for "right hand" or "prosperity," suggesting a position of favor or success. This reflects the cultural importance of names and their meanings in the biblical narrative. The tribe of Asher was known for its prosperity and abundance, as prophesied by Jacob in Genesis 49:20, which may be symbolically connected to Imna's name.

Shelesh is also listed among the sons of Helem. The name Shelesh might mean "third" or "triplet," indicating his position in the family or a characteristic of his birth. The genealogical records in Chronicles often include such details to preserve the history and structure of the Israelite tribes. This meticulous recording of names and relationships highlights the value placed on each individual within the community and their role in the unfolding story of God's people.

What Does 1 Chronicles 7:35 Mean
The sons

1 Chronicles 7:35 records, “The sons of his brother Helem: Zophah, Imna, Shelesh, and Amal”. Genealogical notices like this are more than roll calls:

• They confirm that God fulfills His promise to multiply His people (Genesis 17:6; Psalm 127:3-5).

• They anchor Israel’s tribal structure, which determined land inheritance, military organization, and worship service (Numbers 26:55-56; Joshua 13-19).

• They remind us that every generation matters in God’s unfolding plan, a theme echoed in Matthew 1:1-17 where another genealogy leads to Christ.

of his brother Helem

Helem stands within Asher’s family line (1 Chronicles 7:30-36). By noting “his brother Helem,” the writer highlights:

• Family interconnectedness—each branch supports the identity of the whole tribe.

• Continuity—Helem’s household carries forward the blessing given to Asher, “May he be favored by his brothers and dip his foot in oil” (Deuteronomy 33:24-25).

• Reliability of the record—linking Helem to his brothers Japhlet and Shemer (vv. 33-34) shows the chronicler’s careful preservation of history.

Zophah

First‐named among Helem’s sons:

• Found again in verse 36 with his own offspring (Suah, Harnepher, Shual, Beri, and Imrah), illustrating fruitfulness.

• Demonstrates how God’s covenant faithfulness extends to each new household (Genesis 17:7).

• Reflects the prophetic blessing of abundance on Asher’s tribe (Genesis 49:20).

Imna

Second son listed:

• Variant spellings elsewhere (Imnah in Numbers 26:44) show the same family flourishing across passages.

• His inclusion underscores that even lesser-known figures are valued by God (Luke 12:7).

• Points to shared responsibility within the tribe; later, another Imnah assists in temple duties (2 Chronicles 31:14), showing generational service.

Shelesh

Third in sequence:

• Although Scripture records no exploits, his presence secures legal testimony for land and lineage (1 Kings 21:3 reflects how inheritance laws depended on such records).

• Reminds believers that quiet faithfulness is as significant to God as visible achievements (1 Corinthians 12:22).

Amal

Fourth son:

• His name closes the list, yet verse order does not imply lesser importance; all four inherit Asher’s blessing equally (Joshua 19:24-31).

• Demonstrates God’s impartiality within the covenant community (Acts 10:34-35).

summary

1 Chronicles 7:35 affirms that God faithfully extends His covenant through every branch of Asher’s family. By naming Zophah, Imna, Shelesh, and Amal, Scripture preserves their legacy, secures tribal inheritance, and highlights that each individual—famous or obscure—matters in God’s redemptive story.
