Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
in gold and silver, bronze and iron—craftsmen beyond number.
This phrase highlights the abundance of resources and skilled labor available for the construction of the temple. Gold and silver were precious metals often used in the tabernacle and temple for items of worship, symbolizing purity and divinity. Bronze and iron were used for structural and functional purposes, indicating the temple's strength and durability. The mention of "craftsmen beyond number" suggests a vast workforce, reminiscent of the skilled laborers who constructed the tabernacle in Exodus 31:1-6. This abundance reflects God's provision and the importance of the temple as a central place of worship for Israel.

Now begin the work,
David's command to begin the work signifies a transition from preparation to action. It underscores the urgency and importance of the temple project, which was to be a dwelling place for God's presence among His people. This directive also marks a pivotal moment in Israel's history, as the temple would become the focal point of religious life. The phrase echoes the biblical theme of obedience to God's commands, as seen in the construction of the tabernacle (Exodus 25:8-9).

and may the LORD be with you.”
This blessing invokes God's presence and favor upon the work, emphasizing the need for divine guidance and support. It reflects the biblical principle that human efforts are ultimately dependent on God's blessing for success, as seen in Psalm 127:1. The phrase also foreshadows the New Testament promise of God's presence with His people through Jesus Christ (Matthew 28:20), who is the ultimate fulfillment of the temple as the dwelling place of God among men (John 1:14).

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The King of Israel who is preparing for the construction of the temple. He is speaking to his son Solomon, encouraging him to begin the work.

2. Solomon
David's son, who is chosen to build the temple. David is providing him with resources and encouragement to fulfill this task.

3. Temple Construction
The event of building the temple in Jerusalem, which is a significant project for the Israelites, symbolizing God's presence among His people.

4. Jerusalem
The city where the temple is to be built, serving as the spiritual and political center of Israel.

5. Resources (Gold, Silver, Bronze, Iron)
The materials mentioned are abundant, signifying God's provision and the importance of the temple.
Teaching Points
God's Abundant Provision
God provides abundantly for His purposes. Just as there was no limit to the resources for the temple, God equips us with what we need to fulfill His calling in our lives.

The Call to Action
David's command to "Arise and begin the work" is a reminder that faith requires action. We are called to step out in obedience, trusting in God's presence and provision.

The Importance of God's Presence
The phrase "may the LORD be with you" underscores the necessity of God's presence in our endeavors. Success in God's work is not just about resources but about His guidance and blessing.

Legacy and Preparation
David's preparation for the temple teaches us about leaving a legacy of faith and preparing the next generation to continue God's work.

What Does 1 Chronicles 22:16 Mean
Gold and silver

“gold and silver” (1 Chronicles 22:16)

• David has already “dedicated the gold and silver” he conquered to the house of the LORD (1 Chronicles 18:11). The huge reserves he passes to Solomon underline God’s faithful provision—He supplies every need for His work (Philippians 4:19).

• Gold speaks of purity and divine glory (Exodus 25:10-11; Revelation 21:18). Silver often pictures redemption and value (Exodus 30:11-16). In the temple these metals broadcast that worship must be both pure and costly.

• By listing them first, David reminds Solomon that the most precious resources are already in place. The young king can proceed with confidence, knowing the LORD has front-loaded the project with abundant riches.

Bronze and iron—craftsmen beyond number

“bronze and iron—craftsmen beyond number”

• Bronze and iron emphasize durability and strength (1 Kings 7:15; Deuteronomy 8:9). They will form the pillars, basins, and structural supports that make daily ministry possible.

• “Craftsmen beyond number” echoes how the Spirit filled Bezalel and Oholiab with skill for the tabernacle (Exodus 31:1-6). God once again provides capable people in overwhelming supply (2 Chronicles 2:7).

• The sheer scale—materials piled high and workers without limit—testifies that the temple is no ordinary building. It proclaims the greatness of the God who is about to dwell among His people (2 Chronicles 6:18).

Now begin the work

“Now begin the work”

• Preparation must turn into action. David’s charge mirrors his later words: “Be strong and do it” (1 Chronicles 28:10). Faith expresses itself through obedience (James 2:17).

• Delayed obedience risks drifting; immediate obedience delights the LORD (Psalm 119:60).

• The command answers every excuse Solomon might raise: supplies, workers, plans—everything is ready. All that remains is wholehearted engagement.

And may the LORD be with you

“and may the LORD be with you.”

• David invokes the covenant name, assuring Solomon of God’s personal presence (Exodus 3:14-15). The same promise bookends Scripture—“I am with you always” (Matthew 28:20).

• With God alongside, opposition cannot prevail (Romans 8:31). Success is measured not only in completed stones and ornaments but in the ongoing fellowship between King, people, and their God (1 Chronicles 22:11-13).

• The blessing roots the entire venture in divine partnership: human labor undergirded by heavenly power (Psalm 127:1).

summary

1 Chronicles 22:16 assures Solomon that God has already supplied abundant precious metals, strong materials, and limitless skilled workers. David calls him to translate this provision into decisive action, confident that the LORD Himself will accompany the effort. The verse blends divine generosity, human responsibility, and covenant presence, encouraging every believer to step boldly into God-assigned tasks, trusting that He equips, sustains, and dwells with those who obey.
