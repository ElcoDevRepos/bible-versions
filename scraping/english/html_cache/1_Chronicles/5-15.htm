Ahi son of Abdiel
Ahi is a name meaning "my brother" or "brotherly." In the genealogical records of the tribes of Israel, names often carry significant meanings that reflect character or destiny. Abdiel, meaning "servant of God," suggests a lineage devoted to the worship and service of Yahweh. This lineage emphasizes the importance of faithfulness to God, a recurring theme in the Chronicles. The mention of Ahi and his father Abdiel highlights the continuity of faith and leadership within the tribe of Gad, one of the tribes settled east of the Jordan River.

the son of Guni
Guni is a name that appears in the genealogies of the tribes of Israel, specifically within the tribe of Gad. The tribe of Gad was known for its warrior-like qualities and played a significant role in the defense of Israel's territories. The genealogical record serves to establish the legitimacy and heritage of the tribal leaders, ensuring that leadership is traced back to the patriarchs of Israel. This connection to Guni underscores the importance of maintaining tribal identity and heritage.

was head of their family
Being the head of a family or clan in ancient Israel carried significant responsibility. It involved leadership in both spiritual and practical matters, including the administration of justice, the management of resources, and the defense of the community. The role of a family head was crucial in maintaining the social and religious order. This position also involved representing the family in tribal councils and before God. The emphasis on family leadership reflects the broader biblical theme of covenantal responsibility, where leaders are accountable to God for the welfare of their people.

3. Guni
The grandfather of Ahi. The name Guni means "protected" or "defended" in Hebrew, suggesting a lineage that values divine protection and strength.

4. Tribe of Gad
The context of this verse is within the genealogies of the tribe of Gad, one of the twelve tribes of Israel. The tribe of Gad was known for its warriors and settled on the east side of the Jordan River.

5. Genealogical Record
This verse is part of a larger genealogical record in 1 Chronicles, which serves to establish the heritage and tribal affiliations of the Israelites.

What Does 1 Chronicles 5:15 Mean
Ahi

• Scripture states, “Ahi son of Abdiel, son of Guni, was head of their family” (1 Chronicles 5:15). The mention of Ahi by name reminds us that God records individuals, not merely crowds (compare Exodus 32:32-33; Luke 12:7).

• Ahi appears within the genealogy of the tribe of Gad (1 Chronicles 5:11-17). This positions him in the covenant community that crossed the Jordan and settled east of it (Joshua 13:24-28).

• His placement after a long list of ancestors (1 Chronicles 5:14) underscores God’s faithfulness across generations (Psalm 100:5; Hebrews 13:8).

son of Abdiel

• Identifying Ahi as Abdiel’s son roots him in a specific household, stressing accountability and continuity (Numbers 1:18; 1 Chronicles 9:1).

• “Abdiel” means “servant of God,” and the text implies that this family line maintained devotion to the LORD (Deuteronomy 6:6-9; Joshua 24:15).

• Genealogies mark the fulfillment of God’s promise to multiply His people (Genesis 15:5; Isaiah 51:2).

the son of Guni

• Going back another generation broadens the lens from immediate fatherhood to clan identity, anchoring Ahi in the larger lineage of Gad (Genesis 46:16; Numbers 26:15-18).

• By tracing to Guni, Scripture links Ahi with earlier patriarchal faithfulness, showing how heritage shapes present leadership (Psalm 78:5-7; 2 Timothy 1:5).

• Each named ancestor strengthens the legal and historical credibility of Israel’s tribal records (Ezra 2:59-62; Nehemiah 7:5).

was head of their family

• “Head” signals recognized authority. Like the chiefs listed in Numbers 1:4-16 and 1 Chronicles 5:24, Ahi bore responsibility for judgment, defense, and worship on behalf of his clan.

• Leadership in Israel combined spiritual and practical oversight (Deuteronomy 31:9-13; 1 Samuel 7:15-17). A faithful head modeled obedience and ensured the family’s alignment with God’s law (Joshua 1:8; 1 Timothy 3:4-5).

• The verse sustains the Bible’s pattern that leadership flows along covenant lines and is accountable to God first (2 Samuel 23:3-4; Hebrews 13:17).

summary

1 Chronicles 5:15 highlights one man, Ahi, within the tribe of Gad, tracing him through Abdiel to Guni and identifying him as family head. The verse demonstrates God’s meticulous care for individual lives, affirms the reliability of Israel’s genealogies, and showcases a model of covenant leadership that guards both family and faith across generations.
