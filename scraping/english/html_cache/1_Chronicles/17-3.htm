But that night
This phrase indicates a specific time when God chose to communicate with Nathan. The immediacy of "that night" suggests the urgency and importance of the message. In biblical narratives, nighttime often serves as a setting for divine revelations, as seen in Genesis 28:12 with Jacob's dream. The timing underscores God's active involvement in the unfolding events.

the word of God came to Nathan,
Nathan was a prophet during the reign of King David, playing a crucial role in advising the king. The phrase "the word of God came" signifies divine revelation, a common expression in the Old Testament indicating that God is directly communicating His will. This is similar to how God spoke to other prophets, such as Samuel (1 Samuel 3:1-10) and Elijah (1 Kings 17:2). Nathan's role as a prophet is pivotal, as he conveys God's messages to David, including the Davidic Covenant, which has messianic implications.

saying,
The introduction of God's message highlights the authority and clarity of divine communication. In the context of 1 Chronicles 17, God is about to reveal His plans for David and his descendants, which includes the promise of an everlasting kingdom. This foreshadows the coming of Jesus Christ, who is often referred to as the Son of David, fulfilling the covenantal promise of an eternal kingdom (Luke 1:32-33).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Nathan
A prophet during the reign of King David, Nathan played a crucial role in communicating God's messages to David. He is known for his wisdom and courage in delivering God's word, even when it was challenging.

2. David
The King of Israel, known for his heart after God. In this chapter, David expresses his desire to build a temple for the Lord, which prompts God's response through Nathan.

3. God
The sovereign deity of Israel, who communicates His will and plans through His prophets. In this passage, God speaks to Nathan to convey a message to David.

4. The Word of God
Represents God's direct communication and revelation to His people, often delivered through prophets in the Old Testament.

5. The Night
Symbolically, night can represent a time of revelation and divine communication, as God often spoke to His prophets during the night.
Teaching Points
The Role of Prophets
Prophets are God's messengers, chosen to deliver His word to His people. Nathan's role reminds us of the importance of listening to God's voice and being obedient to His guidance.

God's Sovereignty in Communication
God chooses the time and manner in which He communicates with His people. His message to Nathan at night signifies that God is always at work, even when we are at rest.

The Importance of Divine Revelation
God's word is central to understanding His will and purpose. We should seek His revelation through prayer, scripture, and the guidance of the Holy Spirit.

Obedience to God's Plan
David's desire to build a temple was noble, but God's plan was different. We must be willing to submit our desires to God's greater plan, trusting in His wisdom and timing.

The Continuity of God's Promises
The message to David through Nathan is part of God's unfolding plan of redemption, culminating in Jesus Christ. This reminds us of the faithfulness of God to His promises throughout history.

What Does 1 Chronicles 17:3 Mean
But that night

• God’s timing is intentional. The Lord chooses the very night after David shares his plans with Nathan (1 Chron 17:1–2) to redirect them, underscoring divine initiative (cf. Genesis 28:11–13; 1 Samuel 3:3–4; Psalm 30:5).

• Night often symbolizes quiet dependence, when human activity ceases and God’s activity becomes clear (Psalm 16:7; Job 33:14–16).

• Lesson: When we pause, God speaks; His purposes unfold according to His calendar, not ours.

the word of God

• What arrives is not a hunch or private opinion but God’s revealed, authoritative message (Hebrews 4:12; Isaiah 55:11).

• Scripture repeatedly testifies that the “word of the LORD” confronts human plans and realigns them with heaven’s will (Jeremiah 23:29; Psalm 119:105).

• Here, God’s word will correct Nathan’s earlier assent, proving that even trusted servants must submit to fresh revelation (Proverbs 19:21).

came to Nathan

• Nathan receives the message, confirming his prophetic office (2 Samuel 12:1; 1 Kings 1:23).

• God mediates revelation through chosen spokesmen so His people may hear with clarity (Amos 3:7; 2 Peter 1:21).

• David, though king, must hear from God through a prophet, reminding us that no earthly position exempts anyone from divine authority (2 Samuel 7:17).

saying

• The verb signals that specific words are about to be delivered—words that will form a covenant promise of an enduring house for David (1 Chron 17:4–14).

• Whenever God “says,” His speech carries life-changing power (Genesis 1:3; Jeremiah 1:4; Revelation 1:10).

• Anticipation builds: the forthcoming declaration will reshape Israel’s history and foreshadow Messiah’s eternal reign (Luke 1:32–33).

summary

1 Chronicles 17:3 captures a pivotal moment: in the stillness of night, God interrupts human planning with His infallible word, delivered through His prophet. The verse teaches that God’s timing is sovereign, His revelation authoritative, His messengers essential, and His forthcoming speech transformative.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(3) The same night.--The words indicate a dream as the method of communication (Job 4:13; 1Samuel 27:6).

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 3. - The express word of God came, however, that same night. It proved to be an overruling word. But it brought with it the point of a fresh and most welcome new departure for David. We might glean here by the way a suggestion of the beneficent operation of express revelation, superseding the thought, the method, the reason of man.
