By faith Abraham
Abraham is often referred to as the "father of faith" due to his exemplary trust in God. His faith is foundational in the narrative of the Bible, as it sets the stage for the covenant relationship between God and His people. Abraham's faith is highlighted in Genesis 15:6, where it is said that he "believed the LORD, and He credited it to him as righteousness." This faith is not merely intellectual assent but involves trust and action.

when called to go
The call of Abraham is recorded in Genesis 12:1-3, where God instructs him to leave his country, his people, and his father's household. This call signifies a divine initiative, where God chooses Abraham to be the progenitor of a great nation. The call is both a test and a promise, requiring Abraham to leave behind his past and step into an unknown future.

to a place he would later receive as his inheritance
The land promised to Abraham is Canaan, which is central to the biblical narrative. This promise is reiterated in Genesis 12:7 and further expanded in Genesis 15:18-21. The land is not just a physical territory but symbolizes God's blessing and the future establishment of Israel. The concept of inheritance is significant, as it points to the fulfillment of God's promises and the eventual establishment of His kingdom.

obeyed and went
Abraham's obedience is immediate and without hesitation, demonstrating his trust in God's promises. This obedience is a key aspect of faith, as seen in James 2:21-24, where faith is shown to be active and completed by works. Abraham's journey from Haran to Canaan is a physical manifestation of his spiritual journey of faith.

without knowing where he was going
This aspect of Abraham's journey highlights the uncertainty and risk involved in following God's call. It underscores the nature of faith as trusting in God's plan without having all the details. This mirrors the Christian walk, where believers are called to trust in God's guidance, as seen in Proverbs 3:5-6. Abraham's journey is a type of the Christian pilgrimage, where faith leads the believer into the unknown, relying on God's promises.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Abraham
Known as the father of faith, Abraham is a central figure in the Bible. His journey of faith began when God called him to leave his homeland and go to an unknown land that God promised to give him as an inheritance.

2. Ur of the Chaldeans
This is the place where Abraham originally lived before God called him. It was a prosperous city in ancient Mesopotamia.

3. Canaan
The land that God promised to Abraham and his descendants. It represents the fulfillment of God's promise and is a significant location in biblical history.

4. God's Call
The divine instruction given to Abraham to leave his country and go to a land that God would show him. This call required Abraham to trust in God's promise without knowing the details.

5. Faith
The central theme of this passage, highlighting Abraham's trust and obedience to God's call despite the uncertainty of the journey.
Teaching Points
Obedience in Uncertainty
Abraham's journey teaches us the importance of obeying God's call even when the path is unclear. Trusting in God's plan requires faith beyond our understanding.

Faith as Action
True faith is demonstrated through action. Abraham's willingness to leave his home is a powerful example of faith in motion.

God's Promises Require Patience
Abraham's account reminds us that God's promises may not be fulfilled immediately. Patience and perseverance are essential components of faith.

Trust in God's Sovereignty
Abraham's journey underscores the need to trust in God's sovereignty and His ability to guide us to our promised inheritance.

Legacy of Faith
Abraham's faith set a precedent for future generations. Our faith can also impact those around us and leave a lasting legacy.

What Does Hebrews 11:8 Mean
By faith Abraham

• Hebrews introduces Abraham with the simple phrase “By faith,” highlighting trust that rests wholly on God’s word rather than on circumstances.

• Genesis 15:6 says, “Abram believed the LORD, and it was credited to him as righteousness”. This establishes the pattern of saving faith later echoed in Romans 4:3 and Galatians 3:6.

• Faith is never blind optimism; it is confidence in the character of the One who speaks. Abraham took God’s promises as literal, historical truth and staked his future on them.

• Like Hebrews 11:6 reminds us, “without faith it is impossible to please God”. Abraham shows that pleasing God begins with believing Him.

when called to go to a place

• Genesis 12:1 records God’s clear command: “Go from your country, your people, and your father’s household to the land I will show you”.

• Acts 7:2-3 recalls how this call came when Abraham was still in Mesopotamia, surrounded by idol-worshipping culture and family ties.

• The call was personal (“Abram”), specific (“go”), and forward-looking (“I will show you”). God often reveals the first step before He unveils the whole map.

• Notice that the initiative is entirely God’s. Abraham’s role is simply to hear and respond.

he would later receive as his inheritance

• Although God immediately promised the land (Genesis 12:7), Acts 7:5 notes, “He gave him no inheritance here, not even a foot of ground”. The fulfillment would unfold over centuries, reaching its peak in Joshua’s conquest and yet pointing beyond to a future kingdom (Hebrews 11:9-10).

• This underscores two truths:

– God keeps His promises, though sometimes on a timetable far larger than one lifetime.

– Believers today also await an inheritance “that cannot perish, spoil or fade” (1 Peter 1:4).

• Abraham’s story reassures us that deferred fulfillment is not denial but divine design.

obeyed and went

• Genesis 12:4 provides the straightforward record: “So Abram departed, as the LORD had instructed him”. No bargaining, no delay.

• James 2:21-23 uses Abraham to show that genuine faith produces action. His feet moved because his heart trusted.

• Practical takeaways:

– Obedience often begins with one step, not with full comprehension.

– Faith-driven obedience may look costly—Abraham left home, heritage, and security—but God exchanges temporal loss for eternal gain.

without knowing where he was going

• The final phrase celebrates raw trust. Abraham left Ur not with a detailed itinerary but with a promise.

• 2 Corinthians 5:7 captures the lifestyle: “For we walk by faith, not by sight”.

• Proverbs 3:5-6 encourages, “Trust in the LORD with all your heart…and He will make your paths straight”.

• Living by faith means:

– We surrender the illusion of control.

– We rest in the certainty of God’s guidance even when specifics are unclear.

– We value the Guide more than the map.

summary

Hebrews 11:8 spotlights Abraham as the pattern of authentic faith: he trusted God’s word, responded to God’s call, embraced God’s promise of an inheritance, obeyed immediately, and ventured into the unknown. This verse invites us to do the same—believe God literally, act on His commands, and walk into our future confident that the One who leads us is faithful.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(8) When he was called to go out.--Our older versions are here better than the Authorised, bringing in the word "obeyed" after "called"--"obeyed to go out into," &c.

Which he should after receive.--The English rendering may seem to imply that when "called" Abraham received the promise that the land to which he would be directed should in the future be his inheritance. It is not so (Acts 7:5); for this promise is not found in Genesis 12:1-3, but was bestowed when he had obeyed (Genesis 12:7). The meaning here is, "unto a place which he was to receive."

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 8. - By faith Abraham, when he was called to go out into a place which he should after receive for an inheritance, obeyed (literally, when called, obeyed to go out, etc.); and he went out, not knowing whither he went. The reference is to the first call of Abraham (Genesis 12:1), his obedience to which is the first instance of the faith which the whole life of the father of the faithful so eminently exemplifies. The fact of the place he was to go to being so far unrevealed (intimated only as "a land that I will show thee") enhances the faith displayed, He followed the Divine voice as it were blindly, not seeing whither it was leading him, knowing only that it was right to follow it. So to those who walk by faith now the future may be unknown or dim.
