You rejected the Holy and Righteous One
This phrase highlights the rejection of Jesus by the Jewish leaders and people. The title "Holy and Righteous One" emphasizes Jesus' sinlessness and divine nature, fulfilling Old Testament prophecies such as Isaiah 53:11, which speaks of the Righteous Servant. The rejection is a fulfillment of prophecies like Psalm 118:22, where the stone the builders rejected becomes the cornerstone. This rejection is significant in the context of the Jewish expectation of a Messiah who would be holy and just, yet they failed to recognize Jesus as such.

and asked that a murderer be released to you
This refers to the choice given to the crowd by Pontius Pilate during Jesus' trial, as recorded in the Gospels (e.g., Mark 15:6-15). The crowd chose Barabbas, a known insurrectionist and murderer, over Jesus. This decision underscores the moral and spiritual blindness of the people at that time. It also serves as a stark contrast between the innocence of Jesus and the guilt of Barabbas, highlighting the substitutionary aspect of Jesus' sacrifice. The release of Barabbas can be seen as a type of the atonement, where the guilty are set free because the innocent takes their place.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Peter
One of Jesus' apostles, who is speaking to the crowd in Jerusalem. He is addressing the people with boldness and conviction, filled with the Holy Spirit.

2. The Crowd
The Jewish people in Jerusalem who were present during Jesus' trial and crucifixion. They are being confronted by Peter for their role in rejecting Jesus.

3. The Holy and Righteous One
A title for Jesus Christ, emphasizing His sinlessness and divine nature. This title underscores His unique role as the Messiah.

4. Barabbas
The murderer whom the crowd chose to be released instead of Jesus. His release highlights the grave injustice and the crowd's misguided choice.

5. Jerusalem
The city where these events took place, significant as the center of Jewish worship and the location of Jesus' crucifixion and resurrection.
Teaching Points
The Gravity of Rejection
Recognize the seriousness of rejecting Jesus, the Holy and Righteous One. This rejection is not just a historical event but a spiritual reality that continues today when people choose sin over Christ.

The Injustice of Sin
Understand the injustice of choosing sin (symbolized by Barabbas) over righteousness. This choice reflects the human tendency to prefer darkness over light.

The Call to Repentance
Peter's message is a call to repentance. Acknowledge past rejections of Christ in our lives and turn back to Him with a sincere heart.

The Role of the Holy Spirit
Peter's boldness is a result of the Holy Spirit's empowerment. Seek the Holy Spirit's guidance to stand firm in faith and witness to others.

The Fulfillment of Prophecy
Jesus' rejection was foretold by the prophets. Trust in the reliability of Scripture and God's sovereign plan, even when circumstances seem unjust.

What Does Acts 3:14 Mean
You rejected

Peter is speaking to the crowd in Jerusalem, reminding them that only weeks earlier they had turned away from their promised Messiah.

• John 1:11 says, “He came to His own, but His own did not receive Him,” underscoring the personal nature of this rejection.

• Isaiah 53:3 foretold that the Servant would be “despised and rejected,” and Peter identifies that prophecy as fulfilled in their choice.

• This rejection was not passive; it was a deliberate decision voiced in unison before Pilate (Luke 23:18).

the Holy and Righteous One

Peter highlights Jesus’ sinless character, the very reason His sacrifice can atone for sin.

• Mark 1:24 shows even demons recognizing Him: “I know who You are—the Holy One of God!”

• 2 Corinthians 5:21 affirms, “God made Him who knew no sin to be sin on our behalf,” proving Jesus’ righteousness.

• By calling Him “Holy and Righteous,” Peter sets Jesus apart from every other human, echoing Psalm 16:10 where David’s greater Son would not see decay.

and asked that a murderer be released to you

The crowd’s demand for Barabbas (Matthew 27:21) exposed how far their values had inverted.

• Isaiah 5:20 warns, “Woe to those who call evil good, and good evil,” exactly what occurred when a killer was preferred over the Author of Life.

• Barabbas’s release (John 18:40) paints a vivid picture of substitution: the guilty freed while the innocent is condemned—foreshadowing the gospel itself (Romans 5:8).

• This exchange also revealed the depth of human depravity and the extent of God’s grace, as Jesus willingly accepted the sentence meant for sinners (1 Peter 3:18).

summary

Acts 3:14 confronts us with the stark decision Israel made: rejecting their sinless Messiah and choosing a murderer instead. Peter’s words expose human rebellion, exalt Christ’s absolute holiness, and spotlight the shocking exchange at Calvary that still offers redemption today.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(14) Ye denied the Holy One and the Just.--The language, though startlingly new to the hearers, had been partially anticipated. It had been used of the Christ by the demoniacs (Mark 1:24). The best MSS. give St. Peter's confession in John 6:69 in the form, "Thou art the Holy One of God." Pilate's wife, and Pilate himself, had borne their witness to Jesus as emphatically "Just" (Matthew 27:19; Matthew 27:24). It is interesting to note the recurrence of the word as applied to Christ in the writings of each of the Apostles who were now proclaiming it (1Peter 3:18; 1John 2:1), yet more so to think of this as the result of their three years' converse with their Master. To them He was emphatically, above all the sons of men that they had known, the Holy and the Righteous One. . . .
