There is a generation—
This phrase introduces a specific group of people characterized by certain behaviors or attitudes. In biblical context, "generation" often refers to a group of people living at the same time, but it can also imply a type of people with shared characteristics. This echoes the recurring theme in Proverbs of contrasting the wise and the foolish. The term "generation" can also be seen in other scriptures, such as in Matthew 12:39, where Jesus refers to a "wicked and adulterous generation," highlighting a pattern of behavior that is contrary to God's ways.

how haughty are their eyes
Haughtiness, or pride, is a significant theme throughout the Bible, often associated with a rejection of God and His commandments. The eyes are frequently used metaphorically to represent one's perspective or attitude. In Proverbs 6:16-17, haughty eyes are listed among the things the Lord detests. This phrase suggests an arrogance that elevates oneself above others, contrary to the humility that is a hallmark of godly wisdom. The cultural context of the ancient Near East often viewed pride as a precursor to downfall, as seen in the story of King Nebuchadnezzar in Daniel 4.

and pretentious are their glances—
Pretentiousness implies a false display of importance or worth. This behavior is condemned in scripture as it often leads to deceit and hypocrisy. The glances, like the eyes, symbolize one's outlook and interactions with others. In the New Testament, Jesus warns against such hypocrisy in Matthew 23:5-7, where the Pharisees are criticized for their outward displays of piety that mask inner corruption. This phrase serves as a warning against superficiality and the dangers of valuing appearance over substance, a theme that resonates throughout biblical teachings on integrity and authenticity.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Agur
The author of Proverbs 30, Agur son of Jakeh, is a figure about whom little is known. His words are considered wise sayings and are included in the Book of Proverbs.

2. The Generation
This refers to a group of people characterized by pride and arrogance. It is not limited to a specific time or place but is a recurring theme throughout human history.

3. The Book of Proverbs
A collection of wise sayings and teachings attributed primarily to Solomon, but also includes contributions from other wise men like Agur.
Teaching Points
The Danger of Pride
Pride is a sin that leads to downfall. It blinds individuals to their faults and distances them from God.

Recognizing Arrogance in Ourselves
Self-examination is crucial. We must be vigilant in identifying and confessing prideful attitudes in our own lives.

Cultivating Humility
Humility is the antidote to pride. We should strive to develop a humble heart, recognizing our dependence on God.

The Impact of Our Attitude on Others
Our demeanor affects those around us. A humble and gracious attitude can be a powerful witness to others.

The Generational Cycle of Pride
Each generation faces the temptation of pride. It is important to teach and model humility to the next generation.

What Does Proverbs 30:13 Mean
There is a generation

• Solomon’s collected wisdom (Proverbs 30:11-14) paints four snapshots of a rebellious people, and verse 13 zooms in on pride.

• “Generation” here points to a recurring type of people, not necessarily an age group—those who persist in arrogance throughout history (cf. Psalm 12:8; Matthew 24:34).

• Scripture warns that every era faces the temptation to elevate self above God (Judges 2:10-12; Romans 1:21-22).

how haughty are their eyes

• “Haughty” signals overt pride that shows up in the way we look at others—eyes lifted in self-importance (Proverbs 6:16-17 lists “haughty eyes” first among the seven things the Lord hates).

• This inner attitude of superiority:

– Rejects humility commanded in Micah 6:8.

– Mirrors the sin of Satan, who aspired to a throne “above the stars of God” (Isaiah 14:13).

– Provokes God’s opposition, for “God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble” (James 4:6).

pretentious are their glances

• Pride spreads from eyes to “glances”—the daily, subtle interactions that dismiss others and magnify self (Proverbs 21:4).

• Such pretension breeds:

– Contempt for authority (Proverbs 30:11).

– Injustice toward the weak (Proverbs 30:14; Isaiah 3:15).

– Reliance on appearance instead of character (1 Samuel 16:7).

• Jesus confronts this spirit in the Pharisees who “loved the place of honor” (Luke 11:43) and reminds us that “whoever exalts himself will be humbled” (Matthew 23:12).

summary

Proverbs 30:13 exposes a pattern of proud hearts revealed in lifted eyes and dismissive looks. Each generation faces this sin, but God calls His people to the opposite posture—humble dependence on Him and gracious regard for others. When we choose humility, we invite God’s favor and reflect the character of Christ, “gentle and humble in heart” (Matthew 11:29).

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 13. - A generation, oh, how lofty are their eyes! The third sin is pride and arrogance (see on Proverbs 6:17; 21:4). "Lord," said the psalmist, "my heart is not haughty, nor mine eyes lofty" (Psalm 131:1). The prophet rebukes "the stout heart of the King of Assyria and the glory of his high looks" (Isaiah 10:12). Their eyelids are lifted up; in supercilious disdain. "Inde Proverbio dicimus," says Erasmus ('Adag.'), "attolli supercilium, fastidium indicantes" (s.v. "Arrogantia").
