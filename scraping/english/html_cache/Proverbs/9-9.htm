Instruct a wise man, and he will be wiser still;
This phrase emphasizes the value of wisdom and the openness of a wise person to further instruction. In biblical context, wisdom is highly esteemed, as seen throughout the Book of Proverbs. The wise man is not complacent but seeks continual growth, reflecting the biblical principle that wisdom is a lifelong pursuit (Proverbs 4:7). This aligns with the cultural context of ancient Israel, where wisdom was often associated with the fear of the Lord (Proverbs 1:7). The wise man's willingness to learn more can be seen as a type of Christ, who, though perfect, demonstrated humility and growth in wisdom (Luke 2:52). This phrase also connects with James 1:5, which encourages believers to ask God for wisdom, promising that it will be given generously.

teach a righteous man, and he will increase his learning.
The righteous man, like the wise man, is characterized by a desire for growth and understanding. Righteousness in the Old Testament is often linked to living in accordance with God's laws and commands. The cultural and historical context of ancient Israel placed a high value on righteousness as a reflection of one's relationship with God. This phrase suggests that the righteous are teachable and eager to expand their knowledge, which is a mark of true righteousness. This is echoed in Psalm 119:97-104, where the psalmist expresses love for God's law and a desire to meditate on it continually. The righteous man's increase in learning can also be seen as a reflection of the transformative power of God's Word, as described in 2 Timothy 3:16-17, which equips believers for every good work.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Wise Man
Represents individuals who possess wisdom and are open to further instruction. In the Hebrew context, "wise" (chakam) implies skillful living and moral insight.

2. Righteous Man
Refers to those who live in accordance with God's standards. The Hebrew word for "righteous" (tsaddiq) denotes someone who is just and ethical.

3. Solomon
Traditionally considered the author of Proverbs, Solomon was known for his wisdom and his role as the king of Israel.

4. Proverbs
A book in the Old Testament consisting of sayings and teachings that provide practical guidance for living a life pleasing to God.

5. Ancient Israel
The cultural and historical context in which these proverbs were written, emphasizing the importance of wisdom and righteousness in daily life.
Teaching Points
The Value of Continuous Learning
Wisdom is not a static trait but a dynamic process. Even the wise can grow wiser, and the righteous can deepen their understanding.

Openness to Instruction
A hallmark of wisdom is the willingness to receive instruction. This humility leads to growth and maturity in faith.

Righteousness and Learning
Righteousness involves a commitment to learning and applying God's truth. It is an active pursuit of aligning one's life with God's will.

The Role of Community
Instruction often comes through community—teachers, mentors, and fellow believers. Engaging with others in the faith community can enhance our wisdom and righteousness.

God as the Source of Wisdom
Ultimately, wisdom and righteousness come from God. Seeking His guidance through prayer and Scripture is essential for growth.

What Does Proverbs 9:9 Mean
Instruct

• The verse opens with an action word: “Instruct.” God presents teaching as an intentional act.

• Scripture repeatedly commends those who give clear, faithful instruction (Proverbs 4:1–2; 2 Timothy 2:2).

• Because the command comes from God’s wisdom literature, it carries the expectation that instruction aligns with divine truth, not human speculation (Proverbs 30:5).

A wise man

• The target audience is already described as “wise,” showing that wisdom is not static.

• Wisdom in Proverbs is the practical outworking of fearing the Lord (Proverbs 1:7).

• Cross reference: Solomon’s own request for wisdom in 1 Kings 3:9–12 demonstrates that even leaders need continual instruction.

And he will be wiser still

• God promises multiplication, not mere addition, of wisdom when the teachable receive truth (Proverbs 1:5; Matthew 13:12).

• This mirrors Jesus’ principle that faithful stewardship brings greater understanding (Luke 8:18).

• The phrase stresses growth: current wisdom becomes deeper, broader, and more fruitful.

Teach

• A second action word reinforces the first, showing that instruction includes explanation, demonstration, and life-on-life mentoring (Deuteronomy 6:6–9).

• Teaching is part of the Great Commission pattern—“teaching them to observe” (Matthew 28:20).

A righteous man

• “Righteous” highlights character: those justified before God and walking in integrity (Psalm 1:1–3).

• Righteousness and wisdom are linked; right living flows from right understanding (James 3:13).

• This underscores that moral alignment with God positions a person to grasp deeper truth.

And he will increase his learning

• Learning is portrayed as limitless; growth continues throughout life (Philippians 1:9–10).

• The righteous man’s appetite for truth leads to tangible gains—greater discernment, clearer judgment, and more skillful living (Hebrews 5:14).

• Cross reference: the Bereans “received the word with great eagerness” and “examined the Scriptures daily,” thereby growing in understanding (Acts 17:11).

summary

Proverbs 9:9 assures that godly instruction produces exponential returns in those already walking wisely and righteously. When teachable hearts receive truth, God deepens their insight and enlarges their capacity to live out His purposes. The verse invites believers to both give and receive instruction eagerly, knowing that every faithful exchange of truth advances God’s wisdom in His people.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 9. - Give instruction to a wise man, and he will be yet wiser. The Hebrew is merely "give to the wise," with no object mentioned; but the context suggests "instruction," even though, as in ver. 8, it takes the form of rebuke. Vulgate and Septuagint, "Give an opportunity to a wise man, and he will be wiser" (comp. Matthew 13:12; Matthew 25:29). To make the best use of all occasions of learning duty, whether they present themselves in a winning or a forbidden shape, is the part of one who is wise unto salvation (see Proverbs 1:5, and note there). Teach a just man, and he will increase in learning. Wisdom being a moral and not merely an intellectual, quality. there is a natural interchange of "wise" and "just," referring to the same individual, in the two clauses. Vulgate, festinabit accipere; Septuagint, "Instruct a wise man, and he shall have more given him." The wise are thus rewarded with larger measures of wisdom, because they are simple, humble, and willing to learn, having that childlike spirit which Christ commends (Matthew 18:3).
