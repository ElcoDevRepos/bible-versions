Listen to your father who gave you life
This phrase emphasizes the importance of heeding parental guidance, particularly from the father, who is traditionally seen as the head of the family in biblical times. The father’s role in providing life is both biological and spiritual, as he is responsible for teaching and guiding his children in the ways of the Lord (Deuteronomy 6:6-7). The call to "listen" suggests an active engagement and respect for the wisdom and experience that the father imparts. This aligns with the broader biblical theme of honoring one's parents, as seen in the Ten Commandments (Exodus 20:12). The patriarchal society of ancient Israel placed significant emphasis on the father's authority, reflecting the cultural context in which this proverb was written.

and do not despise your mother when she is old
This part of the verse highlights the enduring respect and care that should be afforded to one's mother, especially as she ages. In ancient Near Eastern cultures, the family unit was central, and caring for elderly parents was a fundamental duty. The term "despise" indicates a warning against neglect or contempt, which can arise as parents become more dependent. The Bible consistently upholds the value of honoring one's mother, as seen in passages like Leviticus 19:3 and Ephesians 6:2. The reference to the mother "when she is old" underscores the lifelong nature of this respect, reflecting the biblical principle of caring for the vulnerable and maintaining family integrity. This also prefigures the care Jesus showed for His own mother, Mary, ensuring her well-being even as He was on the cross (John 19:26-27).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Father
Represents the paternal figure who provides guidance, wisdom, and life. In the context of ancient Israel, the father was often seen as the head of the household and a primary source of instruction.

2. Mother
Symbolizes the maternal figure who nurtures and cares for her children. The verse emphasizes the importance of respecting and valuing her, especially in her old age.

3. Solomon
Traditionally attributed as the author of Proverbs, Solomon was known for his wisdom and his role as a king of Israel. His writings often focus on practical wisdom and moral teachings.

4. Ancient Israel
The cultural and historical context in which the Proverbs were written, where family structures and respect for parents were foundational societal values.

5. Wisdom Literature
Proverbs is part of the wisdom literature in the Bible, which includes teachings on how to live a life pleasing to God through practical and moral instruction.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Listening
Listening to parents is a form of honoring them and acknowledging their role in our lives. It reflects humility and a willingness to learn from their experiences.

Respect for the Elderly
As parents age, they deserve respect and care. This respect is a reflection of our gratitude for their sacrifices and contributions to our lives.

The Role of Parents in Godly Wisdom
Parents are often the first teachers of God's wisdom. Valuing their guidance aligns with seeking God's wisdom in our lives.

Intergenerational Relationships
Healthy relationships between generations are vital for the transmission of values and faith. This verse encourages fostering such relationships.

Practical Obedience to God's Commands
Obeying the command to honor parents is a practical expression of our faith and obedience to God.

What Does Proverbs 23:22 Mean
Listen to your father who gave you life

“Listen to your father who gave you life…”

• Scripture plainly calls us to an attitude of attentive obedience. “Listen” is more than passively hearing; it is the same heart posture urged in Proverbs 1:8–9 and Proverbs 4:1–2—an eager, teachable spirit that receives wisdom.

• God places fathers in the home as a primary source of guidance (Ephesians 6:4). When the verse reminds us that a father “gave you life,” it underlines the debt of gratitude owed to the one God used to bring us into the world (cf. Deuteronomy 32:6).

• Honoring a father is not optional or cultural; it is the fifth commandment’s timeless requirement (Deuteronomy 5:16; Ephesians 6:1–3).

• Active listening bears fruit:

– Protection from destructive paths (Proverbs 6:20–23).

– Growth in wisdom that benefits every generation (Proverbs 13:1).

– A life that pleases the Lord, securing His promise of blessing (Colossians 3:20).

and do not despise your mother when she is old

“…and do not despise your mother when she is old.”

• “Do not despise” pushes beyond mere external respect. It warns against any attitude—verbal, emotional, or practical—that belittles or neglects a mother. Proverbs 20:20 shows the seriousness: contempt invites darkness over one’s life.

• As mothers age, their physical strength may wane, but their value does not. Leviticus 19:32 commands us to “rise in the presence of the aged,” while 1 Timothy 5:3–4 instructs children to repay their parents with tangible care.

• The verse treats a mother’s senior years as a sacred season, rich with hard-earned wisdom (Proverbs 31:26). The faithful child gives honor, support, and affection—mirroring Ruth’s devotion to Naomi (Ruth 1:16–17).

• Practical ways not to despise:

– Speak graciously and listen patiently (James 1:19).

– Provide for her needs—financial, medical, emotional (Mark 7:10–12).

– Defend her dignity publicly and privately (Proverbs 31:28–29).

– Seek her counsel; age deepens discernment (Job 12:12).

summary

Proverbs 23:22 ties the lifelong duty of honoring parents to the fabric of God’s unchanging wisdom. Listening to a father and refusing to belittle an aging mother flow from the same heart attitude: grateful submission to the Lord who designed family order. Those who embrace this command receive protection, wisdom, and blessing, while they reflect the very character of God, who faithfully cares for His children.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 22-25. - An octastich, containing an earnest exhortation to the disciple. Verse 22. - That begat thee. This is a claim on the attention and obedience of the son. When she is old. When old age with its consequent infirmities comes upon thy mother, despise her not, but rather thank God for giving her long life, and profit by her love and long experience (comp. Ecclus. 3:1, etc., where the exhortation to honour parents is very full and touching).
