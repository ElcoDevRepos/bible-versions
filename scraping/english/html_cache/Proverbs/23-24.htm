The father of a righteous man will greatly rejoice
This phrase emphasizes the joy and fulfillment a parent experiences when their child lives a life of righteousness. In biblical context, righteousness is often associated with living in accordance with God's laws and commands. The joy of the father can be seen as a reflection of God's own joy when His children walk in His ways. This aligns with the cultural importance of family honor and legacy in ancient Israel, where a child's behavior could significantly impact the family's reputation. The concept of a righteous man is echoed in other scriptures, such as Psalm 112:1-2, which speaks of the blessings upon the righteous and their descendants. The rejoicing of the father can also be seen as a type of the heavenly Father's joy over His children who follow Christ, the ultimate Righteous One.

and he who fathers a wise son will delight in him
Wisdom in the biblical sense is more than just knowledge; it is the application of understanding and discernment in living a life that pleases God. The delight of the father in a wise son reflects the value placed on wisdom throughout the book of Proverbs. Wisdom is personified and highly esteemed, as seen in Proverbs 3:13-18, where it is described as more precious than rubies. The cultural context of ancient Israel placed a high value on wisdom as a means to navigate life's challenges and maintain a harmonious community. This delight can also be seen as a parallel to God's delight in His Son, Jesus Christ, who is described as the embodiment of divine wisdom (1 Corinthians 1:24). The father's delight in a wise son mirrors the joy of God when His children seek and live by His wisdom.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Father
Represents a parental figure who has invested in the upbringing and moral development of their child. In the context of ancient Israel, the father was often seen as the head of the household and responsible for the spiritual and moral guidance of the family.

2. Righteous Man
Refers to an individual who lives in accordance with God's laws and principles. In Hebrew, the word for "righteous" is (tzaddik), which implies someone who is just, ethical, and in right standing with God.

3. Wise Son
A child who embodies wisdom, which in the Hebrew context ( - chakam) means not only having knowledge but also applying it in a way that honors God and benefits others.
Teaching Points
The Joy of Righteousness
A righteous life not only honors God but also brings joy to those who have invested in our spiritual growth. Parents find deep satisfaction in seeing their children walk in truth and righteousness.

Wisdom as a Source of Delight
Wisdom is more than knowledge; it is the application of God's truth in daily life. A wise child is a source of delight because they reflect the values and teachings imparted by their parents.

Parental Responsibility
Parents are called to nurture and guide their children in the ways of the Lord. The joy described in this verse is a result of diligent parenting and the grace of God working in a child's life.

Legacy of Faith
The verse encourages us to consider the legacy we leave behind. A life lived in righteousness and wisdom impacts not only our immediate family but also future generations.

Encouragement for Parents
This verse serves as encouragement for parents to continue investing in their children's spiritual growth, trusting that their efforts will bear fruit in due time.

What Does Proverbs 23:24 Mean
The father of a righteous man

Proverbs 23:24 opens by spotlighting the parent, not the child: “The father of a righteous man….” Scripture treats righteousness as a life ordered by God’s standards (see Proverbs 12:28; 1 John 3:7). When a son stands upright before the Lord:

• He validates his father’s instruction, echoing Proverbs 4:1–4 where a godly dad passes on wisdom.

• He offers living proof that “children are a heritage from the LORD” (Psalm 127:3–4), meant to be released as arrows aimed at God’s purposes.

• He reminds parents that their labor “in the Lord is not in vain” (1 Corinthians 15:58), even if the process was long and imperfect.

Will greatly rejoice

The verse continues, “…will greatly rejoice.” The Hebrew construction doubles the joy idea, stressing overflowing gladness. This isn’t a polite smile—it’s full-hearted celebration like the father of the prodigal when repentance came (Luke 15:22–24). Comparable promises:

• Proverbs 10:1, “A wise son makes a glad father.”

• 3 John 4, “I have no greater joy than to hear that my children are walking in the truth.”

Such rejoicing is grounded in the conviction that eternal priorities have taken root. Earthly achievements delight for a moment; spiritual fruit thrills for a lifetime.

He who fathers a wise son

The second line parallels the first: “and he who fathers a wise son….” Wisdom in Proverbs is practical godliness—skill for living under God’s rule (Proverbs 1:7; James 3:17). To “father” here implies both biological parenthood and ongoing shaping (Deuteronomy 6:6–7). Key observations:

• Wisdom is pursued, not presumed; the father models and mentors (Proverbs 20:7).

• A son’s wisdom reflects the fear of the Lord, aligning head knowledge with heart obedience (Proverbs 9:10).

• The relationship becomes a partnership in kingdom purposes, echoing Paul and Timothy (Philippians 2:22).

Will delight in him

“…will delight in him.” Delight goes deeper than momentary happiness; it is sustained pleasure and satisfaction. Similar language appears in Proverbs 29:3, “A man who loves wisdom brings joy to his father.” When parents delight, they:

• Experience peace of mind, knowing their child walks securely (Proverbs 3:21–26).

• Enjoy fellowship with their child around shared devotion to Christ (Malachi 4:6; Acts 16:31–34).

• Anticipate generational blessing, for wisdom “builds her house” (Proverbs 24:3–4) and influences grandchildren (2 Timothy 1:5).

summary

Proverbs 23:24 teaches that righteous, wise children are a God-given source of deep parental joy. The verse calls children to pursue godliness, parents to nurture it, and families to celebrate when it appears. Earthly success may impress, but spiritual integrity brings lasting rejoicing and delight, echoing the Father’s own pleasure when His children “walk in the truth.”

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 24. - The father of the righteous shall greatly rejoice. The father of a righteous son who has won truth and profited by the possession has good cause to be glad (Proverbs 10:1). Septuagint erroneously, "A righteous father brings up children well." The second clause repeats the first in different words, with the further idea that the wise son affords his father practical proof of the excellence of his moral training. The contrast is seen in Proverbs 17:21.
