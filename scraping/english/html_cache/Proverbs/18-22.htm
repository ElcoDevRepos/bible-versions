Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
He who finds a wife
In the biblical context, marriage is highly esteemed and considered a divine institution established by God in Genesis 2:24. The act of finding a wife implies a pursuit and a choice, reflecting the cultural practices of arranged marriages in ancient Israel, where families often played a significant role in the selection process. The phrase suggests intentionality and discernment in seeking a life partner, aligning with the wisdom literature's emphasis on making prudent choices.

finds a good thing
The term "good thing" underscores the value and blessing of a marital relationship. In Hebrew, "good" (טוֹב, tov) often denotes something beneficial, pleasing, or morally excellent. This aligns with the creation narrative where God declares His creation "good" (Genesis 1). The goodness of a wife is echoed in Proverbs 31, which describes the virtuous woman whose worth is far above rubies. The phrase suggests that a wife contributes positively to a man's life, bringing companionship, support, and partnership.

and obtains favor from the LORD
The concept of obtaining favor from the LORD indicates divine approval and blessing. In the Old Testament, favor (חֵן, chen) is often associated with God's grace and kindness, as seen in the stories of Noah (Genesis 6:8) and Joseph (Genesis 39:21). This phrase implies that marriage is not only a social contract but also a spiritual blessing, reflecting God's design and purpose. It suggests that a harmonious marriage aligns with God's will, bringing His favor upon the couple. This favor can be seen as a type of Christ's relationship with the Church, where the union is marked by love, sacrifice, and divine blessing (Ephesians 5:25-32).

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Seeker
The verse refers to a man who actively seeks and finds a wife. This implies intentionality and discernment in the pursuit of a life partner.

2. The Wife
The wife is described as a "good thing," indicating her value and the positive impact she brings to the marriage and the man's life.

3. The LORD
The verse highlights the divine aspect of marriage, suggesting that finding a wife is not only a personal achievement but also a blessing and favor from God.
Teaching Points
The Value of a Godly Marriage
Marriage is portrayed as a divine blessing. It is important to recognize the value and sanctity of this union as ordained by God.

Intentionality in Relationships
The verse encourages intentionality in seeking a spouse. This involves prayer, discernment, and aligning one's desires with God's will.

The Role of Favor in Marriage
Finding a spouse is not merely a personal success but a manifestation of God's favor. This perspective encourages gratitude and reliance on God in marital relationships.

The Importance of Character
The description of a wife as a "good thing" underscores the importance of character and virtue in a spouse. This calls for individuals to cultivate godly qualities in themselves and seek them in others.

What Does Proverbs 18:22 Mean
He who finds

• The verse begins with personal responsibility—“He who finds.” It assumes active pursuit, not passive waiting.

• Scripture often highlights the wisdom of seeking God-honoring relationships (Proverbs 31:10; Proverbs 19:8).

• Finding here implies discernment, patience, and prayerful dependence, echoing Genesis 24 where Abraham’s servant prays before seeking a wife for Isaac.

a wife

• Marriage is God’s design from Eden: “It is not good for the man to be alone” (Genesis 2:18).

• A wife is not merely a social partner but a covenant companion (Malachi 2:14).

• The focus is specifically on a wife, underscoring God’s intended order and blessing in the marital bond (Ephesians 5:31-33).

finds a good thing

• God calls His own creation “very good” (Genesis 1:31), and marriage fits within that verdict.

• “A prudent wife is from the LORD” (Proverbs 19:14), reinforcing that a godly spouse is a tangible blessing.

• Good in Scripture always lines up with God’s character and purposes; compare James 1:17: “Every good and perfect gift is from above.”

and obtains favor

• Favor speaks of God’s gracious approval and help. Proverbs 8:35 uses the same phrase: “finds life and obtains favor from the LORD.”

• Marriage lived under God’s principles invites His ongoing kindness—seen in Psalm 5:12, where the righteous are “surrounded with favor like a shield.”

• This favor includes wisdom, provision, protection, and spiritual growth for both husband and wife (1 Peter 3:7).

from the LORD

• The verse anchors every blessing back to its source—God Himself.

• “Unless the LORD builds the house, its builders labor in vain” (Psalm 127:1). A thriving marriage rests on God’s involvement.

• Finding a wife is not luck; it is providence. This truth fosters gratitude and humility, urging couples to keep Christ central (Colossians 3:17).

summary

Proverbs 18:22 assures that when a man actively, prayerfully, and wisely seeks a wife within God’s design, he discovers a true good—a covenant partner—and steps into a sphere of divine favor. Marriage is God’s idea, God’s gift, and God’s channel for blessing, calling husband and wife to honor Him together.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 22. - Whoso findeth a wife findeth a good thing. A good wife is meant (as the Septuagint has it, γυναῖκα ἀγαθήν; mulierem bonam, Vulgate), a virtuous, prudent helpmate, as in Proverbs 12:4; Proverbs 19:14; and 31. The epithet is omitted, because the moralist is thinking of the ideal wife, the one whoso union is blessed, who alone deserves the holy name of wife. Thus in ver. 4 we had the ideal man spoken cf. Septuagint, εϋρε χάριτας," findeth graces," viz. peace, union, plenty, ruder (see a different view, Ecclesiastes 7:26-28). And obtaineth favour of the Lord (Proverbs 8:35; Proverbs 12:2); or, hath obtained (Proverbs 3:13), as shown by the consort whom God has given him. Ratson, "good will," "favour," is rendered by the Septuagint ἱλαρότητα, and by the Vulgate, jucunditatem, "cheerfulness," "joyousness" (see on Proverbs 19:12). Ecclus. 26:1, etc., "Blessed is the man that hath a good wife, for the number of his days shall be double. A virtuous (ἀνδρεία) woman rejoiceth her husband, and he shall fulfil the years of his life in peace. A good wife is a good portion which shall be given in the portion of them that fear the Lord." "A good wife," says the Talmud. "is a good gift; she shall be given to a man that feareth God." And again, "God did not make woman from man's head, that she should not rule over him; nor from his feet, that she should not be his slave; but from his side, that she should be near his heart" (Dukes, p. 69). Γυνή δικαζα τοῦ βίου σωτηρία The Septuagint and Vulgate here introduce a paragraph which is not in the Hebrew, and only partly in the Syriac. It seems to be a further explanation of the statement in the text, founded on the practice prevalent at the time when the Septuagint Version was composed, which appears to have made divorce a recognized necessity in the case of adultery: "He who casteth away a good a wife casteth away good things; but he who retaineth an adulteress is a fool and impious." The advice of Siracides concerning a wicked wife is austere: "If she go not as thou wouldest have her, cut her off from thy flesh" (Ecclus. 25:26). Nothing is here said about the marriage of divorced persons; but the absolute indissolubility of the marriage bond was never held among the Jews, a certain laxity being allowed because of the hardness of their heart (Matthew 5:32; Matthew 19:8, etc.). The original intently of the marriage contract was re-established by Christ.
