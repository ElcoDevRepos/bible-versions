Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Go up to Hilkiah the high priest
Hilkiah was the high priest during the reign of King Josiah, a time of significant religious reform in Judah. The high priest held a crucial role in maintaining the spiritual integrity of the nation, serving as a mediator between God and the people. This command to "go up" indicates the importance of the task and the respect for the high priest's authority. Hilkiah's role in discovering the Book of the Law later in this chapter highlights his significance in the religious revival under Josiah.

and have him count the money
The counting of money suggests a need for accountability and transparency in the handling of temple funds. This practice was essential to ensure that the resources were used appropriately for the maintenance and repair of the temple. It reflects a broader biblical principle of stewardship and integrity in financial matters, as seen in other scriptures like 2 Corinthians 8:20-21, which emphasizes honorable conduct in financial dealings.

that has been brought into the house of the LORD
The "house of the LORD" refers to Solomon's Temple in Jerusalem, the central place of worship for the Israelites. At this time, the temple had fallen into disrepair due to neglect and idolatrous practices by previous kings. The collection of money for its restoration signifies a return to proper worship and reverence for God. The temple's significance as a dwelling place for God's presence is echoed in passages like 1 Kings 8:10-11, where the glory of the LORD fills the temple.

which the doorkeepers have collected from the people
Doorkeepers, or gatekeepers, were responsible for maintaining order and security at the temple entrances. Their role in collecting money indicates a structured system for receiving offerings from the people. This system ensured that contributions were gathered systematically and fairly. The involvement of the people in contributing to the temple's upkeep reflects a communal responsibility for worship and the maintenance of sacred spaces, similar to the collective efforts seen in the construction of the tabernacle in Exodus 35:20-29.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Hilkiah the High Priest
Hilkiah was the high priest during the reign of King Josiah. He played a crucial role in the religious reforms of the time, including the discovery of the Book of the Law in the temple.

2. The House of the LORD
This refers to the temple in Jerusalem, which was the central place of worship for the Israelites. It was a place where offerings were brought and where the presence of God was believed to dwell.

3. The Doorkeepers
These were the temple guards responsible for maintaining order and security at the temple. They also collected offerings from the people, which were used for temple maintenance and other religious activities.

4. King Josiah
Although not directly mentioned in this verse, King Josiah was the ruler of Judah at the time. He was known for his commitment to restoring the worship of Yahweh and purging idolatry from the land.

5. The Money Collected
This refers to the offerings and contributions made by the people of Israel for the upkeep of the temple. It was a common practice for the community to support the temple financially.
Teaching Points
Stewardship and Accountability
The collection and management of funds for the temple highlight the importance of stewardship and accountability in handling resources dedicated to God's work.

The Role of Leadership in Spiritual Renewal
Hilkiah's involvement underscores the critical role of spiritual leaders in guiding and implementing religious reforms and ensuring the proper use of resources.

Community Participation in Worship
The contributions from the people reflect the communal responsibility in supporting the worship and maintenance of God's house, encouraging believers today to actively participate in their faith communities.

Revival and Rediscovery of God's Word
The context of this verse, leading to the discovery of the Book of the Law, illustrates how God can use practical actions, like temple repairs, to bring about spiritual revival and a renewed focus on His Word.

What Does 2 Kings 22:4 Mean
Go up

“Go up” signals decisive, obedient action. Josiah, eight‐years into his reform (2 Kings 22:3), refuses to let complacency settle in.

• 2 Chronicles 34:8 shows the parallel command “to repair the temple of the LORD his God,” underscoring urgency.

• Deuteronomy 12:5 reminds Israel to “seek the place the LORD your God will choose,” tying Josiah’s upward movement to the covenant pattern of meeting God on His terms.

Application: When Scripture directs, delay is disobedience; immediate steps toward God’s purposes honor Him.

to Hilkiah the high priest

The king engages the spiritual leader, not bypassing God’s ordained order.

• Leviticus 21:10 designates the high priest as the one “chief among his brothers,” illustrating why Josiah addresses Hilkiah first.

• Hebrews 5:1 affirms that “every high priest is appointed to represent men in matters relating to God,” highlighting Hilkiah’s mediating role.

Takeaway: God’s work flourishes when civil and spiritual authority cooperate under His Word.

and have him count the money

Accountability matters in God’s house.

• 2 Kings 12:4-15 records Joash ordering a similar audit, proving that stewardship is not new.

• 2 Corinthians 8:20-21 shows Paul arranging for brothers to carry the offering “so that no one can criticize us,” echoing the same transparency.

Key points:

– Counting prevents misuse.

– Proper records dignify the gifts of God’s people.

that has been brought into the house of the LORD

The funds originated as acts of worship, not taxes.

• Exodus 35:5, 21 describes freewill offerings “for the LORD,” linking giving to love for God.

• Malachi 3:10 promises blessing when the tithe is brought “into the storehouse,” reinforcing the temple as the right repository.

Lesson: Resources dedicated to God belong in His service, not personal projects.

which the doorkeepers have collected

Gatekeepers served as both guards and treasurers (1 Chronicles 9:17-27).

• 2 Chronicles 23:19 places them at the gates “so that no one who was in any way unclean could enter,” reflecting integrity.

Points to notice:

– Collection occurred at the point of entry, ensuring every gift was acknowledged.

– Shared responsibility reduced temptation and error.

from the people

Revival always involves the whole congregation.

• Exodus 35:21 says “everyone whose heart stirred him” contributed; the same spirit fuels Josiah’s day.

• Acts 4:32-35 pictures New Testament believers laying proceeds at the apostles’ feet, confirming that God’s work advances through willing hearts.

Implication: Every believer, not just leaders, invests in restoring true worship.

summary

2 Kings 22:4 showcases swift obedience, proper spiritual hierarchy, transparent stewardship, sacred purpose, trustworthy administration, and wholehearted participation. Together these elements illustrate how God’s people, when aligned with His Word, resource and revive His house for His glory.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 4. - Go up to Hilkiah the high priest. Hilkiah is mentioned again in the genealogy of Ezra (Ezra 7:1). He is there called "the son of Shallum." That he may sum the silver which is brought into the house of the Lord. A collection must have been progressing for some time. As in the reign of Joash, after the impieties and idolatry of Athaliah, it was found necessary to collect money for the repair of the temple (2 Kings 12:4-14), so now, after the wicked doings of Manasseh and Amen, a renovation of the sacred building was required, and the money needed was being raised by a collection. Great care was taken in all such cases that an exact account should be kept and rendered. Which the keepers of the door - literally, of the threshold - have gathered of the people. The money had, apparently, been allowed to accumulate in a box or boxes (see 2 Kings 12:9), from the time when the collection was first authorized, probably six years previously. The high priest was now required to count it, to take the sum of it, and undertake the distribution.
