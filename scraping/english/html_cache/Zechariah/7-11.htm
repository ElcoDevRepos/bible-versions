Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
But they refused to pay attention
This phrase highlights the deliberate disobedience of the people. In the context of Zechariah, the Israelites had returned from exile and were being called to genuine repentance and obedience. Historically, this refusal to heed God's word was a recurring issue for Israel, as seen in passages like Jeremiah 7:24, where the people did not listen or incline their ear. This refusal is a heart issue, indicating a willful rejection of God's commands and a preference for their own ways.

and turned a stubborn shoulder;
The imagery of a "stubborn shoulder" suggests resistance and rebellion. In ancient Near Eastern culture, turning the shoulder was a metaphor for rejecting authority or instruction. This phrase can be connected to Deuteronomy 9:6, where Israel is described as a "stiff-necked people," indicating their persistent rebellion against God. The stubbornness here is not just physical but spiritual, showing a hardened heart against divine guidance.

they stopped up their ears from hearing.
This phrase indicates a conscious decision to ignore God's message. The act of stopping up ears is symbolic of a refusal to listen, akin to the behavior described in Isaiah 6:10, where the people's hearts are calloused, and their ears are dull. This deliberate deafness to God's word is a serious spiritual condition, as faith comes from hearing the message of Christ (Romans 10:17). The refusal to hear is a rejection of the opportunity for repentance and restoration, which God continually offers.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Zechariah
A prophet who ministered to the people of Judah after their return from Babylonian exile. His messages often called for repentance and spiritual renewal.

2. Judah
The southern kingdom of Israel, which had been exiled to Babylon but was now returning to rebuild Jerusalem and the temple.

3. The People of Judah
The audience of Zechariah's prophecy, who were being admonished for their disobedience and hard-heartedness.

4. The Exile
The period when the people of Judah were taken captive to Babylon, a significant event that shaped their identity and relationship with God.

5. The Return
The period when the exiles returned to Judah, a time of rebuilding and spiritual reflection.
Teaching Points
The Danger of Spiritual Deafness
Ignoring God's voice leads to spiritual stagnation and separation from His will. We must cultivate a heart that is open and responsive to His guidance.

The Consequences of Stubbornness
A stubborn heart can lead to missed opportunities for growth and blessing. We should strive to be pliable and teachable in our walk with God.

The Importance of Active Listening
True listening involves more than hearing words; it requires understanding and acting upon God's instructions. We should practice active listening in our spiritual lives.

Repentance and Renewal
Just as Zechariah called the people to repentance, we are called to examine our hearts and turn back to God when we stray.

Community Accountability
The collective disobedience of Judah serves as a reminder of the importance of community accountability in maintaining faithfulness to God.

What Does Zechariah 7:11 Mean
But they refused to pay attention

• Zechariah makes clear that the people’s problem was not ignorance but hard-hearted choice. They had heard the prophets “early and often” (2 Chronicles 36:15–16) yet, like their fathers, “they would not listen or incline their ear” (Jeremiah 17:23).

• Refusal to listen is the first step toward judgment. In Jeremiah 7:24 the Lord laments, “They did not listen or incline their ear, but followed the stubbornness of their own hearts.” The same pattern reappears in the New Testament when Jesus warns, “The one who rejects Me and does not receive My words has a judge” (John 12:48).

• True obedience begins with hearing (Romans 10:17). When the ear closes, the heart inevitably drifts.

and turned a stubborn shoulder

• The picture is of an ox stiffening its shoulder to resist the yoke, an image also used in Nehemiah 9:29, “They acted arrogantly and did not obey Your commandments but sinned against Your ordinances…and turned a stubborn shoulder.”

• Stubbornness moves from the ear to the will: once God’s voice is dismissed, resistance to His authority follows (1 Samuel 15:23).

• God calls His people to humble submission (Micah 6:8). A “stubborn shoulder” rejects that call, inviting the discipline described in Psalm 32:9—“Do not be like the horse or mule, which have no understanding but must be controlled by bit and bridle.”

they stopped up their ears from hearing

• The phrase intensifies the rebellion. They not only ignored God’s word; they actively blocked it out, echoing Isaiah 30:9–11 where Israelites say, “Speak to us pleasant words; prophesy illusions.”

• Stephen charges the Sanhedrin with the same crime: “You stiff-necked people…you always resist the Holy Spirit” (Acts 7:51).

• When ears close long enough, hearts harden (Hebrews 3:7–8). Yet Scripture urges, “Today, if you hear His voice, do not harden your hearts.” Persistent rejection ultimately leads to God’s voice growing silent (Amos 8:11–12).

summary

Zechariah 7:11 traces a tragic progression: deliberate refusal to listen, willful resistance, and total shutdown of God’s voice. The verse warns that neglecting God’s word is never neutral; it breeds deeper rebellion and invites discipline. By contrast, blessing flows to those who keep their ears open, shoulders yielded, and hearts soft before the Lord.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 11. - Pulled away the shoulder; they gave a stubborn, refractory shoulder, like an ox which refuses to have the yoke put on his neck, or draws hack when it feels the weight (Nehemiah 9:29; Hosea 4:16). Stopped their ears. Made their ears heavy. Τὰ ω΅τα αὐτῶν ἐβάρυναν (Septuagint); Isaiah 6:10; Isaiah 59:1. Three degrees of obduracy are named in this verse: they refused to listen; they resisted the warners; they exhibited open contempt for them. The full climax is given in the next verse.
