Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
And you are to tell him that this is what the LORD of Hosts says:
This phrase introduces a divine message, emphasizing the authority of God, referred to as the "LORD of Hosts," a title that underscores His sovereignty and command over heavenly armies. This title is frequently used in prophetic literature to assert God's power and authority, especially in contexts of judgment and restoration.

‘Here is a man whose name is the Branch:
The term "Branch" is a messianic title found in other prophetic books such as Isaiah 11:1 and Jeremiah 23:5. It symbolizes new growth and life, indicating a future leader from the line of David who will bring restoration.

and He will branch out from His place:
This phrase suggests expansion and growth, indicating that the Branch will extend His influence and reign. It implies a starting point, likely Jerusalem, from which His impact will spread. This can be seen as a prophecy of the spread of the Gospel and the growth of the Church, beginning in Jerusalem and reaching the ends of the earth, as seen in Acts 1:8.

and build the temple of the LORD:
The rebuilding of the temple is a central theme in post-exilic prophecy, symbolizing the restoration of proper worship and the presence of God among His people. Historically, this refers to the physical rebuilding of the Second Temple under Zerubbabel. However, it also has a deeper, spiritual fulfillment in the New Testament, where Jesus is seen as the cornerstone of a new, spiritual temple, the Church, as described in Ephesians 2:19-22. This dual fulfillment highlights both the immediate and ultimate purposes of God's plan for His people.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Zechariah
A prophet who received visions and messages from God to encourage the Israelites during the post-exilic period.

2. Joshua the High Priest
The immediate recipient of the prophecy, representing the priestly leadership of Israel.

3. The Branch
A messianic figure symbolizing the coming of Jesus Christ, who will fulfill God's promises.

4. The Temple of the LORD
Represents both the physical rebuilding of the temple in Jerusalem and the spiritual temple established through Christ.

5. The LORD of Hosts
A title for God emphasizing His sovereignty and command over heavenly armies.
Teaching Points
Messianic Fulfillment
The prophecy of the Branch is fulfilled in Jesus Christ, who is both priest and king, bridging the gap between God and humanity.

Spiritual Temple
Believers are called to be part of the spiritual temple, living stones built upon the foundation of Christ.

God's Sovereignty
The title "LORD of Hosts" reminds us of God's ultimate authority and power, encouraging trust in His plans.

Hope and Restoration
Just as the prophecy brought hope to the Israelites, it brings hope to us today, assuring us of God's ongoing work of restoration.

Active Participation
We are invited to participate in building God's kingdom, using our gifts and talents to serve and glorify Him.

What Does Zechariah 6:12 Mean
And you are to tell him

• Context: Zechariah is commanded to relay God’s word specifically to Joshua the high priest (Zechariah 6:11).

• Application: God’s messages are personal and purposeful—He addresses real people in real history. Compare with Nathan’s direct word to David (2 Samuel 7:4–5).

• Takeaway: The Lord often delivers His promises through human messengers, underlining both accountability and privilege for those who hear (Amos 3:7).

that this is what the LORD of Hosts says

• “LORD of Hosts” emphasizes God’s sovereign command over every army—angelic and earthly (1 Samuel 17:45).

• The phrase assures that the coming promise carries heaven’s full authority; see similar introductions in Isaiah 44:6 and Haggai 2:4.

• Confidence: When the Captain of all hosts speaks, fulfillment is guaranteed (Joshua 21:45).

Here is a man whose name is the Branch

• “Branch” is a title for Messiah (Zechariah 3:8; Jeremiah 23:5; Isaiah 11:1).

• He is fully human—“a man”—yet uniquely appointed by God, foreshadowing Christ’s incarnation (John 1:14; Philippians 2:7).

• The name reveals purpose: life, growth, restoration—everything dead stumps cannot produce on their own (Romans 15:12).

and He will branch out from His place

• Picture of organic, unstoppable growth: Jesus begins in humble Nazareth yet extends worldwide (Matthew 2:23; Acts 1:8).

• “His place” hints at both earthly roots (Micah 5:2) and heavenly origin (John 6:38).

• Fulfillment traced through the spread of the gospel and the gathering of every nation, echoing Isaiah 53:2 and Daniel 2:35.

and build the temple of the LORD

• Near term: Zerubbabel and Joshua oversee the second-temple project (Ezra 5:2), but the prophecy ultimately looks beyond stone and timber (Hebrews 8:5).

• Christ builds a living temple—His church—founded on Himself (Ephesians 2:20-22; 1 Peter 2:4-5).

• Future completion: the millennial and eternal dwelling of God with humanity (Zechariah 6:13; Revelation 21:22).

• Assurance: the same Branch who saves also sanctifies, fashioning believers into a holy habitation (John 14:2-3).

summary

Zechariah 6:12 points to a promised Messiah who is truly human yet divinely empowered, destined to spread from humble beginnings to global dominion and to construct the ultimate dwelling place of God. The prophecy found its first-century fulfillment in Jesus Christ and continues as He gathers and shapes His people into a living temple, guaranteeing a future where His glory fills all.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 12. - Speak unto him, saying. The prophet is to explain to Joshua the meaning of this public act. Behold the Man whose name is The BRANCH; literally, behold the man, BRANCH is his name (see note on Zechariah 3:8). The Targum has, "Behold the Man, Messiah is his name." It is plain that the term "Branch" or "Shoot" (LXX., Ἀνατολή: Vulgate, Oriens) could not be addressed to Joshua; indeed, the very form of the sentence, "his name," not "thy name," shows this. All who saw the transaction and heard the words must have understood that they had reference to the "Shoot" of David, the Messiah that was to come, to whom was committed the regal and priestly dignity. And he shall grow up out of his place; Septuagint, Καὶ ὑποκάτωθεν αὐτοῦ ἀνατελεῖ, "And item beneath him he shall spring up;" Vulgate, Et subter eum orietur; Drake, "He shall sprout forth from under himself;" Revised Version margin, "And it (or they) shall bud forth under him;" Hitzig, Ewald, "From under him there shall be sprouting." But them is no need to alter the rendering of the Authorized Version, which indicates that the shoot shall grow from its own soil, that Messiah shall arise in his own country and nation, and shall spring from a lowly origin to the highest glory (see Isaiah 11:1; Isaiah 53:2). He shall build the temple of the Lord. He should build, not the material temple whose foundations Zerubbabel had laid, but the spiritual temple of which the tabernacle and the temple of Jerusalem were only the type and shadow - that new sanctuary which Ezekiel beheld (41), a house not made with hands, the Church of the living God (Ephesians 2:20, etc.; 1 Peter 2:5).
