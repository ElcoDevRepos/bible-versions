Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Then Saul sent word to Jesse, saying,
This phrase indicates a formal communication from King Saul to Jesse, David's father. In the ancient Near Eastern context, sending a message through a messenger was a common practice for kings and leaders. This action shows Saul's authority and the importance of the message. Saul's communication with Jesse highlights the familial and tribal connections that were significant in Israelite society. Jesse, being from Bethlehem, is part of the tribe of Judah, which holds prophetic significance as the tribe from which the Messiah would come (Genesis 49:10).

“Let David remain in my service,
David had already been introduced to Saul's court as a skilled harpist to soothe Saul's troubled spirit (1 Samuel 16:14-23). This phrase indicates Saul's desire to keep David close, recognizing his value beyond just musical talent. The term "service" suggests a role of servitude and loyalty, which is significant in understanding David's early relationship with Saul. This period in David's life serves as preparation for his future kingship, illustrating God's providential guidance. David's service to Saul can be seen as a type of Christ, who came to serve and not to be served (Mark 10:45).

for I am pleased with him.”
Saul's pleasure with David is noteworthy, as it reflects David's favor in the eyes of the king, despite Saul's later jealousy and hostility. This favor can be seen as a divine orchestration, as God had already chosen David to be the future king of Israel (1 Samuel 16:1-13). The phrase underscores David's character and abilities, which earned him a place in Saul's court. This favor parallels the way Jesus found favor with God and man (Luke 2:52), highlighting David as a foreshadowing of Christ. Saul's initial approval of David sets the stage for the complex relationship that unfolds between them, marked by both admiration and enmity.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Saul
The first king of Israel, anointed by the prophet Samuel. At this point in the account, Saul is experiencing distressing spirits and seeks relief through music.

2. Jesse
The father of David, a Bethlehemite. Jesse is a significant figure as the patriarch of the family from which David, the future king, comes.

3. David
The youngest son of Jesse, anointed by Samuel to be the future king of Israel. At this time, he is serving Saul by playing the harp to soothe him.

4. Bethlehem
The hometown of Jesse and David. It is a small town in Judah, later known as the birthplace of Jesus Christ.

5. Samuel
The prophet who anointed both Saul and David as kings of Israel. Though not directly mentioned in this verse, his earlier actions set the stage for David's introduction to Saul.
Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty in Placement
God orchestrates circumstances to place His chosen servants where they need to be. David's service to Saul was not just a coincidence but a divine appointment preparing him for future leadership.

Faithfulness in Small Things
David's initial role was humble, yet he served faithfully. This teaches us the importance of being diligent and faithful in the tasks God gives us, no matter how small they may seem.

The Power of Godly Influence
David's presence and music had a calming effect on Saul, demonstrating the impact a godly person can have in a troubled environment. We are called to be a positive influence wherever God places us.

Favor with God and Man
David found favor with Saul because of his character and skills. As believers, we should strive to live in such a way that we find favor with both God and those around us.

Preparation for Greater Responsibility
David's time in Saul's service was preparation for his future role as king. God often uses our current circumstances to prepare us for greater responsibilities.

What Does 1 Samuel 16:22 Mean
Then Saul sent word

• The initiative lies with Saul, Israel’s first king (1 Samuel 9:17).

• He acts promptly after experiencing relief from David’s harp playing (1 Samuel 16:21).

• A royal summons carries legal force; compare the immediacy of a king’s command in Esther 4:11 and Daniel 3:2.

• God’s sovereignty is quietly on display—He guides even flawed rulers to advance His purposes (Proverbs 21:1).

to Jesse

• Jesse is David’s father, introduced earlier as “an Ephrathite of Bethlehem” (1 Samuel 17:12).

• By addressing Jesse, Saul honors family authority, echoing the fifth commandment’s respect for parental roles (Exodus 20:12).

• This moment links the royal court with the Bethlehem household that will later produce the Messiah (Isaiah 11:1; Matthew 1:6).

saying

• Scripture often slows the narrative with “saying” to spotlight the message itself (Genesis 3:14; 2 Samuel 7:5).

• Verbal confirmation is vital; Saul’s words will formalize David’s status.

• God frequently advances His plan through spoken declarations—see Gabriel’s announcement in Luke 1:19.

Let David remain in my service

• David moves from temporary musician (1 Samuel 16:21) to permanent court attendant—an early step toward kingship.

• The phrase highlights God’s pattern of training future leaders through humble service (Genesis 39:4 with Joseph; Luke 2:51 with Jesus).

• Saul’s acceptance fulfills Samuel’s earlier anointing of David (1 Samuel 16:13) without Saul yet knowing its full significance.

• Later, Saul will again keep David close, this time as a warrior (1 Samuel 18:2), showing how divine promotion can be gradual and multifaceted.

for I am pleased with him.

• Saul finds David “pleasing” (also 1 Samuel 18:5) because David’s skill and spirit-filled character meet a real need (1 Samuel 16:18, 23).

• Human favor mirrors divine favor; God had already declared His pleasure in David’s heart (Acts 13:22).

• The pattern anticipates the ultimate Son of David, of whom the Father says, “This is My beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased” (Matthew 3:17).

• Practical take-away: faithfulness in small assignments attracts both earthly and heavenly commendation (Luke 16:10).

summary

Saul’s royal message to Jesse marks a pivotal step in David’s rise. The king initiates, Jesse is honored, a formal declaration is given, service is secured, and delight is expressed. Each phrase reveals God’s quiet orchestration—using authority structures, family ties, spoken words, humble service, and earned favor—to position His chosen servant for future greatness.
