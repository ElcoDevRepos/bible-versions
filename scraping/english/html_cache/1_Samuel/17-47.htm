Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
And all those assembled here will know
This phrase emphasizes the public nature of the event. The confrontation between David and Goliath was witnessed by the armies of Israel and the Philistines, highlighting the importance of God's power being demonstrated before many. This public demonstration serves as a testimony to God's sovereignty and power, reinforcing the idea that God's actions are meant to be witnessed and acknowledged by all.

that it is not by sword or spear that the LORD saves;
This statement underscores the theme of divine intervention over human strength and weaponry. Throughout the Bible, God often chooses the weak and the unlikely to accomplish His purposes, as seen in Gideon's victory over the Midianites (Judges 7) and the fall of Jericho (Joshua 6). This phrase also foreshadows the New Testament teaching that salvation comes through faith and not by human effort (Ephesians 2:8-9).

for the battle is the LORD’s,
This declaration affirms God's ultimate control over the outcome of events. It reflects the biblical principle that God is the true warrior who fights on behalf of His people, as seen in Exodus 14:14, where Moses tells the Israelites, "The LORD will fight for you; you need only to be still." This concept is a recurring theme in Scripture, emphasizing reliance on God's power rather than human might.

and He will give all of you into our hands.”
David's confidence in God's deliverance is evident here. This assurance is rooted in the covenant relationship between God and Israel, where God promises to protect and deliver His people. The phrase also points to the prophetic nature of David's kingship, as he is a type of Christ, who ultimately defeats the spiritual enemies of sin and death. David's victory over Goliath prefigures Christ's victory over Satan, as seen in Colossians 2:15, where Christ disarms the powers and authorities, triumphing over them by the cross.

Teaching Points
Faith Over Fear
David's confidence in God overcomes the fear that paralyzes the Israelite army. Believers are encouraged to trust in God's power rather than be overwhelmed by circumstances.

God's Sovereignty in Battle
The verse underscores that ultimate victory belongs to God, not through human means. Christians are reminded to rely on God's strength in their spiritual battles.

Witness to God's Power
David's victory serves as a testimony to all present that God is the true deliverer. Believers are called to live in a way that demonstrates God's power to those around them.

Spiritual Warfare
The battle against Goliath is a physical representation of the spiritual battles believers face. Christians are encouraged to put on the full armor of God and trust in His deliverance.

God's Glory
The purpose of the battle is to show that God saves, bringing glory to His name. Believers should aim to glorify God in all their endeavors.

What Does 1 Samuel 17:47 Mean
And all those assembled here will know

• David speaks for God’s glory, not his own reputation.

• The whole battlefield—Israelites and Philistines—will witness that the living God is active.

• Similar divine “public demonstrations” appear in Joshua 4:24, “so that all the peoples of the earth may know the hand of the LORD,” and in 1 Kings 18:37, where Elijah prays that the crowd may “know that You, O LORD, are God”.

• God delights in making His power unmistakable to believer and skeptic alike.

that it is not by sword or spear that the LORD saves;

• Military technology and human strategy do not dictate the outcome; God does.

• Psalm 33:16–17 reminds us, “No king is saved by his vast army… a horse is a vain hope for salvation”.

• Zechariah 4:6 echoes the principle: “Not by might nor by power, but by My Spirit, says the LORD of Hosts”.

• Trust must settle on the Savior, not the sword.

for the battle is the LORD’s,

• Ownership of the conflict belongs to God; He is the true combatant.

• Exodus 14:14 declares, “The LORD will fight for you; you need only to be still”.

• 2 Chronicles 20:15 repeats, “The battle is not yours, but God’s”.

• When God claims the battle, the outcome is settled before the first blow is struck.

and He will give all of you into our hands.

• David confidently foretells total victory, crediting God alone.

• Joshua 10:8 provides a parallel promise: “Do not fear them, for I have delivered them into your hand; not one of them shall stand against you”.

• Romans 8:37 applies the same truth spiritually: “In all these things we are more than conquerors through Him who loved us”.

• The Lord’s deliverance is complete, leaving no room for boasting in human strength.

summary

David’s declaration before Goliath proclaims that God alone determines victory, making His power unmistakable to every onlooker. Salvation is never secured by human weapons but by the Lord who owns the battle and guarantees triumph for His people.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(47) For the battle is the Lord's . . .--Although we possess no special ode or psalm composed by David on the occasion of this mortal combat, in which, owing to his sure trust in Jehovah, he won his never-to-be-forgotten victory, yet in many of the compositions attributed to him in the Psalter we find memories of this, his first great triumph. So in Psalm 44:6-8 we read--

"I will not trust in my bow,

Neither shall my sword save me.

In God we boast all the day long,

And praise thy Name for ever."

And in Psalm 33:16-20,

"There is no king saved by the multitude of an host,

A mighty man is not delivered by much strength." . . .
