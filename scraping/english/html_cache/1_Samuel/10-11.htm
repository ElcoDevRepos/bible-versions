Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
When all those who had formerly known Saul
This phrase highlights the transformation in Saul's life. Saul was known to the people as the son of Kish, a wealthy Benjamite, and not as a spiritual leader or prophet. His previous life was characterized by his role in his father's household and his search for lost donkeys, which is a stark contrast to his current prophetic activity. This transformation is significant as it marks the beginning of Saul's divine appointment as king, showing that God can change a person's role and identity for His purposes.

saw him prophesying with the prophets
Prophesying in this context refers to being filled with the Spirit of God and speaking under divine inspiration. The prophets were a group of individuals who were known for their spiritual fervor and connection to God. Saul's participation with them indicates a divine endorsement and empowerment, which was necessary for his future role as king. This event is reminiscent of the Spirit of the Lord coming upon other leaders in Israel, such as the judges, to equip them for their tasks.

they asked one another, “What has happened to the son of Kish?
The question reflects the surprise and disbelief of the people who knew Saul. It underscores the unexpected nature of Saul's transformation and the divine intervention in his life. This moment serves as a public sign of Saul's new calling and the beginning of his journey as Israel's first king. It also highlights the theme of God's sovereignty in choosing and equipping leaders, regardless of their past or social standing.

Is Saul also among the prophets?”
This rhetorical question became a proverbial expression in Israel, indicating an unexpected or surprising change in someone's behavior or status. It points to the miraculous nature of Saul's transformation and the work of the Holy Spirit in his life. This phrase also foreshadows the complexities of Saul's reign, as his spiritual experiences did not always translate into faithful leadership. The question invites reflection on the nature of true spiritual transformation and the importance of ongoing obedience to God.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Saul
The son of Kish, who was anointed by Samuel to be the first king of Israel. This verse marks a significant transformation in his life as he begins to prophesy.

2. Prophets
A group of individuals who were known for their spiritual insight and communication of God's messages. Saul's association with them is unexpected by those who knew him.

3. Kish
Saul's father, a Benjamite, whose lineage is significant in understanding Saul's background and the surprise of his prophetic activity.

4. Israel
The nation over which Saul was anointed king. This event takes place within the broader account of Israel's transition from a theocracy to a monarchy.

5. Samuel
The prophet who anointed Saul and played a crucial role in guiding Israel during this transitional period.
Teaching Points
Transformation by the Spirit
Saul's unexpected prophesying demonstrates the transformative power of the Holy Spirit. God can change anyone, regardless of their past or background.

God's Sovereignty in Choosing Leaders
Saul's anointing and subsequent actions remind us that God chooses leaders according to His purposes, often beyond human expectations.

Community Perception vs. God's Plan
The reaction of those who knew Saul highlights the difference between human perception and God's plan. We should be open to God's work in others, even when it defies our expectations.

The Role of Prophecy
This event underscores the importance of prophecy as a means of communicating God's will and the Spirit's role in equipping individuals for His service.

What Does 1 Samuel 10:11 Mean
When all those who had formerly known Saul

- The crowd knew Saul as a tall, capable young man from Gibeah, but nothing in his past suggested a prophetic gift (1 Samuel 9:1–2).

- Their memory of him highlights how suddenly God can redirect a life—much like Moses at the burning bush (Exodus 3:1–6) or Gideon in the winepress (Judges 6:11–12).

- God’s Spirit makes recognizable change visible to onlookers, proving that transformation is real (2 Corinthians 5:17).

saw him prophesying with the prophets

- Saul is swept up in a band of prophets just as Samuel foretold (1 Samuel 10:5–6).

- This scene underscores that prophetic utterance is Spirit-initiated, not self-generated (Numbers 11:25; Acts 2:17).

- Standing “with the prophets” shows immediate fellowship with God’s servants, foreshadowing how the Spirit forms unity in Christ’s body (Ephesians 4:3–4).

they asked one another, “What has happened to the son of Kish?”

- Shocked neighbors vocalize their amazement, much like Nazareth reacted to Jesus (Mark 6:2–3).

- Their question recognizes that something beyond human lineage is at work; God is intervening despite ordinary ancestry (1 Corinthians 1:26–29).

- The phrase “son of Kish” reminds readers that God chooses specific individuals for His purposes, fulfilling Samuel’s private anointing earlier in the chapter (1 Samuel 10:1).

Is Saul also among the prophets?

- The rhetorical question becomes a proverb (1 Samuel 10:12), capturing the idea that God can bestow spiritual gifting where it’s least expected (Amos 7:14–15).

- It signals a new role for Saul: Israel’s first king will lead under prophetic influence, emphasizing that political authority must submit to divine authority (Deuteronomy 17:18–20).

- The episode foreshadows later times when Saul’s obedience will waver, reminding us that an initial Spirit-empowerment requires ongoing faithfulness (Galatians 5:16–25).

summary

1 Samuel 10:11 reveals God’s sudden, unmistakable work in Saul’s life. Friends who once knew an ordinary man now witness him miraculously speaking God’s words. Their astonishment—“Is Saul also among the prophets?”—testifies that the Lord can equip anyone, regardless of background, for His purposes. The verse invites confidence that when God’s Spirit moves, genuine transformation follows, startling observers and glorifying the One who changes hearts.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(11) What is this?--The natural expression of extreme surprise at the sudden change which had come over one so well known at Gibeah as Saul evidently was, shows us that this was his home. The words, "What is this that is come unto the son of Kish?" seem to tell us that the life hitherto led by Saul was a life very different in all respects to the life led by the sons of the prophets in their schools. It need not be assumed that the youth and early manhood of the future king had been wild and dissolute, but simply that the way of life had been rough and uncultured--a life spent in what we should call "country pursuits," in contradistinction to the pursuit of knowledge and of higher acquirements. It is evident from the statement here and in the following verse that a considerable respect for these schools had already grown up among the people.

Is Saul also among the prophets?--In 1Samuel 19:23 we again find Saul, but under changed circumstances, under the influence of a Divine and coercing power, and uttering strange words, and singing hymns as one trained in the prophets' schools. It was probably this recurrence of the same incident in the king's life which gave rise to the saying, or proverb, which expresses amazement at the unexpected appearance of any man in a position which had hitherto been quite strange to him. "Is Saul among the preachers of Christ? Was a question of wonder asked by the friends of St. Paul" (Galatians 1:23).--Wordsworth.
