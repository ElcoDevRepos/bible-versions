Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Once again the LORD called, “Samuel!”
This phrase indicates the persistence of God in reaching out to Samuel, highlighting the importance of divine calling. In the biblical context, God often calls individuals multiple times to ensure they understand His message, as seen with Moses (Exodus 3) and Gideon (Judges 6). The repetition underscores the significance of Samuel's future role as a prophet. The use of Samuel's name signifies a personal relationship, as God knows His chosen ones intimately.

So Samuel got up, went to Eli, and said, “Here I am, for you have called me.”
Samuel's response demonstrates obedience and respect for Eli, his mentor. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, young apprentices served under priests to learn and assist in temple duties. Samuel's willingness to serve reflects his humility and readiness to learn. This interaction also foreshadows Samuel's future role as a servant leader in Israel, paralleling Christ's servanthood (Mark 10:45).

“My son, I did not call,” Eli replied.
Eli's response shows his paternal relationship with Samuel, addressing him affectionately as "my son." This reflects the familial structure within the temple, where Eli acted as a father figure. Historically, Eli was the high priest, and his guidance was crucial for Samuel's spiritual development. Eli's inability to recognize God's call initially may symbolize the spiritual decline in Israel, contrasting with Samuel's rising prophetic role.

“Go back and lie down.”
Eli instructs Samuel to return to rest, indicating a lack of awareness of the divine encounter. This phrase suggests a period of waiting and preparation, as God often calls individuals during times of rest or solitude (e.g., Jacob's dream in Genesis 28). The instruction to lie down can also symbolize a return to a state of receptivity, preparing Samuel for further divine communication. This moment of waiting is akin to the patience required in spiritual growth and discernment.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Samuel
A young boy serving in the temple under Eli's guidance. He is dedicated to the Lord by his mother, Hannah, and is being called by God for a special purpose.

2. Eli
The high priest and judge of Israel at the time. He is responsible for mentoring Samuel in the ways of the Lord.

3. The LORD
The God of Israel, who is calling Samuel to be His prophet. This calling marks a significant moment in Samuel's life and in the history of Israel.

4. The Temple
The place where Samuel serves and where the Lord calls him. It is a central place of worship and spiritual activity in Israel.

5. The Calling Event
This is the second time God calls Samuel, indicating the persistence and patience of God in reaching out to those He chooses.
Teaching Points
God's Persistent Call
God often calls us more than once, showing His patience and desire for us to respond to His voice.

Recognizing God's Voice
Like Samuel, we may not always recognize God's voice at first. It is important to be attentive and seek guidance from spiritual mentors.

Mentorship in Faith
Eli's role highlights the importance of having spiritual mentors who can guide us in understanding and responding to God's call.

Obedience and Availability
Samuel's willingness to respond, even when he did not fully understand, teaches us the importance of being available and obedient to God's call.

God's Personal Relationship
God's call to Samuel by name emphasizes His personal relationship with us. He knows us individually and calls us for specific purposes.

What Does 1 Samuel 3:6 Mean
Once again the LORD called, “Samuel!”

- The second call shows the Lord’s loving persistence. He does not abandon a heart that can be trained to listen (cf. Exodus 3:4; Isaiah 43:1).

- Repetition underscores certainty—God is truly speaking, not a stray thought (Genesis 41:32).

- The divine voice breaks the night silence, reminding us that revelation often comes when we are quiet enough to hear (Psalm 46:10).

So Samuel got up

- Immediate action reveals tender obedience. A boy rises in the dark because he assumes the voice has authority (Genesis 22:3; Psalm 119:60).

- Physical movement mirrors spiritual responsiveness; God prizes willing feet (Romans 10:15).

- No hesitation hints at a clear conscience and teachable spirit (Proverbs 4:18).

Went to Eli, and said, “Here I am, for you have called me.”

- Samuel seeks guidance from the priest he trusts, honoring God-given leadership (Hebrews 13:17).

- “Here I am” echoes Isaiah 6:8 and Jacob in Genesis 46:2—an open-handed availability that marks prospective servants.

- Mistaking the Lord’s call for Eli’s voice shows how fresh Samuel is to divine communication (1 Samuel 3:7); yet his willingness positions him to receive further light (John 7:17).

“My son, I did not call,” Eli replied.

- Eli’s gentle address, “My son,” exposes pastoral affection, even in spiritual decline (1 Samuel 2:29–30).

- The priest himself fails to discern that the Lord is speaking, a sober warning that familiarity with sacred duty can dull hearing (Judges 2:10; Revelation 3:1).

- Spiritual mentorship involves admitting limits and pointing learners back to God’s presence, not to personal authority (Acts 8:30-31).

“Go back and lie down.”

- Eli counsels rest; waiting becomes part of learning to hear (Psalm 37:7; Lamentations 3:26).

- God’s voice is not forced by frantic activity; it is received in surrendered stillness (1 Kings 19:12).

- Samuel’s return to bed sets the stage for a clearer revelation, teaching that obedience to simple instructions precedes deeper insight (Luke 16:10).

summary

Verse 6 captures a gracious, repeated call from the Lord, a prompt and humble response from young Samuel, and Eli’s imperfect yet caring guidance. Together they reveal a God who persists, a heart that obeys without delay, and a learning process in which patience and quiet availability invite clearer revelation.
