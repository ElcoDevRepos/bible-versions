I prayed for this boy
Hannah's prayer is a profound example of faith and persistence in seeking God's intervention. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, barrenness was often seen as a source of shame and a sign of divine disfavor. Hannah's earnest prayer at the tabernacle in Shiloh reflects her deep desire for a child and her belief in God's power to change her circumstances. This phrase highlights the importance of prayer in the life of a believer, demonstrating that God hears and responds to the heartfelt petitions of His people. Hannah's prayer can be seen as a precursor to the New Testament teaching on prayer, such as in James 5:16, which emphasizes the power of a righteous person's prayer.

and since the LORD has granted me
The acknowledgment of the LORD's role in granting her request underscores the sovereignty and faithfulness of God. In the historical context, the name "LORD" (YHWH) is significant as it denotes the covenant-keeping God of Israel. Hannah's recognition of God's hand in her situation is a testament to her faith and understanding of God's character. This phrase also connects to the broader biblical theme of God as the giver of life and blessings, as seen in Genesis 21:1-2 with the birth of Isaac to Sarah. It reflects the biblical principle that every good and perfect gift comes from above (James 1:17).

what I asked of Him
Hannah's specific request for a son and God's fulfillment of that request illustrates the personal nature of God's relationship with His people. It shows that God is attentive to individual needs and desires, responding to the specific prayers of His followers. This phrase also highlights the importance of aligning one's requests with God's will, as seen in 1 John 5:14-15, where believers are encouraged to ask according to His will. Hannah's vow to dedicate her son to the LORD's service (1 Samuel 1:11) demonstrates her commitment to honoring God with the answer to her prayer, setting a precedent for dedicating one's blessings back to God.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Hannah
A devout woman who was initially barren. She fervently prayed for a child and vowed to dedicate him to the Lord if her prayer was answered.

2. Elkanah
Hannah's husband, who loved her deeply despite her initial barrenness. He supported her spiritual devotion and pilgrimage to Shiloh.

3. Samuel
The child for whom Hannah prayed. His birth was a direct answer to her prayers, and he would grow to become a significant prophet and leader in Israel.

4. Eli
The high priest at Shiloh who initially misunderstood Hannah's prayer but later blessed her, affirming her request to God.

5. Shiloh
The religious center where the Ark of the Covenant was located during this period. It was here that Hannah prayed and made her vow to God.
Teaching Points
The Power of Persistent Prayer
Hannah's account teaches us the importance of persistent and heartfelt prayer. Despite her initial barrenness, she continued to seek God earnestly.

Faith in God's Timing
Hannah's experience reminds us that God's timing is perfect. Her patience and faith were rewarded in due time with the birth of Samuel.

Dedication to God
Hannah's vow to dedicate Samuel to the Lord challenges us to consider how we can dedicate our blessings and talents to God's service.

Trust in God's Faithfulness
The fulfillment of Hannah's prayer encourages us to trust in God's faithfulness and His ability to answer our deepest desires according to His will.

The Role of Community in Faith
Eli's blessing and the support of her husband Elkanah highlight the importance of community and spiritual leadership in nurturing our faith.

What Does 1 Samuel 1:27 Mean
I prayed for this boy

• Hannah’s words are personal and direct; she is recounting a real moment of earnest, persistent prayer (1 Samuel 1:10-11).

• The phrase underscores that prayer is intentional—Hannah chose to pour out her heart rather than accept barrenness as final (James 5:16; Philippians 4:6).

• “This boy” highlights specificity in prayer; like Isaac interceding for Rebekah (Genesis 25:21) or the church praying for Peter (Acts 12:5), Hannah knew exactly what she desired from the LORD.

• Her posture models perseverance; she had prayed over months or years, not merely minutes (Luke 18:1).

and since the LORD has granted me

• Hannah instantly gives credit where it belongs. The conception of Samuel was not coincidence but divine intervention—“the LORD remembered her” (1 Samuel 1:19-20; Psalm 34:4).

• Answered prayer proves God’s faithfulness to His covenant people; He hears and responds (Psalm 116:1-2; Matthew 7:7).

• The word “granted” points to grace: God owed her nothing, yet He freely bestowed a gift (Ephesians 3:20; 1 John 5:14-15).

• Hannah’s testimony encourages you to view every answered prayer as a fresh reason for worship, just as the leper returned to thank Jesus (Luke 17:15-16).

what I asked of Him

• God’s response matched Hannah’s request—no partial fulfillment, no compromise (Mark 11:24; John 16:24).

• This completeness reinforces that the LORD delights to align His will with the righteous desires He Himself plants in His people (Psalm 37:4; Luke 1:13).

• Hannah’s next act is dedication (1 Samuel 1:28). When God answers, stewardship follows; blessings are meant to be offered back for His purposes (Romans 12:1).

• The verse therefore teaches that specific, faith-filled petitions, offered humbly and persistently, receive specific, faith-building answers.

summary

Hannah’s simple sentence is a testimony of steadfast prayer, gracious divine response, and complete fulfillment. She prayed specifically, God granted mercifully, and she recognized the blessing fully—an unbroken chain that invites you to pray with the same confidence and to return every answered request to the LORD who gives so generously.
