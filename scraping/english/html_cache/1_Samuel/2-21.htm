Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
So the LORD attended to Hannah
This phrase highlights the divine intervention and faithfulness of God in response to Hannah's prayers. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, barrenness was often seen as a source of shame and a sign of divine disfavor. Hannah's earlier prayer in 1 Samuel 1:10-11 shows her deep distress and her vow to dedicate her child to the LORD if He granted her a son. The LORD's attention to Hannah signifies His compassion and the fulfillment of His promises, echoing His faithfulness to other women in the Bible, such as Sarah and Rachel, who also experienced miraculous births.

and she conceived and gave birth to three sons and two daughters
This phrase underscores the abundant blessing that God bestowed upon Hannah. After the birth of Samuel, whom she dedicated to the LORD, God continued to bless her with more children. This multiplication of her offspring can be seen as a sign of divine favor and a reversal of her previous barrenness. It also reflects the biblical theme of God blessing those who are faithful to Him, as seen in the stories of other patriarchs and matriarchs. The number of children, five in total, may symbolize completeness and God's generous provision.

Meanwhile, the boy Samuel grew up in the presence of the LORD
Samuel's upbringing in the presence of the LORD indicates his unique role and calling as a prophet and judge in Israel. Raised in the tabernacle under the guidance of Eli the priest, Samuel was set apart for God's service from a young age. This phrase foreshadows his future significance in Israel's history, as he would anoint the first two kings of Israel, Saul and David. The presence of the LORD signifies not only physical proximity to the tabernacle but also spiritual growth and divine favor. Samuel's life parallels that of Jesus Christ, who also grew in wisdom and stature and in favor with God and man (Luke 2:52).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Hannah
A devout woman who was initially barren but fervently prayed for a child. God answered her prayers, and she became the mother of Samuel, whom she dedicated to the LORD.

2. The LORD
The covenant God of Israel, who is attentive to the prayers of His people and acts according to His will and purpose.

3. Samuel
The firstborn son of Hannah, dedicated to the LORD's service. He grew up to be a significant prophet and judge in Israel.

4. Shiloh
The place where the tabernacle of the LORD was located during this time, and where Samuel was raised in the presence of the LORD.

5. Eli
The high priest at Shiloh, under whose care Samuel was raised.
Teaching Points
God's Faithfulness
God is faithful to His promises and attentive to the prayers of His people. Just as He remembered Hannah, He remembers us in our times of need.

The Power of Prayer
Hannah's account emphasizes the importance of persistent and heartfelt prayer. We are encouraged to bring our deepest desires and needs before God.

God's Sovereignty
The LORD's intervention in Hannah's life demonstrates His sovereign control over all circumstances, including the opening and closing of the womb.

Dedication to God
Hannah's dedication of Samuel to the LORD serves as a model for us to dedicate our lives and the lives of our children to God's service.

Growth in God's Presence
Samuel's growth in the presence of the LORD highlights the importance of spiritual growth and development in a godly environment.

What Does 1 Samuel 2:21 Mean
So the LORD attended to Hannah

- “So the LORD attended to Hannah” (1 Samuel 2:21) echoes 1 Samuel 1:19, where the Lord “remembered her.” God’s mindful care moves from promise to fulfillment.

- The phrase underscores His personal involvement, similar to Genesis 21:1 with Sarah and Luke 1:13 with Elizabeth.

- Key truths:

• God hears persistent, faith-filled prayer (1 Samuel 1:10–17; Psalm 34:15).

• He remains faithful to His covenant nature (Deuteronomy 7:9).

• Divine timing often follows seasons of waiting, refining character while magnifying His glory (James 5:11).

And she conceived and gave birth to three sons and two daughters

- Hannah had vowed to dedicate her firstborn (1 Samuel 1:11). In response, the Lord multiplied back five more children.

- This illustrates the principle that surrendered gifts invite greater blessing (Luke 6:38; Proverbs 3:9-10).

- It also fulfills Old-Testament promises that fruitfulness accompanies obedience (Deuteronomy 28:11; Psalm 127:3-5).

- Observations:

• God’s generosity exceeds what we release to Him.

• Each child is a tangible reminder of His covenant mercy (Psalm 103:17).

• The narrative encourages parents to trust God with what they dedicate to Him.

Meanwhile, the boy Samuel grew up in the presence of the LORD

- While Hannah’s home bustles with new life, Samuel matures at Shiloh under Eli’s oversight (1 Samuel 2:18).

- Growth “in the presence of the LORD” points to:

• Spiritual formation anchored in worship and service (Psalm 84:4).

• Ongoing favor—paralleling Jesus’ childhood in Luke 2:40, 52.

• God’s preparation of a prophet during national decline, showing His redemptive plan continues alongside private blessings (Judges 21:25; 1 Samuel 3:1).

- Practical takeaways:

• Children flourish when placed where God is honored (Proverbs 22:6).

• Family blessing and ministry calling are not competing but complementary facets of God’s design.

summary

1 Samuel 2:21 reveals a God who remembers, rewards, and raises up. Hannah’s prayers are answered abundantly; her sacrifice unlocks multiplied blessing; and Samuel’s steady growth assures Israel’s future leadership. The verse invites confidence that the Lord sees, supplies, and shapes His people for His glory.
