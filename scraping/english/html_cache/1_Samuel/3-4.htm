Then the LORD called to Samuel
In this passage, the LORD initiates communication with Samuel, marking a significant moment in biblical history. This calling occurs during a time when "the word of the LORD was rare" (1 Samuel 3:1), indicating a period of spiritual drought in Israel. Samuel, a young boy serving under Eli the priest, is chosen by God to be a prophet, highlighting God's sovereignty in selecting His messengers. This calling is reminiscent of other divine callings in Scripture, such as Moses at the burning bush (Exodus 3:4) and Isaiah's vision in the temple (Isaiah 6:8). The LORD's direct communication with Samuel foreshadows the prophetic role Samuel will play in guiding Israel and anointing its first kings, Saul and David.

and he answered, “Here I am.”
Samuel's response, "Here I am," reflects a readiness and willingness to serve, similar to responses from other biblical figures like Abraham (Genesis 22:1) and Isaiah (Isaiah 6:8). This phrase indicates humility and obedience, qualities that are essential for those called by God. Samuel's immediate response, despite not yet recognizing the voice of the LORD, demonstrates his faithful character and sets the stage for his future role as a prophet. This moment also prefigures the obedience of Christ, who came to do the will of the Father (John 6:38), emphasizing the importance of readiness and submission to God's call.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Samuel
A young boy serving in the temple under the priest Eli. Samuel is dedicated to God by his mother, Hannah, and is being raised in the house of the LORD at Shiloh.

2. The LORD
The God of Israel, who initiates communication with Samuel, marking the beginning of Samuel's prophetic ministry.

3. Eli
The high priest of Israel at the time, under whose guidance Samuel is serving. Eli plays a crucial role in helping Samuel recognize the voice of God.

4. Shiloh
The religious center of Israel during this period, where the tabernacle and the Ark of the Covenant are located. It is here that Samuel receives his calling.

5. The Calling Event
This is a pivotal moment where God directly calls Samuel, signifying a transition in leadership and the beginning of Samuel's role as a prophet.
Teaching Points
God's Initiative in Calling
God takes the first step in calling individuals for His purposes. We must be attentive and open to His voice in our lives.

Recognizing God's Voice
Like Samuel, we may need guidance to discern God's voice. Spiritual mentors and scripture can help us recognize when God is speaking to us.

Readiness to Serve
Samuel's response, "Here I am," reflects a heart ready to serve. We should cultivate a willingness to respond to God's call with obedience and faith.

The Role of Mentorship
Eli's role in helping Samuel understand God's call underscores the importance of spiritual mentorship in our faith journey.

God's Call at Any Age
Samuel was a young boy when God called him, reminding us that God can use anyone, regardless of age, for His purposes.

What Does 1 Samuel 3:4 Mean
Then

• The verse opens with a simple time marker: “Then.” It links directly to 1 Samuel 3:1-3, where God’s word was “rare” and “visions were scarce.” Into that spiritual drought, God acts.

• Scripture often uses small words to show decisive moments—compare Genesis 22:1 (“After these things”) and Acts 2:1 (“When the day of Pentecost came”). Each signals a turning point orchestrated by the Lord.

• The timing reminds us that God is never late; His interventions arrive exactly when He intends (Galatians 4:4).

the LORD called

• The initiative is entirely God’s. Samuel doesn’t seek a revelation; God breaks the silence.

• Similar divine summons appear in Exodus 3:4 (Moses), Isaiah 6:8 (Isaiah), and Acts 9:4 (Saul). Each time, God’s voice is personal, unmistakable, and authoritative.

• The literal wording underscores that the Lord still speaks to real people in real history—His word is not myth but fact (Hebrews 1:1-2).

to Samuel

• God singles out a young boy serving faithfully in the tabernacle (1 Samuel 3:1). Age, status, or experience do not limit divine choice—seen also with Jeremiah 1:5 and Mary in Luke 1:26-30.

• By naming Samuel, the Lord affirms personal relationship; He knows His servants by name (John 10:3).

• This personal call shows grace: Samuel didn’t earn it; God simply chose him (Ephesians 2:8-9).

and he answered

• Samuel’s immediate response models readiness. There is no delay, excuse, or bargaining—just action.

• Comparable obedience appears in Isaiah 6:8 (“Here am I. Send me!”) and Acts 9:10 (“Yes, Lord,” answered Ananias).

• Quick obedience reflects trust that God’s voice is good and authoritative (John 14:15).

"Here I am."

• The phrase (also in Genesis 22:1; Exodus 3:4) conveys availability, attentiveness, and surrender.

• “Here I am” states presence before it seeks instruction; relationship precedes assignment.

• The words prefigure Christ’s own readiness in Hebrews 10:7 (“Here I am…to do Your will, O God”), inviting every believer to echo the same posture (Romans 12:1).

summary

A moment of history—“Then”—becomes a life-changing encounter because the living God speaks into spiritual dryness. The Lord takes the initiative, calls Samuel by name, and the young servant responds without hesitation, “Here I am.” The verse paints a timeless picture: God still calls, personally and purposefully, and He seeks the same willing, immediate answer from every heart that hears His voice.
