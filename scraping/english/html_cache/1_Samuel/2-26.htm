Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
And the boy Samuel
Samuel was dedicated to the Lord by his mother, Hannah, as a fulfillment of her vow. He was raised in the tabernacle at Shiloh under the priest Eli. This setting is significant as it was a time of spiritual decline in Israel, with corrupt priests like Eli's sons, Hophni and Phinehas. Samuel's presence in the tabernacle symbolizes a new beginning and hope for Israel.

continued to grow in stature
This phrase indicates Samuel's physical growth and development. It parallels the growth of other significant biblical figures, such as Jesus, who "grew in wisdom and stature" (Luke 2:52). It suggests that Samuel was healthy and strong, preparing him for the leadership role he would eventually assume as a prophet and judge over Israel.

and in favor with the LORD
Samuel's favor with the Lord highlights his spiritual growth and the divine approval of his life and actions. Unlike Eli's sons, who were corrupt and disobedient, Samuel's life was marked by obedience and dedication to God. This favor is reminiscent of other biblical figures like Joseph and David, who also found favor with God due to their faithfulness.

and with man
Samuel's favor with man indicates that he was respected and admired by those around him. This is significant in a time when the priesthood was marred by corruption. Samuel's integrity and dedication set him apart, earning him the respect of the people. This dual favor with God and man is a precursor to the ministry of Jesus, who also grew in favor with God and man, as noted in the New Testament.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Samuel
A young boy dedicated to the Lord by his mother, Hannah, serving under the priest Eli in the tabernacle at Shiloh. Samuel is a pivotal figure in Israel's history, serving as a prophet, priest, and judge.

2. Eli
The high priest at Shiloh, under whose guidance Samuel is growing. Eli's own sons are corrupt, contrasting with Samuel's righteous development.

3. Shiloh
The religious center of Israel at the time, where the tabernacle and the Ark of the Covenant are located. It is here that Samuel serves and grows.

4. Hannah
Samuel's mother, who prayed fervently for a child and dedicated Samuel to the Lord's service as a fulfillment of her vow.

5. The LORD
The God of Israel, who shows favor to Samuel as he grows, indicating divine approval and blessing.
Teaching Points
Balanced Growth
Just as Samuel grew in stature and favor with both God and men, Christians are called to pursue holistic growth—spiritually, physically, and socially.

Divine Favor
Seeking God's favor should be a priority in our lives. This involves living a life of obedience, faithfulness, and dedication to God's will.

Role Models
Samuel serves as a role model for young believers, demonstrating that age is not a barrier to spiritual maturity and influence.

Contrast with Corruption
Samuel's growth in favor contrasts with the corruption of Eli's sons, reminding us of the importance of integrity and righteousness.

Parental Influence
Hannah's dedication of Samuel to the Lord underscores the impact of parental faith and commitment on a child's spiritual development.

What Does 1 Samuel 2:26 Mean
And the boy Samuel

Samuel is introduced here as “the boy,” reminding us that God can work powerfully through a child.

• His whole life had been dedicated to the LORD before his birth (1 Samuel 1:20, 27-28).

• Even while young, he served at the tabernacle in Shiloh (1 Samuel 2:18).

• The verse signals a contrast with Eli’s corrupt sons (1 Samuel 2:12-17), showing that God was raising up a faithful servant from a tender age.

continued to grow

The wording points to an ongoing, steady process.

• Growth is not a moment but a journey—God’s hand shaping Samuel day by day (1 Samuel 3:19).

• Philippians 1:6 echoes the thought: “He who began a good work in you will perfect it.”

• Believers are called to “grow in the grace and knowledge of our Lord” (2 Peter 3:18).

in stature

Physical maturity mattered because Samuel would need strength to lead the nation.

• Like John the Baptist, he “grew and became strong in spirit” (Luke 1:80).

• Jesus Himself “grew in wisdom and stature” (Luke 2:52), showing God values whole-person development.

• A sound body can serve as a vessel for faithful ministry (1 Corinthians 6:19-20).

and in favor

“Favor” speaks of pleasantness, approval, and goodwill.

• Proverbs 3:3-4 promises that loyalty and faithfulness bring “favor and good repute.”

• Favor is not earned by compromise but by consistent godliness that others recognize (Daniel 1:9).

• God often opens doors for those walking uprightly (Psalm 84:11).

with the LORD

First and foremost, Samuel’s life pleased God.

• The LORD later called him by name (1 Samuel 3:4) and let none of his words fall to the ground (1 Samuel 3:19).

• Joseph’s story parallels this: “The LORD was with Joseph, and he became successful” (Genesis 39:2).

• Humble obedience draws divine favor (James 4:10).

and with man

Samuel’s integrity also impressed people.

• All Israel recognized him as a prophet (1 Samuel 3:20).

• When a person’s ways please the LORD, “He makes even his enemies to be at peace with him” (Proverbs 16:7).

• The early church enjoyed similar reputation, “having favor with all the people” (Acts 2:47).

summary

1 Samuel 2:26 paints a complete picture of healthy, God-given growth: a child dedicated to the LORD, progressing physically, spiritually, and socially. The verse encourages believers to pursue balanced development—maturing outwardly while cultivating inner character—so that we, like Samuel, might walk in continuous favor with God and with those around us.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(26) Grew on, and was in favour.--The very expressions of the biographer of Samuel were adopted by St. Luke when, in the early chapters of his Gospel, he wishes to describe in a few striking words the boyhood and youth of Him who was far greater than the child-prophet of Israel.
