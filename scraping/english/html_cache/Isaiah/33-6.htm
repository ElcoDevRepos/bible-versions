He will be the sure foundation for your times
This phrase emphasizes God's role as a stable and reliable foundation amidst the uncertainties of life. In the context of Isaiah, the people of Judah faced threats from Assyrian invasion, and this assurance would have been a source of comfort. The imagery of a "foundation" is reminiscent of Jesus' parable of the wise and foolish builders (Matthew 7:24-27), where building on the rock symbolizes a life grounded in obedience to God's word. Theologically, this points to God's unchanging nature and His faithfulness throughout history, as seen in Hebrews 13:8, which states that Jesus Christ is the same yesterday, today, and forever.

a storehouse of salvation, wisdom, and knowledge
This phrase highlights the abundance and sufficiency found in God. The "storehouse" suggests a vast, inexhaustible supply, indicating that God provides everything necessary for spiritual well-being. Salvation here refers to deliverance, not only from physical enemies but also from sin and spiritual death, aligning with the New Testament understanding of salvation through Christ (Ephesians 2:8-9). Wisdom and knowledge are often linked in Scripture, as seen in Proverbs 2:6, where the Lord gives wisdom, and from His mouth come knowledge and understanding. This suggests that true wisdom and knowledge are divine gifts, essential for navigating life's challenges.

The fear of the LORD is Zion’s treasure
The "fear of the LORD" is a recurring biblical theme, representing reverence, awe, and obedience to God. Proverbs 9:10 states that the fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom, indicating its foundational role in a believer's life. In the context of Zion, which often symbolizes God's people or Jerusalem, this fear is described as a "treasure," suggesting it is of immense value and worth. This aligns with the idea that a proper relationship with God, marked by reverence and obedience, is the most precious possession one can have. Theologically, this points to the covenant relationship between God and His people, where reverence for God leads to blessings and spiritual prosperity.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Isaiah
A major prophet in the Old Testament, Isaiah's ministry was primarily to the Kingdom of Judah. He prophesied during a time of great political turmoil and spiritual decline.

2. Zion
Often used to refer to Jerusalem, Zion is symbolic of God's chosen people and His dwelling place. It represents the spiritual center of God's kingdom.

3. The LORD
The covenant name of God, Yahweh, emphasizing His eternal, self-existent nature and His relationship with His people.

4. Judah
The southern kingdom of Israel, which faced threats from surrounding nations and internal spiritual decay during Isaiah's time.

5. Assyrian Threat
During Isaiah's ministry, the Assyrian Empire was a dominant force, posing a significant threat to Judah and its neighbors.
Teaching Points
God as Our Foundation
In times of uncertainty, God remains the unshakeable foundation. Just as a building relies on a solid base, our lives must be grounded in Him.

The Storehouse of Salvation
Salvation is not just a one-time event but a continual resource. God provides ongoing deliverance and security for those who trust in Him.

Wisdom and Knowledge from God
True wisdom and knowledge come from God. In a world full of information, discernment rooted in God's truth is essential.

The Fear of the LORD as Treasure
Reverence for God is not just a duty but a treasure. It enriches our lives and aligns us with His purposes.

Zion's Example for the Church
Just as Zion was to treasure the fear of the LORD, the church today is called to prioritize reverence and awe for God in its mission and community life.

What Does Isaiah 33:6 Mean
He will be the sure foundation for your times

• Our days feel shaky, yet the Lord promises immovable stability. Isaiah elsewhere pictures Him as “a stone, a tested stone, a precious cornerstone” (Isaiah 28:16).

• Jesus fulfills this promise: “For no one can lay a foundation other than the one already laid, which is Jesus Christ” (1 Corinthians 3:11).

• When we build on Him, storms cannot destroy us (Matthew 7:24-25), because “The LORD is my rock, my fortress, and my deliverer” (Psalm 18:2).

• This foundation is not temporary; it is “for your times,” covering every season—joy, sorrow, peace, crisis.

A storehouse of salvation

• The word picture shifts from bedrock to a granary packed with provisions. With God, rescue is never in short supply.

• Mary sang, “He has provided redemption for His people” (Luke 1:68), and Paul wrote, “In Him we have redemption through His blood, the forgiveness of sins, in accordance with the riches of His grace” (Ephesians 1:7-8).

• Salvation is available today, fresh and abundant, “kept in heaven for you” (1 Peter 1:4).

• This assures us that no political upheaval, personal failure, or spiritual enemy can exhaust the reserves God has prepared.

Wisdom

• Salvation secures our eternity; wisdom guides our everyday decisions. Proverbs reminds us, “For the LORD gives wisdom” (Proverbs 2:6).

• In Christ “are hidden all the treasures of wisdom” (Colossians 2:3).

• When we face dilemmas, James invites us: “If any of you lacks wisdom, he should ask God, who gives generously” (James 1:5).

• Because God Himself is our wisdom, we need not lean on unstable human reasoning (Proverbs 3:5-6).

And knowledge

• Knowledge is truthful understanding rooted in God’s self-revelation. Eternal life is defined as “knowing You, the only true God, and Jesus Christ” (John 17:3).

• “Grace and peace be multiplied to you through the knowledge of God and of Jesus our Lord” (2 Peter 1:2-3), showing that knowledge deepens fellowship and fortifies peace.

• Without it, “My people are destroyed for lack of knowledge” (Hosea 4:6).

• The Lord offers reliable, unchanging truth so we can stand firm amid cultural confusion (Jeremiah 9:23-24).

The fear of the LORD is Zion’s treasure

• Reverent awe of God is the key that unlocks these storerooms. “The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom” (Psalm 111:10).

• For believers, fear is not terror but humble submission that delights in His majesty (Acts 9:31).

• God esteems those who fear Him as “My treasured possession” (Malachi 3:16-17), and He becomes their treasure in return.

• Zion—God’s people—possess wealth the world cannot counterfeit: awe-filled intimacy with the Holy One.

summary

Isaiah 33:6 stacks promise upon promise: God Himself stabilizes every season, supplies unending rescue, imparts practical wisdom, and grants reliable knowledge. All of this is accessed through reverent fear of the Lord, the greatest treasure His people can hold.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(6) Wisdom and knowledge . . .--The words are used in the higher sense, as in Proverbs 1:1-4, in contrast with the craft and devices of men, just as the "fear of the Lord" is the true treasure, in contrast with the silver and gold in which Hezekiah had been led to place his trust.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 6. - Wisdom and knowledge shall be the stability of thy times; literally, and the stability of thy times shall be (i.e. consist in) a rich store of salvations, wisdom, and knowledge. The prophet here addresses the people of Judah in the second person, though in the next clause he reverts to the third. Such transitions are common in ancient compositions, and especially characterize the writings of Isaiah. The fear of the Lord is his treasure; i.e. the wisdom intended is that which is based upon "the fear of the Lord" (Psalm 111:10). This will be at once Judah's "treasure," and a guarantee of stability to her government and institutions (compare the Homiletics on Isaiah 32:15-17).
