Surely My own hand founded the earth
This phrase emphasizes God's role as the Creator, affirming His sovereignty and power over all creation. The imagery of God's "hand" signifies His personal involvement and authority in the act of creation. This aligns with Genesis 1:1, where God is depicted as the Creator of the heavens and the earth. The use of "hand" is anthropomorphic, helping humans understand divine actions in relatable terms. The creation account in Genesis establishes the foundation for understanding God's relationship with the world and His ultimate authority over it.

and My right hand spread out the heavens
The "right hand" is often symbolic of strength and skill in biblical literature. In ancient Near Eastern culture, the right hand was associated with power and honor. This phrase highlights God's omnipotence and the meticulous care with which He crafted the universe. The spreading out of the heavens can be seen as a reference to the expanse of the sky and the celestial bodies, echoing passages like Psalm 19:1, which speaks of the heavens declaring the glory of God. This also prefigures the New Testament revelation of Christ, through whom all things were made (John 1:3).

when I summon them, they stand up together
This phrase illustrates God's command over creation, where His word alone is sufficient to bring forth order and obedience. The imagery of summoning and standing suggests a military formation, indicating that all elements of creation are subject to God's authority and respond to His command. This reflects the biblical theme of God's word as powerful and effective, as seen in Isaiah 55:11, where God's word accomplishes what He desires. It also foreshadows the authority of Christ, who calms the storm with a word (Mark 4:39), demonstrating His divine power over nature.

What Does Isaiah 48:13 Mean
Surely My own hand founded the earth

• The Lord speaks in the first person, stressing that creation is not delegated but carried out by His very “own hand.”

• Genesis 1:1 records the moment that hand went to work: “In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth”.

• Psalm 102:25 echoes the same certainty: “Long ago You founded the earth, and the heavens are the work of Your hands”.

• Colossians 1:16 ties the physical act of creation to Christ Himself, affirming that “in Him all things were created… all things were created through Him and for Him”.

• Literal foundation language reminds us that the earth is not the product of chance but of deliberate, intelligent design. Because the One who laid that foundation also made covenant promises, those promises are just as firm.

and My right hand spread out the heavens

• “Right hand” in Scripture highlights power, authority, and skill. Psalm 98:1 celebrates “His right hand and His holy arm have worked salvation”.

• Isaiah 40:22 paints the same picture of effortless authority: “He stretches out the heavens like a curtain”. God didn’t merely stack cosmic building blocks; He unfolded the universe with precise intention.

• Jeremiah 10:12 expands on the idea: “He founded the earth by His power… He stretched out the heavens by His wisdom”. Creation bears the stamp of divine craftsmanship—vast yet ordered, majestic yet purposeful.

• The heavens thus become a perpetual witness to God’s glory (Psalm 19:1), inviting worship and assuring us that nothing in the cosmos outranks its Maker.

when I summon them, they stand up together

• Creation not only came from God’s word; it still answers to that word. Psalm 33:9 states, “For He spoke, and it came to be; He commanded, and it stood firm”.

• Isaiah 40:26 draws the same line: God “brings out the host of stars by number… because of His great power and mighty strength, not one of them is missing”.

• The phrase “stand up together” pictures the universe rising at attention when its Commander calls. From the movements of galaxies to the rhythms of earth’s seasons, everything responds in unison.

• Jesus demonstrates this same authority over creation when He stills the storm with a word (Mark 4:39). That moment on the Sea of Galilee confirms that the God who once summoned light now commands wind and waves.

• By showing that all creation remains under His voice, the Lord assures His people that His plans for them will likewise stand.

summary

Isaiah 48:13 anchors faith in the unshakeable reality that the God addressing Israel is the very One who laid the earth’s foundations, unfurled the heavens, and still commands every element of creation. His hand is personal, His right hand is powerful, and His summons is obeyed instantly. Because the physical universe responds to Him without hesitation, His covenant promises are equally dependable. Trusting this Creator means resting in rock-solid assurance: the God who formed all things will faithfully accomplish all He has spoken.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 13. - Mine hand also hath laid the foundation of the earth (comp. Isaiah 40:12, 22, 26, 28; Isaiah 42:5; Isaiah 44:24; Isaiah 45:12, 18). As the Maker of heaven and earth, God is entitled to the attention and obedience of all the dwellers in heaven and earth. My right hand hath spanned the heavens; i.e. measured them, as with a span (Isaiah 41:12)-fixed their limits and dimensions. When I call unto them, they stand up together (comp. Isaiah 40:26). Heaven and earth, and all things that are in them, except man, are prompt to perform God's will, and rise up at once at his call to show their readiness. The metaphor is drawn from the conduct of intelligent agents.
