Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
These are rebellious people
The term "rebellious" in this context refers to the Israelites' persistent disobedience and resistance to God's commands. Historically, the Israelites often turned away from God, seeking alliances with foreign nations like Egypt, which God had explicitly warned against. This rebellion is a recurring theme throughout the Old Testament, highlighting the Israelites' struggle to remain faithful to their covenant with God. The rebellion is not just a physical turning away but a spiritual defiance against God's authority and guidance.

Deceitful children
"Deceitful" indicates a lack of integrity and honesty in their relationship with God. The Israelites are described as children, emphasizing their immaturity and dependency, yet they choose deceit over truth. This deceit is evident in their false worship and insincere repentance. The term "children" also reflects their status as God's chosen people, who are expected to live in truth and righteousness. This deceit is contrasted with the call to be like children in faith, as seen in the New Testament, where Jesus emphasizes the importance of childlike faith and honesty.

Children unwilling to obey the LORD’s instruction
The phrase underscores the Israelites' refusal to listen to and follow God's teachings. The "LORD's instruction" refers to the Torah, the law given to Moses, which was meant to guide the Israelites in living a life pleasing to God. Their unwillingness to obey is a direct rejection of God's authority and wisdom. This disobedience is a central issue in the prophetic books, where prophets like Isaiah call the people back to faithfulness. The New Testament echoes this call to obedience, with Jesus fulfilling the law and urging His followers to live according to God's will. The Israelites' disobedience serves as a warning and lesson for future generations about the consequences of turning away from God's path.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Isaiah
A major prophet in the Old Testament, Isaiah is the author of the book bearing his name. He served as a prophet in Judah during the reigns of several kings and is known for his messages of both judgment and hope.

2. Judah
The southern kingdom of Israel, where Isaiah primarily ministered. During this time, Judah was often tempted to rely on political alliances rather than trusting in God.

3. Rebellious People
Refers to the people of Judah who were turning away from God's commandments and seeking their own paths, often influenced by surrounding nations.

4. Deceitful Children
This term highlights the people's tendency to lie and deceive, both themselves and others, about their spiritual state and intentions.

5. The LORD’s Instruction
Represents God's law and commandments given to guide His people. The Hebrew word for "instruction" is (Torah), which encompasses teaching, direction, and law.
Teaching Points
Understanding Rebellion
Recognize that rebellion against God often begins with small acts of disobedience and grows into a lifestyle of deceit and self-reliance.

The Danger of Deceit
Deceitfulness can blind us to our true spiritual condition. We must seek truth and transparency in our relationship with God.

The Importance of Obedience
Obedience to God's instruction is crucial for a healthy spiritual life. Reflect on areas where you may be resisting God's guidance.

Trust in God Over Alliances
Like Judah, we may be tempted to rely on worldly solutions rather than trusting in God's provision and protection.

Repentance and Restoration
God continually calls His people to repentance. Embrace His grace and seek restoration through genuine repentance.

What Does Isaiah 30:9 Mean
These are rebellious people

- Isaiah is not speaking about a vague moral lapse; he is exposing an intentional turning away from God’s clearly revealed will.

- The word “rebellious” reminds us of Israel’s long pattern, seen as early as Exodus 32:9—“I have seen this people, and behold, they are a stiff-necked people.”

- Rebellion here is active, not passive. They do not drift from God; they push back against Him, much like the generation described in Numbers 14:9-11 that refused to enter the land.

- The passage highlights that rebellion is first a heart issue. Proverbs 28:14 warns, “Blessed is the man who always fears the LORD, but he who hardens his heart falls into trouble.” Israel has chosen the opposite path, hardening itself against divine authority.

Deceitful children

- “Deceitful” (or “lying”) stresses dishonesty toward both God and neighbor. Like Hosea 11:12 says, “Ephraim surrounds Me with lies, and the house of Israel with deceit.”

- Their deceit shows up in religious hypocrisy—offering sacrifices while planning alliances with Egypt (Isaiah 30:1-2). This mirrors Jesus’ indictment in Matthew 15:8, “This people honors Me with their lips, but their hearts are far from Me.”

- God’s people are called to truthfulness because God Himself “does not lie” (Titus 1:2). When His children live in deceit, they deny His character.

- Practical takeaway: lies always accompany rebellion. If we detect habitual dishonesty, it signals a deeper refusal to submit to God.

Children unwilling to obey the LORD’s instruction

- The phrase “unwilling to obey” shows an obstinate refusal to hear. Compare Jeremiah 6:17, where watchmen cry, “Listen to the sound of the trumpet!” yet the people reply, “We will not listen.”

- “Instruction” (torah) is God’s gracious guidance. To reject it is self-destructive, as Proverbs 13:13 states: “He who despises instruction will pay the penalty.”

- Their deafness is willful. 2 Timothy 4:3 predicts a similar future posture: people “will accumulate for themselves teachers to suit their own desires.” Israel is already doing this, demanding “pleasant words” rather than truth (Isaiah 30:10-11).

- The verse underscores covenant responsibility. Deuteronomy 6:4-7 called parents to teach God’s words diligently to their children. Refusal to listen breaks that chain and endangers the next generation.

summary

Isaiah 30:9 paints a sober portrait: God’s covenant people are actively rebellious, habitually deceitful, and stubbornly unwilling to heed His life-giving instruction. The verse warns that rebellion breeds deception and deafness to truth, severing the blessings tied to obedience. For every believer today, the call is clear—submit to God’s authority, walk in truth, and keep ears and heart open to His Word, lest we repeat Israel’s tragic pattern.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(9) That this is a rebellious people.--The words that follow were those which were thus written on the tablet. The people did not know the law of the Lord, the eternal law of right, themselves. They wished the seers, like Isaiah, to be as blind as themselves, and would fain have made the prophets tune their voice according to the time.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 9. - That this is a rebellious people; rather, for this is a rebellious people. The words to be written were those of the preceding prophecy. The reason for their being written is now given (comp. Deuteronomy 31:26, 27). Lying children (comp. Isaiah 59:13). They professed devotion to God; but their acts contradicted their words.
