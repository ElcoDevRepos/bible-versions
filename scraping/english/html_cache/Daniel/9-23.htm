Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
At the beginning of your petitions
Daniel's prayer in chapter 9 is a profound intercession for his people, Israel, during their Babylonian exile. The phrase indicates that God responds to sincere prayer promptly. This reflects the biblical principle that God hears and answers the prayers of the righteous (Psalm 34:15). Daniel's prayer was characterized by confession, supplication, and a deep understanding of God's promises.

an answer went out
This suggests the immediacy of God's response to Daniel's prayer. It highlights the divine initiative in communication with humanity. The answer is delivered by the angel Gabriel, emphasizing the importance of angelic messengers in God's plan (Luke 1:19). This also underscores the theme of divine sovereignty and the fulfillment of God's purposes.

and I have come to tell you
Gabriel's role as a messenger is crucial here. His appearance to Daniel signifies the importance of the message. Gabriel is also seen in the New Testament, announcing the birth of John the Baptist and Jesus (Luke 1:11-38), linking the Old and New Testament revelations.

for you are highly precious
Daniel is described as "highly precious" or "greatly beloved," indicating his esteemed status before God. This reflects the biblical theme of God's favor towards those who are faithful and righteous (Proverbs 3:3-4). Daniel's life of integrity and devotion sets a standard for believers.

So consider the message
Gabriel urges Daniel to pay careful attention to the message, highlighting the importance of understanding divine revelation. This is a call to discernment and wisdom, which are recurring themes in the wisdom literature of the Bible (Proverbs 2:1-5).

and understand the vision
Understanding the vision is crucial for Daniel, as it pertains to the future of Israel and God's redemptive plan. This vision includes the prophecy of the seventy weeks, which is significant in eschatological studies. It points to the coming of the Messiah and the ultimate fulfillment of God's promises (Daniel 9:24-27). The call to understand echoes the biblical emphasis on seeking wisdom and insight (James 1:5).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Daniel
A prophet and a man of prayer, known for his faithfulness and wisdom. He is the recipient of the vision and message.

2. Gabriel
An angel sent by God to deliver messages to Daniel. He is known for bringing clarity and understanding of divine visions.

3. Jerusalem
The city central to Daniel's prayers and the focus of the prophetic visions concerning its future.

4. The Vision
A divine revelation given to Daniel concerning the future of Israel and the coming of the Messiah.

5. The Petition
Daniel's prayer and supplication to God, seeking understanding and intervention for his people.
Teaching Points
The Power of Prayer
Daniel's experience underscores the importance of earnest and persistent prayer. God hears and responds to the prayers of His faithful servants.

Being Esteemed by God
Daniel is described as "highly esteemed," which is a result of his faithfulness and dedication to God. Believers are encouraged to live lives that are pleasing to God.

Understanding God's Messages
Just as Daniel was instructed to "consider the message and understand the vision," believers should seek to understand God's Word and His will for their lives.

Divine Timing
The phrase "at the beginning of your petitions" indicates that God’s response is timely and often begins even as we start to pray. Trust in God’s perfect timing.

Role of Angels
Gabriel’s role as a messenger highlights the reality of spiritual beings who serve God and assist in His plans. Believers can be assured of God’s provision and protection through His angels.

What Does Daniel 9:23 Mean
At the beginning of your petitions

“While I was speaking and praying…” (Daniel 9:20) Gabriel explains that the response started the moment Daniel began praying.

• God hears instantly (Isaiah 65:24; Psalm 34:15).

• Prayer is never wasted time—heaven moves while we speak (Acts 10:4).

• The swiftness underscores God’s readiness to act for His faithful ones (Psalm 145:18).

an answer went out

The issue was settled in God’s throne room before Daniel finished his sentence.

• God’s word travels with authority and speed (Psalm 147:15; Isaiah 55:11).

• This answer includes both comfort and a precise prophetic timetable (Daniel 9:24-27).

• The pattern: prayer—heaven’s decree—earthly revelation (2 Chronicles 7:14).

and I have come to tell you

Gabriel arrives as the courier of that decree.

• Angels are “ministering spirits sent to serve those who will inherit salvation” (Hebrews 1:14).

• Gabriel’s very presence confirms Daniel’s prayer was not a private monologue (Luke 1:19).

• God bridges the gap between heaven and earth through messengers so we grasp His will (Genesis 28:12; Acts 27:23-24).

for you are highly precious

Literally, “greatly beloved.”

• God delights in those who walk faithfully (Proverbs 3:3-4).

• Daniel’s devotion set him apart (Daniel 1:8; 6:10) and drew divine affection (Daniel 10:11, 19).

• Every believer shares this standing in Christ (Ephesians 1:6; John 17:23).

So consider the message and understand the vision

Receiving truth carries the duty to ponder and apply it.

• “Write down the vision and make it plain” (Habakkuk 2:2).

• Blessing follows careful attention (Revelation 1:3; Luke 8:18).

• Daniel records and reflects, giving later generations the prophetic roadmap that leads directly to Messiah’s atoning work (Daniel 9:26-27; Matthew 24:15).

summary

Daniel 9:23 reveals a God who listens the instant His people pray, sends forth answers without delay, dispatches angelic messengers to make His purposes clear, cherishes those who are loyal to Him, and calls us to think deeply about His revealed word so we can live in its light.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(23) The commandment.--The marginal version is to be preferred, which points to the revelation which follows Daniel 9:24-27. The title "greatly beloved" occurs again (Daniel 10:11; Daniel 10:19). It implies that Daniel was worthy of this proof of God's love. St. Jerome compares (2Samuel 12:25) Jedidiah.
