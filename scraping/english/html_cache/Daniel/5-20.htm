But when his heart became arrogant
This phrase refers to King Nebuchadnezzar, whose story is recounted in the Book of Daniel. Arrogance in the biblical context often signifies a turning away from God, as pride leads individuals to rely on their own strength rather than acknowledging God's sovereignty. Proverbs 16:18 warns, "Pride goes before destruction, a haughty spirit before a fall," which is exemplified in Nebuchadnezzar's life. His arrogance is a cautionary tale about the dangers of self-exaltation.

and his spirit was hardened with pride
A hardened spirit indicates a resistance to divine influence and correction. In the biblical narrative, a hardened heart often leads to judgment, as seen with Pharaoh in Exodus. Nebuchadnezzar's prideful spirit prevented him from recognizing God's authority, leading to his downfall. This hardening is a spiritual condition that blinds individuals to truth and righteousness, emphasizing the need for humility before God.

he was deposed from his royal throne
The deposition of Nebuchadnezzar from his throne is a direct consequence of his pride and serves as a fulfillment of divine prophecy. Daniel 4:31-32 records God's warning to Nebuchadnezzar that his kingdom would be taken from him until he acknowledged God's sovereignty. This event underscores the biblical principle that God is the ultimate authority over earthly rulers, as seen in Romans 13:1, which states that all authority is established by God.

and his glory was taken from him
The removal of Nebuchadnezzar's glory signifies the loss of his power, honor, and majesty. In biblical terms, glory often represents the visible manifestation of God's presence and favor. When Nebuchadnezzar's glory was taken, it symbolized God's judgment and the stripping away of what was not rightfully his. This serves as a reminder that human glory is temporary and ultimately subject to God's will, as echoed in Isaiah 42:8, where God declares He will not share His glory with another.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Belshazzar
The king of Babylon during the events of Daniel 5. He is the one whose pride and arrogance led to his downfall, as described in this chapter.

2. Nebuchadnezzar
The former king of Babylon, referenced in Daniel 5 as an example of someone who was humbled by God due to his pride. His account serves as a backdrop to Belshazzar's actions.

3. Babylon
The setting of the events in Daniel 5. It was a powerful empire known for its grandeur and influence during this period.

4. The Writing on the Wall
A miraculous event where a hand appeared and wrote on the wall during Belshazzar's feast, signaling God's judgment on his pride and arrogance.

5. Daniel
A prophet and servant of God who interprets the writing on the wall for Belshazzar, warning him of the consequences of his pride.
Teaching Points
The Danger of Pride
Pride can lead to a hardened heart and spiritual blindness, preventing us from recognizing our need for God.

God's Sovereignty
God is sovereign over all kingdoms and rulers. He can raise up and depose leaders according to His will.

The Importance of Humility
Humility is essential in our relationship with God. Recognizing our dependence on Him keeps us aligned with His purposes.

Learning from History
The accounts of past leaders like Nebuchadnezzar and Belshazzar serve as warnings for us to avoid the pitfalls of pride.

Responding to God's Warnings
When God warns us, whether through scripture, circumstances, or others, we should respond with repentance and humility.

What Does Daniel 5:20 Mean
But when his heart became arrogant

– Scripture points to an internal shift before any outward fall. Nebuchadnezzar’s “heart” speaks of his whole inner life—thoughts, motives, desires—turning inward instead of upward.

 • Daniel 4:30-31 shows the very moment pride spilled from his lips and heaven answered.

 • Proverbs 16:18 warns, “Pride goes before destruction,” underlining the built-in consequence God has placed in His world.

 • Mark 7:21 lists pride among the heart-sins that defile; the issue always starts inside.

and his spirit was hardened with pride

When attitude calcifies, repentance feels unnecessary and God’s voice grows faint.

 • Exodus 8:15 notes Pharaoh “hardened his heart”; the pattern is the same—repeated refusal firms rebellion.

 • Hebrews 3:13 cautions that sin’s deceit “hardens” us if we keep yielding to it.

 • Obadiah 1:3 shows an entire nation lulled into false security by its own conceit.

he was deposed from his royal throne

God acts in real history; the throne was not merely threatened but actually removed for seven years (Daniel 4:31-33).

 • Psalm 75:6-7: “For exaltation comes neither from the east nor the west… but God is the Judge; He brings one down and exalts another.”

 • Luke 1:52 celebrates the same principle: the Mighty One “has brought down rulers from their thrones.”

 • 1 Samuel 2:7-8 highlights that the Lord “raises the poor from the dust” and “brings low” the proud.

and his glory was taken from him

Honor flees when God withdraws it; man cannot cling to borrowed splendor.

 • Isaiah 42:8 insists God will not share His glory with another.

 • Jeremiah 13:18 commands earthly kings, “Humble yourselves… for your crowns have fallen.”

 • 2 Chronicles 26:16-21 records Uzziah losing both health and honor when pride eclipsed gratitude.

summary

Daniel 5:20 traces a straight line: inward pride → hardened spirit → loss of position → loss of glory. God sovereignly safeguards His honor, patiently warns, and then acts decisively when humans will not bow. Nebuchadnezzar’s story, retold to Belshazzar, assures every generation that the Most High still rules kingdoms and hearts alike, exalting the humble and bringing down the proud.
