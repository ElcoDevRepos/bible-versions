Hananiah
The name Hananiah means "Yahweh is gracious." This name is common in the Old Testament, appearing in various genealogies and narratives. Hananiah is a reminder of God's grace and favor, a theme prevalent throughout the Bible. In the context of 1 Chronicles 8, Hananiah is listed among the descendants of Benjamin, highlighting the tribe's significance in Israel's history. The name also appears in the book of Daniel, where Hananiah, also known as Shadrach, is one of the faithful companions of Daniel who remains steadfast in his faith despite persecution. This connection underscores the theme of faithfulness to God.

Elam
Elam is both a personal name and a geographical location in the Bible. As a name, Elam means "eternity" or "hidden." In the genealogical context of 1 Chronicles 8, Elam is a descendant of Benjamin, indicating the continuity and preservation of the tribe. Geographically, Elam was an ancient civilization located east of Mesopotamia, often mentioned in the context of prophecy and judgment (e.g., Isaiah 11:11, Jeremiah 49:34-39). The dual use of the name may symbolize the far-reaching influence and connections of the Israelite tribes.

Anthothijah
Anthothijah is a name that appears only in this genealogical list, and its meaning is not entirely clear, though it may be related to the town of Anathoth. Anathoth was a Levitical city in the territory of Benjamin, known as the hometown of the prophet Jeremiah (Jeremiah 1:1). The mention of Anthothijah may suggest a connection to the priestly or prophetic tradition within the tribe of Benjamin. This connection highlights the spiritual heritage and responsibilities of the tribe, as well as the importance of maintaining a lineage that honors God's covenant.

Teaching Points
The Importance of Genealogy in Scripture
Genealogies in the Bible serve to connect individuals to the larger account of God's people, emphasizing the continuity of God's promises through generations.

Understanding Our Spiritual Heritage
Just as the Israelites valued their genealogical records, Christians are encouraged to understand and appreciate their spiritual heritage, tracing their faith back to the early church and the teachings of Christ.

The Role of Each Individual in God's Plan
Every name in the genealogical records, including those in 1 Chronicles 8:24, represents a life that contributed to the unfolding of God's plan. This reminds us that each person has a role to play in God's kingdom.

Faithfulness Across Generations
The listing of names serves as a reminder of the faithfulness required to pass down the knowledge and worship of God from one generation to the next.

What Does 1 Chronicles 8:24 Mean
Hananiah

“Hananiah” (1 Chronicles 8:24)

• His name appears frequently across Scripture, reminding us that individual lives, however briefly mentioned, matter to God. See 1 Chronicles 9:12; Daniel 1:6–7; Jeremiah 28:1–17.

• This particular Hananiah is one of Benjamin’s descendants, showing God’s preservation of that tribe after the turmoil of Judges 20 and the exile foretold in Jeremiah 29:4–7.

• Genealogies like this fulfill God’s promise to Abraham that his descendants would be countless (Genesis 22:17) and confirm that the post-exilic community returned with their heritage intact (Ezra 2:1).

• Each name links to the coming Messiah, who also sprang from Israel’s recorded lineages (Matthew 1:1–17), assuring us that God’s redemptive plan is rooted in real people and real history.

Elam

“Elam” (1 Chronicles 8:24)

• Elam is both a personal and a geographical name elsewhere (Genesis 10:22; Isaiah 11:11). Here it designates another Benjaminite, spotlighting the tribe’s breadth.

• The listing shows that God’s covenant reaches beyond one family line; it touches every clan and region represented by these shared names. Compare Ezra 2:7 and Nehemiah 10:14, where Elamite families return from exile to restore worship in Jerusalem.

• Including an “Elam” among Benjaminites hints at Israel’s unity amid diversity—a theme Paul later echoes when he calls the church “one body” with many members (1 Corinthians 12:12).

• By preserving Elam’s name, Scripture underscores that humble, otherwise unknown believers help weave the fabric of redemptive history, just as those who rebuilt the wall in Nehemiah 3 did their part.

Anthothijah

“Anthothijah” (1 Chronicles 8:24)

• His name likely ties to Anathoth, a priestly town in Benjamin (Joshua 21:18). Other notable residents include Jeremiah (Jeremiah 1:1) and Abiathar (1 Kings 2:26).

• Mentioning Anthothijah alongside fellow Benjaminites reveals that priestly and royal threads interlace within the tribe, foreshadowing the ultimate union of priest and king in Jesus (Hebrews 7:1–3).

• The brief record points to God’s concern for local communities; even small villages like Anathoth are preserved in Scripture, just as Jesus later ministered in Galilean hamlets (Matthew 9:35).

• Seeing Anthothijah noted encourages believers today that God values faithfulness wherever He plants us, whether in a major city like Jerusalem or a modest locale like Anathoth.

summary

1 Chronicles 8:24 strings together three seemingly ordinary names—Hananiah, Elam, Anthothijah—yet each testifies that God records, remembers, and weaves every life into His unfolding purposes. By preserving these Benjaminites, Scripture reassures us that no servant is overlooked, every promise to Israel stands firm, and the lineage leading to Christ is anchored in genuine history.
