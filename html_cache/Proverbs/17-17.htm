Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
A friend loves at all times
This phrase emphasizes the constancy and reliability of true friendship. In the ancient Near Eastern culture, loyalty and steadfastness were highly valued traits in relationships. The Hebrew word for "friend" here can also imply a close companion or confidant, suggesting a deep bond beyond mere acquaintance. This idea of unwavering love is echoed in other scriptures, such as John 15:13, where Jesus speaks of the greatest love being one that lays down its life for friends. The constancy of a true friend reflects the enduring love of God, as seen in Lamentations 3:22-23, where His mercies are described as new every morning.

and a brother is born for adversity
This part of the verse highlights the role of family, particularly siblings, in times of trouble. In biblical times, family ties were crucial for survival and support, especially during hardships. The term "brother" here signifies not just a biological sibling but also someone who acts with the loyalty and support expected of family. This concept is seen in the story of Joseph and his brothers in Genesis, where despite initial betrayal, familial bonds ultimately lead to reconciliation and support during famine. The phrase can also be seen as a type of Christ, who is described as our brother in Hebrews 2:11-12, born to share in our sufferings and provide help in times of need.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Solomon
Traditionally attributed as the author of Proverbs, Solomon was the son of King David and known for his wisdom. His proverbs offer practical insights into living a life that honors God.

2. Friends and Brothers
The verse contrasts two types of relationships—friends and brothers—highlighting the constancy and purpose of each in times of need.

3. Adversity
This term refers to times of trouble or hardship, which are inevitable in life. The verse suggests that relationships are tested and proven during such times.
Teaching Points
The Constancy of True Friendship
True friendship is marked by unwavering love, regardless of circumstances. This constancy reflects God's love for us and serves as a model for our relationships.

Purpose in Adversity
Adversity reveals the depth and strength of relationships. Brothers, whether by blood or in Christ, are divinely placed in our lives to support and uplift us during difficult times.

Reflecting Christ's Love
As Christians, we are called to love others as Christ loves us. This means being present and supportive, especially when others face trials.

Building Strong Relationships
Invest in relationships that are rooted in love and faithfulness. Such relationships not only provide support but also glorify God.

Community and Accountability
Engage in a community of believers who can act as friends and brothers, offering mutual support and accountability in your spiritual journey.

What Does Proverbs 17:17 Mean
A friend loves at all times

“ A friend loves at all times ” (Proverbs 17:17a) sets the tone of unwavering commitment. Genuine friendship is not seasonal or conditional.

• Love that endures: Jonathan’s loyalty to David never wavered, even when it cost him (1 Samuel 18:3-4).

• At all times means on bright days and dark nights; Jesus points to the same standard: “Greater love has no one than this, that he lay down his life for his friends” (John 15:13).

• Such love sticks “closer than a brother” (Proverbs 18:24) and “bears all things” (1 Corinthians 13:7).

• It mirrors God’s covenant faithfulness, inviting us to reflect His steadfast character in every season.

and a brother is born for adversity

“ …and a brother is born for adversity ” (Proverbs 17:17b) sharpens the focus. Family—by blood or in Christ—is divinely positioned for crisis moments.

• Purpose in hardship: Job’s friends initially came “to sympathize with him and comfort him” (Job 2:11); brothers step in when life turns rough.

• Shared burdens fulfill God’s law: “Carry one another’s burdens, and in this way you will fulfill the law of Christ” (Galatians 6:2).

• Adversity reveals who will stand: two are “better than one… for if either falls, the other can lift him up” (Ecclesiastes 4:9-10).

• Ruth became family to Naomi, vowing, “Where you go I will go… your people will be my people” (Ruth 1:16-17), illustrating the brother-in-adversity principle.

• The family bond is forged for battle; “Let brotherly love continue” (Hebrews 13:1) because hardship is certain, and God provides siblings to meet it.

summary

Proverbs 17:17 celebrates two complementary gifts: friends who love without pause and brothers uniquely equipped for life’s storms. Loyal friends display constant, sacrificial love; brothers, whether by birth or by faith, step forward when trouble strikes. Together they paint a living portrait of God’s steadfast, ever-present care for His people.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(17) A friend loveth at all times . . .--Rather, The (true) friend loveth at all times, and (as) a brother is born for adversity.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 17. - A friend loveth at all times, and a brother is born for adversity. Some find a climax in the two clauses, and translate the last as Revised Version margin, "And is born as a brother for adversity," the same person being meant in both members of the sentence. A real friend loves his friend in prosperity and adversity; yea, he is more than a friend in time of need - he is a brother, as affectionate and as trusty as one connected by the closest ties of relationship (comp. Proverbs 18:24). Siracides gives a very cruel version of this proverb, "A friend cannot be known in prosperity; and an enemy cannot be hidden in adversity. In the prosperity of a man enemies will be grieved; but in his adversity even a friend will depart" (Ecclus. 12:8, etc.). Cicero had a truer notion of the stability of friendship when he quoted Ennius's dictum, "Amicus certus in re incerta cernitur" ('De Amicit.,' 17.). Misfortune, says our maxim, is the touchstone of friendship; and one Greek gnome enjoins -

Ἰδίας νόμιζε τῶν φίλων τὰς συμφοράς

"Thy friend's misfortunes deem to be thine own;"
