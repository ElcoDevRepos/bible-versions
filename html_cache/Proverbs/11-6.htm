Proverbs 11
1. On Deception














Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
The righteousness of the upright delivers them
Righteousness in the biblical context often refers to living in accordance with God's laws and commands. The "upright" are those who strive to live morally and ethically, aligning their lives with divine principles. This phrase suggests that such righteousness acts as a form of deliverance or salvation. In the Old Testament, deliverance often refers to being saved from physical danger or moral corruption. This concept is echoed in other scriptures, such as Psalm 34:19, which states that the righteous person may have many troubles, but the Lord delivers him from them all. The idea of righteousness leading to deliverance is also seen in the life of Noah, whose uprightness led to his and his family's salvation from the flood (Genesis 6:9).

but the faithless are trapped by their own desires
The "faithless" refers to those who lack fidelity to God and His commandments. In biblical terms, faithlessness is often equated with idolatry or turning away from God. The imagery of being "trapped" suggests a snare or a pitfall, indicating that their own desires lead to their downfall. This is reminiscent of James 1:14-15, which describes how desire, when it has conceived, gives birth to sin, and sin, when it is full-grown, gives birth to death. The faithless are ensnared by their own lusts and ambitions, which ultimately lead to spiritual and sometimes physical ruin. This principle is illustrated in the story of King Saul, whose disobedience and desire for power led to his downfall (1 Samuel 15).

What Does Proverbs 11:6 Mean
The righteousness

“The righteousness…” points to the God-given integrity that comes from trusting and obeying Him.

• Proverbs 10:2 notes, “Righteousness delivers from death,” echoing the same promise.

• Genesis 15:6 shows that God counts faith as righteousness, making clear that true righteousness is a gift, not self-manufactured.

• Romans 3:22 confirms that this righteousness is “through faith in Jesus Christ to all who believe,” grounding the concept in both Testaments.

Of the upright

“…of the upright…” identifies those whose hearts, words, and actions line up with God’s standards.

• Psalm 15:2 describes the upright as one “who walks with integrity and practices righteousness.”

• Proverbs 2:7 says God “is a shield to those who walk in integrity,” assuring protection for the upright.

• Philippians 2:15 urges believers to be “blameless and pure, children of God without fault,” underlining that uprightness is practical, everyday obedience.

Delivers them

“…delivers them” promises rescue—both now and eternally.

• Psalm 34:17 reminds us that “The righteous cry out, and the LORD hears; He delivers them from all their troubles.”

• 2 Timothy 4:18 affirms, “The Lord will rescue me from every evil deed and bring me safely into His heavenly kingdom,” linking present help with eternal security.

• Proverbs 21:21 assures, “He who pursues righteousness…finds life,” showing deliverance is life-giving in every sense.

But the faithless

“…but the faithless…” introduces a stark contrast. The faithless reject God’s ways, choosing self-reliance.

• Proverbs 13:15 notes that “the way of the faithless is hard,” illustrating inevitable hardship.

• Matthew 24:51 pictures the fate of the unfaithful servant, stressing accountability.

• Hebrews 3:19 warns that unbelief shuts people out of God’s rest.

Are trapped by their own desires

“…are trapped by their own desires” shows sin’s boomerang effect.

• James 1:14-15 explains that “each one is tempted when he is lured and enticed by his own desire,” which “gives birth to sin,” leading to death.

• Psalm 7:15 portrays the wicked falling into the pit they dug.

• Romans 1:24 states God “gave them over in the desires of their hearts,” depicting desire itself as the snare.

summary

Righteousness—received by faith and lived out in upright conduct—becomes a lifeline, rescuing believers from danger and judgment. In contrast, faithlessness spirals inward; unchecked desires tighten into a self-made trap. Proverbs 11:6 assures that choosing God’s path is not merely moral preference but the difference between deliverance and entanglement.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(6) In their own naughtiness.--Rather, passionate desire, as at Proverbs 10:3. Their own strong passions are their ruin.

His expectation.--What he hoped for, worldly prosperity. (Comp. Wisdom Of Solomon 5:14.)

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 6. - An emphatic reiteration of the preceding sentences. Naughtiness; "strong desire," as Proverbs 10:3, which leads to sin (Proverbs 5:22; Micah 7:3). The indulgence of their passions destroys sinners. Septuagint," Transgressors are taken by lack of counsel."
