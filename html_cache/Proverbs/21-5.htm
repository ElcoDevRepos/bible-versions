The plans of the diligent bring plenty
This phrase emphasizes the value of careful planning and hard work. In biblical context, diligence is often associated with wisdom and righteousness. Proverbs frequently contrasts the diligent with the lazy, highlighting the rewards of perseverance and thoughtful preparation. The Hebrew culture valued agriculture and trade, where planning was essential for success. This principle is echoed in other scriptures, such as Proverbs 10:4, which states that "lazy hands make for poverty, but diligent hands bring wealth." The idea of diligence aligns with the biblical work ethic, where labor is seen as a form of stewardship of God's creation. In the New Testament, the Apostle Paul exemplifies diligence in his ministry, often working as a tentmaker to support himself (Acts 18:3).

as surely as haste leads to poverty
This part of the verse warns against impulsiveness and rash decisions. In the ancient Near Eastern context, haste could lead to poor business decisions, failed harvests, or unwise investments. The Bible often associates haste with folly, as seen in Proverbs 19:2, "Desire without knowledge is not good—how much more will hasty feet miss the way!" The cultural backdrop of this proverb includes a society where patience and long-term planning were necessary for survival and prosperity. Theologically, this can be seen as a call to trust in God's timing and provision, rather than relying on quick fixes or shortcuts. The contrast between diligence and haste serves as a moral lesson on the importance of wisdom and prudence in all aspects of life.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Solomon
Traditionally attributed as the author of Proverbs, Solomon was the son of King David and known for his wisdom. His insights into human behavior and godly living are captured in the book of Proverbs.

2. Israel
The cultural and historical context of Proverbs is ancient Israel, where wisdom literature was a key component of teaching and guiding the community in righteous living.

3. The Diligent
This refers to individuals who are careful, persistent, and hardworking in their endeavors, embodying a key virtue in biblical wisdom literature.

4. The Hasty
Those who act with undue speed or without careful thought, often leading to negative consequences, as highlighted in this proverb.

5. Poverty
A state of lacking material wealth, often used in Proverbs to illustrate the consequences of foolish or unwise actions.
Teaching Points
The Value of Diligence
Diligence is a virtue that leads to abundance and success. It requires consistent effort and careful planning.

The Danger of Haste
Acting hastily without proper planning can lead to poor outcomes and poverty. It is important to take time to consider and plan our actions.

Biblical Wisdom in Planning
The Bible encourages us to seek wisdom and guidance from God in our planning, ensuring our efforts align with His will.

Practical Steps for Diligence
Set clear goals, create actionable plans, and remain committed to following through with perseverance.

Trusting God with the Outcome
While diligence is important, we must also trust God with the results, knowing that He ultimately provides and sustains.

What Does Proverbs 21:5 Mean
The plans

• Scripture treats planning as a wise, God-honoring activity. Proverbs 16:3 urges, “Commit your works to the LORD and your plans will be established,” linking careful thought with divine blessing.

• Jesus endorsed forethought when He asked, “Which of you wishing to build a tower does not first sit down and count the cost…?” (Luke 14:28). Genuine faith never excuses impulsiveness; it reasons, researches, and prays before acting.

Of the diligent

• Diligence is steady, persistent effort, the opposite of laziness. Proverbs 12:24 notes, “The hand of the diligent will rule,” and 10:4 adds, “Idle hands make one poor, but diligent hands bring wealth.”

• New-covenant believers hear the same call: “Whatever you do, work at it with your whole being, for the Lord” (Colossians 3:23). God values industry because it reflects His own faithful character.

Bring plenty

• “Plenty” in Proverbs 21:5 is literal—adequate provision, surplus, the ability to share. When we plan and labor under God’s direction we often enjoy tangible reward: “Honor the LORD… then your barns will be filled with plenty” (Proverbs 3:9-10).

• Generosity grows out of that abundance: “A generous soul will prosper, and he who refreshes others will himself be refreshed” (Proverbs 11:24-25). Careful planning + hard work = resources to bless family, church, and needy neighbors.

As surely as

• The phrase signals a fixed principle, as dependable as gravity. Galatians 6:7 echoes it: “Whatever a man sows, he will reap.” Sow diligence, reap plenty; sow haste, reap want. God woven cause-and-effect into daily life.

Haste

• Haste means rash, shortcut-seeking action. Proverbs 19:2 warns, “He who hurries his footsteps misses the mark,” while 28:20 cautions that the one “eager to be rich will not go unpunished.”

• Common forms of haste today:

– Jumping into debt without counting cost

– Get-rich-quick schemes and speculative gambling

– Skipping training, mentorship, or prayer because “time is money”

Leads to poverty

• Proverbs paints poverty as an almost inevitable harvest for the hasty: “A stingy man hastens after wealth and does not know that poverty will overtake him” (28:22).

• Quick, dishonest gain evaporates: “Dishonest wealth will dwindle” (13:11). Even sudden windfalls disappoint when character is unformed: “An inheritance gained quickly will not be blessed in the end” (20:21).

• Pleasure-driven haste drains resources: “He who loves pleasure will become poor” (21:17).

summary

Proverbs 21:5 offers a clear, time-tested equation. Thoughtful planning joined to steady, diligent effort brings God’s normal provision—and often abundance. Conversely, impatience and shortcut chasing drain resources and invite lack. The verse invites believers to mirror God’s own orderly, purposeful work: think ahead, labor faithfully, wait patiently, and enjoy His promised plenty.

The man who labours God himself assists." But of every one that is hasty only to want. Diligence is contrasted with hastiness.
