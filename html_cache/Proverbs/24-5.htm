A wise man is strong
In biblical context, wisdom is often associated with strength, not just physical but moral and spiritual. The Hebrew word for "wise" (חָכָם, chakam) implies skill and expertise, suggesting that true strength comes from understanding and applying God's principles. This echoes the teachings in Ecclesiastes 7:19, where wisdom is said to make one stronger than ten rulers in a city. The strength here is not merely physical but encompasses the ability to navigate life's challenges with discernment and integrity. Historically, wisdom literature in the ancient Near East often linked wisdom with power and influence, reflecting a cultural understanding that true leadership and strength come from wise decision-making.

and a man of knowledge enhances his strength
Knowledge in the biblical sense goes beyond mere information; it involves a deep understanding of God's will and the world He created. The Hebrew term for "knowledge" (דַּעַת, da'ath) suggests an intimate acquaintance with truth. This phrase implies that knowledge builds upon the foundation of wisdom, further fortifying one's ability to act effectively and righteously. In Proverbs 1:7, the fear of the Lord is described as the beginning of knowledge, indicating that true knowledge is rooted in a reverent relationship with God. This connection between knowledge and strength is also seen in the life of Jesus Christ, who, as the embodiment of divine wisdom and knowledge, demonstrated unparalleled strength in His teachings and actions.

What Does Proverbs 24:5 Mean
Proverbs 24:5

“A wise man is strong, and a man of knowledge enhances his strength.”

A wise man is strong

• Wisdom supplies an inner power that outlasts physical might. Proverbs 21:22 shows “A wise man scales the city of the mighty,” demonstrating that insight can topple brute force.

• True strength begins with reverence for the Lord (Proverbs 1:7). Such fear of God arms a believer with moral courage, like David facing Goliath (1 Samuel 17:45-47).

• Ecclesiastes 7:19 affirms, “Wisdom makes the wise stronger than ten rulers in a city.” This strength stabilizes families, churches, and communities.

• Ephesians 6:10 calls believers to “be strong in the Lord and in His mighty power,” linking spiritual stamina to godly wisdom rather than human muscle.

and a man of knowledge enhances his strength

• Knowledge builds on wisdom; it keeps adding layers of resilience. Colossians 1:10-11 connects “growing in the knowledge of God” with being “strengthened with all power.”

• Daniel 11:32 notes that “the people who know their God will display strength and take action.” Knowing God personally fuels bold obedience.

• Practical ways knowledge boosts strength:

– It clarifies purpose (Proverbs 20:18).

– It equips for spiritual warfare (2 Corinthians 10:4-5).

– It safeguards against deception (Ephesians 4:14).

– It empowers wise counsel to others (Proverbs 11:14).

• Proverbs 9:10 reminds us, “Knowledge of the Holy One is understanding.” The deeper we dig into Scripture, the stronger our convictions and endurance become (2 Peter 3:18).

summary

Proverbs 24:5 pairs wisdom and knowledge with genuine strength. Wisdom grants immediate, God-given power to face life’s battles, while ongoing knowledge multiplies that power, making it sustainable and influential. Together they form a resilient life that triumphs not by physical force but by steadfast reliance on the Lord and continual growth in His truth.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 5, 6. - Wisdom is beneficial in peace and war. Verse 5. - A wise man is strong. בעוז, "in strength," full of strength, because, however feeble in body, he is wise in counsel, firm in purpose, brave in conduct, thoroughly to be depended upon, and supported by his perfect trust in God (comp. Proverbs 21:22). The Septuagint, with which agree the Syriac and Chaldee, reading differently, renders, "A wise man is better than a strong man" - a sentiment which Lesetre compares to Cicoro's "cedant arma togae." A man of knowledge increaseth strength; literally, strengtheneth power; shows greater, superior power, as Amos 2:14. The Septuagint, from some corruption of the text, renders, "And a man having prudence (is better) than a large estate (γεωργίου μεγάλου);" i.e. wisdom will bring a man more worldly advantages than the possession of extensive farms.
