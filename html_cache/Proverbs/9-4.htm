Whoever is simple, let him turn in here!
This phrase is an invitation from Wisdom, personified as a woman, calling out to those who are "simple." In the biblical context, "simple" refers to those who are naive or inexperienced, lacking in understanding or discernment. This invitation is significant as it reflects the open and accessible nature of divine wisdom, which is available to all who seek it. The call to "turn in here" suggests a change of direction or repentance, a common theme throughout Scripture where turning towards God leads to life and understanding. This echoes the call of Jesus in the New Testament, who invites all who are weary and burdened to come to Him for rest (Matthew 11:28).

She says to him who lacks judgment.
The phrase "lacks judgment" further describes the intended audience of Wisdom's call. It highlights a deficiency in discernment or the ability to make sound decisions. In the cultural and historical context of ancient Israel, wisdom was highly valued and seen as essential for living a righteous and prosperous life. The lack of judgment is not a permanent state but one that can be remedied by heeding Wisdom's call. This aligns with the biblical principle that fear of the Lord is the beginning of wisdom (Proverbs 9:10). The invitation to those lacking judgment is a reminder of God's grace and willingness to impart wisdom to those who seek it earnestly. This can be seen as a type of Christ, who is described as the wisdom of God (1 Corinthians 1:24), offering Himself as the ultimate source of truth and understanding.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Wisdom
Personified as a woman calling out to the simple and those lacking judgment. In the context of Proverbs, Wisdom is often depicted as a guiding and nurturing figure, offering insight and understanding.

2. The Simple
Refers to those who are naive or inexperienced, lacking in wisdom and discernment. In Hebrew, the word "simple" (pethi) suggests an openness to influence, whether good or bad.

3. The House of Wisdom
Implied as the place where Wisdom invites the simple to come and learn. It symbolizes a place of learning, growth, and understanding.

4. The Invitation
The act of calling out to the simple, representing the proactive nature of Wisdom in seeking to impart knowledge and understanding.

5. The Lack of Judgment
Describes those who are without discernment or understanding, highlighting the need for guidance and instruction.
Teaching Points
The Call of Wisdom
Wisdom actively seeks out those who are simple and lacking judgment. This demonstrates God's desire for all to grow in understanding and discernment.

Openness to Learning
Being "simple" is not inherently negative; it represents an opportunity for growth. We should remain open to learning and gaining wisdom from God.

The Importance of Discernment
Lacking judgment can lead to poor decisions. Seeking wisdom helps us develop discernment, which is crucial for navigating life's challenges.

Responding to the Invitation
Just as Wisdom calls out, we must choose to respond. This requires humility and a willingness to admit our need for guidance.

Wisdom's Accessibility
Wisdom is accessible to all who seek it. We should be encouraged that God provides the wisdom we need when we ask in faith.

What Does Proverbs 9:4 Mean
Whoever is simple

- “Simple” in Proverbs describes people who are morally naïve—open, untaught, and easily influenced (see Proverbs 1:4; 14:15).

- Scripture treats this condition seriously but optimistically: the simple are not yet hardened fools; they can still be shaped.

- Lady Wisdom’s first word is an open invitation, not a condemnation. God consistently calls the inexperienced to Himself, whether in Isaiah 55:1 (“Come, all you who are thirsty”) or in Jesus’ plea of Matthew 11:28 (“Come to Me, all you who are weary and burdened”).

- By addressing “whoever,” the verse shows that wisdom is universally offered; no one is excluded on the basis of background, education, or past mistakes.

let him turn in here!

- “Turn” implies decisive action—an intentional change of direction away from aimlessness toward God’s revealed path (compare Proverbs 1:23; Isaiah 55:7).

- “Here” points to Wisdom’s prepared house (Proverbs 9:1–3), symbolizing the secure, nourishing environment found in God’s truth.

• The seven hewn pillars (9:1) picture completeness; God’s wisdom is fully sufficient.

• The prepared feast (9:2) echoes Psalm 23:5; God not only instructs but satisfies.

- Responding to this call is neither passive nor theoretical. It means:

• Choosing daily exposure to Scripture (Psalm 19:7–8).

• Welcoming correction (Proverbs 9:8–9).

• Walking in the fear of the LORD, the beginning of wisdom (Proverbs 9:10).

- Refusing to turn leaves a person wandering into the house of folly (Proverbs 9:13–18), a path that “leads down to the chambers of death.”

she says to him who lacks judgment

- “Lacks judgment” further defines the simple: they are missing discernment but can still receive it (James 1:5).

- Wisdom’s voice is personal: “she says to him.” God speaks directly, not in abstractions.

- The contrast throughout Proverbs 9 between Wisdom (vv. 1–6) and Folly (vv. 13–18) heightens the urgency. Both voices address the same audience, but only one leads to life (Proverbs 8:35–36).

- The moment of hearing becomes a moment of decision:

• Accept Wisdom’s counsel and live (Proverbs 9:6).

• Remain indifferent and drift into destruction (Proverbs 1:32).

summary

Proverbs 9:4 portrays God’s gracious initiative. Wisdom calls the untaught and invites them to change direction, enter a place of fullness, and gain the discernment they lack. The verse assures every open-hearted listener that, through immediate response to God’s word, naïveté can be replaced with understanding, wandering with purpose, and vulnerability with life-giving fellowship.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(4) Whoso is simple . . . as for him that wanteth understanding.--So God does not call many "wise men after the flesh, not many mighty, not many noble" (1Corinthians 1:26); but chooses the "foolish," "weak," and "base," whom man might overlook; not being willing that any should perish (2Peter 3:9), especially His "little ones" (Matthew 18:14). who are liable to fall through their inexperience and want of judgment.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 4-12. - Here follows the invitation of Wisdom, urging the attendance of guests at the sumptuous banquet which she has prepared (comp. Revelation 19:9). Verse 4. - Whose is simple, lot him turn in hither. This is a direct address to the imprudent and inexperienced (see on Proverbs 7:7), calling them to turn aside from the way on which they are going, and to come to her. Vulgate, si quis est parvulus veniat ad me, which reminds one of Christ's tender words, "It is not the will of your Father which is in heaven, that one of these little ones should perish" (Matthew 18:14). As for him that wanteth understanding, she saith to him what follows (so ver. 16). Wisdom's own speech is interrupted, and the writer himself introduces this little clause. She calls on the simple and the unwise, both as necessarily needing her teaching, and not yet inveterate in evil, nor wilfully opposed to better guidance. "The world by wisdom knew not God" and he "hath chosen the foolish things of the world to confound the wise, and the weak things of the world to confound the things that are mighty, and base things of the world, and things which are despised, hath God chosen" (1 Corinthians 1:21, 26, etc.; comp. Matthew 11:25).
