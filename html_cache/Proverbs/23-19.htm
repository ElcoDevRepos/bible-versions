Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Listen, my son
This phrase reflects the traditional method of imparting wisdom in ancient Israel, where a father or elder would instruct the younger generation. It emphasizes the importance of listening as a means of acquiring wisdom. The term "my son" is often used in Proverbs to denote a close, mentoring relationship, similar to the way God addresses His people. This echoes the biblical theme of God as a Father, seen in passages like Deuteronomy 32:6 and Isaiah 64:8.

and be wise
Wisdom in the biblical sense is not merely intellectual knowledge but involves moral and spiritual understanding. It is the application of knowledge in a way that aligns with God's will. Proverbs frequently contrasts wisdom with folly, urging the reader to choose the path of wisdom. This aligns with James 1:5, which encourages believers to seek wisdom from God, who gives generously.

and guide your heart on the right course
The heart in biblical terms often represents the center of one's being, including mind, will, and emotions. Guiding the heart implies intentionality in aligning one's desires and decisions with God's commandments. The "right course" suggests a path of righteousness and obedience to God's law, reminiscent of Psalm 119:105, where God's word is described as a lamp to one's feet and a light to one's path. This guidance is essential for living a life that honors God and reflects His character.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Solomon
Traditionally attributed as the author of Proverbs, Solomon was the son of King David and known for his wisdom. He wrote Proverbs to impart wisdom and understanding.

2. The Son
Represents the reader or the recipient of wisdom. In the context of Proverbs, the "son" is often a metaphor for a disciple or learner.

3. The Heart
In Hebrew thought, the heart (Hebrew: "lev") is the center of one's inner life, encompassing mind, will, and emotions.
Teaching Points
The Call to Listen and Be Wise
Wisdom begins with a willingness to listen. In a world full of noise, discerning the voice of wisdom requires intentional focus and humility.

Guiding the Heart
The heart is the seat of decision-making. Guiding it requires aligning one's desires and intentions with God's will, which involves regular self-examination and prayer.

The Importance of a Right Course
Life is a journey, and the path we choose determines our destination. Setting a right course involves making choices that honor God and reflect His character.

The Role of Parental Guidance
The father-son dynamic in Proverbs highlights the importance of mentorship and guidance in spiritual growth. Seek out and value godly counsel.

Wisdom as a Lifelong Pursuit
Wisdom is not a one-time acquisition but a lifelong pursuit. Continually seek to grow in understanding and apply biblical principles to everyday life.

What Does Proverbs 23:19 Mean
Listen

“Listen, my son, and be wise, and guide your heart on the right course.” (Proverbs 23:19)

To listen is more than hearing sound; it is an intentional act of taking in God-given counsel.

• Proverbs 1:5 reminds us, “Let the wise listen and gain instruction.” The posture of listening opens the door to wisdom.

• James 1:19 echoes, “Everyone should be quick to listen, slow to speak, and slow to anger,” underscoring that the first step toward righteousness is attentive ears.

• When Jesus declared, “He who has ears to hear, let him hear,” (Matthew 11:15) He was calling for active, responsive listening.

my son

The fatherly address conveys both affection and authority.

• Proverbs 3:11-12 teaches, “My son, do not reject the discipline of the LORD… for the LORD disciplines the one He loves.” Loving correction is a mark of genuine relationship.

• Hebrews 12:5-7 applies this pattern to every believer, showing that God treats us as true children by guiding us through instruction and discipline.

• This phrase also personalizes the call; wisdom is not an abstract lecture but a family conversation meant for you.

and be wise

Wisdom is not merely information; it is skillful, God-fearing living.

• Proverbs 9:10 states, “The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom,” anchoring wisdom in reverence for God.

• Ephesians 5:15-17 urges believers to “walk as the wise… because the days are evil,” linking wisdom to daily choices that honor Christ.

• Choosing wisdom is a decisive act. Solomon’s son—and all readers—must embrace wisdom’s path rather than drift into folly (Proverbs 14:16).

and guide your heart on the right course

The heart, biblically, is the control center of thoughts, desires, and decisions. Guiding it implies deliberate self-leadership under God.

• Proverbs 4:23 commands, “Guard your heart with all diligence, for from it flow springs of life.” Guidance begins with vigilant protection.

• Psalm 119:105 affirms, “Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path,” showing that Scripture lights the “right course.”

• Galatians 5:16 instructs, “Walk by the Spirit, and you will not gratify the desires of the flesh.” Yielding to the Spirit steers the heart away from sin toward righteousness.

summary

Proverbs 23:19 calls us to:

1. Tune our ears to godly instruction.

2. Embrace our identity as beloved children under our Father’s wise discipline.

3. Actively choose wisdom rooted in the fear of the Lord.

4. Intentionally steer our inner life—thoughts, motives, and desires—along the path illuminated by God’s Word and Spirit.

By listening, recognizing our sonship, pursuing wisdom, and shepherding our hearts, we walk the straight road that honors God and leads to lasting blessing.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 19-21. - An exhortation to temperance, as one of the results of the fear of God, prefaced by an exhortation to wisdom. Verse 19. - Hear thou. The pronoun gives force and personality to the injunction (Job 33:33). Guide thine heart in the way. (For אשׁר, "to guide straight," see on Proverbs 4:14) "The way" is the right way, in distinction to the many wrong paths of life - the way of understanding, as it is called (Proverbs 9:6). Septuagint, "Direct aright the thoughts of thy heart," for right thoughts lead to right actions.
