The fear of the LORD
The concept of "the fear of the LORD" is foundational in biblical wisdom literature. It signifies a deep respect, reverence, and awe for God, acknowledging His power, authority, and holiness. This fear is not about being afraid but about recognizing God's rightful place in one's life. In the context of ancient Israel, this fear was central to the covenant relationship between God and His people, as seen in Deuteronomy 10:12-13, where Israel is called to fear the LORD and walk in His ways. It is the beginning of wisdom (Proverbs 9:10) and is often associated with obedience and trust in God.

is a fountain of life
A "fountain of life" suggests a source of sustenance, refreshment, and vitality. In the arid climate of the ancient Near East, water was a precious resource, and a fountain or spring was a symbol of life and prosperity. This imagery is used throughout Scripture to describe the life-giving and sustaining power of God (Psalm 36:9). In John 4:14, Jesus speaks of the living water He provides, which becomes a spring of water welling up to eternal life, drawing a parallel to the spiritual life and renewal found in Him.

turning a man from the snares of death
The "snares of death" refer to the traps and pitfalls that lead to spiritual and physical destruction. In biblical times, snares were used by hunters to catch animals, symbolizing the dangers and temptations that can entrap a person. The fear of the LORD provides wisdom and discernment to avoid these dangers. This phrase echoes the protective aspect of divine wisdom, as seen in Proverbs 3:7-8, where fearing the LORD and shunning evil brings health and nourishment. It also points to the ultimate deliverance from death through faith in God, as fulfilled in Jesus Christ, who conquered death and offers eternal life (1 Corinthians 15:54-57).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Solomon
Traditionally regarded as the author of Proverbs, Solomon was the son of King David and known for his wisdom. His proverbs often provide practical guidance for living a life that honors God.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God in the Hebrew Bible, emphasizing His eternal nature and His relationship with His people.

3. The Snares of Death
A metaphorical expression representing the various dangers and temptations that can lead to spiritual or physical destruction.
Teaching Points
The Fear of the LORD as Reverence
The Hebrew word for "fear" (yirah) implies reverence and awe. It is not about being afraid of God but having a deep respect and acknowledgment of His power and authority.

Fountain of Life
The imagery of a fountain suggests a continuous and abundant source of life. When we live in reverence to God, we tap into His endless supply of wisdom, guidance, and sustenance.

Avoiding the Snares of Death
The "snares of death" symbolize the traps and pitfalls of sin and poor choices. The fear of the LORD provides discernment and protection, guiding us away from these dangers.

Practical Wisdom for Daily Living
Embracing the fear of the LORD influences our decisions, relationships, and priorities, leading to a life that reflects God's character and purposes.

Eternal Perspective
Understanding the fear of the LORD helps us to live with an eternal perspective, valuing what is truly important and aligning our lives with God's eternal kingdom.

What Does Proverbs 14:27 Mean
The fear of the LORD

• Scripture describes this fear as reverent awe that moves us to worshipful obedience. “The fear of the LORD is the beginning of knowledge” (Proverbs 1:7) and “the beginning of wisdom” (Proverbs 9:10).

• It is not terror that drives us away but reverence that draws us near, much like children who honor a loving Father yet take His words seriously (Psalm 103:13).

• Such fear produces a clean heart and steady walk; “Blessed is the man who always fears the LORD, but he who hardens his heart falls into trouble” (Proverbs 28:14).

Is a fountain of life

• Solomon reaches for the most refreshing picture imaginable: a spring that never runs dry. Proverbs 13:14 echoes, “The teaching of the wise is a fountain of life, turning one from the snares of death.”

• God Himself is called “the fountain of life” (Psalm 36:9) and “the living water” His people foolishly forsook (Jeremiah 2:13).

• Jesus fulfills the image: “Whoever drinks the water I give him will never thirst… a fount of water springing up to eternal life” (John 4:14).

• Practically, fearing the Lord keeps heart, mind, and body vibrant—protecting marriages, stewarding finances wisely, cultivating integrity. Life flourishes wherever He is honored.

Turning a man from the snares of death

• Sin sets traps that look harmless until they clamp shut (James 1:14-15). The fear of the Lord shines a spotlight on those hidden snares.

• “Surely He will deliver you from the snare of the fowler” (Psalm 91:3). Obedience reroutes us before the jaws close.

• Eternal stakes are in view: “For the wages of sin is death, but the gift of God is eternal life in Christ Jesus our Lord” (Romans 6:23).

• Every daily choice to honor God—speaking truth rather than lies, forgiving instead of retaliating—sidesteps a snare that would choke joy and, ultimately, lives.

summary

Reverent awe of the Lord anchors us, opens an endless spring of spiritual vitality, and steers us clear of destruction. Proverbs 14:27 invites us to live every moment conscious of His majesty, drink deeply of His life-giving presence, and walk safely past every trap that would rob us of the abundant and eternal life He intends.

(27) Fountain of life.--Comp. Proverbs 13:14 and John 4:14.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 27. - A repetition of Proverbs 13:14, substituting the fear of the Lord for "the law of the wise." The fear of the Lord can he called a fountain of life, because, showing itself in obedience, it nourishes the flowers and fruits of faith, produces graces and virtues, and prepares the soul for immortality. Septuagint, "The commandment of the Lord is a fountain of life, and makes one decline from the snare of death."
