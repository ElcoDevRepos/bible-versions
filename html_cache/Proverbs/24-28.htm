Proverbs 24
1. Precepts and Warnings














Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Do not testify against your neighbor without cause
This phrase emphasizes the importance of truthfulness and justice in legal and personal matters. In ancient Israel, the legal system relied heavily on the testimony of witnesses. False testimony could lead to unjust outcomes, which is why the ninth commandment, "You shall not bear false witness against your neighbor" (Exodus 20:16), is so crucial. The cultural context of the time placed a high value on community relationships, and false accusations could destroy reputations and lives. This principle is echoed in the New Testament, where Jesus teaches about the importance of loving one's neighbor (Matthew 22:39) and maintaining integrity in all dealings (Matthew 5:37).

and do not deceive with your lips
Deception is condemned throughout Scripture as contrary to God's nature, who is truth (John 14:6). The Hebrew culture valued honesty and integrity, and deceit was seen as a violation of the covenant community's trust. Proverbs frequently warns against the dangers of lying and deceit (Proverbs 12:22, Proverbs 19:9). In the New Testament, believers are called to put away falsehood and speak truthfully to one another (Ephesians 4:25). This phrase also reflects the broader biblical theme of the tongue's power, as seen in James 3:5-10, where the tongue is described as a small part of the body that can cause great harm.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Neighbor
In the context of Proverbs, a neighbor refers to anyone with whom one has a relationship or interaction, not limited to those living nearby. The term emphasizes the importance of community and interpersonal relationships.

2. Testifying
This refers to giving a statement or bearing witness, often in a legal or formal setting. In ancient Israel, testimony was crucial in legal proceedings, and false testimony could lead to severe consequences.

3. Deception
The act of misleading or lying. In the biblical context, deception is strongly condemned as it undermines trust and justice.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Truthfulness
Truthfulness is foundational to justice and community trust. Bearing false witness can destroy relationships and lead to injustice.

Guarding Our Speech
We must be vigilant about the words we use, ensuring they reflect truth and integrity. Deceptive speech can have far-reaching negative consequences.

The Role of Intentions
Our intentions matter. Testifying against someone without cause or with deceitful intentions is not only harmful but also sinful.

Community and Accountability
As members of a community, we are accountable to one another. Upholding truth strengthens communal bonds and reflects God's character.

Reflecting God's Character
God is a God of truth. As His followers, we are called to reflect His nature by being truthful in all our dealings.

What Does Proverbs 24:28 Mean
Do not testify against your neighbor without cause

Proverbs 24:28 opens with a clear call to restraint. False or frivolous testimony is more than a social misstep—it violates God’s moral order. Exodus 20:16 declares, “You shall not bear false witness against your neighbor”, setting the command in stone among the Ten Commandments. Deuteronomy 19:15–19 underscores that a matter must be “established by the testimony of two or three witnesses,” and condemns the “malicious witness” who fabricates a charge. The wisdom writer builds on these truths, urging us to think before we speak in any formal or informal forum where another’s reputation is at stake.

Practical checkpoints before speaking:

•Ask, “Is there a justified, righteous reason to speak?” (Proverbs 21:28; Proverbs 19:5)

•Confirm facts—hearsay is not testimony (John 7:51).

•Consider intent—are you seeking justice or vengeance? (Leviticus 19:18)

When cause is absent, silence honors both God and neighbor.

and do not deceive with your lips

The verse tightens the focus from courtroom falsehood to everyday conversation. Proverbs 6:16-17 lists “a lying tongue” among the seven things the Lord hates. Ephesians 4:25 urges believers, “Each of you must put off falsehood and speak truthfully to his neighbor”. Lying corrupts relationships, sows distrust, and ultimately aligns the tongue with “the father of lies” (John 8:44).

Everyday applications:

•Refuse exaggeration and half-truths; they are still deception (Proverbs 12:22).

•Keep promises promptly (Psalm 15:4).

•Let words match deeds—integrity silences suspicion (Colossians 3:9).

Truth-telling is both a duty and a witness; when believers speak plainly, they mirror the character of the God “who never lies” (Titus 1:2).

summary

Proverbs 24:28 links two commands that protect community life. First, never speak against someone unless justice and verified facts require it. Second, banish deceit from daily speech. Together they guard reputations, cultivate trust, and honor the God of truth who calls His people to reflect His character in every word.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(28) Without cause--i.e., do not mention thy neighbour's faults unless for some good reason, not for malice or love of gossip.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 28. - Be not a witness against thy neighbour without cause (chinnam); gratuitously (Proverbs 3:30; Proverbs 23:29; Proverbs 26:2), when you are not obliged in the performance of a plain duty. Persons are not to put themselves forward to give testimony to a neighbour's discredit, either officiously as busybodies, or maliciously as slanderers. The maxim is expressed in general terms and is not to be confined to one category, as the Syriac and Septuagint render, "Be not a false witness against thy fellow citizen." And deceive not with thy lips. The Hebrew is really interrogative, "And wouldest thou deceive with thy lips?" (Psalm 78:36). The deceit is not so much intentional falsehood as misrepresentation arising from haste and inconsiderateness consequent on this unnecessary eagerness to push forward testimony unsought. Septuagint, "Neither exaggerate (πλατύνου) with thy lips."
