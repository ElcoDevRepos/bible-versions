On what were its foundations set
This phrase refers to the creation of the earth, emphasizing the divine wisdom and power involved in its formation. In ancient Near Eastern cultures, the earth was often depicted as having a solid foundation, reflecting a common understanding of stability and order. The imagery of foundations suggests a deliberate and purposeful act of creation, aligning with the biblical theme that God is the ultimate architect of the universe. This concept is echoed in other scriptures, such as Psalm 104:5, which speaks of God setting the earth on its foundations so it can never be moved. The rhetorical question highlights human limitations in understanding the mysteries of creation, contrasting with God's omniscience.

or who laid its cornerstone
The cornerstone is a critical element in construction, symbolizing the starting point and stability of a structure. In biblical times, the cornerstone was the first stone set in the foundation, determining the position of the entire building. This imagery is used to illustrate God's role as the master builder of the earth. The cornerstone also has messianic implications, as seen in passages like Isaiah 28:16, where the Messiah is described as a precious cornerstone. In the New Testament, Jesus is identified as the cornerstone in Ephesians 2:20 and 1 Peter 2:6, signifying His foundational role in the church and His fulfillment of Old Testament prophecy. This connection underscores the continuity of God's plan from creation to redemption.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty in Creation
Recognize that God alone has the wisdom and power to create and sustain the universe. This should lead us to trust in His plans and purposes, even when we do not understand them.

Human Limitations
Acknowledge our limited understanding compared to God's infinite wisdom. This humility should drive us to seek God's guidance and rely on His understanding rather than our own.

The Foundation of Faith
Just as the earth's foundations are secure, our faith should be firmly grounded in the truth of God's Word and His character.

The Role of Questioning
While questioning is a natural part of faith, we must approach God with reverence and a willingness to accept His answers, even if they challenge our perspectives.

Creation as a Reflection of God's Glory
Use the beauty and complexity of creation as a reminder of God's majesty and as a source of worship and awe.

What Does Job 38:6 Mean
On what were its foundations set

“On what were its foundations set” (Job 38:6) pictures the earth as a house whose stability depends on hidden footings.

• The Lord’s point is that there are no physical supports beneath the planet; its “foundation” exists only because He spoke it into being (Genesis 1:1; Hebrews 11:3).

• Job 26:7 underscores this: “He hangs the earth upon nothing.” All the mass, motion, and balance of the globe rest on God’s invisible power (Colossians 1:17).

• Psalm 104:5 adds, “He set the earth on its foundations, never to be moved.” What seems secure to us is secure only because the Creator sustains it moment by moment (Nehemiah 9:6).

By asking Job where those footings are, God exposes human inability to explain creation apart from divine revelation. The verse invites humble worship, reminding us that the world’s permanence is anchored in the Lord’s faithful word, not in any natural structure.

or who laid its cornerstone

Continuing the house metaphor, the “cornerstone” is the first stone set, aligning and locking every other part into place.

• God Himself “laid” that cornerstone; no creature assisted (Isaiah 40:13–14). Creation is therefore orderly, intentional, and utterly dependent on the Lord (Proverbs 3:19).

• In Scripture the cornerstone image also foreshadows Christ, the One through whom and for whom all things were made (Psalm 118:22; Isaiah 28:16; Ephesians 2:20). The physical cornerstone of the cosmos points to the spiritual Cornerstone who holds every redeemed life together (1 Peter 2:6; Colossians 1:16–17).

• Job is thus invited to see both the material world and its coming Redeemer as gifts of sovereign grace, established by a wise Builder whose plans cannot fail (Romans 11:36).

summary

Job 38:6 uses building language to emphasize that the earth’s stability and design come solely from God. No material footings uphold creation; no human hand set its cornerstone. The Lord alone founded, aligned, and sustains everything, and in Christ—the ultimate Cornerstone—He reveals the purpose and permanence of His work.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 6. - Whereupon are the foundations thereof fastened? These details follow naturally upon the adoption of the particular metaphor of a house or building. They are not to be pressed. The object is to impress on Job his utter ignorance of God's ways in creation. Or who laid the corner-stone thereof? Who gave the last finishing touch to the work (see Psalm 118:22; Zechariah 4:7)? Canst thou tell? If not, why enter into controversy with the Creator?
