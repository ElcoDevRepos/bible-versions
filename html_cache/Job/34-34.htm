Men of understanding will declare to me
This phrase emphasizes the value of wisdom and discernment. In the context of the Book of Job, it reflects the ongoing dialogue between Job and his friends, where understanding and insight are crucial. The "men of understanding" can be seen as those who possess spiritual discernment, akin to the wise men of Israel, such as Solomon, who was renowned for his wisdom (1 Kings 4:29-34). This phrase suggests that true understanding comes from a deep relationship with God, as seen in Proverbs 9:10, "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom."

and the wise men who hear me will say:
This part of the verse highlights the importance of listening and responding to wisdom. In ancient Near Eastern culture, wisdom was often passed down orally, and listening was a key component of learning. The wise men here are those who not only hear but also comprehend and apply the teachings. This echoes the biblical principle found in James 1:22, "Do not merely listen to the word, and so deceive yourselves. Do what it says." The wise men are expected to affirm the truth of what is spoken, much like the Bereans in Acts 17:11, who examined the Scriptures daily to see if what Paul said was true.

Teaching Points
The Value of Wisdom and Understanding
Wisdom is not just knowledge but the ability to apply it rightly. Elihu calls for those who are wise to evaluate his words, emphasizing the importance of discernment.

The Role of Community in Discernment
Elihu's appeal to wise men suggests that understanding is often a communal effort. We should seek counsel from others who are spiritually mature.

Listening to Diverse Perspectives
Elihu represents a different voice among Job's friends. We should be open to hearing different perspectives, especially when they are grounded in wisdom.

The Importance of Humility in Learning
Elihu's confidence in his message is balanced by his appeal to others for validation. We should remain humble and open to correction.

Seeking Godly Wisdom
True wisdom comes from God. We should continually seek His guidance in understanding and applying His truths.

What Does Job 34:34 Mean
Men of understanding

Job 34:34 opens by pointing to “men of understanding,” people who have cultivated godly discernment. Such individuals evaluate life through the lens of revealed truth, much like those described in Psalm 119:99—“I have more insight than all my teachers, for Your testimonies are my meditation.” Proverbs 1:5 reminds us that “a wise man will hear and increase in learning,” underscoring that understanding flows from constant attention to God’s Word. Elihu expects that these discerning listeners will weigh his words carefully against the standard of divine wisdom, not mere opinion.

will declare to me

The phrase signals confidence that genuine insight produces verbal testimony. Truth cannot stay silent; it speaks up (Psalm 50:6). In Job 32:17–18 Elihu has already voiced a similar conviction: “I too will answer my share; I am full of words.” Here he anticipates that other insightful people will feel the same inward stirring to pronounce judgment on the matter at hand. Proverbs 15:2 affirms, “The tongue of the wise commends knowledge,” highlighting how spiritual perception overflows into articulate declaration.

and the wise men who hear me

Elihu is not content with an echo chamber; he invites scrutiny from “wise men who hear.” True wisdom listens first (James 1:19) and then discerns. Proverbs 9:9 states, “Instruct a wise man and he will be wiser still,” implying that wise listeners are learners before they are speakers. By appealing to these hearers, Elihu roots his argument in communal accountability, recalling Deuteronomy 19:15 where multiple witnesses establish a matter.

will say:

What will these wise observers conclude? Verses 35–37 supply the answer: they will affirm that Job has “spoken without knowledge” and must repent of challenging God’s justice. The pattern mirrors Proverbs 18:17—“The first to state his case seems right until another comes and cross-examines him.” Hearing Elihu’s case, the wise will voice discernment that reinforces God’s righteousness and exposes human misjudgment.

summary

Job 34:34 anticipates a chorus of spiritually discerning voices. Those grounded in God’s truth​—men of understanding and wise hearers—​will step forward, evaluate Elihu’s words, and speak out. Their verdict will vindicate God’s justice and correct Job’s misconceptions, demonstrating that authentic wisdom listens to God, discerns truth, and then declares it boldly.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(34) Let men of understanding tell me.--Rather, Men of understanding will say to me, or, agree with me; and every wise man that heareth me will say, &c.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 34. - Let men of understanding tell me, and let a wise man hearken unto me. As Job does not answer him, Elihu turns to his "men of understanding" (supra, vers. 2, 10). He feels sure that he will at least have carried them with him, and that they will join in the condemnation of Job's words as wanting in true wisdom. "Men of understanding," he says, "will say unto me, yea, every wise man that heareth me will say, Job speaketh without knowledge," etc. (see the Revised Version).
