For the ear tests words
This phrase suggests discernment and the ability to evaluate what is heard. In biblical context, the ear's role in testing words is akin to the heart's role in discerning truth (Proverbs 2:2). The ear's function is not merely passive; it actively engages in judgment, much like the Bereans who examined the Scriptures daily to see if what Paul said was true (Acts 17:11). This reflects the importance of wisdom and understanding, which are highly valued in the wisdom literature of the Bible, such as Proverbs and Ecclesiastes.

as the mouth tastes food.
This comparison highlights the natural and instinctive process of discernment. Just as the mouth can distinguish between different flavors, the ear should be able to discern truth from falsehood. This metaphor is consistent with the biblical theme of spiritual nourishment, where the Word of God is often compared to food (Matthew 4:4). The ability to discern is a gift from God, as seen in Solomon's request for a discerning heart to govern the people and to distinguish between right and wrong (1 Kings 3:9). This phrase underscores the necessity of spiritual maturity and the development of discernment skills, which are essential for a faithful life.

Teaching Points
Discernment as a Spiritual Discipline
Just as the mouth discerns flavors, believers are called to develop spiritual discernment to evaluate teachings and words.

The Role of Wisdom in Testing Words
Wisdom, which comes from God, is essential in discerning truth from falsehood. Believers should seek divine wisdom through prayer and study of the Scriptures.

Guarding Against Deception
In a world filled with conflicting messages, Christians must be vigilant in testing words against the truth of God's Word.

The Importance of Listening
Active listening is crucial in understanding and discerning the truth. Believers should be attentive to God's voice through Scripture and the Holy Spirit.

Application in Daily Life
Just as we are careful about what we consume physically, we should be equally cautious about the spiritual and intellectual content we absorb.

What Does Job 34:3 Mean
For

- This small word signals that Elihu is giving a reason for what he has just urged: “Hear my words, you wise men; listen to me, you men of learning” (Job 34:2).

- He is saying, in effect, “Here’s why you should pay attention.” Scripture often uses “for” the same way—linking a call to listen with a clear rationale (see Isaiah 55:3; Romans 10:17).

- By grounding his appeal in reason, Elihu models a pattern found throughout the Bible: truth invites examination, not blind acceptance (Acts 17:11; 1 Thessalonians 5:21).

the ear tests words

- Just as our ears continually sift through sounds, discerning meaning, so believers are to weigh every statement they hear.

• Job himself recognized this earlier: “Does not the ear test words as the palate tastes food?” (Job 12:11).

• Proverbs pictures wisdom entering “your ear” and guarding you from deception (Proverbs 2:2–12).

- Practically, testing words means:

• Comparing what is said with the clear teaching of Scripture (2 Timothy 3:16).

• Noting the fruit that words produce—peace, righteousness, or confusion (James 3:17–18).

• Refusing to rush to judgment until truth is confirmed (Proverbs 18:13).

as the mouth tastes food

- Elihu’s comparison is vivid: just as taste buds instantly register sweet or sour, the inner person should quickly recognize truth or error.

• Psalm 34:8 invites, “Taste and see that the LORD is good,” linking sensory experience with spiritual discernment.

• Hebrews 5:14 speaks of “those who by constant use have trained their senses to distinguish good from evil,” echoing the same metaphor.

- The picture underscores:

• Discernment is learned through practice—regularly feeding on God’s Word sharpens spiritual taste (Jeremiah 15:16).

• Discernment is essential for health—just as spoiled food harms the body, false teaching harms the soul (2 Peter 2:1).

summary

- “For” roots Elihu’s counsel in a logical, God-given reason.

- “The ear tests words” calls every believer to active, Scripture-based discernment.

- “As the mouth tastes food” illustrates how natural and necessary that discernment should become.

Taken together, Job 34:3 urges us to cultivate a well-trained spiritual palate—constantly sampling every word against the unchanging standard of God’s truth.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 3. - For the ear trieth words, as the mouth tasteth meat. A proverbial expression, already used by Job in the dialogue (Job 12:11). "It is as much the business of the ear to discriminate between wise and foolish words, as of the palate to distinguish between wholesome and unwholesome food."
