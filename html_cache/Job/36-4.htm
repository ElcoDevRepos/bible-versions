Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
For truly my words are free of falsehood;
This phrase emphasizes the speaker's confidence in the truthfulness of his statements. In the context of the Book of Job, Elihu is speaking, asserting that his insights are reliable and trustworthy. This claim to truthfulness is significant in the biblical narrative, as it reflects the broader theme of divine wisdom versus human understanding. The Bible often contrasts truth with falsehood, as seen in passages like Proverbs 12:19, which states, "Truthful lips endure forever, but a lying tongue lasts only a moment." Elihu's assertion can be seen as a foreshadowing of the ultimate truth found in God, who is described as the God of truth in passages like Deuteronomy 32:4.

one perfect in knowledge is with you.
Elihu claims to possess perfect knowledge, which can be interpreted as a bold statement of his understanding of God's ways. This phrase can be seen as a reflection of the biblical theme that true wisdom and knowledge come from God alone, as seen in Proverbs 2:6, "For the LORD gives wisdom; from his mouth come knowledge and understanding." The idea of perfection in knowledge is also a type of Christ, who is described in Colossians 2:3 as having "all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge." Elihu's claim, while humanly ambitious, points to the ultimate fulfillment of perfect knowledge in Jesus Christ, who is the embodiment of divine wisdom.

Teaching Points
The Source of True Wisdom
Elihu claims his words are free of falsehood, suggesting that true wisdom and knowledge come from God. Believers should seek divine wisdom rather than relying solely on human understanding.

Humility in Knowledge
While Elihu speaks confidently, his assertion reminds us to approach knowledge with humility, recognizing that only God is perfect in knowledge.

Discernment in Listening
Elihu's speech encourages discernment in evaluating the words of others. Believers should weigh teachings against the truth of Scripture.

The Role of Suffering
Elihu's perspective invites reflection on the purpose of suffering, encouraging believers to trust in God's perfect knowledge and plan.

Integrity in Speech
Elihu's claim of speaking truthfully challenges believers to maintain integrity in their words, ensuring they align with God's truth.

What Does Job 36:4 Mean
For truly

Elihu begins with an emphatic assurance, signaling that what follows deserves careful attention. Other servants of God use similar openings to underline conviction—“Truly, truly, I tell you” (John 5:24), or Paul’s “I tell you the truth in Christ” (Romans 9:1). Such wording points to a sincere, weighty declaration, inviting listeners to lean in and weigh the message rather than dismiss it as mere opinion.

my words are free of falsehood

• Elihu claims absolute honesty, echoing Proverbs 8:7, “For my mouth will speak truth.”

• Scripture repeatedly stresses that righteous speech must be without deceit (Psalm 34:13; Ephesians 4:25).

• While Elihu later proves imperfect, this moment reminds us that God expects truth on human lips, and that His Spirit can prompt truthful testimony even through fallible messengers (2 Peter 1:21).

• Job had charged his friends with “worthless physicians” (Job 13:4), so Elihu distinguishes himself by asserting a commitment to integrity.

one perfect in knowledge

• The phrase points ultimately to the Lord, who alone is “perfect in knowledge” (Job 37:16).

• Elihu may also be stating that the flawless wisdom of God undergirds his words, much like a prophet speaks under divine oversight (Jeremiah 1:9).

• Psalm 147:5 affirms, “Great is our Lord and mighty in power; His understanding has no limit.” By invoking perfection of knowledge, Elihu frames the discussion around God’s omniscience rather than human speculation.

is with you

• Elihu comforts Job with the truth that God has not abandoned him: “The LORD is near to the brokenhearted” (Psalm 34:18).

• The wording anticipates Jesus’ later promise, “I am with you always” (Matthew 28:20).

• Amid Job’s agony and confusion, the reminder that the perfect-knowing One is present counters despair and encourages trust (Job 23:10).

summary

Job 36:4 presents Elihu’s earnest pledge of truthful speech and highlights God’s flawless knowledge accompanying Job in his suffering. The verse reassures that honest words can point to the ever-present, all-knowing Lord, encouraging believers today to rest in His nearness and trust the integrity of His wisdom.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(4) He that is perfect in knowledge.--We may presume that he meant God; but in the Authorised Version it looks very much as though he meant himself. (Comp. Job 37:16.) So apparently Vulg., "perfecta scientia probabitur tibi."
