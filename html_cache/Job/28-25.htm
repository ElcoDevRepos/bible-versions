When God fixed the weight of the wind
This phrase highlights God's sovereignty and wisdom in creation. The concept of "fixing the weight of the wind" suggests an understanding of the atmospheric conditions and the balance necessary for life on Earth. In ancient times, the wind was often seen as a mysterious and powerful force, and attributing its control to God emphasizes His omnipotence. This aligns with other biblical passages that speak of God's control over nature, such as Psalm 135:7, which mentions God bringing forth the wind from His storehouses. The idea of God setting the weight of the wind also reflects the precision and order in creation, a theme seen in Genesis 1, where God creates the world with intentionality and structure.

and measured out the waters
The imagery of God measuring the waters underscores His authority over the seas and all bodies of water. In the ancient Near Eastern context, water often symbolized chaos and disorder, yet here it is depicted as being measured and controlled by God, indicating His power to bring order out of chaos. This echoes the creation narrative in Genesis 1:6-10, where God separates the waters and establishes boundaries for the seas. The act of measuring also suggests careful planning and design, reinforcing the idea of God as the ultimate architect of the universe. Additionally, this phrase can be connected to the wisdom literature theme of God's understanding being beyond human comprehension, as seen in Proverbs 8:27-29, where wisdom is present at the creation, witnessing God setting limits for the sea.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty in Creation
God’s ability to "fix the weight of the wind" and "measure out the waters" demonstrates His supreme authority over creation. This should lead us to trust in His control over the universe and our lives.

The Precision of God's Design
The specific mention of the weight of the wind and the measurement of waters highlights the precision and intentionality in God’s creation. Believers are encouraged to appreciate the intricacy of God’s work and see His hand in the details of life.

The Search for Wisdom
Job 28 as a whole is a meditation on the search for wisdom, which ultimately is found in the fear of the Lord. This verse reminds us that true wisdom acknowledges God’s order and sovereignty.

Humility Before God’s Wisdom
Recognizing God’s wisdom in creation should humble us, as we realize our limited understanding compared to His infinite knowledge and power.

What Does Job 28:25 Mean
When God

“When God fixed the weight of the wind and measured out the waters” (Job 28:25) opens with a reminder that all true wisdom begins with God Himself. Long before human instruments or formulas existed, He alone knew exactly how creation should function. • Genesis 1:1 shows God speaking everything into existence; Job 38:4-11 has Him questioning Job about the origins of earth and sea; Psalm 148:5 says, “For He commanded, and they were created.” Every observable force, every natural law, finds its source in the will and word of God. Recognizing this sets our hearts in the right posture—humble, worshipful, and ready to learn.

Fixed the weight of the wind

Wind feels weightless to us, yet Scripture declares God “fixed the weight” (or “established the force”) of it. This speaks to His precise calibration of air pressure, density, and motion. • Proverbs 30:4 asks, “Who has gathered the wind in His hands?”—answering with a clear implication: only the Lord. • Nahum 1:3 calls Him “the LORD… in whirlwind and storm.” • Matthew 8:27 marvels, “Even the winds and the sea obey Him!” Appreciate how Job, written thousands of years ago, hints at modern meteorology: air really does have mass, and weather systems operate by exact balances God set in place.

Practical take-aways:

• The winds that refresh the earth, drive ships, and scatter seeds are not random; they reveal divine order.

• Trust grows when we remember that the very air we breathe lies under His governance.

• Storms—literal or figurative—cannot blow outside parameters God has already determined.

And measured out the waters

God “measured out the waters,” assigning limits, volume, and boundaries. • Genesis 1:9-10 records Him gathering the seas so dry land could appear. • Isaiah 40:12 asks, “Who has measured the waters in the hollow of His hand?” • Psalm 104:9 says He “set a boundary they cannot cross.” The vast oceans, rivers, and clouds operate on cycles He scripted (Ecclesiastes 1:7).

Consider:

• Every drop follows schedules of evaporation, precipitation, and runoff that keep life possible.

• Floods may test us, yet Psalm 29:10 assures, “The LORD sits enthroned over the flood.”

• The same God who numbers the oceans also counts our tears (Psalm 56:8), inviting personal trust.

summary

Job 28:25 lifts our eyes from human speculation to divine precision: God personally set the exact weight of the wind and the precise measure of the waters. Creation is neither chaotic nor self-invented; it is purpose-built, lovingly overseen, and continually sustained by its Maker. When we grasp this, we see wisdom not as a human achievement but as reverent alignment with the One who designed every breath of air and every drop of water.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 25. - To make the weight for the winds. God by his wisdom gives to winds their exactly fitting degree of force and violence, so that they perform the work in the world which they were intended to perform, and which would not be performed, were they either of a less or of a greater intensity. And he weigheth the waters by measure (comp. Isaiah 40:12, "Who hath measured the waters in the hollow of his hand, and meted out heaven with a span, and comprehended the dust of the earth in a measure, and weighed the mountains in scales, and the hills in a balance?"). Everything in creation is duly proportioned to every other thing. All is ordered "by weight and with care."
