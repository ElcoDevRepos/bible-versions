Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
that You attend to him every morning,
This phrase reflects the belief in God's constant and attentive care over human life. In the context of Job, it highlights the paradox of God's attention, which can be both comforting and overwhelming. The idea of God attending to humans every morning suggests a daily renewal of His mercies, as seen in Lamentations 3:22-23, where God's mercies are described as new every morning. This daily attention can also be seen as a form of divine oversight, reminiscent of God's provision of manna to the Israelites each morning during their wilderness journey (Exodus 16:21). The morning is often symbolic of new beginnings and hope, yet for Job, it is a reminder of his ongoing suffering and scrutiny.

and test him every moment?
The concept of testing is a recurring theme in the Bible, often associated with refining and proving one's faith, as seen in passages like James 1:2-4 and 1 Peter 1:6-7. In Job's case, the testing is intense and relentless, occurring every moment, which underscores the severity of his trials. This continuous testing can be compared to the refining process of precious metals, where constant heat is applied to remove impurities. Job's experience foreshadows the ultimate testing and suffering of Jesus Christ, who was also tested and tempted in every way (Hebrews 4:15) yet remained without sin. The testing of Job serves as a type of Christ's own trials, pointing to the greater purpose and eventual vindication that comes through enduring faith.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Job
A man described as blameless and upright, who feared God and shunned evil. He is the central figure in the Book of Job, experiencing intense suffering and questioning God's justice.

2. God
The Almighty Creator, who is sovereign over all creation. In the context of Job, God allows Job's faith to be tested by Satan.

3. Satan
The adversary who challenges Job's righteousness, suggesting that Job is faithful only because of his prosperity and protection from God.

4. Uz
The land where Job lived, often associated with the region east of Israel, possibly in Edom or northern Arabia.

5. Job's Friends
Eliphaz, Bildad, and Zophar, who visit Job to comfort him but end up arguing that his suffering must be due to sin.
Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty and Attention
God is intimately involved in the details of our lives, attending to us daily. This can be a source of comfort or a challenge, depending on our circumstances and perspective.

Purpose of Testing
Trials and tests are part of God's refining process. They are not necessarily punitive but can be used to strengthen faith and character.

Human Perspective vs. Divine Perspective
Our understanding of suffering is limited. While we may see trials as burdensome, God sees the bigger picture and works all things for good.

Faith in the Midst of Suffering
Like Job, we are called to maintain our faith even when we do not understand our circumstances. Trusting in God's wisdom and timing is crucial.

Daily Reflection and Dependence on God
Recognizing God's daily involvement should lead us to seek Him every morning, relying on His strength and guidance for each moment.

What Does Job 7:18 Mean
You attend to him every morning

Job marvels that the LORD “attend[s] to him every morning” (Job 7:18), highlighting God’s constant, personal oversight.

• Daily attention shows God’s intentional care—He does not leave humanity to drift. Psalm 5:3 describes David’s morning prayers, confident that God “hears my voice in the morning.”

• Morning visitations underline new mercies. Lamentations 3:22-23 affirms, “His compassions never fail; they are new every morning.”

• Even in suffering, Job recognizes that God is actively involved, not absent. Psalm 8:4 echoes the astonishment: “What is man that You are mindful of him?”

• The regular rhythm—every morning—assures that God’s watchful eye never lapses (Psalm 121:3-4).

God’s faithfulness at daybreak invites trust, reminding believers that each sunrise bears evidence of His steadfast love.

and test him every moment?

The second phrase exposes Job’s tension: the same God who cares also “test[s] him every moment.”

• Continuous testing refines faith. James 1:2-4 urges believers to “consider it pure joy” when trials produce perseverance.

• Trials reveal genuineness. 1 Peter 1:6-7 explains that faith, “tested by fire,” results in praise and honor when Christ is revealed.

• God’s testing is purposeful, never arbitrary. Deuteronomy 8:2 recounts how Israel’s wilderness journey tested hearts “to know what was in your heart.”

• Moment-by-moment testing underscores God’s sovereignty. Hebrews 12:7-11 links discipline with loving sonship, shaping believers into holiness.

• For Job, the unrelenting scrutiny feels overwhelming, yet it also testifies to God’s intimate involvement—a truth echoed in Psalm 139:23-24 where David invites, “Search me, O God….”

Tests are not signs of abandonment but of divine investment, molding character and revealing God’s glory.

summary

Job 7:18 teaches that God’s engagement is both tender and probing. He visits each morning with sustaining mercy and watches every moment to refine faith. His constant presence comforts and challenges, assuring believers that every dawn and every trial come under His wise, loving hand.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 18. - And that thou shouldset visit him every morning, and try him every moment? Our whole life is a probation, not merely particular parts of it. God "tries us every moment" if not with afflictions, then with blessings; if not with pains, then with pleasures. He is with us all the day long, and all our life long, equally in his mercies and in his chastisements. But Job was probably thinking only of the latter.
