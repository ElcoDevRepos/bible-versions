Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Yet he did not waver through disbelief in the promise of God
This phrase highlights Abraham's unwavering faith. In the context of Genesis 15:6, Abraham believed God's promise of numerous descendants despite his and Sarah's old age. This steadfastness is a model of faith, contrasting with the Israelites' frequent disbelief during their wilderness journey (Numbers 14:11). Abraham's faith is also a precursor to the New Testament call to live by faith, as seen in Hebrews 11:1, which defines faith as confidence in what we hope for and assurance about what we do not see.

but was strengthened in his faith
Abraham's faith grew stronger over time, even as the fulfillment of God's promise seemed impossible. This strengthening is akin to the process described in James 1:2-4, where trials test and develop perseverance, leading to maturity. The Greek term used here implies an active empowerment, suggesting that faith is not static but dynamic, growing through reliance on God. This concept is echoed in Philippians 4:13, where Paul speaks of being able to do all things through Christ who strengthens him.

and gave glory to God
Abraham's response to God's promise was to glorify Him, acknowledging His power and faithfulness. This act of giving glory is central to the Christian life, as seen in 1 Corinthians 10:31, which instructs believers to do everything for the glory of God. Abraham's life exemplifies the ultimate purpose of humanity: to glorify God and enjoy Him forever, as stated in the Westminster Shorter Catechism. His faith and actions foreshadow the ultimate glorification of God through Jesus Christ, who perfectly fulfilled God's promises and brought salvation to humanity.

Teaching Points
Unwavering Faith
Abraham's faith did not waver despite circumstances. Believers are encouraged to trust in God's promises even when they seem impossible.

Strengthened by Faith
Faith itself can be a source of strength. As we trust in God, our faith grows stronger, enabling us to face challenges.

Glorifying God
True faith results in giving glory to God. Our lives should reflect His glory as we trust in His promises.

The Power of God's Promises
God's promises are sure and reliable. Believers can rest in the assurance that God will fulfill what He has promised.

Faith in Action
Abraham's faith was not passive; it was active and demonstrated through his actions. Our faith should also be evident in how we live.

What Does Romans 4:20 Mean
Yet he did not waver

Paul points to Abraham, a man who faced impossibilities—his own age and Sarah’s barrenness—yet stayed steady. James 1:6 warns that doubt leaves a person tossed like a wave; Abraham refused that instability. Hebrews 11:11 highlights the same steadiness, noting that Sarah herself received power to conceive because she “considered Him faithful who had promised.”

• Faith took Abraham beyond what his eyes could see.

• He anchored on God’s proven character rather than fluctuating feelings.

through disbelief in the promise of God

Disbelief never gained a foothold. When Zechariah doubted Gabriel’s message in Luke 1:20, he was struck silent; Abraham’s trust brought a very different outcome. Mark 9:24 shows a father confessing, “I do believe; help my unbelief!”—honest doubt seeking help. In contrast, Abraham’s heart rested on Genesis 15:5-6, where God showed him the starry sky and promised descendants without number.

• God’s promises are certain because His nature is unchanging (Numbers 23:19).

• Disbelief questions God’s integrity; faith affirms it.

but was strengthened in his faith

Faith is not static; it grows as God supplies strength. 2 Corinthians 12:9 records the Lord saying, “My grace is sufficient for you, for My power is perfected in weakness.” Abraham experienced the same strengthening. 1 Peter 5:10 assures that God Himself will “restore, secure, strengthen, and establish” His people.

• Each delay became a workout for Abraham’s faith­-muscles.

• Strength came from God, not from Abraham’s optimism.

and gave glory to God

A heart rooted in faith naturally overflows in worship. Psalm 115:1 declares, “Not to us, O LORD, not to us, but to Your name be the glory.” Abraham’s unwavering trust magnified God’s faithfulness long before Isaac arrived. John 9:3 shows Jesus framing miracles as platforms for divine glory, and 1 Corinthians 10:31 calls every action to that same purpose.

• Glorifying God is the inevitable result of trusting Him.

• Praise becomes proof that we believe His word is already as good as done.

summary

Romans 4:20 portrays Abraham as a model believer: he refused to wobble, held tight to God’s promise, drew supernatural strength, and poured out glory to the Promise-Keeper. This verse invites every follower of Christ to cherish Scripture as literal, stand firm without doubting, lean on the Lord’s enabling power, and live in continual worship that spotlights His unfailing faithfulness.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(20) In faith.--Better, through or by faith, corresponding to "through unbelief" in the preceding clause. Unbelief did not make him doubt, but faith made him confident and strong.

Giving glory to God.--This phrase does not necessarily refer to a verbal ascription of praise, but may be used of anything which tends to God's glory, whether in thought, word, or deed (comp. Joshua 7:19; Ezra 10:11; Jeremiah 13:16; Luke 17:18; John 9:24; Acts 12:23); here it seems to be applied to the frank recognition of God's omnipotence involved in Abraham's faith. . . .
