Each curtain was twenty-eight cubits long
The length of twenty-eight cubits (approximately 42 feet or 12.8 meters) for each curtain signifies the grandeur and scale of the Tabernacle, reflecting the majesty of God. The number 28, being a multiple of 7, can symbolize completeness and perfection in biblical numerology. The curtains' length ensured that the Tabernacle was a significant and imposing structure, emphasizing the importance of God's dwelling place among His people.

and four cubits wide
The width of four cubits (approximately 6 feet or 1.8 meters) for each curtain indicates the practical design for covering the Tabernacle. The number four often represents universality or the earth in biblical symbolism, suggesting that God's presence was meant to be accessible and encompassing. The dimensions allowed for a precise fit, ensuring the Tabernacle was both functional and aesthetically pleasing.

all the curtains were the same size
Uniformity in the size of the curtains highlights the order and precision in God's instructions for the Tabernacle's construction. This consistency reflects God's nature as a God of order and detail. The identical size of the curtains ensured a harmonious and unified appearance, symbolizing the unity and equality of the tribes of Israel under God's covenant. This uniformity can also be seen as a type of Christ, who brings unity and equality to all believers.

Teaching Points
Precision in Obedience
The specific dimensions of the curtains highlight the importance of following God's instructions precisely. This teaches us the value of obedience in our walk with God.

Unity and Uniformity
The fact that all the curtains were the same size symbolizes unity and uniformity in the body of Christ. We are called to be united in purpose and mission.

God's Dwelling Among Us
The Tabernacle was a physical representation of God's presence with His people. Today, believers are the temple of the Holy Spirit, and we should live in a way that reflects His presence in us.

Generosity and Contribution
The Israelites contributed materials and labor for the Tabernacle. This encourages us to use our resources and talents for the work of God's kingdom.

Temporary vs. Eternal
The Tabernacle was a temporary structure pointing to the eternal dwelling of God with His people. We should focus on eternal values and not just temporary, earthly concerns.

What Does Exodus 36:9 Mean
Each curtain

- The verse opens with a focus on the single unit, “Each curtain.” Earlier instructions had called for ten beautiful curtains for the Tabernacle (Exodus 36:8; cf. Exodus 26:1).

- God’s design highlights individual pieces that together form one covering—much like the way individual believers form one body in Christ (1 Corinthians 12:12-14).

- Hebrews 8:5 reminds us that these earthly items were “a copy and shadow of what is in heaven,” underscoring that the details matter because they reflect a heavenly pattern.

Was twenty-eight cubits long

- Twenty-eight cubits (about forty-two feet) gave each curtain ample length to drape over the Tabernacle’s framework, reaching down the sides to guard the holy space within (Exodus 26:2).

- The generous dimension speaks of complete coverage—nothing inside was left exposed. Psalm 5:12 echoes the thought spiritually: “For You bless the righteous, O LORD; You surround him with favor as with a shield.”

- The length also points ahead to the perfection of Christ’s atoning work that fully covers sin (Romans 3:24-25).

And four cubits wide

- Four cubits (about six feet) provided balanced proportion, enabling the ten curtains to be joined in two sets of five (Exodus 36:10).

- Precision in width ensured stability, preventing gaps. In the same way, God’s Word is exact and reliable (Proverbs 30:5), leaving no “gaps” in truth or promise.

- The orderly width anticipates the orderliness God desires in worship (1 Corinthians 14:40).

All the curtains were the same size

- Uniformity promoted seamless joining, reflecting the unity of God’s people. Ephesians 4:4-6 affirms, “There is one body and one Spirit… one hope… one Lord, one faith, one baptism; one God and Father of all.”

- Consistency also shows God’s impartiality—every curtain received identical honor and function (James 2:1).

- Standard size guaranteed that the glory within would not be diminished by human innovation; God—not man—set the measurements (Exodus 25:40).

summary

Exodus 36:9 records precise dimensions because every detail of the Tabernacle foreshadows greater spiritual realities. Each curtain mattered individually, yet all worked together in unified purpose. Their length assured complete coverage, their width supplied balanced proportion, and their identical size protected unity and order. The verse teaches that God’s design is exact, His protection is thorough, and His people are called to uniform obedience that displays His glory.
