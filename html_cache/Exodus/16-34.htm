Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
And Aaron placed it in front of the Testimony
Aaron, as the high priest, had the responsibility of handling sacred objects. The "Testimony" refers to the Ark of the Covenant, which contained the tablets of the Ten Commandments. This act signifies the importance of preserving God's provision and commands. The placement in front of the Testimony indicates a position of honor and remembrance, emphasizing the manna as a divine provision and a testament to God's faithfulness. The Ark itself was housed in the Most Holy Place within the Tabernacle, a sacred space that symbolized God's presence among His people.

to be preserved
The preservation of the manna was a physical reminder of God's miraculous provision during the Israelites' journey through the wilderness. It served as a tangible witness to future generations of God's faithfulness and care. This act of preservation also prefigures the enduring nature of God's Word and promises, which are to be remembered and cherished by His people. The concept of preservation here can be seen as a type of Christ, who is the Bread of Life, preserved for all eternity.

just as the LORD had commanded Moses
This phrase underscores the importance of obedience to God's commands. Moses, as the leader and prophet, received direct instructions from God, which were to be followed precisely. This obedience reflects the covenant relationship between God and Israel, where blessings and provisions were contingent upon adherence to His commands. The phrase also highlights the role of divine authority and the necessity of following God's directives, a theme that runs throughout the Scriptures. Moses' obedience is a model for believers, pointing to the ultimate obedience of Christ, who fulfilled the Law perfectly.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant-keeping God of Israel who provides for His people and gives specific instructions for their obedience.

2. Moses
The leader of the Israelites, chosen by God to deliver His people from Egypt and guide them through the wilderness.

3. Aaron
Moses' brother and the first high priest of Israel, who acts in obedience to God's command through Moses.

4. The Testimony (Ark of the Covenant)
The sacred chest that held the tablets of the Law, representing God's covenant with Israel and His presence among them.

5. Preservation of Manna
The event where a portion of manna, the miraculous bread from heaven, is placed before the Testimony as a perpetual reminder of God's provision.
Teaching Points
God's Faithful Provision
Just as God provided manna for the Israelites, He provides for our needs today. Trust in His provision and remember His past faithfulness.

Obedience to God's Commands
Aaron's obedience in placing the manna before the Testimony highlights the importance of following God's instructions precisely. Reflect on areas in your life where God is calling you to obedience.

The Significance of Remembrance
The preserved manna served as a reminder of God's provision. Establish practices in your life that help you remember and celebrate God's faithfulness.

Symbolism of the Manna
Manna symbolizes God's Word and Jesus as the Bread of Life. Engage with Scripture regularly to nourish your spiritual life.

The Presence of God
The placement of manna before the Testimony signifies God's presence. Cultivate an awareness of God's presence in your daily life.

What Does Exodus 16:34 Mean
And Aaron placed it

• The “it” is the jar of manna Moses told Aaron to gather (Exodus 16:33).

• Aaron, the newly appointed high priest (Exodus 28:1), acts as mediator for the nation, illustrating how God uses appointed leaders to safeguard His revelations (Numbers 17:10; Hebrews 5:4).

• His physical action underscores active obedience rather than mere agreement with God’s word (Exodus 7:6; James 1:22).

in front of the Testimony

• “The Testimony” refers to the stone tablets of the covenant housed in the Ark of the Covenant (Exodus 25:16, 21; Deuteronomy 10:5).

• Placing the manna before the Ark ties daily provision to the covenant promises, visually linking God’s law and His sustaining grace (Psalm 19:7–10; Hebrews 9:4).

• This spot—inside the Holy Place but before the Most Holy Place—signals that God’s provision is meant to lead His people toward deeper worship (Exodus 40:20–22).

to be preserved

• God wanted future generations to see tangible evidence of His faithfulness (Exodus 16:32; Joshua 4:6–7).

• The manna became a perpetual reminder that every need is met by God, foreshadowing Christ, the true “bread from heaven” (John 6:32–35).

• Preservation also protected against forgetfulness, a recurring danger for Israel (Deuteronomy 8:2–3; Psalm 78:11).

just as the LORD had commanded Moses

• The phrase highlights total submission to divine instruction (Exodus 39:42–43; Numbers 3:51).

• It affirms that obedience—not innovation—secures blessing and maintains covenant order (Deuteronomy 28:1–2; John 14:15).

• Moses receives the command, but Aaron’s compliance shows cooperative leadership under God’s authority (Exodus 17:11–12).

summary

Exodus 16:34 records a simple act with deep significance. Aaron physically places the jar of manna before the Ark, linking God’s daily provision to His enduring covenant. The jar is kept for posterity, ensuring every generation remembers the Lord’s faithfulness. All is done exactly as commanded, underscoring that blessing flows through obedient response to God’s revealed word.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 34, - Aaron laid it up before the testimony. "The testimony" is not the Ark of the Covenant, which is never so called, but the Covenant itself, or the two tables of stone engraved by the finger of God, which are termed "the testimony" in Exodus 25:16-21; Exodus 40:20; etc. The pot of manna was laid up inside the ark (Hebrews 9:4) in front of the two tables.
