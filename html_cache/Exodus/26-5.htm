Make fifty loops on one curtain
The number fifty in the Bible often symbolizes completeness or fullness, as seen in the Year of Jubilee (Leviticus 25:10). The loops were likely made of blue, purple, or scarlet yarn, colors associated with royalty and divinity, reflecting the sacred nature of the tabernacle. The use of loops suggests a method of joining, indicating unity and cohesion among the curtains, symbolizing the unity of God's people.

and fifty loops on the end curtain of the second set
The repetition of fifty loops on the second set of curtains emphasizes symmetry and order, reflecting God's nature as a God of order (1 Corinthians 14:33). The "end curtain" signifies the completion of a section, ensuring that the tabernacle's structure was both functional and aesthetically pleasing. This meticulous design points to the importance of precision in worship and obedience to God's instructions.

so that the loops line up opposite one another
The alignment of the loops opposite each other ensured a perfect fit, symbolizing harmony and alignment with God's will. This precise arrangement can be seen as a type of Christ, who perfectly fulfills the law and the prophets (Matthew 5:17). The concept of things lining up opposite each other also reflects the idea of covenant, where two parties come together in agreement, much like the Old and New Covenants are fulfilled in Christ.

What Does Exodus 26:5 Mean
The Tabernacle Blueprint

“Make fifty loops on one curtain and fifty loops on the end curtain of the second set, so that the loops line up opposite one another.” (Exodus 26:5)

• The verse drops us into God’s architectural plan for the wilderness Tabernacle (Exodus 25–31).

• These curtains formed the first layer over the Most Holy and Holy Place (Exodus 26:1).

• Every detail came “according to the pattern shown you on the mountain” (Exodus 25:40; Hebrews 8:5), underscoring that God—not man—defined worship.

God of Order and Detail

• Fifty loops on each panel, precisely matching, reveal a God who values exactness (1 Corinthians 14:33).

• Even what seems mundane is sacred when God speaks it (Leviticus 10:1-3).

• The detail protects Israel from improvising; obedience keeps them under covenant blessing (Deuteronomy 28:1-2).

Unity Through Connection

• The loops faced “opposite one another,” awaiting gold clasps (Exodus 26:6) that would join separate pieces into one covering.

• Picture the twelve tribes, distinct yet bound together in worship (Numbers 2:1-2).

• Jesus later prayed “that they may all be one” (John 17:21); Paul said the church is “held together by every supporting ligament” (Ephesians 4:16).

Provision for Ministry

• Proper alignment kept light out and glory in, safeguarding priests as they served (Exodus 40:20-21).

• God supplies hardware before asking for service, mirroring Ephesians 2:10: we are “created in Christ Jesus for good works, which God prepared beforehand.”

Foreshadowing of Christ

• The loops paired with clasps point to Christ’s mediating work—He joins sinful people to a holy God (1 Timothy 2:5).

• Like the curtains, His body “torn” yet not divided (John 19:36-37), forms the sole covering that shelters us from judgment (Isaiah 61:10).

A Call to Obedience

• Moses “did everything just as the LORD commanded” (Exodus 40:16).

• Believers today echo that heart, building lives that match God’s pattern revealed in Scripture (James 1:22-25).

• Small acts of faithfulness—our own “loops”—fit into God’s larger masterpiece (1 Corinthians 3:9).

summary

Exodus 26:5 showcases a meticulous, covenant-keeping God who unites His people through precise, purposeful design. Fifty matching loops remind us that every stitch of obedience matters, every connection fosters unity, and every detail ultimately points to Christ, our perfect covering.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(5) That the loops may take hold one of another.--Rather, correspond one to another. They were not to "take hold," but to be attached by golden links.

Taches, or clasps. These might be split-rings, or links like modern sleeve-links.

And it shall be one tabernacle.--Rather, and (so) the tabernacle shall be one. The division of the curtain which formed the roof into two portions tended to make a division in the tabernacle itself. To prevent this, the two curtains were to be so looped together as to be practically one. Thus the tabernacle itself became one.
