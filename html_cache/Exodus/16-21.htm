Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Every morning
The Israelites were instructed to gather manna daily, emphasizing reliance on God's provision. This daily routine reflects the importance of consistent spiritual discipline and dependence on God. It parallels the Lord's Prayer, "Give us this day our daily bread," highlighting daily reliance on God for sustenance.

each one gathered as much as was needed
This phrase underscores the principle of sufficiency and contentment. Each person gathered according to their need, illustrating God's provision for all. It reflects the New Testament teaching in 2 Corinthians 8:15, where Paul references this event to encourage equality and sharing among believers.

and when the sun grew hot
The timing of gathering manna was crucial, as it had to be collected before the sun's heat caused it to melt. This detail emphasizes the importance of obedience and diligence in following God's instructions. It also serves as a reminder of the fleeting nature of earthly provisions and the need to prioritize spiritual matters.

it melted away
The melting of manna symbolizes the temporary nature of earthly sustenance and the need for timely obedience. It also foreshadows the impermanence of worldly things compared to the eternal nature of God's word and promises. This can be seen as a type of Christ, who is the true bread from heaven, offering eternal sustenance.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Israelites
The people of God who were delivered from slavery in Egypt and are now journeying through the wilderness.

2. The Wilderness of Sin
The location where the Israelites are currently encamped, a place of testing and reliance on God's provision.

3. Manna
The miraculous bread from heaven provided by God to sustain the Israelites during their journey through the wilderness.

4. Moses
The leader of the Israelites, through whom God communicates His instructions regarding the gathering of manna.

5. God's Provision
The central event of this passage, highlighting God's daily provision and care for His people.
Teaching Points
Daily Dependence on God
Just as the Israelites gathered manna daily, we are called to rely on God each day for our needs.

Obedience to God's Instructions
The Israelites had to follow specific instructions to gather manna, teaching us the importance of obedience in our walk with God.

Trust in God's Timing
The manna melted when the sun grew hot, reminding us to trust God's timing and provision without hoarding or worrying about the future.

Spiritual Nourishment
Manna symbolizes the spiritual nourishment we receive from God's Word and through Christ, the bread of life.

Community and Sharing
The gathering of manna was a communal activity, encouraging us to support and share with one another in our faith communities.

What Does Exodus 16:21 Mean
Every morning

- God’s provision was punctual, arriving with the dawn every single day (Exodus 16:35).

- The timing echoes Lamentations 3:22-23, where new mercies “are new every morning.”

- Jesus alludes to this rhythm when He teaches us to pray, “Give us today our daily bread” (Matthew 6:11), showing that the manna schedule foreshadows a life of daily dependence.

- Seeking the Lord early is a consistent biblical pattern (Psalm 5:3).

each one gathered

- Gathering could not be delegated; every household had to participate (Exodus 16:16).

- This personal action pictures the gospel call: “to all who received Him…He gave the right to become children of God” (John 1:12).

- Paul applies the same principle of personal responsibility in 2 Thessalonians 3:10: “If anyone is not willing to work, neither shall he eat.”

- Individual obedience built corporate strength, forging unity through shared effort.

as much as was needed

- The portion was perfect; “he who gathered much had nothing left over, and he who gathered little had no lack” (Exodus 16:18).

- God curbs both excess and deficiency, teaching contentment (1 Timothy 6:8).

- Paul cites this event in 2 Corinthians 8:13-15 to encourage generous but measured giving, proving that God supplies exactly what is required.

and when the sun grew hot

- Provision came with a deadline; diligence was rewarded, procrastination was not (Proverbs 10:5).

- Jesus reinforces the urgency: “We must work the works of Him who sent Me while it is day” (John 9:4).

- The warming sun reminds believers that spiritual opportunities are time-sensitive.

it melted away

- The manna vanished in the heat, confirming its supernatural nature (Psalm 78:24-25).

- Vanishing provisions forced Israel to trust God daily, echoing Matthew 6:34: “Do not worry about tomorrow.”

- Ignored truth evaporates just as quickly; James 1:22-24 warns that hearers who fail to act soon forget what they have seen.

summary

Exodus 16:21 illustrates a daily cycle of grace: God reliably supplies, each believer must personally gather, the portion is precisely sufficient, the window of obedience is brief, and unattended blessings disappear. The verse urges us to seek God first, labor faithfully, live contentedly, act promptly, and trust Him anew every morning.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 21. - When the sun waxed hot it melted. The manna had to be gathered early. What had not been collected before the sun grew hot, melted away and disappeared from sight. In this respect the miraculous manna resembled both the manna of commerce and the "air-honey."
