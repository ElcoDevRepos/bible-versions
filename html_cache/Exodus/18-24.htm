Moses listened to his father-in-law
Moses, a central figure in the Exodus narrative, demonstrates humility and wisdom by listening to Jethro, his father-in-law. Jethro, a Midianite priest, offers advice on leadership and delegation. This interaction highlights the importance of seeking counsel and valuing wisdom from others, regardless of their background. The relationship between Moses and Jethro also underscores the significance of family ties and respect for elders, which is a recurring theme in biblical texts (Proverbs 12:15, Proverbs 19:20).

and did everything he said.
Moses' obedience to Jethro's advice reflects his willingness to implement practical solutions for the well-being of the Israelites. This action demonstrates effective leadership and the ability to adapt, qualities that are essential for guiding a large community. The implementation of Jethro's advice leads to the establishment of a judicial system, which is a precursor to the more formalized legal structures found later in the Torah. This decision also foreshadows the delegation of responsibilities seen in the New Testament, such as the appointment of deacons in Acts 6:1-7. Moses' actions can be seen as a type of Christ, who also listened to and fulfilled the will of His Father (John 5:19).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Moses
The central figure in this passage, Moses is the leader of the Israelites, chosen by God to lead His people out of Egypt. He is known for his humility and obedience to God.

2. Jethro
Moses' father-in-law, a Midianite priest, who visits Moses and offers wise counsel on leadership and delegation.

3. Israelites
The people of God, whom Moses is leading through the wilderness after their exodus from Egypt.

4. Midian
The region where Jethro comes from, representing a place of wisdom and counsel outside of the immediate Israelite community.

5. Delegation of Leadership
The event where Jethro advises Moses to appoint capable men to help judge the people, thus easing Moses' burden.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Wise Counsel
Seeking and heeding wise counsel is crucial for effective leadership. Moses' willingness to listen to Jethro demonstrates humility and wisdom.

Delegation as a Leadership Principle
Effective leaders delegate responsibilities to capable individuals, ensuring that tasks are managed efficiently and leaders are not overwhelmed.

Humility in Leadership
Moses' example shows that even great leaders need guidance and should be open to advice from others, regardless of their status or background.

Community and Shared Responsibility
The body of Christ functions best when responsibilities are shared among its members, allowing each person to use their gifts for the common good.

Obedience to Godly Advice
Acting on godly advice leads to better outcomes and aligns with God's will, as seen in Moses' implementation of Jethro's counsel.

What Does Exodus 18:24 Mean
Moses listened

Moses, the man God used to lead Israel out of Egypt, models teachability here. His ears stayed open even after spectacular encounters with the LORD (Exodus 3; 19).

• Proverbs 19:20 reminds, “Listen to counsel and receive instruction, that you may be wise in your latter days”.

• James 1:19 calls every believer to be “quick to listen,” a trait Moses displays.

• Numbers 12:3 underscores his humility, the soil in which listening grows.

to his father-in-law

Jethro was not merely kin; he was an older, God-fearing priest who cared for Moses’ well-being and Israel’s welfare (Exodus 18:10–12).

• Exodus 3:1 already introduced Jethro as a shepherd and spiritual leader, establishing credibility.

• Ephesians 6:2–3 echoes the blessing promised to those who honor parental voices.

• Proverbs 1:8 tells children, “Do not forsake your mother’s teaching,” showing that family wisdom can be God’s channel.

• 1 Peter 5:5 urges younger men to submit to elders, mirroring Moses’ posture.

and did everything

Moses didn’t half-apply the advice; he embraced it fully.

• James 1:22 cautions against being “hearers only.” Moses avoided that trap.

• 1 Samuel 15:22 teaches that obedience surpasses sacrifice; Moses obeyed without delay.

• Colossians 3:23—“Whatever you do, work at it with your whole heart”—fits Moses’ complete follow-through.

• Joshua 11:15 later notes Joshua learned this same trait from Moses: exact obedience to commands given.

he said

What Jethro said (Exodus 18:17–23) centered on delegation: appoint capable men to share the judging load so the people receive justice and Moses endures.

• Acts 6:3 shows the early church echoing this pattern—selecting Spirit-filled men to serve tables so apostles could focus on prayer and the word.

• Numbers 11:16–17 reveals God affirming shared leadership again with seventy elders.

• Deuteronomy 1:9–18 records Moses retelling how he followed Jethro’s counsel, proving its long-term value.

• 1 Corinthians 14:40—“everything must be done in a proper and orderly manner”—captures the principle behind Jethro’s plan.

summary

Exodus 18:24 spotlights a leader who stays teachable, honors family counsel, obeys completely, and implements wise, God-honoring structure. Moses’ example urges us to value godly advice, act on it fully, and organize life and ministry so burdens are shared and God’s people thrive.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(24, 25) Moses hearkened.--The appointment of judges, according to Jethro's advice, was not made until after the giving of the Law and the setting up of the Tabernacle. (See Deuteronomy 1:9-15.) In one particular Moses departed from the counsel given to him. Instead of directly choosing the "able men" himself, he left the selection to the people (Deuteronomy 1:13). And contented himself with investing the men chosen with their authority. Comp. the course taken by the apostolic college with respect to the first deacons (Acts 6:3-6).

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 24. - So Moses hearkened. Moses took the advice tendered him, not immediately, but after the law had been given at Sinai, and the journeying was about to be resumed. See Deuteronomy 1:9-15.
