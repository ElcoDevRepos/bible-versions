Bezalel son of Uri, the son of Hur, of the tribe of Judah
Bezalel is introduced as a skilled craftsman chosen by God for the construction of the Tabernacle. His lineage is significant, as he is from the tribe of Judah, which is later associated with leadership and kingship in Israel. Judah is the tribe from which King David and ultimately Jesus Christ descend, highlighting a connection to the Messianic line. The mention of his grandfather, Hur, may connect him to the Hur who supported Moses during the battle against the Amalekites (Exodus 17:10-12), suggesting a family legacy of faithfulness and leadership.

made everything that the LORD had commanded Moses
This phrase emphasizes Bezalel's obedience and faithfulness in executing the divine instructions given to Moses. It underscores the importance of following God's commands precisely, as the Tabernacle was to be a dwelling place for God's presence among His people. The meticulous construction of the Tabernacle foreshadows the perfect obedience of Christ, who fulfilled the Law and the Prophets (Matthew 5:17). Bezalel's work is a type of Christ's work in building the spiritual house of God, the Church (1 Peter 2:5). The phrase also highlights the collaborative effort between divine command and human action in accomplishing God's purposes.

A skilled craftsman chosen by God to oversee the construction of the Tabernacle. His name means "in the shadow of God," indicating divine protection and guidance.

2. Uri
Bezalel's father, whose lineage is traced back to the tribe of Judah, highlighting the importance of heritage and divine selection.

3. Hur
Bezalel's grandfather, possibly the same Hur who supported Moses during the battle against the Amalekites, indicating a family legacy of faithfulness.

4. Tribe of Judah
One of the twelve tribes of Israel, known for leadership and strength, from which King David and ultimately Jesus Christ would come.

5. The LORD's Command to Moses
The divine instructions given to Moses for the construction of the Tabernacle, emphasizing obedience and precision in following God's will.
Teaching Points
Divine Calling and Equipping
God calls and equips individuals for specific tasks. Just as Bezalel was filled with the Spirit for his work, believers are empowered by the Holy Spirit for their unique callings.

Obedience to God's Commands
Bezalel's work was a direct response to God's commands through Moses. Our service should align with God's instructions as revealed in Scripture.

Heritage and Legacy
Bezalel's lineage from the tribe of Judah underscores the importance of spiritual heritage. Believers are encouraged to honor their spiritual lineage and pass on a legacy of faith.

Faithfulness in Service
Bezalel's faithful execution of his duties serves as a model for believers to be diligent and faithful in their service to God, regardless of the task's visibility or recognition.

Role of Community in God's Work
The construction of the Tabernacle was a communal effort, highlighting the importance of working together in the body of Christ to accomplish God's purposes.

What Does Exodus 38:22 Mean
Bezalel

“Bezalel” means “in the shadow (protection) of God.” The Lord personally chose him and “filled him with the Spirit of God, with wisdom, understanding, and ability in all kinds of craftsmanship” (Exodus 31:2-5; 35:30-35).

• God’s call is specific; He names His servants.

• Gifts come from the Spirit, not mere human talent (1 Colossians 12:4-6).

• When God appoints, He equips—Bezalel lacked nothing needed to honor the Lord’s design (2 Peter 1:3).

son of Uri

Mentioning his father places Bezalel in a living family line. Exodus 31:2 repeats the phrase, underscoring accountability within the household.

• Faithfulness often passes through generations (Deuteronomy 6:6-9).

• God values family heritage; the work done for Him blesses those who come after (Psalm 112:1-2).

the son of Hur

Hur stood with Moses and Aaron, holding up Moses’ hands during Israel’s battle with Amalek (Exodus 17:10-12). He also helped lead while Moses was on Sinai (Exodus 24:14).

• Spiritual legacy: a grandfather’s courage shapes a grandson’s calling.

• Partnership in ministry spans generations; Hur’s earlier obedience paved the way for Bezalel’s craftsmanship (Proverbs 13:22).

of the tribe of Judah

Judah carried prophetic prominence: “The scepter will not depart from Judah” (Genesis 49:8-10). Later, the Messiah would come from this tribe (Matthew 1:1-3).

• Leadership and worship intertwine; Judah means “praise.”

• God delights to raise artisans and leaders from unexpected places within the same tribe (Micah 5:2).

made everything

Bezalel completed the entire tabernacle project—structures, furnishings, and priestly garments—“exactly as the LORD had commanded” (Exodus 39:32; 40:33).

• Obedience is measured by completion, not mere intention (James 1:22).

• Faithful stewardship turns divine blueprints into visible glory (1 Colossians 4:2).

that the LORD had commanded Moses

God’s revelation came to Moses; Bezalel translated it into reality. This chain underscores divine order: revelation → leadership → execution (Exodus 25:40; Hebrews 8:5).

• True ministry never invents its own agenda; it follows God’s word (John 14:15).

• Collaboration within God’s design fosters unity: Moses received, Bezalel built, Israel worshiped (Ephesians 4:16).

summary

Exodus 38:22 highlights a Spirit-filled craftsman who, grounded in a godly heritage and tribal promise, finished every task God revealed through Moses. The verse celebrates complete obedience, generational faithfulness, and the beauty of gifts surrendered to God’s precise commands—an enduring reminder that our work, when aligned with His Word, becomes a dwelling place for His glory.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 22. - Bezaleel made all. The direction of the whole work by Bezaleel is here asserted more definitely and decidedly than elsewhere. Compare Exodus 31:2-6; Exodus 36:1, 2.
