Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
He has filled them with skill
This phrase highlights the divine endowment of skills and abilities. In the context of Exodus, God specifically equips individuals for the construction of the Tabernacle. This reflects the biblical theme that God provides what is necessary for His purposes, as seen in 1 Corinthians 12:4-11, where spiritual gifts are given for the common good.

to do all kinds of work
The phrase underscores the variety and diversity of tasks required for the Tabernacle's construction. It emphasizes that God's work encompasses a wide range of skills and talents, reflecting the multifaceted nature of His creation and the body of Christ, as seen in Romans 12:4-8.

as engravers, designers,
Engraving and designing were crucial for the intricate details of the Tabernacle. These roles required precision and creativity, reflecting the beauty and order of God's creation. The skills of Bezalel and Oholiab, mentioned earlier in Exodus 31:1-6, are examples of God equipping individuals for specific tasks.

embroiderers in blue, purple, and scarlet yarn and fine linen,
The use of specific colors and materials has deep symbolic meaning. Blue, purple, and scarlet were colors associated with royalty and divinity, often used in the garments of priests and the Tabernacle itself (Exodus 26:1). Fine linen represents purity and righteousness, as seen in Revelation 19:8.

and as weavers
Weaving was an essential skill for creating the fabrics used in the Tabernacle. This craft required patience and attention to detail, symbolizing the intricate and interconnected nature of God's plan and His people, as seen in Psalm 139:13-14, where God is described as weaving us together in the womb.

as artistic designers of every kind of craft.
This phrase highlights the creativity and artistry involved in the Tabernacle's construction. It reflects the image of God as the ultimate Creator, who instills creativity in humanity. The diversity of crafts points to the comprehensive nature of God's work and the unity of diverse gifts in the body of Christ, as seen in Ephesians 2:10, where believers are described as God's workmanship.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Bezalel and Oholiab
These two individuals were specifically chosen by God and filled with His Spirit to lead the construction of the Tabernacle. They were endowed with wisdom, understanding, and knowledge in all kinds of craftsmanship.

2. The Israelites
The broader community of Israelites was called to contribute their skills and resources to the building of the Tabernacle, demonstrating a collective effort in worship and obedience to God.

3. The Tabernacle
This was the portable dwelling place for the presence of God among the Israelites during their journey through the wilderness. It was a central place of worship and sacrifice.

4. The Wilderness
The setting for the construction of the Tabernacle, representing a place of testing, reliance on God, and preparation for entering the Promised Land.

5. Moses
As the leader of the Israelites, Moses communicated God’s instructions for the construction of the Tabernacle and oversaw the work.
Teaching Points
God Equips for His Work
Just as God filled Bezalel and Oholiab with skill, He equips each believer with unique gifts and talents for His purposes. We should seek to identify and use these gifts in service to God and others.

The Role of Community in Worship
The construction of the Tabernacle was a communal effort, highlighting the importance of working together in the body of Christ to achieve God’s purposes.

Excellence in Craftsmanship
The detailed and skilled work required for the Tabernacle reflects the value of excellence in our work. As believers, we should strive to do our best in all tasks as an act of worship.

Obedience to God’s Instructions
The Israelites followed God’s specific instructions for the Tabernacle, reminding us of the importance of obedience in our walk with God.

The Presence of God
The Tabernacle was a symbol of God’s presence among His people. Today, believers are the temple of the Holy Spirit, called to live in a way that honors His presence within us.

What Does Exodus 35:35 Mean
He has filled them

“Bezalel and Oholiab, and every skilled person in whom the LORD has put skill and understanding” (Ex-36:1) highlight that the subject is God Himself. Just as “the Spirit of God was moving over the surface of the waters” (Genesis 1:2), here the same Source moves in people. • Notice that filling is God-initiated, not self-generated (Acts 4:8; 1 Corinthians 12:11). • The verse assures that ordinary work can be Spirit-filled, echoing Colossians 3:23-24.

with skill

The term embraces both aptitude and wisdom. “I have endowed him with a divine spirit of skill, understanding, and knowledge in all kinds of craftsmanship” (Exodus 31:3). Skill is a stewardship, paralleling the parable of the talents (Matthew 25:14-30). • God equips for both sacred and practical tasks (1 Chronicles 28:12, 19). • James 1:17 affirms every good gift—including artistic ability—“is from above.”

to do all kinds of work

The purpose clause shows giftedness is meant for action. • Ephesians 2:10: “We are His workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works.” • Titus 3:8 encourages believers “to devote themselves to good works.” Variety of service is celebrated in 1 Corinthians 12:4-6: “There are different kinds of gifts… but the same God works all of them in all people.”

engravers, designers, embroiderers, weavers

Specific crafts are named, tying art to worship. The tabernacle’s beauty preached God’s holiness (Exodus 26:1, 31). • Stone engraving (Exodus 28:11-12), metal design (Exodus 37:1-9), fabric artistry (Exodus 26:36) all pointed to the coming Christ who is the true dwelling place of God with man (John 1:14). • The colors—blue, purple, scarlet—symbolize heaven, royalty, and sacrifice (Numbers 4:6-13; Matthew 27:28-29).

artistic designers of every kind of craft

The final phrase broadens the scope: no skill is outside God’s interest. • 1 Corinthians 10:31 calls believers to “do all to the glory of God.” • Proverbs 22:29 commends excellence: “Do you see a man skilled in his work? He will stand before kings.” Creativity reflects the Creator (Psalm 104:24).

summary

Exodus 35:35 teaches that God personally equips His people with artistic skill so that every task—from carving to weaving—can honor Him. Giftedness is both spiritual and practical, meant to serve the community and point to His glory. Our response is to recognize the Source, exercise the gift faithfully, and let workmanship become worship.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 35. - Them hath he filled with wisdom of heart - i.e., "with talent or genius." Of the engraver. Rather, "of the artificer," a general term, under which working in metal, Gem-engraving, and wood-carving are included. And of the cunning workman. Rather, "and of the skilful weaver." This clause seems to apply to Aholiab (Exodus 38:23), the preceding one to Bezaleel. And of the embroiderer. This also applies to Aholiab (1.s.c.). And of the weaver - i.e., "the ordinary, weaver," who wove a cloth all of one colour. The "skilful weaver" produced a patterned fabric. (See Exodus 26:1.) The methods of working here spoken of are, all of them, such as were well known in Egypt at the time, and which, consequently, it would have been quite natural for some of the Israelites to have learnt. We are not to suppose that God supernaturally communicated to Bezaleel and Aholiab the technical knowledge required in their occupations, but only that he gave them genius and artistic skill, so that both their designs, and their execution of them, were of unusual excellence.
