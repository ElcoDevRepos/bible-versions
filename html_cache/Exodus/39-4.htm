They made shoulder pieces for the ephod
The ephod was a sacred garment worn by the high priest, integral to the priestly attire described in Exodus. The shoulder pieces were significant as they held the onyx stones engraved with the names of the twelve tribes of Israel, symbolizing the high priest bearing the people before God. This act of bearing the tribes on the shoulders can be seen as a type of Christ, who bears the burdens of His people. The ephod's design reflects the importance of intercession and representation before God, a role fulfilled by Jesus as our High Priest (Hebrews 4:14-16).

which were attached at two of its corners
The attachment at the corners indicates a precise and intentional design, ensuring the ephod was secure and functional. This detail underscores the importance of order and structure in worship, reflecting God's nature as a God of order (1 Corinthians 14:33). The craftsmanship required for such attachments highlights the skill and dedication of the artisans, who were filled with the Spirit of God for this task (Exodus 31:1-6).

so it could be fastened
The fastening of the ephod ensured that it remained in place during the high priest's duties, symbolizing stability and readiness in service to God. This stability can be seen as a metaphor for the steadfastness required in spiritual leadership and service. The concept of being "fastened" or "secured" is echoed in the New Testament, where believers are encouraged to stand firm in their faith (Ephesians 6:13-14). The ephod's secure fastening also points to the security believers have in Christ, who holds them firmly in His care (John 10:28-29).

What Does Exodus 39:4 Mean
They made shoulder pieces for the ephod

The ephod was the distinctive, richly woven outer garment of the high priest (Exodus 28:6).

• Craftsmanship—Skilled artisans “made” the pieces exactly as God had commanded, underscoring the trustworthiness and precision of His word (Exodus 31:1-6).

• Purpose—The shoulder pieces formed part of the priestly attire that set Aaron apart for holy service (Exodus 28:1).

• Continuity—Later, Samuel ministered “wearing a linen ephod” (1 Samuel 2:18), showing the enduring pattern God established.

which were attached at two of its corners

• Specific design—The ephod’s upper corners received the shoulder pieces, matching the instructions given earlier: “It shall have two shoulder pieces attached to its two edges” (Exodus 28:7).

• Obedience in detail—By following God’s blueprint down to “two … corners,” the builders demonstrated that divine directives are not suggestions but commands to be followed precisely (Deuteronomy 5:32-33).

• Symbolism of unity—The attachment joins front and back panels into a single garment, reflecting the unity of God’s people under His covenant (Ephesians 4:3-4).

so it could be fastened

• Stability for service—A secure ephod enabled the high priest to minister confidently without distraction, a picture of the believer standing firm in God’s provision (Ephesians 6:13-14).

• Bearing remembrance—The fastened shoulders later held onyx stones engraved with the names of Israel’s tribes “for remembrance” (Exodus 39:7; 28:12). The priest literally carried God’s people before the Lord, foreshadowing Christ who bears us continually (Hebrews 7:25).

• Fulfillment—Every fastening point testifies that God’s plans are trustworthy and complete; what He instructs, He empowers His servants to accomplish (Philippians 1:6).

summary

Exodus 39:4 records faithful obedience to God’s precise instructions for the ephod’s construction. The shoulder pieces, attached at two corners and firmly fastened, provided a stable, unified garment on which the names of Israel would rest. The verse highlights God’s meticulous care for worship, the necessity of exact obedience, and the foreshadowing of Christ, our great High Priest, who securely bears His people before the Father.
