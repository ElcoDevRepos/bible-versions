For the breastpiece
The breastpiece, also known as the breastplate of judgment, was a crucial part of the high priest's garments. It was worn over the ephod and held the Urim and Thummim, which were used for divine decision-making. This piece symbolized the high priest's role in representing the people before God and carrying their concerns into the Holy of Holies. The breastpiece's design and function are detailed in Exodus 28, emphasizing its importance in the priestly duties.

they made braided chains
The craftsmanship involved in making the breastpiece was intricate and required skilled artisans. The use of braided chains suggests a level of complexity and durability, ensuring that the breastpiece was securely attached to the ephod. This detail highlights the care and precision in following God's instructions for the tabernacle and priestly garments, reflecting the order and beauty of God's creation.

like cords
The comparison to cords indicates the strength and flexibility of the chains. This imagery can be seen as a metaphor for the strength and unity required in the community of Israel, bound together by their covenant with God. The cords also symbolize the connection between the high priest and the divine, as he intercedes on behalf of the people.

of pure gold
Gold, a symbol of purity, divinity, and kingship, was extensively used in the tabernacle and its furnishings. Its use in the breastpiece signifies the holiness and sacredness of the high priest's role. Gold's incorruptible nature points to the eternal and unchanging nature of God. In a typological sense, the use of gold can be seen as a foreshadowing of Christ, who is pure and without blemish, serving as the ultimate high priest.

What Does Exodus 39:15 Mean
For the breastpiece

- The breastpiece was the centerpiece of the high priest’s garments, resting over his heart as he ministered before the LORD (Exodus 28:15, 29).

- Worn only by the high priest, it held the twelve precious stones engraved with the names of Israel’s tribes, testifying that God’s people were perpetually remembered in His presence (Leviticus 8:8).

- Exodus 39:15 reminds us that every detail—down to the chains—was crafted specifically “for the breastpiece,” underscoring God’s concern that His representative carry the nation close to His heart.

they made

- The craftsmen faithfully followed God’s earlier instructions (compare Exodus 28:22 to Exodus 39:15).

- Bezalel, Oholiab, and their helpers were “filled with the Spirit of God, with wisdom, understanding, and ability” to complete this sacred work (Exodus 31:1-6; 36:1).

- Their precise obedience models how God equips His people to accomplish what He commands.

braided chains like cords

- Instead of simple links, the artisans fashioned “braided chains,” giving both strength and beauty (Exodus 28:22-25).

- Braiding three strands into one evokes durability and unity—qualities suitable for bearing the symbolic weight of the tribes (Ecclesiastes 4:12 offers a broader principle about strengthened cords).

- The chains connected the breastpiece securely to the ephod, preventing any shifting as the priest served (Exodus 28:26-28), illustrating that God’s provision for His people is steadfast.

of pure gold

- Gold, the most precious metal in the tabernacle, was reserved for objects nearest God’s presence (Exodus 25:11; 28:14).

- “Pure” signals refinement—free from defilement—mirroring God’s own holiness (Psalm 12:6).

- The splendor of gold also points forward to the heavenly city, whose streets are “pure gold” (Revelation 21:18, 21), assuring believers that earthly worship foreshadows eternal glory.

summary

Exodus 39:15 shows meticulous obedience to God’s design: braided golden chains attached the breastpiece so the high priest could carry Israel’s names securely and beautifully before the LORD. The verse highlights God’s holiness, the value He places on His people, and His provision of Spirit-empowered craftsmanship to accomplish His flawless plan.
