Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Likewise there are to be curtains on the north side
The instructions for the Tabernacle's construction emphasize symmetry and order, reflecting God's nature. The north side mirrors the south, indicating the importance of balance in worship. The north often symbolizes mystery and hiddenness in biblical literature, as seen in Job 37:22 and Isaiah 14:13.

a hundred cubits long
A cubit is approximately 18 inches, making this side about 150 feet long. This length signifies the grandeur and majesty of God's dwelling place among His people. The specific measurements highlight the precision and intentionality in God's design, paralleling the detailed instructions given for Noah's Ark in Genesis 6:15.

with twenty posts and twenty bronze bases
The use of bronze, a durable and strong metal, symbolizes judgment and endurance. The posts provide structure and stability, representing the steadfastness required in faith. The number twenty, often associated with completeness, suggests the sufficiency of God's provision for His people.

and with silver hooks and bands on the posts
Silver in the Bible often symbolizes redemption and purity, as seen in the redemption money in Exodus 30:11-16. The hooks and bands, which hold the curtains, signify the connection and unity within the community of believers, akin to the unity of the body of Christ described in Ephesians 4:16.

Teaching Points
God's Attention to Detail
The specific instructions for the Tabernacle, including the courtyard, reflect God's attention to detail and His desire for order and beauty in worship.

Symbolism of Materials
The use of bronze, silver, and other materials in the Tabernacle's construction symbolizes various aspects of God's character and His relationship with His people. Bronze often represents judgment, while silver can symbolize redemption.

Holiness and Separation
The courtyard served as a boundary, separating the sacred space of the Tabernacle from the common areas, teaching us about the holiness of God and the need for reverence in worship.

Community and Worship
The construction of the Tabernacle involved the entire community of Israel, highlighting the importance of collective participation in worship and service to God.

Foreshadowing of Christ
The Tabernacle and its elements point to Jesus Christ, who is the ultimate fulfillment of God's presence among His people, as He is the true Temple and High Priest.

What Does Exodus 27:11 Mean
Likewise there are to be curtains on the north side

- The phrase “Likewise” points back to the identical instructions already given for the south side (Exodus 27:9), underscoring God’s symmetrical design.

- North often symbolizes stability and security in Scripture (Psalm 48:2; Isaiah 14:13). By assigning the same covering to the north, God shows His protection is complete in every direction.

- Just as the tabernacle faced east (Exodus 26:22), the balanced treatment of north and south demonstrates impartiality—echoed later when Christ’s salvation is offered to Jew and Gentile alike (Acts 10:34-35).

A hundred cubits long

- “A hundred cubits” (about 150 feet/45 meters) matches the south side’s length (Exodus 38:11), revealing perfect proportion.

- Repetition of precise measurements highlights that God is a God of order (1 Corinthians 14:33).

- The sizable span ensures the courtyard could hold the people as they approached the altar, prefiguring the invitation Jesus extends to all who come to Him (Matthew 11:28).

With twenty posts

- Twenty equally spaced posts kept the curtains upright, a vivid picture of steadfast support.

- Posts (or pillars) appear elsewhere as symbols of faithful witness (Galatians 2:9) and of overcoming believers (Revelation 3:12).

- Since the south side also had twenty posts (Exodus 27:10), the number reinforces balance and strength—qualities God desires in His dwelling and in His people (Ephesians 2:21-22).

And twenty bronze bases

- Each post rested on a bronze base, anchoring it securely. Bronze, first introduced in the altar of burnt offering (Exodus 27:1-2), consistently speaks of righteous judgment that can bear fire.

- The bronze serpent lifted up in Numbers 21:8-9 prefigures Christ bearing sin’s judgment (John 3:14-15). Likewise, these bases remind us that holy fellowship stands on the completed judgment carried by our Substitute (Romans 8:3).

With silver hooks and bands on the posts

- Silver in Scripture often typifies redemption (Exodus 30:12-16; 1 Peter 1:18-19). The people’s atonement money was silver; here, silver secures the curtains.

- Hooks joined the fabric to the posts, just as redemption joins sinners to God (Colossians 1:13-14).

- Bands, also silver, added stability, illustrating how Christ not only redeems but also upholds us (Jude 24).

summary

Exodus 27:11 mirrors verse 10, showing God’s insistence on symmetry, stability, and solidity in His earthly dwelling. Every detail—location, measurement, materials—teaches:

• God’s protection surrounds His people on every side.

• Access to His presence is orderly and secure, resting on judgment already borne (bronze) and redemption already paid (silver).

• Those redeemed become upright supports in His house, displaying His glory to a watching world.
