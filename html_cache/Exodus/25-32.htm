Six branches are to extend from the sides of the lampstand—
The lampstand, or menorah, is a significant element in the Tabernacle, symbolizing the light of God and His presence among His people. The design of the lampstand, with its branches, is reminiscent of a tree, possibly symbolizing the Tree of Life from Genesis. The number six, representing the branches, is often associated with humanity, as man was created on the sixth day. The lampstand itself, made of pure gold, signifies purity and divinity, pointing to the holiness required to approach God. The branches extending from the central shaft can be seen as a representation of the unity and diversity within the body of believers, with Christ as the central vine (John 15:5).

three on one side and three on the other.
The symmetry of the lampstand, with three branches on each side, emphasizes balance and order, reflecting God's nature as a God of order (1 Corinthians 14:33). The number three is often associated with divine completeness and perfection, seen in the Trinity. This design may also foreshadow the unity of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. The lampstand's structure, with its central shaft and branches, can be seen as a type of Christ, who is the light of the world (John 8:12) and the source from which all spiritual life and light emanate. The menorah's light was to burn continually, symbolizing the perpetual presence and guidance of God, much like the eternal light of Christ in the lives of believers.

The portable sanctuary that served as the dwelling place of God among the Israelites during their journey through the wilderness.

A sacred object within the Tabernacle, symbolizing the light of God and His presence among His people. It was crafted from pure gold and had a central shaft with three branches on each side.

Skilled artisans appointed by God to oversee the construction of the Tabernacle and its furnishings, including the lampstand.
Teaching Points
Symbolism of Light
The lampstand represents God's presence and guidance. Just as the lampstand illuminated the Tabernacle, believers are called to reflect God's light in a dark world.

Divine Design
The specific instructions for the lampstand highlight the importance of following God's design and purpose in our lives. We are to seek His guidance in all aspects of our lives.

Unity and Diversity
The lampstand's branches, extending from a central shaft, symbolize unity in diversity. In the body of Christ, we are diverse members united in purpose and mission.

Holiness and Purity
Made of pure gold, the lampstand signifies the holiness and purity required in worship. Believers are called to live lives of holiness, set apart for God's purposes.

God's Provision
The lampstand provided continuous light, reminding us of God's constant provision and presence. We can trust Him to meet our needs and guide us daily.

What Does Exodus 25:32 Mean
Six branches are to extend

- God prescribed exact details for the lampstand (Exodus 25:31–40), demonstrating that every element of worship was to follow His revealed pattern (Hebrews 8:5).

- “Six branches” signals completeness and intentional design. Nothing was left to human improvisation; obedience meant crafting all six.

- These extensions allowed additional lamps, ensuring fuller light inside the Holy Place (Exodus 26:35; 27:20–21). In the same way, Jesus calls His people to “let your light shine before men” (Matthew 5:16), reflecting the original, God-given source.

- The number “six” points to man’s work within God’s plan—created on the sixth day (Genesis 1:26–31) and reliant on divine provision for true illumination (John 8:12).

from the sides of the lampstand

- The branches did not stand alone; they rose out of the central shaft. Connection kept every lamp supplied by the same reservoir of pure oil (Leviticus 24:1–4).

- This pictures the unity of God’s people drawing life from one root, as Jesus teaches, “I am the vine; you are the branches” (John 15:5).

- Orientation “from the sides” maintained balance and symmetry, mirroring the orderly character of God (1 Corinthians 14:33).

- The construction was hammered from one piece of gold (Exodus 25:36), showcasing strength through unity—no weak joints, no mixed metals (2 Corinthians 6:14–16).

three on one side

- The first trio of branches symbolically covers half the lampstand’s outreach. Scripture often uses “three” to speak of established witness (Deuteronomy 19:15; 1 John 5:7–8).

- Together these three formed a coordinated testimony to the light of God’s presence—just as Peter, James, and John stood as a threefold witness at the Transfiguration (Matthew 17:1–9).

- Their placement on “one side” underscores that ministry can be diverse yet remain anchored to a single source (Ephesians 4:4–6).

and three on the other

- The matching set produced equilibrium—truth balanced with grace, mercy with justice (Psalm 89:14; John 1:17).

- Equal distribution prevented shadows on either side of the Holy Place, hinting that God’s revelation is impartial and comprehensive (James 1:17; Acts 10:34).

- Six total branches, three per side, together with the central shaft yielded seven lamps (Exodus 25:37), the number of divine perfection later echoed in Zechariah 4:2 and Revelation 4:5.

summary

Exodus 25:32, in commanding six balanced branches to extend from a single lampstand, reveals God’s meticulous care for worship, His desire for unified yet varied witness, and His provision of perfect light to drive out darkness. The pattern points forward to Christ, the true Light, and to His people—firmly connected, evenly balanced, and continually shining by His design.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 32. - Six branches. The representation on the Arch of Titus exactly agrees with this description. It was a peculiarity of the "candlestick," as compared with other candelabra, that all the branches were in the same plane.
