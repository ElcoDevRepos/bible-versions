So they set it aside until morning as Moses had commanded
This phrase highlights the obedience of the Israelites to Moses' instructions, which were ultimately God's commands. The context is the provision of manna in the wilderness, a miraculous sustenance from God. The Israelites were instructed to gather only enough manna for each day, except on the sixth day when they were to gather enough for two days to honor the Sabbath rest (Exodus 16:5). This act of setting aside manna until morning was a test of faith and obedience, reflecting the broader theme of reliance on God's provision. The command to set aside manna for the Sabbath prefigures the concept of rest and trust in God's provision, which is fulfilled in Christ, who offers spiritual rest (Matthew 11:28-30).

and it did not smell or contain any maggots
This phrase underscores the miraculous nature of God's provision. Normally, manna would spoil if kept overnight (Exodus 16:20), but in this instance, it remained fresh, demonstrating God's control over creation and His ability to sustain His people. The absence of spoilage is a sign of divine intervention, emphasizing God's faithfulness and the importance of following His instructions. This preservation of manna can be seen as a type of Christ, the Bread of Life (John 6:35), who is incorruptible and provides eternal sustenance. The miracle of the manna not spoiling on the Sabbath also points to the sanctity and divine blessing of the Sabbath rest, a principle that finds its ultimate fulfillment in the rest offered through faith in Jesus Christ (Hebrews 4:9-10).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Moses
The leader of the Israelites, chosen by God to lead His people out of Egypt. He is the one who relays God's commands to the Israelites regarding the collection and preservation of manna.

2. The Israelites
The people of God who were delivered from slavery in Egypt and are now journeying through the wilderness. They are learning to trust in God's provision.

3. Manna
The miraculous food provided by God to the Israelites in the wilderness. It symbolizes God's provision and care for His people.

4. The Wilderness
The setting where the Israelites are traveling after their exodus from Egypt. It is a place of testing and reliance on God.

5. God's Command
The directive given through Moses to collect manna daily, with specific instructions for the Sabbath, teaching reliance on God's provision.
Teaching Points
Trust in God's Provision
Just as the Israelites had to trust God for their daily manna, we are called to trust God for our daily needs. This trust is built through obedience and faith in His promises.

Obedience to God's Commands
The Israelites' obedience in setting aside the manna as instructed resulted in a miracle. Our obedience to God's Word can lead to blessings and spiritual growth.

Sabbath Rest and Provision
The preservation of manna for the Sabbath highlights the importance of rest and reliance on God. We are reminded to honor the Sabbath and trust God to provide for us even when we rest.

God's Faithfulness
The consistent provision of manna demonstrates God's faithfulness. Reflect on how God has been faithful in your life and how you can respond with gratitude and trust.

What Does Exodus 16:24 Mean
So they set it aside

– This simple act shows the Israelites finally obeying after earlier failures (Exodus 16:20).

– Obedience always begins with a choice to trust that God’s instructions are better than our instincts (Proverbs 3:5-6).

– Gathering an extra portion on the sixth day (Exodus 16:5) required faith that God would provide for the Sabbath.

until morning

– Leaving the manna untouched overnight forced the people to rest instead of labor on the seventh day (Exodus 16:23).

– God’s mercies are “new every morning” (Lamentations 3:23), so He could certainly keep bread fresh for one night.

– Waiting until morning models patient dependence—an echo of Psalm 30:5, where joy comes with the dawn.

as Moses had commanded

– Moses spoke for God; obeying him was obeying the Lord (Exodus 16:28; Deuteronomy 5:33).

– Spiritual authority protects and blesses when received humbly (Hebrews 13:17; John 14:15).

– Their compliance here contrasts with earlier grumbling (Exodus 16:2-3), reminding us that repentance is proven by new actions.

and it did not smell

– Earlier leftovers “smelled” (Exodus 16:20); the difference now highlights a deliberate miracle of preservation.

– God can override natural decay (Isaiah 40:8); His word sustains both bread and people (Matthew 4:4).

– The sweet aroma points ahead to Christ, “a pleasing aroma” to God (2 Corinthians 2:15), whose body saw no corruption (Acts 2:31).

or contain any maggots

– Maggots symbolized judgment for disobedience (Exodus 16:20); their absence now signals divine approval.

– Treasure kept in heaven “where moths and rust do not destroy” (Matthew 6:19-20) mirrors this incorruptibility.

– The preserved manna foreshadows the imperishable life Jesus offers: “I am the living bread…whoever eats of this bread will live forever” (John 6:51).

summary

Exodus 16:24 shows that when God’s people act in obedient faith, He actively preserves their provision. The Israelites trusted enough to set aside the manna, waited through the night, honored the command given through Moses, and witnessed a miraculous absence of decay. The verse reassures believers that obedience invites God’s sustaining power, pointing ultimately to the incorruptible life found in the true Bread from heaven.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 24. - They laid it up. The great bulk of the Israelites obeyed Moses, and laid by a portion (half?) of the manna gathered on the sixth day. On the morning of the seventh, this was found to be perfectly good, and not to have "bred worms" in the night. Either this was a miracle, or the corruption previously noticed (ver. 20) was miraculous.
