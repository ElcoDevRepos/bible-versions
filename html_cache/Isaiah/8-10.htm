Devise a plan, but it will be thwarted;
This phrase reflects the futility of human schemes against God's sovereign will. In the context of Isaiah, the Assyrian empire and other nations plotted against Judah, but their plans were destined to fail because they were not aligned with God's purposes. This echoes Psalm 33:10, which states, "The LORD foils the plans of the nations; he thwarts the purposes of the peoples." Historically, the Assyrians were a dominant force, yet their plans against God's people were ultimately unsuccessful. This serves as a reminder of the divine protection over those who are faithful to God.

state a proposal, but it will not happen.
This reinforces the idea that human intentions, no matter how well-conceived, cannot succeed if they are contrary to God's will. The cultural context of the time was one of political alliances and military strategies, often excluding reliance on God. The phrase underscores the futility of relying on human wisdom and strength. It also connects to Proverbs 19:21, "Many are the plans in a person's heart, but it is the LORD's purpose that prevails." This highlights the supremacy of divine purpose over human ambition.

For God is with us.”
This phrase is a declaration of divine presence and assurance, encapsulated in the name "Immanuel," which means "God with us." It is a central theme in Isaiah, emphasizing that God's presence is the ultimate security for His people. This is prophetically significant as it points to the coming of Jesus Christ, who is called Immanuel in Matthew 1:23, fulfilling the prophecy of God's presence among His people. Theologically, it reassures believers of God's constant presence and support, echoing the promise found in Romans 8:31, "If God is for us, who can be against us?" This assurance is foundational for faith, providing comfort and confidence in the face of opposition.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Isaiah
A major prophet in the Old Testament, Isaiah is the author of the book bearing his name. He prophesied during the reigns of several kings of Judah and is known for his messages of both judgment and hope.

2. Judah
The southern kingdom of Israel, which was often under threat from surrounding nations. Isaiah's prophecies were directed towards Judah, warning them of impending judgment but also offering hope of deliverance.

3. Assyria
A powerful empire during Isaiah's time, often seen as a threat to Judah. The Assyrians were known for their military might and were a constant source of fear for the smaller nations around them.

4. Immanuel
Meaning "God with us," this name is significant in Isaiah's prophecies, symbolizing God's presence and protection over His people.

5. Ahaz
King of Judah during part of Isaiah's ministry. He is known for his lack of faith and reliance on foreign alliances rather than trusting in God.
Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty Over Human Plans
No matter how carefully devised, human plans cannot succeed if they are against God's will. This should encourage believers to seek God's guidance in their planning.

The Assurance of God's Presence
The phrase "For God is with us" is a powerful reminder of God's constant presence and protection. Believers can find comfort and courage in knowing that God is always with them.

Trust in God Over Human Alliances
Like King Ahaz, we may be tempted to rely on human solutions in times of trouble. Isaiah 8:10 teaches us to place our trust in God rather than in human alliances or strategies.

The Fulfillment of Prophecy in Christ
The prophecy of Immanuel finds its ultimate fulfillment in Jesus Christ. This connection encourages believers to see the continuity of God's plan from the Old Testament to the New Testament.

What Does Isaiah 8:10 Mean
Devise a plan, but it will be thwarted

Isaiah starts with a direct challenge to any coalition opposing God’s covenant people: “Devise a plan, but it will be thwarted”.

• Historically, this addressed the Syro-Ephraimite alliance plotting against Judah (Isaiah 7:1–2). No matter how sophisticated their strategy, the LORD guaranteed its collapse.

• Scripture consistently shows human schemes crumbling when they collide with God’s purpose. Psalm 33:10-11 says He “thwarts the plans of the peoples,” while Proverbs 19:21 reminds us, “Many plans are in a man’s heart, but the purpose of the LORD will prevail.”

• This truth offers assurance today: any plot—political, social, or spiritual—formed against believers ultimately meets the same divine veto (Psalm 2:1-4).

state a proposal, but it will not happen

Isaiah reinforces the warning: “state a proposal, but it will not happen.” Words and treaties, however impressive, cannot override God’s decree.

• The prophet pictures diplomats drafting agreements and generals issuing orders, yet heaven has already ruled them null and void (Isaiah 8:9).

• Job 42:2 echoes the lesson: “No purpose of Yours can be thwarted.” Likewise, Lamentations 3:37 asks, “Who can speak and have it happen if the Lord has not decreed it?”

• When circumstances appear to favor evil, this line calls believers to rest in God’s supremacy; even the proudest manifesto disintegrates under His sovereignty (Isaiah 14:27).

For God is with us

The verse ends with the grounding reality: “For God is with us.” The Hebrew name Emmanuel, first introduced in Isaiah 7:14, surfaces again to seal the promise.

• God’s presence safeguards His people. In Hezekiah’s day, that presence shattered Assyria’s siege (2 Kings 19:32-35).

• The New Testament amplifies the theme: Matthew 1:23 identifies Jesus as “God with us,” showing the ultimate fulfillment in Christ’s incarnation. Romans 8:31 asks, “If God is for us, who can be against us?”—an unbroken thread from Isaiah’s prophecy to the church.

• Practically, “God with us” means:

– We face opposition with courage, not panic (Isaiah 41:10).

– We cling to holiness, knowing the Holy One dwells among us (2 Corinthians 6:16).

– We anticipate final victory, for His presence is perpetual (Matthew 28:20).

summary

Isaiah 8:10 dismantles every threat in three strokes: plots fail, proclamations fizzle, because God Himself stands with His people. History, prophecy, and personal experience unite to prove that any opposition to God’s will is doomed, while those who trust Emmanuel live under an unassailable promise of protection and triumph.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 10. - Take counsel together; literally, devise a device; i.e. form some plan, even the cleverest possible, against God's people, and the result will be utter failure. It shall not stand (comp. Isaiah 7:7). For God is with us. In the Hebrew, ki 'immanu-El, "for with us (is) God" words declarative of the true meaning of the name which God had made a sign to his people (Isaiah 7:14). It was his being "with them" that could alone save them from their enemies.
