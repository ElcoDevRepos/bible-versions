The people I formed for Myself
This phrase emphasizes God's sovereign act of creation and selection. In the context of Isaiah, it refers to the nation of Israel, whom God chose as His covenant people (Deuteronomy 7:6). The idea of forming suggests intentionality and purpose, echoing Genesis 2:7 where God forms man from the dust. This formation is not just physical but spiritual, indicating a people set apart for God's purposes. Historically, Israel's identity was deeply tied to being God's chosen people, distinct from surrounding nations. This concept is also seen in the New Testament, where believers are described as a new creation in Christ (2 Corinthians 5:17), indicating a spiritual formation for God's purposes.

will declare My praise
The purpose of God's formation of His people is ultimately for His glory. Declaring praise is an act of worship and acknowledgment of God's sovereignty and goodness. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, praise was often expressed through song, sacrifice, and obedience to God's commandments. This declaration is not just verbal but is demonstrated through a lifestyle that honors God. The prophetic nature of this phrase points to a future where all nations will recognize and worship God, as seen in Revelation 7:9-10. It also foreshadows the role of the Church, which is called to proclaim the excellencies of God (1 Peter 2:9). The ultimate fulfillment of this declaration is found in Jesus Christ, who perfectly glorified the Father and through whom all creation will one day acknowledge God's praise (Philippians 2:10-11).

Teaching Points
Purposeful Creation
God intentionally formed His people to declare His praise. This underscores the purpose and identity of believers as worshippers.

Identity in God
Understanding our identity as God's chosen people helps us live out our calling to reflect His glory in the world.

Praise as a Lifestyle
Declaring God's praise is not limited to worship services but is a daily expression of gratitude and acknowledgment of His sovereignty.

Redemption and Restoration
Even in times of exile or hardship, God's purpose for His people remains. He redeems and restores us to fulfill our calling.

Community of Praise
As a collective body, the Church is called to be a community that consistently declares God's praise, impacting the world around us.

What Does Isaiah 43:21 Mean
The people

• God names a distinct group—“The people.” In context, He is speaking of Israel, the nation He chose (Exodus 19:5-6).

• Yet Scripture widens the circle: “Blessed is the nation whose God is the LORD, the people He has chosen as His inheritance” (Psalm 33:12). In Christ, Gentile believers are grafted in, becoming “a chosen people, a royal priesthood” (1 Peter 2:9).

• The phrase reminds us that identity begins with belonging to God, not with human merit or achievement (Isaiah 43:1).

I formed

• The verb is intentional. We are not random; we are God’s workmanship (Ephesians 2:10).

• He formed Israel in the Exodus, forging a nation out of slavery. He forms the church through the new birth, shaping lives by the Spirit (2 Corinthians 5:17).

• “Know that the LORD is God. It is He who made us, and we are His” (Psalm 100:3). Every heartbeat is a reminder of the Potter’s skilled hands.

for Myself

• Purpose is personal. God forms a people “for His own possession” (Titus 2:14).

• Relationship comes first: “You are Mine!” (Isaiah 43:1). Ownership means security, value, and accountability.

• He reserves the right to our loyalty, time, bodies, talents—“You were bought at a price; therefore glorify God with your body” (1 Corinthians 6:20).

will declare

• God’s people have a clear assignment: proclamation. Not maybe, but will.

• This includes lips: “He put a new song in my mouth… many will see and fear” (Psalm 40:3).

• It includes lives: “Created in Christ Jesus to do good works” (Ephesians 2:10). Service, compassion, integrity—all speak.

• The aim is outward-facing: “Declare His glory among the nations” (Psalm 96:3).

My praise

• God is the sole object of the praise we declare. “Let them give glory to the LORD and declare His praise in the islands” (Isaiah 42:12).

• Praise is both doxology and mission. The crescendo reaches its fullness in heaven: “By Your blood You purchased for God those from every tribe… they sang a new song” (Revelation 5:9-10).

• Daily worship offers a foretaste: “Offer your bodies as living sacrifices… your spiritual service of worship” (Romans 12:1).

summary

Isaiah 43:21 shows God claiming a people, crafting them deliberately, binding them to Himself, and sending them out as heralds of His glory. We exist because He formed us; we belong because He wants us; we live to declare His praise—now and forever.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 21. - This people have I formed for myself (see above, ver. 7, and comp. Proverbs 16:4). They shall show forth my praise; i.e. their restoration to their own land shall cause them to glorify me both with songs of praise (for the fulfilment, see Ezra 3:9-11; Nehemiah 12:27; and the post-Captivity psalms), and also by a life in accordance with my laws.
