He who testifies to these things says
This phrase refers to Jesus Christ, who is the faithful witness (Revelation 1:5). Throughout the Book of Revelation, Jesus is depicted as the one who reveals divine truth and testifies to the events that are to come. The role of a witness is significant in biblical terms, as it underscores the reliability and authority of the message being conveyed. In the context of Revelation, Jesus' testimony is a guarantee of the truthfulness of the prophetic visions John has recorded.

“Yes, I am coming soon.”
This declaration emphasizes the imminence of Christ's return. The phrase "coming soon" has been a source of hope and anticipation for Christians throughout history. It reflects the eschatological promise that Jesus will return to establish His kingdom and bring about the fulfillment of God's plan. The urgency of this promise is echoed in other New Testament passages, such as Matthew 24:30-31 and 1 Thessalonians 4:16-17, which describe the second coming of Christ. The expectation of His return serves as a call for believers to remain faithful and vigilant.

Amen.
The term "Amen" is a Hebrew word meaning "truly" or "so be it." It is used throughout the Bible to affirm the truthfulness of a statement or to express agreement with a prayer or declaration. In this context, it serves as a confirmation of the certainty of Christ's promise to return. The use of "Amen" here reflects the confidence and assurance that believers have in the fulfillment of God's promises.

Come, Lord Jesus!
This is a prayerful response from the Apostle John, expressing the longing and desire for Christ's return. It echoes the Aramaic expression "Maranatha," found in 1 Corinthians 16:22, which also means "Come, Lord." This plea captures the heart of Christian eschatological hope, as believers eagerly anticipate the day when Jesus will return to bring justice, peace, and restoration to the world. The invocation of Jesus' return is a central theme in Christian worship and theology, underscoring the hope and expectation that define the Christian faith.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jesus Christ
The central figure in this verse, Jesus is the one who testifies and promises His imminent return. His role as the coming King and Judge is emphasized.

2. John the Apostle
The author of the Book of Revelation, who records the vision and the words of Jesus. He responds with a heartfelt "Amen" and a plea for Jesus' return.

3. Patmos
The island where John received the Revelation. It serves as the backdrop for the entire book, including this final promise.

4. The Church
The collective body of believers who eagerly await the return of Christ, as expressed in John's response.

5. The Second Coming
The anticipated event when Jesus will return to fulfill His promises, bring judgment, and establish His eternal kingdom.
Teaching Points
The Certainty of Christ's Return
Jesus' promise, "I am coming soon," assures believers of the certainty and imminence of His return. This should inspire hope and perseverance in the faith.

The Response of the Believer
John's "Amen. Come, Lord Jesus!" reflects the heart posture of a believer who longs for Christ's return. It encourages us to live with an eager expectation and readiness.

Living in Light of Eternity
The promise of Jesus' return should influence our daily lives, prompting us to live righteously and prioritize eternal values over temporal concerns.

Encouragement in Trials
The assurance of Christ's return provides comfort and strength to endure hardships, knowing that ultimate justice and restoration are coming.

Evangelistic Urgency
The imminence of Jesus' return should motivate believers to share the gospel with urgency, as the time for repentance is limited.

What Does Revelation 22:20 Mean
He who testifies to these things

- John, the inspired author, has faithfully recorded every vision and word Jesus entrusted to him (Revelation 1:2, 19).

- The “He” here is Jesus Himself, affirming that all the prophecies just shared are reliable (John 3:33; John 5:31–32).

- Because the risen Christ testifies, we have unshakable confidence that the judgments, blessings, and eternal realities described in Revelation will unfold exactly as written (Revelation 19:9).

“Yes, I am coming soon.”

- Jesus echoes His earlier promises (Revelation 3:11; John 14:3), underscoring that His return is imminent from heaven’s perspective.

- “Soon” encourages readiness, not date-setting; God measures time differently (2 Peter 3:8–9).

- The certainty of His coming:

• Guarantees final justice (Acts 17:31).

• Sustains believers amid suffering (James 5:7–8).

• Motivates holy living and evangelism (1 John 3:2–3; 2 Corinthians 5:10–11).

Amen

- A declaration of absolute trust: “So be it.”

- Echoes the congregational response to God’s promises throughout Scripture (Deuteronomy 27:15–26; 2 Corinthians 1:20).

- The church aligns its heart with Christ’s sure word, sealing it with unquestioning agreement.

Come, Lord Jesus!

- This is the Spirit-inspired cry of every redeemed heart (Romans 8:23; Philippians 3:20).

- It reflects:

• Longing for full fellowship with Christ (John 17:24).

• Desire for the end of sin, sorrow, and death (Revelation 21:4).

• Expectation of vindication for God’s people and judgment on evil (2 Thessalonians 1:6–10).

- The prayer is confident, not anxious; it rests in the promise that the same Jesus who ascended will return in like manner (Acts 1:11).

summary

Revelation 22:20 closes Scripture with Jesus’ personal word of guarantee and the church’s eager response. The One who spoke every prophecy vouches for its truth, assures His imminent return, invites our wholehearted “Amen,” and stirs a yearning plea for His appearing. Until that day, believers stand watchful, hopeful, and obedient—anchored in the certainty that the Lord who testifies is indeed coming soon.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(20) He which testifieth these things . . .--Better, He saith, who testifieth these things, Yea, I am coming quickly. We have here the final witness; it is in the words of the faithful and true witness Himself. It is the answer to the repeated cry, "Come;" it is the warning to those who forget Him; it is introduced with the emphatic yea! "Yea, I am coming quickly." The answer breaks forth in prayer from the prophet's lips--Amen. (We must omit "even so") Come, Lord Jesus. The prophet is the mouthpiece of the Church; his desire is one with the desire of all who love Christ's appearing.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 20. - He which testifieth these things saith - viz, the Lord Jesus, as in ver. 16 - Surely I come quickly; yea, I come quickly. As the book opens, so it closes with this promise. This is the anchor and stay of the faithful, the sound of an alarm and a warning cry to the wicked. Amen. Even so, come, Lord Jesus. Omit "even so." Thus in calm and patient hope the apostle answers his Lord. So the writer who delivers the message is the first to proclaim his belief in what is herein contained.
