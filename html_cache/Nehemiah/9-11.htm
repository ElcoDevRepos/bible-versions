You divided the sea before them
This phrase refers to the miraculous event of the parting of the Red Sea, as recorded in Exodus 14:21-22. This event is a pivotal moment in Israel's history, symbolizing God's deliverance and power. The division of the sea is a demonstration of God's sovereignty over creation, fulfilling His promise to deliver Israel from Egyptian bondage. The act of dividing the sea is also a type of baptism, as mentioned in 1 Corinthians 10:1-2, where Paul describes the Israelites' passage through the sea as a form of baptism into Moses, prefiguring Christian baptism into Christ.

and they crossed through it on dry ground
The crossing on dry ground emphasizes the completeness of God's miracle, ensuring the safety and ease of passage for the Israelites. This detail underscores God's provision and care, contrasting with the natural impossibility of such an event. The dry ground signifies a path made by God, a theme echoed in Isaiah 43:16, where God is described as making a way through the sea. This miraculous provision is a testament to God's faithfulness and His ability to make a way where there seems to be none.

You hurled their pursuers into the depths
This phrase highlights God's judgment against the Egyptians, who pursued the Israelites with the intent to enslave them again. The "depths" refer to the waters of the Red Sea returning to their place, as described in Exodus 14:27-28. This act of divine retribution serves as a warning against opposing God's purposes and people. It also fulfills God's promise to fight for Israel, as stated in Exodus 14:14, and demonstrates His role as a divine warrior who protects His covenant people.

like a stone into raging waters
The imagery of a stone sinking into raging waters conveys the sudden and complete destruction of the Egyptian forces. This simile emphasizes the powerlessness of the Egyptians against God's might. The "raging waters" symbolize chaos and judgment, often associated with God's wrath in biblical literature, as seen in Psalm 69:14-15. This imagery also foreshadows the ultimate defeat of all God's enemies, as depicted in Revelation 20:10, where evil is cast into the lake of fire. The phrase serves as a reminder of God's ultimate authority and the futility of resisting His will.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty and Power
God is sovereign over all creation, including the natural elements. His power is unmatched, and He can use it to deliver His people from seemingly impossible situations.

Faith in God's Deliverance
The Israelites' crossing of the Red Sea is a powerful reminder of the importance of faith. Trusting in God's promises and His ability to deliver us is crucial in our spiritual journey.

God's Faithfulness in History
Reflecting on God's past acts of deliverance, as Nehemiah does, strengthens our faith and reminds us of His unchanging nature. He is the same God today as He was then.

The Defeat of Our Enemies
Just as God defeated the Egyptians, He is capable of overcoming the spiritual enemies we face. We can trust Him to fight our battles and bring us to victory.

The Importance of Remembrance
Recounting God's past faithfulness, as seen in Nehemiah's prayer, is vital for maintaining a strong faith. It encourages us to trust Him in our present circumstances.

What Does Nehemiah 9:11 Mean
You divided the sea before them

• The verse recalls the historical moment recorded in Exodus 14:21 – “Then Moses stretched out his hand over the sea, and the LORD drove the sea back with a strong east wind and turned it into dry land.”

• God’s action is deliberate and personal—“You divided”—emphasizing His direct involvement, not a natural accident.

• This miracle reveals His sovereignty over creation (Psalm 74:13) and His covenant faithfulness to deliver His people (Genesis 15:13-14; Exodus 3:7-8).

• It invites us to trust Him when obstacles loom as immovable as a sea.

They crossed through it on dry ground

• Exodus 14:22 says, “The Israelites went through the sea on dry ground, with walls of water on their right and on their left.”

• “Dry ground” underscores the completeness of the miracle; God doesn’t provide a muddy escape but a firm, safe path (Isaiah 43:16).

• The detail highlights His care for every step His people take (Psalm 77:19) and reassures believers that His provision is thorough, not partial.

• Crossing affirms that deliverance requires obedient movement—Israel had to walk forward (Exodus 14:15).

You hurled their pursuers into the depths

• Exodus 15:4-5 celebrates, “Pharaoh’s chariots and his army He has thrown into the sea… the floods covered them.”

• God is both Savior and Judge; the same path that rescued Israel became the place of judgment for Egypt (Psalm 136:15).

• “Hurled” conveys forceful, decisive action, reminding us that evil does not merely fade away—it is actively overthrown by God (Psalm 9:5-6).

• This demonstrates His commitment to protect His people from those who persist in rebellion against Him.

Like a stone into raging waters

• The imagery echoes Exodus 15:10—“They sank like lead in the mighty waters.”

• A stone sinks instantly and irreversibly, showing the finality of God’s judgment (Jeremiah 51:63-64).

• Raging waters picture His power unleashed; no power of man can resist Him (Job 11:10).

• For believers, the image offers comfort: threats that seem overwhelming to us are effortlessly subdued by the Lord.

summary

Nehemiah 9:11 celebrates a literal, historical rescue at the Red Sea to affirm God’s unchanging character. He personally divides the impossible, provides a sure path, protects His people, and decisively defeats their enemies. Remembering this event fuels confident trust that the same God still makes a way, still judges evil, and still keeps every promise.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 11. - As a stone. This phrase is taken from the "song of Moses" (Exodus 15:5). The composer of the address has also in his mind Exodus 15:10. The epithet given to the "waters" is not, however, the same, as might appear from the A.V.
