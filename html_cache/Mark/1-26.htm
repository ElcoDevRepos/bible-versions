Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
At this,
This phrase indicates a response to a preceding action or command. In the context of Mark 1, Jesus has just rebuked the unclean spirit, demonstrating His authority over demonic forces. This moment is pivotal, showcasing the immediate power of Jesus' words.

the unclean spirit
Unclean spirits, or demons, are spiritual beings opposed to God, often associated with impurity and evil. In Jewish culture, they were believed to cause physical and mental afflictions. The presence of an unclean spirit highlights the spiritual warfare theme prevalent in the Gospels, where Jesus confronts and overcomes evil.

threw the man into convulsions
The convulsions indicate the violent reaction of the demon as it is forced to leave. This physical manifestation underscores the struggle between the spiritual and physical realms. It also reflects the destructive nature of demonic possession, which contrasts with the peace and healing Jesus brings.

and came out
The departure of the unclean spirit signifies Jesus' victory and authority over demonic forces. This act fulfills prophecies about the Messiah's power to liberate captives (Isaiah 61:1). It also prefigures the ultimate defeat of evil through Jesus' death and resurrection.

with a loud shriek
The loud shriek is a final act of defiance and recognition of Jesus' authority. It serves as a public acknowledgment of Jesus' power, reinforcing His divine nature. This reaction is consistent with other Gospel accounts where demons recognize and fear Jesus (Mark 3:11, Luke 4:41).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jesus
- The central figure in the Gospel of Mark, Jesus is performing an exorcism in this passage, demonstrating His authority over unclean spirits.

2. Unclean Spirit
- A demonic entity possessing a man, representing the forces of evil that Jesus confronts and overcomes.

3. The Man
- An unnamed individual possessed by the unclean spirit, who experiences convulsions as the spirit is cast out.

4. Capernaum
- The location where this event takes place, a town in Galilee where Jesus performed many miracles and taught in the synagogue.
Teaching Points
Authority of Jesus
Jesus' command over the unclean spirit demonstrates His divine authority. Believers can trust in Jesus' power over all spiritual forces.

Reality of Spiritual Warfare
The presence of unclean spirits in the world reminds us of the ongoing spiritual battle. Christians are called to be vigilant and equipped with the armor of God.

Deliverance and Freedom
Jesus' deliverance of the possessed man illustrates His mission to set captives free. Believers can find hope and freedom in Christ from any form of bondage.

Faith in Action
The response of the unclean spirit to Jesus' command shows the power of faith in action. Christians are encouraged to exercise their faith in Jesus' name in confronting evil.

Witness to Others
The public nature of this miracle serves as a witness to Jesus' authority and power. Believers are called to share their testimonies of Jesus' work in their lives.

What Does Mark 1:26 Mean
At this

– The verse connects to Jesus’ command in Mark 1:25, “Be silent, and come out of him!”.

– Immediately, spiritual forces respond to Jesus’ authority; there is no delay or debate.

– Similar instant obedience appears in Matthew 8:27 when even the winds and sea obey Him.

the unclean spirit

– Scripture consistently labels demons as “unclean” (Matthew 10:1; Acts 8:7), underscoring their opposition to God’s holiness.

– The reality of an actual demon—not mere illness—is affirmed by Luke 4:33-36, where the same event is recorded.

– Demonic presence in the synagogue shows evil can lurk even among outwardly religious settings (James 2:19).

threw the man into convulsions

– The violent reaction highlights the destructive intent of demons (John 10:10).

– Jesus’ authority overpowers evil but does not guarantee a gentle exit; conflict often precedes deliverance (Ephesians 6:12).

– Yet, unlike false exorcists (Acts 19:13-16), Jesus’ command keeps the man safe; Luke 4:35 notes “without injuring him.”

came out

– The deliverance is complete; the demon’s grip is broken (Colossians 1:13).

– No ritual, formula, or aid is needed—only Christ’s word (Psalm 33:9).

– This foreshadows the ultimate defeat of Satan promised in Revelation 20:10.

with a loud shriek

– The scream exposes demonic rage and unwilling submission (Mark 5:7).

– Evil may protest, but it is powerless against the Son of God (1 John 3:8).

– The noise also serves as public proof that a real departure occurred, silencing skepticism (Luke 11:20).

summary

Mark 1:26 shows Jesus’ sovereign power over demons: His spoken command brings immediate, visible deliverance, illustrating that no unclean spirit can withstand His authority. The episode assures believers that the same Lord who freed this man still rescues today, decisively transferring lives from darkness to light.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 26. - And when the unclean spirit had torn him; and cried with a loud voice, he came out of him (καὶ σπαράξαν αὐτὸν). The Greek word σπαράσσω may be rendered in the passive to be convulsed. It is so used by medical writers, as Galen. It could hardly here mean physically "laceration," for St. Luke (Luke 4:35) is careful to say that "when the devil had thrown him down in the midst, he came out of him, having done him no hurt." At all events, the expression indicates the close union of the evil spirit with the possessed man's consciousness and with his physical frame. And the manner in which he departed showed his malignity, as though, being compelled by the supreme authority of Christ to leave the man, he would injure him as far as he was able to do so. But the power of Christ prevented him from doing any real injury. And all this was done

(1) that there might be clear evidence that the man was actually possessed by the evil spirit;

(2) that the anger and malice of the evil spirit might be shown; and

(3) that it might be manifest that the unclean spirit came out, not of his own accord, but constrained and vanquished by Christ. We may observe also that the power of Christ restrained him from the use of any articulate words. While he was in possession he used the possessed man's organs of speech; but when he came out there was no articulate speech - it was nothing but a cry.
