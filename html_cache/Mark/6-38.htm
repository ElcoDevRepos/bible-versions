Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
“Go and see how many loaves you have,” He told them.
In this passage, Jesus instructs His disciples to assess their resources before performing the miracle of feeding the five thousand. This directive emphasizes the importance of recognizing and utilizing what is available, even when it seems insufficient. The act of going and seeing reflects a practical approach to faith, where human effort and divine intervention work together. This mirrors the Old Testament principle seen in 2 Kings 4:2, where Elisha asks the widow what she has in her house before performing a miracle. The loaves mentioned here are likely small barley loaves, common in the diet of the poor in first-century Palestine, as indicated in John 6:9. This setting underscores the humble beginnings of the miracle.

And after checking, they said, “Five—and two fish.”
The disciples report back with five loaves and two fish, highlighting the meager resources available to feed such a large crowd. This scarcity sets the stage for Jesus to demonstrate His divine power and provision, reminiscent of God's provision of manna in the wilderness (Exodus 16). The number five often symbolizes grace in biblical numerology, while fish were a staple food in Galilee, caught in the Sea of Galilee, a significant location in Jesus' ministry. The two fish may also symbolize the dual nature of Christ's mission to both Jews and Gentiles. This miracle prefigures the Last Supper and the Eucharist, where Jesus provides spiritual sustenance, and it foreshadows the abundance of God's kingdom, as seen in the eschatological banquet described in Isaiah 25:6.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jesus
- The central figure in this passage, Jesus is the one who asks the disciples to assess their resources. His question sets the stage for the miracle of feeding the five thousand.

2. The Disciples
- They are tasked by Jesus to find out how many loaves and fish are available. Their role is crucial as they are the ones who will distribute the food to the crowd.

3. The Crowd
- A large group of people who have followed Jesus and are in need of physical sustenance. Their presence highlights the need for a miracle.

4. The Loaves and Fish
- The seemingly insufficient resources that Jesus uses to perform a miracle. They symbolize the small offerings that, when given to God, can be multiplied.

5. The Wilderness
- The setting of this event, which emphasizes the isolation and the need for divine provision, reminiscent of the Israelites' experience in the desert.
Teaching Points
God's Provision in Scarcity
Even when resources seem insufficient, God can provide abundantly. Trust in His ability to multiply what we offer.

Faith in Action
Jesus asks the disciples to "Go and see," prompting them to take action. Our faith should lead us to actively seek and trust God's provision.

The Power of Small Offerings
The loaves and fish were small, yet they fed thousands. God values and uses our small offerings when given in faith.

Dependence on Divine Power
The miracle underscores the importance of relying on God's power rather than our own resources or understanding.

Community and Sharing
The distribution of food to the crowd highlights the importance of community and sharing what we have with others.

What Does Mark 6:38 Mean
Go and see

Jesus begins with action. “Go” moves the disciples from passive concern to practical obedience. “See” demands honest assessment rather than guesswork.

• Mark 6:37 records their earlier objection, yet Christ’s directive overrides their doubt.

• John 6:6 notes that He already knew what He would do, proving this step was for their benefit, not His.

• Exodus 4:2 shows the same pattern with Moses—God starts by asking what is already in hand.

How many loaves you have

The focus narrows to specifics. Counting resources forces the disciples to confront their insufficiency.

• Matthew 15:34—Jesus repeats the question before feeding the four-thousand, underscoring the principle.

• Luke 14:28 encourages counting the cost; stewardship begins with inventory.

• 2 Kings 4:2—Elisha asks the widow what she possesses before the miracle of oil.

He told them

Authority is explicit. The disciples act because Jesus speaks, not because the numbers make sense.

• Mark 1:22 highlights that His word carries unmatched authority.

• John 2:5—Mary’s advice at Cana echoes the same trust: “Do whatever He tells you.”

And after checking

Obedience is immediate and practical. They do not debate; they investigate.

• Luke 5:5 shows Peter obeying “at Your word,” leading to a miraculous catch.

• James 1:22 warns against hearing without doing; the disciples model doing.

Five—and two fish

The tally confirms inadequacy from a human standpoint, setting the stage for divine sufficiency.

• John 6:9 identifies the boy who offers this small lunch, illustrating childlike generosity.

• 2 Kings 4:42-44—twenty loaves feed a hundred, foreshadowing Christ’s far greater multiplication.

• 1 Corinthians 1:27 reveals God’s delight in using what the world deems weak to display His power.

summary

Mark 6:38 showcases a pattern: Christ commands, His followers assess honestly, and their meager supply becomes the raw material for a miracle. By moving in obedience, acknowledging limited resources, and submitting to His authority, the disciples witness provision far beyond expectation. The verse invites every believer to place whatever is on hand—however small—into the Lord’s capable hands, confident that He still multiplies loaves and fish today.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 38. - Five (loaves), and two fishes. St. John tells us (John 6:9) that the loaves were of barley, and that the fishes were small (ὀψάρια); St. Mark says δύο ἰχθύας. Barley bread was considered an inferior and homely kind of food, very inferior to bread made of wheat flour. The comparative value of the two kinds of bread is given in Revelation 6:6. "A measure of wheat for a penny, and three measures of barley for a penny." The psalmist alludes to the greater excellence of wheat flour: "He would have fed them also with the finest wheat flour" (Psalm 81:16).
