Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
His clothes became radiantly white
This phrase describes the transfiguration of Jesus, a pivotal moment where His divine nature is revealed to Peter, James, and John. The radiance of His clothes symbolizes purity and holiness, reflecting the glory of God. In biblical context, white garments often represent righteousness and divine presence (Revelation 3:5, Daniel 7:9). This transformation signifies Jesus' divine authority and foreshadows His resurrection glory.

brighter than any launderer on earth could bleach them
The comparison to earthly laundering emphasizes the supernatural aspect of the transformation. No human effort could achieve such purity and brilliance, highlighting the divine intervention. This imagery connects to the Old Testament, where God's presence is often associated with overwhelming brightness (Exodus 34:29-35, Ezekiel 1:4, 27-28). It underscores the theme of Jesus as the light of the world (John 8:12), revealing His identity as the Son of God and the fulfillment of messianic prophecies.

Teaching Points
Divine Revelation
The Transfiguration is a powerful revelation of Jesus' divine nature, reminding us of His authority and glory.

Purity and Holiness
The radiantly white clothing symbolizes purity and holiness, calling believers to pursue a life of righteousness.

Witness to Glory
Like Peter, James, and John, we are called to be witnesses to the glory of Christ in our lives and share it with others.

Fulfillment of the Law and Prophets
Jesus' appearance with Moses and Elijah signifies the fulfillment of the Law and the Prophets, affirming the continuity of God's redemptive plan.

Transformation in Christ
Just as Jesus was transfigured, believers are called to be transformed by the renewing of their minds, reflecting Christ's glory in their lives.

What Does Mark 9:3 Mean
His clothes became

– Mark notes a visible, physical change in Jesus’ garments, grounding the event in history, not symbolism (Mark 9:2).

– Similar moments of divine radiance appear when Moses descends Sinai with a shining face (Exodus 34:29-35) and when Stephen’s face glows before the council (Acts 6:15).

– The clothing itself shares in the glory, showing that even matter responds to Christ’s unveiled majesty (Luke 9:29).

Radiantly white

– “His clothes became radiantly white—whiter than any launderer on earth could bleach them.” – Mark 9:3

– White in Scripture pictures absolute purity (Daniel 7:9; Revelation 1:14).

– The brightness is not reflected light but emitted glory; Jesus is the source, echoing “I am the light of the world” (John 8:12).

– Angels at the tomb also appear in dazzling white (Matthew 28:3), further tying this color to heavenly presence.

Brighter than any launderer on earth could bleach them

– Mark uses an everyday comparison—laundering—to underline that no human skill can match the holiness revealed here (Isaiah 64:6).

– The phrase recalls Isaiah 1:18, where only the LORD can make scarlet sins “white as snow,” and Malachi 3:2, where the Messiah is “like launderer’s soap.”

– Psalm 104:1-2 says God “wraps Himself in light,” and that same divine light now surrounds Jesus, confirming His deity.

– Revelation 7:14 links washed, white robes to those cleansed by the Lamb’s blood, foreshadowing the cleansing work Christ will accomplish.

summary

Mark 9:3 portrays a literal, historical burst of divine glory. Jesus’ garments change, displaying His majesty; the radiant whiteness proclaims His sinless purity; the surpassing brightness proves His glory dwarfs all human effort. Together these details affirm that Jesus is the divine Son whose holiness both overwhelms and cleanses, inviting faith in the One who is Light itself.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(3) Shining.--Better, perhaps, glittering.

Exceeding white as snow.--The two last words are wanting in the best MSS. The comparison of the bright raiment with clothes that had just passed through the fuller's or bleacher's hands, is, in its homely vividness, peculiar to St. Mark.
