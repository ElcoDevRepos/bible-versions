Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Immediately the girl got up and began to walk around
This phrase highlights the immediacy and completeness of Jesus' miracle. The word "immediately" underscores the power and authority of Jesus over life and death, as seen in other instances such as the healing of the paralytic (Mark 2:12). The girl's ability to "walk around" signifies not just a return to life but a restoration to full health, contrasting with the gradual healings sometimes seen in other biblical accounts. This miracle takes place in the region of Galilee, a setting for many of Jesus' teachings and miracles, emphasizing His ministry's reach and impact. The act of walking also symbolizes new life and resurrection, prefiguring Jesus' own resurrection and the promise of eternal life for believers.

(she was twelve years old)
The mention of the girl's age is significant. In Jewish culture, twelve is the age of transition from childhood to adulthood, marking the beginning of a girl's eligibility for marriage and her responsibilities under the Law. This detail may also connect to the woman with the issue of blood, who suffered for twelve years (Mark 5:25), suggesting a thematic link between the two stories. Both accounts demonstrate Jesus' compassion and power to restore life and purity, fulfilling the messianic prophecies of healing and restoration found in Isaiah 61:1-3.

And at once they were utterly astounded
The reaction of those present reflects the awe and wonder that often accompany divine encounters. The phrase "utterly astounded" indicates a profound recognition of Jesus' divine authority and the miraculous nature of the event. This response is consistent with other reactions to Jesus' miracles, such as the calming of the storm (Mark 4:41) and the feeding of the five thousand (Mark 6:51-52). The astonishment of the witnesses serves to authenticate the miracle and reinforce the identity of Jesus as the Messiah, the Son of God, who fulfills the prophecies and expectations of the Old Testament.

What Does Mark 5:42 Mean
Immediately

- “Immediately the girl got up…” (Mark 5:42).

- The Gospel of Mark repeatedly highlights the instant nature of Jesus’ miracles (see Mark 1:31; 1:42; 10:52).

- Instant results demonstrate His divine authority—no rituals, no delays, just a spoken command (Mark 5:41).

- Similar immediacy appears when Peter’s mother-in-law is healed “immediately” (Mark 1:31) and the leper is cleansed “immediately” (Mark 1:42).

- Each swift act affirms that Christ’s power over sickness—and even death—is absolute and unquestionable.

The girl got up

- Rising from death shows Jesus’ authority over the final enemy (1 Corinthians 15:26).

- Other resurrection accounts reinforce this truth: the widow’s son at Nain (Luke 7:14-15), Jairus’s daughter here, and Lazarus (John 11:43-44).

- These events foreshadow Christ’s own resurrection, guaranteeing hope for all who believe (John 14:19).

And began to walk around

- Walking testifies to complete restoration, not mere resuscitation.

- Comparable scenes of full mobility follow miracles in Mark 2:12 (the paralytic “got up, picked up his mat, and walked out in full view of them all”) and Acts 3:7-8 (the lame man “jumped to his feet and began to walk”).

- Wholeness is a hallmark of divine healing; Jesus finishes what He starts (Philippians 1:6).

(She was twelve years old)

- A precise detail, pointing to eyewitness reliability (Luke 1:3; 2 Peter 1:16).

- Twelve links her to the woman healed of a twelve-year hemorrhage earlier in the chapter (Mark 5:25)—two intertwined stories showing Jesus’ power over chronic suffering and sudden death alike.

- Age twelve marked the threshold of maturity in Jewish life (cf. Luke 2:42, Jesus at the temple), underscoring the significance of her restoration to family and community.

And at once they were utterly astounded

- Witnesses respond with overwhelming amazement, a frequent reaction to Jesus’ works (Mark 1:27; 2:12; Luke 5:26).

- Such astonishment underscores that no natural explanation suffices; the only reasonable conclusion is God’s direct intervention (Mark 4:41).

- For Jairus, his household, and the gathered mourners, the miracle turns despair into wonder, revealing Christ as the Lord of life (John 11:25).

summary

Mark 5:42 presents a real, historical event in which Jesus instantly raises a twelve-year-old girl, restores her completely, and leaves every onlooker in speechless awe. Each phrase in the verse showcases His immediate power, total healing, attention to detail, and the profound impact on all who witness His authority over death. The passage invites confident faith that the same Lord still holds absolute power to give life—physically and eternally—to all who trust Him.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(42) She was of the age of twelve years.--St. Mark gives the age at the end of the narrative, St. Luke at the beginning, St. Matthew not at all; a proof of a certain measure of independence in dealing with the materials upon which the three narratives were severally founded.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 42, 43. - Here, as in other miracles, the restoration was immediate and complete: straightway the damsel rose up, and walked. Well might the father and the mother of the maiden and the three chosen apostles be amazed with a great amazement (ἐξέστησαν ἐκστάσει μεγάλῃ). And then, for the purpose of strengthening that life which he rescued from the jaws of the grave, our Lord commanded that something should be given her to eat. It has often been observed that in the examples of his resurrection power given by Christ there is a gradation:

1. The daughter of Jairus just dead.

2. The widow's son from his bier.

3. Lazarus from his grave. . . .
