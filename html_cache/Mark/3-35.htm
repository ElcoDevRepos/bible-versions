For whoever does the will of God
This phrase emphasizes the importance of obedience to God's will as a defining characteristic of true discipleship. In the context of Mark 3, Jesus is addressing a crowd while His biological family is seeking Him. This statement redefines familial bonds in spiritual terms, prioritizing spiritual kinship over biological ties. The will of God is a central theme throughout Scripture, seen in passages like Matthew 7:21, where Jesus states that not everyone who calls Him "Lord" will enter the kingdom of heaven, but only those who do the will of His Father. This underscores the necessity of aligning one's life with God's purposes and commands.

is My brother and sister and mother.”
Here, Jesus expands the concept of family to include all who follow God's will, indicating that spiritual relationships can be as significant, if not more so, than blood relations. This redefinition of family would have been radical in the first-century Jewish context, where family ties were paramount. By including "brother and sister and mother," Jesus affirms the equal value of all believers in the family of God, regardless of gender or social status. This inclusivity is echoed in Galatians 3:28, where Paul writes that in Christ, there is neither Jew nor Greek, slave nor free, male nor female. The mention of "mother" alongside "brother and sister" may also reflect the nurturing and caring aspects of spiritual relationships within the community of believers.

Teaching Points
Spiritual Kinship Over Biological Ties
Jesus emphasizes that spiritual relationships, defined by obedience to God's will, are more significant than biological ones.

The Will of God as a Unifying Factor
Doing God's will is what unites believers as a family, transcending earthly relationships.

Active Faith
True discipleship involves actively doing God's will, not just hearing or knowing it.

Inclusivity of God's Family
Jesus' statement opens the door for all who follow God's will to be part of His spiritual family, regardless of their background.

Prioritizing God's Will
Believers are called to prioritize God's will in their lives, which may sometimes mean making difficult choices regarding earthly relationships.

What Does Mark 3:35 Mean
For whoever

The sentence opens with an open‐armed invitation. “Whoever” signals that the Lord is not drawing lines around ethnicity, social status, or past failures (John 3:16; Acts 10:34-35). Everyone listening—and everyone reading today—can step into what comes next. Revelation 22:17 pictures the same wide door: “Whoever desires, let him take the water of life freely.” In other words, no one is disqualified from belonging to Jesus except the one who refuses the invitation.

does the will of God

Belonging to Christ is confirmed by obedience. Scripture repeatedly weds love for God with doing His will:

• Jesus: “If anyone loves Me, he will keep My word” (John 14:23).

• James: “Be doers of the word, and not hearers only” (James 1:22).

• Paul: “Do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed…to prove what is the good and pleasing and perfect will of God” (Romans 12:2).

Doing the Father’s will is not a merit badge we earn; it is the evidence that Christ has already made us new (Ephesians 2:10). Our obedience flows from faith, not fear.

is My brother

Jesus elevates obedient disciples to family status. The One who is “the firstborn among many brothers” (Romans 8:29) shares His Father with us. Hebrews 2:11 marvels that He “is not ashamed to call them brothers.” This is more than a warm metaphor; it is a literal, covenantal reality sealed by His blood (John 1:12-13).

and sister

Women are not honorary members but equal heirs: “You are all sons of God through faith in Christ Jesus…there is neither male nor female” (Galatians 3:26-28). By including “sister,” Jesus sweeps away cultural hierarchies and affirms that every obedient believer—male or female—stands on identical family ground.

and mother

The most surprising word. Jesus is saying that an obedient disciple can enjoy intimacy and influence akin to a mother’s place in a household. When a woman in the crowd once cried, “Blessed is the womb that bore You,” He answered, “Blessed rather are those who hear the word of God and obey it” (Luke 11:27-28). Obedience nurtures the life of Christ in others just as a mother nurtures her child. In John 19:26-27 He even restructured earthly ties from the cross, underscoring that spiritual family can transcend biological bonds.

summary

Mark 3:35 throws the door of Jesus’ household wide open. Anyone—absolutely anyone—who hears and obeys the Father’s will is welcomed by the Son as close family: brother, sister, even mother. Obedience does not purchase that place; it proves it. The Lord’s words invite us to respond, live out His will, and enjoy the full privileges and responsibilities of His eternal family.
