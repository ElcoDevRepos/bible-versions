Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Vengeance is Mine; I will repay.
This phrase emphasizes God's sovereignty and justice. In the context of Deuteronomy, Moses is delivering a song to the Israelites, reminding them of God's power and righteousness. The concept of divine vengeance is rooted in the understanding that God alone has the authority to judge and execute justice. This is echoed in the New Testament, particularly in Romans 12:19, where Paul advises believers to leave room for God's wrath. The idea is that human retribution is flawed, but God's justice is perfect and will be executed in His timing. This phrase also reflects the covenant relationship between God and Israel, where God promises to protect and avenge His people against their enemies.

In due time their foot will slip;
This phrase suggests the inevitability of judgment for those who oppose God. The imagery of a foot slipping indicates a sudden and unexpected downfall, highlighting the precariousness of relying on one's own strength or defying God. In the broader biblical narrative, this can be seen as a warning to those who are arrogant or complacent, thinking they are secure without God. The phrase serves as a reminder of the moral and spiritual laws that govern the universe, which God enforces in His timing. It also connects to the wisdom literature, such as Proverbs, where the consequences of wickedness are often depicted as a fall or stumble.

For their day of disaster is near,
This part of the verse underscores the certainty and imminence of divine judgment. The "day of disaster" is a common theme in prophetic literature, often referring to a time when God will act decisively against sin and rebellion. This can be seen in the context of the Old Testament prophets who warned Israel and other nations of impending judgment due to their disobedience. The phrase serves as both a warning and a call to repentance, urging people to turn back to God before it is too late. It also foreshadows the ultimate day of the Lord, a concept that is further developed in the New Testament, particularly in the book of Revelation.

And their doom is coming quickly.
The final phrase reinforces the urgency and certainty of God's judgment. The use of the word "quickly" suggests that while God's patience is long, His justice will not be delayed indefinitely. This serves as a sobering reminder of the consequences of sin and the need for repentance. In the broader biblical context, this can be seen as a call to live righteously and to trust in God's timing for justice. Theologically, it points to the ultimate fulfillment of God's plan for redemption and judgment, culminating in the return of Christ, who will judge the living and the dead. This phrase assures believers that God's justice will prevail, providing hope and encouragement in the face of injustice and suffering.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty in Justice
Trust in God's perfect timing and justice. He sees all and will act according to His righteous plan.

Patience in Adversity
Believers are called to be patient and not take matters into their own hands, trusting that God will address wrongs in His time.

Warning Against Pride
The phrase "their foot will slip" serves as a warning against pride and self-reliance. Humility before God is essential.

Hope in God's Deliverance
For those who are wronged, there is hope and assurance that God will bring about justice and deliverance.

Living Righteously
In light of God's justice, believers are encouraged to live righteously, knowing that their actions are seen by God.

What Does Deuteronomy 32:35 Mean
Vengeance is Mine

God begins by staking an exclusive claim to vengeance, reminding us that ultimate justice belongs to Him alone.

• Psalm 94:1 calls the LORD “the God of vengeance,” reinforcing that He alone has the wisdom and purity to judge righteously.

• Romans 12:19 echoes, “Beloved, do not avenge yourselves… for it is written: ‘Vengeance is Mine; I will repay.’ ” The New Testament invites believers to trust this truth rather than retaliate.

• Nahum 1:2 portrays the LORD as “a jealous and avenging God,” assuring us that no wrongdoing escapes His notice.

I will repay

The promise moves from ownership of vengeance to a personal commitment: God Himself will settle every account.

• Hebrews 10:30 repeats this line verbatim, adding weight to its certainty.

• 2 Thessalonians 1:6 affirms that “God is just: He will repay with affliction those who afflict you,” assuring persecuted believers that retribution is not forgotten.

• Proverbs 20:22 urges, “Do not say, ‘I will avenge this evil!’ Wait on the LORD, and He will deliver you,” encouraging patient faith.

In due time their foot will slip

Judgment may seem delayed, but divine timing is perfect. The imagery of a foot slipping conveys sudden, unavoidable ruin.

• Psalm 73:18-19 speaks of the arrogant: “You set them on slippery ground… they are utterly swept away,” illustrating how quickly security can vanish.

• 1 Corinthians 10:12 warns, “Therefore let him who thinks he stands take heed, lest he fall,” applying the principle to every generation.

• Psalm 37:23-24 reassures the righteous that even if they stumble, the LORD upholds them—contrasting the fate of the wicked.

Their day of disaster is near

God fixes a specific “day” for reckoning; it is nearer than the unrepentant realize.

• Obadiah 1:15 declares, “The day of the LORD is near for all nations,” broadening the scope to universal judgment.

• Isaiah 13:6 cries, “Wail, for the day of the LORD is near; it will come as destruction from the Almighty.”

• James 5:9 reminds complainers and oppressors, “The Judge is standing at the door,” underscoring the nearness of accountability.

Their doom is coming quickly

The final phrase presses the urgency. Though God may appear patient, when judgment arrives it is swift.

• 2 Peter 2:1-3 warns that false teachers’ “destruction will not be delayed,” mirroring the speed implied here.

• Revelation 22:12 records Jesus saying, “Behold, I am coming soon, and My reward is with Me,” linking quick recompense with His return.

• Zephaniah 1:14 proclaims, “The great day of the LORD is near—near and coming quickly,” echoing the cadence of Deuteronomy 32:35.

summary

Deuteronomy 32:35 assures us that God alone owns vengeance, personally guarantees repayment, times judgment perfectly, and will execute it swiftly when the appointed moment arrives. This brings comfort to the faithful, motivation to forsake personal retaliation, and a sober warning to anyone presuming upon God’s patience.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(35) To me belongeth vengeance, and recompence.--In the Epistle to the Hebrews (Hebrews 10:30) this sentence is quoted with the first clause of Deuteronomy 32:36, "For we know Him that said, Vengeance belongeth unto me. I will recompense, saith the Lord." And so in Romans 12:19.

Their foot shall slide in due time.--Rather, for the time when their foot shall slide.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 35. - Render: Vengeance is mine, and retribution for the time when their feet shall totter; for the day of their calamity is at hand, and that which is prepared for them maketh haste. The tottering of the feet represents the incipient fall. God would manifest himself as the Avenger when their calamity began to come upon them.
