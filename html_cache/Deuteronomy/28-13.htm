The LORD will make you the head and not the tail;
This phrase signifies a position of leadership and prominence. In the ancient Near Eastern context, being the "head" implies authority and influence, while the "tail" suggests subordination and insignificance. This promise reflects God's covenantal blessings for Israel, contingent upon their obedience. It echoes the blessings outlined in Deuteronomy 28, contrasting with the curses for disobedience. The imagery of head and tail is also seen in Isaiah 9:14-15, where leaders are described as the head, and false prophets as the tail, emphasizing the importance of righteous leadership.

you will only move upward and never downward,
This promise of continual progress and elevation is tied to Israel's faithfulness. It suggests prosperity, success, and divine favor in all endeavors. Historically, Israel experienced periods of ascendancy, such as under King David and Solomon, when they adhered to God's commandments. This upward movement is symbolic of spiritual growth and blessing, contrasting with the downward spiral associated with disobedience and idolatry, as seen in the later history of Israel and Judah.

if you hear and carefully follow the commandments of the LORD your God,
The condition for receiving these blessings is obedience to God's commandments. This reflects the covenantal relationship between God and Israel, where blessings are contingent upon adherence to the law. The emphasis on hearing and careful following underscores the importance of not just knowing the law but living it out. This principle is reiterated throughout Deuteronomy and is foundational to the Mosaic Covenant. It is echoed in the New Testament, where Jesus emphasizes obedience to God's word (John 14:15).

which I am giving you today.
This phrase highlights the immediacy and relevance of God's commandments. The use of "today" underscores the urgency and present application of the law to the Israelites as they prepare to enter the Promised Land. It serves as a reminder of the fresh commitment required from each generation. The giving of the law is a pivotal moment in Israel's history, marking their identity as God's chosen people and setting the standard for their conduct and relationship with God.

Teaching Points
Obedience Brings Elevation
The promise of being the "head and not the tail" is contingent upon obedience to God's commandments. This principle remains true for believers today; obedience to God's Word leads to spiritual and sometimes material elevation.

God's Sovereignty in Blessing
The passage emphasizes that it is the LORD who makes one the head. Recognizing God's sovereignty in our successes keeps us humble and dependent on Him.

Conditional Promises
The blessings are conditional upon hearing and carefully following God's commandments. This teaches us the importance of not just knowing God's Word but actively living it out.

Spiritual Leadership
Being the "head" implies leadership and influence. Christians are called to lead by example, reflecting Christ in their actions and decisions.

Continuous Growth
The promise of moving "upward and never downward" suggests a life of continuous growth and improvement in our walk with God, as we remain faithful to His commandments.

What Does Deuteronomy 28:13 Mean
The LORD will make you

“The LORD will make you…” (Deuteronomy 28:13a) places the entire promise in God’s hands. He is the initiator, not human effort. Scripture consistently shows that every promotion and safeguard comes from Him:

• 1 Samuel 2:7-8—“The LORD sends poverty and wealth; He brings low, He also exalts.”

• Psalm 75:6-7—“Exaltation does not come from the east, west, or desert, but God is the Judge; He brings one down and lifts another up.”

• James 1:17—“Every good and perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of the heavenly lights.”

Because the promise starts with the LORD’s action, confidence rests in His character and covenant faithfulness rather than our own abilities.

the head and not the tail

To be “the head” is to occupy the place of leadership, influence, and victory, while “the tail” pictures subservience and defeat. Earlier in the same chapter Moses warned that disobedience would reverse the roles (v. 44). God’s intent for His obedient people is clear:

• Deuteronomy 28:1—obedience places Israel “high above all the nations of the earth.”

• Isaiah 9:14-15 contrasts “head” and “tail,” identifying the head with honor and the tail with false leadership.

• Romans 8:37 assures believers that “in all these things we are more than conquerors through Him who loved us.”

Literally for Israel and spiritually for all who walk in faith, God sets His people in positions where they guide rather than merely react.

you will only move upward and never downward

Upward momentum speaks of continual progress and blessing; downward motion signals decline. God promises a trajectory of growth:

• Psalm 18:33—“He makes my feet like the feet of a deer; He causes me to stand on the heights.”

• Proverbs 4:18—“The path of the righteous is like the first gleam of dawn, shining ever brighter till the full light of day.”

• Philippians 3:13-14 urges pressing “toward the goal for the prize of the upward call of God in Christ Jesus.”

The covenant assurance is steady advancement—influence that expands and a life increasingly marked by God’s favor.

if you hear and carefully follow the commandments of the LORD your God

The promise is conditional. Blessing follows obedience, a theme woven through Scripture:

• Deuteronomy 11:26-28—blessing or curse depends on listening to God’s commands.

• John 14:15—“If you love Me, you will keep My commandments.”

• James 1:22 warns against being hearers only; doers are blessed.

Hearing involves attentive listening, while “carefully follow” calls for wholehearted, detailed obedience. God’s standards aren’t suggestions; they are life-giving directives that channel His favor.

which I am giving you today

Moses roots the commands in the present moment. God’s Word is always timely and accessible:

• Deuteronomy 30:11-14 stresses that the command is “not too difficult” nor “beyond reach… the word is very near you.”

• Hebrews 3:7-8 urges, “Today, if you hear His voice, do not harden your hearts.”

Each generation must freshly receive and act on God’s revealed will; the promise is alive for every “today.”

summary

Deuteronomy 28:13 reveals a divine pattern: God Himself elevates obedient people, making them leaders rather than followers, advancing them upward instead of allowing decline. The blessings are literal for Israel under the Mosaic covenant and remain a trustworthy reflection of God’s heart for all who heed His Word. As we listen and respond to His commands, we can expect His faithful hand to position us for influence, progress, and enduring victory—all to His glory.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 13. - They should be manifestly superior to other nations, heading them and being above them, their leader and not their subject or follower (cf. Isaiah 9:13). Note the contrast in vers. 43, 44.
