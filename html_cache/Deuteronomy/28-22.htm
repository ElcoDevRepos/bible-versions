Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
The LORD will strike you with wasting disease
This phrase indicates divine judgment, reflecting the covenantal relationship between God and Israel. Wasting disease can be understood as a physical ailment that gradually deteriorates the body, symbolizing the spiritual decay that results from disobedience. In the ancient Near East, health was often seen as a sign of divine favor, while illness was viewed as a curse or punishment. This aligns with the broader Deuteronomic theme of blessings for obedience and curses for disobedience (Deuteronomy 28:1-14).

with fever and inflammation
Fever and inflammation are specific afflictions that further illustrate the physical consequences of breaking the covenant. In the historical context, these symptoms could be associated with various diseases prevalent in the ancient world, such as malaria or typhoid. The specificity of these ailments underscores the comprehensive nature of the curses, affecting both the body and the land.

with scorching heat and drought
Scorching heat and drought are natural phenomena that would have had devastating effects on an agrarian society like Israel. These conditions would lead to crop failure and famine, emphasizing the dependence of the Israelites on God's provision. The mention of drought connects to other biblical passages where God withholds rain as a form of judgment (1 Kings 17:1, Jeremiah 14:1-6).

and with blight and mildew
Blight and mildew refer to plant diseases that destroy crops, further compounding the agricultural disaster caused by drought. These terms highlight the vulnerability of the Israelites' livelihood and the direct impact of divine displeasure on their sustenance. The imagery of blight and mildew is also used in Haggai 2:17, where God calls the people to repentance by reminding them of past judgments.

these will pursue you until you perish
The relentless nature of these curses is emphasized by the phrase "pursue you until you perish," indicating that there is no escape from the consequences of disobedience. This relentless pursuit serves as a warning to the Israelites of the seriousness of covenantal faithfulness. The idea of being pursued by curses can be seen as a reversal of the blessings that were meant to overtake them if they obeyed (Deuteronomy 28:2). This phrase also foreshadows the ultimate exile and destruction that would come upon Israel if they continued in disobedience, as seen in the later historical books and the prophetic literature.

Teaching Points
The Seriousness of Disobedience
God's warnings are not to be taken lightly. The consequences of turning away from His commands are severe and affect all aspects of life.

The Sovereignty of God
God is in control of all circumstances, including natural phenomena. His power is evident in both blessings and curses.

The Call to Repentance
Even in the face of judgment, there is always a call to return to God. Repentance can restore the relationship and bring healing.

The Importance of Covenant Faithfulness
The covenant relationship with God requires faithfulness. Our actions have spiritual and physical consequences.

The Role of Community
The curses affect the entire community, highlighting the importance of collective obedience and accountability.

What Does Deuteronomy 28:22 Mean
The LORD will strike you

– Divine action is front-and-center. The verse begins by reminding Israel that covenant blessings or curses ultimately flow from God Himself (Deuteronomy 28:15; 1 Samuel 2:6-7).

– His “strike” is corrective and judicial, echoing earlier warnings in Leviticus 26:14-17.

– Cross references show God personally intervening when His people rebel (Jeremiah 30:23; Isaiah 45:7), underscoring that consequences are not random but relational.

with wasting disease

– “Wasting disease” pictures a slow, debilitating illness that drains strength, fulfilling the opposite of God’s promise of health in Exodus 15:26.

– Similar language in Leviticus 26:16 describes consumption that “wastes the eyes and causes sorrow of heart.”

– Job 30:30 and Psalm 102:3 graphically portray bodies reduced by prolonged sickness, illustrating how sin’s toll touches every part of life.

with fever and inflammation

– Acute physical afflictions follow the chronic ones. Psalm 38:3 reflects David’s sense that “there is no health in my bones because of my sin.”

– Deuteronomy 32:24 warns of “wasting hunger, consuming pestilence and bitter plague,” revealing that fevered bodies mirror a fevered, unrepentant heart.

with scorching heat and drought

– The curse moves from the body to the climate, linking personal disobedience to national hardship.

– Jeremiah 17:7-8 pictures the blessed man as a tree unaffected by heat, but here the disobedient nation feels relentless sun.

– Amos 4:7-9 recounts how God withheld rain “yet you did not return to Me,” confirming that discipline aims at restoration.

and with blight and mildew

– Agricultural disasters strike both field and storage. Moses had promised abundant crops for obedience (Deuteronomy 28:11); blight and mildew now reverse that blessing.

– 1 Kings 8:37 and Haggai 2:17 list these very plagues as covenant wake-up calls.

– Amos 4:9 again pairs “blight and mildew” with stubborn refusal to repent.

these will pursue you until you perish

– The series of afflictions is relentless, as if armed guards chase a fugitive—a vivid contrast to Psalm 23:6 where “goodness and mercy” pursue the faithful.

– Deuteronomy 28:45 summarizes: “All these curses will come upon you…until you are destroyed.”

– Isaiah 30:28 likens God’s judgment to a narrowing torrent that sweeps everything away, emphasizing that ignoring repeated warnings ends in total loss.

summary

Deuteronomy 28:22 details a cascade of covenant curses—physical illness, bodily fever, environmental extremes, and crop failure—each progressively severe, each divinely administered, and all designed to call a rebellious nation back to wholehearted obedience. The verse underscores that sin’s consequences are comprehensive, touching health, climate, and livelihood, and they persist “until you perish” unless repentance intervenes. God’s justice is as certain as His mercy; the same Lord who strikes also heals when His people return to Him (2 Chronicles 7:14).

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(22) Consumption.--Only here and in Leviticus 26:16. "With which the flesh is consumed and puffed out" (Rashi).

Fever.--Only here and in Leviticus 26:16, where it is rendered "burning ague." ( Comp. Deuteronomy 32:22 : "A fire is kindled in mine anger.")

Inflammation.--Here only. The word is derived from a verb signifying to burn, or pursue hotly, like a fire that hastens on its way. "A heat greater than the fever" (Rashi).

Extreme burning.--Here only. "A disease which heats the body inwardly" (Rashi).

Blasting and mildew.--"I have smitten you with blasting and with mildew" (Amos 4:9, same words). (See also 1Kings 8:37, where "pestilence, blasting, and mildew" are contemplated as possibilities, very probably in view of this curse. Also Haggai 2:17.) . . .
