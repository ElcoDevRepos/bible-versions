He will teach Your ordinances to Jacob
This phrase highlights the role of the Levites as teachers of God's law. The tribe of Levi was set apart for religious duties, and teaching was a significant part of their responsibilities (Deuteronomy 31:9-13). The term "Jacob" refers to the nation of Israel, emphasizing the covenant relationship between God and His people. Teaching the ordinances was crucial for maintaining the covenant and ensuring that the Israelites lived according to God's commands. This role of teaching is echoed in the New Testament, where Jesus is often referred to as a teacher (Rabbi) who expounds on the law (Matthew 5:17-19).

and Your law to Israel;
The law given to Israel was central to their identity as God's chosen people. It included moral, ceremonial, and civil laws that governed every aspect of life. The Levites were responsible for ensuring that the Israelites understood and adhered to these laws. This teaching function is seen as a precursor to the New Covenant, where the law is written on the hearts of believers (Jeremiah 31:33, Hebrews 8:10). The emphasis on teaching the law underscores the importance of Scripture in guiding the lives of believers.

he will set incense before You
Incense in the Old Testament was symbolic of prayer and worship (Psalm 141:2, Revelation 8:3-4). The Levites, particularly the priests, were tasked with burning incense on the altar of incense in the Tabernacle and later in the Temple (Exodus 30:7-8). This act represented the prayers of the people ascending to God. The use of incense is a type of Christ's intercessory work, as He continually intercedes for believers before the Father (Hebrews 7:25).

and whole burnt offerings on Your altar.
Whole burnt offerings were a type of sacrifice where the entire animal was consumed by fire on the altar (Leviticus 1:3-9). This offering symbolized complete dedication and atonement for sin. The Levites facilitated these offerings, which were central to the sacrificial system. The whole burnt offering is a type of Christ's ultimate sacrifice, as He offered Himself completely for the atonement of humanity's sins (Ephesians 5:2, Hebrews 10:10). The altar, a place of sacrifice, points to the cross where Jesus was crucified, fulfilling the sacrificial system once and for all.

Teaching Points
Role of the Priesthood
The Levites were set apart to teach God's law and facilitate worship. This highlights the importance of spiritual leadership and the responsibility to guide others in understanding God's Word.

Worship and Sacrifice
The offering of incense and sacrifices signifies a life dedicated to worship. Believers today are called to offer themselves as living sacrifices, maintaining a lifestyle of worship and devotion.

Teaching God's Word
Just as the Levites taught the ordinances to Israel, Christians are called to teach and share God's Word with others, ensuring that His statutes are known and followed.

Holiness and Service
The Levites' role underscores the call to holiness and service. As believers, we are to live set apart, serving God and others with integrity and dedication.

Continuity of God's Plan
The priestly duties of the Levites point to the continuity of God's plan from the Old Testament to the New Testament, where all believers are called to be priests in His kingdom.

What Does Deuteronomy 33:10 Mean
He will teach Your ordinances to Jacob

Moses blesses Levi with a clear mandate: the tribe is responsible for passing on God’s righteous rules to the covenant family.

• Levi’s calling in Exodus 32:26-29 and Numbers 3:11-13 sets the foundation—God chooses this tribe to guard the sanctuary and to handle sacred things.

• Teaching is central. In 2 Chronicles 17:8-9, Levites travel through Judah “teaching in Judah, having the Book of the Law of the LORD with them.”

• Nehemiah 8:7-8 shows Levites “making the meaning clear” so the people could understand.

• Living instruction mattered as much as spoken words (Malachi 2:6-7). Faithful teaching preserves Israel from drift; silence lets confusion grow (Hosea 4:6).

and Your law to Israel

The second phrase widens the audience from Jacob (the patriarch’s immediate descendants) to the whole nation.

• Levi’s influence touches every tribe at every altar, festival, and gate (Deuteronomy 31:9-13).

• Psalm 78:5-7 reminds parents to recount God’s law so that “a generation yet to come would know.” The Levites model this task.

• When Israel obeys the law delivered through Levi, blessing follows (Deuteronomy 28:1-2). When they reject it, discipline comes (2 Chronicles 36:15-17).

• Jesus later upholds this teaching ministry, urging hearers to “listen to those who sit in Moses’ seat—but do not do what they do” when hypocrisy surfaces (Matthew 23:2-3).

he will set incense before You

The blessing shifts from word ministry to worship ministry. Incense pictures intercession and praise rising to God.

• Exodus 30:7-8 assigns Aaron’s sons to burn incense “morning after morning” and “when evening comes.” Consistency reflects constant dependence on God.

• Psalm 141:2 links prayer to incense: “May my prayer be set before You like incense.” The Levites’ ritual action teaches Israel how to approach God spiritually.

• Luke 1:8-10 shows the same pattern in the temple centuries later—Zechariah offers incense while “the whole multitude… was praying.” Word and worship intertwine.

• Revelation 8:3-4 reveals heavenly continuity: incense mingled with “the prayers of the saints” before God’s throne.

and whole burnt offerings on Your altar

Finally, Levi mediates atoning sacrifice. Whole burnt offerings are entirely consumed, symbolizing total consecration.

• Leviticus 1 outlines this offering: the worshiper places a hand on the animal, signifying substitution; the priest then “makes atonement on his behalf.”

• Hebrews 9:22 reminds us “without the shedding of blood there is no forgiveness.” These sacrifices foreshadow Christ’s once-for-all offering (Hebrews 10:10-12).

• The altar ministry safeguards the nation from judgment (Numbers 16:46-48) and keeps fellowship with God open (Psalm 51:19).

• The Levites embody the gospel pattern—teaching truth, leading worship, securing atonement—until the true High Priest arrives (John 1:29).

summary

Deuteronomy 33:10 sketches Levi’s three-fold calling: instruct the people in God’s unchanging word, lift continual prayerful worship, and present blood sacrifices that point to ultimate redemption. When these ministries flourish together, the covenant community thrives—mind, heart, and conscience anchored in the living God.
