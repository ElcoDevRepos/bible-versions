You are to pay them in silver
This directive emphasizes the importance of fair trade and respect for the property of others. In the context of Deuteronomy, the Israelites were passing through the land of the Edomites, descendants of Esau, Jacob's brother. The command to pay in silver reflects a principle of justice and integrity, ensuring that the Israelites did not take advantage of their kin. Silver, a common medium of exchange in the ancient Near East, signifies the value and fairness expected in transactions. This principle is echoed in other scriptures, such as Romans 13:7, which speaks to giving everyone what you owe them.

for the food you eat
The Israelites were instructed to purchase food, highlighting the importance of sustaining life through legitimate means. This reflects God's provision and the expectation of self-sufficiency, even during their wilderness journey. The act of buying food rather than taking it by force underscores a peaceful coexistence with neighboring nations. This approach contrasts with the conquest of Canaan, where God commanded the Israelites to take possession of the land. The emphasis on purchasing food also points to the broader biblical theme of God providing for His people, as seen in Matthew 6:11, "Give us this day our daily bread."

and the water you drink.
Water, a vital resource in the arid regions of the Near East, was to be purchased, underscoring the respect for the resources of others. This command reflects the cultural and historical context where water rights were crucial for survival. The Israelites' need to buy water highlights their dependence on God’s guidance and provision during their journey. This act of purchasing water also serves as a reminder of the spiritual thirst that only God can quench, as seen in John 4:14, where Jesus speaks of the living water that leads to eternal life.

Teaching Points
Respect for Others' Property
The Israelites are instructed to pay for what they consume, teaching us the importance of respecting others' resources and property.

Peaceful Coexistence
God commands the Israelites to pass through Edom peacefully, reminding us of the value of living in harmony with others, even those with whom we have historical tensions.

Obedience to God's Instructions
The Israelites' adherence to God's command to pay for food and water demonstrates the importance of obedience in our walk with God.

Trust in God's Provision
Despite being in the wilderness, God provides for the Israelites, teaching us to trust in His provision in our own lives.

Financial Integrity
The command to pay in silver underscores the importance of financial integrity and fair dealings in our interactions.

What Does Deuteronomy 2:6 Mean
You

- The address is personal and direct: the LORD speaks to the nation, but He singles out each hearer.

- Scripture consistently stresses individual responsibility within the community (Deuteronomy 5:1; Joshua 24:15).

- This “you” reminds every believer that obedience is never merely national or collective; it is my duty before God.

are to pay them

- God commands payment, not plunder. Israel must treat the Edomites justly, respecting their property (Deuteronomy 2:4-5; Numbers 20:14-21).

- Property rights are rooted in God’s own justice (Leviticus 25:14; Exodus 22:14).

- New-covenant echoes: “Render to all what is due them…” (Romans 13:7).

- Practical takeaways:

• Integrity in financial dealings is worship.

• God’s people honor Him by honoring others, even outsiders (1 Thessalonians 4:12).

in silver

- A specific medium of exchange underscores tangible, fair compensation.

- Abraham modeled this when he “weighed out the silver” for Sarah’s burial site (Genesis 23:16).

- True faith handles money transparently (Proverbs 20:14; Matthew 22:19).

- The instruction discourages any hint of exploitation under the guise of spiritual privilege.

for the food you eat

- Israel must buy, not seize, what sustains them.

- This respects the labor behind every loaf (1 Samuel 25:11; 2 Thessalonians 3:10).

- Jesus affirmed the principle: “The worker is worthy of his wages” (Luke 10:7).

- Bullet reminders:

• Consume responsibly.

• Pay promptly.

• Recognize God’s provision through human channels.

and the water you drink

- Even something as basic as water deserves compensation—nothing is to be taken for granted (Numbers 20:17; Deuteronomy 2:28).

- Caring for another’s resources foreshadows Christ’s teaching: “I was thirsty and you gave Me drink” (Matthew 25:35).

- God notices the smallest transactions; integrity in little things proves faithfulness in much (Luke 16:10).

summary

Deuteronomy 2:6 calls God’s people to personal accountability, honest payment, tangible fairness, and grateful respect for even the simplest necessities. By commanding Israel to purchase food and water with silver, the LORD models justice, neighbor-love, and practical holiness for every generation.
