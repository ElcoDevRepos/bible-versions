They are new every morning;
This phrase emphasizes the renewal of God's mercies and compassion each day. In the context of Lamentations, the prophet Jeremiah is lamenting the destruction of Jerusalem and the suffering of the people. Despite the dire circumstances, he finds hope in the daily renewal of God's mercies. This reflects the cyclical nature of time in the Hebrew calendar, where each day begins at sunset, symbolizing a fresh start. The idea of daily renewal is echoed in the Lord's Prayer, where Jesus teaches to ask for "daily bread" (Matthew 6:11), signifying reliance on God's provision each day. The concept of morning as a time of new beginnings is also seen in Psalm 30:5, "Weeping may stay for the night, but rejoicing comes in the morning."

great is Your faithfulness!
This declaration of God's faithfulness is a central theme in the Bible, highlighting His unchanging nature and reliability. In the historical context, the Israelites had experienced God's faithfulness through their deliverance from Egypt, the provision in the wilderness, and the establishment in the Promised Land. Despite their unfaithfulness, God remained steadfast in His covenant promises. This faithfulness is ultimately fulfilled in Jesus Christ, who is described as "the same yesterday and today and forever" (Hebrews 13:8). The Hebrew word for faithfulness, "emunah," conveys a sense of steadfastness and trustworthiness, qualities that are foundational to God's character. This assurance of God's faithfulness provides comfort and hope, especially in times of trial and uncertainty, as seen throughout the Psalms and the prophetic writings.

Teaching Points
Daily Renewal of God's Mercies
God's mercies are not a one-time event but are renewed every morning, offering us a fresh start each day.

Understanding God's Faithfulness
Despite our circumstances, God's faithfulness remains constant. This should encourage us to trust Him even in difficult times.

Hope in Times of Despair
Lamentations was written in a time of great sorrow, yet it speaks of hope. We can find hope in God's promises even when life seems bleak.

Living in Gratitude
Recognizing the daily renewal of God's mercies should lead us to live with gratitude and reliance on Him.

Reflecting God's Faithfulness
As recipients of God's faithfulness, we are called to reflect His character in our relationships and commitments.

What Does Lamentations 3:23 Mean
They are new

Jeremiah has just declared, “Because of the LORD’s loving devotion we are not consumed, for His compassions never fail” (Lamentations 3:22). The “they” points back to those compassions—God’s tangible acts of mercy.

• God never recycles yesterday’s grace; He brings fresh, present-tense help, just as He provided fresh manna each day in the wilderness (Exodus 16:4–5).

• Psalm 40:3 shows God giving David a “new song,” illustrating how mercy produces renewed hope.

• Isaiah 43:19 reminds us that the Lord delights in doing “something new,” assuring His people of an ongoing stream of deliverance.

every morning

Jeremiah places God’s mercies on the schedule of the sunrise.

• Psalm 30:5 echoes, “Weeping may stay the night, but joy comes in the morning,” linking dawn with divine turnaround.

• Jesus rose at dawn on the first day of the week (Mark 16:2), forever tying morning light to resurrection and renewal.

• Practically, this means no day begins with an empty account. Whatever yesterday depleted, today’s first light brings a refill straight from heaven.

great

The prophet piles on this single, emphatic word to keep us from shrinking God’s goodness to our own size.

• Psalm 145:3 exclaims, “Great is the LORD and greatly to be praised,” showing greatness as an attribute of His very being.

• Ephesians 2:4 speaks of God’s “great love,” underscoring that His measure is always abundance, never scarcity.

• By declaring God’s mercy “great,” Jeremiah pushes our expectations past ordinary relief to extraordinary sufficiency.

is Your faithfulness!

Jeremiah turns from describing God to addressing Him directly: “Your faithfulness!”

• Deuteronomy 7:9 anchors this faithfulness in covenant: God “keeps His covenant of loving devotion for a thousand generations.”

• 1 Corinthians 1:9 reassures believers that “God is faithful, who has called you into fellowship with His Son,” linking steadfastness in the Old Testament to the New.

• 2 Timothy 2:13 promises, “If we are faithless, He remains faithful,” highlighting that His reliability is not contingent on ours.

• The exclamation mark matters. Jeremiah is still sitting amid ruins, yet he erupts in praise, showing that observable circumstances never override the settled fact of God’s loyalty to His word.

summary

Lamentations 3:23 lifts weary hearts by spotlighting God’s daily, dawn-timed mercies, the greatness that characterizes them, and the unshakable faithfulness that delivers them. No matter the darkness of the night, sunrise brings proof that God’s covenant love has not run dry, and His promises remain certain for all who trust Him.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(23) They are new.--The subject of the sentence is found in the "compassions" of the preceding verse. With the dawn of every day there dawn also the mercies of Jehovah.
