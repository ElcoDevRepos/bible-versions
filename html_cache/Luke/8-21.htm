But He replied
In this passage, Jesus is responding to a situation where His biological family is seeking Him. This reply emphasizes His teaching method, often using direct and thought-provoking statements to convey deeper spiritual truths. Jesus frequently used such moments to teach about the Kingdom of God, shifting focus from earthly relationships to spiritual ones.

“My mother and brothers
Here, Jesus redefines familial relationships in spiritual terms. In the cultural context of first-century Judea, family ties were paramount, and loyalty to one's family was a significant social expectation. By referring to His followers as His family, Jesus elevates the spiritual bond above biological connections, emphasizing the importance of spiritual kinship in the Kingdom of God.

are those who hear the word of God
Hearing the word of God implies more than just listening; it involves understanding and accepting the message of the Gospel. This phrase connects to the parable of the sower earlier in Luke 8, where Jesus explains the different responses to the word of God. The emphasis is on being receptive and open to God's message, which is a recurring theme throughout Scripture (e.g., Romans 10:17).

and carry it out.”
Carrying out the word of God involves obedience and action, reflecting a life transformed by faith. This concept is echoed in James 1:22, which urges believers to be doers of the word, not merely hearers. Jesus highlights that true discipleship is demonstrated through living out God's commands, aligning with His teachings on bearing fruit (John 15:8). This phrase underscores the active nature of faith, where belief is evidenced by works.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jesus Christ
The central figure in this passage, Jesus is teaching about the true nature of spiritual kinship.

2. Mother and Brothers of Jesus
They are mentioned in the context as seeking to speak with Jesus, representing His earthly family.

3. Disciples and Followers
Those present who are listening to Jesus' teaching, representing His spiritual family.

4. Crowd
The larger group gathered around Jesus, illustrating the diverse audience of His ministry.

5. Location
While not specified in this verse, the context of Luke 8 suggests Jesus is teaching in a setting where many can gather, likely in Galilee.
Teaching Points
Spiritual Kinship Over Biological Ties
Jesus emphasizes that true family is defined by obedience to God's word, not merely by blood relations.

Hearing and Doing the Word
The Greek word for "hear" (akou?) implies not just listening but understanding and acting upon the word of God.

Obedience as a Mark of Discipleship
True discipleship is marked by carrying out God's commands, reflecting a life transformed by His word.

Inclusivity of God's Family
The invitation to be part of Jesus' family is open to all who choose to follow Him, transcending cultural and social boundaries.

Practical Faith
Faith is demonstrated through actions; believers are called to live out their faith in tangible ways.

What Does Luke 8:21 Mean
But He replied

- The scene opens with Jesus surrounded by a crowd while His earthly family waits outside (Mark 3:31-32; Matthew 12:46). Instead of stepping away, He answers the messenger, revealing a priority that transcends human ties.

- His response is immediate and deliberate—He is teaching everyone present that spiritual relationships outweigh biological ones (John 3:3-6).

My mother and brothers

- Jesus is not dismissing Mary or His half-brothers; He is expanding the definition of family to include all who belong to God (John 1:12-13).

- This redefinition invites anyone, regardless of background, into intimate kinship with Christ (Romans 8:15-17).

- Believers become part of a household of faith, caring for one another as true relatives (Galatians 6:10; Ephesians 2:19).

are those who hear the word of God

- Hearing in Scripture means more than receiving sound; it implies attentive, receptive listening (Luke 8:15).

- Faith itself is born through such hearing (Romans 10:17).

- Jesus elsewhere blesses “those who hear the word of God and observe it” when a woman praises His mother (Luke 11:27-28), underscoring the same truth.

and carry it out

- Obedience seals genuine hearing: “If you love Me, you will keep My commandments” (John 14:15).

- The blessed life is described as looking into the perfect law “and continues in it” (James 1:25).

- True discipleship involves doing God’s will, not merely acknowledging it (Matthew 7:21; 1 John 2:3-5).

- By acting on God’s word, believers demonstrate the family resemblance of their heavenly Father.

summary

Luke 8:21 teaches that Jesus counts as His closest family anyone who listens to God’s word with an obedient heart. Biological connections are honored, but spiritual kinship is supreme. Hearing leads to faith; obedience proves faith genuine. Those who both receive and live out Scripture enjoy the privilege and warmth of being called Christ’s own mother, brothers, and sisters today.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(21) My mother and my brethren.--The answer agrees very closely with that in the other Gospels. But note the use of "the word of God," instead of "the will of God" in St. Mark, and "the will of my Father" in St. Matthew, as throwing light on the meaning of the former phrase, and showing its fulness and width of meaning.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 21. - And he answered and said unto them, My mother and my brethren are these which hear the Word of God, and do it. The Master used the opportunity to send home into the hearts of the many listeners the stern, grave lesson that there was something more solemn even than family ties, and that these, holy and binding though they were, must not be allowed to stand in the way of plain, unmistakable duty.
