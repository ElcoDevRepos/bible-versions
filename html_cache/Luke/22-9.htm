Where do You want us to prepare it?
This question is asked by the disciples in the context of preparing for the Passover meal, a significant Jewish festival commemorating the Israelites' deliverance from Egypt. The Passover is a time of remembrance and celebration, deeply rooted in Jewish tradition and law as outlined in Exodus 12. The disciples' inquiry reflects their obedience and readiness to follow Jesus' instructions, highlighting their role as His followers who seek to fulfill His will.

they asked.
The disciples' question indicates their dependence on Jesus for guidance and direction. This moment underscores the relationship between Jesus and His disciples, where they look to Him as their teacher and leader. It also reflects the communal aspect of the Passover, which is traditionally celebrated with family and close community, emphasizing the bond between Jesus and His disciples. This setting foreshadows the Last Supper, where Jesus will institute the New Covenant through the breaking of bread and sharing of the cup, as recorded in the synoptic Gospels.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jesus Christ
The central figure of the New Testament, Jesus is preparing for the Passover meal with His disciples. This event is significant as it leads to the Last Supper.

2. The Disciples
Specifically, Peter and John are tasked with preparing the Passover meal. They are followers of Jesus, learning from His teachings and actions.

3. Jerusalem
The city where the Passover is to be prepared. It holds great significance in Jewish history and prophecy.

4. Passover
A Jewish festival commemorating the Israelites' exodus from Egypt. It is a time of remembrance and celebration of God's deliverance.

5. The Upper Room
The location where the Last Supper takes place. It becomes a significant place for the early Christian community.
Teaching Points
Obedience in Preparation
The disciples' question, "Where do You want us to prepare it?" reflects their readiness to obey Jesus' instructions. As believers, we should seek to follow Christ's guidance in our daily lives.

Significance of the Passover
Understanding the Passover's historical and spiritual significance helps us appreciate Jesus' role as the Lamb of God, who takes away the sins of the world.

The Role of Community
The preparation of the Passover meal was a communal activity. Similarly, our faith journey is strengthened through fellowship and shared experiences with other believers.

Spiritual Readiness
Just as the disciples prepared for the Passover, we are called to prepare our hearts for worship and service, ensuring we are spiritually ready to meet with God.

Trust in God's Provision
The disciples trusted Jesus to provide the place and means for the Passover. We, too, should trust in God's provision for our needs.

What Does Luke 22:9 Mean
Where

• The disciples have celebrated Passover with Jesus before, yet they still wait for His direction on the exact spot (Luke 22:7–8; Mark 14:12).

• Their question shows Jerusalem is crowded; suitable rooms are scarce during the feast (Deuteronomy 16:5–6).

• Jesus will give precise guidance (Luke 22:10–13), revealing His sovereign knowledge of every detail, just as He earlier foretold the location of the colt (Luke 19:29–34).

do You want

• The focus is on Jesus’ will, not their own. True discipleship asks, “Lord, what do You desire?” (John 14:15; Matthew 6:10).

• By seeking His preference, they acknowledge His messianic authority—He is more than a rabbi; He is the Lord of the feast (Exodus 12:11; 1 Corinthians 5:7).

• Their request anticipates obedient action; desire and duty merge under Christ’s command (James 1:22).

us to prepare it?

• “Prepare” involves securing a room, removing leaven, roasting the lamb, setting bread and wine (Exodus 12:8–11).

• The Passover points forward to the sacrifice Jesus is about to offer (Luke 22:15–20; John 1:29).

• Participation requires readiness:

– Physical: logistical arrangements.

– Spiritual: hearts cleansed from sin (1 Corinthians 11:28).

• Their joint labor pictures the church serving together under Christ’s direction (Ephesians 4:16).

they asked.

• The disciples voice a united question; none act independently (Acts 1:14).

• Asking reflects humility—before stepping forward, they seek the Master’s word (Proverbs 3:6).

• Jesus welcomes such questions and answers with clear instruction, reinforcing relational intimacy (John 15:15).

summary

Luke 22:9 captures a simple query that unveils profound truths: the Lord’s precise sovereignty over circumstances, His disciples’ desire to honor His will, the call to diligent preparation for worship, and the humble, communal spirit of those who follow Him.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 9. - And they said unto him, Where wilt thou that we prepare? It is probable that the disciples, in asking this question, concluded that the Passover was to be eaten by them and their Master at the same time with the rest of the Jews on the following day; but our Lord gave directions for its being eaten the same evening.
