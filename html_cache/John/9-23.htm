That was why his parents said
In the context of John 9, Jesus heals a man who was born blind. The Pharisees, skeptical of this miracle, interrogate the man and his parents. The parents' response is cautious, reflecting the social and religious pressures of the time. The Jewish leaders had already decided that anyone confessing Jesus as the Messiah would be expelled from the synagogue (John 9:22). This fear of excommunication highlights the tension between the early followers of Jesus and the established religious authorities. The parents' statement is a strategic move to avoid conflict with the Pharisees.

“He is old enough.
The phrase indicates that the man was of age to speak for himself, suggesting he was at least 13 years old, the age of religious maturity in Jewish culture, known as Bar Mitzvah. This cultural detail underscores the parents' attempt to distance themselves from the controversy, placing the responsibility of testimony on their son. It also reflects the Jewish legal principle that an adult is responsible for their own testimony.

Ask him.”
By directing the Pharisees to their son, the parents avoid making a direct statement about Jesus, thus protecting themselves from potential repercussions. This response is a reflection of the fear and division that Jesus' ministry caused among the Jewish people. It also emphasizes the personal nature of faith and testimony, as each individual must confront the truth of Jesus' identity. The man's willingness to testify about his healing contrasts with his parents' reluctance, highlighting the transformative impact of encountering Christ.

Teaching Points
Fear of Man vs. Fear of God
The parents' fear of the Pharisees highlights the struggle between fearing human authority and standing firm in faith. Believers are encouraged to prioritize their allegiance to God over the fear of human judgment.

Courage in Witnessing
The blind man's willingness to testify about Jesus contrasts with his parents' reluctance. Christians are called to boldly share their testimony of Christ's work in their lives, regardless of potential backlash.

Spiritual Blindness
The Pharisees' inability to see the truth of Jesus' miracle reflects spiritual blindness. Believers should pray for discernment and openness to God's work in their lives.

Parental Influence and Responsibility
The parents' response shows the impact of fear on family dynamics. Christian parents are encouraged to guide their children in truth and faith, even in the face of societal pressures.

Community and Belonging
The fear of being expelled from the synagogue underscores the importance of community. Believers should seek fellowship with those who encourage and support their faith journey.

What Does John 9:23 Mean
That was why

– The phrase reaches back to verse 22, where “his parents said these things because they feared the Jews, for the Jews had already determined that anyone who confessed Jesus as the Christ would be put out of the synagogue.”

• Scripture presents clear cause-and-effect. Their fear produced their reply, just as Proverbs 29:25 warns, “The fear of man brings a snare.”

• The account underscores the literal reality of religious pressure. Jesus had foretold such opposition (John 15:20).

• God’s Word ties events together with purpose; here, the Spirit shows us that nothing occurs randomly (Romans 8:28).

his parents said

– Parents normally protect and teach (Proverbs 1:8); yet these parents speak defensively.

• Their words expose divided loyalties: honoring their son’s miracle while shrinking back from open allegiance to Christ (Matthew 10:37).

• Even godly roles can falter when fear intrudes (Genesis 3:12–13).

• Still, Scripture records their speech verbatim, confirming its historical accuracy.

He is old enough

– They declare their son’s maturity: personal responsibility now rests on him.

• The Bible affirms individual accountability: “The soul who sins shall die” (Ezekiel 18:20); “Fathers are not to be put to death for their children, nor children for their fathers” (Deuteronomy 24:16).

• Their statement acknowledges that faith in Christ cannot be outsourced; each person must answer for himself (Romans 14:12).

Ask him.

– A direct invitation: investigate the one whose eyes were opened.

• Personal testimony stands as powerful evidence. The healed man soon proclaims, “One thing I do know: I was blind, but now I see” (John 9:25).

• Scripture consistently elevates firsthand witness (Acts 4:20; 1 Peter 3:15).

• By insisting the leaders speak with their son, the parents unintentionally put the spotlight where God wants it—on the life-changing work of Jesus (John 5:36).

summary

The parents’ cautious words reveal fear of man, the reality of individual responsibility, and the power of personal testimony. God records this dialogue to show that every believer must stand for Christ, trusting that His truth remains unshakable despite opposition.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 23. - Therefore said his parents, He is of full age; ask him. They would not incur responsibility for the opinions of their son about his Healer. They knew perfectly well that it was the Jesus who was said to be the Christ of the nation, and they would not implicate themselves in giving any judgment on his claims.
