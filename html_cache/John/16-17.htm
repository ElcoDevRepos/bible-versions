Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Then some of His disciples asked one another
This phrase highlights the disciples' confusion and their tendency to discuss among themselves rather than directly asking Jesus for clarification. It reflects a common human behavior of seeking understanding through peer discussion. The disciples often struggled to grasp Jesus' teachings, as seen in other instances like Mark 9:32, where they did not understand His prediction of His death and resurrection.

“Why is He telling us,
The disciples are questioning Jesus' words, indicating their lack of understanding of His mission and the events that were about to unfold. This reflects the broader theme of misunderstanding that runs throughout the Gospels, where Jesus' followers often fail to comprehend His teachings until after His resurrection and the coming of the Holy Spirit (John 14:26).

‘In a little while you will not see Me,
This phrase refers to Jesus' impending death and burial. The "little while" signifies the short period between His crucifixion and resurrection. The disciples were unaware of the exact timeline of these events, which added to their confusion. This phrase also connects to the prophecy of the suffering servant in Isaiah 53, which speaks of the Messiah's death.

and then after a little while you will see Me’
Here, Jesus is alluding to His resurrection. The "little while" after His death is the three days before He rises again. This promise of seeing Him again is a source of hope and foreshadows the joy of the resurrection, as later described in John 20:20 when the disciples see the risen Christ.

and ‘Because I am going to the Father’?”
This phrase refers to Jesus' ascension, which occurs after His resurrection. It signifies the completion of His earthly mission and His return to divine glory. The ascension is a pivotal event that fulfills Old Testament prophecies, such as Psalm 110:1, and is essential for the sending of the Holy Spirit (John 16:7). It also emphasizes Jesus' divine nature and His role as the mediator between God and humanity.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jesus Christ
The central figure in this passage, Jesus is speaking to His disciples about His impending death, resurrection, and ascension.

2. The Disciples
Followers of Jesus who are confused by His statement and are seeking to understand His words.

3. The Father
Refers to God the Father, to whom Jesus is returning after His resurrection.

4. The Event of Jesus' Death and Resurrection
Jesus is alluding to His death, the period in the tomb, and His resurrection.

5. The Ascension
Jesus' return to the Father after His resurrection, which is part of the context of His statement.
Teaching Points
Understanding Jesus' Words
Jesus often spoke in ways that required spiritual insight. We should seek the Holy Spirit's guidance to understand His teachings.

The Reality of Jesus' Death and Resurrection
Jesus' statement points to the core of the Gospel: His death and resurrection. This is the foundation of our faith and hope.

The Promise of His Presence
Even when Jesus is not physically present, He promises to be with us through the Holy Spirit. We can rely on His presence in our daily lives.

Trust in God's Plan
The disciples were confused, but Jesus had a plan. We may not always understand God's ways, but we can trust His perfect plan for our lives.

Hope in the Return of Christ
Just as the disciples would see Jesus again, we have the hope of His return. This should encourage us to live faithfully and expectantly.

What Does John 16:17 Mean
Then some of His disciples asked one another

The disciples still struggled to grasp Jesus’ words, so they whispered among themselves instead of bringing their questions to Him.

• John 16:5 shows Jesus noticing that “none of you asks Me, ‘Where are You going?’” Their hesitation here repeats that pattern.

• Mark 9:32 records a similar moment after the Transfiguration: “they did not understand the statement and were afraid to ask Him.”

• Honest doubt is natural, but silence can keep us from receiving the clarity the Lord is ready to give (James 1:5).

Why is He telling us, “In a little while you will not see Me”

Jesus’ first “little while” points to the coming hours of His arrest, trial, and crucifixion.

• John 12:35—He had already warned, “The Light will be with you only a little while longer.”

• John 13:33—“Where I am going, you cannot come.” They would literally “not see” Him while His body lay in the tomb.

• Matthew 26:31 predicts their scattering, underlining the brief but real separation.

and then after a little while you will see Me

This second “little while” refers to His bodily resurrection appearances beginning the first day of the week.

• John 20:19-20—“Jesus came and stood among them… The disciples rejoiced when they saw the Lord.”

• Luke 24:36-43 and 1 Corinthians 15:5-7 confirm multiple post-resurrection sightings.

• The gap between cross and resurrection was only three days, yet it turned their sorrow to lasting joy (John 16:20-22).

and “Because I am going to the Father”

Even after they saw Him risen, He would soon ascend, completing His mission and sending the Spirit.

• John 14:28—“If you loved Me, you would rejoice that I am going to the Father.”

• John 16:10 links His departure with proving “righteousness.” The Father’s acceptance of the Son validates the cross.

• Acts 1:9-11 describes the literal ascent, while John 14:19 promises ongoing spiritual sight: “Because I live, you also will live.” Through the Spirit (John 16:7), believers would “see” Him by faith until His bodily return (Revelation 1:7).

summary

John 16:17 captures the disciples’ confusion over Jesus’ prophecy of His imminent death, resurrection, and ascension. The two “little whiles” map out:

1. a brief separation at the cross,

2. a joyful reunion after the resurrection,

3. a final departure to the Father that opens a new era of Spirit-empowered fellowship.

Their question reminds us that short seasons of darkness often precede overwhelming revelation of the risen Christ, whose triumph and ongoing presence are certain for every believer.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(17, 18) Then said some of his disciples among themselves.--Better, Therefore said . . . The question arises out of what He has said. They draw aside and discuss the matter privately. It is beyond their comprehension, and seems to be contradictory.

A little while, and ye shall not see me.--Better, A little while, and ye behold Me not, as in John 16:16.

Because I go to the Father.--So far they have quoted word for word what He had said in the previous verse. They now connect it with what He had said in John 16:7; John 16:10, and this forms the ground of their surprise. There He had spoken of their beholding Him no more because He goeth to the Father. Here He speaks of a little while, after which they shall not behold Him, and again a little while, after which they shall see Him. They cannot reconcile these things. They cannot tell what He saith.
