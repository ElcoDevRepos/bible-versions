The total number of men in the divisions of the camp of Ephraim
Ephraim was one of the tribes of Israel, descended from Joseph's younger son, whom Jacob blessed with a greater blessing than his older brother Manasseh (Genesis 48:19). The tribe of Ephraim held a prominent position among the tribes, often leading the northern tribes. The census here reflects the military organization of Israel as they prepared to enter the Promised Land. The divisions indicate a structured and orderly approach to their journey, emphasizing God's command for order and preparation.

is 108,100;
The specific number of 108,100 men indicates the size and strength of the tribe of Ephraim at this time. This number reflects the men who were able to go to war, highlighting the tribe's capacity for battle. The precision of the number underscores the importance of each tribe's contribution to the collective strength of Israel. This also shows God's provision and blessing upon the tribe, as they had grown significantly since their time in Egypt.

they shall set out third.
The order of march was divinely appointed, with Ephraim positioned to set out third. This placement signifies Ephraim's role as a leader among the tribes, following Judah and Reuben. The order of march was not only practical but also symbolic, reflecting the hierarchy and roles within the nation. Ephraim's position as third in line underscores their importance and the fulfillment of Jacob's blessing over Joseph's descendants. This order also foreshadows the later division of the kingdom, where Ephraim would become a leading tribe in the northern kingdom of Israel.

Teaching Points
Order and Structure in God's Plan
God is a God of order, as seen in the specific arrangement of the tribes. This teaches us the importance of structure and organization in our spiritual and daily lives.

Significance of Leadership
Ephraim's position as the third to set out highlights the tribe's leadership role. We are reminded of the importance of stepping into leadership roles when called by God.

Unity and Cooperation
The tribes had to work together, each in their assigned place, to move efficiently. This illustrates the need for unity and cooperation within the body of Christ.

Faithfulness in Following God's Instructions
The Israelites' adherence to God's instructions for their camp arrangement is a model of obedience. We are encouraged to faithfully follow God's guidance in our lives.

Legacy and Influence
Ephraim's prominence among the tribes shows how our actions and faithfulness can leave a lasting legacy. We should strive to positively influence those around us.

What Does Numbers 2:24 Mean
The total number of men

• “The total number of men in the divisions of the camp of Ephraim” reminds us that God ordered Moses to conduct an exact census of fighting-age males (Numbers 1:1-3).

• Scripture repeatedly stresses these tallies—Numbers 1:44-46 lists the national total; Numbers 26 recounts the second census—showing the Lord’s care for every individual (cf. Matthew 10:30).

• The head-count is not approximate; it is an historical record of real people, underscoring the reliability of the narrative.

Divisions of the camp of Ephraim

• Ephraim led a three-tribe group: Ephraim, Manasseh, and Benjamin (Numbers 2:18-22).

• Stationed on the west side of the tabernacle, this division guarded one of the four points of the compass, echoing the cherubim surrounding God’s glory in Ezekiel 1:10 and Revelation 4:7.

• As Joseph’s younger son, Ephraim received the firstborn’s blessing (Genesis 48:17-20). His leadership here fulfills that prophetic word in a concrete way.

108,100

• “108,100” combines 40,500 from Ephraim, 32,200 from Manasseh, and 35,400 from Benjamin (Numbers 2:19-23).

• The precision demonstrates God’s sovereign organization—nothing is random in the march toward the promised land (Psalm 37:23).

• The sizable force testifies to the Lord’s fruitful multiplication of Abraham’s seed (Genesis 15:5-6).

They shall set out third

• When the cloud lifted, Judah’s camp broke first (Numbers 10:14), followed by Reuben (10:18); Ephraim moved “third” (10:22).

• This middle position balanced the nation: two tribes’ divisions marched ahead, one behind, with the tabernacle furnishings at the center (Numbers 2:17).

• God’s order safeguarded worship—His dwelling traveled protected, foreshadowing how Christ, “the power of God and the wisdom of God” (1 Corinthians 1:24), remains central in every pilgrimage.

summary

Numbers 2:24 records an exact head-count, a divinely arranged leadership role for Ephraim’s division, a precise total of 108,100 men, and a strategic third-place departure in the march. Each detail highlights God’s meticulous care, covenant faithfulness, and orderly presence among His people as they journey to the inheritance He promised.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 24. - All that were numbered of the camp of Ephraim. All the descendants of Rachel, forming at this time the smallest of the four divisions, although destined to become very numerous. Their association in the camp was continued in the promised land, for the greater part of their territory was coterminous. Subsequently, however, the great division of the kingdom separated Benjamin for ever from his brethren. In the third rank. Immediately behind the tabernacle. This position is clearly alluded to in Psalm 80:1, 2.
