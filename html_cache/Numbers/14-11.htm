And the LORD said to Moses
This phrase indicates a direct communication between God and Moses, highlighting Moses' unique role as a mediator between God and the Israelites. In the context of the Pentateuch, Moses often intercedes on behalf of the people, reflecting his leadership and prophetic role. This communication underscores the personal relationship God had with Moses, as seen in Exodus 33:11, where it is said that the LORD spoke to Moses face to face.

How long will this people treat Me with contempt?
The term "contempt" here refers to the Israelites' repeated disobedience and lack of trust in God, despite His continuous provision and guidance. This reflects a pattern of behavior seen throughout the wilderness journey, where the Israelites frequently complained and rebelled against God's commands. The question "How long" suggests God's patience and long-suffering nature, yet also His justice and righteousness, as seen in similar expressions of divine frustration in Exodus 16:28 and Psalm 78:40.

How long will they refuse to believe in Me
This phrase highlights the core issue of unbelief, which is a recurring theme in the Israelites' journey. Despite witnessing God's miraculous works, the people struggled with faith. This unbelief is not just a lack of trust but a willful rejection of God's promises and power. Hebrews 3:19 reflects on this unbelief as the reason the Israelites could not enter the Promised Land, serving as a warning to future generations.

despite all the signs I have performed among them?
The "signs" refer to the miraculous events God performed, such as the plagues in Egypt, the parting of the Red Sea, and the provision of manna and quail. These signs were meant to demonstrate God's power and faithfulness. The Israelites' failure to trust God despite these signs is a testament to the hardness of their hearts. This mirrors the New Testament accounts where Jesus performed signs and wonders, yet many still did not believe, as seen in John 12:37. The signs serve as both a testament to God's power and a test of the people's faith.

2. Moses
The leader and prophet chosen by God to lead the Israelites out of Egypt and through the wilderness.

3. The Israelites
The chosen people of God, who were delivered from slavery in Egypt but frequently doubted and disobeyed God.

4. The Wilderness of Paran
The location where the Israelites camped and from where the spies were sent to explore the Promised Land.

5. The Signs and Wonders
Miraculous acts performed by God, including the plagues in Egypt, the parting of the Red Sea, and provision in the wilderness, intended to demonstrate His power and faithfulness.
Teaching Points
The Danger of Unbelief
Unbelief is not just a lack of faith but an active rejection of God's revealed truth and power.

Remembering God's Faithfulness
Reflect on past experiences of God's faithfulness to strengthen your trust in Him during current challenges.

The Consequences of Contempt
Treating God with contempt by doubting His promises can lead to missed blessings and opportunities.

The Importance of Obedience
Obedience to God is a demonstration of faith and trust in His character and promises.

God's Patience and Justice
While God is patient, persistent unbelief and rebellion can lead to His righteous judgment.

What Does Numbers 14:11 Mean
And the LORD said to Moses

“And the LORD said to Moses” (Numbers 14:11a).

• The narrative pauses to show God Himself breaking in—He is not distant or indifferent (cf. Exodus 3:7-10; Psalm 34:15).

• God’s direct speech underscores His covenant relationship with Israel; He addresses Moses, the appointed mediator (Exodus 3:10-12; Hebrews 3:2-5).

• By recording the words verbatim, Scripture reminds us that every word is accurate and trustworthy (2 Timothy 3:16).

How long will this people treat Me with contempt?

“How long will this people treat Me with contempt?” (Numbers 14:11b).

• “This people” signals distance; their unbelief has created a relational rift (cf. Deuteronomy 32:5).

• Treating God “with contempt” is more than irritation—it is outright despising His character and commands (1 Samuel 2:30; Malachi 1:6).

• The question “How long?” reveals divine patience being stretched, yet still extended (2 Peter 3:9).

• Contempt for God is ultimately contempt for His holiness and righteousness (Isaiah 6:3; Revelation 4:8).

How long will they refuse to believe in Me

“How long will they refuse to believe in Me” (Numbers 14:11c).

• Unbelief isn’t ignorance but conscious refusal; the heart rebels against clear revelation (Hebrews 3:8-12).

• Faith was the simple requirement—trust God’s promise of the land (Genesis 15:18; Exodus 6:8).

• Repeated unbelief kept them wandering, showing that unbelief always delays or forfeits blessings (Hebrews 4:1-2).

despite all the signs I have performed among them?

“despite all the signs I have performed among them?” (Numbers 14:11d).

• “Signs” recalls the plagues in Egypt, the Red Sea crossing, manna, water from the rock—visible proofs of God’s power and care (Exodus 7–14; 16:4-15; 17:5-7).

• Miracles are meant to foster faith, not entertain; rejecting them intensifies guilt (John 12:37; Matthew 11:20-24).

• The question exposes the irrational nature of unbelief: overwhelming evidence still met by hardened hearts (Psalm 78:11-22).

summary

God personally confronts Israel’s contempt and unbelief, highlighting His patience yet affirming He will not overlook persistent rebellion. The verse teaches that ignoring God’s clear works invites discipline, while trusting His proven faithfulness leads to promised rest.
