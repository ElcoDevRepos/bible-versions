They spread out like palm groves
This phrase evokes the image of lush, expansive growth, symbolizing prosperity and abundance. Palm groves were common in the ancient Near East, particularly in oasis regions, and were associated with life and sustenance. In biblical symbolism, palms often represent victory and triumph, as seen in the use of palm branches during Jesus' triumphal entry into Jerusalem (John 12:13). The spreading out suggests a flourishing and thriving community, reminiscent of the Israelites' growth and establishment in the Promised Land.

like gardens beside a stream
Gardens in the ancient world were places of beauty and fertility, often associated with divine blessing. The mention of a stream indicates a source of life-giving water, essential for sustaining such gardens. This imagery parallels the Garden of Eden, where a river watered the garden (Genesis 2:10), symbolizing God's provision and care. Streams are also symbolic of spiritual nourishment and refreshment, as seen in Psalm 1:3, where the righteous are likened to a tree planted by streams of water.

like aloes the LORD has planted
Aloes, known for their fragrant and medicinal properties, were highly valued in ancient times. The reference to the LORD planting them suggests divine intervention and intentionality in the growth and establishment of His people. This imagery can be seen as a type of Christ, who is described as a fragrant offering and sacrifice to God (Ephesians 5:2). The planting by the LORD emphasizes His sovereignty and the special care He takes in nurturing His chosen people.

like cedars beside the waters
Cedars, particularly those from Lebanon, were renowned for their strength, durability, and majestic stature. They were used in the construction of the temple (1 Kings 6:9-10), symbolizing stability and permanence. The placement beside waters suggests a continual source of nourishment and strength, ensuring their growth and resilience. This imagery reflects the enduring nature of God's covenant with Israel and foreshadows the eternal kingdom established through Jesus Christ, who is often associated with strength and kingship.

What Does Numbers 24:6 Mean
They spread out like palm groves

“They spread out like palm groves”. In this oracle, Balaam looks over the camp of Israel and compares God’s people to palms—trees that thrive in hot, harsh places yet remain upright and fruitful.

• Palms symbolize victory and resilience (John 12:13; Revelation 7:9).

• God had brought Israel through the wilderness, and their very existence testified to His sustaining power (Exodus 13:21-22).

• Just as palms line an oasis, Israel would soon line the Promised Land, standing tall as a visible witness to the nations (Deuteronomy 4:6-8).

Like gardens beside a stream

“Like gardens beside a stream”. The image shifts from the arid to the lush.

• Gardens flourish when watered continually—echoing God’s promise that “your soul will be like a well-watered garden” (Isaiah 58:11).

• Israel’s inheritance included “a land with brooks, streams, and deep springs” (Deuteronomy 8:7).

• Spiritually, God would satisfy them with His presence, just as He still promises rivers of living water to all who believe (John 7:38).

Like aloes the LORD has planted

“Like aloes the LORD has planted”. Aloes were prized for fragrance and healing (Psalm 45:8; John 19:39).

• The phrase “the LORD has planted” underlines divine initiative (Isaiah 60:21). Israel did not plant itself; God did.

• Aloes release their aroma when crushed. Israel’s trials refined them so that their testimony would spread God’s fragrance among the nations (2 Corinthians 2:14-15).

Like cedars beside the waters

“Like cedars beside the waters”. Cedars were the giants of the ancient forests—tall, strong, enduring.

• Planted “beside the waters,” cedars sink deep roots and stand firm (Jeremiah 17:7-8).

• God intended Israel to be unshakable, influencing surrounding kingdoms just as Solomon’s cedar temple later proclaimed divine glory (1 Kings 5:6-7, 9-12).

• The image anticipates believers who “grow like a cedar in Lebanon” when planted in God’s house (Psalm 92:12-13).

summary

Numbers 24:6 paints four pictures—palms, gardens, aloes, and cedars—to describe Israel’s God-given vitality, fragrance, fruitfulness, and strength. Each metaphor assures that when the LORD plants His people near His living water, they thrive in every season and become a testimony of His power to the world.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(6) As gardens by the river's side.--It is probable that the allusion may be to the Euphrates, although the definite article is not prefixed to the word nahar (river) in the Hebrew. (Comp. Isaiah 7:20.)

As cedar trees beside the waters.-The difference between cedars which grow beside running water which their roots can reach, and the ordinary type of cedars which throw out their strength in lateral branches is illustrated in Ezekiel 31:3-4, where the proud Assyrian is compared to a cedar having "his top among the thick boughs" (or, the clouds), which "the waters made great." (Comp. Psalm 1:3; Psalm 92:12.)

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 6. - As the valleys, or, "as the torrents" (נְחָלִים), which pour down in parallel courses from the upper slopes. As gardens by the river's side. The river (נָהָר), as in Numbers 22:5) means the Euphrates. Balaam combines the pleasant imagery of his own cultivated land with that of the wilder scene amidst which he now stood. As the trees of lign aloes. אָהָלִים. Aloe trees, such as grew in the further east, where Balaam had perhaps seen them. Which the Lord hath planted, or, "the Lord's planting," a poetical way of describing their beauty and rarity (cf. Psalm 1:3; Psalm 104:16).
