and laid his hands on him
The act of laying hands is a significant biblical gesture symbolizing the transfer of authority, blessing, or the Holy Spirit. In this context, Moses laying hands on Joshua signifies the transfer of leadership. This practice is seen throughout Scripture, such as in the ordination of priests (Leviticus 8:14) and the commissioning of the seven deacons in the early church (Acts 6:6). It represents a public acknowledgment of God's choice and the empowerment of the individual for a specific task.

and commissioned him
Commissioning involves appointing someone to a specific role or task. Here, Joshua is being appointed as the leader of Israel, succeeding Moses. This commissioning is not just a human decision but a divine mandate, as Joshua is to lead the Israelites into the Promised Land. The concept of commissioning is echoed in the New Testament, where Jesus commissions His disciples to spread the Gospel (Matthew 28:19-20), indicating a continuity of God's mission through chosen leaders.

as the LORD had instructed
This phrase underscores the importance of divine guidance and obedience to God's commands. Moses acts according to God's specific instructions, highlighting the theme of obedience that runs throughout the book of Numbers. It reflects the biblical principle that leadership and authority come from God and must align with His will. This obedience is crucial for the success of Joshua's leadership and the fulfillment of God's promises to Israel.

through Moses
Moses serves as the intermediary between God and the people of Israel, a role that foreshadows the mediatory work of Jesus Christ. Just as Moses faithfully conveys God's instructions to the people, Jesus is the ultimate mediator between God and humanity (1 Timothy 2:5). This phrase also emphasizes the continuity of God's plan, as the leadership transition from Moses to Joshua is part of the divine strategy to bring Israel into the Promised Land. Moses' role as a prophet and leader is foundational, and his actions set a precedent for future leaders in Israel.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Moses
The leader of the Israelites, chosen by God to lead His people out of Egypt and through the wilderness. In this passage, Moses is following God's command to appoint a successor.

2. Joshua
The son of Nun, chosen by God to succeed Moses as the leader of Israel. Joshua is known for his faithfulness and courage, qualities that make him a fitting leader for the Israelites as they prepare to enter the Promised Land.

3. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant God of Israel, who provides guidance and instruction to His people through His chosen leaders.

4. Israelites
The people of God, who are on the brink of entering the Promised Land after years of wandering in the wilderness.

5. Laying on of Hands
A symbolic act of commissioning and transferring authority, signifying the passing of leadership from Moses to Joshua.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Godly Leadership
Godly leadership is crucial for guiding God's people. Moses' obedience in appointing Joshua demonstrates the importance of following God's direction in leadership transitions.

The Role of Obedience in God's Plan
Moses' actions show that obedience to God's instructions is vital. Even great leaders like Moses must submit to God's will and plan.

The Significance of Spiritual Commissioning
The laying on of hands is more than a ritual; it is a spiritual act that signifies the transfer of authority and the empowerment of the Holy Spirit for the task ahead.

Preparation for Future Challenges
Joshua's commissioning prepares him for the challenges of leading Israel into the Promised Land. Similarly, believers must be prepared and equipped for the tasks God calls them to.

Continuity in God's Work
The transition from Moses to Joshua illustrates the continuity of God's work through different leaders. God's purposes remain constant even as His servants change.

What Does Numbers 27:23 Mean
and laid his hands on him

“Then Moses laid his hands on Joshua…” (Numbers 27:23).

• This visible, physical act confirms God’s choice in front of the whole congregation, removing any doubt (see Numbers 27:18-20).

• Scripture regularly pairs the laying on of hands with blessing and impartation: Jacob blesses Ephraim and Manasseh this way (Genesis 48:14), Aaron transfers sin to the scapegoat (Leviticus 16:21), and the apostles set apart servants for ministry (Acts 6:6; 1 Timothy 4:14).

• The gesture communicates three truths:

– God’s authority is real and can be publicly recognized.

– Leadership comes from God, not personal ambition.

– Spiritual empowerment accompanies the call (Deuteronomy 34:9 notes that Joshua was “filled with the spirit of wisdom” after Moses laid hands on him).

and commissioned him

“…and commissioned him…” (Numbers 27:23).

• Commissioning means granting Joshua the right and responsibility to lead Israel into the Promised Land (Deuteronomy 31:7-8).

• It carries both a promise and a charge:

– Promise: God’s abiding presence (Joshua 1:5; Matthew 28:20).

– Charge: courage and careful obedience to God’s word (Joshua 1:7-9).

• By passing on the commission, Moses models servant leadership that prepares the next generation (2 Timothy 2:2 reminds believers to do the same).

as the LORD had instructed

“…as the LORD had instructed…” (Numbers 27:23).

• Every detail—from selecting Joshua (Numbers 27:18) to the public ceremony—originated with God, underscoring that obedience, not innovation, safeguards God’s people (Exodus 40:16; John 14:31).

• Following divine instruction ensures the transition is blessed and fruitful; ignoring it leads to confusion and loss (contrast Numbers 20:10-12, where Moses’ partial obedience cost him entrance into Canaan).

through Moses

“…through Moses.” (Numbers 27:23).

• God works through chosen servants; Moses acts as mediator, but the authority remains God’s (Exodus 3:10; Hebrews 3:5-6).

• This channel maintains order, preventing rival claims and affirming that true leadership arises from submission to God’s revealed will.

• The pattern anticipates later transitions—Elijah to Elisha (2 Kings 2:9-15) and ultimately Jesus commissioning the apostles (John 20:21-22).

summary

Numbers 27:23 records a divinely directed transfer of leadership. Moses lays hands on Joshua, publicly affirming him, empowers him, and formally charges him, all exactly as God commanded. The verse teaches that legitimate authority, spiritual empowerment, and successful ministry flow from God’s clear instruction, recognized and obeyed within the gathered people of God.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 23. - And gave him a charge. This charge is nowhere recorded, for it cannot possibly be identified with the passing words of exhortation in Deuteronomy 31:7.
