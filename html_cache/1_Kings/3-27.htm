Then the king gave his ruling:
This phrase highlights King Solomon's role as a judge, a position of great responsibility in ancient Israel. Solomon's wisdom, granted by God (1 Kings 3:12), is demonstrated in his ability to discern the truth in difficult situations. This moment is a fulfillment of God's promise to give Solomon a wise and discerning heart, setting a precedent for his reign.

“Give the living baby to the first woman:
The context of this ruling involves two women claiming to be the mother of the same child. Solomon's decision to give the baby to the first woman reveals his insight into human nature and maternal instincts. This reflects the cultural importance of motherhood and the value placed on life in ancient Israel. The decision also underscores the biblical principle of justice and mercy.

By no means should you kill him;
This command emphasizes the sanctity of life, a core biblical value. The preservation of the child's life aligns with the broader scriptural theme that life is a gift from God (Genesis 2:7). Solomon's ruling avoids the potential tragedy of infanticide, which was not uncommon in ancient times, and instead upholds the dignity and worth of the child.

she is his mother.”
Solomon's declaration identifies the true mother through her willingness to sacrifice her claim to save her child's life. This reflects the deep, sacrificial love that is often associated with motherhood. The resolution of this case not only demonstrates Solomon's wisdom but also points to the deeper truth of God's love for His children, as seen in the sacrificial nature of Christ (John 3:16). Solomon's wisdom here can be seen as a type of Christ's ultimate wisdom and justice.

Persons / Places / Events
1. King Solomon
The son of King David and Bathsheba, Solomon is known for his wisdom, which he received from God. In this passage, he demonstrates his wisdom by discerning the true mother of the child.

2. The Two Women
These women are described as prostitutes who come to King Solomon with a dispute over a living child, each claiming to be the mother. Their identities are not as important as the moral and ethical dilemma they present.

3. Jerusalem
The city where King Solomon reigned and where this judgment took place. It is the political and spiritual center of Israel during Solomon's reign.

4. The Living Baby
The child at the center of the dispute. Solomon's decision regarding the child reveals his wisdom and understanding of human nature.

5. The Court of Solomon
The setting for this event, where Solomon's wisdom is publicly demonstrated, establishing his reputation as a wise and just ruler.
Teaching Points
The Value of Godly Wisdom
Solomon's decision exemplifies the importance of seeking wisdom from God. Believers are encouraged to prioritize divine wisdom over worldly knowledge.

Discernment in Leadership
Effective leadership requires discernment and the ability to make just decisions. Solomon's ruling teaches us the importance of understanding human nature and motives.

The Heart's True Intentions
The account reveals that true intentions are often revealed under pressure. As believers, we should strive for purity of heart and sincerity in our actions.

The Role of Justice in Society
Justice is a foundational principle in God's kingdom. Solomon's judgment underscores the need for fairness and righteousness in our dealings with others.

The Power of Compassion
Solomon's compassion for the child and the true mother reflects God's compassion for His people. We are called to act with compassion in our interactions.

What Does 1 Kings 3:27 Mean
Then the king gave his ruling

• Solomon acts in the very role for which he prayed, having asked the LORD, “Give Your servant an understanding heart to judge Your people” (1 Kings 3:9).

• His decision springs from God’s promise, “I will give you a wise and discerning heart” (1 Kings 3:12).

• The king’s prompt, decisive judgment pictures righteous leadership envisioned in Deuteronomy 17:18-20 and echoed when Jesus urges, “Judge with righteous judgment” (John 7:24).

• James 1:5 reminds us that the same God gladly grants wisdom to all who ask in faith.

Give the living baby to the first woman

• Life is preserved first; justice never ignores mercy. Psalm 82:3-4 commands rulers to “uphold the cause of the weak… deliver them from the hand of the wicked.”

• Solomon’s choice reflects the heart of God, who “delivers the afflicted” (2 Samuel 14:16) and calls His people to the same tenderness (Luke 6:36).

• By placing the child safely in his true mother’s arms, the king demonstrates that real wisdom seeks restoration, not merely a verdict.

By no means should you kill him

• The command protects innocent life, aligning with “You shall not murder” (Exodus 20:13) and God’s post-flood decree that human life is sacred (Genesis 9:6).

• Solomon exposes the counterfeit mother’s cruelty, mirroring how Jesus came “that they may have life” (John 10:10) and how the Father is “not willing that any of these little ones should perish” (Matthew 18:14).

• True justice defends life even when expediency tempts us to harm or dismiss it.

she is his mother

• A mother’s self-sacrificing love becomes the decisive evidence. Isaiah 49:15 asks, “Can a woman forget her nursing child?”—a rhetorical question Solomon effectively answers.

• The apostle Paul pictures such care when he says, “We were gentle among you, like a nursing mother caring for her own children” (1 Thessalonians 2:7).

• At the cross, Jesus similarly honored maternal devotion, entrusting Mary to John (John 19:26-27).

• Solomon thus recognizes that authentic love, not mere biology or words, establishes true relationship.

summary

Solomon’s ruling showcases God-given wisdom that values life, uncovers truth, and honors sacrificial love. The episode calls every believer to seek the Lord’s discernment, defend the vulnerable, and let genuine compassion guide our judgments.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 27. - Then the king answered and said [He simply echoes the exact words of the mother. This is clear from the fact that the word יָלוּד - natus, "the one born," here and in ver. 26 rendered "child," is a very unusual one], Give her the living child, and in no wise slay it [The LXX., which reads "Give the child to her who said, Give it to her," etc., obscures the evidently designed repetition] she is the mother thereof [Heb. she, his mother].
