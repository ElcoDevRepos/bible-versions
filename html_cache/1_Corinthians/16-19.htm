Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
The churches in the province of Asia send you greetings.
The province of Asia refers to the western part of modern-day Turkey, a significant region for early Christianity. This area included cities like Ephesus, Smyrna, and Pergamum, which are also mentioned in Revelation. The churches in Asia were established during Paul's missionary journeys, and their greeting signifies the unity and fellowship among early Christian communities. This reflects the interconnectedness of the early church, emphasizing the importance of mutual support and encouragement among believers, as seen in other epistles like Romans 16:16 and 1 Thessalonians 5:26.

Aquila and Prisca greet you warmly in the Lord,
Aquila and Prisca (also known as Priscilla) were a Jewish Christian couple who played a crucial role in the early church. They were tentmakers by trade, like Paul, and had previously lived in Rome before being expelled by Emperor Claudius. Their mention here highlights their ongoing commitment to the ministry and their close relationship with Paul. They are noted for their theological understanding and teaching, as seen in Acts 18:26, where they instruct Apollos. Their greeting "in the Lord" underscores the spiritual bond and shared faith that transcends geographical distances.

and so does the church that meets at their house.
House churches were common in the early Christian era due to the lack of formal church buildings. Aquila and Prisca's home served as a meeting place for believers, indicating their leadership and hospitality. This practice of meeting in homes is seen throughout the New Testament, such as in Romans 16:5 and Philemon 1:2, and reflects the intimate and communal nature of early Christian worship. The mention of their house church also highlights the role of laypeople in the spread of Christianity and the importance of the domestic sphere in the life of the early church.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Aquila and Priscilla
A married couple who were tentmakers by trade and close companions of Paul. They played a significant role in the early church, hosting a congregation in their home and mentoring Apollos, a learned man in the Scriptures.

2. The Province of Asia
A Roman province in what is now western Turkey. It was a significant region for early Christian missionary activity, with cities like Ephesus being central to Paul's ministry.

3. The Church that Meets at Their House
This refers to the early Christian practice of meeting in homes for worship and fellowship. House churches were common due to the lack of formal church buildings and the need for intimate community gatherings.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Hospitality in Ministry
Aquila and Priscilla exemplify the role of hospitality in the early church. Their home was a place of worship and teaching, showing that our homes can be centers for ministry and fellowship.

The Role of Lay Leaders in the Church
As tentmakers, Aquila and Priscilla were not full-time ministers, yet they significantly impacted the early church. This highlights the vital role of lay leaders in spreading the Gospel and discipling others.

Unity and Connection Among Churches
The greetings from the churches in Asia reflect the interconnectedness of the early Christian communities. This unity is a model for modern churches to support and encourage one another across geographical and denominational lines.

Mentorship and Discipleship
Aquila and Priscilla's mentorship of Apollos underscores the importance of investing in others' spiritual growth. We are called to teach and guide others in their faith journey.

What Does 1 Corinthians 16:19 Mean
The churches in the province of Asia send you greetings

• Paul is writing from Ephesus (1 Corinthians 16:8), the chief city of the Roman province called “Asia.”

• These congregations—including Ephesus, Smyrna, Pergamum, Thyatira, Sardis, Philadelphia, and Laodicea (Revelation 1:4, 11)—join Paul in extending personal love to the believers at Corinth.

• Their greeting reminds us that the body of Christ is larger than any one fellowship. Just as Acts 19:10 records that “all who lived in Asia heard the word of the Lord,” so Christians today share a global kinship and responsibility to encourage one another (Hebrews 3:13).

Aquila and Prisca greet you warmly in the Lord

• Aquila and Prisca (also called Priscilla) were Jewish tentmakers who first met Paul in Corinth (Acts 18:2-3). Their partnership with him in ministry was deep and sacrificial (Romans 16:3-4).

• “Warmly in the Lord” shows that genuine affection among believers is rooted in Christ, not mere sentimentality (Philippians 1:8).

• Their greeting highlights constancy in friendship: years after leaving Corinth, they still care for the church there (2 Timothy 4:19).

and so does the church that meets at their house

• Early believers often gathered in homes (Romans 16:5; Colossians 4:15; Phm 2). Aquila and Prisca offered their dwelling as a meeting place, demonstrating stewardship of their resources for gospel purposes.

• A house church setting fostered close fellowship, mutual edification, and accountability (Acts 2:46-47).

• Their example encourages every household to view its space as a potential hub for hospitality and ministry (1 Peter 4:9-10).

summary

Paul’s brief sentence in 1 Corinthians 16:19 weaves together three cords: the unity of geographically scattered churches, the enduring friendship of faithful co-laborers, and the warmth of believers who open their homes for worship. Together they paint a picture of a vibrant, interconnected, and loving body of Christ, urging us to maintain strong ties across congregations, cherish long-standing partnerships, and use our homes and resources for the advance of the gospel.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(19) The churches of Asia salute you.--This and the following verse are occupied with the salutations from the churches throughout Asia; from the church in the house of Aquila and Priscilla; and finally, from "all the brethren." Aquila and Priscilla had been the Apostle's friends at Corinth (Acts 18:1-3), and he now was with them at Ephesus. (See Romans 16:3-5; 2Timothy 4:19.) Probably by "the church in their house" is meant a group of foreigners then resident in Ephesus, and accustomed to meet there for worship, as distinct from those who had been converted in Ephesus.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 19-24. - Salutations and autograph conclusion. Verse 19. - The Churches of Asia. Proconsular Asia. There was a constant interchange of voyages between the western coast of Asia and Corinth. Aquila and Priscilla. This admirable Christian husband and wife had no small share in founding the Churches both of Corinth and Ephesus. Being St. Paul's partners in trade, he spent much time with them. (For all that is known of them. see Acts 18:1, 2, 26; Romans 16:3, 5.) Priscilla. Most of the uncials have the shorter form, Prisca. In some manuscripts (D, E, F, G) and versions (e.g. the Vulgate) we find the addition, "with whom also I am lodging." The Church that is in their house. The time for large common churches for public worship had not yet arrived, Hence, when the Christian community numbered more than could meet in one place, the congregations were held in separate houses (Romans 16:4, 15; Acts 2:46; Colossians 4:15; Philemon 1:2).
