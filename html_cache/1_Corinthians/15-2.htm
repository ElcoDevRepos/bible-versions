Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
By this gospel you are saved
The term "gospel" refers to the good news of Jesus Christ's death, burial, and resurrection, which is central to Christian faith. This message is the power of God for salvation to everyone who believes (Romans 1:16). The concept of salvation is deeply rooted in the Old Testament, where God is often depicted as a deliverer (Psalm 18:2). In the New Testament, salvation is understood as deliverance from sin and its consequences, achieved through faith in Jesus Christ (Ephesians 2:8-9). The assurance of salvation is a key theme in Paul's letters, emphasizing that it is not by works but by grace through faith.

if you hold firmly to the word I preached to you
The condition "if you hold firmly" suggests the necessity of perseverance in faith. This echoes Jesus' teaching in the Parable of the Sower, where only those who hold fast to the word bear fruit (Luke 8:15). The "word" refers to the apostolic teaching, which is consistent with the teachings of Jesus and the Scriptures. The early church faced various challenges, including false teachings, which required believers to remain steadfast (Galatians 1:6-9). The call to hold firmly underscores the importance of doctrinal purity and personal commitment to the truth of the gospel.

Otherwise, you have believed in vain
The phrase "believed in vain" warns against a superficial or temporary faith that does not result in genuine transformation or perseverance. This is reminiscent of James 2:17, which states that faith without works is dead. The historical context of Corinth, a city known for its moral and spiritual challenges, highlights the need for a faith that is both genuine and enduring. Paul emphasizes that true belief must be accompanied by a life that reflects the gospel's transformative power (2 Corinthians 5:17). The warning serves as a reminder that mere intellectual assent is insufficient for salvation; it must be accompanied by a living faith that endures to the end (Matthew 24:13).

3. The Gospel
The good news of Jesus Christ's death, burial, and resurrection, which is central to Christian faith and salvation.

4. Salvation

The act of trusting in the gospel message, which is essential for salvation according to Paul's teaching.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Perseverance in Faith
Salvation is not just a one-time event but a continuous journey of faith. Believers are called to hold firmly to the gospel throughout their lives.

The Danger of Believing in Vain
A superficial or temporary belief that does not endure is considered vain. True faith is evidenced by perseverance and commitment to the gospel.

The Centrality of the Gospel
The gospel is the foundation of Christian faith. Understanding and adhering to its core message is crucial for salvation.

The Role of the Preached Word
The preached word is a means through which God communicates His truth. Believers should value and respond to sound biblical teaching.

Assurance of Salvation
Assurance comes from a steadfast faith in the gospel. Believers can have confidence in their salvation as they continue to trust in Christ.

What Does 1 Corinthians 15:2 Mean
By this gospel you are saved

The sentence opens with an unshakable promise. Salvation rests on the gospel Paul has just defined (1 Corinthians 15:3-4).

• The gospel is not advice but “the power of God for salvation to everyone who believes” (Romans 1:16).

• It is entirely grace-based: “For it is by grace you have been saved through faith… not by works” (Ephesians 2:8-9).

• Because Jesus died, was buried, and rose again “according to the Scriptures,” the believer’s rescue is already accomplished, not tentative.

if you hold firmly to the word I preached to you

Here Paul highlights the necessary response to that finished work.

• Genuine faith continues; it is “established and firm, not moved from the hope of the gospel” (Colossians 1:23).

• Jesus said, “If you continue in My word, you are truly My disciples” (John 8:31). Perseverance is not an added requirement; it is the evidence that faith is real.

• Think of the seed in good soil: it “hears the word and understands it,” then produces a harvest (Matthew 13:23). The grip on the gospel is active and lifelong.

Otherwise, you have believed in vain

A sobering caution follows.

• Superficial belief can spring up quickly and then fade: “They believe for a while, but in the time of testing they fall away” (Luke 8:13).

• James warns that “faith by itself, if it does not result in action, is dead” (James 2:17). Empty belief never unites a person to Christ.

• Hebrews echoes Paul: “We have come to share in Christ if we hold firmly to the end the confidence we had at first” (Hebrews 3:14). The point is not losing salvation but revealing whether it was ever possessed.

summary

1 Corinthians 15:2 packs comfort and challenge into one line. The gospel alone saves—because Jesus finished the work. Those truly rescued will cling to that message, proving its reality over time. A temporary or casual association with the gospel, however enthusiastic at first, ultimately shows itself hollow. Hold tight to the word proclaimed, and rest assured that the salvation secured by Christ is eternally yours.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(2) If ye keep in memory what I preached unto you.--Better, if ye hold fast with what word I preached the gospel to you, unless you believed in vain. The idea here is not, as implied in the English version, that they were converted, and yet that heretofore no results have followed from their belief; it is the same thought which comes out more fully in 1Corinthians 15:17. They are saved by their faith in the gospel as preached by St. Paul, unless (which is impossible) the whole gospel be false, and so their faith in it be vain and useless.
