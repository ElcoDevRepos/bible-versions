Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
As it is,
This phrase introduces a conclusion drawn from the preceding discussion about the diversity of spiritual gifts and the unity of the body of Christ. Paul is addressing the Corinthian church, which was experiencing division and competition among its members. The phrase sets the stage for a statement of fact about the current state of the church, emphasizing the reality of diversity within unity.

there are many parts,
Paul uses the metaphor of the human body to illustrate the diversity of roles and functions within the church. Each "part" represents individual believers with unique gifts and callings. This reflects the broader biblical theme of diversity within God's creation, as seen in Genesis 1, where God creates a variety of living beings. The diversity of parts is essential for the functioning of the whole, just as different members of the church contribute to its overall mission.

but one body.
The unity of the body is a central theme in Paul's letters, particularly in Ephesians 4:4-6, where he speaks of one body, one Spirit, and one hope. This unity is not uniformity but a harmonious integration of diverse parts working together. The "one body" metaphor underscores the interconnectedness of believers, who are united in Christ, the head of the body (Colossians 1:18). This unity is a reflection of the unity within the Trinity and serves as a witness to the world of God's reconciling work through Jesus Christ.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Apostle Paul
The author of 1 Corinthians, Paul was addressing the church in Corinth, a city known for its diversity and complexity. He wrote to correct issues and provide guidance on living a Christian life.

2. Corinthian Church
A diverse congregation with various spiritual gifts, facing issues of division and misunderstanding about the nature of spiritual gifts and unity.

3. Body of Christ
A metaphor used by Paul to describe the church, emphasizing unity and diversity among believers.
Teaching Points
Unity in Diversity
The church is made up of diverse individuals with different gifts and roles, yet all are essential to the functioning of the body. Embrace and celebrate diversity within the church.

Interdependence
Just as the human body relies on each part to function properly, so too does the church rely on each member. Recognize and appreciate the contributions of others.

Avoiding Division
Division weakens the body. Strive for unity and harmony, understanding that each member is vital to the health of the church.

Using Gifts for the Common Good
Spiritual gifts are given for the benefit of the whole body. Use your gifts to serve others and build up the church.

Christ as the Head
Remember that Christ is the head of the body, and all members should align their actions and attitudes with His teachings and example.

What Does 1 Corinthians 12:20 Mean
As it is

• Paul has just finished illustrating how God arranges the members of the church with intentionality (1 Corinthians 12:18). He now draws a settled conclusion: what God has done “stands.”

• The phrase underlines divine sovereignty; the present condition of the church is not accidental but ordained (Romans 9:20–21; Ephesians 1:11).

• Readers are invited to accept God’s design rather than resist it, echoing the submissive attitude called for in James 4:7.

there are many parts

• Diversity is real and valuable. Eyes differ from ears, hands from feet, and each member of Christ’s body has a unique contribution (1 Corinthians 12:14–19).

• This diversity reflects God’s creative wisdom displayed in nature (Psalm 104:24) and in the distribution of spiritual gifts (Romans 12:4–6).

• No believer is redundant; everyone—from apostles to helpers—has been placed by God to serve His purpose (Ephesians 4:16).

but one body

• Unity balances diversity. While gifts and roles vary, all believers share one spiritual life in Christ (Galatians 3:28; Colossians 3:15).

• The single body draws its identity from its Head, Jesus Christ (Ephesians 1:22–23).

• This oneness calls for mutual honor and care: “If one part suffers, every part suffers with it; if one part is honored, every part rejoices with it” (1 Corinthians 12:26).

• Practical outworking: avoid envy and pride, pursue loving cooperation (Philippians 2:1–4).

summary

1 Corinthians 12:20 anchors Paul’s teaching on spiritual gifts in two complementary truths: God-given diversity and Christ-centered unity. Each believer is intentionally different, yet all belong inseparably to one body. Embracing both realities guards the church from division and releases every member to serve for the common good and the glory of God.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(20) But now are they.--From the reductio ad absurdum of the previous verses the Apostle turns to the fact as it is, and proceeds (in 1Corinthians 12:21) to state that there is a mutual interdependence in the members of the body. The eye is dependent on the hand, the head upon the feet. Here, no doubt, the illustration is drawn out in this particular direction to rebuke those who being themselves possessed of what were considered important spiritual gifts despised the gifts which the Spirit had bestowed on others.
