Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
So Sarah conceived
This phrase marks the fulfillment of God's promise to Abraham and Sarah, highlighting the miraculous nature of Sarah's conception. Sarah was well past the age of childbearing, emphasizing the divine intervention required for her to conceive. This event underscores the theme of God's faithfulness and power to fulfill His promises, even when circumstances seem impossible. It also prefigures the miraculous birth of Jesus Christ, born of the Virgin Mary, as both births were divinely orchestrated.

and bore a son to Abraham
The birth of Isaac is significant as it establishes the lineage through which God's covenant with Abraham would continue. Isaac is the child of promise, through whom God would build a great nation. This event is a pivotal moment in the biblical narrative, as it sets the stage for the development of the Israelite people. The birth of a son to Abraham also fulfills the cultural importance of having an heir, which was crucial in ancient Near Eastern societies for the continuation of one's lineage and inheritance.

in his old age
Abraham was 100 years old when Isaac was born, highlighting the miraculous nature of the event. This detail emphasizes God's ability to work beyond human limitations and expectations. It also serves as a testament to Abraham's faith, as he believed God's promise despite his and Sarah's advanced age. This aspect of the story is echoed in the New Testament, where Abraham's faith is commended in Romans 4:19-21.

at the very time God had promised
This phrase underscores the precision and reliability of God's timing. God's promise to Abraham regarding the birth of Isaac was specific, and its fulfillment at the appointed time demonstrates God's sovereignty and faithfulness. This theme of divine timing is prevalent throughout Scripture, reminding believers that God's plans unfold according to His perfect schedule. The fulfillment of God's promise to Abraham also foreshadows the fulfillment of messianic prophecies, culminating in the birth of Jesus Christ at the appointed time (Galatians 4:4).

Persons / Places / Events

2. Abraham
The patriarch of the Israelites, who was promised by God to be the father of many nations. His faith and obedience are central themes in his account.

3. Isaac
The son born to Abraham and Sarah in their old age, representing the fulfillment of God's promise and the continuation of Abraham's lineage.

4. God's Promise
The event of Isaac's birth is a direct fulfillment of God's promise to Abraham and Sarah, showcasing God's faithfulness and timing.

5. Old Age
The advanced age of Abraham and Sarah highlights the miraculous nature of Isaac's birth and underscores the power of God to accomplish His will against all odds.
Teaching Points
God's Faithfulness
God's promises are sure and will be fulfilled in His perfect timing, regardless of human limitations or circumstances.

The Power of Faith
Abraham and Sarah's account encourages believers to maintain faith in God's promises, even when situations seem impossible.

Divine Timing
God's timing is perfect, and His plans are often beyond human understanding. Trusting in His timing is crucial for spiritual growth.

Miracles and God's Sovereignty
The birth of Isaac is a reminder of God's sovereignty and His ability to perform miracles, reinforcing the belief in His omnipotence.

Legacy of Faith
Isaac's birth is not just a personal blessing to Abraham and Sarah but a pivotal moment in the history of God's people, showing how individual faithfulness can impact future generations.

What Does Genesis 21:2 Mean
So Sarah conceived

- “So Sarah conceived…” (Genesis 21:2) moves the promise from spoken word (Genesis 18:10) to physical reality.

- God opens a womb man had called barren (Genesis 11:30).

- The event echoes later declarations that “nothing will be impossible with God” (Luke 1:37).

and bore a son

- The phrase continues: “…and bore a son…”. Promise progresses to completion (Genesis 17:16).

- God always finishes what He begins (Philippians 1:6).

- The safe delivery underscores His tender care, just as He carried Israel “as on eagles’ wings” (Exodus 19:4).

to Abraham

- The birth is specifically “to Abraham,” knitting Isaac into the covenant line of Genesis 12:2–3; 15:4–6.

- Romans 4:18–19 celebrates Abraham’s faith when circumstances screamed otherwise.

- Through this son, the nations will be blessed (Galatians 3:8).

in his old age

- Abraham is one hundred (Genesis 21:5). Sarah is ninety (Genesis 17:17).

- Their frailty magnifies God’s strength (2 Corinthians 12:9).

- The miracle mirrors other “against-all-odds” victories, like Gideon’s tiny army (Judges 7:2).

at the very time God had promised

- The birth arrives “at the very time” announced in Genesis 17:21 and 18:14.

- God’s calendar is exact: “My word… will accomplish what I please” (Isaiah 55:11).

- Galatians 4:4 later shows the same precision in sending Christ “when the fullness of time had come.”

summary

Genesis 21:2 affirms—literally and historically—that God did exactly what He said: the barren became fruitful, the aged became parents, and the promise became a living child right on schedule. The verse invites every believer to rest confidently in the God whose word never fails.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 2. - For Sarah conceived, - through faith receiving strength from God for that purpose (Hebrews 11:11); the fruit of the womb, in every instance God's handiwork (Isaiah 44:2), being in her case a special gift of grace and product of Divine power - and bare - the usual construction (Genesis 29:32; Genesis 30:5) is here somewhat modified by the Jehovist (Kalisch); but the clause may be compared with Genesis 30:22, 23, commonly assigned to the Elohlst - Abraham (literally, to Abraham) a son in his old age, - literally, to his old age; εἰς τὸ γῆρας (LXX.) - at the set time (vide Genesis 17:21; Genesis 18:10, 14) of which God had spoken to him. God's word gave Abraham strength to beget, Sarah to conceive, and Isaac to come forth. Three times repeated in two verses, the clause points to the supernatural character of Isaac's birth.
