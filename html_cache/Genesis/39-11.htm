Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
One day, however,
This phrase sets the scene for a specific event in Joseph's life, indicating a turning point. The use of "however" suggests a contrast to previous events, highlighting the unexpected nature of what is about to occur. Joseph's life is marked by divine providence, and this moment is pivotal in God's plan for him.

Joseph went into the house
Joseph, a Hebrew slave in Egypt, had been entrusted with significant responsibilities in Potiphar's household. His entry into the house signifies his diligence and commitment to his duties. This setting is crucial as it becomes the backdrop for a significant moral test. The house, a place of both opportunity and temptation, reflects the dual nature of Joseph's circumstances.

to attend to his work,
Joseph's work ethic is emphasized here, showcasing his integrity and dedication. Despite being a slave, he serves faithfully, which is a testament to his character. This diligence is a recurring theme in Joseph's life, aligning with Proverbs 22:29, which speaks of skilled work leading to positions of honor.

and not a single household servant was inside.
The absence of other servants sets the stage for the ensuing temptation. This detail highlights the isolation Joseph faces, making him vulnerable to Potiphar's wife's advances. The lack of witnesses underscores the gravity of the situation, as Joseph's integrity is tested in private. This moment foreshadows the trials of Jesus, who also faced temptation alone in the wilderness (Matthew 4:1-11).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Joseph
A Hebrew man sold into slavery by his brothers, now serving in the house of Potiphar, an Egyptian officer. Joseph is known for his integrity and faithfulness to God.

2. Potiphar's House
The setting of this event, where Joseph is entrusted with significant responsibility due to his trustworthy nature and God's favor upon him.

3. Potiphar's Wife
Although not mentioned directly in this verse, she plays a crucial role in the surrounding account, attempting to seduce Joseph and later falsely accusing him.

4. The Household Servants
Their absence is significant in this verse, setting the stage for the temptation and false accusation that follows.

5. The Event
Joseph enters the house to perform his duties, unaware that he is alone, which leads to a pivotal moment of temptation and testing of his character.
Teaching Points
Integrity in Isolation
Joseph's situation reminds us that true integrity is demonstrated not only in public but also in private, when no one else is watching.

The Importance of Fleeing Temptation
Joseph's response to temptation is a model for us; he physically removes himself from the situation, illustrating the importance of taking decisive action against sin.

God's Presence in Trials
Despite the absence of others, God is with Joseph, providing strength and guidance. We can trust in God's presence and support during our own trials.

Consequences of False Accusations
Joseph's account highlights the reality of suffering unjustly, yet it also shows that God can use such situations for His greater purpose.

Faithfulness Leads to Favor
Joseph's faithfulness to God and his duties leads to favor, even in difficult circumstances. Our faithfulness can similarly lead to God's blessings in our lives.

What Does Genesis 39:11 Mean
One day, however

• Scripture notes, “One day, however…”. The phrase signals an ordinary day suddenly charged with spiritual significance, much like David’s “one evening” in 2 Samuel 11:2.

• Temptation often strikes without warning; vigilance must remain constant (1 Peter 5:8; 1 Corinthians 10:12).

• God’s providence governs even the timing of such moments (Genesis 50:20), assuring us that every test fits within His sovereign plan.

Joseph went into the house

• Joseph enters the very place where Potiphar’s wife has previously tried to lure him (Genesis 39:7–10).

• Faithful believers cannot always avoid places where sin lurks, but they can guard their hearts (Psalm 119:11; John 17:15).

• Joseph’s continued presence in the house shows commitment to duty rather than cowardly flight from responsibility (Luke 16:10).

• His situation foreshadows later saints who must work in worldly settings yet remain untainted (Philippians 2:15).

to attend to his work

• The text highlights Joseph’s diligence: he “went into the house to attend to his work”.

• Work itself is honorable and God-given (Genesis 2:15; Colossians 3:23).

• Joseph’s integrity contrasts with the predatory intentions of Potiphar’s wife, underscoring that temptation often targets those who serve faithfully (Job 1:8–12).

• Doing right does not exempt us from trials; it may invite them (2 Timothy 3:12).

and not a single household servant was inside

• Isolation intensifies temptation. Potiphar’s wife deliberately engineered privacy (Proverbs 7:18–20).

• Joseph now faces a test of character with no human witnesses, but “the eyes of the LORD are in every place” (Proverbs 15:3).

• The verse exposes the enemy’s tactic: remove accountability, then press for sin (Ecclesiastes 4:10; Ephesians 5:11).

• Joseph’s subsequent flight (v. 12) models the New Testament command to “flee sexual immorality” (1 Corinthians 6:18) and confirms that the way of escape promised in 1 Corinthians 10:13 is real and practical.

summary

Genesis 39:11 sets the stage for a sudden, high-stakes temptation. On an ordinary day Joseph faithfully steps into his assigned work, only to find himself alone with a persistent seductress. The verse teaches that:

• Everyday obedience can intersect with extraordinary testing.

• Faithful service does not shield us from temptation but equips us to overcome it.

• Isolation demands heightened vigilance, yet God always sees and provides a way out.

The narrative urges believers to stay diligent, keep accountable, and trust the Lord who watches over every “one day, however” in life.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 11, 12. - And it came to pass about this time (literally, at this day, i.e. it one day happened), that Joseph went into the house to do his business (i.e. to attend to his accustomed duties); and there was none of the men of the house there within (or, in the hour). And she caught him by his garment (this was probably the long loose robe or mantle, with short sleeves, used in Oriental full dress), saying, Lie with me: and he left his garment in her hand, and fled, and got him out - literally, and went forth into the place without, i.e. out of the house and into the street.
