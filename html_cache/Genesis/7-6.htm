Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Now Noah was 600 years old
Noah's age at the time of the flood is significant in several ways. First, it highlights the longevity of pre-flood generations, which is a common theme in the genealogies of Genesis. This longevity is often interpreted as a sign of the antediluvian world’s unique conditions. Noah's age also underscores his wisdom and experience, qualities that would have been necessary for the monumental task of building the ark and leading his family through the flood. In the broader biblical narrative, Noah's age connects him to the patriarchs, who also lived long lives, suggesting a continuity of God's covenantal promises through these chosen individuals.

when the floodwaters came upon the earth
The floodwaters are a central element of the Genesis flood narrative, representing God's judgment on a corrupt and violent world. This event is often compared to other ancient flood stories, such as the Epic of Gilgamesh, though the biblical account is unique in its theological emphasis on divine justice and mercy. The floodwaters symbolize both destruction and purification, as they cleanse the earth of wickedness while preserving Noah and his family. This duality is echoed in later biblical themes, such as baptism, which represents both death to sin and new life in Christ. The flood also prefigures the final judgment, as described in passages like 2 Peter 3:6-7, where the earth is reserved for fire, not water, in the ultimate purification and renewal.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Noah
A righteous man chosen by God to survive the flood and preserve human and animal life. His obedience and faith are central to the account.

2. The Flood
A cataclysmic event sent by God to cleanse the earth of its widespread wickedness and corruption.

3. The Earth
The entire world is affected by the flood, symbolizing God's judgment on sin and His power over creation.
Teaching Points
Obedience in Faith
Noah's life exemplifies the importance of obeying God's commands, even when they seem daunting or unprecedented. His faith led to action, which ultimately resulted in salvation for him and his family.

God's Judgment and Mercy
The flood serves as a reminder of God's righteous judgment against sin but also His mercy in providing a way of salvation through the ark. This foreshadows the ultimate salvation through Jesus Christ.

The Importance of Righteous Living
Noah's righteousness set him apart in a corrupt world. Believers are called to live righteously, standing firm in their faith amidst a culture that often opposes God's ways.

Preparedness for God's Timing
Just as Noah prepared for the flood, Christians are called to be ready for Christ's return, living in a way that reflects their hope and trust in God's promises.

What Does Genesis 7:6 Mean
Now

The single word cues a turning point, linking God’s instructions (Genesis 7:1–5) with their fulfillment. It signals immediacy—God’s warning is about to become history, just as He had promised (Hebrews 11:7; 2 Peter 2:5).

Noah was 600 years old

• The age is literal, fitting the extended lifespans recorded before the Flood (Genesis 5:5–32; 9:28–29).

• It underlines God’s patience; six centuries of Noah’s life precede judgment, echoing the long‐suffering nature of the Lord (2 Peter 3:9).

• Noah’s advanced years spotlight divine strength over human frailty: obedience is possible at any stage of life (Genesis 6:22).

When the floodwaters came

• The exact timing is detailed in Genesis 7:11—“on the seventeenth day of the second month”, underscoring historical precision.

• God controls the calendar; nothing is random (Psalm 104:6–9).

• Judgment arrives suddenly after lengthy warning, prefiguring future divine interventions (Matthew 24:37–39).

Upon the earth

• The scope is global: “all the high mountains under all the heavens were covered” (Genesis 7:19).

• Totality of the waters parallels totality of human corruption (Genesis 6:11–13).

• The same planet later receives God’s covenant promise never again to be flooded worldwide (Genesis 9:11–17), highlighting both judgment and mercy.

summary

Genesis 7:6 records a factual milestone—Noah’s 600th year—marking the exact moment God’s long-proclaimed Flood engulfed the whole earth. The verse weaves together God’s patience, Noah’s faithful longevity, precise divine timing, and the universal reach of judgment, all reinforcing the trustworthiness of God’s word and the call to heed it today.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(6) Noah was six hundred years old.--It follows that Shem was about one hundred years of age (comp. Genesis 5:32), and his two brothers younger; but all were married, though apparently without children. (Comp. Genesis 11:10.)

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 6. - And Noah was six hundred years old. Literally, a som of six hundred years, i.e. in his 600th year (cf. ver. 11). The number six "is generally a Scriptural symbol of suffering. Christ suffered on the sixth day. In the Apocalypse the sixth seal, the sixth trumpet, the sixth vial introduce critical periods of affliction" (Wordsworth). When the flood of waters was upon the earth.
