the descendants of Ater
The name "Ater" means "left-handed" or "shut up" in Hebrew. This family is listed among those returning from the Babylonian exile, indicating their commitment to reestablishing their heritage and worship in Jerusalem. The mention of Ater highlights the importance of family lineage and continuity in Jewish culture, as well as the fulfillment of God's promise to restore His people to their land.

(through Hezekiah)
This phrase suggests a connection to King Hezekiah, a righteous king of Judah known for his religious reforms and trust in God (2 Kings 18:5-7). The association with Hezekiah may imply a noble or significant lineage, emphasizing the importance of godly heritage. It also reflects the broader biblical theme of God preserving a faithful remnant through whom He accomplishes His purposes.

98;
The specific number of 98 descendants underscores the precision and care with which the genealogies were recorded. This attention to detail reflects the importance of each family and individual in the restoration process. It also serves as a reminder of God's faithfulness in preserving His people, even in small numbers, to fulfill His covenant promises.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Ater
Ater is a family name mentioned in the genealogical records of those returning from the Babylonian exile. The name "Ater" means "left-handed" or "maimed" in Hebrew, indicating perhaps a physical characteristic or a notable ancestor.

2. Hezekiah
This is likely a reference to a descendant of King Hezekiah, a righteous king of Judah known for his reforms and trust in God. The connection to Hezekiah may indicate a noble lineage or a family that maintained faithfulness to God during the exile.

3. The Return from Exile
This event marks the return of the Jewish people from Babylonian captivity to Jerusalem, as decreed by King Cyrus of Persia. It was a significant moment of restoration and rebuilding for the Jewish community.
Teaching Points
Faithfulness Across Generations
The mention of Ater through Hezekiah highlights the importance of maintaining faithfulness to God across generations. Families today can strive to pass down a legacy of faith and trust in God.

The Significance of Heritage
Understanding one's spiritual heritage can provide encouragement and a sense of identity. Believers are encouraged to explore their spiritual lineage and draw strength from the faith of those who have gone before.

Restoration and New Beginnings
The return from exile symbolizes God's ability to restore and renew. In our lives, God offers new beginnings and opportunities for restoration, no matter our past circumstances.

The Role of Community in God's Plan
The collective return of families and individuals underscores the importance of community in God's redemptive plan. Believers are called to participate actively in their faith communities, supporting and encouraging one another.

What Does Ezra 2:16 Mean
Setting within Ezra 2

• Ezra 2 is a meticulous census of those whom “the LORD stirred to go up” from Babylon to Jerusalem (Ezra 2:1).

• Every line, including “the descendants of Ater (through Hezekiah), 98,” records real families who answered God’s promise of restoration spoken by Jeremiah 29:10.

• Nehemiah later confirms almost the same list (Nehemiah 7:5-7, 21), underscoring literal accuracy.

The Name “Ater”

• “Descendants of Ater” identifies a distinct family clan, just as other lines mention “the sons of Pahath-moab” or “of Zattu.”

• Each surname kept tribal memory alive; God knows households as well as individuals (Numbers 1:2).

• Their mention assures every reader that no obedient family is too small for God’s record (Isaiah 49:16).

Through Hezekiah

• The parenthetical note “through Hezekiah” points to a particular forefather—likely not King Hezekiah but another notable ancestor bearing the same faithful name.

• Scripture often marks lineages by godly progenitors (1 Chronicles 4:14; Ezra 8:2), anchoring identity in a righteous heritage.

• It reminds us that covenant faith runs through generations when parents walk steadfastly (Deuteronomy 7:9).

Numbered at Ninety-Eight

• “98” is precise, echoing Ezra’s careful stewardship (Luke 16:10).

• Counting mattered for allocating temple duties, land, and offerings (Ezra 2:68-70).

• The definitive tally reveals God’s ability to preserve a remnant exactly as foretold (Isaiah 10:20-22; Romans 11:5).

Faithfulness Carried Home

• These ninety-eight travelers left comfort for rubble, believing God would “rebuild the ruins” (Amos 9:14-15).

• Their obedience aligned with leaders like Zerubbabel and Jeshua (Haggai 1:12).

• Though unnamed beyond this verse, their step of faith advanced the lineage that would cradle Messiah (Matthew 1:12-13).

summary

Ezra 2:16 records a literal family—ninety-eight descendants of Ater, traced through an ancestor named Hezekiah—who returned from exile. Their inclusion proves that God values specific households, preserves faithful lineage, and fulfills prophecy exactly, member by member.
