Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
As you go
This phrase indicates a directive for active movement and mission. It reflects the itinerant nature of Jesus' ministry and the early disciples' mission. The instruction to "go" echoes the Great Commission in Matthew 28:19, emphasizing the importance of spreading the Gospel. The context is Jesus sending out the twelve apostles, highlighting the urgency and importance of their mission.

preach this message
The act of preaching is central to the mission of the apostles. It involves proclaiming and teaching the truths of the Gospel. This directive underscores the role of the apostles as heralds of the Good News. The Greek word for "preach" (κηρύσσω) implies a public proclamation, often associated with a royal announcement, which aligns with the message of the coming kingdom.

‘The kingdom of heaven is near.’
This message is the core of Jesus' teaching and ministry. The "kingdom of heaven" refers to God's sovereign rule, which Jesus inaugurated through His life, death, and resurrection. The phrase "is near" suggests both immediacy and fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies, such as those found in Isaiah 9:6-7 and Daniel 2:44. It indicates that the kingdom is both a present reality and a future hope. This message connects to John the Baptist's proclamation in Matthew 3:2 and Jesus' own preaching in Matthew 4:17, emphasizing continuity in the divine plan.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jesus Christ
The central figure in the Gospel of Matthew, Jesus is the one giving the instructions to His disciples. He is the Messiah, the Son of God, who came to bring salvation to humanity.

2. The Disciples
The immediate audience of Jesus' command. They were chosen by Jesus to spread His teachings and perform miracles in His name.

3. The Kingdom of Heaven
A central theme in Jesus' teachings, representing God's sovereign rule and the fulfillment of His promises. It is both a present reality and a future hope.

4. Israel
The initial target audience for the disciples' mission, as Jesus instructed them to focus on the "lost sheep of Israel" in the surrounding context.

5. Galilee
The region where Jesus and His disciples were ministering at the time, known for its diverse population and as a center of Jesus' early ministry.
Teaching Points
The Urgency of the Message
The phrase "is near" implies immediacy and urgency. Believers are called to live with an awareness of the kingdom's nearness and to prioritize sharing this message.

The Nature of the Kingdom
Understanding the kingdom of heaven as both a present reality and a future hope encourages believers to live in a way that reflects God's reign in their lives now.

The Role of the Messenger
Just as the disciples were sent, all believers are called to be messengers of the kingdom, sharing the good news through words and actions.

Repentance and Transformation
The message of the kingdom calls for a response of repentance and transformation, aligning one's life with the values and priorities of God's kingdom.

Faithfulness in Mission
The disciples' mission to Israel reminds us of the importance of being faithful in the specific tasks and callings God has given us, trusting Him for the results.

What Does Matthew 10:7 Mean
As you go

• Jesus sends the Twelve out from village to village (Matthew 10:5-6). Their obedience is not optional; movement is assumed.

• Similar sending language repeats after the resurrection: “Go therefore and make disciples of all nations” (Matthew 28:19-20).

• Mark 16:15, Luke 10:3, and Acts 1:8 echo the same outward thrust, showing that believers are expected to take the initiative rather than wait for seekers to arrive.

• Practical takeaway: wherever daily life leads—workplace, school, neighborhood—the follower of Christ is to be intentionally “going” with gospel purpose.

preach this message

• “Preach” means heralding good news, not merely living a quiet example (Romans 10:14-15; 1 Corinthians 1:21).

• The command is singular: one specific proclamation, not a menu of personal opinions (2 Timothy 4:2).

• Reliance is on God’s authority, not the messenger’s charisma (Luke 24:47-49).

• Practical takeaway: spoken words that center on Christ’s gospel are indispensable; kindness alone is not the Great Commission.

‘The kingdom of heaven is near.’

• John the Baptist (Matthew 3:2) and Jesus Himself (Matthew 4:17) used the identical announcement; the disciples now share their Master’s message.

• “Is near” points to a literal, present arrival because the King is among them (Luke 17:20-21) while also foreshadowing the future visible reign (Revelation 11:15).

• Mark 1:15 couples the phrase with a call to repent and believe—urgency is embedded in the statement.

• Hebrews 12:28 reminds believers they are already receiving an unshakable kingdom, affirming both present reality and coming fulfillment.

• Practical takeaway: the nearness of God’s rule demands immediate response—repentance, faith, and allegiance today, hopeful anticipation of Christ’s return tomorrow.

summary

Matthew 10:7 charges every disciple to live on the move, verbally declare the singular gospel, and announce the urgent, present-and-coming reign of God through Christ. The King has drawn near, so His followers must go out.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(7) Preach--i.e., "proclaim--act as heralds," as elsewhere. The repetition of the self-same words as had described first the Baptist's teaching and then our Lord's, seems to suggest that this was actually a formula of proclamation. The two envoys of the King were to enter into town or village, and there, standing in the gate, to announce that His kingdom had come near, and then, when this had drawn crowds to listen, to call men to the repentance without which they could not enter it.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 7. - Parallel passages: Luke 9:2 (the twelve); 10:9 (the seventy; observe that the substance of the proclamation was to be the same). And as ye go. For your journey is not to one place, but many. Preach. Aloud and publicly. Saying, The kingdom of heaven is at hand. That which men had so long been desiring (vide Matthew 3:2; Matthew 4:17) was now near. But had it not come (Matthew 12:28; Matthew 11:12)? Not in full realization. But its near realization was then a possibility, and was only not brought about because, as a nation, they rejected him who introduced it.
