Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
But he had no union with her
This phrase emphasizes Joseph's obedience and righteousness. In the cultural context of first-century Judea, marriage was a significant covenant, and sexual relations were expected after the betrothal period. However, Joseph refrained from consummating the marriage until after Jesus' birth, highlighting his respect for God's plan and the miraculous nature of Jesus' conception. This action also fulfills the prophecy of the virgin birth found in Isaiah 7:14, underscoring the divine nature of Jesus' origin.

until she gave birth to a Son
The phrase indicates the fulfillment of the prophecy concerning the virgin birth. The birth of a son to a virgin was a sign of the Messiah, as foretold in Isaiah 7:14. This event marks a pivotal moment in salvation history, as it introduces Jesus, who is both fully divine and fully human. The birth of Jesus in Bethlehem, as recorded in Matthew 2:1, aligns with the prophecy in Micah 5:2, which foretold the birthplace of the Messiah.

And he gave Him the name Jesus
The naming of Jesus by Joseph is significant for several reasons. In Jewish tradition, the father naming the child signifies legal paternity and acceptance into the Davidic line, fulfilling the messianic prophecy that the Messiah would come from the house of David (2 Samuel 7:12-16). The name "Jesus" is the Greek form of "Yeshua" or "Joshua," meaning "The Lord is salvation." This name reflects His mission to save His people from their sins, as stated in Matthew 1:21. The act of naming also signifies Joseph's obedience to the angel's command in Matthew 1:21, demonstrating his faithfulness to God's instructions.

Teaching Points
The Importance of Obedience
Joseph's obedience to God's command, despite societal norms and personal doubts, serves as a model for us to trust and follow God's will.

The Fulfillment of Prophecy
The virgin birth of Jesus is a testament to God's faithfulness in fulfilling His promises, encouraging us to trust in His Word.

The Significance of Names
The name "Jesus" means "The Lord saves," reflecting His mission to save humanity from sin. This reminds us of the power and purpose behind names in Scripture.

The Role of Faith in Uncertainty
Both Joseph and Mary demonstrated faith in God's plan despite the unknowns, encouraging us to rely on God in uncertain times.

The Divine Nature of Jesus
The virgin birth underscores Jesus' divine nature, affirming His identity as both fully God and fully man, which is central to our faith.

What Does Matthew 1:25 Mean
But

• The verse opens with a contrast, linking back to Joseph’s earlier resolve to obey the angel’s command (Matthew 1:20-24).

• “But” underscores Joseph’s decisive turn from normal marital expectations to a divinely directed restraint, highlighting obedience over personal desire (cf. Genesis 6:22; Luke 1:38).

he had no union with her

• Joseph and Mary lived together after their betrothal, yet Joseph chose abstinence, protecting the miracle of the virgin conception (Isaiah 7:14; Luke 1:34-35).

• This deliberate purity testifies that Jesus’ origin is heavenly, not humanly engineered (John 1:14; Galatians 4:4).

• It models self-discipline for the sake of God’s purpose, echoing Paul’s teaching on yielding personal rights for the gospel (1 Corinthians 9:12-15).

until she gave birth to a Son

• “Until” marks the completion of prophecy without implying perpetual virginity; Scripture later records Jesus’ brothers and sisters (Matthew 13:55-56; Mark 6:3).

• The time-marker stresses that, by the moment of birth, every messianic prediction regarding a virgin birth stood fulfilled (Micah 5:2 alongside Luke 2:1-7).

• Joseph’s restraint ensured unmistakable evidence that the Child is “Immanuel”—God with us (Matthew 1:23).

And he gave Him the name Jesus

• Naming was a father’s legal act, formally acknowledging the Child as his own, thereby grafting Jesus into David’s royal line (2 Samuel 7:12-16; Romans 1:3).

• Joseph’s obedience completes the angel’s instructions (Matthew 1:21), displaying faith through action (James 2:17).

• “Jesus” means “The Lord saves,” capturing the essence of His mission: to save His people from their sins (Luke 19:10; 1 Timothy 1:15).

summary

Matthew 1:25 spotlights Joseph’s faithful obedience, Mary’s maintained virginity until Jesus’ birth, and the legally significant naming of the Child. Each detail safeguards the prophecy of a virgin-born Messiah, secures Jesus’ rightful place in David’s lineage, and proclaims His saving purpose.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(25) Till she had brought forth her first-born son.--The word "firstborn" is not found in the best MSS. The questions which meet us here, unprofitable as they are, cannot be altogether passed over. What bearing have these words on the widespread belief of Christendom in the perpetual maidenhood of Mary? On what grounds does that belief itself rest?

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 25. - And knew her not. The tense (ἐγίνωσκεν) brings out the continuance of Joseph's obedient self-restraint. "He was dwelling in holiness with her" (Tatian's 'Diatess.'). Till she had brought forth her firstborn Son. Thus the angel's promise is so far fulfilled. A son (Revised Version); "her firstborn," though found as early as Tatian's.' Diatess.,' having been added from Luke 2:7. Though no great stress can be laid on the word "till" (ἕως [οϋ], Basil refers to Genesis 8:7; comp. also Psalm exit. 8), nor even on "firstborn," which suggested to a Jew rather consecration (Luke 2:23) than the birth of other children (comp. Bishop Lightfoot on Galatians, p. 270, edit. 1890); yet it is a reasonable inference from the passage as a whole that the οὐκ ἐγίνωσκεν was not continued after the birth of the Son. Whether, however, other children were born to Mary or not, the true text of this passage gives no hint. And he called his name JESUS (ver. 21, note). Observe that this name had already occurred in Joseph's family (Luke 3:29). It is, however, now given in sign of Joseph's faith in him and his work.
