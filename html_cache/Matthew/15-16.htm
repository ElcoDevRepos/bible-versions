Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Jesus asked.
This phrase reflects Jesus' ongoing effort to teach His disciples spiritual truths. The context of this passage is a discussion about what defiles a person, following a confrontation with the Pharisees over ceremonial handwashing. Jesus emphasizes that spiritual insight is more important than ritualistic practices. The disciples' lack of understanding highlights their human limitations and need for divine revelation. This moment is a reminder of the patience Jesus shows in teaching His followers, a theme seen throughout the Gospels. It also connects to other instances where Jesus questions the understanding of His disciples, such as in Mark 8:17-21, where He addresses their concerns about bread after feeding the multitudes. This phrase underscores the importance of spiritual discernment over mere intellectual comprehension, a recurring theme in Jesus' teachings.
Persons / Places / Events
1. Jesus
The central figure in this passage, Jesus is addressing His disciples, challenging them to understand His teachings more deeply.

2. Disciples
The immediate audience of Jesus' question, they are struggling to grasp the spiritual truths Jesus is imparting.

3. Pharisees and Scribes
Earlier in the chapter, these religious leaders confront Jesus about His disciples not following traditional handwashing rituals, setting the stage for this teaching moment.

4. Gennesaret
The region where Jesus and His disciples are located during this discourse, as mentioned earlier in the chapter.

5. Crowds
The larger group of people who follow Jesus and witness His teachings and miracles, providing context for the disciples' need for deeper understanding.
Teaching Points
Spiritual Understanding Over Rituals
Jesus emphasizes the importance of understanding spiritual truths over merely following religious rituals. Believers are encouraged to seek deeper comprehension of God's Word.

Heart Condition Matters
The focus is on the condition of the heart rather than external observance. Christians should examine their hearts and motives in their walk with God.

Growth in Discipleship
Just as Jesus expected His disciples to grow in understanding, believers today are called to mature in their faith and knowledge of Scripture.

Challenge of Complacency
Jesus' question challenges complacency in spiritual learning. Christians should continually seek to deepen their relationship with God and understanding of His Word.

Role of the Holy Spirit
Understanding spiritual truths requires the guidance of the Holy Spirit. Believers should pray for wisdom and discernment in their study of Scripture.

What Does Matthew 15:16 Mean
Do

This opening word shows Jesus taking initiative. He is not waiting for the disciples to stumble onto truth but lovingly confronting them. Throughout the Gospels He often begins with a pointed question to reveal hearts (see John 6:67: “Do you want to leave too?”). By asking rather than merely lecturing, He invites response and growth, just as Proverbs 20:5 pictures wise counsel drawing out what is hidden.

you

The focus is personal and direct. Jesus speaks to His closest followers, those who had witnessed miracles such as the feeding of the five thousand (Matthew 14:17-21). That intimacy underscores accountability: greater exposure to truth brings greater responsibility (Luke 12:48).

Bullet points on the significance:

• Relationship—He addresses friends, not the crowd.

• Expectation—disciples are expected to grasp lessons quicker than outsiders (Matthew 13:11).

still

This word signals that time and experience have gone by. They have repeatedly heard His teaching and seen His power, yet comprehension lags. Compare Mark 8:17-21, where He asks, “Do you still not see or understand?” The term highlights patient yet firm admonition, showing that persistent dullness is not excusable when revelation has been abundant.

not

The negative emphasizes a gap—what should be present is absent. In John 12:37, “Although Jesus had performed so many signs in their presence, they still did not believe in Him.” The same principle applies here: disbelief or misunderstanding after clear evidence exposes the heart more than the mind (Hebrews 3:12).

understand?

Comprehension in Scripture is more than mental grasp; it involves spiritual insight that births obedience (Psalm 119:34). Jesus wants them to perceive that defilement comes from the heart, not external rituals (Matthew 15:17-20). Without this insight, the disciples cannot lead others faithfully (Luke 6:39).

Jesus asked

The narrator reminds us who is speaking—the sovereign Lord whose questions carry authority and grace. Every inquiry from His lips is an invitation to repentance and deeper faith, echoing Genesis 3:9 (“Where are you?”) and Job 38:4 (“Where were you when I laid the foundations of the earth?”). His questions expose, teach, and heal.

summary

Matthew 15:16 records Jesus’ gentle but firm rebuke to disciples slow to grasp His teaching on inner purity. Each word reveals His loving authority: He initiates (“Do”), addresses them personally (“you”), highlights lingering dullness (“still”), confronts their deficiency (“not”), seeks true insight (“understand?”), and stands as the divine Teacher (“Jesus asked”). The verse calls believers to move beyond surface observance to heart-level understanding, embracing the full light already given so our lives align with the truth we profess.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(16) Are ye also yet without understanding?--The pronoun is emphatic: "Ye, My disciples, who have heard from My lips the spiritual nature of My kingdom, are ye too, like the Pharisees, still such backward scholars?"

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 16. - Are ye also yet without understanding? Even yet; ἀκμήν: adhuc. In spite of all that has passed - my teaching, my life, my miracles - do you not understand in what real purity consists? Often had Jesus to complain of the dulness of his disciples' intelligence, the slow appreciation of his meaning, the indifference to the spiritual side of his acts and doctrine. Up to the very last they failed to apprehend his mission; nor was it till the Day of Pentecost, when the Holy Spirit was poured upon them, that they really and in fulness understood the Lord's teaching and their own duties and powers.
