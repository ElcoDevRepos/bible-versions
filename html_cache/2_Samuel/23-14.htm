Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
At that time
This phrase sets the historical context for the events described. It refers to a period during David's life when he was engaged in ongoing conflict with the Philistines, a recurring theme throughout the books of Samuel. This time frame is likely during David's early reign or possibly even before he became king, as he was often in conflict with the Philistines.

David was in the stronghold
The "stronghold" likely refers to the cave of Adullam or another fortified location where David sought refuge. This was a common practice for David during his fugitive years, as he often had to hide from King Saul and later from his enemies. The stronghold symbolizes a place of safety and strategic advantage, reflecting David's reliance on God for protection and guidance.

and the garrison of the Philistines was at Bethlehem
Bethlehem, David's hometown, was under Philistine control at this time. This occupation by the Philistines highlights the ongoing threat they posed to Israel and the personal nature of the conflict for David. The presence of a Philistine garrison in Bethlehem underscores the strategic importance of the town and the severity of the Philistine threat. This situation also foreshadows the eventual liberation and establishment of David's kingdom, as Bethlehem would later become the birthplace of Jesus Christ, the "Son of David," fulfilling messianic prophecies.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The anointed king of Israel, known for his leadership, military prowess, and deep relationship with God. At this point, he is in a stronghold, a place of refuge and defense.

2. The Stronghold
A fortified place where David and his men were staying. It symbolizes a place of safety and strategic advantage during times of conflict.

3. Philistines
A perennial enemy of Israel, known for their military strength and opposition to God's people. They had established a garrison in Bethlehem, David's hometown.

4. Bethlehem
The birthplace of David, significant in biblical history as the future birthplace of Jesus Christ. It was under Philistine control at this time, representing a personal and national challenge for David.

5. Garrison
A military post or fortification. The presence of a Philistine garrison in Bethlehem indicates the strategic and oppressive control the Philistines had over the region.
Teaching Points
God as Our Stronghold
Just as David found refuge in a physical stronghold, believers are called to find their spiritual refuge in God. He is our ultimate protector and source of strength in times of trouble.

Facing Personal and Spiritual Battles
David's situation with the Philistines in Bethlehem reminds us that we often face battles in our own "Bethlehems"—areas of personal significance or vulnerability. We must rely on God's guidance and strength to overcome these challenges.

The Importance of Spiritual Heritage
Bethlehem's significance as David's hometown and the future birthplace of Christ underscores the importance of spiritual heritage. Believers are encouraged to honor and uphold their spiritual roots and identity in Christ.

Strategic Prayer and Action
David's strategic positioning in the stronghold teaches us the importance of being spiritually prepared and vigilant. Prayer and discernment are crucial in navigating life's battles.

What Does 2 Samuel 23:14 Mean
At that time

• The verse drops us into a very specific moment during David’s long conflict with the Philistines (2 Samuel 23:13; 1 Chronicles 11:15).

• Earlier events show that whenever David’s anointing threatened Philistine dominance, they mobilized quickly (2 Samuel 5:17).

• Scripture speaks literally and accurately here: this is a real wartime snapshot, not a parable.

• By marking “that time,” the writer ties this scene to the heroic exploits that follow in the chapter, highlighting God’s faithfulness to David in every season (Psalm 18:2–3).

David was in the stronghold

• A “stronghold” was a natural fortress—often a cave or rugged hill (1 Samuel 24:22). David had taken refuge in such places before, trusting God while remaining prudent (Psalm 57 superscription; 1 Samuel 22:4–5).

• The phrase reminds us that even the Lord’s anointed can experience pressure, isolation, and strategic retreat. Yet these retreats never signal defeat; they become stages where God’s protection is displayed (Psalm 31:2–3).

• David’s presence in the stronghold shows responsible leadership. He secures his men first, waits for God’s timing, and refuses reckless action—an example echoed later by Jesus withdrawing to desolate places for prayer (Mark 1:35).

and the garrison of the Philistines was at Bethlehem

• A “garrison” means an occupying military post. The Philistines controlled Bethlehem—David’s own hometown (1 Samuel 17:12).

• This detail underlines the humiliation Israel faced: the enemy sat comfortably in territory promised to Abraham (Genesis 13:14–15).

• Spiritually, the scene sharpens the coming contrast: when David longs for water from Bethlehem’s well (2 Samuel 23:15), his men’s daring act highlights covenant loyalty, while the Philistine garrison highlights pagan intrusion.

• God is about to turn apparent enemy dominance into an occasion for His glory, anticipating how Messiah would later be born in that very town under foreign occupation yet fulfill God’s redemptive plan (Micah 5:2; Luke 2:4–7).

summary

2 Samuel 23:14 pictures a real historical moment: David, sheltered in a fortified hideout, faces the shame of seeing Philistines entrenched in his own birthplace. The verse underscores God’s ongoing protection of His servant, Israel’s need for deliverance, and the certainty that enemy strongholds never cancel divine promises.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 14. - An hold; Hebrew, the hold. The definite article here and in ch. 5:17, and the mention of the Philistines as being in the valley of Rephaim, seem to indicate that David had abandoned Jerusalem upon the invasion of the Philistines, and sought refuge at Adullam (see note on 2 Samuel 5:17). In its neighbourhood is an isolated hill, on which, probably, was a frontier fortress, in which David prepared to defend himself.
