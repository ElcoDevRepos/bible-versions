Then the king said to the woman
This phrase refers to King David, who is approached by a woman from Tekoa. The context is a carefully orchestrated plan by Joab, David's military commander, to reconcile David with his estranged son Absalom. The woman is acting under Joab's instructions to present a parable to the king. This reflects the wisdom and discernment often required of leaders in biblical narratives, as well as the use of intermediaries to communicate sensitive matters.

I am going to ask you something; do not conceal it from me!
David's request for honesty underscores the importance of truthfulness in biblical interactions. The king's authority is evident, as he expects transparency from the woman. This moment highlights the tension between royal authority and the need for genuine communication. It also reflects the biblical theme of seeking truth, as seen in Proverbs 12:22, which values honesty.

Let my lord the king speak
The woman's response shows respect and submission to the king's authority, a common cultural expectation in ancient Israel. Her deference is typical of the hierarchical social structures of the time, where kings were seen as God's appointed rulers. This phrase also illustrates the biblical principle of respecting authority, as emphasized in passages like Romans 13:1-2, which speaks to the divine ordination of governing authorities.

she replied
Her willingness to engage in dialogue with the king demonstrates the role of women in biblical narratives, often as vessels of wisdom and intermediaries in God's plans. This interaction can be compared to other instances where women play pivotal roles, such as Esther's intercession for her people or Abigail's wise counsel to David in 1 Samuel 25. The woman's role here is instrumental in the unfolding of God's purposes through David's reign.

Persons / Places / Events
1. King David
The ruler of Israel, known for his heart after God, yet also for his personal and familial struggles. In this context, he is dealing with the aftermath of his son Absalom's actions.

2. The Wise Woman of Tekoa
A woman sent by Joab to speak to King David with a parable, aiming to reconcile David with his estranged son Absalom.

3. Joab
David's military commander who orchestrates the plan to bring Absalom back to Jerusalem by using the woman of Tekoa to appeal to David's sense of justice and mercy.

4. Absalom
David's son, who had fled after killing his brother Amnon. His estrangement from David is the central issue being addressed.

5. Tekoa
A town in Judah, known for its wise inhabitants, including the woman who approaches David.
Teaching Points
The Power of Parables
Parables can reveal deep truths and prompt introspection. Like Nathan's account to David, the woman of Tekoa uses a parable to reach David's heart.

Seeking Reconciliation
David's situation with Absalom reminds us of the importance of seeking reconciliation in our relationships, even when it is difficult.

Wisdom in Counsel
The woman of Tekoa exemplifies the impact of wise counsel. We should seek and value godly wisdom in our decision-making.

Transparency and Truth
David's request for honesty from the woman underscores the importance of transparency in our interactions, especially when seeking resolution.

God's Mercy and Justice
The account reflects God's balance of mercy and justice, encouraging us to emulate these attributes in our lives.

What Does 2 Samuel 14:18 Mean
Then the king said to the woman

• David has listened patiently to the parable-like plea brought by the woman of Tekoa, but now he turns from judging a case to examining a witness.

• Kings were charged with discerning truth (2 Samuel 14:17; Deuteronomy 17:18–20). David embraces that duty by taking the initiative.

• His approach mirrors earlier moments when he probed a story to uncover hidden motives (2 Samuel 12:5–7 with Nathan; Joshua 7:19 with Achan).

“I am going to ask you something;

• The statement signals a shift from courtroom sympathy to investigative clarity.

• David senses a deeper layer beneath her words, displaying the “discerning heart” prayed for by Solomon later (1 Kings 3:9), and modeled by David here.

• Scripture commends careful inquiry before judgment (Proverbs 18:13; John 7:51).

do not conceal it from me!”

• Kings held authority to demand full disclosure (Esther 7:2–6). Hiding truth from God’s anointed ruler was both disrespectful and dangerous (Acts 5:3–5).

• David frames the command with personal earnestness—he wants light, not flattery. Truthfulness before human authority reflects our required honesty before divine authority (Psalm 51:6; Proverbs 12:17).

“Let my lord the king speak,”

• The woman answers with humble consent, echoing Samuel’s “Speak, for Your servant is listening” (1 Samuel 3:10).

• Her words show submission and readiness to cooperate, contrasting with those who resist godly questioning (Jeremiah 42:2–6 vs. Jeremiah 43:2).

• Respectful address (“my lord the king”) honors the office God established (Romans 13:1; 1 Peter 2:17).

she replied.

• The narrative pauses to underline her willingness, paving the way for David to expose Joab’s orchestration (2 Samuel 14:19–20).

• Honest dialogue, once embraced, becomes the turning point for resolution—just as confession preceded reconciliation in other accounts (Genesis 44:18–34; 2 Samuel 12:13).

summary

2 Samuel 14:18 captures the pivotal moment when King David shifts from empathetic listener to discerning investigator. He exercises God-given authority to seek truth, insists on transparency, and receives a respectful, cooperative response. The verse models responsible leadership, honest submission, and the biblical principle that uncovering truth is essential for righteous judgment.
