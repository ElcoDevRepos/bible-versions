Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
The woman ran quickly to tell her husband
This phrase highlights the urgency and importance of the message the woman received. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, women often played crucial roles in the family and community, though they were typically not the primary decision-makers. Her immediate action underscores the significance of the encounter with the angelic figure. This reflects the biblical theme of God using unexpected individuals to fulfill His purposes, similar to how He used Mary, the mother of Jesus, to bring the Savior into the world.

“Behold, the man who came to me the other day has reappeared!”
The term "behold" is used to draw attention to something significant. The woman's recognition of the "man" as the same figure who appeared earlier suggests a divine encounter, as the "man" is later revealed to be the Angel of the LORD. This is a theophany, a manifestation of God in a form visible to humans. The reappearance emphasizes the continuity and certainty of God's message and plan. The Angel of the LORD is often seen as a pre-incarnate appearance of Christ, as He speaks with authority and is worshiped, which aligns with the nature of Christ as the eternal Word. This encounter foreshadows the coming of Jesus, who would also be announced by an angelic messenger, as seen in the accounts of the Annunciation to Mary and Joseph.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Woman (Manoah's Wife)
She is the wife of Manoah and the mother of Samson. Her name is not mentioned in the text, but she plays a crucial role in the account by receiving the angelic message about Samson's birth.

2. Manoah
The husband of the woman and father of Samson. He is from the tribe of Dan and is characterized by his desire to understand God's will for his son.

3. The Angel of the LORD
A divine messenger who appears to Manoah's wife to announce the birth of Samson, who will begin to deliver Israel from the Philistines.

4. Zorah
The town in the territory of Dan where Manoah and his wife lived. It is significant as the birthplace of Samson.

5. The Event of the Angel's Reappearance
The angel of the LORD reappears to Manoah's wife, confirming the divine message about the birth of Samson and his Nazirite vow.
Teaching Points
Divine Encounters
God often uses unexpected means and people to communicate His plans. We should remain open to His guidance in our lives.

Faith and Obedience
Manoah's wife demonstrates faith by quickly sharing the angel's message with her husband. We should be eager to share God's revelations and act upon them.

God's Sovereignty
The reappearance of the angel underscores God's control over events and His faithfulness in fulfilling His promises.

Role of Women in Scripture
Manoah's wife is a pivotal figure in this account, reminding us of the significant roles women play in God's redemptive history.

Preparation for God's Work
Just as Samson was set apart from birth, we are called to live lives dedicated to God's purposes.

What Does Judges 13:10 Mean
The woman ran quickly

– Immediate movement shows faith expressed through action.

– Like Rebekah who “ran and told her mother’s household” (Genesis 24:28), the wife of Manoah does not delay obedience.

– Swift response reflects the urgency seen in the women at the empty tomb who “ran to tell His disciples” (Matthew 28:8).

to tell her husband

– Marriage partners share God’s revelations; spiritual matters are family matters (Genesis 2:24; 1 Peter 3:7).

– Respect for her husband’s headship is evident: she seeks his presence before engaging the visitor, echoing Priscilla and Aquila teaching together (Acts 18:26).

– Unity prepares both to receive fuller instruction from the Lord (compare Judges 13:11–12).

Behold

– The word signals something worthy of focused attention, much as John the Baptist declared, “Behold, the Lamb of God” (John 1:29).

– It calls Manoah to shift from normal routine to divine encounter, paralleling Moses’ turning aside at the burning bush (Exodus 3:3–4).

the man who came to me

– She identifies the visitor as “the man,” yet earlier Scripture reveals He is “the Angel of the LORD” (Judges 13:3), underscoring the mystery of God appearing in human form (Genesis 18:1–2).

– Personal testimony matters; she recounts what she has witnessed, echoing the healed man in Mark 5:19 who was told, “Tell them how much the Lord has done for you.”

the other day

– Time has passed, but expectation remained; God’s timing may stretch faith (Habakkuk 2:3).

– Her memory of the initial encounter stayed sharp, reflecting meditation on God’s words as urged in Deuteronomy 6:6–7.

has reappeared!

– God confirms His word; the Angel’s return guarantees Samson’s promised birth (Judges 13:7).

– Scripture often shows the Lord revisiting His servants when purposes advance (1 Samuel 3:10; Acts 7:35).

– Joy erupts when promises move toward fulfillment, much like the disciples’ joy when the risen Christ stood among them again (John 20:20).

summary

Manoah’s wife models eager obedience, marital partnership, and unwavering expectancy. Her swift report draws her husband into God’s unfolding plan, reminding us that when the Lord speaks, He also confirms. Faith moves quickly, speaks plainly, waits patiently, and rejoices when God shows up again.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 10. - And the woman... ran, etc. Acting in the true spirit of a loving and trustful wife, and showing that she felt that neither angel nor man of God stood before her own husband in the claim to her confidence and obedience.
