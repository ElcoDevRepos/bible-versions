And Jehoiada was buried with the kings in the City of David
Jehoiada, the high priest, was honored with a burial among the kings, a rare privilege for someone not of royal blood. This reflects his significant influence and leadership in Judah, particularly during the reign of King Joash. The City of David, located in Jerusalem, was the burial site for the kings of Judah, emphasizing Jehoiada's esteemed status. His role in preserving the Davidic line by protecting Joash from Queen Athaliah's massacre (2 Kings 11:1-3) and his guidance in restoring the temple (2 Chronicles 24:4-14) were pivotal in maintaining the spiritual and political stability of the nation.

because he had done what was good in Israel
Jehoiada's actions were seen as righteous and beneficial for the nation of Israel, which in this context refers to the southern kingdom of Judah. His leadership was marked by a return to covenant faithfulness, including the renewal of the covenant between the Lord, the king, and the people (2 Chronicles 23:16). His influence led to the destruction of Baal worship and the restoration of proper worship practices, aligning with the Deuteronomic emphasis on covenant loyalty (Deuteronomy 6:4-9).

for God and His temple
Jehoiada's dedication to God and the temple underscores his commitment to the spiritual welfare of Judah. He orchestrated the repair and maintenance of the temple, ensuring that it remained the center of worship and national identity (2 Chronicles 24:12-14). This focus on the temple prefigures the New Testament understanding of Jesus Christ as the ultimate high priest and the true temple (John 2:19-21), highlighting the continuity of God's redemptive plan through the preservation of worship and covenant faithfulness.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jehoiada
A high priest who played a crucial role in preserving the Davidic line by protecting Joash, the rightful heir to the throne, from Queen Athaliah's massacre. His influence was pivotal in guiding King Joash to restore the temple and lead the nation in righteousness.

2. City of David
This is a term often used to refer to Jerusalem, specifically the ancient part of the city where King David established his capital. It is significant as the burial place of the kings of Judah.

3. Kings of Judah
The lineage of rulers from the tribe of Judah, starting with David. Jehoiada was honored by being buried among them, highlighting his significant contribution to the kingdom.

4. Temple of the Lord
The central place of worship for the Israelites, which Jehoiada helped restore during King Joash's reign. It symbolizes the spiritual heart of the nation.

5. Athaliah
The mother of King Ahaziah of Judah, who seized power and attempted to destroy the royal line of David. Her actions set the stage for Jehoiada's intervention.
Teaching Points
Legacy of Faithfulness
Jehoiada's life teaches us the importance of faithfulness to God and His purposes. His actions had a lasting impact on the nation of Israel.

Influence of Godly Leadership
The positive influence of Jehoiada on King Joash highlights the importance of godly counsel and leadership in guiding others toward righteousness.

Honoring Those Who Serve God
The honor given to Jehoiada in his burial reflects the biblical principle of honoring those who have served God and His people well.

Commitment to God's House
Jehoiada's dedication to restoring the temple underscores the importance of prioritizing worship and the maintenance of God's house in our lives.

What Does 2 Chronicles 24:16 Mean
And Jehoiada was buried with the kings

• Such a burial was the highest public honor in Judah, normally reserved for royalty (2 Chron 21:20; 2 Chron 26:23).

• By granting Jehoiada a royal tomb, the nation acknowledged that faithfulness to God outweighs bloodline or title (1 Samuel 2:30).

• His life shows how spiritual leadership can shape a nation’s destiny (2 Kings 11:17–20).

in the City of David

• This historic site, Jerusalem’s fortified heart (2 Samuel 5:7), underscores the covenant promises tied to David’s line and the temple (1 Kings 8:16–17).

• Placing Jehoiada there linked his legacy to God’s ongoing plan for Judah (Psalm 48:1–8).

because he had done what was good in Israel

• Jehoiada protected young Joash from Athaliah’s massacre, preserving David’s messianic line (2 Chron 22:10–12).

• He led the overthrow of idolatry and renewed the covenant between the LORD, the king, and the people (2 Kings 11:17; 2 Chron 23:16).

• His acts fulfilled the standard echoed in other commendations of faithful leaders (2 Chron 19:3; Nehemiah 13:14).

for God

• Jehoiada’s motive was devotion to the LORD, not personal power (2 Chron 24:2).

• His obedience illustrates the principle that “the LORD looks at the heart” (1 Samuel 16:7) and rewards those who honor Him (Proverbs 3:35).

and His temple

• He organized offerings, restored priestly order, and led the temple’s physical repairs (2 Chron 24:4–14).

• These actions mirrored Moses’ and David’s concern that worship remain pure and God-centered (Exodus 40:33–38; 1 Chron 28:9–10).

• By elevating the temple’s importance, Jehoiada pointed Israel back to their covenant identity (Deuteronomy 12:5–12).

summary

Jehoiada’s royal burial marks divine approval of a life spent safeguarding David’s line, cleansing worship, and restoring the temple. His story affirms that wholehearted service to God brings honor surpassing earthly rank and secures a lasting legacy within God’s redemptive plan.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(16) Among the kings.--Literally, with. "Because he had done good;" and also, perhaps, as having been regent for so many years, and connected by marriage with the royal house (2Chronicles 22:11).

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 16. - The honour done Jehoiada well belonged to him, not only for his goodness, his greatness, his practical services to the kingdom, but for the fact that those practical services had entailed the necessity of his standing in loco regis for some time. His royal alliance with Jeheram's daughter, and, if it were so, his extreme patriarchal age, may all have contributed to the honour now put upon him. Little stress can be laid, however, upon this last consideration, failing any other allusion to it, or any emphasized statement of what we have in our ver. 15.
