In the third month
The third month in the Hebrew calendar is Sivan, which corresponds to late May and early June. This period is significant as it follows the Feast of Weeks (Shavuot), a time of harvest and thanksgiving. The timing indicates that the people were bringing in the firstfruits of their harvest, a practice commanded in the Law of Moses (Exodus 23:16, Leviticus 23:15-21). This act of obedience reflects a renewed commitment to the covenant and the Law under King Hezekiah's reforms.

they began building up the heaps
The heaps refer to the piles of offerings brought by the Israelites, consisting of grain, new wine, oil, honey, and all the produce of the field. This abundance signifies the people's generosity and the blessings of God upon the land. The act of building up heaps symbolizes a collective effort to restore proper worship and support the Levitical priesthood, as prescribed in the Law (Deuteronomy 14:22-29). It also demonstrates the people's response to Hezekiah's call for religious reform and revival.

and they finished in the seventh month
The seventh month, Tishri, corresponds to September and October. This month is marked by significant religious observances, including the Feast of Trumpets, the Day of Atonement, and the Feast of Tabernacles (Leviticus 23:23-44). The completion of the heaps by this time suggests a period of sustained dedication and effort by the people. It also aligns with the agricultural cycle, as the end of the harvest season would naturally conclude with the final gathering of produce. This period of completion underscores the themes of repentance, renewal, and celebration of God's provision, paralleling the spiritual renewal under Hezekiah's leadership.

What Does 2 Chronicles 31:7 Mean
In the third month

“In the third month…” (2 Chronicles 31:7) places us at the start of the summer harvest season, around the time of the Feast of Weeks (Pentecost) spoken of in Leviticus 23:15-17 and Deuteronomy 16:10. Under King Hezekiah’s renewal of temple worship (2 Chronicles 31:4-5), the people responded promptly.

• The firstfruits of grain were ready, so families gladly hauled in their tithes.

• Their quick obedience echoes Israel’s earlier zeal when Moses called for offerings to build the tabernacle (Exodus 36:5-7).

• The timing shows that giving was not a random burst of emotion; it was tied to God’s calendar, reminding us that worship permeates ordinary work and harvest.

They began building up the heaps

The phrase highlights visible piles of produce, flocks, and dedicated goods. 2 Chronicles 31:5 records that “the Israelites gave abundantly…”—so much that priests and Levites started stacking gifts in great mounds.

• Heaps witnessed to God’s blessing promised in Malachi 3:10; when the windows of heaven open, even barns—or temple storerooms—struggle to hold it all.

• Their generosity also supported those set apart for ministry, freeing priests and Levites “to devote themselves fully to the Law of the LORD” (2 Chronicles 31:4).

• The scene anticipates New-Testament giving where cheerful liberality leads to overflowing sufficiency (2 Corinthians 9:6-8).

And they finished in the seventh month

From the third to the seventh month (roughly Sivan to Tishri) the heaps kept growing, covering the entire harvest period that culminated in the Feast of Tabernacles (Leviticus 23:34-43; Deuteronomy 16:13-15).

• The extended timeline shows sustained faithfulness, not a one-time surge.

• As 2 Chronicles 31:8-10 tells us, Hezekiah and the leaders came, saw the mountains of provision, and glorified God, confirming that when people honor Him first, there is “plenty left over.”

• Ending in the seventh month fits the biblical theme of completion and rest (Genesis 2:2-3), underscoring that God’s order brings both abundance and peace.

summary

2 Chronicles 31:7 records more than dates; it captures a four-month testimony of a nation revived. Prompt, orderly giving began with the first harvest and continued until the last, forming visible heaps that declared God’s faithfulness. The verse teaches that consistent obedience to God’s clear commands produces overflowing provision—not only for those who serve in His house but for every worshiper who trusts Him.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(7) In the third month.--And so at the end of wheat-harvest, the third month (Sivan) answering to our May--June. Pentecost, the Feast of Harvest, or Firstfruits, fell in this month.

To lay the foundation.--To found, or lay. Heb., lisod, a curious form only met with here. (Comp. lisod, Isaiah 51:16.)

In the seventh month.--Tisri (September to October), in which was held the great Feast of Tabernacles, after all the fruits had been gathered in, and the vintage was over.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 7. - The third month... the heaps... the seventh month. The grain harvest closed with the Feast of Weeks, about the sixth day of the third month so that tithe in kind would be paid. The seventh month brought the Feast of Ingathering, when the vintage was over. For illustration of the despatch with which Hezekiah proceeded in his reforming works, comp. our 2 Chronicles 29:3; 2 Chronicles 30:2, 13.
