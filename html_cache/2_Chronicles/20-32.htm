And Jehoshaphat walked in the way of his father Asa
Jehoshaphat, the fourth king of Judah, is noted for following the example set by his father, Asa. Asa was known for his religious reforms and efforts to rid Judah of idolatry, as seen in 2 Chronicles 14-16. This phrase highlights the importance of generational faithfulness and the impact of a godly legacy. Asa's reign was marked by a return to the worship of Yahweh, and Jehoshaphat's adherence to his father's ways suggests a continuation of these reforms. This continuity is significant in the context of the divided kingdom, where the northern kingdom of Israel often strayed from the worship of the true God.

and did not turn away from it;
Jehoshaphat's steadfastness in following Asa's path indicates his commitment to maintaining the religious purity and reforms initiated by his father. This phrase underscores the theme of perseverance in faith and obedience, which is a recurring motif in the Old Testament. The idea of not turning away is reminiscent of the exhortations found in Deuteronomy 5:32-33, where the Israelites are commanded to walk in the ways of the LORD without deviation. Jehoshaphat's consistency in his faithfulness serves as a model for believers to remain steadfast in their devotion to God.

he did what was right in the eyes of the LORD.
This phrase is a common biblical expression used to describe the actions of kings who were faithful to God's commandments. It signifies divine approval and aligns Jehoshaphat with other righteous kings like David and Hezekiah. Doing what is right in the eyes of the LORD involves adherence to the covenant, promoting justice, and leading the people in true worship. This phrase also connects to the broader biblical narrative of God's desire for His people to live in righteousness and justice, as seen in Micah 6:8. Jehoshaphat's reign is characterized by efforts to educate the people in the Law of the LORD (2 Chronicles 17:7-9), further illustrating his commitment to righteousness.

Teaching Points
Faithfulness in Leadership
Jehoshaphat's commitment to walking in the ways of his father Asa demonstrates the importance of faithfulness in leadership. Leaders today are called to uphold godly principles and guide others in righteousness.

Generational Influence
The influence of Asa on Jehoshaphat highlights the impact of parental and generational faithfulness. Parents and mentors should strive to set a godly example for the next generation.

Consistency in Righteousness
Jehoshaphat's refusal to turn aside from doing what was right underscores the value of consistency in our walk with God. Believers are encouraged to remain steadfast in their faith and actions.

The Importance of Obedience
Walking in the way of the LORD requires obedience to His commands. This obedience is a testament to our love and reverence for God.

Legacy of Faith
Jehoshaphat's life serves as a reminder that our actions and faith can leave a lasting legacy. We should aim to leave a legacy that honors God and inspires others to follow Him.

What Does 2 Chronicles 20:32 Mean
And Jehoshaphat walked in the way of his father Asa

The verse opens by connecting Jehoshaphat’s spiritual course to that of his godly father.

• “Walked” points to a life-long pattern, not a momentary decision (see Psalm 1:1-2).

• Asa’s reforms were substantial: “He removed the foreign altars… and commanded Judah to seek the LORD” (2 Chronicles 14:3-4). By echoing that path, Jehoshaphat embraced the same uncompromising loyalty to the covenant.

• God consistently highlights generational faithfulness; Proverbs 22:6 and Deuteronomy 6:6-7 show that righteous habits learned in the home can shape an entire reign.

• This statement is historical fact. Scripture records it so believers may see that genuine obedience can be modeled and imitated (1 Corinthians 11:1).

and did not turn away from it

Steadfastness sets Jehoshaphat apart. Many kings started well and drifted, but he held course.

• 2 Chronicles 17:3-4 affirms, “He followed the earlier ways of his father… he sought the God of his father.”

• Joshua 1:7 urges, “Do not turn from it to the right or to the left,” a charge Jehoshaphat clearly fulfilled.

• Perseverance is a New Testament expectation too: “Let us not grow weary in doing good” (Galatians 6:9).

• By refusing compromise, he shielded Judah from idolatry’s ruin, illustrating that a single unwavering leader can bless an entire nation (Proverbs 14:34).

he did what was right in the eyes of the LORD

The final clause supplies God’s verdict on Jehoshaphat’s life.

• “Right” is defined by the Lord, not culture or politics (Isaiah 5:20). Jehoshaphat’s reforms of chapter 19—appointing honest judges and teaching God’s Law—demonstrate practical righteousness.

• 1 Samuel 15:22 reminds that obedience outweighs ritual; Jehoshaphat’s actions matched that principle.

• Micah 6:8 distills God’s requirement: “to act justly, to love mercy, and to walk humbly.” Jehoshaphat’s reign exemplified that trio.

• Divine evaluation brings both encouragement and accountability: “The eyes of the LORD range throughout the earth to strengthen those whose hearts are fully devoted to Him” (2 Chronicles 16:9).

summary

2 Chronicles 20:32 is more than a footnote—it is heaven’s commendation of a king who learned from a faithful father, stayed the course without deviation, and consistently fulfilled God’s standard. The verse assures believers that steadfast, generational obedience is noticed, recorded, and rewarded by the Lord who sees all.
