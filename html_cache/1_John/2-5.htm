Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
But if anyone keeps His word
This phrase emphasizes the importance of obedience to God's commandments as a demonstration of genuine faith. In the biblical context, "keeping His word" refers to living according to the teachings and commandments of Jesus Christ. This echoes the teachings of Jesus in John 14:15, "If you love Me, you will keep My commandments." The concept of obedience is deeply rooted in the Old Testament, where adherence to God's law was a sign of covenant faithfulness (Deuteronomy 6:17). In the New Testament, this obedience is not merely legalistic but is a response to the grace and love received through Christ.

the love of God has been truly perfected in him
This phrase suggests that the love of God reaches its intended goal or maturity in those who obey His word. The Greek word for "perfected" (τετελείωται) implies completion or fulfillment. This reflects the transformative power of God's love, which is made complete in believers as they live out their faith. The idea of love being perfected is also seen in 1 John 4:12, where it is stated that God's love is perfected in us when we love one another. This perfection is not about human effort but about the work of the Holy Spirit in the believer's life, producing the fruit of love (Galatians 5:22).

By this we know that we are in Him:
This phrase provides assurance of one's relationship with God. The concept of being "in Him" is a recurring theme in the writings of John, indicating a deep, abiding relationship with Christ (John 15:4-5). It signifies union with Christ, where believers are spiritually connected to Him and share in His life. This assurance is not based on feelings but on the evidence of a transformed life characterized by obedience and love. The phrase also connects to the broader biblical theme of covenant relationship, where being "in" God or Christ signifies belonging and identity (2 Corinthians 5:17).

Teaching Points
Obedience as Evidence of Love
Keeping God's word is a tangible expression of our love for Him. True love for God is demonstrated through our actions and obedience to His commandments.

Perfection of Love
The phrase "the love of God has been truly perfected" suggests a process of maturation. As we obey God's word, His love is brought to completion in us, transforming our character and actions.

Assurance of Salvation
By keeping God's word, we gain assurance that we are in Him. This assurance is not based on feelings but on the evidence of a life aligned with His teachings.

The Role of the Holy Spirit
The Holy Spirit empowers us to keep God's word and perfects His love in us. We are not left to our own strength but are guided and enabled by the Spirit.

Community and Accountability
Engaging with a community of believers helps us to keep God's word. Accountability and encouragement from others are vital in our spiritual growth and obedience.

What Does 1 John 2:5 Mean
But if anyone keeps His word

• The verse opens with a clear condition: “keeps His word.” To keep is more than to hear; it is to obey, guard, cherish, and practice God’s commands day by day (John 14:23; James 1:22).

• This obedience springs from a heart transformed by grace, not from mere duty (Psalm 119:11).

• It demonstrates a lifestyle that aligns with Scripture, showing that God’s Word is the believer’s final authority (John 15:10).

the love of God has been truly perfected in him

• “Perfected” speaks of mature, completed love. God’s love reaches its intended goal as we obey (1 John 4:12, 17).

• The believer’s obedience becomes evidence that God’s love has filled every corner of the heart, driving out competing affections (Romans 5:5).

• This perfected love is both vertical (toward God) and horizontal (toward others), proving the reality of the new birth (Philippians 1:6).

By this we know

• John ties obedience and perfected love to assurance. The phrase signals a practical test for genuine faith (1 John 3:19; 5:13).

• God does not leave His children guessing; He provides tangible evidence so that “our hearts may be confident before Him” (2 Corinthians 13:5).

• Assurance grows as obedience and love mature, giving peace in daily fellowship with the Lord.

that we are in Him

• “In Him” expresses union with Christ—safe, secure, and inseparable (John 15:4; Colossians 3:3).

• This union is not mystical sentiment but a living reality where the believer’s identity, purpose, and destiny are bound to the Savior (Galatians 2:20).

• Evidence of being “in Him” is not a one-time confession alone but an ongoing pattern stamped by obedience and love (Ephesians 1:13).

summary

Keeping God’s Word reveals a heart ruled by His perfected love, and that observable pattern of obedience grants solid assurance that we truly dwell in Christ.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 5. - Once more (cf. 1 John 1:7, 9) the opposite is stated and the thought carried further. But whoso keepeth his word (his doctrine as a whole, including the separate commandments), of a truth in him hath the love of God been perfected; i.e., as an accomplished fact; the relation of love has been established. In St. John ἀληθῶς is no mere expletive; it expresses reality, and reality that is known. From verse 4 we might have expected "of a truth he knoweth God;" but the apostle goes beyond this, and shows that really knowing God involves loving him (comp. 1 John 4:11). The context shows that τοῦ Θεοῦ is objective - his love of God rather than God's love of him. The insertion of τοῦ Θεοῦ here, and the drift of the Epistle thus far, are in favour of αὐτόν and αὐτοῦ in verses 3-5 meaning God rather than Christ, although αὐτός in verse 2 tells the other way. The last clause sums up and reaffirms, but as usual with a new turn of thought, the whole section (verses 3-5), which begins and ends with ἐν τούτῳ γινώσκομεν. Knowing God implies keeping his Word; and keeping his Word involves loving him; and all this implies being in him, i.e., having that fellowship with him and his Son in which the Christian's life (which is eternal life) consists, and to promote which St. John publishes his Gospel (1 John 1:3, 4).
