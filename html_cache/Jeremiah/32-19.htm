Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
the One great in counsel and mighty in deed
This phrase highlights God's omniscience and omnipotence. The term "great in counsel" reflects God's wisdom and perfect understanding, as seen in Isaiah 40:13-14, where God's unparalleled wisdom is emphasized. "Mighty in deed" underscores His power and ability to act, reminiscent of His mighty works in creation and history, such as the Exodus (Exodus 15:11). This duality of wisdom and power is a recurring theme in Scripture, affirming God's sovereignty and reliability.

whose eyes are on all the ways of the sons of men
This indicates God's omnipresence and omniscience, suggesting that nothing escapes His notice. Proverbs 15:3 states, "The eyes of the LORD are in every place, keeping watch on the evil and the good," reinforcing the idea that God is aware of all human actions. This awareness is not passive; it is active and discerning, as God evaluates the moral and spiritual quality of human conduct.

to reward each one according to his ways
This phrase speaks to the principle of divine justice and retribution. It echoes the biblical concept of sowing and reaping found in Galatians 6:7-8, where individuals are held accountable for their actions. This principle is also evident in the Old Testament, such as in Deuteronomy 28, where blessings and curses are contingent upon obedience to God's commandments. It underscores the moral order established by God, where actions have consequences.

and according to the fruit of his deeds
This emphasizes the results or outcomes of one's actions. The "fruit" metaphor is common in Scripture, symbolizing the visible evidence of one's inner character and choices, as seen in Matthew 7:16-20, where Jesus speaks about recognizing people by their fruits. This phrase reinforces the idea that God evaluates not just actions but their impact and the heart behind them, aligning with the biblical theme of integrity and righteousness.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jeremiah
A prophet called by God to deliver His messages to the people of Judah. He is known for his prophecies of judgment and restoration.

2. God
The central figure in this verse, described as "great in counsel and mighty in deed," highlighting His wisdom and power.

3. Judah
The southern kingdom of Israel, facing impending judgment due to their disobedience and idolatry.

4. Babylonian Siege
The historical context of this chapter, where Jerusalem is under siege by the Babylonians, leading to the eventual exile of the people.

5. The Sons of Men
Refers to humanity in general, emphasizing God's omniscience and His ability to see and judge all human actions.
Teaching Points
God's Omniscience and Omnipresence
God sees all actions and knows all hearts. We should live with the awareness that nothing is hidden from Him.

Divine Justice
God rewards each person according to their deeds. This should encourage us to live righteously and align our actions with His will.

Wisdom in Counsel
God is "great in counsel," meaning His guidance is perfect. We should seek His wisdom in all decisions and trust His plans for our lives.

Mighty in Deed
God's power is unmatched, and He is capable of accomplishing His purposes. We can trust in His ability to act on our behalf.

Accountability
Understanding that God evaluates our actions should lead us to self-examination and repentance, striving to bear good fruit in our lives.

What Does Jeremiah 32:19 Mean
The One great in counsel

- Jeremiah calls the Lord “great in counsel,” reminding us that every purpose He forms is perfect and wise. Proverbs 19:21 notes, “Many plans are in a man’s heart, but the purpose of the LORD will prevail.” We can trust that His guidance never falters, because Isaiah 28:29 says, “The LORD of Hosts… is wonderful in counsel and excellent in wisdom.”

- This gives believers unshakable confidence when making decisions. When we pray for guidance (James 1:5), we rely on the same counsel that designed creation (Psalm 104:24) and orchestrates history (Isaiah 46:9-10).

Mighty in deed

- God’s plans are never empty rhetoric; He acts powerfully to accomplish them. Jeremiah had just acknowledged, “Ah, Lord GOD… nothing is too difficult for You” (Jeremiah 32:17).

- Think of the Exodus (Exodus 15:6), the conquest of Canaan (Joshua 23:3), and above all the resurrection of Christ (Ephesians 1:19-20). Each displays that His arm is strong enough to fulfill His counsel.

- For us, this means no situation is beyond His reach. Whatever struggle we face, we stand on His proven record of “mighty deeds” (Psalm 77:14).

Whose eyes are on all the ways of the sons of men

- Nothing escapes His notice. “The eyes of the LORD roam to and fro over all the earth” (2 Chronicles 16:9). Psalm 139:1-3 affirms He discerns every path and thought.

- Because His gaze is continual, we cannot hide sin (Hebrews 4:13) nor can our faithfulness be overlooked (Matthew 6:4).

- This awareness is both a holy safeguard against compromise and a comfort that He sees the unseen labors of love (Hebrews 6:10).

To reward each one according to his ways

- God’s justice is personal and precise. Romans 2:6 states, “He will repay each one according to his deeds.” Revelation 22:12 echoes the same principle at Christ’s return.

- Far from arbitrary, His rewards reflect our real choices. Proverbs 24:12 assures that He “will repay a man according to his work.”

- For believers, this truth encourages faithful stewardship (1 Corinthians 3:12-15). For unbelievers, it warns that wrongdoing carries inevitable consequences (John 5:29).

According to the fruit of his deeds

- Scripture pictures actions as seeds that bear fruit (Galatians 6:7-8). Righteous deeds yield blessing; sinful deeds harvest sorrow.

- Jeremiah 17:10 repeats almost the same words, underscoring that personal conduct truly matters.

- Yet the gospel clarifies that salvation rests on Christ’s finished work (Ephesians 2:8-9). Our “fruit” reveals the reality of that faith (Ephesians 2:10; Matthew 7:17-20). Because He judges perfectly, genuine obedience is never wasted, and hypocrisy is never missed (Matthew 12:33-37).

summary

Jeremiah 32:19 celebrates a God whose wisdom is flawless, whose power is unstoppable, whose knowledge is exhaustive, and whose justice is exact. He counsels perfectly, acts decisively, observes continually, and rewards justly. Knowing this, we respond with trust in His guidance, confidence in His power, integrity under His gaze, and diligent obedience that bears good fruit for His glory.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(19) Great in counsel . . .--So far as this is more than the continuance of the adoring ecstasy of the previous verse, it gives a fresh prominence to the law of direct, equitable, individual retribution. No law of the transmission of the inheritance of good or evil will be found, in the long-run, to clash with that.
