When Jeremiah had finished telling all the people
Jeremiah, a prophet called by God, often delivered messages to the people of Judah. This phrase indicates the completion of a prophetic message, emphasizing Jeremiah's role as a faithful messenger. The context here is post-exilic, where the remnant of Judah is seeking guidance. Jeremiah's role as a prophet is crucial, as he serves as a conduit for divine communication, similar to other prophets like Isaiah and Ezekiel.

all the words of the LORD their God
This phrase underscores the divine origin of Jeremiah's message. The words are not his own but are from the LORD, highlighting the authority and authenticity of the prophecy. The use of "the LORD their God" reinforces the covenant relationship between God and Israel, reminding the people of their unique identity and responsibilities. This is consistent with the Deuteronomic emphasis on obedience to God's commands.

everything that the LORD had sent him to say
This indicates the completeness and thoroughness of the message delivered by Jeremiah. It suggests that nothing was omitted, reflecting Jeremiah's obedience and integrity as a prophet. The phrase also implies that the message was divinely appointed, aligning with the biblical theme of God's sovereignty in guiding His people. This mirrors the prophetic tradition seen in Amos 3:7, where God reveals His plans through His prophets.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Jeremiah
A major prophet in the Old Testament, called by God to deliver His messages to the people of Judah. Known for his perseverance in the face of opposition and his deep emotional connection to his prophetic mission.

2. The People of Judah
The audience to whom Jeremiah was speaking. They were often resistant to his messages, which frequently called for repentance and warned of impending judgment.

3. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant God of Israel, who communicates His will and guidance through His prophets. In this context, He is the source of the message delivered by Jeremiah.

4. The Words of the LORD
The specific messages and instructions given by God to Jeremiah to convey to the people. These words often included warnings, calls to repentance, and promises of restoration.

5. The Event of Delivering the Message
This refers to the act of Jeremiah faithfully communicating God's words to the people, despite their resistance and the potential for personal danger.
Teaching Points
Faithfulness in Delivering God's Message
Jeremiah's example teaches us the importance of faithfully communicating God's truth, even when it is unpopular or met with resistance.

The Authority of God's Word
The passage underscores the authority of God's words, which are not merely human opinions but divine instructions that demand a response.

Courage in the Face of Opposition
Jeremiah's courage in delivering God's message despite opposition encourages believers to stand firm in their faith and convictions.

The Role of Prophets and Preachers
This passage highlights the critical role of those called to speak God's truth, reminding us of the responsibility and accountability that comes with such a calling.

Listening to God's Voice
The people's response to Jeremiah's message challenges us to examine our own receptiveness to God's word and our willingness to act upon it.

What Does Jeremiah 43:1 Mean
When Jeremiah had finished

- Jeremiah’s task was completed; he had delivered the word in full, not partially.

- His faithfulness mirrors his original commission: “Get yourself ready; stand up and tell them everything I command you” (Jeremiah 1:17).

- Completion highlights accountability; like Paul, he could say, “I have fought the good fight… I have finished the race” (2 Timothy 4:7; cf. Acts 20:27).

…telling all the people

- The message was not reserved for leaders only; everyone who had asked for guidance (Jeremiah 42:1–6) now heard the answer.

- Public proclamation reinforces that God’s word is meant for the whole covenant community (Deuteronomy 31:12; Matthew 28:19–20).

- By addressing “all the people,” Jeremiah removes any excuse of ignorance (Romans 10:17).

…all the words of the LORD their God

- “All” stresses completeness; nothing was trimmed to avoid offense (Jeremiah 26:2).

- The content was divine, not Jeremiah’s opinion (2 Peter 1:21).

- Faithful teachers today must likewise give the whole counsel of God (Revelation 22:18–19; 2 Timothy 3:16).

…everything that the LORD had sent him to say

- The prophet speaks under commission; authority rests in the Sender (Jeremiah 1:7; John 12:49–50).

- The wording underscores obedience: Jeremiah said exactly what God “sent” him to say—nothing more, nothing less (1 Thessalonians 2:13).

- The verse sets the stage for the people’s response in the following verses; rejection will be rejection of God Himself (Hebrews 1:1–2).

summary

Jeremiah 43:1 portrays a faithful prophet who has fully discharged his duty by delivering every word God gave him to every person who needed to hear it. The verse underscores completeness, public accountability, and divine authority, reminding believers today to speak and heed the entirety of God’s revelation without omission or alteration.
