But the woman did conceive
This phrase highlights the miraculous nature of the conception, as the woman was previously barren. In the biblical context, barrenness was often seen as a significant challenge and a source of social stigma. The miraculous conception parallels other biblical accounts, such as Sarah (Genesis 21:1-2) and Hannah (1 Samuel 1:19-20), where God intervenes to fulfill His promises. This event underscores God's power to bring life and fulfill His word through His prophets.

and at that time the next year
The specific timing emphasizes the accuracy and reliability of prophetic words. In the ancient Near Eastern context, time was often marked by agricultural cycles, and the phrase suggests a full cycle of seasons. This precise fulfillment of prophecy reinforces the authority of Elisha as a true prophet of God, whose words are trustworthy and come to pass as spoken.

she gave birth to a son
The birth of a son was particularly significant in ancient Israelite culture, as sons were seen as carriers of the family lineage and inheritors of the family estate. This birth not only brought personal joy to the woman and her husband but also had broader implications for their social standing and future security. The son can be seen as a type of Christ, a miraculous child who brings hope and fulfills divine promises.

just as Elisha had told her
This phrase confirms the fulfillment of Elisha's prophecy, highlighting the role of prophets as God's messengers. Elisha, as a successor to Elijah, continues the prophetic tradition of speaking God's truth and demonstrating His power. The fulfillment of his words serves to authenticate his ministry and the divine source of his message. This also connects to the broader biblical theme of God's faithfulness to His promises, as seen throughout Scripture.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Elisha
A prophet of God who succeeded Elijah. Known for his miracles and deep faith, Elisha plays a central role in this account as the one who prophesies the birth of the son.

2. The Shunammite Woman
A notable woman from Shunem who showed hospitality to Elisha. Her faith and kindness are rewarded with the promise and fulfillment of a son.

3. Shunem
A town in the territory of Issachar, where the Shunammite woman lived. It serves as the setting for this miraculous event.

4. The Promise of a Son
The event where Elisha promises the Shunammite woman that she will have a son, which is fulfilled in this verse.

5. The Miracle of Conception
The miraculous event where the Shunammite woman, previously childless, conceives and bears a son as prophesied by Elisha.
Teaching Points
Faith in God's Promises
Trust in God's timing and promises, even when circumstances seem impossible. The Shunammite woman's account reminds us that God is faithful to His word.

The Power of Hospitality
The Shunammite woman's hospitality to Elisha opened the door for God's blessing. We are encouraged to show kindness and hospitality, as it can lead to unexpected blessings.

God's Sovereignty in Life's Miracles
Recognize God's hand in the miraculous events of life. The birth of the Shunammite woman's son was a direct result of divine intervention, reminding us of God's sovereignty.

The Role of Prophets and God's Word
Elisha's role as a prophet emphasizes the importance of listening to and valuing God's word. We should seek to understand and apply biblical teachings in our lives.

What Does 2 Kings 4:17 Mean
But the woman did conceive

“​But the woman did conceive…” (2 Kings 4:17)

• This simple statement celebrates the power of God to overcome barrenness, echoing earlier miracles for Sarah (Genesis 21:1–2) and Hannah (1 Samuel 1:19–20).

• The conception confirms that Elisha’s promise in verse 16 was not mere optimism but a divinely backed word, much like Elijah’s earlier proclamation that the widow’s flour and oil would not run out (1 Kings 17:14–16).

• God’s faithfulness here underscores His unchanging nature (Numbers 23:19; Hebrews 13:8).

and at that time the next year

“…and at that time the next year…” (2 Kings 4:17)

• The specific timeframe shows God’s mastery over seasons (Ecclesiastes 3:1) and His precise control of history.

• Fulfillment “next year” mirrors the angelic promise to Abraham: “About this time next year” Sarah would have a son (Genesis 18:10, 14).

• Such timing highlights that God is neither early nor late (Habakkuk 2:3); He acts at the perfect moment for His purposes.

she gave birth to a son

“…she gave birth to a son…” (2 Kings 4:17)

• A son secured the family line, property inheritance, and social security for the Shunammite woman (Numbers 27:8).

• The birth aligns with God’s repeated pattern of providing sons who advance His redemptive plan: Isaac (Genesis 21:3), Samuel (1 Samuel 1:20), John the Baptist (Luke 1:57).

• Each miraculous birth points forward to the ultimate promised Son, Jesus (Isaiah 9:6; Luke 2:7).

just as Elisha had told her

“…just as Elisha had told her.” (2 Kings 4:17)

• The phrase vindicates Elisha’s prophetic authority, much like Samuel’s words “let none of his words fall to the ground” (1 Samuel 3:19).

• Fulfilled prophecy distinguishes the true God from idols (Isaiah 46:9–10) and authenticates His messenger (Deuteronomy 18:21–22).

• The reliability of Elisha’s word invites trust in every promise in Scripture, including eternal life through Christ (John 3:16; 2 Corinthians 1:20).

summary

God honored His promise through Elisha with precise, timely, and miraculous fulfillment. The Shunammite’s conception, the exact timing, the birth of a son, and the confirmation of the prophet’s word together spotlight the Lord’s faithfulness, power, and covenant love. Believers today can rest assured that the same God who kept His word then still keeps every promise now.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 17. - And the woman conceived, and bare a son at that season that Elisha had said unto her, according to the time of life; rather, as the Revised Version gives the passage, the woman conceived, and bare a son at that season, when the time came round, as Elisha had said unto her. The event was exactly as predicted; the child was born at the same season of the ensuing year.
