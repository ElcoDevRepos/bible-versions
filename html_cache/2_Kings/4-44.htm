So he set it before them
This phrase refers to the servant of Elisha who obeyed the prophet's command to distribute the food to the people. The act of setting the food before them demonstrates obedience and faith in the prophetic word. This mirrors the actions of Jesus in the New Testament, particularly in the feeding of the 5,000 (Matthew 14:19), where He also instructed His disciples to distribute food to the crowd. The act of setting food before others is a recurring biblical theme symbolizing provision and hospitality.

and they ate
The people eating signifies the fulfillment of God's promise through Elisha. In the context of a famine, as described earlier in 2 Kings 4, this act of eating is not just a physical necessity but a divine provision. It reflects God's care for His people, reminiscent of the manna provided in the wilderness (Exodus 16:15). Eating together also signifies community and fellowship, a central aspect of Israelite culture and religious practice.

and had some left over
The abundance of food, with leftovers remaining, highlights the miraculous nature of God's provision. This abundance is a sign of divine blessing and parallels the miracles of Jesus, where after feeding the multitudes, there were baskets of leftovers (John 6:12-13). It underscores the theme that God's provision is not only sufficient but overflowing, a testament to His generosity and power.

according to the word of the LORD
This phrase emphasizes the authority and reliability of God's word. The miracle occurred precisely as Elisha had prophesied, demonstrating that God's promises are trustworthy and will come to pass. This aligns with the broader biblical narrative that God's word is infallible and sovereign (Isaiah 55:11). It also reinforces the role of prophets as God's messengers, whose words carry divine authority.

Teaching Points
God's Provision is Abundant
Just as God provided more than enough for the sons of the prophets, He is able to meet our needs abundantly.

Faith in God's Word
Elisha acted on the word of the LORD, demonstrating that faith in God's promises leads to miraculous outcomes.

God Uses Ordinary Means
The miracle was performed using ordinary food, showing that God often uses simple means to accomplish His purposes.

Trust in God's Timing
The provision came at the right time for those in need, reminding us to trust in God's perfect timing for our needs.

Community and Sharing
The miracle involved sharing among a community, highlighting the importance of generosity and fellowship in the body of Christ.

What Does 2 Kings 4:44 Mean
So he set it before them

• Elisha receives twenty barley loaves and fresh grain (2 Kings 4:42). In simple obedience, he places this small gift in front of a hundred hungry prophets.

• The act mirrors earlier moments when servants of God present inadequate resources that the Lord then multiplies—see “Elijah said to her, ‘Do not be afraid…’” (1 Kings 17:13-15) and “Jesus then took the loaves” (John 6:11).

• Elisha’s confidence comes from God’s promise, not from the size of the offering (2 Kings 4:43).

and they ate

• The hundred men eat fully, just as the five thousand did centuries later when “they all ate and were satisfied” (Matthew 14:20).

• Physical nourishment here underscores God’s care for His faithful remnant during apostasy in Israel (cf. Psalm 34:10).

• Eating together also reinforces community life around God’s word—anticipating Acts 2:42-46, where the early church “broke bread” together.

and had some left over

• The surplus is literal. God not only meets needs; He exceeds them (Ephesians 3:20).

• Leftovers recall the widow’s jar of oil that “did not run dry” (1 Kings 17:16) and the twelve baskets gathered after Jesus feeds the multitude (John 6:13).

• Abundance testifies that divine provision is not a zero-sum equation; when God gives, He gives generously (2 Corinthians 9:8).

according to the word of the LORD

• The entire miracle rests on God’s spoken promise in 2 Kings 4:43: “They will eat and have some left.” His word never fails (Joshua 21:45).

• “Has the LORD’s arm grown short?” (Numbers 11:23) echoes here; the answer is always no.

• Trusting God’s word produces obedience, which unlocks His power—illustrated again in John 2:5: “Do whatever He tells you.”

summary

Elisha obeys God’s directive, sets minimal provisions before many, watches the men eat their fill, and collects leftovers—all exactly “according to the word of the LORD.” The verse spotlights God’s unfailing ability to meet and exceed human need, invites confident obedience to His word, and foreshadows the even greater provision revealed in Christ.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(44) And they did eat, and left thereof.--Comp. our Lord's miracles, already referred to. Bahr denies any miraculous increase of the food. He makes the miracle consist in the fact that the one hundred men were satisfied with the little they received, and even had some to spare. Similarly, Thenius thinks that the provisions were not inconsiderable for a hundred men (?), and that the emphasis of the narrative lies rather on Elisha's absolute confidence in God than on His wonder-working powers; but this is certainly opposed to the sacred writer's intention. Keil rightly calls attention to the fact that Elisha does not perform, but only predicts, this miracle.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 44. - So he set it before them, and they did eat, and left thereof, according to the word of the Lord. We are not expressly told how the miracle was wrought, whether by an augmentation of the quantity of the food supernaturally produced, or by a lessening of the appetites of the men, as Bahr supposes. But the analogy of our Lord's miracles of feeding the multitudes, whereof this is a manifest type, makes it probable that in this case also there was a miraculous increase of the food. The object of the writer in communicating the account is certainly not merely to show how the Lord cared for his servants, but to relate another miracle wrought by Elisha, of a different kind from those previously related. He is occupied with Elisha's miracles through this entire chanter and through the three next.
