God raised Him up on the third day
This phrase emphasizes the divine power and authority of God in the resurrection of Jesus Christ. The resurrection is a central tenet of Christian faith, affirming Jesus as the Son of God and validating His teachings and sacrifice. The "third day" is significant, fulfilling Jesus' own predictions (e.g., Matthew 16:21) and aligning with Jewish understanding of time, where part of a day is considered a whole day. This timing also connects to the prophecy of Jonah (Jonah 1:17), which Jesus referenced as a sign of His resurrection (Matthew 12:40).

and caused Him to be seen—
The visibility of the resurrected Christ is crucial for the establishment of the early church's witness. Jesus appeared to many, including the apostles and over 500 others (1 Corinthians 15:5-8), providing undeniable evidence of His resurrection. This visibility counters any claims that the resurrection was a spiritual or metaphorical event, affirming it as a historical and physical reality. The appearances also fulfilled Jesus' promise to His disciples that they would see Him again (John 16:16-22), strengthening their faith and empowering them to spread the Gospel.

Teaching Points
The Centrality of the Resurrection
The resurrection of Jesus is the foundation of Christian faith. Without it, our faith is in vain. It validates Jesus' divinity and His victory over sin and death.

God's Sovereignty and Power
The resurrection demonstrates God's ultimate authority over life and death, encouraging believers to trust in His power in their own lives.

The Fulfillment of Prophecy
Jesus' resurrection on the third day fulfills Old Testament prophecies and His own predictions, reinforcing the reliability of Scripture.

The Witness of the Resurrection
The fact that Jesus was seen by many after His resurrection provides credible testimony to the truth of the gospel. Believers are called to be witnesses of this truth in their own lives.

Inclusion of the Gentiles
The context of this passage, where Peter shares the gospel with Cornelius, highlights the breaking down of barriers and the inclusion of all people in God's redemptive plan.

What Does Acts 10:40 Mean
God raised Him up

Acts 10:40 begins with a clear declaration: “God raised Him up.”

• This is the Father’s direct, historical action, reversing the verdict of human courts and vindicating His Son (Acts 2:24; Romans 6:4).

• The resurrection is physical, not symbolic; the same Jesus who died now stands alive (Luke 24:39; John 20:27).

• Because God did the raising, every promise tied to Jesus’ life and ministry stands unshakably true (2 Corinthians 4:14; Ephesians 1:19–20).

• For believers, this means hope in our own future resurrection is anchored in God’s proven power (1 Corinthians 15:20–22).

on the third day

The phrase supplies the specific timing God chose.

• Jesus Himself foretold it repeatedly: “The Son of Man will be raised on the third day” (Luke 18:33; see also Matthew 16:21).

• It fulfills Scripture: “that He was raised on the third day according to the Scriptures” (1 Corinthians 15:4), echoing prophetic patterns like Jonah 1:17 and Hosea 6:2.

• The timetable confirms divine intentionality—God is never late; He acts exactly when He said He would.

• The early church proclaimed this detail as a core gospel fact (Acts 13:30–31), grounding faith in verifiable history rather than vague legend.

and caused Him to be seen

Resurrection was not private; God made sure witnesses encountered the risen Christ.

• Jesus “presented Himself to them with many convincing proofs” over forty days (Acts 1:3).

• Eyewitnesses included:

– Mary Magdalene and other women (Matthew 28:9)

– Peter (Luke 24:34)

– The Twelve (John 20:19–23)

– More than 500 brethren at once (1 Corinthians 15:6)

– James and, later, Paul (1 Corinthians 15:7–8)

• These appearances move the resurrection from creed to courtroom—observable, testable, and therefore trustworthy (Acts 2:32; 4:20).

• Because He was “seen,” our faith rests on solid testimony, and our mission is to share what the first witnesses saw (Acts 10:41–42).

summary

Acts 10:40 packs the heart of the gospel into three connected truths: God Himself decisively raised Jesus, He did it precisely on the third day as promised, and He supplied abundant eyewitness evidence. Each element is essential—divine power, prophetic fulfillment, and historical verification—so that every believer can rest assured that the resurrection is factual, foundational, and forever life-changing.
