He is staying with Simon the tanner
In the context of Acts 10, this phrase refers to Peter, who is residing with Simon, a tanner, in Joppa. Tanning was considered an unclean profession by Jewish standards due to the handling of dead animals, which makes Peter's stay significant. It demonstrates the breaking down of Jewish purity laws and foreshadows the inclusion of Gentiles into the Christian faith. This setting highlights Peter's openness to God's leading, preparing him for the vision he receives and his subsequent visit to Cornelius, a Gentile.

whose house is by the sea
Joppa, a port city on the Mediterranean Sea, is the location of Simon the tanner's house. The proximity to the sea is practical for a tanner, as water was essential for the tanning process. This geographical detail underscores the historical accuracy of the account. Joppa is also significant in biblical history as the port from which Jonah attempted to flee from God's command. The mention of the sea may symbolize the expansive reach of the Gospel, as Peter's vision and subsequent actions lead to the spread of Christianity beyond Jewish boundaries, fulfilling the Great Commission to go into all the world.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Cornelius
A centurion in the Roman army, described as a devout and God-fearing man who prayed regularly and gave generously to those in need. He receives a vision from an angel instructing him to send for Peter.

2. Peter (Simon Peter)
One of Jesus' apostles, staying in Joppa at the house of Simon the tanner. He is about to receive a vision that will challenge Jewish dietary laws and open the way for Gentile inclusion in the early Church.

3. Simon the Tanner
A resident of Joppa who works with animal hides, a profession considered unclean by Jewish standards. His house by the sea becomes the setting for Peter's transformative vision.

4. Joppa
A port city on the Mediterranean Sea, significant as a place where Peter receives a vision that leads to the inclusion of Gentiles in the Christian faith.

5. The Angelic Vision
Cornelius receives a vision from an angel instructing him to send for Peter, setting the stage for a pivotal moment in the early Church's mission to the Gentiles.
Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty in Salvation
Acts 10:6 shows God's orchestration in bringing Peter and Cornelius together, emphasizing that God is actively involved in the salvation of individuals.

Breaking Down Barriers
The setting of Simon the tanner's house, a place considered unclean, symbolizes the breaking down of traditional barriers, encouraging believers to reach out beyond their comfort zones.

Obedience to Divine Guidance
Cornelius' immediate obedience to the angel's instructions serves as a model for responding to God's guidance in our lives.

Hospitality and Openness
Simon the tanner's willingness to host Peter, despite societal norms, teaches the importance of hospitality and openness to God's work in unexpected ways.

The Role of Visions and Revelations
Peter's vision challenges preconceived notions and opens the way for new understanding, reminding us to be open to God's revelations that may challenge our current beliefs.

What Does Acts 10:6 Mean
He

• The pronoun points to Simon Peter, the apostle already introduced in Acts 10:5.

• God singles out a real, identifiable man—Peter—underscoring that the gospel’s next step hinges on a historic person, not a vague idea (John 21:17; Galatians 2:7-8).

• Peter’s life up to this point shows a track record of obedience (Acts 9:32-35), making him a fitting instrument for the moment.

is staying

• “Is staying” shows the present, ongoing nature of God’s arrangement. Peter is not passing through; he is settled where the Lord wants him, ready for the next assignment (Psalm 37:23; Acts 10:33).

• The word also hints at availability. Cornelius’s men will find Peter because God has him precisely where they can reach him (Proverbs 16:9; Acts 8:26-31).

with Simon the tanner

• Tanning involved handling dead animals, making a tanner ceremonially unclean under the Law (Leviticus 11:39-40). Peter’s willingness to lodge here foreshadows the vision about clean and unclean foods (Acts 10:28).

• The pairing of two Simons—one an apostle, one a tradesman—illustrates the gospel’s leveling power (Ephesians 2:13-14).

• Acts 9:43 notes that Peter “stayed for many days in Joppa with a tanner named Simon,” showing this was already his chosen base of ministry.

whose house

• God works through ordinary homes, not just temples or synagogues (Luke 19:5; Acts 16:15).

• The mention of the house assures Cornelius’s servants of a definite, physical address—no guesswork, just obedience.

• The detail highlights God’s care over logistics; nothing in His plan is random (Matthew 10:29-31).

is by the sea

• Practical: tanning required copious water and sea breezes to dissipate odor. God’s directions are sensible as well as spiritual.

• Providential: a seaside location in Joppa recalls Jonah 1:3, where another servant wrestled with God’s global mission. Peter, unlike Jonah, will embrace it (Acts 11:17-18).

• Accessible: travelers from Caesarea can follow the coastline straight to the house (Acts 10:32). God clears every obstacle so that Jew and Gentile can meet in Christ.

summary

Acts 10:6 assures us that God masterfully orchestrates people, places, and timing. Peter—a real man in a real house at a real seaside address—becomes the bridge for the gospel to cross fully into the Gentile world. Every phrase of the verse shows God’s precision and grace: the chosen servant, his settled readiness, the unlikely host, the welcoming home, and the strategic location. Nothing is incidental; everything serves the unfolding plan that “everyone who believes in Him receives forgiveness of sins through His name” (Acts 10:43).
