Leviticus 13
1. The laws whereby the priest is to be guided in discerning the leprosy.














Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
The priest shall examine it
In ancient Israel, priests served not only as religious leaders but also as health inspectors. Their role in examining skin conditions highlights the integration of spiritual and physical well-being. This examination process underscores the importance of discernment and careful observation, reflecting the priestly duty to maintain the community's purity.

and if it appears to be beneath the skin
This phrase indicates a deeper infection, suggesting a more serious condition. In biblical times, skin diseases were often feared due to their potential to spread and cause ritual impurity. The depth of the infection symbolizes the pervasive nature of sin, which can penetrate beneath the surface of one's life.

and the hair in it has turned white
White hair in a skin infection was a key diagnostic feature, signifying a change in the natural order and indicating a severe condition. This transformation can be seen as a metaphor for spiritual decay, where sin alters one's natural state of holiness.

the priest shall pronounce him unclean
The declaration of uncleanness had significant social and religious implications, leading to isolation from the community. This reflects the biblical principle that sin separates individuals from God and others, necessitating purification and atonement.

it is a diseased infection that has broken out in the boil
The description of the infection as breaking out suggests an active and spreading condition. This can be likened to the nature of sin, which, if left unchecked, can grow and affect all areas of life. The imagery of a boil emphasizes the painful and visible consequences of sin, calling for repentance and healing.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Moses
The author of Leviticus, who received the laws from God on Mount Sinai. He was responsible for conveying God's laws to the Israelites.

2. Aaron
The brother of Moses and the first high priest of Israel. He and his descendants were responsible for performing the priestly duties, including examining skin diseases.

3. Priests
The descendants of Aaron who were tasked with examining skin diseases and determining the cleanliness or uncleanliness of individuals.

4. Israelites
The people to whom the laws in Leviticus were given. They were to follow these laws to maintain purity and holiness before God.

5. Leprosy
A term used in the Bible to describe various skin diseases, not necessarily the modern disease known as leprosy (Hansen's disease).
Teaching Points
Holiness and Purity
The laws concerning skin diseases remind us of God's call for His people to be holy and pure. We are to examine our lives for spiritual "blemishes" and seek God's cleansing.

Role of the Priesthood
The priests served as mediators between God and the people, pointing to the ultimate High Priest, Jesus Christ, who intercedes for us.

Community Responsibility
The examination and isolation procedures underscore the importance of community health and responsibility. We are called to care for one another and maintain the spiritual health of our communities.

Symbolism of Sin
Leprosy in the Bible often symbolizes sin, which can spread and defile. We must be vigilant in addressing sin in our lives and seek God's forgiveness and healing.

Faith and Obedience
The process of examination and cleansing required faith and obedience to God's instructions. Similarly, we are called to trust and obey God's Word in our daily lives.

What Does Leviticus 13:20 Mean
The priest shall examine it

- God establishes qualified oversight for the health of His people. The priest serves as both medical inspector and spiritual guardian (Leviticus 13:2–3).

- By requiring a personal, hands-on look rather than a distant judgment, the Lord models thorough, compassionate care—anticipating Christ our great High Priest who “sympathizes with our weaknesses” (Hebrews 4:15).

- For believers today, this underscores the value of accountable leadership and honest self-examination (2 Corinthians 13:5).

and if it appears to be beneath the skin

- The key test is depth. A surface irritation could clear up, but a lesion that sinks “beneath the skin” signals something entrenched (Leviticus 13:4).

- Sin works the same way—it starts visibly but quickly burrows into the heart (Mark 7:20-23). The Lord who “looks at the heart” (1 Samuel 16:7) calls His people to deal with issues before they take root.

- Practical takeaway: don’t dismiss early warning signs in your life; seek cleansing while the matter is still small.

and the hair in it has turned white

- White hairs meant the normal, pigmented hair had died and been replaced—proof the infection was advancing (Leviticus 13:25).

- Scripture often links whiteness with a change of condition: purity when God cleanses (Isaiah 1:18) or decay when disease sets in, as here.

- In spiritual terms, small compromises eventually show outwardly; what began hidden becomes visible for all to see (Numbers 32:23).

the priest shall pronounce him unclean

- The declaration carries authority; it is not merely medical opinion but covenantal judgment (Leviticus 13:46).

- Separation was required to protect the camp (Numbers 5:2) and to drive the sufferer to seek God’s remedy.

- The pattern anticipates Christ, who has both the right to call out uncleanness and the power to cleanse it (Luke 5:12-14).

- Believers are reminded that confession aligns us with God’s verdict so we can receive His cleansing (1 John 1:9).

it is a diseased infection that has broken out in the boil

- A boil was a previous wound; the new outbreak shows how old hurts can harbor fresh danger.

- James 1:15 speaks of desire conceiving sin that “gives birth to death”—a graphic parallel to an infection erupting from a boil.

- The verse teaches vigilance: yesterday’s injuries, if unattended, may spawn today’s crisis.

summary

Leviticus 13:20 provides a concrete, literal guideline for diagnosing leprous disease, safeguarding Israel’s physical and spiritual welfare. Its sequence—careful examination, assessment of depth and visible change, authoritative verdict, and mandated separation—mirrors the way God deals with sin. He inspects hearts, exposes what lies beneath, declares the true condition, and then offers cleansing through the ultimate Priest, Jesus Christ. The verse calls us to honest self-examination, respect for godly authority, and prompt response to the Lord’s provision for our purity.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(20) Behold, it be in sight lower than the skin.--Better, Behold, its appearance is lower than the other skin. If upon examination the priest finds that the spot has assumed a deeper appearance than the rest of the skin, and the hair turned white--which were the two critical symptoms--he forthwith declared it leprosy.
