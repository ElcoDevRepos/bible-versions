Then the LORD said to Moses,
This phrase indicates a direct communication from God to Moses, a common occurrence in the Pentateuch, where Moses serves as the primary mediator between God and the Israelites. The phrase underscores the divine authority behind the instructions that follow, emphasizing that these are not merely human ideas but commands from God Himself. This direct communication is foundational to the covenant relationship between God and Israel, as Moses is often seen as a type of Christ, serving as a mediator and leader. The phrase also highlights the importance of revelation in the biblical narrative, where God reveals His will to His chosen leaders. This communication is part of the broader context of Leviticus, which details the laws and regulations for worship and holiness, reflecting God's desire for His people to be set apart.
Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant-keeping God of Israel, who is holy and requires holiness from His people.

2. Moses
The prophet and leader of the Israelites, chosen by God to deliver His laws and commandments to the people.

3. Israelites
The chosen people of God, who are receiving instructions on how to live in a way that honors God.
Teaching Points
God's Authority
Recognize that God, as the ultimate authority, has the right to set standards for His people. His commands are not arbitrary but are rooted in His holy nature.

Holiness as a Lifestyle
Understand that holiness is not just about ritual purity but encompasses all aspects of life. As believers, we are called to reflect God's holiness in our daily actions and decisions.

Obedience to God's Word
Emphasize the importance of obedience to God's instructions as a demonstration of our love and reverence for Him. Obedience is a key aspect of our relationship with God.

Role of Mediators
Acknowledge the role of Moses as a mediator between God and the Israelites, which foreshadows the ultimate mediation of Christ. This highlights the need for a mediator in our relationship with God.

Continuity of God's Character
Reflect on the consistency of God's character from the Old Testament to the New Testament. His call to holiness remains unchanged, and His desire for a relationship with His people is evident throughout Scripture.

What Does Leviticus 22:26 Mean
Then

• The word “Then” signals a real moment in history, following the instructions about acceptable offerings in Leviticus 22:17-25.

• It anchors the narrative in a sequence of events—first God clarified the purity of sacrifices, then He moved to the next point (cf. Exodus 40:34-35, where one completed command leads directly to another).

• This simple connector reminds us that God’s revelation unfolds step-by-step; obedience to earlier truth prepares the way for further insight (John 14:21).

the LORD

• “the LORD” translates God’s covenant name, underscoring His unchanging faithfulness (Exodus 3:14; Malachi 3:6).

• Because He is holy, the regulations that follow carry divine authority, not human opinion (Leviticus 19:2).

• Scripture records that it is the LORD who sets the terms for worship; Israel does not invent them (Deuteronomy 12:32).

said

• God speaks—He is not silent or distant (Genesis 1:3; Hebrews 1:1-2).

• His spoken word carries creative and binding power; what He utters becomes law for His people (Psalm 33:9).

• The verse confirms the literal inspiration of Scripture: “All Scripture is God-breathed” (2 Timothy 3:16), including these sacrificial details.

to Moses

• Moses is identified as the mediator who receives and relays God’s commands (Exodus 33:11; Numbers 12:7-8).

• By naming Moses, the text roots the instruction within covenant leadership, ensuring the people can trust its authenticity (Deuteronomy 34:10).

• The phrase highlights personal relationship; the LORD addresses Moses directly, modeling how He desires to guide His servants (Psalm 25:14).

summary

Leviticus 22:26—“Then the LORD said to Moses,”—serves as a hinge verse. It links prior teaching on sacrificial purity with the forthcoming law about the age of animals offered to God. Every word reinforces key truths: God reveals His will in an orderly sequence; His covenant name guarantees authority and faithfulness; His spoken word is inspired and binding; and He works through chosen mediators so His people can hear, understand, and obey.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(26) And the Lord spake unto Moses.--Though beginning with a separate formula, and thus indicating that it is a distinct communication, the regulations here laid down about the age of the sacrificial animals are necessarily connected with the preceding statutes, and exhibit a logical sequence.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 26, 27. - Extreme youth is to be regarded as a blemish in an animal in the same way as other defects. During the young creature's first week of existence it is not considered as having arrived at the perfection of its individual and separate life, and therefore only from the eighth day and thenceforth it shall be accepted for an offering made by fire unto the Lord. Up to what ago an animal might be offered is not stated. Gideon is narrated as offering a bullock of seven years old (Judges 6:25).
