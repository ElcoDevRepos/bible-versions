so that he does not defile his offspring among his people
This phrase emphasizes the importance of maintaining purity and holiness within the priestly lineage. In the context of Leviticus, the priests were set apart to serve God and were required to adhere to strict standards of conduct and purity. The defilement of offspring could refer to marrying someone who was not considered pure according to the Levitical laws, which could lead to a compromised priestly line. This reflects the broader biblical theme of holiness and separation from sin, as seen in passages like 1 Peter 1:16, "Be holy, because I am holy." The concern for purity among the people of Israel is a recurring theme, highlighting the need for a community that reflects God's holiness.

for I am the LORD who sanctifies him.
This phrase underscores the divine authority and role of God in the sanctification process. The sanctification of the priests was not merely a result of their adherence to the law but was ultimately an act of God. This reflects the broader biblical principle that holiness and sanctification are gifts from God, as seen in passages like Hebrews 10:10, "And by that will, we have been made holy through the sacrifice of the body of Jesus Christ once for all." The phrase also points to the covenant relationship between God and Israel, where God sets apart His people for His purposes. The sanctification of the priests serves as a type of Christ, who is the ultimate High Priest, sanctifying believers through His sacrifice.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Priests
The primary focus of Leviticus 21 is on the priests, specifically the high priest, who is held to a higher standard of holiness due to his role in mediating between God and the people.

2. The Offspring
The verse refers to the offspring of the priests, emphasizing the importance of maintaining purity and holiness within the priestly lineage.

3. The LORD
God Himself is the one who sanctifies the priests, setting them apart for His service and requiring them to adhere to His standards of holiness.

4. The Israelites
The broader community among whom the priests serve and whose spiritual well-being is impacted by the priests' adherence to God's commands.

5. The Tabernacle
The central place of worship where the priests perform their duties, symbolizing God's presence among His people.

What Does Leviticus 21:15 Mean
So that he does not defile

• The phrase signals purpose: the priest’s marriage restrictions (v. 14) exist “so that” impurity never enters his life or ministry.

• Scripture consistently links priestly defilement to disobedience (Leviticus 10:1-3; Ezekiel 44:21-23).

• Defilement is real, not symbolic only; it disqualifies a priest from serving (Leviticus 22:2-3).

His offspring

• God’s concern stretches beyond the priest to the next generation.

• Offspring carry the priestly line (Numbers 25:13) and must remain holy to serve (Leviticus 6:22).

• Marrying within Israel preserves covenant identity (Deuteronomy 7:3-4) and guards children from idolatry (Malachi 2:11-15).

Among his people

• Holiness is a public matter; the priest ministers “among” Israel, not in private corners (Leviticus 21:24).

• When leaders sin, the whole community suffers (Joshua 7:1-12).

• God calls Israel “a kingdom of priests and a holy nation” (Exodus 19:6), so priestly purity models national purity.

For I am the LORD

• The authority behind the command is God’s own character.

• Every “I am the LORD” anchors obedience in who He is (Leviticus 18:2-5; 19:36).

• His holiness defines morality; He does not ask what He Himself is not (1 Peter 1:15-16).

Who sanctifies him

• Sanctification is God-initiated; the priest cooperates by obeying (Leviticus 20:7-8).

• God alone appoints and maintains priests (Exodus 28:1; Hebrews 5:4).

• The New Covenant parallels remain: believers are sanctified by God through Christ (1 Corinthians 1:30; Hebrews 10:14) and must live accordingly.

summary

Leviticus 21:15 teaches that a priest’s marriage choices protect the holiness of his lineage, prevent community pollution, and honor the God who alone sets him apart. Obedience safeguards future generations, upholds public witness, and reflects the sanctifying character of the LORD.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(15) Neither shall he profane his seed.--Better, And he shall not profane; that is, he is not to contract any of these forbidden marriages, lest he should thereby degrade his offspring, since the children of such an issue, as well as their mother, were debarred the privileges of the priesthood, and were not permitted to partake of those portions of the sacrifices which formed the perquisites of the officiating priests.
