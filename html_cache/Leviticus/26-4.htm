I will give you rains in their season
This promise of rain is a direct blessing from God, emphasizing His control over nature and His provision for His people. In the ancient Near East, agriculture was heavily dependent on seasonal rains, particularly the early and latter rains mentioned in Deuteronomy 11:14. The Israelites understood rain as a sign of divine favor, as seen in 1 Kings 18:41-45, where Elijah prays for rain after a drought. The phrase underscores the covenant relationship between God and Israel, where obedience leads to blessings (Deuteronomy 28:12). Rain in its season also symbolizes spiritual blessings and the outpouring of the Holy Spirit, as seen in Joel 2:23-29 and Acts 2:17-18.

and the land will yield its produce
This promise highlights the fertility of the Promised Land, a land "flowing with milk and honey" (Exodus 3:8). The land's productivity is contingent upon Israel's faithfulness to God's commandments. The produce of the land is a tangible sign of God's provision and faithfulness, as seen in the blessings of Deuteronomy 28:4-5. The land yielding its produce also points to the eschatological hope of a restored creation, where the earth will be renewed and fruitful (Isaiah 35:1-2). This phrase can be seen as a type of the spiritual fruitfulness expected of believers, as described in John 15:5 and Galatians 5:22-23.

and the trees of the field will bear their fruit
The fruit-bearing trees symbolize abundance and prosperity, reflecting the Edenic ideal where God provides abundantly for His creation (Genesis 1:29). In the context of Leviticus, this promise is part of the blessings for obedience, contrasting with the curses for disobedience that include agricultural failure (Leviticus 26:20). The imagery of fruitful trees is also used in prophetic literature to describe the Messianic age, where peace and prosperity will prevail (Ezekiel 34:27). In the New Testament, Jesus uses the metaphor of trees and fruit to teach about the evidence of a righteous life (Matthew 7:17-20), and believers are encouraged to bear spiritual fruit as a sign of their connection to Christ (John 15:8).

Teaching Points
Obedience Brings Blessings
The verse underscores the principle that God's blessings are often contingent upon our obedience to His commands.

God's Provision
Trust in God's provision is essential, as He promises to meet our needs when we align our lives with His will.

Seasonal Timing
Just as rain comes in its season, God's blessings often come in His perfect timing, requiring patience and faith.

Fruitfulness as a Sign of Faithfulness
The fruitfulness of the land is a metaphor for the spiritual fruitfulness that results from a life lived in obedience to God.

Covenant Relationship
Understanding our relationship with God as a covenant helps us see the importance of faithfulness and the blessings that follow.

What Does Leviticus 26:4 Mean
I will give you rains in their season

- God Himself promises timely rainfall, underscoring His direct control over the natural world (Job 38:25–28; Psalm 65:9–10).

- The phrase “in their season” stresses order and reliability. Just as Genesis 8:22 affirms that seedtime and harvest will not cease, here the Lord guarantees a dependable rhythm that sustains life.

- This blessing is explicitly linked to covenant obedience (Leviticus 26:3). Similar cause-and-effect language appears in Deuteronomy 11:13-15, where faithful devotion results in “rain for your land in season.”

- Tangibly, timely rain meant security: crops could germinate, grow, and mature without fear of drought (1 Kings 8:35-36; James 5:17-18).

and the land will yield its produce

- With water supplied, the soil responds. The earth that once produced “thorns and thistles” because of sin (Genesis 3:17-18) now, under God’s favor, brings forth abundance.

- “Yield” conveys overflow rather than mere survival—an echo of Psalm 67:6: “The earth has yielded its harvest; God, our God, blesses us.”

- This promise anticipates life in the land as a foretaste of Eden restored, pointing ahead to the future renewal pictured in Isaiah 55:12-13 and Romans 8:19-21.

- Practically, a fruitful land meant social stability: granaries full (Proverbs 3:9-10), freedom from famine (Psalm 37:18-19), and ability to share with the needy (Leviticus 19:9-10).

and the trees of the field will bear their fruit

- The blessing reaches upward to orchards and vineyards, completing the food chain. Joel 2:22-24 mirrors this threefold flourishing—pasture, grain, and trees—following repentance.

- Fruit trees take years to mature; their productivity signals long-term peace. No invading armies or locust plagues interrupt the growth (Amos 9:13-15).

- Spiritually, fruitful trees picture the believer who delights in God’s law, “like a tree planted by streams of water” (Psalm 1:3; Jeremiah 17:7-8). The land’s visible fruit mirrors the people’s inner fruit of obedience.

summary

Leviticus 26:4 presents a layered promise: God grants timely rain, the soil responds with crops, and trees overflow with fruit. Each step builds on the last, illustrating how covenant faithfulness unlocks comprehensive blessing—material provision, security, and a reflection of restored Edenic harmony.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(4) Then I will give you rain in due season.--Better, then I will give you your rains in due season, that is, the former and latter rains (Deuteronomy 11:14). In Palestine the proper season for the early rain is from about the middle of October until December, thus preparing the ground for receiving the seed, whilst that of the latter or vernal rain is in the months of March and April, just before the harvest. Thus, also, in the covenant which God is to make with His people, a similar promise is made, "I will cause the showers to come down in his season; there shall be showers of blessing" (Ezekiel 34:26).

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 4-6. - These verses appear to have been in the mind, not of Joel only, as already pointed out, but of Ezekiel (Ezekiel 34:20-31). In Leviticus we find, Then I will give you rain in due season; in Ezekiel, "And I will cause the shower to come down in his season; there shall be showers of blessing." In Leviticus, And the land shall yield her increase, and the trees of the field shall yield their fruit; in Ezekiel, "And the tree of the field shall yield her fruit, and the earth her increase." In Leviticus, Ye shall dwell in your land safely; in Ezekiel, "They shall dwell safely in the wilderness, and sleep in the woods." In Leviticus, And I will give peace in the land, and ye shall lie down, and none shall make you afraid: and will rid evil beasts out of the land, neither shall the sword go through your land; in Ezekiel, "And I will make with them a covenant of peace, and will cause the evil beasts to cease out of the land.... And they shall no more be a prey to the heathen, neither shall the beast of the land devour them; but they shall dwell safely, and none shall make them afraid." The promise, Your threshing shall reach unto the vintage, and the vintage shall reach unto the sowing time, is similar to that in the prophet Amos, "Behold, the days come, saith the Lord, that the plowman shall overtake the reaper, and the treader of grapes him that soweth seed" (Amos 9:13).
