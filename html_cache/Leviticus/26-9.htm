Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
I will turn toward you
This phrase signifies God's favor and attention towards His people. In the context of Leviticus, it reflects the covenant relationship between God and Israel, where obedience to His commandments results in His blessings. The turning of God's face is often associated with His presence and approval, as seen in the Aaronic blessing in Numbers 6:24-26. It implies a personal and relational aspect of God's interaction with His people.

and make you fruitful and multiply you
This promise echoes the original blessing given to Adam and Eve in Genesis 1:28 and later to Noah in Genesis 9:1. It signifies not only physical multiplication but also prosperity and success in various aspects of life. In the agrarian society of ancient Israel, fruitfulness was directly linked to agricultural success and the growth of families, which were seen as signs of divine favor. This promise also connects to the Abrahamic covenant in Genesis 17:6, where God promises to make Abraham exceedingly fruitful.

and I will establish My covenant with you
The covenant mentioned here refers to the Mosaic Covenant, which was established at Mount Sinai. This covenant was conditional, based on Israel's obedience to God's laws. The establishment of the covenant implies a reaffirmation and continuation of the relationship between God and Israel. It also points forward to the New Covenant prophesied in Jeremiah 31:31-34, which is fulfilled in Jesus Christ, who establishes an eternal covenant through His sacrifice. The concept of covenant is central to understanding the biblical narrative, as it outlines the terms of the relationship between God and His people.

Teaching Points
God's Faithfulness
God is faithful to His promises. Just as He promised to make Israel fruitful and multiply them, He remains faithful to His promises to us today.

Covenant Relationship
Our relationship with God is based on His covenant. Understanding this helps us appreciate the depth of His commitment to us.

Obedience and Blessing
The blessings of fruitfulness and multiplication are tied to obedience. We are called to live in obedience to God's commands to experience His full blessings.

Spiritual Fruitfulness
While the promise in Leviticus is physical, it points to spiritual fruitfulness in the New Testament. Believers are called to bear spiritual fruit as evidence of their relationship with Christ.

Community and Growth
God's promise to multiply His people emphasizes the importance of community and growth. We are called to invest in our spiritual communities and encourage growth in faith.

What Does Leviticus 26:9 Mean
I will turn toward you

- The Lord’s personal attention is promised; He is not distant but actively “turns” toward His people.

- When God faces His people, favor replaces alienation (Psalm 80:3; Numbers 6:25).

- This turning follows obedience spelled out earlier in Leviticus 26; blessing is relational, not mechanical (Deuteronomy 30:9–10; 2 Chronicles 7:14).

- Assurance: “If God is for us, who can be against us?” (Romans 8:31).

make you fruitful

- Fruitfulness includes abundant harvests and thriving families (Genesis 1:28; Deuteronomy 28:4).

- God Himself is the source; human effort alone cannot secure lasting increase (Psalm 127:1–3).

- Spiritual application: believers bear “fruit of righteousness” when abiding in Him (John 15:5; Philippians 1:11).

and multiply you

- Expansion echoes God’s covenant words to Abraham: “I will make you exceedingly fruitful” (Genesis 17:6).

- Multiplication signals stability and future security (Exodus 1:7; Ezekiel 36:10–11).

- For the church, it foreshadows the spread of the gospel to “all nations” (Matthew 28:19; Acts 9:31).

I will establish My covenant with you

- God’s blessings rest on a covenant foundation, not fleeting emotion (Genesis 17:7; Exodus 6:4).

- “Establish” underscores certainty; His promises are irrevocable (Psalm 89:34; Hebrews 6:17–18).

- The ultimate fulfillment is the new covenant in Christ’s blood, extending grace to Jew and Gentile alike (Jeremiah 31:31–33; Luke 22:20; Galatians 3:14).

summary

Leviticus 26:9 assures obedient Israel of God’s attentive favor, abundant increase, and unshakable covenant. These promises reveal His character: personally involved, generous in blessing, and faithful to His word—truths that continue to anchor faith today.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(9) For I will have respect unto you.--Better, And I will turn unto you, as it is rendered in the Authorised Version in Ezekiel 46:9, the only other passage where this phrase occurs; that is, be merciful to them and bless them. (Comp. 2Kings 13:23; Psalm 25:16; Psalm 69:17, &c.)

And multiply you, and establish my covenant with you.--That is, by multiplying them as the stars of heaven and the sand of the sea, God fulfil the covenant which He made with their fathers (Genesis 12:2; Genesis 13:16; Genesis 15:5; Genesis 22:17; Exodus 23:26). . . .
