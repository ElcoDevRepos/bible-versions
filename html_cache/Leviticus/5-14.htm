Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Then the LORD
This phrase emphasizes the direct communication from God, highlighting the divine authority and origin of the instructions that follow. In the context of Leviticus, God is establishing a covenantal relationship with the Israelites, providing them with laws and guidelines for worship and community living. This divine communication underscores the holiness and sovereignty of God, a recurring theme throughout the Pentateuch.

said to Moses
Moses serves as the mediator between God and the Israelites. His role as a prophet and leader is central to the narrative of the Exodus and the giving of the Law. Moses' unique relationship with God is highlighted in passages such as Exodus 33:11, where it is said that the LORD spoke to Moses face to face. This phrase also connects to the broader narrative of Moses receiving the Law on Mount Sinai, as seen in Exodus 19-20, establishing the foundation for Israel's identity as God's chosen people.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant God of Israel, who is holy and righteous, giving instructions to His people through Moses.

2. Moses
The prophet and leader of the Israelites, chosen by God to deliver His laws and commandments to the people.

3. Israelites
The chosen people of God, who are receiving the laws and instructions for living a holy life in accordance with God's will.

4. The Tabernacle
The place where God dwells among His people and where the sacrificial system is carried out.

5. Sin Offering
A type of sacrifice prescribed in the Mosaic Law for atonement of unintentional sins, highlighting the need for purification and reconciliation with God.
Teaching Points
Understanding God's Holiness
God's instructions to Moses highlight His holiness and the seriousness of sin. Believers are called to recognize the gravity of sin and the need for atonement.

The Role of the Mediator
Moses serves as a mediator between God and the Israelites, prefiguring Christ as the ultimate mediator who intercedes on behalf of humanity.

The Necessity of Atonement
The sin offering underscores the necessity of atonement for sin. In Christ, believers find the fulfillment of this need, as He is the perfect sacrifice.

Living a Life of Repentance
The sacrificial system teaches the importance of repentance and turning back to God. Christians are encouraged to live a life marked by continual repentance and faith.

The Continuity of God's Plan
The instructions given in Leviticus are part of God's unfolding plan of redemption, culminating in Jesus Christ. Understanding this continuity helps believers appreciate the depth of God's love and grace.

What Does Leviticus 5:14 Mean
Then

- Signals a specific moment; God initiates the next instruction after laying out prior offerings (Leviticus 1–5:13).

- Reminds us that God’s directives are orderly and timely, building one truth upon another (Leviticus 1:1; Exodus 40:17).

- God’s grace meets Israel right when it is needed—after showing how sin offerings work, He now clarifies guilt offerings.

the LORD

- The covenant name stresses His unchanging holiness and mercy (Exodus 34:6-7; Malachi 3:6).

- Because He alone is God (Isaiah 45:5-6), His words carry absolute authority; no human opinion can revise them.

- All sacrificial details flow from His character, pointing forward to Christ, the perfect fulfillment (Hebrews 10:1-10).

said

- God speaks; revelation does not arise from Moses’ imagination (Deuteronomy 18:18; 2 Peter 1:21).

- His spoken word creates, commands, and corrects (Genesis 1:3; Psalm 33:9).

- By verbal communication, He makes His will plain so that obedience is possible (Deuteronomy 30:11-14).

- Scripture’s reliability rests on this divine speech, not on human tradition (Hebrews 1:1-2).

to Moses

- Moses functions as mediator for the people (Exodus 3:10-12; Galatians 3:19).

- God chooses a faithful servant to transmit precise instructions, emphasizing leadership accountability (Numbers 12:6-8).

- Through Moses, Israel receives the guilt-offering law that protects fellowship with God when sacred things are violated (Leviticus 5:15-19).

- The personal address shows God’s nearness—He involves Himself with humanity rather than remaining distant (Exodus 33:11).

summary

Leviticus 5:14 underscores that at a definite moment God, the covenant Lord, deliberately spoke to His chosen mediator. Every word that follows about the guilt offering comes from His sovereign, trustworthy mouth. The verse reminds us that worship regulations are not human inventions; they are divine provisions ensuring that sinful people can live in covenant relationship with a holy God, a relationship ultimately realized in Jesus Christ, our perfect sacrifice and mediator.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(14) And the Lord spake unto Moses.--As the introductory formula implies, this is another communication made to the lawgiver at a different time, and sets forth a further development of the laws respecting the trespass offering.

Pulpit Commentary
Verses 14, 15. - If a soul commit a trespass. Two previous conditions were required of the Israelite before he might offer his trespass offering.

1. He must make compensation for any harm or injury that he had done.

2. He must give to the injured party a fine equal to one-fifth (i.e., two-tenths) of the value of the thing of which he had deprived him, if the wrong was capable of being so estimated. In performing his sacrifice, he had
