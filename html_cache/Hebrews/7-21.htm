Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
but Jesus became a priest with an oath
This phrase highlights the unique nature of Jesus' priesthood. Unlike the Levitical priests, who were appointed based on lineage, Jesus' priesthood was established by a divine oath. This reflects Psalm 110:4, where God Himself declares the eternal priesthood of the Messiah. The use of an oath signifies the unchangeable and eternal nature of Jesus' priesthood, contrasting with the temporary and imperfect priesthood of the Old Covenant.

by the One who said to Him:
This refers to God the Father, emphasizing the divine authority behind Jesus' priesthood. The Father’s declaration underscores the legitimacy and supremacy of Jesus' role as High Priest. This divine appointment is rooted in the eternal counsel of God, highlighting the Trinitarian relationship and the fulfillment of God's redemptive plan.

“The Lord has sworn and will not change His mind:
This part of the verse underscores the irrevocable nature of God's promise. In biblical context, an oath from God is a solemn and binding declaration. The assurance that God "will not change His mind" reflects His immutability and faithfulness. This is a direct reference to Psalm 110:4, a messianic psalm that prophesies the eternal priesthood of the Messiah, affirming the certainty and permanence of Jesus' priestly role.

‘You are a priest forever.’”
This phrase confirms the eternal nature of Jesus' priesthood, distinguishing it from the temporary Levitical priesthood. The term "forever" indicates that Jesus' priestly ministry is unending, aligning with the order of Melchizedek, who is a type of Christ. Melchizedek's priesthood, mentioned in Genesis 14 and expounded in Hebrews 7, serves as a foreshadowing of Christ's eternal and superior priesthood. This eternal aspect assures believers of a perpetual intercessor and mediator in Jesus Christ.

Teaching Points
The Certainty of God's Promises
God's oath underscores the certainty and unchangeable nature of His promises. Believers can trust in the eternal priesthood of Jesus as a foundation for their faith.

The Superiority of Christ's Priesthood
Jesus' priesthood is superior to the Levitical priesthood because it is eternal and established by God's oath. This assures believers of a perfect mediator who intercedes for them continually.

The Unchanging Nature of God
The phrase "will not change His mind" highlights God's immutability. Believers can find comfort in knowing that God's purposes and promises are steadfast.

The Role of Jesus as Our High Priest
As our eternal high priest, Jesus provides a direct and permanent way to God. Believers are encouraged to draw near to God with confidence, knowing that Jesus intercedes on their behalf.

Living in Light of Christ's Eternal Priesthood
Understanding Jesus' eternal priesthood should inspire believers to live lives of worship and obedience, recognizing His ongoing work and presence in their lives.

What Does Hebrews 7:21 Mean
But Jesus became a priest

- “But Jesus became a priest” (Hebrews 7:21) contrasts Him with the sons of Levi, who “became priests without an oath” (v. 20).

- His priesthood rests not on lineage but on divine designation. Hebrews 7:16 affirms He was appointed “on the basis of the power of an indestructible life.”

- Unlike the many priests who “were prevented by death from continuing in office” (v. 23), Jesus stands as “a great High Priest who has passed through the heavens” (Hebrews 4:14).

with an oath

- God’s oath elevates Christ’s priesthood above every human arrangement.

- Where the law named Aaron’s sons without swearing, God seals Jesus’ role with His own word, echoing the divine promise to Abraham “By Myself I have sworn” (Genesis 22:16).

- Hebrews 6:17–18 notes that two unchangeable things—God’s promise and His oath—make strong consolation for believers.

by the One who said to Him

- The Father personally addresses the Son, underscoring their distinct persons yet perfect unity (John 5:19–23).

- At Jesus’ baptism the Father declared, “This is My beloved Son, in whom I am well pleased” (Matthew 3:17), confirming heavenly endorsement of every aspect of His mission—including His eternal priesthood.

The Lord has sworn

- Psalm 110:4, quoted here, is the cornerstone: “The LORD has sworn and will not change His mind.”

- Divine swearing signals absolute certainty. Titus 1:2 affirms God “who cannot lie,” so His sworn word is the believer’s sure foundation.

and will not change His mind

- God’s immutability guarantees permanence. Numbers 23:19 states He “does not change His mind,” and Malachi 3:6 adds, “I, the LORD, do not change.”

- Because the oath can never be revoked, neither can the office it secures.

You are a priest forever

- The term “forever” means endless duration, fulfilled because Jesus “lives forever” (Hebrews 7:24).

- His eternal advocacy ensures that “He is able to save to the uttermost those who draw near to God through Him” (v. 25).

- Revelation 1:18 portrays the risen Christ declaring, “I am alive forevermore,” matching Psalm 110:4’s promise of an everlasting priesthood.

summary

Hebrews 7:21 teaches that Jesus’ priesthood is uniquely established by God’s irrevocable oath. Unlike the temporary, hereditary Levitical order, His office rests on the Father’s sworn, unchangeable word and endures forever. Believers therefore have an eternal High Priest whose saving work and intercession can never fail.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(21) For those priests.--Rather, For they indeed have been made priests without an oath.

By him that said unto him.--Better, by (or, through) Him that saith of Him. The last five words of the verse are absent from the best authorities: they were not needed for this part of the argument, and are therefore omitted from the quotation. All that has been said in chap. 6 (Hebrews 6:13-18) on confirmation by oath must be brought in here (see Notes on Hebrews 6:16-17): the words of the Psalm are really words of promise, and the "more abundant encouragement" is given us by means of the oath that shall never be reversed.
