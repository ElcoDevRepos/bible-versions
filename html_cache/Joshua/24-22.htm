Then Joshua told them,
Joshua, the leader of Israel after Moses, is addressing the Israelites at Shechem. This location is significant as it is where God first promised the land to Abraham (Genesis 12:6-7). Joshua is nearing the end of his life and is reaffirming the covenant between God and Israel. His role as a leader is to guide the people in their commitment to God, emphasizing the importance of serving the LORD faithfully.

“You are witnesses against yourselves
Joshua is emphasizing personal accountability. In the ancient Near Eastern context, covenants often required witnesses to ensure the terms were upheld. Here, the Israelites themselves are the witnesses, highlighting the seriousness of their commitment. This self-witnessing underscores the personal responsibility each individual has in their relationship with God.

that you have chosen to serve the LORD.”
The choice to serve the LORD is a central theme in the book of Joshua. This decision is not just a verbal agreement but a commitment to live according to God's laws and commands. The act of choosing is significant, as it reflects the free will given by God to His people. This echoes the call to choose life and blessings by obeying God, as seen in Deuteronomy 30:19-20.

“We are witnesses!” they said.
The response of the Israelites indicates their agreement and acceptance of the covenant. This collective affirmation is crucial, as it shows unity and shared responsibility among the people. Their declaration is a public acknowledgment of their commitment to God, similar to the public confessions seen in other parts of Scripture, such as Nehemiah 9. This moment foreshadows the New Testament call for believers to be witnesses of Christ (Acts 1:8), emphasizing the continuity of God's call for His people to testify to their faith.

2. The Israelites
The people of God who have settled in the Promised Land. They are gathered at Shechem to renew their commitment to serve the LORD.

3. Shechem
A significant location in Israel's history, where God first promised the land to Abraham and where Joshua renews the covenant with the people.

4. Covenant Renewal
The event where Joshua calls the Israelites to reaffirm their commitment to serve the LORD and forsake other gods.

5. Witnesses
The Israelites themselves, who declare their choice to serve the LORD, thus holding themselves accountable to their promise.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Choice
Every believer must consciously choose to serve the LORD, recognizing the weight of this decision.

Accountability in Faith
By declaring their choice, the Israelites hold themselves accountable. Similarly, Christians should be accountable to their commitments to God.

The Role of Witnesses
Being a witness to one's own promises to God emphasizes personal responsibility in the faith journey.

Renewal of Commitment
Regularly renewing one's commitment to God is vital for spiritual growth and alignment with His will.

Forsaking Other 'Gods'
Just as the Israelites were called to forsake other gods, Christians must identify and turn away from modern-day idols that compete for their devotion.

What Does Joshua 24:22 Mean
Then Joshua told them

Joshua has just concluded a sweeping recount of God’s faithfulness from Abraham to the present conquest (Joshua 24:1-13). Standing in Shechem, he presses the people for a decisive response.

• The setting echoes earlier covenant renewals (Exodus 24:3-8), reminding the nation that history with God carries present obligation.

• Joshua, Israel’s appointed leader (Joshua 1:5-9), speaks with the same authority Moses carried (Deuteronomy 31:7-8), underscoring the gravity of the moment.

• By calling for a verbal acknowledgment, Joshua follows the biblical pattern that confession is made “with the mouth” (Romans 10:9-10) and testimony is established “by two or three witnesses” (Deuteronomy 19:15).

You are witnesses against yourselves

A witness in Scripture is more than an observer; it is someone whose testimony can confirm or condemn (Matthew 12:37).

• Joshua places the people in the witness stand of their own hearts. Their words will later be evidence if they break the covenant (Deuteronomy 30:19-20).

• The phrase also anticipates the twelve-stone monument erected earlier (Joshua 4:3-7) and the large stone soon to be set up at Shechem (Joshua 24:26-27); both are silent but enduring witnesses.

• Personal accountability is highlighted. Just as Achan’s sin had corporate fallout (Joshua 7:1-26), so national disobedience would bring consequences (Judges 2:1-3).

That you have chosen to serve the LORD

Service to the LORD (YHWH) is voluntary yet covenantal.

• Their “choice” affirms free, conscious commitment—mirroring Moses’ call, “Choose life” (Deuteronomy 30:19).

• Service (Hebrew concept of worship-work) involves exclusive loyalty (Exodus 20:3-5). Mixing devotion with the foreign gods just mentioned (Joshua 24:14-15) would nullify their pledge.

• The line looks ahead to Joshua’s well-known declaration, “But as for me and my household, we will serve the LORD” (Joshua 24:15). Leadership models obedience; people must follow.

• Covenant blessing or curse hinges on this service (Leviticus 26:3-46; Deuteronomy 28:1-68).

“We are witnesses!” they said

The congregation immediately affirms Joshua’s charge.

• Their unified response reflects corporate solidarity seen when they shouted at Jericho (Joshua 6:20) and agreed to the covenant oath concerning Rahab (Joshua 2:17-21).

• An audible, collective “amen” seals the vow (Nehemiah 8:6). This echoes Israel’s earlier acceptance, “All that the LORD has spoken we will do” (Exodus 19:8).

• Their declaration leaves no room for later denial—mirroring Peter’s bold pledge (Mark 14:29-31), with the sober reminder that promises must be kept (Ecclesiastes 5:4-6).

• Joshua’s ensuing actions—recording the words in the Book of the Law and setting up a stone (Joshua 24:25-27)—convert their spoken witness into permanent record.

summary

Joshua 24:22 crystallizes a covenant moment. God’s leader confronts Israel with their own words, making them legal witnesses to their declared choice. The verse teaches that confession of loyalty to the LORD carries binding accountability, demands exclusive service, and establishes a testimony that will stand for or against us in the future.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 22 - Ye are witnesses against yourselves. Joshua has not disguised from them the difficulty of the task they have undertaken. Like a true guide and father, he has placed the case fully and fairly before them, and they have made their choice. He reminds them that their own words so deliberately uttered will be forever witnesses against them, should they afterwards refuse to keep an engagement into which they entered with their eyes open. They do not in any way shrink from the responsibility, and by accepting the situation as it is placed before them, render it impossible henceforth to plead ignorance or surprise as an excuse for their disobedience. And it is well to observe, as has been remarked above, that such an excuse never was pleaded afterwards, that the obligation, though evaded, was never disavowed.
