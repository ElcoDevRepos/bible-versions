For you hate My instruction
This phrase highlights a rejection of divine guidance and wisdom. In the biblical context, "instruction" often refers to the Torah or God's law, which was given to guide the Israelites in righteousness. The hatred of God's instruction suggests a willful disobedience and rebellion against His commandments. This attitude is reminiscent of the Israelites' frequent turning away from God throughout the Old Testament, as seen in the wilderness wanderings (Exodus 32) and during the period of the Judges (Judges 2:11-19). The rejection of divine instruction is a theme that echoes in the prophetic writings, where prophets like Jeremiah and Isaiah lament the people's refusal to heed God's word (Jeremiah 6:10, Isaiah 30:9).

and cast My words behind you
This imagery of casting words behind oneself signifies a deliberate disregard and dismissal of God's commands. In ancient Near Eastern culture, to throw something behind you was to treat it as worthless or irrelevant. This act of casting away God's words can be seen as a metaphor for the spiritual state of Israel when they ignored the covenant and pursued idolatry (2 Kings 17:15). The phrase also connects to the New Testament, where Jesus criticizes the Pharisees and teachers of the law for nullifying God's word for the sake of tradition (Mark 7:9). This rejection of God's word is a stark contrast to the call for believers to treasure and meditate on His word, as seen in Psalm 119:11. The casting away of God's words can also be seen as a type of Christ's rejection by the people, as He is the Word made flesh (John 1:14), and was despised and rejected by men (Isaiah 53:3).

Teaching Points
The Importance of Obedience
True worship involves not just rituals but a heart that seeks to obey God's commands. Reflect on areas in your life where you might be ignoring God's instructions.

The Danger of Hypocrisy
God desires sincerity in our relationship with Him. Evaluate whether your outward expressions of faith align with your inner commitment to His word.

The Consequences of Disregard
Ignoring God's words leads to spiritual stagnation and judgment. Consider the long-term effects of neglecting God's guidance in your life.

The Call to Repentance
God’s rebuke is an invitation to return to Him. Seek forgiveness and make a conscious effort to align your actions with His teachings.

The Role of Scripture in Daily Life
Regular engagement with the Bible is crucial for spiritual growth. Develop a habit of studying and applying God's word consistently.

What Does Psalm 50:17 Mean
For

Psalm 50:17 opens with the little word “For,” linking God’s charge to what He has just said in verse 16: “But to the wicked God says…”.

• The “For” signals a courtroom explanation—God is laying out evidence for why His rebuke is justified (Psalm 50:4–6).

• It reminds us that outward religious activity without sincere obedience is unacceptable (1 Samuel 15:22; Isaiah 29:13; Matthew 15:8–9).

• By introducing the accusations that follow, the verse makes clear God’s standard is not pagan morality but His own revealed Word (Deuteronomy 4:1–2).

you hate My instruction

God now states the first count: detesting His instruction (literally His “discipline” or “Torah”).

• Hatred here is willful rejection, not mere indifference (Proverbs 1:29–30; Jeremiah 44:16).

• The people love sacrifices (Psalm 50:8) but bristle at correction, proving their hearts are still in rebellion (Zechariah 7:11–12).

Practical implications:

– When we dismiss passages that confront our sin, we imitate this hatred (2 Timothy 4:3–4).

– True delight in God always treasures His commands, even the hard ones (Psalm 119:97; John 14:15).

and cast My words behind you

The second charge pictures someone flinging God’s words over the shoulder, then walking away.

• It conveys total disregard—treating the Word as refuse (Nehemiah 9:26; Ezekiel 20:13).

• Disobedience follows quickly: verse 18 shows theft and adultery, the natural fruit when Scripture is discarded (Romans 1:28–32).

Every believer can guard against this pattern by:

– Receiving the Word with meekness (James 1:21).

– Remembering that forgetting God’s words leads to forgetting God Himself (Deuteronomy 8:19–20).

– Putting truth into practice so it does not slip away (James 1:22–25; Luke 6:46–49).

summary

Psalm 50:17 exposes a heart posture that looks religious but inwardly rejects God’s authority. Hating His instruction and tossing His words aside invite divine rebuke. The antidote is humble submission: welcoming Scripture, obeying it, and letting it shape every part of life. When God’s people treasure His Word, their worship becomes pleasing, and their lives display the reverence He desires.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 17. - Seeing thou hatest instruction (comp. Proverbs 1:25, 29). God, by his Law, teaches men their duties; but many men "hate" to be instructed. And castest my words behind thee (comp. 1 Kings 14:9; Nehemiah 9:26). They proceed from "inward alienation" to "open rejection" of the moral law.
