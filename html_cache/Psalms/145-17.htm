The LORD is righteous in all His ways
This phrase emphasizes the absolute moral perfection and justice of God. In the Hebrew context, "righteous" (צַדִּיק, tzaddik) conveys the idea of being just, lawful, and morally right. Throughout the Old Testament, God's righteousness is a central theme, as seen in Deuteronomy 32:4, which describes God as a "God of faithfulness and without iniquity, just and upright is He." The righteousness of God is foundational to His character and is reflected in His laws and commandments given to Israel. This righteousness is also a standard for human conduct, as seen in the call for believers to pursue righteousness in their own lives (Proverbs 21:21). Theologically, God's righteousness is fully revealed in the person of Jesus Christ, who embodies divine justice and offers His righteousness to believers through faith (Romans 3:22).

and kind in all His deeds
The kindness of God, often translated as "lovingkindness" or "mercy" (חֶסֶד, chesed), is a recurring theme in the Psalms and throughout Scripture. This attribute highlights God's benevolent and compassionate nature. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, chesed was a covenantal term, reflecting God's steadfast love and faithfulness to His people. This kindness is evident in God's actions throughout biblical history, from His deliverance of Israel from Egypt to His provision and protection in the wilderness. The New Testament continues this theme, with Jesus Christ exemplifying God's kindness through His ministry, healing, and ultimate sacrifice on the cross (Titus 3:4-5). The kindness of God invites believers to trust in His goodness and to reflect His character in their interactions with others (Ephesians 4:32).

Teaching Points
Understanding God's Righteousness
God's righteousness is inherent in His nature, meaning He always acts in accordance with what is right and just.

Believers are called to trust in God's righteous ways, even when circumstances are challenging or unclear.

Experiencing God's Kindness
God's kindness is evident in His deeds, which are motivated by love and compassion for His creation.

Christians are encouraged to reflect God's kindness in their interactions with others, serving as a testament to His character.

Living in Response to God's Character
As followers of Christ, we are to emulate God's righteousness and kindness in our daily lives.

This involves making ethical decisions, showing mercy, and extending grace to others, as God has done for us.

The Assurance of God's Consistent Character
God's character does not change; He is consistently righteous and kind, providing a firm foundation for our faith.

This assurance allows believers to approach God with confidence, knowing He is just and merciful.

Reflecting on God's Deeds
Regularly recounting and meditating on God's deeds helps believers to remain grounded in His truth and character.

This practice strengthens faith and encourages a deeper relationship with God.

What Does Psalm 145:17 Mean
The LORD

• The verse begins by naming the covenant God—“The LORD”—the personal, faithful Creator who revealed Himself to Moses as “I AM.”

• Because His character never changes (Malachi 3:6), every statement that follows rests on the certainty of who He is.

• Other psalms echo this focus on His name: “The LORD is my light and my salvation” (Psalm 27:1). When David speaks of God, he is anchoring us in a relationship, not an abstract force.

is righteous

• Righteousness means God always does what is morally perfect and just. There is never a blemish or inconsistency in Him.

• Scripture reinforces this truth: “He is the Rock, His work is perfect; all His ways are just” (Deuteronomy 32:4).

• Because His righteousness is absolute, we can trust His judgments, laws, and promises without fear of arbitrariness (Psalm 19:9).

in all His ways

• “All” sweeps in every action, plan, and command of God—nothing is excluded.

• Whether in creation (Genesis 1 repeatedly states, “God saw that it was good”) or in salvation history (Romans 8:28 assures us He works “all things together for good”), His ways display flawless integrity.

• Even when His paths confuse us, they remain righteous: “Your way was through the sea… yet Your footprints were unseen” (Psalm 77:19). The hiddenness of His methods never negates their perfection.

and kind

• God’s righteousness is never cold or detached; it is warmly expressed through kindness.

• Psalm 103:8 mirrors this balance: “The LORD is compassionate and gracious, slow to anger, abounding in loving devotion”.

• His kindness flows to the undeserving, highlighted in Matthew 5:45 where He “causes His sun to rise on the evil and the good.” Justice and mercy meet perfectly in Him.

in all His deeds

• Every deed—whether mighty acts of deliverance or quiet daily provisions—carries the stamp of His kindness.

• Lamentations 3:22–23 reminds us, “Because of the loving devotion of the LORD we are not consumed… His mercies never fail; they are new every morning”.

• From the cross of Christ (Romans 5:8) to the smallest answered prayer (Philippians 4:19), His actions consistently manifest benevolence.

summary

Psalm 145:17 paints a comprehensive portrait of God: the covenant LORD whose unchanging righteousness governs every path He takes, and whose kind heart colors every deed He performs. We can rely on Him completely—His justice will never fail us, and His kindness will never run dry.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 17. - The Lord is righteous in all his ways, and holy (rather, gracious, or merciful) in all his works. Mercy and truth meet in God (Psalm 85:10). He is at once perfectly just, and absolutely tender and compassionate. "All his works" experience both his justice and his tenderness (comp. Psalm 25:8; Psalm 116:5, etc.).
