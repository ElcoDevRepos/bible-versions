May his name endure forever;
This phrase emphasizes the eternal nature of the king's name, which in biblical terms often signifies his reputation, authority, and legacy. In the context of Psalm 72, traditionally attributed to Solomon, it reflects the hope for a lasting dynasty. This echoes God's covenant with David in 2 Samuel 7:16, where God promises that David's house and kingdom will endure forever. Theologically, this points to the Messianic expectation fulfilled in Jesus Christ, whose kingdom is eternal (Luke 1:32-33). The enduring name also signifies the unchanging nature of God's promises and His faithfulness throughout generations.

may his name continue as long as the sun shines.
This phrase uses the imagery of the sun, a constant and enduring element in creation, to illustrate the perpetual nature of the king's reign. The sun is often used in Scripture to symbolize constancy and reliability (Psalm 89:36-37). In ancient Near Eastern culture, the sun was a symbol of life and sustenance, reinforcing the idea of a kingdom that provides and endures. This also foreshadows the eternal reign of Christ, the "Sun of Righteousness" (Malachi 4:2), whose kingdom will have no end.

In him may all nations be blessed;
This phrase recalls the Abrahamic covenant in Genesis 12:3, where God promises that all nations will be blessed through Abraham's offspring. It highlights the universal scope of God's plan for salvation, extending beyond Israel to all peoples. This is fulfilled in Jesus Christ, through whom the blessing of salvation is offered to all nations (Galatians 3:8, 14). The phrase underscores the inclusivity of the Gospel and the mission of the Church to spread this blessing worldwide (Matthew 28:19).

may they call him blessed.
The final phrase suggests a response of praise and recognition from the nations. To "call him blessed" is to acknowledge the king's role in bringing about God's blessing. In a broader sense, it points to the worship and adoration due to Christ, who is the ultimate source of blessing. This reflects the eschatological vision in Revelation 7:9-10, where people from every nation, tribe, and language worship the Lamb. It emphasizes the rightful response to God's anointed king, culminating in the universal acknowledgment of Jesus as Lord (Philippians 2:10-11).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Solomon
Traditionally attributed to Solomon, this psalm is a royal psalm that prays for the king's reign and prosperity. It reflects the ideal of a righteous and just ruler.

2. David
Solomon's father, King David, is often associated with the promises of a lasting dynasty, which this psalm echoes.

3. Nations
The psalm speaks of all nations being blessed through the king, which points to a universal scope beyond Israel.

4. The Sun
Used metaphorically to signify eternity and the enduring nature of the king's name and influence.

5. Messiah
While originally about Solomon, Christians see a prophetic reference to Jesus Christ, the ultimate fulfillment of the Davidic covenant.
Teaching Points
The Enduring Name of Christ
The name of Jesus is eternal and will be praised as long as the sun shines. Believers are called to honor and proclaim His name in their daily lives.

Universal Blessing through Christ
Just as the psalm speaks of all nations being blessed, Christians are reminded of their mission to share the gospel, bringing the blessing of salvation to all people.

The Fulfillment of God's Promises
God's promises to David and Abraham find their ultimate fulfillment in Jesus. Believers can trust in God's faithfulness to His promises.

Living as a Blessing to Others
As followers of Christ, we are called to be a blessing to others, reflecting the character and love of Jesus in our interactions.

What Does Psalm 72:17 Mean
May his name endure forever

“May his name endure forever” (Psalm 72:17a) points to a name that never fades, never weakens, and never falls out of honor. Scripture consistently treats the Lord’s name as inseparable from His character and authority.

• Exodus 3:15 states, “This is My name forever, and this is how I am to be remembered in every generation.” That same divine permanence lies behind the psalmist’s words.

• Isaiah 9:6 declares titles—“Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace”—that reach beyond any earthly ruler.

• Philippians 2:9 affirms that God “gave Him the name above all names,” underscoring eternal exaltation.

Because God’s promises are sure, the endurance of the Messiah’s name is not wishful thinking; it is guaranteed reality rooted in God’s own faithfulness.

may his name continue as long as the sun shines

The second phrase, “may his name continue as long as the sun shines” (72:17b), reinforces the first with a vivid image of unbroken daylight.

• Psalm 89:36-37 parallels this, saying David’s lineage “will endure forever… like the sun before Me.”

• Malachi 1:11 proclaims, “My name will be great among the nations, from where the sun rises to where it sets.”

• Revelation 1:8 records Jesus’ words: “I am the Alpha and the Omega… who is, and was, and is to come.”

The rising and setting of the sun are constants in human experience; attaching the Messiah’s name to that cycle drives home His ceaseless reign. What the sun is to the earth—dependable, life-sustaining—Christ’s name is to all creation.

In him may all nations be blessed

“In him may all nations be blessed” (72:17c) recalls God’s covenant with Abraham.

• Genesis 22:18 promises, “In your seed all nations of the earth shall be blessed.” Psalm 72 identifies that Seed with the coming King.

• Acts 3:25-26 explains that God sent Jesus “to bless you by turning each of you from your wicked ways,” extending the blessing first to Israel, then through them to the world.

• Galatians 3:8 says, “Scripture foresaw that God would justify the Gentiles by faith, and announced the gospel in advance to Abraham: ‘All nations will be blessed through you.’”

The blessing is comprehensive—spiritual restoration, forgiveness, peace with God—and it is offered to “all nations,” leaving no ethnic or cultural boundary outside Christ’s saving reach.

may they call him blessed

Finally, “may they call him blessed” (72:17d) envisions a global chorus acknowledging the King’s goodness.

• Psalm 145:1-2 models this kind of praise: “I will exalt You, my God and King, and bless Your name forever and ever.”

• Luke 1:48 records Mary’s declaration, “From now on all generations will call me blessed,” anticipating the same worldwide acknowledgment of God’s favor.

• Philippians 2:10-11 anticipates the day “every knee should bow… and every tongue confess that Jesus Christ is Lord.”

When nations “call Him blessed,” they willingly testify to the grace they have received. Praise completes the blessing cycle: God blesses His people; His people bless His name.

summary

Psalm 72:17 layers four petitions that ultimately converge on Jesus Christ. His name is everlasting, shining like the sun across all ages; His work brings blessing to every nation; and the rightful response of a redeemed world is to declare Him blessed forever.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(17) Shall be continued.--Rather, have issue. Literally, send out new shoots.

As long as the sun.--See Note on Psalm 72:5.

Shall be blessed in him.--Or, bless themselves in him. The meaning is clear, though the Hebrew is rather vague. The monarch will himself be a source of blessing to his people, who will never tire of blessing him. The psalmist's prayer finds a genuine echo in the noble dedication of In Memoriam:

"May you rule us long,

And leave us rulers of your blood

As noble, till the latest day!

May children of our children say,

'She wrought her people lasting good.' "

For the doxology closing the second book, and for the note apparently appended by the collector of this book, "the prayers of David the son of Jesse are ended," see General Introduction.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 17. - His Name shall endure forever (comp. Psalm 45:2, 6; Psalm 102:12; Isaiah 9:7). "The eternity of the Name is based upon the eternity of the kingdom" (Hengstenberg). His Name shall be continued as long as the sun (comp. ver. 5); or, his Name shall be renewed - shall spring again to fresh life. Dr. Kay compares an expression of Renan's, "Son culte se rajeunira." And men shall be blessed in him; literally, men shall bless themselves in him (comp. Genesis 22:18; Genesis 26:4). All nations shall call him blessed. With these words the psalm, properly speaking, ends. The doxology (vers. 18, 19) and the note (ver. 20) were probably appended by the arranger of the book.
