Let them praise the name of the LORD
This phrase calls all creation to worship God, emphasizing the importance of acknowledging His sovereignty and majesty. The "name of the LORD" signifies His character and authority. In biblical times, a name was more than a label; it represented the essence of a person. The call to praise is universal, extending to all beings, reflecting the theme of creation's purpose found in Genesis 1. The Psalms frequently emphasize the importance of praising God (e.g., Psalm 150), and this verse aligns with the broader biblical narrative that all creation exists to glorify God (Isaiah 43:7).

for He gave the command
This highlights God's omnipotence and the power of His word. The act of creation by divine command is a central theme in Genesis 1, where God speaks the universe into existence. This underscores the concept of God as the ultimate authority and creator, whose will is enacted through His word. The phrase also connects to the New Testament, where Jesus is described as the Word (John 1:1-3), indicating the divine power and authority inherent in God's commands.

and they were created
This phrase affirms the immediate and effective nature of God's creative power. It reflects the biblical teaching that creation is not a result of chance but a deliberate act of God. The passive construction "were created" emphasizes God's sole agency in creation, aligning with passages like Colossians 1:16, which states that all things were created through and for Christ. This also points to the idea of creation ex nihilo, meaning "out of nothing," a doctrine that underscores God's unique ability to bring forth existence from non-existence.

Teaching Points
The Authority of God's Command
God's word is powerful and authoritative, bringing creation into existence. This underscores the importance of trusting in His commands and promises in our lives.

The Purpose of Creation
All creation exists to praise and glorify God. As part of His creation, our lives should reflect worship and gratitude towards Him.

The Universality of Worship
Every part of creation, from the heavens to the earth, is called to worship God. This reminds us of the communal and universal nature of worship, transcending individual and cultural boundaries.

The Sovereignty of God
God's ability to create by command demonstrates His sovereignty over all things. Recognizing His control can bring peace and assurance in times of uncertainty.

Living in Awe of the Creator
Reflecting on the majesty and complexity of creation should lead us to a deeper awe and reverence for God, inspiring us to live in a way that honors Him.

What Does Psalm 148:5 Mean
Let them praise

- The psalmist’s invitation stretches to every corner of creation already named in Psalm 148—angels, heavenly bodies, earth, mountains, animals, kings, and children. Nothing is exempt.

- Praise is not optional; it is the natural and rightful response to God’s glory. “Let everything that has breath praise the LORD” (Psalm 150:6).

- Even inanimate creation will not stay silent. Jesus said, “If they remain silent, the very stones will cry out” (Luke 19:40).

- Revelation pictures the same universal chorus: “I heard every creature… saying: ‘To Him who sits on the throne and to the Lamb be praise… forever’” (Revelation 5:13).

the name of the LORD

- In Scripture, God’s “name” embodies His character, reputation, and authority. “O LORD, our Lord, how majestic is Your name in all the earth!” (Psalm 8:1).

- When God revealed Himself to Moses, He “proclaimed His name, the LORD… compassionate and gracious” (Exodus 34:5-7). Creation praises because it reflects that character—power, wisdom, faithfulness.

- His name is also a refuge: “The name of the LORD is a strong tower; the righteous run to it and are safe” (Proverbs 18:10). Praising His name is both acknowledgment and trust.

for He gave the command

- The reason for praise is rooted in God’s authoritative word. In Genesis, He simply speaks: “And God said, ‘Let there be light,’ and there was light” (Genesis 1:3).

- Psalm 33:9 reinforces the idea: “For He spoke, and it came to be; He commanded, and it stood firm”.

- God’s word is never empty: “My word… will accomplish what I please” (Isaiah 55:11). Creation itself is the first evidence of that reliability.

and they were created

- The immediate obedience of creation to God’s command highlights His unmatched sovereignty. “In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth” (Genesis 1:1).

- The New Testament affirms the same truth in Christ: “All things were created through Him and for Him” (Colossians 1:16).

- Hebrews points to the foundation of faith: “By faith we understand that the universe was formed at God’s command” (Hebrews 11:3). Every molecule and galaxy exists because He willed it so.

summary

Psalm 148:5 calls all creation to celebrate God because He alone spoke reality into being. His name reflects perfect character, His command wields absolute authority, and the resulting universe stands as an enduring witness. Praising Him is not merely a duty; it is the fitting echo of creation back to its Creator.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 5. - Let them praise the Name of the Lord. Frequent changes of person, when no doubt can arise as to the meaning, are a sort of Hebrew idiom. They give liveliness and variety. For he commanded, and they were created (Genesis 1:3, 6, 9, 11, 14, 15, etc.). All creation sprang into being at the word of God (Psalm 33:8).
