Oh, how I love Your law!
The psalmist expresses a deep affection for God's law, which refers to the Torah, the first five books of the Old Testament. This love is not merely emotional but is rooted in a recognition of the law's divine origin and its role in guiding righteous living. The Torah was central to Jewish life and worship, serving as the foundation for moral and spiritual conduct. The psalmist's declaration echoes the sentiment found in Psalm 1:2, where the blessed man delights in the law of the Lord. This love for the law is a reflection of a heart aligned with God's will, as seen in Deuteronomy 6:5, which commands love for God with all one's heart, soul, and strength. The law is not seen as a burden but as a source of joy and wisdom, pointing to the character of God and His covenant with His people.

All day long it is my meditation.
The practice of meditating on God's law throughout the day indicates a continuous and intentional focus on His word. Meditation in the biblical sense involves deep reflection and contemplation, allowing the truths of Scripture to permeate one's thoughts and actions. This practice is encouraged in Joshua 1:8, where God instructs Joshua to meditate on the Book of the Law day and night to ensure success and prosperity. The psalmist's commitment to meditation signifies a life centered around God's word, influencing decisions and behavior. This constant engagement with Scripture is a means of spiritual growth and transformation, aligning the believer's mind with God's purposes. The emphasis on "all day long" suggests an ongoing relationship with God, where His word is a constant companion and guide, much like the presence of Christ in the believer's life as the living Word (John 1:1).

Love for God's Word
Cultivate a genuine love for Scripture by spending time in it daily. This love will naturally lead to a desire to meditate on it.

Meditation as a Discipline
Make meditation on God's word a regular part of your daily routine. This discipline will help you internalize God's teachings and apply them to your life.

Transformation through Meditation
Allow the truths of Scripture to transform your mind and heart. As you meditate, ask the Holy Spirit to reveal new insights and applications.

Consistency in Meditation
Strive for consistency in your meditation practice. Even short, focused times of reflection can have a profound impact on your spiritual growth.

Integration into Daily Life
Look for ways to integrate the principles of God's law into your daily decisions and interactions. This will help you live out your faith in practical ways.

What Does Psalm 119:97 Mean
Oh, how I love Your law!

“​Oh, how I love Your law!”

• The psalmist bursts out with affection, not for an abstract idea, but for God’s revealed commands. He treats Scripture as living truth that deserves wholehearted love, just as Psalm 19:10 says the law is “sweeter than honey.”

• Love for God and love for His Word are inseparable (John 14:15; 1 John 5:3). Embracing the commands shows devotion to the Commander.

• This delight is not selective; it covers the whole counsel of God. Like Joshua 1:8, the believer is called to embrace every statute, precept, and ordinance as trustworthy and good.

• Why such passion? Because God’s law exposes sin (Psalm 119:11), preserves life (Psalm 119:93), and points to the Savior (Galatians 3:24). The psalmist loves the law because through it he meets the Lawgiver.

• Loving Scripture guards against the pull of the world. When the affections are fixed on God’s Word, competing loves lose their grip (Psalm 119:165).

All day long it is my meditation.

“All day long it is my meditation.”

• Meditation here is active engagement—turning God’s words over in the mind, letting them shape perspective and choices (Psalm 1:2).

• “All day long” shows constancy. The truth is not quarantined to morning devotions; it saturates work, conversation, and rest. This echoes Deuteronomy 6:6-7, where parents are told to speak of the commands “when you sit in your house and when you walk along the road.”

• Continuous meditation yields wisdom and discernment (Psalm 119:98-100). When decisions arise, Scripture is already on the tip of the tongue.

• Practical ways to keep God’s Word before you:

– Memorize short passages and recite them while driving or waiting in line.

– Write a verse on a card and glance at it during breaks.

– Turn verses into prayer for people and situations you meet throughout the day.

• Philippians 4:8 calls believers to dwell on whatever is true and honorable; nothing fits that description better than God’s own words.

• Colossians 3:16 urges, “Let the word of Christ dwell in you richly.” Meditation moves the text from the page to the heart, where it can govern attitudes and actions.

summary

Psalm 119:97 models a life where love for God’s Word fuels unceasing reflection on it. The believer treasures every command as a gift from a trustworthy Father and keeps those commands in constant view, allowing them to guide, protect, and satisfy the soul from morning to night.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 97. - O how love I Thy law! (comp. vers. 47, 48, 113, 119, 127, etc.). As the psalmist goes on with his meditation he becomes more and more rated with a deep love of the Law of God, which is not to him a restraint or a burden, but a solace and a "delight." It is my meditation all the day (see ver. 15, 23, 48, 78, etc.).
