I delight in Your commandments
The psalmist expresses a deep joy and satisfaction in God's commandments, reflecting a heart that finds pleasure in divine law. This delight is not merely an obligation but a genuine affection for God's instructions. In the historical context of ancient Israel, the Law was central to the community's identity and relationship with God. The commandments were seen as a guide to living a life that was pleasing to God and beneficial to the community. This delight can be compared to Psalm 1:2, where the righteous person meditates on the law day and night, indicating a continuous engagement and enjoyment of God's word. The psalmist's delight also prefigures the attitude of Jesus Christ, who perfectly fulfilled the law and found joy in doing the Father's will (John 4:34).

because I love them
The psalmist's love for the commandments is the motivation behind his delight. This love is not superficial but rooted in a deep understanding of the value and purpose of God's laws. In the cultural context of the time, love for God's law was synonymous with loyalty and commitment to the covenant relationship with God. This love is echoed in the Shema (Deuteronomy 6:4-5), where the Israelites are commanded to love the Lord with all their heart, soul, and strength, which includes adherence to His commandments. The love for God's commandments is also a reflection of the love for God Himself, as Jesus stated in John 14:15, "If you love Me, you will keep My commandments." This connection between love and obedience is a recurring theme throughout Scripture, emphasizing that true love for God is demonstrated through obedience to His word.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Psalmist
The author of Psalm 119, traditionally believed to be King David, who expresses a deep love and commitment to God's law.

2. God
The divine author of the commandments, whose laws are the focus of the psalmist's delight and love.

3. Israel
The nation to whom the commandments were originally given, serving as a historical context for the psalmist's devotion.

4. Mount Sinai
The place where God gave the commandments to Moses, representing the origin of the laws the psalmist loves.

5. The Law (Torah)
The body of commandments and teachings given by God, which the psalmist finds joy in following.
Teaching Points
Delight in God's Word
The psalmist's joy in God's commandments challenges us to find pleasure in reading and meditating on Scripture.

Love as Motivation
Our obedience to God's commandments should stem from love, not obligation, reflecting a heart transformed by His grace.

Internalization of God's Law
Like the psalmist, we should internalize God's commandments, allowing them to guide our thoughts and actions.

Consistency in Devotion
Regular engagement with God's Word fosters a deeper love and understanding, leading to a more consistent walk with Him.

Joyful Obedience
Embrace the joy that comes from living according to God's ways, recognizing that His commandments are designed for our good.

What Does Psalm 119:47 Mean
I delight

• The psalmist’s first word is all about emotion—“delight.” This is not reluctant obedience but joyful celebration, echoing Psalm 1:2, where “his delight is in the law of the LORD.”

• Delight arises from recognizing that God’s Word is a source of life and refreshment (Psalm 19:8; Nehemiah 8:10).

• When we delight in something, we naturally return to it again and again; Philippians 4:4 reminds believers to “Rejoice in the Lord always,” linking delight in God with steady, constant joy.

in Your commandments

• The focus shifts from the psalmist’s feeling to God’s objective revelation—His commandments. Unlike human rules, these come from a perfect, loving Father (Psalm 19:7–11).

• God’s commands show His character and guard our steps (Psalm 119:105). They are “not burdensome” (1 John 5:3) but freeing, because they steer us away from sin’s slavery (John 8:31-32).

• Jesus affirmed this same posture: “If you love Me, you will keep My commandments” (John 14:15), so delight in Scripture is inseparable from allegiance to Christ.

• By treasuring the commandments, we affirm 2 Timothy 3:16—that every word is “God-breathed” and profitable for us today.

because I love them

• Love is the motive behind delight and obedience. Psalm 119:97 cries, “Oh, how I love Your law!”—the same heartbeat heard here.

• This love is relational, springing from Deuteronomy 6:5’s call to love the LORD with all heart, soul, and strength, fulfilled and reaffirmed by Jesus in Matthew 22:37.

• Love transforms duty into devotion; Romans 7:22 says, “In my inner being I delight in God’s law,” showing that redeemed hearts cherish what once condemned them.

• Such love is empowered by the Spirit, who writes God’s law on our hearts (Jeremiah 31:33) so that obedience flows from affection, not mere obligation.

summary

Psalm 119:47 blends feeling, focus, and motive: the psalmist delights (feeling) in God’s commandments (focus) because he loves them (motive). Delight shows Scripture is treasured, commandments affirm God’s authority, and love drives both joy and obedience. When those three strands weave together, believers experience the freedom, guidance, and intimacy God designed His Word to give.
