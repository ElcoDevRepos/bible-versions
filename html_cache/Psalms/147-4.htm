He determines the number of the stars;
This phrase highlights God's omniscience and omnipotence. In the ancient Near Eastern context, the stars were often associated with deities or seen as divine entities themselves. However, this verse emphasizes that God is the Creator and Sovereign over all celestial bodies, contrasting with pagan beliefs. The stars, innumerable to humans, are precisely counted by God, showcasing His infinite knowledge and control over the universe. This reflects Genesis 1:16, where God creates the stars, and Isaiah 40:26, which also speaks of God calling the stars by name, emphasizing His power and authority.

He calls them each by name.
Naming in the biblical context signifies authority and relationship. By naming the stars, God demonstrates His intimate knowledge and personal care for His creation. This act of naming is reminiscent of Adam naming the animals in Genesis 2:19-20, indicating dominion and stewardship. The personal nature of God is further highlighted, as He is not a distant creator but one who is involved with His creation. This can be seen as a type of Christ, who knows His sheep by name (John 10:3), indicating a personal relationship with His followers. The verse reassures believers of God's meticulous care and attention to detail, extending from the vast cosmos to individual lives.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty and Omniscience
God's ability to determine the number of stars and call them by name demonstrates His absolute sovereignty and omniscience. This should lead us to trust in His control over the universe and our lives.

The Intimacy of God's Knowledge
Just as God knows each star by name, He knows each of us personally and intimately. This should encourage us to seek a deeper relationship with Him, knowing that we are valued and known.

The Majesty of Creation
Reflecting on the vastness of the stars can lead us to worship and awe of God's majesty. It reminds us of our place in creation and the greatness of the Creator.

Encouragement in Times of Doubt
When we feel insignificant or forgotten, remembering that God knows each star by name can reassure us of His attention and care for us.

What Does Psalm 147:4 Mean
He determines the number of the stars

• The psalmist points first to God’s sovereign act of counting the celestial bodies. Nothing in the universe is random or outside His calculation (Genesis 1:16; Job 38:31-33).

• He is not estimating; He “determines,” setting an exact limit. This underscores both His omniscience and His omnipotence—real, measurable facts, not poetic exaggeration (Isaiah 40:26: “Lift up your eyes… He brings out the starry host one by one and calls each by name. Because of His great power and mighty strength, not one of them is missing”).

• Knowing that God literally keeps track of every star assures us He also keeps track of every detail in our lives (Matthew 6:26-30; Psalm 139:1-4).

• For believers, this truth anchors faith: the Creator who set the cosmos in order also ordains personal circumstances (Proverbs 16:9; Romans 8:28).

He calls them each by name

• Naming in Scripture signifies ownership and intimate knowledge (Genesis 2:19-20; Revelation 2:17). By naming every star, God declares personal authority over the whole universe.

• The same care He extends to the vast sky He extends to us. Jesus said, “He calls His own sheep by name” (John 10:3), connecting cosmic care with personal relationship.

• God’s individual naming highlights His limitless capacity: billions of stars, yet none are anonymous. Likewise, “even the hairs of your head are all numbered” (Luke 12:7).

• The thought that no star is forgotten encourages believers that no prayer, tear, or quiet act of faith escapes His notice (Psalm 56:8; 1 Peter 5:7).

summary

Psalm 147:4 presents two simple, literal facts that reveal profound truth: God counts every star and assigns each one a name. These acts display His absolute sovereignty, perfect knowledge, and personal care. If the Lord governs the unfathomable heavens with such precision and attention, we can rest confident that He governs our lives with the same faithful, intimate oversight.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(4) Stars.--This proof of God's power to help, by reference to the stars of heaven, which are beyond man's power to count, much more to name, but which the Almighty both numbers and names, seems rather abruptly introduced, but the train of thought is clear. To assemble the dispersed of Israel, however numerous and scattered, was easy to the ruler of the hosts of heaven. The original promise to Abraham was, of course, in the poet's mind, but still more Isaiah 40:26-28, from which the expression may have been taken. The dramatic "Lift your eyes on high and behold" supplies the link needed in the abrupt entrance of the thought of the psalm.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 4. - He telleth the number of the stars. Nothing escapes God's knowledge. He knew the number of the exiles, and the place and name of each, just as he knows the number of the stars and their names (comp. Isaiah 40:26). He calleth them all by their names (see Job 9:9; Isaiah l.s.c.).
