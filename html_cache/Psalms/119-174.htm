I long for Your salvation, O LORD
This phrase expresses a deep yearning for deliverance and redemption, a common theme throughout the Psalms. The psalmist's longing for salvation reflects a recognition of human insufficiency and a dependence on divine intervention. In the Old Testament context, salvation often referred to deliverance from physical enemies or distress, but it also pointed to spiritual salvation. The use of "O LORD" invokes the covenant name of God, Yahweh, emphasizing a personal relationship and trust in God's promises. This longing can be seen as a foreshadowing of the ultimate salvation through Jesus Christ, who is often referred to as the fulfillment of God's redemptive plan (Luke 2:30-32).

and Your law is my delight
The psalmist finds joy and satisfaction in God's law, which refers to the Torah or the first five books of the Bible. This delight in the law underscores a love for God's instructions and commandments, which are seen as life-giving and wise (Psalm 19:7-11). In the historical and cultural context of ancient Israel, the law was central to religious and communal life, guiding moral conduct and worship. The delight in the law also highlights a contrast with those who reject or ignore God's commandments. This phrase connects to the New Testament where Jesus affirms the law's value and fulfills it (Matthew 5:17-18). The psalmist's delight can be seen as a type of Christ's perfect obedience and love for God's will.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Psalmist
The author of Psalm 119, traditionally believed to be King David, who expresses a deep longing for God's salvation and a love for His law.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal and unchanging nature, and His relationship with His people.

3. Salvation
In the context of the Old Testament, salvation often refers to deliverance from enemies or distress, but it also points to the ultimate salvation found in the Messiah.

4. The Law (Torah)
Refers to God's instructions and commandments given to His people, which are a source of guidance and joy for the believer.

5. The Hebrew People
The original audience of the Psalms, who were called to live according to God's law and anticipate His salvation.
Teaching Points
Longing for Salvation
The psalmist's deep yearning for God's salvation is a model for believers to earnestly desire spiritual deliverance and redemption. This longing should drive us to seek a closer relationship with God and trust in His promises.

Delight in God's Law
The law is not merely a set of rules but a source of joy and guidance. As believers, we should find delight in God's Word, allowing it to shape our lives and bring us closer to Him.

The Role of the Law in the Believer's Life
While the law cannot save us, it reveals God's character and His standards for holy living. It points us to our need for a Savior and guides us in righteous living.

Anticipation of Christ's Fulfillment
The psalmist's longing for salvation finds its ultimate fulfillment in Jesus Christ. As Christians, we live in the reality of this fulfilled promise and should continually look to Christ as the source of our salvation.

What Does Psalm 119:174 Mean
I long for Your salvation, O LORD

• The psalmist’s heart is stretched toward God’s rescue. “I long” pictures steady yearning rather than a passing wish (cf. Psalm 119:166, “I wait for Your salvation, O LORD”).

• “Salvation” here embraces every form of deliverance God provides—spiritual, physical, present, and future. David had tasted many temporal rescues (Psalm 34:4), yet he still looked ahead to ultimate redemption (Isaiah 25:9; Titus 2:13).

• This longing rests on certainty, not uncertainty. Psalm 62:1 declares, “In God alone my soul finds rest; my salvation comes from Him.” Confidence in God’s promised rescue fuels patience during trials (Romans 8:23–25).

• Personal address—“O LORD” (YHWH)—shows that salvation is relational. The psalmist doesn’t crave an abstract escape but the saving presence of the covenant-keeping God (Exodus 3:15; John 17:3).

and Your law is my delight

• Delight is the Bible’s way of describing wholehearted pleasure. It is not grudging duty; it is joyful absorption (Psalm 1:2, “his delight is in the law of the LORD”).

• Calling God’s law “my delight” means Scripture is treasured for what it is—God’s flawless, life-giving instruction (Psalm 19:7–11).

• Notice the pairing: longing for salvation isn’t separated from loving God’s commands. True hope in God’s deliverance always walks alongside cheerful obedience (John 14:23; 1 John 5:3).

• Delighting in the law guards the heart during the wait for salvation. While circumstances may not yet change, God’s Word provides stability, wisdom, and joy (Psalm 119:92; Romans 15:4).

• The psalmist delights in “Your law,” highlighting a personal bond—these are the Lord’s words, flowing from His character. To cherish the law is to cherish the Lawgiver (Psalm 119:97; Romans 7:22).

summary

Psalm 119:174 unites yearning and enjoyment: we passionately await God’s full salvation while wholeheartedly delighting in His Word. The verse invites believers to let hopeful expectation and joyful obedience reinforce each other—resting in God’s promised rescue and reveling in the Scripture that sustains us until that day.
