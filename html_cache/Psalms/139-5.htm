You hem me in behind and before;
This phrase suggests God's comprehensive protection and guidance. The imagery of being "hemmed in" evokes the idea of being surrounded or enclosed, indicating God's omnipresence and omnipotence. In biblical times, cities were often fortified with walls for protection, and this metaphor reflects a similar sense of security provided by God. The concept of God being both "behind and before" emphasizes His eternal nature, as He exists outside of time, knowing the past, present, and future. This aligns with other scriptures such as Isaiah 52:12, which speaks of God going before and being the rear guard, and Deuteronomy 31:8, where God promises to go before His people. Theologically, this can be seen as a type of Christ, who is the Alpha and Omega, the beginning and the end (Revelation 22:13).

You have laid Your hand upon me.
The laying of God's hand signifies His personal involvement and blessing. In the cultural context of the Bible, the laying on of hands was a significant act, often associated with blessing, commissioning, or imparting authority, as seen in Genesis 48:14 when Jacob blesses Ephraim and Manasseh. This phrase indicates God's intimate care and guidance, suggesting a personal relationship with the believer. It also reflects God's sovereignty and control over one's life, as His hand directs and sustains. This can be connected to the New Testament, where Jesus lays hands on individuals to heal and bless them, demonstrating His divine authority and compassion (Mark 10:16). The hand of God is a recurring biblical motif representing power, provision, and protection, as seen in passages like Isaiah 41:10, where God reassures His people with His righteous right hand.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
- The author of Psalm 139, King David, is expressing his intimate relationship with God and his understanding of God's omnipresence and omniscience.

2. God
- The central figure in this verse, God is depicted as the one who surrounds and protects David, demonstrating His sovereignty and care.

3. Israel
- While not directly mentioned in this verse, the context of David's life and his role as king of Israel provides a backdrop for understanding the communal and personal implications of God's protection.
Teaching Points
God's Omnipresence and Protection
God's presence is not limited by time or space; He is always with us, providing protection and guidance.

Divine Sovereignty
The imagery of being "hemmed in" reflects God's control over our circumstances, reminding us that nothing happens outside His will.

Personal Relationship with God
David's acknowledgment of God's intimate involvement in his life encourages us to seek a deeper personal relationship with God.

Trust in God's Plan
Understanding that God has "laid His hand upon us" should inspire trust in His plan, even when we cannot see the full picture.

Comfort in God's Presence
The assurance of God's surrounding presence offers comfort and peace, especially in times of uncertainty or fear.

What Does Psalm 139:5 Mean
You hem me in

David begins with the image of being “hemmed in,” an expression of God’s protective enclosure (Psalm 139:5). Far from confinement that stifles, it is the sure shelter of a Father who places secure boundaries around His child.

• Compare Job 1:10, where Satan admits God has “put a hedge around” Job—divine security that even the enemy must respect.

• In Exodus 14:19-20 the Angel of God moves “behind” Israel, forming a barrier between them and Pharaoh’s army, a living illustration of being “hemmed in.”

• Isaiah 52:12 comforts exiles with the promise that “the LORD will go before you, and the God of Israel will be your rear guard,” reinforcing that the One who encircles us is utterly trustworthy.

Behind and before

David specifies the scope of God’s encirclement—“behind and before.” The phrase conveys total coverage in both space and time.

• God occupies the believer’s past, redeeming what lies “behind” (Romans 8:28; Joel 2:25).

• He stands in the future “before” us, preparing the way (Deuteronomy 31:8; John 14:3).

• Psalm 34:7 adds that “The angel of the LORD encamps around those who fear Him,” underscoring an all-directional guardianship.

For David, this truth transforms any unknown into safe territory, because the God who authored history also writes tomorrow’s pages.

You have laid Your hand upon me

To ancient readers, a king’s hand resting on someone meant favor, commissioning, and authority. Likewise, God’s hand on David signals:

• Personal ownership—Isaiah 49:16 affirms, “I have inscribed you on the palms of My hands.”

• Guidance—Psalm 23:2-3 describes the Shepherd leading His sheep, a hand that both directs and calms.

• Empowerment—Acts 11:21 reports, “the hand of the Lord was with them, and a great number believed,” showing the hand of God equips His people to fulfill His purposes.

The same hand that fashioned the universe (Isaiah 48:13) now rests gently on the believer, assuring intimate involvement in every detail.

summary

Psalm 139:5 paints a picture of absolute security: God surrounds us on every side and places His own hand upon us. The verse affirms His protective boundaries, His timeless presence in our past and future, and His personal, guiding touch. For those who trust Him, life is never adrift; it is safely enclosed within the loving, sovereign grip of the One who guards, guides, and empowers His children.
