Faithfulness sprouts from the earth
This phrase suggests a divine interaction where human faithfulness emerges as a response to God's grace. The imagery of "sprouting" evokes agricultural growth, symbolizing new life and renewal. In biblical context, the earth often represents humanity or the physical realm. This can be seen in Genesis 2:7, where God forms man from the dust of the ground. The idea of faithfulness emerging from the earth may also reflect the covenant relationship between God and His people, where human faithfulness is a response to divine faithfulness. The agricultural metaphor aligns with passages like Hosea 10:12, which calls for sowing righteousness and reaping steadfast love. This phrase can be seen as a type of Christ, who is the ultimate expression of faithfulness and truth, as He came to earth to fulfill God's promises.

and righteousness looks down from heaven
This part of the verse portrays righteousness as a divine attribute that originates from God. The imagery of "looking down" suggests oversight, care, and the bestowal of blessings from above. In the biblical narrative, heaven is often depicted as God's dwelling place, where His will and righteousness are perfectly established. This aligns with passages like Isaiah 45:8, which speaks of righteousness pouring down from the skies. The connection between heaven and earth in this verse reflects the biblical theme of God's kingdom coming to earth, as seen in the Lord's Prayer (Matthew 6:10). Righteousness looking down can also be seen as a prophetic anticipation of Christ, who embodies divine righteousness and brings it to humanity. This dual imagery of faithfulness and righteousness highlights the harmonious relationship between God's actions and human response, emphasizing the covenantal theme throughout Scripture.

Teaching Points
The Harmony of Truth and Righteousness
Truth and righteousness are depicted as working in harmony, with truth emerging from the earth and righteousness descending from heaven. This illustrates the divine partnership between God and humanity in the pursuit of holiness.

The Role of Believers in Manifesting Truth
As truth sprouts from the earth, believers are called to be vessels of truth in the world. This involves living out the truth of the Gospel in daily life and being a witness to others.

God's Sovereign Righteousness
Righteousness looking down from heaven signifies God's sovereign oversight and His desire to impart righteousness to His people. Believers can trust in God's righteous judgment and His provision of righteousness through Christ.

The Fulfillment in Christ
Jesus Christ embodies the fulfillment of this verse, as He is the truth that came to earth and the righteousness that comes from God. Believers find their ultimate hope and restoration in Him.

What Does Psalm 85:11 Mean
Faithfulness sprouts from the earth

• The picture is agricultural: seeds hidden in soil suddenly break through, visible and vibrant. In the Psalmist’s mind, that “seed” is covenant-keeping loyalty among God’s people.

• Verse 10 has just joined “faithfulness and truth” with “righteousness and peace” kissing; v.11 shows how that union becomes public. When redeemed people live out God’s Word, faithfulness is no longer theoretical—it erupts into everyday life.

• Cross references confirm this earthly emergence:

– Isaiah 45:8: “Rain down, O heavens, from above… let the earth open that salvation may sprout, and righteousness spring up with it.”

– Hosea 10:12: “Sow for yourselves righteousness and reap the fruit of steadfast love.”

– James 3:18: “Peacemakers who sow in peace reap the fruit of righteousness.”

• The line reminds us that true revival is not merely inward feeling but outward obedience. Fields of faithfulness—acts of honesty, mercy, purity—become proof that the Lord has indeed restored His people (Psalm 85:1-7).

and righteousness looks down from heaven

• The second half answers the first: righteous blessing descends before human faithfulness ascends. The initiative is God’s.

• “Looks down” is watchful, approving, and sustaining. The same verb appears in Psalm 113:6: He “stoops down to look on the heavens and the earth.” Heaven is not distant; God bends toward His renewed land.

• Cross references highlight this downward gaze:

– Psalm 85:12 continues, “The LORD will indeed provide what is good.”

– Psalm 33:13-14: “The LORD looks down from heaven; He sees all mankind… He watches all who live on the earth.”

– James 1:17: “Every good and perfect gift is from above, coming down from the Father of lights.”

• Ultimately, righteousness “looking down” culminates in Christ, the righteousness of God revealed from heaven (Romans 1:17). His incarnation brought heaven’s holiness to earth, guaranteeing the harvest of faithfulness among His people.

summary

Psalm 85:11 pictures a holy exchange: God’s righteousness descends, and His people’s faithfulness ascends. Heaven supplies, earth responds. When the Lord revives His own, visible obedience springs up in daily life, while His righteous favor continually rests upon that renewed ground.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(11) Truth, or "faithfulness," is here depicted as springing out of the earth, because the renewal of fertility has re-established the conviction of the faithfulness of Jehovah towards His people, which had been shaken.

Look down.--Used of bending forwards as from a window or battlement (Song of Solomon 6:10, Note).

This "righteousness" (here in direct parallelism with faithfulness) had, as it were, been hidden like the sun behind a cloud, but now is seen showing its benign face once more in the skies.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 11. - Truth shall spring out of the earth (comp. Isaiah 45:8). One result of the reconciliation of God's mercy and truth shall be a growth of righteousness among men. The pardoned people of God shall bring forth much fruit. And righteousness shall look down from heaven. God's righteousness "looks down from heaven" (like the sun), to draw up and mature the feeble plant of man's righteousness, which, without it, would come to nothing.
