How precious to me are Your thoughts, O God
This phrase emphasizes the value and esteem the psalmist places on God's thoughts. In biblical context, "thoughts" can refer to God's plans, purposes, and intentions. The Hebrew word for "precious" suggests something rare and valuable, akin to jewels or treasures. This reflects the psalmist's deep appreciation for God's wisdom and understanding. The phrase echoes the sentiment found in Isaiah 55:8-9, where God's thoughts and ways are described as higher than human thoughts and ways. It also connects to the idea of God's omniscience, as seen earlier in Psalm 139, where God's intimate knowledge of the psalmist is detailed. The reverence for God's thoughts can be seen as a type of Christ, who is described as the wisdom of God in 1 Corinthians 1:24.

how vast is their sum!
The vastness of God's thoughts indicates their infinite nature, suggesting that they are beyond human comprehension. This aligns with the biblical theme of God's omnipotence and omniscience, as seen in Job 11:7-9, where God's mysteries are described as higher than the heavens and deeper than the depths. The psalmist's awe at the magnitude of God's thoughts reflects a recognition of God's sovereignty and the complexity of His creation and plans. This vastness can also be seen in the context of God's covenantal promises, which are numerous and encompass all of history, pointing to the fulfillment in Jesus Christ, who embodies the culmination of God's redemptive plan. The phrase invites believers to trust in God's comprehensive understanding and perfect will, as seen in Romans 11:33-36, where Paul marvels at the depth of God's wisdom and knowledge.

What Does Psalm 139:17 Mean
How precious to me are Your thoughts

- The psalmist delights in the reality that God actively thinks about him. This is personal, not abstract.

- Scripture consistently shows God’s mindful care for His people:

• “Many, O LORD my God, are the wonders You have done, and the plans You have for us—none can compare to You” (Psalm 40:5).

• Jesus points to the same intimate attention: “Even the very hairs of your head are all numbered” (Matthew 10:30).

- Because God’s thoughts are flawless, their “precious” nature means they are both priceless and trustworthy. There is no higher counsel to rest in than the thoughts of the One who cannot lie (Titus 1:2).

O God

- By naming God directly, David underlines relationship as well as reverence.

- The verse sits in a psalm celebrating God’s omniscience and omnipresence (Psalm 139:1-6, 7-12). Every thought comes from the One who formed every cell (Psalm 139:13-16).

- Addressing God personally invites worship. Just as Moses bowed at the revelation of God’s name (Exodus 34:8), David’s heart bows at the revelation of God’s thoughts.

how vast is their sum!

- God’s thoughts are innumerable—far beyond any human calculation.

• “Your faithfulness reaches to the clouds” (Psalm 36:5).

• “Great is our Lord and mighty in power; His understanding has no limit” (Psalm 147:5).

• Paul echoes this awe: “Oh, the depth of the riches of the wisdom and knowledge of God!” (Romans 11:33).

- The vastness includes both scope and detail. God has plans spanning eternity (Ephesians 1:4-11) and yet thinks about individual sparrows (Luke 12:6-7).

- Since the total is infinite, believers can never exhaust God’s wisdom. Each day unveils fresh mercies (Lamentations 3:22-23), assuring us that there is always more grace and insight available.

summary

Psalm 139:17 invites us to marvel at the personal, priceless, and immeasurable thoughts God directs toward His children. His flawless counsel, revealed in Scripture and proven in providence, offers unshakable security and endless wonder for every believer who trusts His Word.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(17) Precious.--Rather, weighty, the first meaning of the word. The parallelism requires this, as also the peculiar word for "thoughts," for which see Psalm 139:2. We have here the antithesis to that verse: while the Divine penetration discovers the most intimate thought of man, man finds God's secrets incomprehensible.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 17. - How precious also are thy thoughts unto me, O God! If God's works are admirable, and, therefore, precious, so still more are his thoughts - those deep counsels of his, which must have preceded all manifestation of himself in act or work. How great is the sum of them! Were they all added together, how immeasurable would be the amount! What a treasure of wisdom and knowledge;
