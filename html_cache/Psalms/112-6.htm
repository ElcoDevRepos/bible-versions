Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Surely he will never be shaken;
This phrase emphasizes the stability and steadfastness of the righteous. In biblical context, being "shaken" often refers to being disturbed or overthrown by life's trials or adversities. The assurance that the righteous will not be shaken is a recurring theme in Scripture, reflecting God's protection and support. Psalm 55:22 echoes this sentiment, encouraging believers to cast their burdens on the Lord, who will sustain them. The imagery of an unshakable foundation is also seen in Jesus' parable of the wise and foolish builders in Matthew 7:24-27, where the house built on the rock withstands storms. This stability is not due to human strength but is rooted in a life aligned with God's will and purposes.

the righteous man will be remembered forever.
The concept of being "remembered forever" speaks to the enduring legacy and honor of the righteous. In the ancient Near Eastern culture, memory and legacy were highly valued, often associated with one's name and deeds being preserved through generations. This phrase suggests that the righteous leave a lasting impact, not only in human memory but also in God's eternal record. Proverbs 10:7 states, "The memory of the righteous is a blessing," reinforcing the idea that their influence persists beyond their lifetime. This eternal remembrance can also be seen as a type of Christ, whose righteous life and sacrificial death are eternally commemorated and celebrated by believers. The promise of eternal remembrance offers hope and encouragement to live a life of integrity and faithfulness.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Righteous Man
This refers to an individual who lives in accordance with God's laws and principles. In the context of Psalm 112, the righteous man is characterized by his fear of the Lord and delight in His commandments.

2. Psalm 112
This is a wisdom psalm that highlights the blessings and characteristics of a person who fears the Lord. It is often paired with Psalm 111, which praises God's works and faithfulness.

3. The Psalmist
Traditionally attributed to King David, the psalmist is the author who conveys the message of God's faithfulness to the righteous.

What Does Psalm 112:6 Mean
Surely

“Surely” opens the verse with unshakable certainty. The psalmist is not speculating—he is standing on the fixed promises of God.

• Numbers 23:19 reminds us that “God is not a man, that He should lie.”

• Isaiah 55:11 echoes the assurance that God’s word “will not return to Me empty.”

• Hebrews 6:18 underscores the same confidence: “It is impossible for God to lie.”

Because God’s character guarantees His words, everything that follows in this verse is absolutely dependable.

He will never be shaken

This is a promise of stability amid life’s storms. The idea is not that troubles vanish, but that the righteous stand firm when they come.

• Psalm 15:5 affirms, “He who does these things will never be shaken.”

• Psalm 55:22 adds, “Cast your burden upon the LORD and He will sustain you; He will never let the righteous be shaken.”

• Jesus pictures this security in Matthew 7:24-25, where the house built on rock withstands wind and rain.

What keeps the righteous steady is not inner grit but the solid ground of God’s faithfulness.

The righteous man

Verse 1 of the same psalm defines this person: “Blessed is the man who fears the LORD, who greatly delights in His commandments”. Righteousness here is practical and observable:

• Psalm 1:1-3 speaks of a man who avoids ungodly counsel and “is like a tree planted by streams of water.”

• Proverbs 20:7 notes, “The righteous man walks with integrity; blessed are his children after him.”

Walking in reverent obedience, this believer displays the life God approves and sustains.

Will be remembered forever

God ensures that the influence of the righteous reaches beyond their lifetime.

• Proverbs 10:7: “The memory of the righteous is a blessing.”

• Hebrews 6:10 reassures believers that “God is not unjust; He will not forget your work and the love you have shown His name.”

• Revelation 14:13 records, “Blessed are the dead who die in the Lord… their deeds will follow them.”

Earthly recognition may fade, but God’s own remembrance is eternal, and He weaves their legacy into His unfolding story.

summary

Psalm 112:6 promises rock-solid security and lasting honor to the one who fears the LORD. Because God’s word is certain (“Surely”), the righteous can stand firm through every upheaval (“never be shaken”). Their God-honoring life (“the righteous man”) leaves an imprint that time cannot erase (“remembered forever”). Confidence, stability, integrity, and eternal significance—all flow from trusting and obeying the faithful God of Scripture.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 6. - Surely he shall not be moved forever. God's blessing shall abide with him, and make his happiness sure and stable. (On stability as a necessary element in happiness, see Aristotle, 'Eth. Nic.,' 1. 10. § 7, 8.) The righteous shall be in everlasting remembrance; i.e. everlastingly remembered by God.
