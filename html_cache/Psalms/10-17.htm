You have heard, O LORD, the desire of the humble;
This phrase emphasizes God's attentiveness to the prayers and desires of the humble. In biblical context, humility is a key virtue, often associated with righteousness and a contrite spirit (Isaiah 57:15). The humble are those who recognize their dependence on God, contrasting with the proud who rely on their own strength. This theme is consistent throughout Scripture, as seen in James 4:6, which states that God gives grace to the humble. The acknowledgment that God hears the desires of the humble reflects His intimate involvement in the lives of His people, a recurring theme in the Psalms.

You will strengthen their hearts.
The promise of strengthening the hearts of the humble indicates God's provision of inner fortitude and courage. In the ancient Near Eastern context, the heart was considered the center of thought, emotion, and will. Strengthening the heart implies empowering individuals to face challenges with resilience and faith. This assurance is echoed in other scriptures, such as Isaiah 40:31, where those who hope in the Lord renew their strength. It also points to the transformative work of God in the believer's life, aligning with the New Testament teaching of the Holy Spirit's role in empowering believers (Ephesians 3:16).

You will incline Your ear,
The imagery of God inclining His ear suggests a personal and attentive response to prayer. This anthropomorphic expression conveys God's willingness to listen closely and respond to the cries of His people. In the cultural context of the Bible, a king or ruler who inclined his ear to a subject was showing favor and readiness to act on their behalf. This assurance of God's attentiveness is a source of comfort and encouragement, reinforcing the idea that God is not distant but actively engaged with His creation. This concept is mirrored in 1 Peter 3:12, which affirms that the eyes of the Lord are on the righteous and His ears are attentive to their prayer.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal presence and faithfulness to His people.

2. The Humble
Those who are lowly in spirit, often oppressed or afflicted, who seek God earnestly.

3. The Psalmist
Traditionally attributed to David, the psalmist expresses a deep trust in God's justice and care for the humble.
Teaching Points
God Hears the Humble
The Hebrew word for "hear" (shama) implies not just listening but responding. God is attentive to the cries of the humble, indicating His active involvement in their lives.

Strengthening of the Heart
The promise that God will "strengthen their hearts" suggests an inner fortitude and courage given by God. This is a reminder that true strength comes from reliance on Him.

God's Inclined Ear
The imagery of God inclining His ear signifies His willingness to listen closely and attentively. This reassures believers that their prayers are not in vain.

The Role of Humility
Humility is a key virtue in the Christian life, opening the door to God's grace and strength. It involves recognizing our dependence on God and submitting to His will.

Trust in God's Justice
The broader context of Psalm 10 deals with the apparent triumph of the wicked. This verse reassures believers that God is just and will ultimately vindicate the humble.

What Does Psalm 10:17 Mean
You have heard, O LORD

“You have heard, O LORD…” (Psalm 10:17a)

• The psalmist begins with confident assurance: God hears. This echoes Psalm 34:15, “The eyes of the LORD are on the righteous and His ears are inclined to their cry,” and reminds us of 1 John 5:14, where believers are urged to pray boldly because the Lord listens.

• God’s hearing is not passive; Exodus 2:24 shows Him responding to Israel’s groans. Here, we are invited to rest in a Father who never misses a whisper from His children.

the desire of the humble

“…the desire of the humble…” (Psalm 10:17a)

• “Humble” signals those who bow under God’s authority, not the self-sufficient. Isaiah 57:15 affirms that God “revives the spirit of the lowly,” and James 4:6 promises grace to the humble.

• Their “desire” is more than a passing wish—it is a heart-cry for justice and deliverance, aligning with Psalm 9:12, where God “does not ignore the cry of the afflicted.”

• The verse assures every believer who feels small or overlooked that humility positions us to be heard.

You will strengthen their hearts

“…You will strengthen their hearts.” (Psalm 10:17b)

• Divine hearing leads to divine enabling. Psalm 27:14 urges, “Wait for the LORD; be strong and let your heart take courage,” and Philippians 4:13 shows that strength flows from Christ.

• Ways God fortifies the heart:

– Renewed courage in trials (Isaiah 40:29-31)

– Inner resilience through the Spirit (Ephesians 3:16)

– Steadfast peace that guards against fear (John 14:27)

• The Lord doesn’t merely sympathize; He equips the humble to stand firm.

You will incline Your ear

“…You will incline Your ear,” (Psalm 10:17c)

• The phrase pictures God bending down, intent on every syllable. Psalm 116:2 celebrates this same tenderness: “Because He has inclined His ear to me, I will call on Him as long as I live.”

• Inclining implies ongoing attention—He keeps listening until the answer comes. Compare Psalm 31:2, “Turn Your ear to me; come quickly to my rescue,” demonstrating that God’s nearness fuels expectancy.

• 2 Chronicles 7:15 underscores that His ears are perpetually open to the prayers of His people, confirming that intimacy with God is not momentary but constant.

summary

Psalm 10:17 assures believers that the Lord hears, cherishes, and responds to the earnest longings of the humble. He not only listens but actively strengthens the hearts of those who trust Him, leaning in to every plea. Living with this confidence encourages us to approach God freely, remain humble, and rely on His promised courage for every challenge.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 17. - Lord, thou hast heard the desire of the humble (comp. Psalm 9:12). It is not the psalmist's prayer alone that he regards as heard and answered. The oppressed have cried to God against their oppressors, and their cry has "come before him, and entered into his ears." Thou wilt prepare their heart; rather, thou dost establish (or, make firm) their heart. Through their conviction that thou art on their side, and art about to help them. Thou wilt cause thine ear to hear; or, thou causest.
