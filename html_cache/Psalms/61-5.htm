For You have heard my vows, O God;
This phrase reflects a personal relationship with God, where the psalmist acknowledges that God listens to his promises and commitments. Vows in the biblical context often refer to solemn promises made to God, sometimes in times of distress or as acts of devotion. The psalmist's confidence that God has heard his vows suggests a history of answered prayers and a deep trust in God's faithfulness. This echoes the broader biblical theme of God as a responsive and attentive deity, as seen in passages like 1 Samuel 1:11, where Hannah makes a vow to God, and He responds to her plea.

You have given me the inheritance
The concept of inheritance in the Bible often refers to the blessings and promises given by God to His people. In the Old Testament, inheritance typically involved the land promised to the Israelites, as seen in Genesis 15:18-21. Spiritually, it can also signify the eternal blessings and promises of God, such as those mentioned in Ephesians 1:11, where believers are said to have obtained an inheritance in Christ. This phrase suggests that the psalmist sees himself as a recipient of God's promises, which are assured and secure.

reserved for those who fear Your name.
The fear of the Lord is a recurring theme in Scripture, denoting reverence, awe, and respect for God. It is often associated with wisdom and obedience, as seen in Proverbs 9:10, "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom." The inheritance reserved for those who fear God's name implies a special blessing or favor for those who live in reverence and obedience to Him. This reflects the covenant relationship between God and His people, where blessings are promised to those who honor and follow Him. The phrase underscores the idea that God's promises are not arbitrary but are given to those who live in accordance with His will.

Persons / Places / Events

2. God

Teaching Points
The Importance of Vows
Making vows to God is a serious commitment. We should be mindful of the promises we make and strive to fulfill them, reflecting our integrity and devotion.

Inheritance Through Reverence
The inheritance mentioned is not just material but spiritual, encompassing the peace, joy, and eternal life promised to those who fear God. Our reverence for God should be evident in our daily lives.

God's Faithfulness
God hears and responds to the prayers and vows of His people. We can trust in His faithfulness to fulfill His promises, encouraging us to remain steadfast in our faith.

Community of Believers
Being part of a community that fears God's name is vital. It provides support, accountability, and encouragement in our spiritual journey.

Living in Fear of the Lord
Fearing God is not about being afraid but having a deep respect and awe for His holiness and authority. This fear should guide our actions and decisions.

What Does Psalm 61:5 Mean
For You have heard my vows, O God

• David’s opening words pulse with confidence: God has listened. Similar assurance shines in Psalm 66:19, “God has surely heard; He has attended to the sound of my prayer.”

• A vow is a solemn promise of devotion or praise (Psalm 56:12). David does not treat vows lightly (Ecclesiastes 5:4).

• God’s hearing implies approval. When Hannah fulfilled her vow regarding Samuel, the Lord blessed her further (1 Samuel 1:27–28; 2:20).

• The line encourages believers that sincere commitments matter; the Lord notes and remembers each one (Hebrews 6:10).

You have given me the inheritance

• “Given” underscores grace, not wage. As Joshua received Canaan by promise, not merit (Joshua 1:6), so David recognizes divine generosity.

• The inheritance includes present protection (Psalm 16:5) and future glory (1 Peter 1:4).

• It is already “given,” echoing Colossians 1:12, where saints are “qualified to share in the inheritance of the holy ones in light.”

• Romans 8:17 widens the vista: in Christ we are “heirs of God and co-heirs with Christ,” sharing both His sufferings and His coming reign.

reserved for those who fear Your name

• The promise is not indiscriminate. “The LORD confides in those who fear Him” (Psalm 25:14), marking out a distinct circle of reverent hearts.

• To fear God is to treasure Him with awe-filled obedience (Proverbs 14:27). David himself models this posture (Psalm 34:9).

• Malachi 3:16-17 illustrates the reservation: God writes a scroll of remembrance for those who fear His name and promises, “They will be Mine…in the day when I prepare My treasured possession.”

• Luke 1:50 extends the principle across covenants: “His mercy is upon those who fear Him, from generation to generation.”

summary

David looks back on kept vows and forward to a secured inheritance. Because God attentively hears, believers can pledge wholehearted devotion with confidence that every promise is noticed. Because God graciously gives, they can rest in an inheritance that is both present and future. And because God reserves that inheritance for those who fear His name, a life of awe-filled obedience is never wasted but woven into eternal reward.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(5) Heritage.--As the Authorised Version runs, the heritage is length of days, one promised generally to those who fear Jehovah (Proverbs 10:27; Proverbs 19:23), and particularly to Israel (Deuteronomy 6:2) and its kings (Deuteronomy 17:19-20, which passage may have been in the psalmist's mind). But the LXX. and Vulg. read, "to them that fear thy name," meaning, of course, by the heritage, Canaan.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 5. - For thou, O God, hast heard my vows. Thou hast heard me so often in the past, thou hast granted so many of my prayers, accepted so many of my vows, that I am emboldened to make further requests. Thou hast given me the heritage of those that fear thy Name. All the blessed inheritance of thy saints thou hast made mine, and, included in it, boldness to approach the throne of grace in full assurance of faith, and to present to thee my petitions.
