Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
I look on the faithless with loathing
The psalmist expresses a strong emotional reaction towards those who are unfaithful to God's covenant. This sentiment reflects a deep commitment to God's law and a desire for righteousness. The term "faithless" often refers to those who have turned away from God's commandments, similar to the Israelites who worshipped idols (Exodus 32). The loathing is not personal animosity but a righteous indignation towards sin, akin to Jesus' reaction to the money changers in the temple (John 2:13-17).

because they do not keep Your word
The psalmist's disdain is rooted in the failure of the faithless to adhere to God's commandments. This highlights the importance of obedience in the biblical narrative, as seen in Deuteronomy 28, where blessings and curses are tied to the observance of God's law. The phrase underscores the centrality of God's word in guiding moral and spiritual conduct. It also echoes the New Testament teaching in James 1:22, which emphasizes being doers of the word, not just hearers. The psalmist's lament can be seen as a type of Christ, who perfectly kept God's word and grieved over Jerusalem's unfaithfulness (Matthew 23:37).

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Psalmist
The author of Psalm 119, traditionally believed to be King David, who expresses a deep love for God's law and a desire for righteousness.

2. The Faithless
Individuals who do not adhere to God's commandments, often seen as those who reject or neglect the covenant relationship with God.

3. God's Word
The divine commandments and teachings given to the people of Israel, which the Psalmist holds in high regard.
Teaching Points
The Importance of God's Word
God's word is a guiding light and a source of truth. As believers, we are called to cherish and uphold it in our daily lives.

Righteous Indignation
The Psalmist's loathing of the faithless is not personal hatred but a righteous indignation against sin and disobedience to God.

The Call to Faithfulness
We are encouraged to remain faithful to God's commandments, recognizing the spiritual danger of neglecting His word.

Compassion for the Lost
While we may feel a sense of righteous anger towards sin, we are also called to have compassion and pray for those who are lost, seeking their repentance and salvation.

Self-Examination
This verse challenges us to examine our own lives and ensure that we are not falling into faithlessness by neglecting God's word.

What Does Psalm 119:158 Mean
I look

The psalmist is not indifferent; he is actively observing the people around him.

• This gaze is intentional, shaped by love for God’s standards (Psalm 119:15).

• Scripture often speaks of righteous people “considering” their surroundings—see Psalm 37:37.

• His careful look models discernment rather than blind tolerance (Philippians 1:9–10).

on the faithless

“Faithless” refers to those who have rejected God’s covenant.

• Comparable language appears in Deuteronomy 32:20, where the LORD calls a rebellious generation “faithless children.”

• The psalmist’s focus is moral, not merely social; their lack of fidelity to God places them outside the community of obedience (Malachi 2:11).

• Their actions reveal hearts hardened against God’s revealed truth (Romans 1:21).

with loathing

His reaction is strong, even visceral, yet it is rooted in righteous zeal.

• Proverbs 8:13 states, “To fear the LORD is to hate evil.”

• Jesus Himself demonstrated holy indignation toward sin (Mark 3:5).

• This hatred is not personal malice but a moral revulsion at anything opposed to God’s purity (Psalm 97:10).

because they do not keep

The cause of his abhorrence is crystal clear: a willful refusal to obey.

• Obedience is the hallmark of genuine love for God (John 14:15).

• Psalm 119 repeatedly links blessing with keeping God’s precepts (verses 1–2, 31).

• Rejecting the Word is tantamount to rejecting God Himself (1 Samuel 15:23).

Your word

The standard is not human opinion but the objective revelation of God.

• “Your word is a lamp to my feet and a light to my path” (Psalm 119:105).

• The Word is living, active, and judges the thoughts and intentions of the heart (Hebrews 4:12).

• By elevating God’s Word as the final authority, the psalmist exposes the seriousness of ignoring it (James 1:22–24).

summary

Psalm 119:158 pictures a believer who, out of deep loyalty to God, looks upon covenant-breakers with righteous loathing. His emotion is not spiteful; it is holy displeasure grounded in love for God’s flawless Word. Refusing to keep that Word is the defining mark of faithlessness, and discerning believers rightly recoil from such rebellion while remaining firmly anchored in Scripture’s authority.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
RESH.

(158) Transgressors.--Better, the faithless (or, traitors).

Was grieved.--The Hebrew is a far stronger word, and the sense is intensified by the rare conjugation: was filled with loathing at; sickened with disgust.

"The recreants I survey,

And loathing turn away."--KEBLE.
