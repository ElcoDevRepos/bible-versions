He who affixed the ear, can He not hear?
This phrase emphasizes God's omniscience and creative power. The rhetorical question suggests that the Creator, who designed the human ear, is fully capable of hearing. In biblical context, this is a reminder of God's awareness of human actions and prayers. The ear, as a symbol of hearing, is often used in Scripture to denote understanding and obedience (Proverbs 20:12). Theologically, this underscores God's intimate involvement with His creation, as seen in Genesis 1, where God creates with purpose and precision. The idea that God hears is a recurring theme, assuring believers of His attentiveness (Psalm 34:15).

He who formed the eye, can He not see?
This phrase highlights God's ability to see all things, reinforcing His omnipresence and omniscience. The eye, a complex organ, signifies perception and insight. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, seeing was associated with knowledge and judgment. This rhetorical question challenges any notion that God is unaware of human affairs. Biblically, God's sight is often linked to His justice and righteousness (Proverbs 15:3). The formation of the eye also points to God's intricate design in creation, as described in Psalm 139:13-16. This assurance of God's watchful presence is echoed in the New Testament, where Jesus, as the embodiment of God, sees and knows the hearts of men (John 2:24-25).

Teaching Points
God's Omniscience
God is fully aware of all that happens in the world. We can take comfort in knowing that nothing escapes His notice, including our struggles and injustices we face.

Accountability
Since God sees and hears everything, we are accountable to Him for our actions. This should encourage us to live righteously and justly.

Trust in Divine Justice
Even when it seems like the wicked prosper, we can trust that God sees their actions and will bring about justice in His perfect timing.

Encouragement for the Oppressed
Those who are suffering can find solace in the fact that God is aware of their plight and is a righteous judge who will act on their behalf.

Reflection on Creation
Recognizing God as the Creator of our senses should lead us to worship Him and use our abilities to honor Him.

What Does Psalm 94:9 Mean
He who affixed the ear

• The psalmist points to God as the literal Maker of the human ear. Scripture consistently presents the ear not as an accident of biology but as an intentional work of the Creator (Proverbs 20:12; Exodus 4:11).

• By highlighting the ear’s intricate design, the verse reminds us that God is actively involved in forming every part of human life (Psalm 139:13–14).

• This truth affirms His sovereignty: the One who “affixed” the ear has complete authority over what He has made (Genesis 1:31).

can He not hear?

• If God formed the organ of hearing, it follows that His own hearing is perfect—far surpassing human limits.

• He hears every cry for justice (Exodus 2:24), every whispered prayer (Psalm 34:15), and even thoughts unspoken (Psalm 139:4).

• For the righteous, this brings comfort: “In my distress I called upon the LORD… from His temple He heard my voice” (Psalm 18:6).

• For the wicked, it is a sober warning: nothing said or plotted escapes His notice (Romans 2:16).

He who formed the eye

• The human eye, with its complexity and beauty, stands as a testimony to the wisdom of its Designer (Psalm 19:1).

• Just as He fashioned ears, God “formed” eyes intentionally, underscoring His intimate involvement in creation (Psalm 139:13–14).

• This formation extends beyond physical sight; it anticipates spiritual vision—eyes opened to recognize His works (Ephesians 1:18).

can He not see?

• The rhetorical question demands an obvious answer: God’s sight is flawless and unblinking. “The eyes of the LORD are in every place, observing the evil and the good” (Proverbs 15:3).

• He sees injustice and will act (Psalm 11:4–7). He also sees faithfulness and will reward it (2 Chronicles 16:9).

• Nothing is hidden: “Nothing in all creation is hidden from God’s sight” (Hebrews 4:13). For believers, His watchful eye brings assurance; for rebels, it means unavoidable accountability.

summary

Psalm 94:9 uses the design of ears and eyes to affirm God’s omniscience and omnipresence. The Maker of hearing hears perfectly; the Former of sight sees perfectly. Therefore, He is fully aware of every act, word, and motive. This brings comfort to those who trust Him and a solemn warning to those who oppose Him.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 9. - He that planted the ear, shall he not hear? he that formed the eye, shall he not see? This argument for a real, personal, intelligent God appears here, for the first time. It is of irresistible force. "Can it be possible that God, who planned and made the curious mechanism of hearing and vision, is himself without those faculties, or something analogous to them? Must he not hear those cries, and see those outrages, which men, who are his creatures, see and hear? Is it conceivable that he can be an unobservant and apathetic God?" (Cheyne).
