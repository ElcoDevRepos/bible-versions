My frame was not hidden from You
This phrase emphasizes God's omniscience and intimate knowledge of human beings. The term "frame" refers to the physical structure or skeleton, highlighting the intricacy of human creation. In biblical context, this reflects the belief that God is aware of every detail of our existence, even before birth. This aligns with passages like Jeremiah 1:5, where God declares knowledge of Jeremiah before he was formed in the womb, underscoring the personal relationship God has with each individual.

when I was made in secret
The "secret" place refers to the womb, a hidden and protected environment where life begins. This phrase suggests the mysterious and sacred nature of human development, a process known only to God. In ancient times, the understanding of embryology was limited, making the formation of life even more wondrous and attributed to divine craftsmanship. This aligns with the biblical theme of God as the Creator, as seen in Genesis 1:27, where humanity is made in God's image.

when I was woven together
The imagery of being "woven together" suggests a careful and deliberate act of creation, akin to a skilled weaver crafting a tapestry. This metaphor highlights the complexity and intentionality of God's design. The use of weaving imagery is also found in Job 10:11, where Job speaks of being clothed with skin and flesh, further illustrating the intricate work of God in forming human life.

in the depths of the earth
This phrase is metaphorical, referring to the hidden and mysterious process of creation. The "depths of the earth" symbolize a place of origin and formation, much like the womb. In ancient Near Eastern thought, the earth was often seen as a source of life and sustenance. This imagery can also be connected to the resurrection theme, where the earth is a place of burial and new life, as seen in the prophecy of Jesus' resurrection in Matthew 12:40, drawing a parallel between the earth's depths and the tomb.

What Does Psalm 139:15 Mean
My frame was not hidden from You

- David marvels that even the skeletal structure of his tiny, forming body was fully visible to God.

• Hebrews 4:13 reminds us that “Nothing in all creation is hidden from God’s sight.”

• Job 10:8–9 echoes, “Your hands shaped me and made me… remember that You molded me like clay.”

- The verse underscores God’s personal involvement in forming every bone, ligament, and cell—well before any human eye could detect them.

when I was made in secret

- “Secret” points to the mother’s womb, a hidden chamber where life begins.

• Psalm 139:13 had already declared, “For You formed my inmost being; You knit me together in my mother’s womb.”

• Jeremiah 1:5 parallels this thought: “Before I formed you in the womb I knew you.”

- The phrase affirms that God’s creative work is both intimate and purposeful, carried out away from public view yet always in His sight.

• Proverbs 15:3 assures us, “The eyes of the LORD are in every place,” including that unseen sanctuary where new life takes shape.

when I was woven together in the depths of the earth

- “Woven together” paints the picture of an artisan threading strands into a tapestry—God interlacing DNA, organs, and personality.

• Job 10:11–12 uses similar imagery: “You clothed me with skin and flesh, and knit me together with bones and sinews.”

- “Depths of the earth” is poetic language for the womb, likening that hidden recess to the most remote places of creation.

• Ephesians 2:10 complements this: “For we are God’s workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works…”

• Psalm 63:9’s “depths of the earth” metaphor shows that nothing is out of God’s reach—He is sovereign over the unseen as well as the seen.

summary

Psalm 139:15 celebrates God’s omniscience and meticulous craftsmanship in forming human life. Long before anyone else is aware of a child’s existence, the Lord is intimately involved—seeing every bone, shaping every organ, and weaving every detail according to His perfect design. This verse reassures us that our lives are not accidental or overlooked; from conception onward we are fully known, lovingly crafted, and purposefully planned by God.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(15) Substance.--Aquila "bones," LXX. and Vulg. "bone," Symmachus "strength." Perhaps, generally, body. But the common Hebrew word for bone differs only in the pointing.

In secret.--Comp. 'sch. Eum. 665.

Curiously wrought.--From the use of the verb in Exodus 26:36; Exodus 27:16, it plainly refers to some kind of tapestry work, but whether of the nature of weaving or embroidery is matter of controversy. The English sufficiently suggests the figure.

In the lowest parts of the earth.--This figurative allusion to the womb is intended no doubt to heighten the feeling of mystery attaching to birth. There may also be a covert allusion to the creation from dust as Ecclesiasticus 40:1, "From the day that they go out of their mother's womb, till the day that they return to the mother of all things." This allusion falls in with the view which meets us in other parts of the Old Testament, that the creation of Adam is repeated at every birth (Job 33:6, and see above, Psalm 139:13). . . .

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 15. - My substance was not hid from thee, when I was made in secret. The formation of the embryo in the womb seems to be intended. This remains as much a mystery as ever, notwithstanding all the pryings of modern science. And curiously wrought; literally, and embroidered, or woven with threads of divers colors (comp. ver. 13b; and note that modern science speaks of the various "tissues" of the human frame, and calls a portion of medical knowledge "histology"). In the lowest parts of the earth. This is scarcely to be taken literally. It is perhaps only a variant for the "secretly" of the preceding clause.
