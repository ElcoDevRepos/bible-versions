But My people would not listen to Me
This phrase highlights the recurring theme of Israel's disobedience and spiritual deafness. Throughout the Old Testament, God repeatedly calls His people to listen and obey (Deuteronomy 6:4-5). The Hebrew word for "listen" implies not just hearing but also responding with action. This reflects the covenant relationship where God speaks, and His people are expected to respond in faith and obedience. The failure to listen is a rejection of God's authority and wisdom, which is a central issue in the prophetic books (Isaiah 30:9-11).

and Israel would not obey Me
The term "obey" in Hebrew is often linked with the concept of "hearing" and "doing." Israel's refusal to obey is not just a matter of disobedience but a breach of the covenant established at Sinai (Exodus 19:5-6). This disobedience is a recurring issue that leads to various consequences, including exile and suffering. The historical context shows that Israel's disobedience often involved idolatry and alliances with pagan nations, contrary to God's commands (2 Kings 17:7-18). This phrase also foreshadows the need for a new covenant, as prophesied in Jeremiah 31:31-34, which is fulfilled in Jesus Christ, who perfectly obeyed the Father and enables believers to do the same through the Holy Spirit (Hebrews 8:6-13).

Persons / Places / Events
1. God
- The speaker in this verse, expressing His lament over Israel's disobedience.

2. Israel
- The chosen people of God who are being addressed in this Psalm, representing the collective nation that has turned away from God's commands.

3. Psalm 81
- A psalm attributed to Asaph, focusing on God's deliverance and the people's disobedience.

4. Asaph
- A Levite and one of the leaders of David's choir, traditionally credited with writing several psalms, including Psalm 81.

5. The Exodus
- The historical backdrop of God's deliverance of Israel from Egypt, referenced earlier in the psalm as a reminder of God's faithfulness.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Listening to God
Listening to God is crucial for maintaining a relationship with Him. Just as Israel's refusal to listen led to consequences, so too can our spiritual deafness lead us away from God's blessings.

Obedience as a Response to God's Faithfulness
God desires obedience not as a burden but as a response to His faithfulness and love. Reflect on how God has been faithful in your life and let that inspire your obedience.

The Consequences of Disobedience
Disobedience leads to spiritual and sometimes physical consequences. Consider the areas in your life where you might be resisting God's guidance and the potential outcomes of that resistance.

God's Persistent Call to His People
Despite Israel's disobedience, God continues to call His people back to Him. This demonstrates His patience and enduring love, encouraging us to return to Him when we stray.

Reflecting on Our Own Hearts
Use this verse as a mirror to examine your own heart. Are there areas where you are not listening to God? Seek to align your heart with His will.

What Does Psalm 81:11 Mean
But My people

• God addresses Israel with possessive warmth—“My people”—affirming covenant relationship (Exodus 19:5-6; Hosea 11:1).

• The term highlights that rebellion is not from strangers but from those He redeemed (Deuteronomy 14:2).

• This intensifies the grief of divine disappointment, echoing Isaiah 1:3, “Israel does not understand.”

• Even today, believers bear His name (1 Peter 2:9), so the warning transcends time.

would not listen to Me

• “Listen” in Scripture always implies responsive obedience (James 1:22).

• Repeated prophetic pleas—“Hear, O Israel” (Deuteronomy 6:4; Jeremiah 6:16-17)—went unheeded.

• Refusal to listen closes the door to blessing promised in Deuteronomy 28:1-14.

• Jesus laments the same heart posture in Matthew 23:37, showing continuity of God’s grief.

and Israel

• By repeating the nation’s covenant name, the psalm underscores accountability to privileges enjoyed (Romans 9:4-5).

• Corporate identity means collective consequences (Numbers 14:1-4; Judges 2:10-13).

• The church likewise bears collective responsibility (Revelation 2–3).

would not obey Me

• Disobedience moves from ears to actions (Ezekiel 33:31-32).

• Psalm 81 connects refusal with forfeited abundance (vv. 13-16), mirroring Judges 2:20-23.

• Persistent hard-heartedness invites divine “giving them over” (Romans 1:24; Acts 7:42).

• Yet God’s discipline aims at restoration (Hebrews 12:5-11).

summary

Psalm 81:11 exposes the tragedy of a covenant people who enjoy God’s nearness yet close their ears and hearts. The verse warns that intimacy with God is maintained by attentive listening and active obedience. Blessing and fellowship flow where His voice is welcomed; loss and discipline follow where it is ignored.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 11. - But my people would not hearken to my voice (comp. Psalm 78:10, 41, 56; 2 Kings 17:14; 2 Chronicles 36:15, 16). And Israel would none of me; literally, would not obey me (see the Prayer book Version).
