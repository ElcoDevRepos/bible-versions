Long ago
This phrase suggests a deep-rooted understanding and experience with God's Word. It implies a historical continuity and a longstanding tradition of faith. The psalmist's reflection on the past can be seen as a reminder of the enduring nature of God's truth, which has been known and trusted by generations. This echoes the biblical emphasis on remembering God's works and teachings, as seen in Deuteronomy 32:7, where the Israelites are encouraged to remember the days of old.

I learned from Your testimonies
The term "testimonies" refers to God's laws and decrees, which are a witness to His character and will. The psalmist's learning from these testimonies indicates a personal and intentional engagement with Scripture. This learning is not merely intellectual but involves a heart transformation, as seen in Psalm 119:11, where the psalmist treasures God's word in his heart. The testimonies are a source of wisdom and guidance, as highlighted in Psalm 19:7, which states that the law of the Lord is perfect, reviving the soul.

that You have established them
This phrase underscores the divine origin and authority of God's testimonies. The establishment of God's laws is not a human endeavor but a divine act, reflecting God's sovereignty and unchanging nature. The idea of God establishing His word is consistent with the biblical theme of God's eternal decrees, as seen in Isaiah 40:8, where the word of God is said to stand forever. This establishment assures believers of the reliability and trustworthiness of Scripture.

forever
The eternal nature of God's testimonies is emphasized here, highlighting their unchanging and perpetual relevance. This aligns with the biblical understanding of God's word as eternal, as seen in Matthew 24:35, where Jesus states that heaven and earth will pass away, but His words will never pass away. The concept of eternity in Scripture often points to God's timeless nature and His plans that transcend human history. This assurance of permanence provides believers with a foundation of hope and stability in a changing world.

Teaching Points
Eternal Nature of God's Word
God's testimonies are not subject to change or decay; they are as relevant today as they were when first given.

Learning from the Past
The psalmist's acknowledgment of learning from God's testimonies "long ago" encourages us to value historical and scriptural wisdom.

Foundation for Life
Understanding that God's word is established forever provides a stable foundation for our faith and daily living.

Trust in God's Unchanging Nature
In a world of constant change, we can find peace and assurance in the unchanging nature of God's word.

Commitment to Scripture
As believers, we are called to commit ourselves to studying and living by God's eternal testimonies.

What Does Psalm 119:152 Mean
Long ago

- The psalmist remembers an early, settled encounter with God’s Word. He is not dabbling in something new; he is recalling a truth he embraced years before.

- Psalm 71:17 echoes this lifelong familiarity: “O God, You have taught me from my youth, and to this day I proclaim Your marvelous deeds.”

- 2 Timothy 3:15 confirms the blessing of an early grounding in Scripture. The verse teaches that longevity with the Word nurtures stability and discernment.

I learned

- “Learned” describes deliberate study that moves truth from the page into the heart. It implies discipline, meditation, and obedience.

- Psalm 119:11 shows what this learning looks like: “I have hidden Your word in my heart that I might not sin against You.”

- The psalmist’s confidence flows from personal experience, not secondhand information.

From Your testimonies

- “Testimonies” points to God’s recorded acts, promises, and laws—His whole revealed Word.

- Psalm 19:7 reminds us, “The testimony of the LORD is trustworthy, making wise the simple,” highlighting both reliability and transforming power.

- Deuteronomy 4:45 demonstrates that God’s testimonies are intended to be heard, remembered, and obeyed.

You have established them

- “Established” signals permanence and authority. God’s Word is fixed; it does not shift with culture or opinion.

- Psalm 119:89 declares, “Your word, O LORD, stands firm in the heavens,” confirming that the Word is anchored beyond earthly change.

- Jesus affirms the same in Matthew 24:35: “Heaven and earth will pass away, but My words will never pass away.”

Forever

- The verse culminates with an eternal perspective. Not only has God set His testimonies in place, He has done so for all time.

- Isaiah 40:8 underscores this permanence: “The grass withers and the flowers fall, but the word of our God stands forever.”

- 1 Peter 1:25 repeats the assurance, rooting the believer’s confidence in the unending reliability of Scripture.

summary

Psalm 119:152 celebrates a long-held, personal conviction: God’s Word—learned early, studied diligently, and trusted completely—is permanently fixed by God Himself. Its authority is unwavering, its relevance timeless, and its truth eternally secure.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(152) The more obvious rendering of this verse is, Of old I was instructed out of Thy testimonies, for--not for a brief time, but for ever--Thou didst found them, where for ever expresses indefinite past as well as indefinite future.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 152. - Concerning thy testimonies, I have known of old that thou hast founded them forever; rather, from thy testimonies. The study of God's commandments has long ago convinced the psalmist that they are no passing or temporary enactments, but eternal laws, decreed and laid down forever. The "testimonies" spoken of are, of course, the moral precepts of the Law, not its economical or political enactments.
