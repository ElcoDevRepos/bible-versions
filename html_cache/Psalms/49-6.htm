They trust in their wealth
This phrase highlights the misplaced confidence that individuals place in material possessions. Biblically, wealth is often seen as a temporary and unreliable source of security. In the context of ancient Israel, wealth was typically measured in terms of livestock, land, and precious metals. Trusting in wealth is cautioned against in Proverbs 11:28, which states, "He who trusts in his riches will fall, but the righteous will thrive like a green leaf." This reflects a broader biblical theme that true security and salvation come from God, not material wealth. Jesus also addresses this in the New Testament, particularly in Matthew 6:19-21, where He advises storing up treasures in heaven rather than on earth.

and boast in their great riches.
Boasting in riches is a demonstration of pride and self-reliance, which is contrary to the humility and dependence on God that Scripture advocates. In the cultural context of the psalm, boasting was a way to assert one's status and power, often leading to social inequality and injustice. The Bible consistently warns against pride, as seen in James 4:6, "God opposes the proud but shows favor to the humble." This phrase also connects to the parable of the rich fool in Luke 12:16-21, where Jesus illustrates the futility of hoarding wealth without being "rich toward God." The emphasis is on the transient nature of earthly riches and the eternal value of spiritual wealth.

Teaching Points
The Illusion of Security in Wealth
Wealth can create a false sense of security, leading individuals to trust in their riches rather than in God.

The Danger of Pride
Boasting in riches can lead to pride, which distances us from God and others. True security and identity should be found in Christ.

Eternal Perspective
Material wealth is temporary and cannot secure eternal life. Believers are called to focus on spiritual riches and eternal rewards.

Generosity as a Reflection of Faith
Christians are encouraged to use their resources to bless others, reflecting God's generosity and love.

Contentment in Christ
True contentment comes from a relationship with Christ, not from accumulating wealth. Believers should cultivate a heart of gratitude and trust in God's provision.

What Does Psalm 49:6 Mean
They trust in their wealth

The psalmist first exposes the false security people draw from money.

• Psalm 49 already sets the stage in vv. 5–9, reminding us that “no man can redeem his brother or give to God a ransom for him.” Wealth cannot buy off death or judgment.

• Proverbs 11:28 supports this: “He who trusts in his riches will fall, but the righteous will flourish like foliage.”

• Jesus echoes the warning in Luke 12:15–21, where the rich fool says, “Soul, you have many goods laid up,” only to meet God that very night.

• 1 Timothy 6:17 drives the point home: instruct the rich “not to put their hope in the uncertainty of riches.”

Taken together, Scripture insists that putting confidence in wealth is not merely unwise—it is spiritually deadly, because it replaces dependence on the Lord with a fragile, earthly crutch.

and boast in their great riches

The second phrase moves from silent trust to open celebration of wealth. Boasting reveals the heart’s treasure (Matthew 6:21).

• Jeremiah 9:23 warns, “Let not the wise man boast of his wisdom nor the strong man of his strength nor the rich man of his riches.”

• Psalm 52:7 pictures the arrogant man who “trusted in the abundance of his riches” and made wealth his stronghold.

• James 4:13–16 confronts merchants who brag about tomorrow’s profits, calling such boasting “evil” because life is a vapor.

• Mark 10:24–25 records Jesus saying, “How hard it is for those who trust in riches to enter the kingdom of God,” underscoring that proud confidence in money bars the door to grace.

Boasting in riches is more than bad taste; it is idolatry. It shifts glory from God, the true Provider (Deuteronomy 8:18), to the creaturely work of human hands. The psalm reminds us that bragging about wealth cannot hide the reality that, as v. 10 notes, “everyone must die.” Eternal worth is found only in knowing the Redeemer.

summary

Psalm 49:6 exposes two linked sins: relying on wealth for safety and exalting it as a badge of honor. The rest of the psalm, reinforced by passages like Proverbs 11:28, Luke 12:15–21, and 1 Timothy 6:17, shows that money cannot redeem a soul, extend a life, or satisfy God’s justice. Trust and glory belong to the Lord alone; all earthly riches fade, but those who seek Him “will receive the crown of life” (James 1:12).

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 6. - They that trust in their wealth; rather, even of them that trust in their wealth. The sense runs on from the preceding verse (so Hengstenberg and Professor Cheyne). And boast themselves in the multitude of their riches. Such men are always persecutors of the righteous. They are worldly, carnal, godless.
