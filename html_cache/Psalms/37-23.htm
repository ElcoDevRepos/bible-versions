The steps of a man are ordered by the LORD
This phrase emphasizes the sovereignty of God in guiding the lives of individuals. The concept of "steps" suggests a journey or path, indicating that God is involved in the day-to-day decisions and directions of a person's life. This aligns with Proverbs 16:9, which states, "In their hearts humans plan their course, but the LORD establishes their steps." The idea of God ordering steps reflects His omniscience and omnipotence, ensuring that His divine will is accomplished. Historically, this reflects the belief in divine providence, a central theme in the lives of the Israelites, who saw God's hand in their history from the Exodus to the establishment of the kingdom.

who takes delight in his journey
This part of the verse highlights God's pleasure in the righteous path of a believer. The "journey" implies not just the destination but the process and experiences along the way. This is reminiscent of Psalm 1:6, "For the LORD watches over the way of the righteous." The delight God takes can be seen as a reflection of His relational nature, desiring fellowship with those who walk in His ways. Culturally, this reflects the ancient Near Eastern understanding of a deity who is personally involved with His people, contrasting with the distant gods of surrounding nations. Theologically, it points to the joy and satisfaction God finds in the obedience and faithfulness of His followers, a theme echoed in the New Testament with the parable of the talents in Matthew 25:21, where the faithful servant is commended and invited to share in the master's happiness.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal and unchanging nature. In this verse, He is portrayed as the one who orders or establishes the steps of a person.

2. The Man (or Person)
Represents any individual who seeks to live a righteous life under God's guidance. The Hebrew word used here, (geber), often refers to a strong or mighty man, emphasizing the potential strength found in following God's path.

3. The Journey
Symbolizes the life path or course of an individual. It is not just the destination but the process and experiences along the way that God delights in.
Teaching Points
Divine Guidance
God actively participates in the lives of those who seek Him, guiding their steps and ensuring their path aligns with His will.

God's Delight in Our Journey
The LORD takes pleasure in the journey of the righteous, not just the end result. This encourages believers to focus on the process and growth in their walk with God.

Trust in God's Sovereignty
Believers are called to trust that God knows the best path for their lives, even when circumstances seem uncertain or challenging.

Active Participation in God's Plan
While God orders our steps, we are called to actively walk in obedience and faith, participating in His divine plan for our lives.

Strength in Righteous Living
The use of "geber" suggests that strength and might are found in living a life ordered by God, encouraging believers to find their strength in Him.

What Does Psalm 37:23 Mean
The steps of a man

• “Steps” picture every movement of life—big decisions and small routines. They include career choices, parenting, friendships, and even the seemingly random detours of an ordinary day (Psalm 37:5).

• Scripture repeatedly treats each step as significant: “Order my steps in Your word” (Psalm 119:133); “A man’s heart plans his course, but the LORD determines his steps” (Proverbs 16:9).

• Nothing is outside God’s notice; every footfall is part of His purposeful care.

are ordered

• “Ordered” means firmly established, not haphazard. God sets each step on solid ground, making it stand (Psalm 40:2).

• The phrase also carries the sense of being directed: like a master builder placing stones exactly where they belong (1 Samuel 2:9).

• Because the Lord orders, life is neither fatalistic nor chaotic; it is divinely structured for our good (Romans 8:28).

by the LORD

• The One arranging those steps is Yahweh, the covenant-keeping God who cannot fail (Deuteronomy 7:9).

• His sovereignty is personal: “A man’s steps are of the LORD; how then can man understand his own way?” (Proverbs 20:24).

• We rest, not in our ability to foresee, but in His perfect authority and intimate involvement (Jeremiah 10:23).

who takes delight in his journey

• The Lord is not a distant manager; He “takes delight in his journey.” He rejoices over the believer’s path just as a father rejoices in a child’s first steps (Zephaniah 3:17).

• God’s pleasure is tied to righteousness: “The LORD delights in those who fear Him” (Psalm 147:11). Walking in faith invites His smile.

• Even when the path winds through hardship, His delight remains, because the journey is leading to Christ-like maturity (James 1:2-4).

summary

Psalm 37:23 teaches that every move of the righteous is lovingly planned by the Lord. He secures each step, exercises full authority over the route, and finds joy in the pilgrim’s progress. Our confidence, then, is not in flawless planning but in the God who maps the way and walks it with us.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(23) The steps.--Comp. Proverbs 20:24; Proverbs 16:9, passages which are in favour of a general interpretation here, not confined to the good man. Render, man's steps are established by Jehovah, i.e., all the stability in human conduct comes from His guidance.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 23. - The steps of a good man are ordered by the Lord; rather, established; i.e. upheld, and made firm. It is not the general superintendence of men's steps and goings by God (Proverbs 16:9; Proverbs 20:24) which is here spoken of; but the special strengthening and supporting of the steps of the pious. The wont גבר must be understood, not of the ordinary man, but of the good man. ("גבר, viri, scilicet justi, et Jova benedicti," Roseumuller). And he delighteth in his way. He "knows" it (Psalm 1:6), and looks upon it with favour, and even "has pleasure" in it (Psalm 35:27).
