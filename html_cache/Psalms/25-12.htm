Who is the man who fears the LORD?
The concept of fearing the LORD is central to biblical wisdom literature, particularly in Proverbs (Proverbs 1:7). This fear is not about terror but reverence and awe, recognizing God's holiness and authority. In the ancient Near Eastern context, fear of a deity often involved respect and obedience, aligning with the covenant relationship between God and Israel. The question posed here is rhetorical, emphasizing the rarity and value of such a person. This fear leads to wisdom and understanding (Psalm 111:10), and it is foundational for a life aligned with God's will.

He will instruct him in the path chosen for him.
The promise of divine instruction is a recurring theme in the Psalms and reflects God's personal guidance for those who seek Him. The "path" signifies a way of life or moral direction, often associated with righteousness and obedience to God's commandments (Psalm 119:105). The phrase suggests a personal relationship where God actively guides the believer, reminiscent of the shepherd imagery in Psalm 23. This guidance is not arbitrary but tailored to the individual's life, reflecting God's sovereignty and intimate knowledge of each person. The idea of a "chosen path" aligns with the biblical theme of God's providence and purpose for His people, as seen in Jeremiah 29:11.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
Traditionally attributed as the author of Psalm 25, David was the second king of Israel, known for his deep relationship with God and his role in establishing Jerusalem as the central place of worship.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal existence and faithfulness to His promises. In this context, it highlights God's role as the divine instructor and guide.

3. The Fear of the LORD
A recurring biblical theme representing reverence, awe, and respect towards God, which is foundational for wisdom and understanding.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Reverence
Fearing the LORD is not about being afraid but about having a deep respect and awe for God, which positions us to receive His guidance.

Divine Guidance
God promises to instruct those who fear Him, indicating that a relationship with God involves active listening and obedience to His direction.

Choice and Responsibility
While God provides instruction, the individual is responsible for choosing the path. This highlights the partnership between divine guidance and human responsibility.

Wisdom as a Gift
Seeking God's wisdom is essential for making choices that align with His will, and it is available to those who earnestly seek it.

Living in God's Will
Understanding and following God's instruction leads to a life that is aligned with His purposes, bringing peace and fulfillment.

What Does Psalm 25:12 Mean
Who is the man who fears the LORD?

• The verse opens by spotlighting a specific kind of person: “the man who fears the LORD.” In Scripture, this “fear” is not terror but reverent awe that leads to loving obedience (Proverbs 1:7; Ecclesiastes 12:13).

• “The man” is representative language; anyone—male or female—who bows in humble submission to God is included (Acts 10:34-35).

• This posture of reverence is the gateway to wisdom and intimacy with God (Psalm 111:10; Isaiah 66:2).

He will instruct him

• “He” refers to the LORD Himself, pledging personal guidance. The promise echoes Psalm 32:8, “I will instruct you and teach you in the way you should go”.

• Divine instruction is not abstract; it is relational. God draws near to those who draw near to Him (James 4:8).

• The Spirit’s role in leading believers is affirmed throughout Scripture—seen in Nehemiah 9:20 and, in the New Covenant, John 16:13.

in the path chosen for him

• The Hebrew concept behind “path” pictures a well-trodden road. God has a definite route prepared, not a vague set of options (Psalm 23:3; Ephesians 2:10).

• “Chosen” underscores God’s sovereignty. Our steps are ordered, not random (Proverbs 16:9; Jeremiah 10:23).

• Walking this path brings peace and purpose, even when circumstances are unclear (Isaiah 30:21; Proverbs 3:5-6).

summary

Psalm 25:12 assures that anyone who lives in reverent awe of the LORD receives personal, ongoing direction from Him. God Himself becomes the Teacher, leading each obedient believer along the specific, sovereignly chosen way that fulfills His good purposes.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(12) What man is he . . .?--For the emphatic question compare Psalm 34:12.

The way that he shall choose.--Rather, the way that he should choose--i.e., the way of right choice. The LXX. and Vulg., however, refer it to God--"the way in which He took delight."

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 12. - What man is he that feareth the Lord? Once more we have a series of reflections (vers. 12-15) - first, with respect to the God-fearing man. Every such man shall have favour shown him by God - him shall he (i.e. God) teach in the way that he shall choose. This is, of course, the right way - the way of God's commandments (Psalm 119:30, 173). God shall make his way plain to the God-fearing man.
