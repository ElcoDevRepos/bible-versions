He provides food for the animals
This phrase highlights God's providential care over all creation, emphasizing His role as the sustainer of life. In the biblical context, God's provision is a recurring theme, seen in Genesis 1:30 where He provides every green plant for food to all creatures. This reflects the order and care God established in creation. The mention of animals can also be seen as a reminder of God's covenant with all living creatures, as noted in Genesis 9:9-10 after the flood. Theologically, this provision underscores God's omnipotence and benevolence, ensuring that all His creation is sustained. It also serves as a reminder of the interconnectedness of life, where God's care for animals reflects His care for humanity.

and for the young ravens when they call
Ravens are often seen in the Bible as symbols of God's provision despite their unclean status according to Levitical law (Leviticus 11:15). In Job 38:41, God is described as providing for the raven's young, reinforcing the idea that God's care extends even to creatures that might be overlooked or deemed insignificant. The calling of the young ravens can be seen as a metaphor for dependence and need, illustrating that God hears and responds to the cries of His creation. This can be connected to Jesus' teaching in Matthew 6:26, where He assures that if God feeds the birds, He will surely care for His people. The raven, often associated with desolation and wilderness, becomes a powerful symbol of God's provision in the most unlikely circumstances, pointing to His faithfulness and mercy.

Teaching Points
God's Comprehensive Care
God's provision is not limited to humans; it extends to all of His creation. This should remind us of His omnipotence and omnipresence.

Value of the Seemingly Insignificant
Even creatures like young ravens, which might be considered unimportant, are cared for by God. This teaches us that no one is insignificant in God's eyes.

Trust in God's Provision
Just as God provides for the animals, He will provide for us. We are encouraged to trust in His provision and not be anxious about our needs.

God's Sovereignty Over Creation
God's ability to provide for all creatures demonstrates His sovereignty and control over the universe. This should lead us to worship and reverence Him.

Call to Stewardship
Recognizing God's care for creation should inspire us to be good stewards of the environment and the resources He has entrusted to us.

What Does Psalm 147:9 Mean
He provides food

- The verse opens by putting the spotlight on God as the active Giver: “He provides food” (Psalm 147:9).

- His provision is constant, personal, and generous (Psalm 145:15-16; Matthew 6:11).

- This line reminds us that abundance flows from His character, not from impersonal forces of nature (James 1:17).

for the animals

- God’s care is not limited to people; every living creature benefits from His hand (Psalm 104:27-28).

- From creation onward, He assigned vegetation “to every beast of the earth” (Genesis 1:30), showing that animals are within His covenant of care.

- His compassion even shaped Jonah’s lesson about Nineveh: “and many cattle as well” (Jonah 4:11).

and for the young ravens

- Ravens—unclean birds in Israel’s dietary law—illustrate that God’s kindness reaches the overlooked and undervalued (Leviticus 11:15; Job 38:41).

- Jesus echoed this: “Consider the ravens… God feeds them” (Luke 12:24).

- God once used ravens to feed Elijah (1 Kings 17:4-6), highlighting their surprising role in His provision plan.

when they call

- The fledgling ravens’ cries are answered, portraying God’s attentiveness (Psalm 34:15).

- He responds the moment need is voiced (Jeremiah 33:3; Matthew 7:11).

- The line invites trust: if He hears the instinctive squawk of a bird, He certainly hears the articulate prayers of His people (Psalm 50:15).

summary

Psalm 147:9 presents a vivid snapshot of God’s sustaining grace. He personally supplies nourishment, cares for all creatures large and small, singles out even despised ravens, and does so in direct response to their cries. If He is that faithful to the animal kingdom, we can rest assured He will not overlook those who bear His image.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(9) Comp. Psalm 104:14; Psalm 145:15; Job 38:41; Luke 12:24.

The proper attitude towards one who is thus "great to grant as mighty to make," is not conceit of wisdom and strength, but humble dependence and trust.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 9. - He giveth to the beast his food (comp. Psalm 104:27; Psalm 145:15, 16). The constant supplies of their own proper food to all classes of animals are among the principal proofs of God's power and goodness. And to the young ravens which cry. Even the unclean raven, with his harsh croak and inelegant form, is not neglected (comp. Luke 12:24, "God feedeth them").
