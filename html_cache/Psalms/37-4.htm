Delight yourself in the LORD
This phrase emphasizes the importance of finding joy and satisfaction in a relationship with God. In the biblical context, "delight" implies a deep, abiding pleasure and commitment. The Hebrew word used here suggests a sense of softness or pliability, indicating a heart open to God's influence. This concept is echoed in other scriptures, such as Psalm 1:2, where the righteous person delights in the law of the Lord. Historically, the Israelites were called to find their joy in God rather than in worldly pursuits, a theme prevalent throughout the Old Testament. This delight is not merely emotional but involves a commitment to God's will and ways, aligning one's life with His purposes.

and He will give you the desires of your heart
This promise is contingent upon the first phrase, suggesting that when one's delight is truly in the Lord, their desires will naturally align with His will. The "desires of your heart" are not simply any personal wishes but those that are in harmony with God's character and plans. This reflects a transformation of the heart, where one's desires become reflective of God's desires. In the New Testament, Jesus speaks to this alignment in John 15:7, where abiding in Him and His words leads to answered prayers. Theologically, this indicates a deep trust in God's provision and timing, recognizing that He knows what is best for His children. The fulfillment of these desires is not always immediate or in the way one might expect, but it is assured for those who truly delight in the Lord.

Teaching Points
Delight as a Priority
Delighting in the Lord should be a primary focus of a believer's life. It involves finding joy and satisfaction in God's presence, character, and promises.

Alignment of Desires
When we truly delight in the Lord, our desires begin to align with His will. This means that the desires of our heart become those that are pleasing to Him.

Active Pursuit of God
Delighting in the Lord is an active pursuit. It involves engaging with Scripture, prayer, worship, and fellowship with other believers.

Trust in God's Timing
Trust that God knows the best timing and way to fulfill the desires of your heart. His understanding and plans are perfect.

Transformation of the Heart
As we delight in God, He transforms our hearts, making us more like Christ and aligning our desires with His purposes.

What Does Psalm 37:4 Mean
Delight yourself

– The psalm opens with an invitation, not a suggestion. We are told to take active pleasure in the Lord, embracing Him as our highest joy.

• Psalm 1:2 reminds us that the righteous person’s “delight is in the Law of the LORD; and on His law he meditates day and night.”

• Psalm 16:11 promises that in God’s presence “there is fullness of joy.”

• Choosing delight is possible regardless of circumstances (Philippians 4:4), because it rests on who God is, not on what is happening around us.

in the LORD

– Our delight has a specific object: the covenant-keeping LORD.

• Jeremiah 9:23-24 urges us to boast not in wisdom, might, or riches, “but let him who boasts boast in this, that he understands and knows Me.”

• Psalm 73:25-26 echoes the same focus: “Whom have I in heaven but You? And on earth I desire no one besides You.”

• Fixing our joy on Him anchors us when circumstances shift, because “Jesus Christ is the same yesterday and today and forever” (Hebrews 13:8).

and He will give you

– God Himself commits to act. This is not mere sentiment; it is a promise from the One who cannot lie.

• Matthew 7:11 says, “how much more will your Father in heaven give good things to those who ask Him!”

• Romans 8:32 boldly argues that if God did not spare His own Son, He will “freely give us all things” we truly need.

• His giving is generous and wise, never capricious; every gift is “from above, coming down from the Father of lights” (James 1:17).

the desires of your heart

– When we delight in Him, our inner longings are shaped by His character.

• Ezekiel 36:26 speaks of a new heart God places within His people, aligning their desires with His will.

• John 15:7 ties answered prayer to abiding in Christ: “ask whatever you wish, and it will be done for you.” The wish grows out of abiding communion.

• Psalm 145:19 affirms, “He fulfills the desire of those who fear Him; He hears their cry and saves them.” God is pleased to meet desires that reflect His goodness.

– This is not a blank check for selfish cravings; it is a promise that as we find our greatest pleasure in the Lord, He plants, purifies, and then grants the very desires He creates within us.

summary

Psalm 37:4 calls us to make God Himself our chief delight. When we do, He faithfully responds by granting desires that have been transformed through relationship with Him. The verse is both an invitation to deeper fellowship and a guarantee that every God-shaped longing will ultimately be satisfied, either now or in the fullness of eternity.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 4. - Delight thyself also in the Lord. Draw from communion with God all that inward intensity of joy which it is capable of giving. And he shall give thee the desires of thine heart. God will then grant thee all thy desires, and make thee perfectly happy.
