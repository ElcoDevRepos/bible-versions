The testimonies You have laid down
This phrase refers to the divine laws and decrees given by God, often seen as a reflection of His character and will. In the context of the Old Testament, "testimonies" can be understood as the covenantal stipulations given to Israel, such as those found in the Torah. These laws were not merely legal requirements but were intended to guide the Israelites in living a life that was pleasing to God. The use of "laid down" suggests a sense of permanence and authority, indicating that these testimonies are not subject to change or human alteration. This aligns with the biblical theme of God's word being eternal and unchanging, as seen in passages like Isaiah 40:8, which states, "The grass withers, the flower fades, but the word of our God will stand forever."

are righteous
The righteousness of God's testimonies underscores their moral perfection and justice. In the biblical context, righteousness is often associated with God's nature and His actions. The laws and decrees given by God are a reflection of His righteous character, and they serve as a standard for human conduct. This righteousness is not merely legalistic but is deeply rooted in the covenant relationship between God and His people. The concept of righteousness is central to the biblical narrative, as seen in passages like Psalm 19:9, which declares, "The fear of the LORD is clean, enduring forever; the judgments of the LORD are true and righteous altogether."

and altogether faithful
This phrase emphasizes the reliability and trustworthiness of God's testimonies. The faithfulness of God's word is a recurring theme in Scripture, highlighting that what God has spoken will surely come to pass. This faithfulness is not only about the accuracy of God's promises but also about His unwavering commitment to His covenant people. The idea of God's faithfulness is echoed in Lamentations 3:22-23, which states, "The steadfast love of the LORD never ceases; his mercies never come to an end; they are new every morning; great is your faithfulness." In the New Testament, Jesus Christ is seen as the ultimate fulfillment of God's faithful promises, as He embodies the truth and reliability of God's word (John 1:14, Revelation 19:11).

The nation to whom the testimonies were originally given, serving as a context for understanding the importance of God's laws.

Refers to the Torah, the first five books of the Bible, which contain God's commandments and instructions.

Teaching Points
The Righteousness of God's Word
God's testimonies are inherently righteous, reflecting His perfect nature. As believers, we are called to trust and uphold these righteous standards in our lives.

The Faithfulness of God's Testimonies
The faithfulness of God's word assures us that His promises and commands are reliable. We can depend on His guidance in every aspect of life.

Living by God's Standards
As followers of Christ, we are encouraged to align our lives with God's righteous testimonies, allowing them to shape our character and decisions.

The Role of Scripture in Spiritual Growth
Engaging with God's word is essential for spiritual maturity. It equips us for every good work and helps us discern truth from falsehood.

The Eternal Nature of God's Word
God's testimonies are not only relevant for the past but remain applicable today. They provide a foundation for living a life that honors God.

What Does Psalm 119:138 Mean
The testimonies

• Scripture often calls God’s written words His “testimonies,” emphasizing that they witness to who He is and what He has done (Psalm 19:7; Revelation 1:2).

• These testimonies are not human opinions; they come straight from God, so they carry divine authority (2 Timothy 3:16).

• Because God Himself is truth (John 14:6), His testimonies provide a dependable measuring line for belief and behavior (Psalm 119:24).

You have laid down

• The verse highlights that God is the One who “laid down” or appointed these testimonies, underscoring intentionality and permanence (Isaiah 40:8).

• Just as a builder securely places a cornerstone (Isaiah 28:16), God has fixed His word in place, giving believers a foundation that cannot shift (Matthew 7:24-25).

• Since the Source is perfect, the standard He sets is unchangeable (Malachi 3:6).

are righteous

• God’s testimonies perfectly align with His own character, which is righteous in every respect (Psalm 145:17).

• They expose sin, rescue from error, and point the way to holy living (Romans 7:12; Psalm 119:142).

• When life feels unfair, these righteous testimonies reassure us that God’s moral order still governs the universe (Psalm 97:2).

and altogether faithful

• “Altogether” stresses completeness; not one word of God fails (Joshua 23:14).

• Faithful means dependable, trustworthy, proven over time (Psalm 33:4).

• Practical implications:

– God’s promises can be claimed with confidence (2 Corinthians 1:20).

– His warnings should be taken seriously (Hebrews 2:1-3).

– Daily obedience rests on the certainty that His word will never mislead (Proverbs 3:5-6).

summary

Psalm 119:138 assures us that every word God has planted in Scripture stands firm—righteous in character and utterly reliable in practice. Because these testimonies come from the never-changing Lord, they provide an unshakable guide for faith, conduct, and hope.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(138) Thy testimonies.--Better, Thou hast commanded Thy testimonies in righteousness and very faithfulness. But unquestionably another arrangement of the text of these two verses is correct. It takes the verb commandest with Psalm 119:137, and gets the simple and obvious "righteous art Thou, O Lord, and upright in the judgments which Thou hast commanded. Thy testimonies are righteous, and faithful to the uttermost" (Burgess). (See Psalm 7:6 and Psalm 119:144.)

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 138. - Thy testimonies that thou hast commanded are righteous and very faithful; literally, righteousness and very faithfulness (see the Revised Version). "Harsh and severe as thy testimonies may seem, they are all thoroughly for man's highest good" (Kay).
