He who admonishes the nations
This phrase highlights God's sovereignty over all nations, emphasizing His role as a moral authority. Throughout the Old Testament, God is depicted as the judge of nations, as seen in the prophetic books like Isaiah and Jeremiah. The admonishment of nations can be seen in historical events such as the fall of Babylon and the Assyrian Empire, where God's judgment was executed. This phrase underscores the belief that God holds all nations accountable to His standards, reflecting His universal authority and justice.

Does He not discipline?
Discipline is a recurring theme in Scripture, often associated with God's love and desire for correction and growth, as seen in Proverbs 3:11-12. The discipline of God is not punitive but corrective, aiming to bring about repentance and righteousness. Hebrews 12:5-11 further elaborates on this, explaining that God's discipline is a sign of His fatherly love. This phrase reassures believers that God's actions are purposeful and aimed at the ultimate good of His people.

He who teaches man
God as a teacher is a central theme in the Bible, with wisdom literature like Proverbs and Ecclesiastes emphasizing the importance of divine instruction. The role of God as a teacher is also evident in the life of Jesus Christ, who taught with authority and imparted divine wisdom to His followers. This phrase suggests that God imparts knowledge and understanding to humanity, guiding them in truth and righteousness.

Does He lack knowledge?
This rhetorical question affirms God's omniscience, a fundamental attribute of His nature. Scriptures such as Psalm 139 and Isaiah 40:28 highlight God's infinite understanding and knowledge. The question challenges any notion that God could be unaware or uninformed, reinforcing the belief that His judgments and teachings are based on perfect knowledge. This assurance of God's omniscience provides comfort to believers, knowing that He is fully aware of all circumstances and needs.

Persons / Places / Events
1. God (Yahweh)
The central figure in this verse, God is portrayed as the ultimate teacher and disciplinarian, emphasizing His omniscience and authority over nations and individuals.

2. Nations
Refers to the collective groups of people across the earth. In the context of this Psalm, nations are subject to God's admonishment and discipline, highlighting His sovereignty over all human affairs.

3. Man (Humanity)
Represents individual human beings who are recipients of God's teaching and discipline. This underscores the personal relationship between God and each person.
Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty Over Nations
God is not only concerned with individuals but also with entire nations. His discipline extends to all levels of human society, reminding us of His ultimate authority.

The Purpose of Divine Discipline
God's discipline is not punitive but corrective. It is designed to guide us back to righteousness and align us with His will.

The Omniscience of God
God, who teaches man, possesses complete knowledge. We can trust His guidance and correction because He knows what is best for us.

Personal Growth Through Correction
Embrace God's discipline as an opportunity for personal growth. It is a sign of His love and commitment to our spiritual development.

Responding to God's Teaching
Be open to learning from God in all circumstances. His teachings are rooted in His infinite wisdom and are meant to lead us to a deeper understanding of His truth.

What Does Psalm 94:10 Mean
He who admonishes the nations

“He who admonishes the nations…” (Psalm 94:10)

• The psalmist first fixes our eyes on God’s global authority. Nations rise and fall at His word (Psalm 22:28; Isaiah 40:15).

• Admonish carries the idea of rebuke with corrective intent. Like a loving Father, the Lord confronts collective evil—whether it is Egypt’s oppression (Exodus 7–12), Assyria’s arrogance (Isaiah 10:5-19), or any power that defies His rule (Psalm 2:1-6).

• Acts 17:30-31 shows Paul echoing this truth: God “now commands all people everywhere to repent, for He has set a day when He will judge the world with justice.” Every kingdom is accountable to Him.

• This opening phrase assures believers that international chaos is never outside God’s oversight; He still speaks, warns, and intervenes.

does He not discipline?

“…does He not discipline?” (Psalm 94:10)

• The question assumes an emphatic “Yes!” Hebrews 12:5-11 reminds us that the Lord disciplines those He loves, “for our good, so that we may share in His holiness.”

• Proverbs 3:11-12 frames discipline as a sign of sonship, not rejection.

• God’s corrective work is purposeful:

– To restrain sin (Jeremiah 30:11)

– To produce righteousness (Hebrews 12:11)

– To restore fellowship (Revelation 3:19)

• Whether His hand falls on nations or individuals, the goal is repentance and renewed obedience, never mere punishment.

He who teaches man

“He who teaches man…” (Psalm 94:10)

• The psalmist shifts from nations to each person, affirming that God is our ultimate Teacher.

• Psalm 32:8 voices God’s promise: “I will instruct you and teach you the way you should go.”

• Isaiah 48:17 calls Him “your Redeemer…the One who teaches you for your own benefit.”

• Today He instructs through:

– His written Word (2 Timothy 3:16-17)

– The Holy Spirit (John 14:26)

– The wise counsel of mature believers (Colossians 3:16)

• Because the Lord Himself is teaching, His lessons are never unclear or irrelevant; they perfectly match our needs and His purposes.

does He lack knowledge?

“…does He lack knowledge?” (Psalm 94:10)

• Another rhetorical question—answer: absolutely not. God’s omniscience is complete and effortless.

• Psalm 139:1-4 shows He knows every thought before it forms; Hebrews 4:13 says “nothing in all creation is hidden from His sight.”

• Psalm 147:5 celebrates, “His understanding has no limit,” while 1 John 3:20 reassures hurting hearts that “He knows everything.”

• Because His knowledge is total:

– His judgments are never mistaken.

– His discipline is perfectly calibrated.

– His teaching is flawlessly timed.

• We can therefore trust His timing, wisdom, and methods even when circumstances seem bewildering.

summary

Psalm 94:10 strings together four piercing questions to underline one majestic truth: the Lord who governs nations also shapes each individual life with perfect wisdom. He corrects collective rebellion and personal sin alike, teaching us paths of righteousness through flawless knowledge. Every act of His discipline or instruction flows from absolute authority and unfailing love, inviting us to repent, learn, and rest secure under His sovereign care.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(10) He that chastiseth.--Or, He who instructeth. The thought to some extent anticipates St. Paul's teaching about the divine education of the heathen, in Romans 1.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 10. - He that chastiseth the heathen, shall not he correct? i.e. if God does not leave even the heathen without rebukes and chastisements, shall he not much more punish those among his own people who do amiss? He that teacheth man knowledge, shall not he know? Our version supposes an ellipse, which it fills up with great boldness, producing a very excellent sense. But the insertion made does not appear necessary (see the Revised Version).
