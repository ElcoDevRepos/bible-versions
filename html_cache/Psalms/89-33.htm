But I will not withdraw My loving devotion from him
This phrase emphasizes God's steadfast love, often translated as "chesed" in Hebrew, which signifies a loyal, covenantal love. In the context of Psalm 89, this refers to God's promise to David and his descendants, highlighting the Davidic Covenant found in 2 Samuel 7:12-16. This covenant assures that David's lineage will endure forever, ultimately fulfilled in Jesus Christ, the Son of David (Matthew 1:1). The concept of "loving devotion" is central to God's character, as seen in Exodus 34:6-7, where God describes Himself as "abounding in steadfast love and faithfulness."

nor ever betray My faithfulness
This phrase underscores God's unwavering reliability and truthfulness. The term "faithfulness" in Hebrew is "emunah," which conveys firmness and stability. God's faithfulness is a recurring theme throughout Scripture, as seen in Lamentations 3:22-23, where His mercies are described as new every morning. The assurance that God will not betray His faithfulness is crucial for understanding His unchanging nature, as stated in Malachi 3:6, "For I the LORD do not change." This promise is ultimately fulfilled in Christ, who is the embodiment of God's faithfulness (Hebrews 13:8).

Persons / Places / Events
1. Ethan the Ezrahite
The author of Psalm 89, who is traditionally believed to be a wise man during the time of Solomon. He is credited with writing this psalm, which reflects on God's covenant with David and His faithfulness.

2. David
The psalm reflects on God's covenant with King David, promising that his line would endure forever. This covenant is central to the themes of faithfulness and loving devotion in the psalm.

3. God's Covenant
The central event in this psalm is God's covenant with David, which is a promise of enduring love and faithfulness. This covenant is a key theme throughout the psalm.
Teaching Points
God's Unchanging Nature
God's character is consistent and reliable. His loving devotion and faithfulness are not subject to change based on human actions.

The Assurance of God's Promises
Believers can trust in the promises of God, knowing that He will not withdraw His love or betray His faithfulness.

Covenant Relationship
Understanding our relationship with God as a covenant helps us grasp the depth of His commitment to us, similar to His covenant with David.

Faithfulness in Our Lives
As God is faithful to us, we are called to reflect His faithfulness in our relationships and commitments.

Hope in God's Faithfulness
In times of doubt or difficulty, we can find hope and reassurance in the knowledge that God's faithfulness endures.

What Does Psalm 89:33 Mean
But I will not withdraw

The verse begins with a strong adversative “But,” signaling God’s deliberate choice to act differently from what people might expect when sin or failure enters the picture (see Psalm 89:30-32, where discipline is promised).

• God reserves the right to discipline yet refuses to sever the relationship.

• Cross references: 2 Samuel 7:14-15 “I will discipline him…but My loving devotion will never be removed”; Hebrews 12:10-11 shows discipline without abandonment.

My loving devotion

“Loving devotion” (steadfast love, covenant love) is more than sentiment; it is God’s binding commitment to keep every promise He made to David, ultimately fulfilled in Christ.

• This love is rooted in God’s character, not David’s performance (Psalm 103:17; Lamentations 3:22).

• It guarantees that the covenant line will survive setbacks, exile, even apparent silence (Isaiah 54:10).

from him

“Him” first points to David, then to each king in David’s line, and finally to the Messiah Himself. Because Christ is the ultimate Son of David, God’s devotion reaches everyone united to Him by faith (Acts 13:34; Galatians 3:29).

• Practical takeaway: believers rest in the same covenant security granted to David’s greater Son (John 10:28-29).

nor ever betray

God not only keeps love in place; He vows never to act deceitfully toward His covenant partner. Betrayal is impossible for the One who “cannot lie” (Titus 1:2).

• Numbers 23:19 “God is not a man, that He should lie.”

• 2 Timothy 2:13 “If we are faithless, He remains faithful.”

• This assurance anchors prayer and worship when circumstances seem to contradict the promise (Psalm 89:46-49).

My faithfulness

Faithfulness is God’s unchanging reliability to do exactly what He has pledged.

• Romans 11:29 “God’s gifts and His call are irrevocable.”

• Deuteronomy 7:9 links love and faithfulness as twin attributes.

• For David’s line, faithfulness means a throne established forever, culminating in Jesus’ eternal reign (Luke 1:32-33; Revelation 11:15).

summary

Psalm 89:33 declares that even when discipline falls, God’s covenant love and faithfulness remain immovable. He will never pull back His steadfast love, never violate His own faithfulness, and never renounce the promises bound up in Christ. That assurance fuels confidence, repentance, and hope for every believer who trusts the God whose “loving devotion endures forever.”

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 33. - Nevertheless my loving kindness will I not utterly take from him, nor suffer my faithfulness to fail. Compare the original promise (2 Samuel 7:15); and see also 1 Kings 11:12, 13, 34-39; 1 Kings 15:4, 5, etc. The seed of David was not allowed to fail, but was continued on, until, in the fulness of time, there was born into the world, of David's seed and in David's city, One in whom all the promises made to David could be, and were, accomplished in their utmost fulness.
