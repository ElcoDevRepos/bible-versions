The eyes of all look to You
This phrase emphasizes the universal dependence on God as the provider. In the biblical context, it reflects the understanding that all creation, not just humanity, relies on God for sustenance. This is consistent with the creation narrative in Genesis, where God is depicted as the sustainer of life. The phrase also echoes the sentiment found in Psalm 104:27-28, where all creatures look to God for provision. It highlights the trust and expectation that God will meet the needs of His creation, a theme that runs throughout the Psalms.

and You give them their food
This part of the verse underscores God's role as the provider of daily needs. In the historical and cultural context of ancient Israel, agriculture was central to life, and the dependence on God for rain and harvest was a constant reality. The provision of food is a recurring theme in Scripture, seen in the manna provided to the Israelites in the wilderness (Exodus 16) and in Jesus' teaching on prayer, where He instructs His followers to ask for their "daily bread" (Matthew 6:11). This phrase also points to God's faithfulness and generosity, as He provides not just for the righteous but for all His creation.

in season
The phrase "in season" indicates God's perfect timing and order in creation. It reflects the agricultural cycles that were crucial to the livelihood of the people in biblical times. This timing is seen as part of God's divine plan, where He orchestrates the seasons and ensures that the earth yields its produce at the right time. Ecclesiastes 3:1 speaks to this divine timing, stating that there is a season for everything. This concept is also prophetic, pointing to the ultimate fulfillment of God's promises in His perfect timing, as seen in the coming of Christ and the establishment of His kingdom.

Teaching Points
Dependence on God
Recognize that all creation is dependent on God for sustenance. This verse reminds us to trust in God's timing and provision.

God's Faithfulness
Reflect on God's faithfulness in providing for our needs. Just as He provides for the animals, He will provide for us.

Gratitude for Provision
Cultivate a heart of gratitude for God's provision. Acknowledge His role in meeting our daily needs.

Trust in God's Timing
Understand that God provides "in due season." Trust His timing, even when it doesn't align with our own expectations.

Prayer for Provision
Encourage regular prayer, asking God to meet our needs and the needs of others, while also thanking Him for His ongoing provision.

What Does Psalm 145:15 Mean
The eyes of all look to You

• The verse begins with a picture of universal expectation—every creature, not just humanity, instinctively turns toward the Lord for sustenance. Psalm 104:27 echoes this same posture: “All creatures look to You to give them their food in due season.”

• By saying “all,” David highlights God’s lordship over the entire created order. This aligns with Acts 17:25, which reminds us that He “Himself gives everyone life and breath and everything else.” Nothing lives independently of His care.

• The phrase “look to You” suggests hope and trust. Jesus picks up this theme in Matthew 6:26, urging His followers to observe the birds that rely on the Father’s provision.

• Our daily routines can dull this sense of dependence, but verses like Job 38:41 pull back the curtain, showing that even young ravens cry to God for food.

You give them their food in season

• God does more than inspire hope; He acts. “You give” places Him at the center of every harvest and paycheck. James 1:17 affirms that “every good and perfect gift is from above.”

• “Their food” speaks of specific, personal provision. Philippians 4:19 promises that God meets “every need,” not in a generic way but tailored to each creature and believer.

• “In season” teaches timing. Deuteronomy 11:14 describes God sending rain “in its season,” demonstrating His wisdom in perfectly timed supply. We are called to trust His schedule, resisting the urge to doubt during waiting periods.

• Psalm 104:28 further illustrates this timing: “When You give it to them, they gather it up.” The cadence of gathering after God’s giving underscores our role—receiving gratefully and working responsibly.

summary

Psalm 145:15 paints a simple yet profound reality: every living thing depends on God’s timely, tailored provision. Our eyes—like the eyes of all creation—should remain fixed on Him, trusting that at the right moment He will supply exactly what is needed.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 15. - The eyes of all wait upon thee; and thou givest them their meat in due season (cf. Psalm 104:21, 27; Psalm 136:25; Psalm 147:9). The constant supply of all living creatures with their necessary food is little less than a standing miracle.
