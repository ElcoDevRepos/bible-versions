Remember the wonders He has done
This phrase calls believers to recall the miraculous acts of God throughout history. In the context of the Israelites, this includes the plagues in Egypt, the parting of the Red Sea, and the provision of manna in the wilderness. These wonders serve as a testament to God's power and faithfulness. The act of remembering is a recurring theme in Scripture, emphasizing the importance of recalling God's past deeds to strengthen faith and trust in His future promises. This aligns with Deuteronomy 7:18-19, where the Israelites are encouraged to remember God's mighty acts as a source of courage.

His marvels
The term "marvels" refers to extraordinary events that reveal God's divine intervention and sovereignty. These acts are not only historical but also serve as a foreshadowing of the ultimate marvel in the New Testament: the resurrection of Jesus Christ. The marvels of God are meant to inspire awe and worship, reminding believers of His omnipotence and the miraculous nature of His works. In the broader biblical narrative, marvels often accompany God's deliverance and salvation, as seen in the miracles performed by Jesus during His earthly ministry.

and the judgments He has pronounced
This phrase highlights God's righteous judgments, which are both a warning and a guide for His people. In the Old Testament, God's judgments often came in the form of prophetic declarations and were fulfilled through historical events, such as the exile of Israel. These judgments serve as a reminder of God's holiness and justice, as well as His covenantal relationship with His people. They also point forward to the final judgment, as described in Revelation, where God's justice will be fully realized. The judgments are a call to repentance and obedience, underscoring the seriousness of sin and the need for redemption through Christ.

Persons / Places / Events
1. God (Yahweh)
The central figure in this verse, God is the one whose wonders, marvels, and judgments are to be remembered. He is the covenant-keeping God of Israel.

2. Israel
The nation of Israel is the primary audience of this Psalm. They are called to remember God's acts throughout their history.

3. Wonders and Marvels
Refers to the miraculous acts God performed, such as the plagues in Egypt, the parting of the Red Sea, and provision in the wilderness.

4. Judgments
These are the decrees and decisions God made, often seen in His deliverance of Israel and His justice against their enemies.

5. Covenant History
The historical context of God's relationship with Israel, including the Abrahamic, Mosaic, and Davidic covenants.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Remembrance
Remembering God's past deeds strengthens faith and trust in His future promises.

God's Faithfulness in History
Reflecting on God's actions in history assures us of His unchanging nature and reliability.

The Role of Testimony
Sharing testimonies of God's wonders encourages others and builds a community of faith.

Judgments as Acts of Justice
God's judgments are not only punitive but also protective and redemptive for His people.

Personal Reflection and Gratitude
Regularly recalling God's work in our lives fosters a heart of gratitude and worship.

What Does Psalm 105:5 Mean
Remember the wonders He has done

• “Remember” is a call to active, deliberate recollection. God’s people are not to let His works slip into the background of memory (Deuteronomy 4:9; Psalm 77:11–12).

• “The wonders” look back to concrete acts—plagues on Egypt, the Red Sea crossing, manna in the wilderness (Psalm 105:26–41). They also reach forward, urging every generation to rehearse fresh testimonies of God’s faithfulness (Joshua 4:6–7).

• This remembering fuels faith for present challenges. When David faced Goliath, he recalled earlier deliverances (1 Samuel 17:37). The pattern is the same for us: yesterday’s wonders anchor today’s obedience.

His marvels

• “Marvels” highlights the extraordinary, jaw-dropping nature of God’s deeds. They are not random surprises but purposeful displays of His character—power, mercy, covenant love (Exodus 15:11; Psalm 98:1).

• Marvels stir worship. After Elijah’s fire-from-heaven moment, the people fell on their faces and cried, “The LORD, He is God!” (1 Kings 18:39).

• They also serve as signs to the nations. Rahab heard about the Red Sea miracle and concluded Israel’s God is “God in heaven above and on earth beneath” (Joshua 2:10–11). Our testimony of God’s marvels still opens hearts to the gospel today.

and the judgments He has pronounced

• “Judgments” refers to God’s decisive interventions that vindicate righteousness and expose sin—plagues on Egypt (Exodus 12:12), the drowning of Pharaoh’s army (Exodus 14:30), and later acts like the exile (2 Kings 17:18).

• Such judgments warn against unbelief (1 Corinthians 10:5–11) and reassure believers that evil will not finally triumph (Revelation 19:1–2).

• Remembering them balances our view of God: He is both Savior and Judge (Isaiah 33:22). Awe keeps familiarity from breeding contempt, and gratitude keeps awe from sliding into terror.

summary

Psalm 105:5 urges God’s people to keep three truths in constant view: His mighty acts, their breathtaking quality, and His righteous verdicts. Active remembrance fuels faith, sparks worship, and maintains holy fear—so we live today with confidence that the God who worked wonders, marvels, and judgments still rules and keeps covenant with us.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 5. - Remember his marvellous works that he hath done (see ver. 2). These "wondrous works" are apter than anything else to stir up the heart to gratitude and thankfulness to God; and therefore they naturally lead on to the utterance of praise and thanksgiving. His wonders; or, "miracles" - τὰ τέρατα αὐτοῦ, LXX. - such as those touched on in vers. 27-36 and 39-41. And the judgments of his mouth. His sentences upon sinners, as upon the Egyptians (vers. 28, et seqq.) and upon the Canaanites (vers. 11, 44).
