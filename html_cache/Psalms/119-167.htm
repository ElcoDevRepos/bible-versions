I obey Your testimonies
The psalmist expresses a commitment to following God's decrees, which are often referred to as "testimonies." In the context of ancient Israel, testimonies were the laws and commandments given by God, particularly those inscribed on the tablets of the covenant. This obedience reflects a covenantal relationship, where adherence to God's laws is a sign of faithfulness. The concept of obedience is central throughout the Bible, as seen in Deuteronomy 6:17, which emphasizes the importance of keeping God's commandments. Obedience is not merely a legalistic duty but a heartfelt response to God's revealed will, as seen in the life of Jesus Christ, who perfectly obeyed the Father (John 14:31).

and love them greatly
The psalmist not only obeys God's testimonies but also loves them deeply. This love for God's law is a recurring theme in Psalm 119, highlighting the psalmist's delight and reverence for divine instruction. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, love for the law was synonymous with devotion to God Himself, as the law was seen as a reflection of God's character and will. This love is not superficial but profound, indicating a deep-seated affection and appreciation for the guidance and wisdom found in God's commandments. The New Testament echoes this sentiment, where love for God and His commandments is intertwined (1 John 5:3). This love is also a type of Christ's love for the Father and His will, as Jesus declared His love for the Father through His obedience (John 14:31).

Teaching Points
Love and Obedience
True love for God is demonstrated through obedience to His Word. The psalmist's love for God's testimonies is not passive but active, leading to a life of obedience.

The Heart's Affection
The Hebrew word for "love" ('ahav) implies a deep emotional attachment. Our affection for God's Word should be heartfelt and genuine, influencing our actions and decisions.

Commitment to God's Word
The psalmist's commitment to God's testimonies serves as a model for believers. We are called to prioritize God's Word in our daily lives, allowing it to guide and shape us.

The Joy of Obedience
Obedience to God's commandments is not burdensome but a source of joy and fulfillment. The psalmist finds great delight in following God's law, which should inspire us to find joy in our obedience.

Consistency in Devotion
The psalmist's consistent love and obedience to God's testimonies challenge us to remain steadfast in our faith, even amidst life's challenges.

What Does Psalm 119:167 Mean
I obey

• The psalmist speaks in the first person, showing personal responsibility; obedience is not delegated (Psalm 119:60).

• Obedience is active: “But be doers of the word, and not hearers only” (James 1:22).

• It springs from faith, not mere ritual—Abraham “obeyed and went” (Hebrews 11:8).

• Obedience is proof of devotion: “If you love Me, you will keep My commandments” (John 14:15).

Your testimonies

• “Testimonies” highlights God’s revealed, trustworthy witness about Himself (Psalm 19:7).

• They include commands, promises, warnings—everything God has made known (Deuteronomy 6:17).

• Because Scripture is perfect, the believer treats each word as authoritative (2 Timothy 3:16).

• The personal pronoun “Your” reminds us the Bible is God-breathed, not human opinion (2 Peter 1:21).

And love

• Obedience without love drifts into legalism; love fuels heartfelt surrender (1 John 5:3).

• Love for God’s word is love for God Himself: “You shall love the LORD your God with all your heart” (Deuteronomy 6:5; Mark 12:30).

• Love deepens with exposure—regular reading fans affection into flame (Jeremiah 15:16).

Them greatly

• “Greatly” raises the bar from casual respect to passionate delight (Psalm 119:97).

• Overflowing love brings joy: “I delight in Your commandments, because I love them” (Psalm 119:47).

• Such love endures pressure; even when persecuted, the psalmist clings to God’s word (Psalm 119:161).

• Valuing Scripture above all else mirrors Paul’s cry: “I consider everything a loss compared to the surpassing worth of knowing Christ” (Philippians 3:8).

summary

Psalm 119:167 portrays a believer who personally, actively, and lovingly obeys God’s flawless testimonies, valuing them above every competing claim. Great love fuels wholehearted obedience, and wholehearted obedience in turn magnifies that love—a cycle of devotion God desires for every disciple.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 167. - My soul hath kept thy testimonies. In will and intention I have always been faithful unto thee; I have striven to keep all thy commandments. And I love them exceedingly (comp. vers. 97, 127, 159, etc.).
