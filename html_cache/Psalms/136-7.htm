Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
He made the great lights—
This phrase acknowledges God as the Creator of the celestial bodies, referring to the sun, moon, and stars. In the biblical context, this creation is first described in Genesis 1:14-19, where God sets the lights in the sky to separate day from night and to serve as signs for seasons, days, and years. The "great lights" symbolize God's power and sovereignty over the universe. In ancient Near Eastern cultures, celestial bodies were often deified, but the Bible emphasizes that they are creations of the one true God, not deities themselves. This distinction underscores the monotheistic belief central to the faith. The creation of these lights also points to God's order and purpose in creation, reflecting His wisdom and care for the world.

His loving devotion endures forever.
This refrain, repeated throughout Psalm 136, highlights the enduring and steadfast nature of God's love, often translated as "lovingkindness" or "mercy." The Hebrew word "hesed" conveys a sense of covenantal loyalty and faithfulness. This phrase assures believers of God's unchanging commitment to His people, a theme echoed in Lamentations 3:22-23, which speaks of God's mercies being new every morning. The enduring nature of God's love is a source of comfort and hope, reminding believers that His promises are reliable and eternal. This concept is fulfilled in the New Testament through Jesus Christ, whose sacrificial love and resurrection affirm God's eternal covenant with humanity.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty in Creation
God is the ultimate Creator, and His creation of the great lights demonstrates His power and authority over the universe. Recognizing His sovereignty should lead us to worship and trust in His perfect plan.

Enduring Loving Devotion
The phrase "His loving devotion endures forever" is a reminder of God's unchanging love and faithfulness. Just as the sun rises each day, God's love is constant and reliable.

The Purpose of Creation
The great lights serve a purpose in God's creation, providing light and marking time. Similarly, we are created with purpose and should seek to fulfill God's will in our lives.

Reflecting God's Light
As the sun, moon, and stars reflect God's glory, we are called to reflect His light in the world. Our lives should be a testament to His love and truth.

What Does Psalm 136:7 Mean
He made

• Psalm 136 pauses here to celebrate God as the active Creator. Unlike impersonal forces or accidental beginnings, Scripture presents the Lord as intentionally crafting all that exists.

• Genesis 1:1 affirms, “In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth.” That same creative power is echoed in Psalm 33:6–9, where God speaks and it comes to be.

• John 1:3 and Colossians 1:16 later reveal that everything “was made through Him,” binding the work of creation to the person of Christ.

• The word “made” in Psalm 136 anchors our confidence: the universe has purpose because its Maker has purpose.

the great lights

• The psalmist zeroes in on the sun, moon, and by implication the stars—called “great lights” in Genesis 1:16.

• These lights govern day and night, marking seasons and days for human benefit (Genesis 1:14; Jeremiah 31:35).

• Psalm 74:16 reminds us, “The day is Yours, and also the night; You established the moon and the sun.”

• By singling out these luminaries, the verse underlines how God fills even the sky with gifts that bless, guide, and sustain life on earth.

His loving devotion endures forever

• Every verse of Psalm 136 ends with this refrain, but here it ties God’s enduring love to the very fabric of the cosmos.

• If the sun rises every morning and the moon faithfully waxes and wanes, how much more dependable is the covenant love that set them in place (Psalm 89:2; Lamentations 3:22-23).

• James 1:17 tells us that with God “there is no variation or shadow of turning,” assuring us His character is as steady as the orbits He ordained.

• The continuity of daylight and moonlight becomes a living sermon: God’s mercy is constant, reliable, and personal.

summary

Psalm 136:7 links creation’s grandeur to God’s steadfast love. He personally fashioned the “great lights,” ensuring rhythms of day and night that bless His creatures. Each sunrise and moonrise testifies that the Creator’s loving devotion never wavers, giving believers daily, visible proof of His unending faithfulness.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(7) Lights.--An unusual word, meaning light itself, and not luminaries. But possibly the poet wished in one phrase to combine Genesis 1:3; Genesis 1:14-15.
