Rise up;
This phrase is a call for God to take action. In the context of the Psalms, it often signifies a plea for divine intervention. The psalmist is urging God to awaken and respond to the plight of His people. This echoes other biblical instances where God is called to "rise" or "awake," such as in Psalm 3:7 and Psalm 7:6, where the psalmist seeks deliverance from enemies. The imagery suggests a God who is powerful and capable of changing circumstances when He chooses to act.

be our help!
The request for help indicates a reliance on God as the ultimate source of aid and deliverance. Throughout the Old Testament, God is frequently depicted as a helper to Israel, providing assistance in times of trouble (e.g., Exodus 18:4, Psalm 33:20). This reflects the covenant relationship between God and His people, where God promises to be their protector and provider. The plea for help underscores human dependence on divine strength and guidance.

Redeem us
Redemption in the biblical sense involves deliverance from bondage or distress, often with a connotation of a price being paid. In the Old Testament, God is seen as the Redeemer of Israel, having delivered them from slavery in Egypt (Exodus 6:6). This concept is further developed in the New Testament, where Christ is portrayed as the Redeemer who delivers humanity from sin (Ephesians 1:7). The psalmist's cry for redemption is both a request for immediate rescue and a foreshadowing of the ultimate redemption through Jesus Christ.

on account of Your loving devotion.
The term "loving devotion" is often translated as "steadfast love" or "mercy" (Hebrew: hesed). It refers to God's covenantal love and faithfulness towards His people. This attribute of God is a recurring theme in the Psalms and throughout Scripture, emphasizing His unwavering commitment to His promises (e.g., Psalm 136). The appeal to God's loving devotion highlights the basis for the psalmist's confidence in seeking redemption—not because of their own merit, but because of God's faithful character. This reflects the biblical understanding that God's actions are motivated by His love and covenant loyalty.

What Does Psalm 44:26 Mean
Rise up

• This cry recognizes that God is not distant but sovereignly present, fully able to act in real time.

• The psalmists remember moments when the LORD “arose” to scatter enemies (Psalm 68:1) and protect His people (Exodus 14:13-14).

• By calling on God to rise, they express faith that He is the same yesterday, today, and forever (Malachi 3:6; Hebrews 13:8).

Be our help!

• The request places total dependence on the LORD as the only true helper, echoing “My help comes from the LORD, the Maker of heaven and earth” (Psalm 121:2).

• Israel’s history confirms that human strength fails, but God consistently intervenes—whether for Gideon’s small army (Judges 7) or Hezekiah under siege (2 Kings 19; Isaiah 37:33-36).

• This plea models humble confidence: “God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble” (Psalm 46:1).

Redeem us

• Redemption points to God’s decisive action to buy back, liberate, and restore His people.

• In the Exodus He redeemed Israel “with an outstretched arm” (Deuteronomy 7:8).

• The psalm’s longing foreshadows the ultimate redemption accomplished by Christ, “the Lamb…slain” (Revelation 5:9) who “gave Himself as a ransom for all” (1 Timothy 2:6; 1 Peter 1:18-19).

• The cry trusts that God’s covenant promises guarantee rescue, both immediate and eternal (Isaiah 43:1; Titus 2:14).

On account of Your loving devotion

• The foundation for every request is God’s “loving devotion” (hesed)—His steadfast, covenant-keeping love.

• Psalm 136 repeats “His loving devotion endures forever,” reinforcing that deliverance rests not on human merit but on God’s unwavering character (Exodus 34:6-7; Lamentations 3:22-23).

• By appealing to hesed, the psalmists align with saints like Daniel, who sought mercy “for Your own sake” (Daniel 9:18-19), and with believers today who approach God “according to His mercy” (Titus 3:5).

summary

Psalm 44:26 distills a believer’s urgent plea: God, act now, because only You can help and redeem, and Your steadfast love guarantees You will. The verse reminds us that in every crisis we cry out to the living Lord who rises to defend, helps with omnipotent strength, redeems by the blood of His Son, and does it all on the sure basis of His everlasting loving devotion.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 26. - Arise for our help; literally, arise as a help unto us; i.e. arise, and come to our aid. Help against the enemy is the one object of the entire prayer. And redeem us; or, save us - "deliver us" (comp. Psalm 25:22). For thy mercies' sake (comp. Psalm 6:4; Psalm 31:16).
