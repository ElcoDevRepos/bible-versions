Wait patiently for the LORD;
This phrase emphasizes the importance of patience and trust in God's timing. In the biblical context, waiting on the Lord is a recurring theme, seen in the lives of figures like Abraham, who waited for the promise of Isaac, and David, who waited to become king despite being anointed years earlier. The Hebrew word for "wait" can also imply hope and expectation, suggesting an active, faith-filled waiting. This patience is not passive but involves prayer, worship, and obedience. The call to wait is a reminder of God's sovereignty and faithfulness, encouraging believers to trust in His perfect plan.

be strong and courageous.
This exhortation echoes God's command to Joshua in Joshua 1:9, where he is encouraged to be strong and courageous as he leads Israel into the Promised Land. It reflects a call to inner strength and bravery in the face of adversity, rooted in faith in God's presence and promises. Historically, this strength and courage were necessary for the Israelites as they faced numerous challenges and enemies. Spiritually, it signifies the believer's reliance on God's power rather than their own, a theme also seen in Ephesians 6:10, where Paul urges believers to be strong in the Lord.

Wait patiently for the LORD!
The repetition of this phrase underscores its importance and serves as a poetic device to reinforce the message. It highlights the necessity of perseverance in faith, especially during trials and uncertainties. This call to wait is a reminder of the eschatological hope found throughout Scripture, pointing to the ultimate fulfillment of God's promises in Christ's return. The repetition also serves as an encouragement to maintain steadfastness, as seen in James 5:7-8, where believers are urged to be patient until the Lord's coming. This waiting is an act of worship and trust, aligning the believer's heart with God's eternal purposes.

What Does Psalm 27:14 Mean
Wait patiently for the LORD

• David’s opening charge is simple yet weighty: “Wait patiently for the LORD” (Psalm 27:14).

• Waiting is not passive resignation; it is an active, expectant trust that God will act at the proper time. Psalm 37:7 urges the same posture—“Be still before the LORD and wait patiently for Him.”

• Waiting keeps our eyes on the Lord instead of on circumstances. Lamentations 3:25 reminds us, “The LORD is good to those who wait for Him.”

• This call is grounded in the certainty of God’s character. Because His Word is true and unfailing, the believer can confidently endure delays, knowing that “those who wait upon the LORD will renew their strength” (Isaiah 40:31).

• Practically, waiting includes prayer, reflection on Scripture, and obedient living while we look for His intervention—never a wasted season, but a refining one.

Be strong and courageous

• Sandwiched between the two commands to wait, the psalmist exhorts, “be strong and courageous.” God does not ask for stoic patience; He supplies courage for the wait.

• Joshua 1:9 parallels this: “Have I not commanded you to be strong and courageous? … the LORD your God is with you wherever you go.” The same Presence that upheld Joshua undergirds every believer today.

• Strength here is rooted in the Lord, not in self-will. Ephesians 6:10 says, “Be strong in the Lord and in His mighty power,” while 1 Corinthians 16:13 couples courage with faithfulness: “Stand firm in the faith… be strong.”

• Courage resists fear and discouragement that often accompany long waits. By recalling God’s past faithfulness (Psalm 27:1,3), we choose confidence over panic.

• Action steps:

– Speak truth to your soul (Psalm 42:5).

– Surround yourself with believers who will remind you of God’s promises (Hebrews 10:24-25).

– Keep serving; strength grows through exercised obedience.

Wait patiently for the LORD!

• The repetition is deliberate. David echoes his first line to drive the point home: perseverance is seldom achieved in a single moment.

• Repetition is Scripture’s way of underlining urgency. Psalm 33:20 affirms, “Our soul waits for the LORD; He is our help and our shield.”

• The double call also brackets the command to “be strong,” showing that courage rests on continual, determined waiting. We do not wait merely once; we wait and keep on waiting until God’s answer comes.

• Perseverance brings reward. Hebrews 10:36-37 promises, “You need to persevere… ‘In just a little while, He who is coming will come and will not delay.’”

• While you wait:

– Keep trusting (Proverbs 3:5-6).

– Keep hoping (Romans 8:25).

– Keep praising (Psalm 42:11).

– Keep obeying (James 5:7-8).

summary

Psalm 27:14 calls believers to an active, hopeful patience anchored in God’s proven character. We are to wait—confident that He acts in perfect timing—while drawing inner strength and courage from His presence and promises. In repeating the command, Scripture stresses perseverance: hold fast, stay brave, and keep trusting the Lord who never fails to fulfill His Word.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 14. - Wait on the Lord. This is an exhortation, not to others, but to himself (comp. Psalm 62:5; and see also Psalm 42:5, 11; Psalm 43:5). His stronger self exhorts his weaker self not to despair, but to wait upon God - to tarry, i.e., the Lord's leisure - and, meanwhile to be of good courage; or, be strong (comp. Deuteronomy 31:6; Joshua 1:6; 1 Chronicles 22:13), as the phrase is elsewhere generally translated. "Be strong," he says to himself, and he (i.e. God) shall strengthen thine heart. "Aide-tot, le ciel l'aidera." Make an effort to be strong, and the strength will be given thee, as thou makest it. Then in this strength, thus given, continue till waiting - Wait, I say, on the Lord.
