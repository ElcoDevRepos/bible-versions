For surely You, O LORD, bless the righteous;
This phrase emphasizes the certainty of God's blessing upon those who are righteous. In biblical context, righteousness is often associated with living in accordance with God's laws and commands. The assurance of blessing is a recurring theme throughout Scripture, seen in passages like Deuteronomy 28:1-14, where obedience to God results in blessings. The use of "LORD" in all caps signifies the covenant name of God, Yahweh, highlighting His faithfulness to His promises. The concept of blessing in the Old Testament often includes material prosperity, protection, and spiritual well-being, reflecting God's favor and approval.

You surround them with the shield of Your favor.
The imagery of a shield suggests protection and defense, a common metaphor in the Psalms (e.g., Psalm 3:3, Psalm 18:2). In ancient Near Eastern culture, a shield was a vital part of a warrior's armor, symbolizing safety and security. God's favor acts as a divine shield, offering not just physical protection but also spiritual and emotional security. This protection is not merely defensive but also indicative of God's proactive care and love. The idea of being surrounded implies comprehensive coverage, suggesting that God's favor is all-encompassing. This phrase can be connected to the New Testament, where believers are assured of God's protection and favor through faith in Jesus Christ (Ephesians 6:16, Romans 8:31-39). The shield of favor can also be seen as a type of Christ, who is the ultimate expression of God's favor and protection for believers.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant-keeping God of Israel, who is characterized by His holiness, justice, and mercy. In this Psalm, He is the one who blesses and protects the righteous.

2. The Righteous
Those who are in right standing with God, often characterized by their faithfulness and obedience to His commandments. In the context of the Psalms, the righteous are those who seek God and His ways.

3. David (implied author)
Traditionally attributed as the author of many Psalms, including Psalm 5. David often wrote about his reliance on God for protection and guidance.
Teaching Points
God's Blessing on the Righteous
The verse assures believers that God actively blesses those who live righteously. This blessing is not just material but encompasses spiritual well-being and peace.

Divine Protection as a Shield
God's favor acts as a shield, offering protection from spiritual and physical harm. Believers can trust in God's encompassing protection in their daily lives.

Living in God's Favor
To experience God's favor, believers are encouraged to pursue righteousness through obedience to God's Word and a faithful relationship with Him.

The Assurance of God's Presence
The imagery of being surrounded by God's favor provides comfort and assurance that God is always present with His people, especially in times of trouble.

What Does Psalm 5:12 Mean
For surely

- The verse opens with confident certainty, not a tentative wish. David is convinced of God’s action.

- Scripture often couples faith with certainty (Hebrews 11:1; Romans 4:20-21).

- This assurance rests on God’s proven character rather than changing circumstances (Psalm 9:10).

You, O LORD

- The personal, covenant name invites intimacy; David addresses the One who revealed Himself to His people (Exodus 3:15; Psalm 100:3).

- Relationship is central: the blessings that follow flow from being known by the LORD, not from impersonal fate (John 10:14).

bless the righteous

- “Bless” is tangible: life, protection, provision, joy (Psalm 1:1-3; Proverbs 10:6).

- “The righteous” are those declared right with God and walking in His ways (Genesis 15:6; Romans 3:22).

- Obedience positions a believer under God’s favor, but the foundation is God’s gracious declaration, not human perfection (Ephesians 2:8-10).

You surround them

- God’s care isn’t occasional; it is encircling.

- Picture a city walled on every side (Psalm 125:2) or a hedge that thwarts attack (Job 1:10).

- His presence hems the believer in “behind and before” (Psalm 139:5).

with the shield of Your favor

- Favor is pictured as a protective shield, large enough to cover the whole person (Psalm 3:3; Psalm 18:30).

- God’s grace does more than forgive; it actively deflects harm and advances good (Genesis 50:20; Romans 8:28).

- The New Testament echoes the imagery in the armor of God—“the shield of faith” (Ephesians 6:16)—reminding us that trusting His favor is itself protection.

summary

Psalm 5:12 declares that God unquestionably blesses those who are right with Him, wrapping them in the comprehensive protection of His gracious favor. The believer can stand and move forward with confidence, knowing the Lord’s surrounding presence and blessing are as sure as His own unchanging nature.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 12. - For thou, Lord, wilt bless the righteous. All the joy of the righteous springs from the fact that God's blessing is upon them. The sense of his favour fills their hearts with rejoicing. With favour wilt thou compass him as with a shield. Tsinnah (צִנָּה)is the large, long shield that protected the whole body (see 'Ancient Monarchies,' vol. 1. p. 445). God's favour, thus encompassing a man, effectually secured him against all dangers.
