They failed to keep God’s covenant
The Israelites' failure to keep God's covenant is a recurring theme throughout the Old Testament. The covenant refers to the agreement established between God and the people of Israel, beginning with Abraham and later formalized through Moses at Mount Sinai (Exodus 19-24). This covenant required the Israelites to obey God's commandments and, in return, they would be His chosen people. Their failure to uphold this covenant is seen in their repeated disobedience and idolatry, which led to various consequences, including exile. This failure highlights the human tendency to stray from divine instructions and the need for a savior, pointing to the ultimate fulfillment of the covenant through Jesus Christ, who perfectly obeyed God's law and established a new covenant through His sacrifice (Hebrews 8:6-13).

and refused to live by His law.
The law given to the Israelites included moral, ceremonial, and civil instructions, primarily found in the first five books of the Bible, known as the Torah. Living by God's law was intended to set Israel apart as a holy nation and a kingdom of priests (Exodus 19:6). Their refusal to live by these laws often led to societal and spiritual decay, as seen in the periods of the Judges and the divided kingdom. This refusal is not just a historical account but serves as a warning and lesson for believers today about the importance of obedience to God's commands. The law also serves as a tutor leading to Christ (Galatians 3:24), showing humanity's inability to achieve righteousness on their own and the necessity of grace through faith in Jesus.

Teaching Points
The Importance of Covenant Faithfulness
God's covenant is not just a set of rules but a relationship that requires faithfulness and commitment.

Consequences of Disobedience
Disobedience to God's law leads to spiritual and sometimes physical consequences, as seen in the history of Israel.

The Need for a New Heart
The failure of the Israelites to keep the covenant points to the need for a transformation of the heart, fulfilled in the new covenant through Christ.

Learning from the Past
Reflecting on the failures of the Israelites can help us understand the importance of obedience and reliance on God's grace.

Living by God's Law Today
While Christians are not under the old covenant, the principles of God's law guide us in living a life that honors Him.

What Does Psalm 78:10 Mean
They failed

Psalm 78 lays out a cycle of God’s faithfulness and Israel’s forgetfulness. The opening word “failed” signals more than an innocent stumble; it points to a deliberate neglect.

• Earlier in the psalm the men of Ephraim “did not keep God’s covenant” (v. 9), showing that the failure had roots.

• Judges 2:10-12 records a similar lapse: “They forsook the LORD… and followed other gods.”

• Isaiah 24:5 echoes the tragedy: “They have transgressed the laws, violated the statutes, broken the everlasting covenant.”

When God’s people lose their memory of His works, collapse follows.

to keep God’s covenant

A covenant is God’s binding pledge and the people’s vowed obedience. Israel pledged, “All that the LORD has spoken we will do” (Exodus 19:8).

• Deuteronomy 28:1-2 promises blessing “if you fully obey the LORD your God and carefully follow all His commands.”

• 2 Kings 17:15 laments that they “rejected His statutes… and followed idols.”

Failing to “keep” the covenant is like dropping a lifeline that God Himself has thrown.

and refused

Refusal highlights willful resistance, not mere weakness.

• Nehemiah 9:16-17 confesses, “Our fathers became arrogant and stiff-necked, and they did not obey Your commandments.”

• Jeremiah 6:16-17 shows God pleading, “Walk in it, and you will find rest… But they said, ‘We will not walk in it.’”

The heart attitude matters: refusal turns disobedience into defiance.

to live by His law

God’s law was meant to shape everyday life. Ignoring it affects every domain—family, worship, justice.

• Joshua 1:8 urges, “This Book of the Law must not depart from your mouth; meditate on it day and night, so that you may be careful to do everything written in it.”

• Psalm 119:1 calls blessed “those whose way is blameless, who walk in the law of the LORD.”

• James 1:22 brings the same standard forward: “Be doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving yourselves.”

Living by His law is the only safe path; stepping off it invites ruin.

summary

Psalm 78:10 exposes a three-fold breakdown: they failed, they violated covenant, and they flatly refused God’s law. Each phrase intensifies the charge, moving from neglect to rebellion. The verse warns every generation that spiritual memory loss leads to covenant breach and open resistance. God’s faithfulness remains, but blessing rests on remembering His works, embracing His covenant, and gladly obeying His Word.
