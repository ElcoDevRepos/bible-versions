The LORD is exalted over all the nations
This phrase emphasizes the sovereignty and supremacy of God over all earthly powers and kingdoms. In the biblical context, nations often represent human authority and power structures. The assertion that the LORD is exalted over all nations highlights His ultimate authority and control over the world, transcending any human government or empire. This is consistent with the biblical theme of God's dominion, as seen in passages like Psalm 47:8, which states, "God reigns over the nations; God is seated on His holy throne." Historically, this would have been a powerful reminder to Israel, surrounded by powerful empires like Egypt, Assyria, and Babylon, that their God was greater than any earthly ruler. It also foreshadows the New Testament revelation of Jesus Christ as King of Kings and Lord of Lords (Revelation 19:16), who will ultimately establish His reign over all nations.

His glory above the heavens
This phrase speaks to the transcendence and majesty of God, whose glory surpasses even the vastness of the heavens. In ancient Near Eastern cultures, the heavens were often seen as the realm of the divine, a place of ultimate power and mystery. By stating that God's glory is above the heavens, the psalmist underscores the idea that God's majesty and splendor are beyond human comprehension and the natural world. This aligns with other scriptural affirmations of God's incomprehensible greatness, such as Isaiah 55:9, "For as the heavens are higher than the earth, so are My ways higher than your ways and My thoughts than your thoughts." Theologically, this points to the concept of God's transcendence, a key attribute that distinguishes Him from His creation. It also reflects the New Testament revelation of Christ's divine nature, as seen in Colossians 1:16-17, where Christ is described as the one through whom all things were created and who holds all things together, further affirming His preeminence and glory.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty
Recognize that God is sovereign over all nations and rulers. This truth should bring comfort and assurance, especially in times of political or social unrest.

Worship and Reverence
Since God's glory is above the heavens, our response should be one of worship and reverence. We are called to honor Him in our daily lives.

Global Perspective
Understanding that God is exalted over all nations encourages us to have a global perspective in our prayers and mission work, knowing that His authority extends to every corner of the earth.

Humility Before God
Acknowledge our place before an exalted God. This should lead us to humility, recognizing that our achievements and status are insignificant compared to His glory.

Hope in God's Reign
In a world that often seems chaotic, we can find hope in the truth that God reigns supreme. His plans and purposes will ultimately prevail.

What Does Psalm 113:4 Mean
The LORD

- “The LORD” in Psalm 113:4 points to the covenant God who revealed Himself to Moses (Exodus 3:15).

- Scripture consistently distinguishes Him from every false god: “But You, O LORD, abide forever” (Psalm 102:12).

- Because He never changes, His promises, mercies, and standards remain certain for every generation.

is exalted

- “Exalted” communicates being lifted high, enthroned in unrivaled majesty. Isaiah saw “the Lord seated on a high and lofty throne” (Isaiah 6:1).

- The Psalms echo the theme: “For You, O LORD, are Most High over all the earth” (Psalm 97:9).

- In the New Testament, God the Father “exalted Him to the highest place” (Philippians 2:9–11), showing that Christ shares in this same supremacy.

over all the nations

- No culture, empire, or political power lies outside His rule. “For dominion belongs to the LORD and He rules over the nations” (Psalm 22:28).

- Daniel testified that God “does as He pleases with the army of heaven and the people of the earth” (Daniel 4:35).

- Revelation looks forward to the day when “the kingdom of the world has become the kingdom of our Lord and of His Christ” (Revelation 11:15).

• Every government is temporary; Christ’s reign is forever.

His glory

- Glory speaks of the visible, weighty display of God’s character—His holiness, power, and beauty.

- Moses pleaded, “Please show me Your glory” (Exodus 33:18), and God revealed His goodness and name.

- The heavens “declare the glory of God” (Psalm 19:1), inviting all creation to recognize His splendor.

- In the face of Jesus Christ “shines the light of the knowledge of the glory of God” (2 Corinthians 4:6), bringing that majesty near to us.

above the heavens

- Even the vast cosmos cannot contain Him. “You have set Your glory above the heavens” (Psalm 8:1).

- As high as the heavens are above the earth, so are His ways higher than ours (Isaiah 55:9).

- Christ is now “seated at His right hand in the heavenly realms, far above every rule and authority” (Ephesians 1:20–21).

• God’s greatness surpasses every boundary we can measure or imagine.

summary

Psalm 113:4 proclaims that the one true God is enthroned in unmatched greatness, exercising sovereign rule over every nation while displaying glory that outshines the heavens themselves. The verse invites us to lift our eyes beyond earthly powers and earthly limits, recognizing that the Lord alone is worthy of universal praise, confident trust, and wholehearted obedience—today and forever.

Verse 4. - The Lord is high above all nations. As being "the great King over all the earth" (Psalm 47:2). And his glory above the heavens.
