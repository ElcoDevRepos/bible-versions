I will be glad and rejoice in Your loving devotion
This phrase reflects a deep sense of joy and gratitude towards God's steadfast love, often translated as "lovingkindness" or "mercy" in other versions. The Hebrew word "chesed" signifies a covenantal love that is loyal and enduring. This concept is central to the Old Testament, highlighting God's unwavering commitment to His people. The psalmist's response of gladness and rejoicing is a common theme in the Psalms, where worship and praise are often expressions of trust in God's character. This mirrors the joy found in other scriptures, such as Philippians 4:4, where believers are encouraged to rejoice in the Lord always.

for You have seen my affliction
The acknowledgment that God has seen the psalmist's affliction underscores His omniscience and personal involvement in the lives of His people. This is reminiscent of God's awareness of the Israelites' suffering in Egypt (Exodus 3:7), where He declares that He has seen their misery and heard their cries. The personal nature of God's attention is a source of comfort and assurance, affirming that no suffering goes unnoticed by Him. This aligns with the New Testament teaching in Hebrews 4:15, where Jesus is described as a High Priest who sympathizes with our weaknesses.

You have known the anguish of my soul
The phrase emphasizes God's intimate knowledge of the psalmist's inner turmoil. The word "known" implies a deep, experiential understanding, suggesting that God is not distant but is fully aware of the psalmist's emotional and spiritual struggles. This reflects the biblical theme of God's intimate relationship with His people, as seen in Psalm 139:1-4, where God is described as knowing every detail of our lives. The anguish of the soul can be likened to Jesus' own experience in the Garden of Gethsemane (Matthew 26:38), where He expressed deep sorrow and distress, highlighting His identification with human suffering.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
Traditionally attributed as the author of Psalm 31, David is expressing his trust and joy in God's steadfast love despite his afflictions.

2. God
The central figure in this verse, God is portrayed as the one who sees and knows the depths of human suffering and responds with loving devotion.

3. Affliction and Anguish
These terms describe the emotional and physical distress that David is experiencing, which God is fully aware of and compassionate towards.
Teaching Points
Rejoicing in God's Love
Despite circumstances, believers are called to find joy in God's unwavering love and faithfulness.

God's Omniscience
God sees and knows every detail of our lives, including our deepest pains, which should bring comfort and assurance.

Trust in God's Compassion
Understanding that God is aware of our afflictions encourages us to trust in His compassionate response.

Emotional Honesty with God
Like David, we can be honest with God about our struggles, knowing He understands and cares.

Encouragement in Trials
This verse reminds us that our trials are not unnoticed by God, and His loving devotion is a source of strength.

What Does Psalm 31:7 Mean
I will be glad and rejoice

• David makes a deliberate choice of joy, not a reaction to changing circumstances.

• This resolve echoes Psalm 5:11, “Let all who take refuge in You rejoice.”

• Joy is anchored in the Lord’s presence (Psalm 16:11) and endures even in trial (James 1:2).

• Rejoicing is an act of trust, much like Paul’s “Rejoice in the Lord always” (Philippians 4:4).

in Your loving devotion

• The source of gladness is God’s loyal love—His unbreakable covenant faithfulness (Psalm 32:10).

• This love is active and steadfast, as first revealed to Moses: “abounding in loving devotion and truth” (Exodus 34:6).

• Because God’s character never changes (Malachi 3:6), His devotion is a sure foundation for praise (Lamentations 3:22-23).

for You have seen my affliction

• God’s love is not distant; He “sees” His people’s suffering just as He saw Israel’s bondage in Egypt (Exodus 3:7).

• The all-seeing Lord notices every tear (Psalm 56:8) and watches over the righteous (Psalm 34:15).

• Knowing we are seen brings comfort and courage, replacing isolation with divine companionship (2 Chronicles 16:9).

You have known the anguish of my soul

• God’s knowledge is intimate—He “searches me and knows me” (Psalm 139:1-4).

• He understands internal agony others might miss, for He “is near to the brokenhearted” (Psalm 34:18).

• Christ Himself “was despised and rejected… a man of sorrows” (Isaiah 53:3) and now sympathizes with our weaknesses (Hebrews 4:15).

• Because He knows, He provides real help (Psalm 46:1) and guarantees that nothing can separate us from His love (Romans 8:38-39).

summary

Psalm 31:7 is a personal declaration of joy rooted in God’s steadfast love, a love proven by His attentive eye and compassionate understanding of every hurt. David’s words invite believers to rejoice, confident that the Lord both sees and knows every affliction, and that His unwavering devotion is more than enough to sustain a glad heart.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 7. - I will be glad and rejoice in thy mercy. Anticipating the "mercy" which he has craved (vers. 2-4), the psalmist determines to "be glad and rejoice in it." For thou hast considered my trouble. When God looks upon trouble and considers it, he is sure to compassionate the sufferer, and to grant him some relief. Thou hast known my soul in adversities (comp. Psalm 1:6, "The Lord knoweth the way of the righteous" ). God is said to "know" those on whom he looks with approval.
