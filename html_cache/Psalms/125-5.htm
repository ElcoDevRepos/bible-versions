Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
But those who turn to crooked ways
This phrase addresses individuals who deviate from the path of righteousness. In biblical context, "crooked ways" often symbolize moral and spiritual corruption, contrasting with the straight and narrow path of God's commandments (Proverbs 2:15). The imagery of a crooked path is used throughout Scripture to describe those who choose sin over obedience (Isaiah 59:8). Historically, Israel faced many temptations to follow the practices of surrounding pagan nations, which often led them away from God's laws. This serves as a warning against apostasy and the consequences of turning away from God's truth.

the LORD will banish with the evildoers.
This part of the verse emphasizes divine justice. The LORD's action of banishing reflects His role as a righteous judge who separates the wicked from the righteous (Psalm 1:5-6). The term "evildoers" is frequently used in the Psalms to describe those who oppose God's will and oppress His people (Psalm 37:1). The concept of banishment can be linked to the ultimate judgment where the unrighteous are separated from God's presence (Matthew 25:41). This also echoes the fate of those who reject God's covenant, as seen in the history of Israel's exile due to disobedience.

Peace be upon Israel.
This concluding phrase is a blessing and a prayer for the well-being of God's chosen people. "Peace" (shalom in Hebrew) encompasses not only the absence of conflict but also the presence of wholeness, prosperity, and harmony with God. Throughout the Old Testament, Israel's peace is contingent upon their faithfulness to God's covenant (Leviticus 26:3-6). This blessing is prophetic, pointing to the ultimate peace brought by the Messiah, Jesus Christ, who is the Prince of Peace (Isaiah 9:6). It also connects to the New Testament promise of peace for those who are in Christ (John 14:27).

Teaching Points
The Danger of Deviating from Righteousness
Turning aside to crooked ways leads to separation from God and His blessings.

The path of righteousness requires steadfastness and commitment to God's commands.

God's Justice and Judgment
God will judge those who choose crooked paths alongside evildoers.

His justice ensures that righteousness is upheld and wickedness is punished.

The Promise of Peace for the Faithful
True peace is promised to those who remain faithful to God and His ways.

Israel, as God's people, symbolizes the peace and security found in obedience to Him.

The Call to Self-Examination
Believers are encouraged to examine their paths and ensure alignment with God's righteousness.

Repentance and realignment with God's ways are necessary for those who have strayed.

Encouragement to Persevere in Faith
Despite challenges, believers are called to persevere in faith and righteousness.

Trust in God's promises and His ultimate justice provides strength and hope.

What Does Psalm 125:5 Mean
Those who turn to crooked ways

“But those who turn to crooked ways…” (Psalm 125:5a)

• The phrase pictures a deliberate choice to leave the straight path of God’s righteousness and head down winding, deceptive roads (Proverbs 2:15; Isaiah 59:8).

• Scripture often contrasts the “way of the righteous” with the “way of the wicked” (Psalm 1:6). Turning aside is not a minor detour; it signals a heart that no longer trusts the Lord (Jeremiah 17:5).

• Even among those who once identified with God’s people, some choose rebellion, proving their allegiance by their actions (1 John 2:19).

the LORD will banish with the evildoers

“…the LORD will banish with the evildoers.” (Psalm 125:5b)

• God’s justice is personal: “the LORD” Himself acts. He does not outsource judgment (Psalm 94:1-2).

• “Banish” points to separation—removing the unfaithful from the security promised to the upright (Psalm 37:9-10).

• They are sent away “with the evildoers,” sharing the destiny of all who persist in wickedness (Matthew 25:41; Revelation 20:15). There is no neutral ground; paths diverge toward blessing or banishment (Psalm 1:5-6).

Peace be upon Israel

“Peace be upon Israel.” (Psalm 125:5c)

• The psalm ends with a benediction of shalom—wholeness, safety, flourishing—for God’s covenant people (Numbers 6:24-26).

• While judgment falls on the crooked, peace rests on those who trust the Lord (Psalm 125:1; John 14:27).

• This blessing reaches beyond ethnic Israel to all who are united to the Messiah, the true Prince of Peace (Galatians 6:16; Ephesians 2:13-14).

summary

Psalm 125:5 draws a sharp line: deviating from God’s straight path leads to decisive judgment, yet steadfast trust in Him brings enduring peace. The verse warns against compromise, assures us that God will right every wrong, and closes by reminding the faithful of the profound, covenant-grounded peace He delights to give.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(5) Turn aside unto their crooked ways.--Or, bend their crooked ways, i.e., pursue evil courses.

But peace.--Better, as an innovation on the customary form, peace be in Israel. (See Note on Psalm 122:6, and comp. Psalm 128:6.)

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 5. - As for such as turn aside unto their crooked ways. The word translated "crooked ways" occurs only here and in Judges 5:6. It means properly "by-paths," deviations from the straight path of right. The Lord shall lead them forth with the workers of iniquity. God shall give them no better portion than he assigns to the open evildoers, since their heart is not really whole with him. But peace shall be upon Israel; rather, but peace be upon Israel. The psalmist winds up with a prayer, not a prophecy.
