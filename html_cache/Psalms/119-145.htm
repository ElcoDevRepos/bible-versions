I call with all my heart;
This phrase reflects a deep, earnest plea to God, indicating sincerity and total commitment in prayer. The heart, in biblical terms, often represents the center of one's being, including emotions, will, and intellect. This wholehearted calling is reminiscent of Deuteronomy 6:5, where believers are commanded to love God with all their heart, soul, and strength. It underscores the importance of approaching God with undivided devotion, as seen in the prayers of figures like David and Daniel, who sought God with their entire being.

answer me, O LORD!
The psalmist's request for an answer highlights a personal relationship with God, characterized by trust and expectation. This plea is consistent with the covenant relationship between God and His people, where God promises to hear and respond to the cries of His faithful (Psalm 34:17). The use of "LORD" (YHWH) emphasizes the covenant name of God, reminding the reader of His faithfulness and the assurance that He listens to those who call upon Him (Jeremiah 33:3).

I will obey Your statutes.
This commitment to obedience reflects the psalmist's dedication to living according to God's laws. The statutes refer to the decrees and commandments given by God, which are seen as a path to righteousness and blessing (Deuteronomy 28:1-2). Obedience is a recurring theme in the Psalms and throughout Scripture, where it is often linked to love for God (John 14:15) and is a response to His grace and salvation. This phrase also points to the transformative power of God's Word, which guides and shapes the believer's life, aligning it with divine will.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Psalmist
The author of Psalm 119, traditionally believed to be King David, who is expressing a deep, heartfelt plea to God.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, to whom the psalmist directs his prayer and from whom he seeks an answer.

3. The Statutes
Refers to God's laws and commandments, which the psalmist commits to obeying.
Teaching Points
Wholehearted Prayer
The psalmist's call "with all my heart" emphasizes the importance of sincerity and total commitment in our prayers. We are encouraged to approach God with genuine intent and focus.

Dependence on God
The plea for God to "answer me" reflects a deep dependence on God for guidance and support. It reminds us to rely on God in every aspect of our lives.

Commitment to Obedience
The psalmist's vow to "obey Your statutes" highlights the connection between prayer and obedience. Our prayers should be accompanied by a commitment to live according to God's Word.

God's Faithfulness
The expectation of an answer from God is rooted in His faithfulness. We can trust that God hears and responds to the prayers of His people.

What Does Psalm 119:145 Mean
I call with all my heart

• The psalmist’s cry is wholehearted—no half-measures, no hidden reservations. It echoes Deuteronomy 6:5, where God commands His people to “love the LORD your God with all your heart.” Genuine relationship with God involves the entire inner being, not a casual nod toward heaven.

• Jeremiah 29:13 reinforces this: “You will seek Me and find Me when you search for Me with all your heart.” The promise of divine nearness is linked to sincerity.

• The phrase also reminds us that God welcomes transparent passion. Psalm 62:8 says, “Pour out your hearts before Him; God is our refuge.”

answer me, O LORD!

• Having poured out his heart, the psalmist expects a real reply. Scripture presents God as a Father who listens (Psalm 34:17, “The righteous cry out, and the LORD hears”).

• Isaiah 65:24 highlights God’s readiness: “Before they call, I will answer; while they are still speaking, I will hear.” Such verses encourage confidence that prayer is never a monologue.

• New-covenant believers share the same assurance: 1 John 5:14 declares, “If we ask anything according to His will, He hears us.” The psalmist’s plea anticipates that certainty.

I will obey Your statutes

• The response to answered prayer is not mere gratitude but concrete obedience. Love for God naturally produces submission. Jesus states it plainly in John 14:15: “If you love Me, you will keep My commandments.”

• This commitment guards against treating prayer as a transaction. Psalm 119:33 blends petition with resolve: “Teach me, O LORD, the way of Your statutes, and I will keep them to the end.”

• James 1:22 urges believers to be “doers of the word, and not hearers only.” The psalmist embodies that principle centuries earlier, linking God’s faithfulness to his own faithful living.

summary

Psalm 119:145 paints a vivid picture of authentic devotion: an all-in cry to God, confidence that He truly answers, and a determined pledge to walk in obedience. Taken together, these elements show that wholehearted prayer, assured faith, and practical submission belong together in a believer’s life, because God’s living and trustworthy Word calls for nothing less.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 145. - I cried with my whole heart; hear me, O Lord. "Earnest and patient prayer for grace" (Kay) seems to be intended, not mere prayer for deliverance. I will keep thy statutes. If thou hearest and answerest my prayer, I shall have both the will and the power to keep thy commandments.
