Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
For our hearts rejoice in Him
This phrase emphasizes the joy and gladness found in a relationship with God. In biblical context, the heart often represents the center of emotions and will. Rejoicing in God is a recurring theme throughout the Psalms, highlighting a deep, personal connection with the divine. This joy is not dependent on external circumstances but is rooted in the character and presence of God. The concept of rejoicing in God is echoed in Philippians 4:4, where believers are encouraged to "rejoice in the Lord always." This joy is a response to God's faithfulness and goodness, as seen in the history of Israel and the personal experiences of the psalmist.

since we trust in His holy name
Trust in God's name signifies reliance on His character and attributes. In ancient Israel, a name was more than a label; it represented the essence and reputation of a person. God's name is holy, set apart, and worthy of reverence. Trusting in His name implies confidence in His power, promises, and faithfulness. This trust is foundational to the covenant relationship between God and His people, as seen in passages like Proverbs 3:5-6, which calls for trust in the Lord with all one's heart. The holiness of God's name is a reminder of His otherness and purity, calling believers to a life of faith and dependence on Him. This trust is ultimately fulfilled in Jesus Christ, whose name is above every name (Philippians 2:9), and who embodies the fullness of God's character and promises.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Psalmist
The author of Psalm 33, traditionally attributed to David, who expresses trust and joy in the Lord.

2. The Lord (Yahweh)
The central figure in the Psalm, whose holy name is the source of trust and joy for the believers.

3. The Congregation of Israel
The implied audience of the Psalm, representing the collective people of God who are called to trust and rejoice in Him.
Teaching Points
Joy Rooted in Trust
Our joy is deeply connected to our trust in God. When we place our confidence in His holy name, we find a profound sense of joy that transcends circumstances.

The Power of God's Name
The name of the Lord is not just a label but a representation of His character and promises. Trusting in His name means relying on His faithfulness and sovereignty.

Heartfelt Rejoicing
True rejoicing comes from the heart. It is an internal response to the external reality of God's trustworthiness and love.

Trust as a Foundation
Trusting in God is foundational to our relationship with Him. It is through trust that we experience His peace and joy.

Community of Believers
As a community, believers are called to collectively trust and rejoice in the Lord, encouraging one another in faith.

What Does Psalm 33:21 Mean
for

- The word “for” ties Psalm 33:21 back to the flow of verse 20: “Our soul waits for the LORD; He is our help and our shield”.

- Scripture often links a statement of confidence with the reason behind it (1 John 4:19; Psalm 118:14). Here, “for” signals that what follows is the explanation for the waiting, hoping people in verse 20.

- The psalmist is about to show why waiting on the Lord is not grim endurance but glad expectancy.

our hearts rejoice in Him

- “Hearts” points to the whole inner person, not a momentary feeling (Proverbs 4:23).

- Rejoicing “in Him” shifts attention from circumstances to God Himself. Similar language appears in Psalm 5:11—“let all who take refuge in You rejoice.”

- Joy is described as a settled response to God’s presence and character (John 15:11; Philippians 4:4).

- Notice the plural “our”; worship is communal as well as personal (Psalm 95:6-7).

since

- This tiny hinge word grounds joy in something rock-solid. The psalmist is not advocating positive thinking; he is pointing to a cause.

- Throughout Scripture, rejoicing and trusting travel together (Romans 5:2-3; 1 Peter 1:8). “Since” tells us the joy just mentioned rests on the trust about to be named.

we trust

- Trust is an act of the will based on the truth God has revealed (Proverbs 3:5-6).

- It involves:

• Confidence in God’s promises (Numbers 23:19)

• Reliance on His power (Psalm 28:7)

• Rest in His timing (Isaiah 26:3-4)

- Trust is not mere mental assent; it is leaning the whole weight of life on the Lord (Jeremiah 17:7).

in His holy name

- “Name” stands for God’s revealed character (Exodus 34:5-7).

- Calling it “holy” underscores His absolute purity and separateness (Psalm 99:3).

- Trusting in the Lord’s name means banking on who He has shown Himself to be—faithful, righteous, and unfailing (Psalm 20:7; Proverbs 18:10).

- The psalmist’s joy is secure because the object of his trust is unchanging (Hebrews 13:8).

summary

Psalm 33:21 teaches that genuine, enduring joy springs from trusting the Lord’s impeccable character. Because God is holy and unfailingly true to His name, believers can rest their whole hearts on Him. The result is a deep, shared rejoicing that is independent of shifting circumstances and anchored in the unchangeable God.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 21. - For our heart shall rejoice in him, because we have trusted in his holy Name (comp. Psalm 13:5, where the sentiment is the same). Trust in God secures his help, and this brings the deliverance at which the heart rejoices.
