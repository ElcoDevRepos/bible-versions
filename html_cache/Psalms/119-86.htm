All Your commandments are faithful;
The psalmist affirms the reliability and trustworthiness of God's commandments. This reflects the belief that God's laws are perfect and unchanging, as seen in other scriptures like Psalm 19:7, which states, "The law of the LORD is perfect, refreshing the soul." The faithfulness of God's commandments is a recurring theme throughout the Bible, emphasizing that they are a reflection of His character—righteous, just, and true. This phrase underscores the idea that God's word is a firm foundation, as echoed in Matthew 24:35, where Jesus says, "Heaven and earth will pass away, but my words will never pass away."

I am persecuted without cause—
The psalmist expresses a personal lament, highlighting the experience of unjust suffering. This mirrors the experiences of many biblical figures, such as Joseph, who was persecuted by his brothers without cause (Genesis 37), and David, who was pursued by Saul despite his loyalty (1 Samuel 24:11). The phrase also prophetically points to Jesus Christ, who was persecuted and crucified despite His innocence, fulfilling prophecies like Isaiah 53:7, "He was oppressed and afflicted, yet he did not open his mouth." This unjust persecution is a common theme in the lives of those who follow God, as Jesus warned in John 15:20, "If they persecuted me, they will persecute you also."

help me!
The plea for divine assistance is a central element of the psalmist's prayer, reflecting a deep dependence on God for deliverance and support. This cry for help is consistent with the biblical understanding that God is a refuge and strength for His people, as seen in Psalm 46:1, "God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble." The request for help also aligns with the New Testament teaching on prayer, where believers are encouraged to present their requests to God with thanksgiving (Philippians 4:6). This plea is a reminder of the believer's reliance on God's grace and mercy in times of distress, trusting in His power to save and deliver.

Teaching Points
Faithfulness of God's Commandments
God's commandments are reliable and trustworthy, providing a solid foundation for our lives.

In times of trouble, we can rely on the truth and faithfulness of God's word.

Enduring Persecution
Believers may face unjust persecution, but they are called to remain steadfast in their faith.

Trusting in God's faithfulness can provide strength and comfort during trials.

Seeking Divine Help
It is appropriate and necessary to cry out to God for help when facing unjust treatment.

Prayer is a powerful tool for seeking God's intervention and support.

Living a Godly Life
Following God's commandments may lead to opposition, but it is a testament to living a life pleasing to God.

Our response to persecution should reflect our trust in God's ultimate justice and faithfulness.

What Does Psalm 119:86 Mean
All Your commandments are faithful

Psalm 119:86 opens by declaring, “All Your commandments are faithful”. The psalmist is staking everything on the absolute reliability of what God has spoken.

• God’s words are not merely true in principle; they are unfailingly true in practice (Psalm 19:7; Psalm 111:7).

• Because the Lord cannot lie (Titus 1:2), every directive—whether promise, warning, or instruction—is a sure foundation (Matthew 7:24–25).

• Knowing this, believers can obey with confidence, certain that His statutes are for our good and His glory (Deuteronomy 10:13; Romans 7:12).

• Jesus echoed this certainty when He prayed, “Your word is truth” (John 17:17).

I am persecuted without cause

Immediately after affirming God’s faithfulness, the writer sighs, “I am persecuted without cause.”

• Innocent suffering is not new; David experienced it (Psalm 69:4), Job endured it, and Christ Himself fulfilled it (John 15:25).

• Scripture prepares us for similar opposition when we live righteously (2 Timothy 3:12; 1 Peter 4:12–14).

• The clash is never between equal powers; the faithful word of God outlasts every slander and assault (Isaiah 40:8).

• Remembering this keeps bitterness at bay and steadies a heart under fire (Romans 12:17–21).

Help me!

The cry is brief yet loaded with confidence: “Help me!”

• The psalmist appeals to the very God whose commands are faithful, expecting timely aid (Psalm 121:1–2; Hebrews 4:16).

• Scripture repeatedly links God’s reliability with His readiness to intervene (Psalm 46:1; Isaiah 41:10).

• He rescues through encouragement, wisdom, strength, and sometimes direct deliverance (2 Corinthians 12:9–10; Acts 18:9–10).

• Our part is to keep praying, trusting, and obeying while waiting for His perfect response (Luke 18:1; Philippians 4:6–7).

summary

Psalm 119:86 blends unwavering confidence in God’s trustworthy commands with an honest cry for rescue from unjust persecution. Because every word God speaks is faithful, His people can stand firm when mistreated, knowing He hears their plea for help and will act in His perfect time.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 86. - All thy commandments are faithful; literally, are faithfulness. This is said in respect of the promises attached to them. They (i.e. my enemies) persecute me wrongfully; or, "with lies" - lyingly (comp. vers. 69, 78). Help thou me; i.e. help me against them, so that their "lies" may do me no harm.
