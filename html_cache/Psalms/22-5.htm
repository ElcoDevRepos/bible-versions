They cried out to You and were set free;
This phrase reflects the historical context of Israel's relationship with God, where the Israelites often found themselves in distress and cried out to God for deliverance. This is seen throughout the Old Testament, such as in the Book of Judges, where the cycle of sin, oppression, repentance, and deliverance is repeated. The act of crying out signifies a deep dependence on God, acknowledging Him as the ultimate source of salvation. The phrase "set free" can be connected to the Exodus narrative, where God delivered the Israelites from slavery in Egypt, symbolizing spiritual liberation. This deliverance is a type of the ultimate freedom found in Jesus Christ, who sets believers free from the bondage of sin.

they trusted in You and were not disappointed.
Trust in God is a central theme throughout the Bible, emphasizing faith and reliance on His promises. This phrase highlights the reliability and faithfulness of God, as those who place their trust in Him are never let down. This is echoed in other scriptures, such as Isaiah 49:23, which assures that those who hope in God will not be put to shame. The historical context includes numerous instances where God proved faithful to His people, such as the provision of manna in the wilderness and the conquest of Canaan. This trust is ultimately fulfilled in the New Testament through Jesus Christ, who embodies God's faithfulness and assures believers that their hope in Him will not lead to disappointment, as seen in Romans 5:5.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
Traditionally attributed as the author of Psalm 22, David is expressing a deep sense of trust in God despite his circumstances.

2. Israelites
The collective people of God who have historically cried out to Him in times of distress and have been delivered.

3. God
The central figure to whom the cries are directed, representing divine faithfulness and deliverance.

4. Historical Deliverances
Events such as the Exodus, where the Israelites cried out to God and were delivered from slavery in Egypt.

5. Messianic Foreshadowing
Psalm 22 is often seen as a prophetic psalm pointing to the sufferings and ultimate trust of Jesus Christ.
Teaching Points
Trust in God's Faithfulness
The historical deliverances of Israel serve as a testament to God's unchanging faithfulness. Believers today can trust that God hears their cries and will deliver them in His perfect timing.

The Power of Crying Out to God
Crying out to God is an act of faith and dependence. It acknowledges our need for His intervention and opens the door for His deliverance.

Avoiding Disappointment through Trust
Trusting in God leads to freedom from disappointment. When our hope is anchored in Him, we can rest assured that He will not fail us.

Messianic Fulfillment in Christ
Psalm 22 points to the ultimate deliverance through Jesus Christ. His trust in the Father, even in suffering, is a model for believers to follow.

Encouragement in Community
Just as the Israelites collectively experienced God's deliverance, believers are encouraged to support one another in faith, reminding each other of God's past faithfulness.

What Does Psalm 22:5 Mean
They cried out to You

• The verse opens with urgency: God’s people “cried out,” not whispered. This is the instinct of faith—running to the Father first (Psalm 50:15; Psalm 18:6).

• Israel’s history is full of such cries. When enslaved in Egypt they “groaned” and God “heard their cry” (Exodus 2:23-25). David, chased by Saul, “called to the LORD” and was heard (1 Samuel 23:1-5).

• The pattern is personal as well. Whenever you face distress, the invitation remains: “Call upon Me in the day of trouble” (Psalm 50:15).

and were set free

• God responds. Deliverance is His signature. From the Red Sea crossing (Exodus 14:29-30) to Hezekiah’s rescue from Assyria (2 Kings 19:32-36), crying out leads to tangible freedom.

• Notice the certainty: “were set free,” not “might be.” The Lord’s intervention breaks chains—physical, emotional, and spiritual (Psalm 34:4; 2 Corinthians 1:10).

• For believers today, ultimate freedom came at Calvary (John 8:36). Yet God still steps into present trials, proving His faithfulness in everyday crises.

they trusted in You

• Trust is more than a momentary plea; it’s an ongoing posture. “In You my God, I trust” (Psalm 25:2).

• Trust rests on God’s character—His power (Jeremiah 32:17), His love (Psalm 136:1), and His covenant faithfulness (Deuteronomy 7:9).

• Practical outworking:

– Refusing self-reliance (Proverbs 3:5-6)

– Waiting on God’s timing (Isaiah 30:18)

– Holding fast to promises when circumstances contradict them (Hebrews 10:23)

and were not disappointed

• The outcome of trust is never shame. “No one who waits for You will be put to shame” (Psalm 25:3; Isaiah 49:23).

• In the New Testament the theme echoes: “Hope does not disappoint us, because God’s love has been poured into our hearts” (Romans 5:5).

• God’s track record with Israel, with David, with the early church—each proves He keeps His word. Therefore believers can face uncertainty without fear of humiliation or defeat.

summary

Psalm 22:5 compresses Israel’s entire salvation history into one line: they cried, God freed; they trusted, God never failed. The verse invites every believer to follow the same rhythm—honest prayer, steadfast faith, confident expectation—knowing the Lord who answered then is unchanged today.
