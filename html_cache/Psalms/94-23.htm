He will bring upon them their own iniquity
This phrase emphasizes the principle of divine justice, where individuals face the consequences of their own actions. Biblically, this reflects the concept of "reaping what you sow" as seen in Galatians 6:7. The idea that God allows the natural consequences of sin to unfold is consistent with the justice seen throughout the Old Testament, such as in the stories of the Israelites' disobedience leading to exile. This principle is also evident in the wisdom literature, like Proverbs, which often speaks of the self-destructive nature of wickedness.

and destroy them for their wickedness
The destruction mentioned here is a direct result of wickedness, underscoring the holiness and righteousness of God. In the historical context of Israel, this could refer to the judgment upon nations or individuals who opposed God's people or His laws. The destruction of Sodom and Gomorrah (Genesis 19) serves as a historical example of divine judgment against wickedness. This phrase also aligns with the prophetic warnings given by prophets like Isaiah and Jeremiah, who spoke of impending judgment due to the people's sins.

The LORD our God will destroy them
This statement reaffirms the sovereignty and active role of God in executing judgment. The use of "LORD" (YHWH) highlights the covenantal relationship between God and His people, reminding them of His faithfulness and justice. The phrase "our God" personalizes this relationship, indicating that the psalmist speaks on behalf of the community of believers. This assurance of divine intervention is echoed in other scriptures, such as Deuteronomy 32:35, where God declares that vengeance belongs to Him. The ultimate fulfillment of God's justice is seen in the New Testament through Jesus Christ, who will judge the living and the dead (Acts 10:42), bringing final justice to all.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant-keeping God of Israel, who is just and righteous, and who acts as the ultimate judge over all creation.

2. The Wicked
Those who act in opposition to God's laws and righteousness, often oppressing the righteous and engaging in iniquity.

3. The Psalmist
The author of the psalm, who is likely expressing a communal or individual lament and seeking God's justice against the wicked.
Teaching Points
Divine Justice
God is the ultimate judge who will bring justice to the wicked. Believers can trust in His timing and righteousness.

The Consequences of Iniquity
The wicked will face the consequences of their actions. This serves as a warning to avoid sin and live righteously.

Trust in God's Sovereignty
In times of injustice, believers are encouraged to trust in God's sovereign plan and His ability to right all wrongs.

The Role of Patience
Patience is essential as we wait for God's justice. We are called to endure and remain faithful, knowing that God will act.

Encouragement for the Righteous
The righteous can find comfort in knowing that God sees their plight and will ultimately vindicate them.

What Does Psalm 94:23 Mean
He will bring upon them their own iniquity

“​He will bring upon them their own iniquity”.

• God’s justice is perfectly fair: the consequences that return to the wicked are the exact results of their own choices (Psalm 7:15-16; Proverbs 26:27).

• This mirrors the sowing-and-reaping principle seen throughout Scripture (Galatians 6:7-8).

• The line reassures believers that evil never gets the last word; even when sin seems to prosper, it is already planting the seeds of its own downfall (Romans 1:24-25).

and destroy them for their wickedness

“​and destroy them for their wickedness”.

• The term destroy underscores the finality of divine judgment (Psalm 1:4-6; Nahum 1:2-3).

• Destruction here is not random; it is God’s righteous response to persistent, unrepentant rebellion (Revelation 19:2).

• For God’s people, the verse also draws a contrast: discipline refines the righteous, while destruction awaits those who harden their hearts (Jeremiah 23:19-20).

The LORD our God will destroy them

“​The LORD our God will destroy them”.

• By repeating the certainty of judgment and adding “our God,” the psalmist invites personal trust: vengeance belongs to the Lord, not to us (Deuteronomy 32:35; Romans 12:19).

• The covenant name “LORD” reminds readers that the Judge is also the faithful protector of His people (Psalm 94:22; 2 Thessalonians 1:6-9).

• Knowing that God Himself settles accounts frees believers to respond to evil with patience and integrity.

summary

• Evil ultimately collapses under its own weight—God makes sure of it.

• Divine judgment is measured, righteous, and certain; no wicked act escapes His notice.

• Believers can rest in God’s justice, refusing personal retaliation while trusting Him to set things right.

• Psalm 94:23 offers sober warning to the unrepentant and steady hope to those who cling to the Lord.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 23. - And he shall bring upon them their own iniquity. Most manifestly when he makes them fall into their own snare (Psalm 7:15; Psalm 35:8; Psalm 57:6; Psalm 141:9, 10), but really also whenever he punishes them for their sins. And shall cut them off; or, "destroy," "exterminate" them. In their own wickedness; or, "by their wickedness." The wicked man is often "hoist with his own petard." Yea, the Lord our God shall cut them off. The repetition, like that in ver. 1, is emphatic, and solemnly confirms the entire section (vers. 20-23).
