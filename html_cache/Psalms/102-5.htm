Through my loud groaning
This phrase captures the intense emotional and physical distress experienced by the psalmist. Groaning is often associated with deep sorrow or pain, reflecting a state of lamentation. In biblical context, groaning is a common expression of suffering, as seen in Exodus 2:23, where the Israelites groaned under Egyptian bondage. This groaning can also be seen as a form of prayer, a non-verbal cry for divine intervention, similar to Romans 8:26, where the Spirit intercedes with groans too deep for words.

my skin hangs on my bones
This vivid imagery suggests severe physical emaciation, often associated with prolonged suffering or fasting. In the ancient Near Eastern context, such a description would indicate a state of extreme weakness or illness. This imagery is echoed in Job 19:20, where Job describes his own suffering in similar terms. The physical decline of the psalmist can be seen as a metaphor for spiritual desolation, pointing to the need for God's restoration. This phrase also foreshadows the suffering of Christ, who endured physical torment and was described in Isaiah 53:3-5 as a man of sorrows, familiar with suffering.

Teaching Points
Understanding Suffering
Suffering is a part of the human experience, and the Bible does not shy away from expressing deep anguish and pain.

The Physical Impact of Emotional Distress
Emotional and spiritual struggles can manifest physically, as seen in the Psalmist's description of his bones clinging to his flesh.

Crying Out to God
The Psalmist's loud groaning is a reminder that we can and should bring our deepest pains and struggles before God in prayer.

Hope in God's Deliverance
Despite the immediate suffering, the broader context of the Psalm points to hope and restoration through God's intervention.

Empathy and Support
As believers, we are called to empathize with those who are suffering and offer support, reflecting God's compassion and love.

What Does Psalm 102:5 Mean
Through my loud groaning

• The psalmist gives voice to deep distress: “Through my loud groaning…” (Psalm 102:5).

• This is more than a silent sigh; it is a continuous, audible cry that reflects anguish of soul.

– Similar expressions appear in Psalm 22:1-2, where David laments, “I cry out by day… but You do not answer.”

– Lamentations 3:56 records Jeremiah pleading, “You heard my plea: ‘Do not ignore my cry for relief.’”

• Such groaning acknowledges God as the only One who can hear and act, illustrating genuine dependence (Psalm 34:17; Romans 8:26).

• It also invites the Lord to draw near to the brokenhearted (Psalm 34:18), affirming that honest lament is a pathway to hope (Psalm 42:11).

my skin hangs on my bones

• The result of prolonged sorrow is physical deterioration: “my skin hangs on my bones” (Psalm 102:5).

• The wording pictures extreme weight loss—emaciation that often accompanies grief, prolonged fasting, or illness.

– Job 19:20 echoes this: “My skin and flesh cling to my bones.”

– In Psalm 32:3-4, David speaks of unconfessed sin draining his strength “as in the heat of summer.”

• Scripture consistently links spiritual or emotional agony with tangible bodily effects (Proverbs 17:22; 2 Corinthians 1:8-9).

• By admitting visible weakness, the psalmist underscores that human strength fails but God’s mercy endures (Isaiah 40:29-31).

summary

Psalm 102:5 describes the psalmist’s heartache so intense that it spills out in constant cries and carves visible hollows in his body. His loud groaning signals utter dependence on God, while his wasted frame testifies to the cost of enduring sorrow. Together these phrases invite believers to bring every pain to the Lord, confident that He hears, sustains, and ultimately restores those who trust Him.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(5) Skin.--See margin. In Lamentations 4:8, more correctly, "my skin cleaveth to my bones;" a picture of emaciation, the result of fasting.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 5. - By reason of the voice of my groaning; i.e. "by reason of the affliction which causes my groaning." My bones cleave to my skin; literally, to my flesh, but the Authorized Version rightly expresses the meaning (comp. Job 19:20; Lamentations 4:8).
