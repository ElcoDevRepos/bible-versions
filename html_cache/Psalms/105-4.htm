Seek out the LORD and His strength;
This phrase emphasizes the importance of actively pursuing a relationship with God. In the historical context of ancient Israel, seeking the LORD involved worship, prayer, and adherence to the covenant. The strength of the LORD refers to His power and might, which were evident in the deliverance of Israel from Egypt and other acts of divine intervention. This call to seek God's strength is echoed in other scriptures, such as Isaiah 40:31, which speaks of those who wait on the LORD renewing their strength. Theologically, this pursuit is a recognition of human dependence on divine power, contrasting with self-reliance. The strength of the LORD is also seen as a type of Christ, who embodies God's power and offers spiritual strength to believers.

seek His face always.
Seeking God's face implies a desire for His presence and favor. In the cultural context of the Bible, seeking someone's face was a metaphor for seeking their attention and approval. This phrase suggests a continuous, ongoing effort to live in a way that is pleasing to God. The idea of seeking God's face is found throughout scripture, such as in 2 Chronicles 7:14, where God promises to hear from heaven if His people humble themselves and seek His face. This pursuit is not just for times of trouble but is a constant endeavor, reflecting a life of devotion and worship. In the New Testament, this is fulfilled in the person of Jesus Christ, who provides believers with access to the Father, as seen in John 14:6.

What Does Psalm 105:4 Mean
Seek out the LORD

“Seek out the LORD” (Psalm 105:4a) calls for deliberate pursuit, not casual curiosity.

• To seek is to set the heart, mind, time, and choices toward Him—echoing King David’s charge, “Set your hearts and your souls to seek the LORD your God” (1 Chronicles 22:19).

• It means approaching His Word expecting truth, as Ezra did when he “set his heart to study the Law of the LORD, to practice it, and to teach” (Ezra 7:10).

• It involves drawing near in worship, confident that “You will seek Me and find Me when you search for Me with all your heart” (Jeremiah 29:13).

The verse places the LORD Himself—His person, presence, and revealed will—at the center of life’s quest.

and His strength

We are urged to pursue “His strength” (Psalm 105:4b) rather than rely on our own.

• Scripture never flatters human ability; instead, it reminds us, “The LORD is my strength and my shield” (Psalm 28:7).

• His strength sustains daily obedience—Paul testified, “I labor, striving with His power, which so powerfully works in me” (Colossians 1:29).

• It also secures victory in trials, as promised: “Those who wait upon the LORD will renew their strength” (Isaiah 40:31).

To seek His strength is to admit our frailty and receive the enabling grace that accomplishes His purposes through us.

seek His face always

“Seek His face always” (Psalm 105:4c) speaks of constant, eager fellowship.

• The face of God represents His favor and intimate presence: “Make Your face shine upon Your servant” (Psalm 31:16).

• This pursuit is continual—“Pray without ceasing” (1 Thessalonians 5:17)—because relationship with God is not an event but a lifestyle.

• It is a forward-looking hope: “Your face, LORD, I will seek” (Psalm 27:8), foreshadowing the ultimate joy of seeing Him “face to face” (1 Corinthians 13:12).

Steady communion guards the heart, guides decisions, and fuels worship every moment.

summary

Psalm 105:4 strings together three imperatives that form one seamless call: intentionally pursue the LORD Himself, depend on His limitless power, and maintain unbroken fellowship with Him. Life centered on these realities stands strong, stays joyful, and walks in step with the God who faithfully reveals Himself to those who seek Him.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(4) Seek the Lord.--Better, Enquire after Jehovah and his power. The congregation is directed to the historical survey which follows. This sense seems settled by Psalm 111:2 : "The works of Jehovah are great, enquired into by all those who take delight in them." And hence the word "strength" must be understood as used generally of the manifestation of Divine power in the wondrous deeds now to be mentioned.
