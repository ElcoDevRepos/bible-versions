Show me Your ways, O LORD;
This phrase is a plea for divine guidance and understanding. The psalmist seeks to know God's ways, which implies a desire for a deeper relationship with God and an understanding of His will. In biblical context, "ways" often refers to God's laws, commandments, and the moral path He sets for His people. This request reflects a heart of humility and dependence on God, acknowledging that human wisdom is insufficient. The phrase echoes the sentiment found in Exodus 33:13, where Moses asks God to show him His ways so that he may know Him better. It also connects to the wisdom literature, such as Proverbs 3:5-6, which advises trusting in the Lord and not leaning on one's own understanding.

teach me Your paths.
The request for God to "teach" indicates a willingness to learn and be instructed by the divine. "Paths" in this context can be understood as the specific directions or courses of action that align with God's will. This reflects the cultural and historical context of a disciple learning from a master, a common practice in ancient times. The psalmist's desire to be taught by God highlights the importance of divine instruction over human knowledge. This phrase can be linked to the teachings of Jesus, who often spoke of the narrow path that leads to life (Matthew 7:13-14). It also resonates with the prophetic promise of the New Covenant in Jeremiah 31:33-34, where God writes His law on the hearts of His people, teaching them directly.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of Psalm 25, King David, is expressing a heartfelt plea for guidance and instruction from God. Known as a man after God's own heart, David often sought divine direction in his life.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God used here emphasizes His eternal, self-existent nature and His relationship with His people. David is appealing to Yahweh for personal guidance.

3. Paths and Ways
These terms symbolize the moral and spiritual direction that God provides. In Hebrew, "ways" (derek) and "paths" (orach) suggest a journey or course of life that aligns with God's will.
Teaching Points
Seeking Divine Guidance
As believers, we should regularly seek God's guidance in our lives, recognizing that His ways are higher than ours.

The Importance of Scripture
God's paths are revealed through His Word. Regular study and meditation on Scripture are essential for understanding His will.

Prayer as a Means of Guidance
Like David, we should approach God in prayer, asking Him to reveal His ways and teach us His paths.

Trust in God's Sovereignty
Trust that God's guidance is perfect and that He will lead us in the right direction, even when we do not understand His ways.

Living a Life Aligned with God's Will
Our daily decisions and actions should reflect a commitment to walking in the paths God has set before us.

What Does Psalm 25:4 Mean
Show me Your ways, O LORD

David’s opening plea is an open-handed request for divine direction—“Show me Your ways, O LORD” (Psalm 25:4a). He is not asking for a vague hint; he wants God’s ways displayed plainly so he can follow them.

• Humble dependence: David shifts attention from his own instincts to the Lord’s proven ways (Psalm 119:33-34; Proverbs 3:5-6).

• Expectation of clarity: Because God “makes known the path of life” (Psalm 16:11), David trusts the Lord will actually reveal what to do next.

• Relational aspect: “Your ways” points to God’s character expressed in action—His covenant faithfulness, mercy, and righteousness (Psalm 25:10; Exodus 34:6-7). Knowing God’s ways means knowing God Himself.

• Everyday relevance: The request covers all arenas—family, work, worship. Just as the pillar of cloud visibly guided Israel (Exodus 13:21-22), David asks for unmistakable guidance today.

teach me Your paths

The prayer moves from seeing to learning: “teach me Your paths” (Psalm 25:4b). David desires more than information; he wants formation.

• Lifelong discipleship: “Teach” implies ongoing instruction, echoing Psalm 86:11, “Teach me Your way, O LORD, that I may walk in Your truth.”

• Walking, not wandering: A “path” is a well-trodden route. God’s paths are established, safe, and straight (Psalm 23:3; Isaiah 35:8).

• Moral alignment: God “guides the humble in what is right” (Psalm 25:9). Learning His paths means adopting His standards of holiness, justice, and compassion (Micah 6:8).

• Empowered obedience: The same God who commands also enables. His Word is “a lamp to my feet and a light to my path” (Psalm 119:105), empowering daily choices that match His will.

• Forward momentum: Paths lead somewhere. David expects progress toward the destination God intends—ultimately conformity to Christ (Romans 8:29) and arrival in God’s presence (Psalm 73:24).

summary

Psalm 25:4 is a two-part prayer for revelation and instruction. David looks to the Lord to (1) uncover the right way and (2) train him to walk it. With confidence in the literal truthfulness of Scripture, we can echo this request, trusting the God who speaks to also guide, teach, and sustain us on every step of His dependable path.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 4. - Show me thy ways, O Lord; teach me thy paths. An echo of the prayer of Moses when his people were rebellious at Sinai (Exodus 33:13), reiterated by David in Psalm 27:11, and perhaps again in Psalm 86:11 (see also Psalm 119:33). Man is so wanting in spiritual understanding, so morally blind and ignorant, that, unless enlightened from on high, he cannot discern aright the "way of godliness;" he does not know at any given moment what God would have him to do. Hence it is the constant prayer of every religious man that God will "lighten his darkness;" "make his way plain before his face;" "show him the path that he should walk in;" enable him to see, if no more, at any rate the next step which it is his duty to take. The idea has been beautifully expressed by a modern poet -

"Lead, kindly Light, amid the encircling gloom
Lead thou me on.
The night is dark, and I am far from homo;
Lead thou me on.
Keep thou my feet; I do not ask to see
The distant scene; one step enough for me."
