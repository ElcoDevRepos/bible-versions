The right hand of the LORD is exalted!
The "right hand" in biblical terms often symbolizes power and authority. In ancient Near Eastern cultures, the right hand was considered the hand of strength and skill. The phrase "is exalted" suggests that God's power is supreme and lifted high above all others. This can be connected to Exodus 15:6, where the right hand of God is described as glorious in power, defeating the enemies of Israel. The exaltation of God's right hand signifies His ultimate authority and victory over all adversaries, a theme that resonates throughout the Psalms and the entire Bible.

The right hand of the LORD performs with valor!
The repetition of "the right hand of the LORD" emphasizes the certainty and reliability of God's intervention. "Performs with valor" indicates acts of bravery and strength, suggesting that God's actions are not only powerful but also courageous and noble. This can be seen in the historical context of Israel's deliverance from Egypt and their victories in the Promised Land, where God's intervention was both mighty and honorable. The valor of God's right hand is a type of Christ, who is the ultimate manifestation of God's power and courage, fulfilling prophecies and bringing salvation through His death and resurrection. This phrase echoes the assurance found in Isaiah 41:10, where God promises to uphold His people with His righteous right hand.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The central figure in this verse, representing God's power and authority. The "right hand" symbolizes His strength and active intervention in the world.

2. Psalmist (Author of Psalm 118)
Traditionally attributed to King David, though the exact authorship is uncertain. The psalmist expresses gratitude and praise for God's deliverance and might.

3. Israel
The nation of Israel is often the recipient of God's mighty acts, and this psalm is a communal song of thanksgiving for His deliverance.

4. Temple Worship
Psalm 118 is part of the Hallel, a series of psalms (113-118) sung during Jewish festivals, particularly Passover, highlighting God's deliverance and faithfulness.

5. Exodus Event
While not directly mentioned, the themes of deliverance and God's mighty hand echo the Exodus, where God delivered Israel from Egypt with a "mighty hand and an outstretched arm."
Teaching Points
God's Sovereign Power
The "right hand of the LORD" symbolizes His ultimate authority and power over all creation. Believers can trust in His sovereign control over their lives.

Divine Intervention
Just as God intervened for Israel, He continues to act in the lives of His people today. We should remain vigilant and thankful for His ongoing work.

Exaltation of God
The exaltation of God's right hand calls us to worship and praise Him for His mighty deeds. Our response should be one of reverence and gratitude.

Courage in God's Strength
Knowing that God's right hand performs with valor gives believers courage to face challenges, relying on His strength rather than their own.

Community of Faith
Psalm 118 is a communal song, reminding us of the importance of gathering with fellow believers to celebrate and acknowledge God's mighty works.

What Does Psalm 118:16 Mean
The right hand of the LORD is exalted!

• “Right hand” pictures supreme power and authority. Exodus 15:6 celebrates, “Your right hand, O LORD, is majestic in power; Your right hand, O LORD, shatters the enemy.”

• The psalmist lifts that hand up for all to see. Isaiah 26:11 notes, “Your hand is lifted up.” God’s deeds stand above human schemes.

• Exaltation also foreshadows Christ. Acts 2:33 says Jesus was “exalted to the right hand of God.” The same mighty hand that parted seas now enthrones the risen Savior.

• For worshipers, exaltation invites praise. Psalm 63:8 affirms, “My soul clings to You; Your right hand upholds me,” so we respond by lifting our voices and lives in gratitude.

The right hand of the LORD performs with valor!

• Valor stresses courageous, victorious action. Psalm 118’s context is rescue from hostile nations; God fights and wins for His people.

• Scripture repeatedly depicts that valor:

– Deuteronomy 26:8: “The LORD brought us out of Egypt with a mighty hand and an outstretched arm.”

– Psalm 98:1: “His right hand and holy arm have worked salvation for Him.”

• The ultimate valiant act is Christ’s resurrection. Ephesians 1:19-20 speaks of “the immeasurable greatness of His power…when He raised Him from the dead and seated Him at His right hand.”

• Because God’s hand still “performs with valor,” believers face trials with confidence. Romans 8:37 declares, “In all these things we are more than conquerors through Him who loved us.”

summary

Psalm 118:16 highlights God’s unrivaled power (“right hand”), His public honor (“is exalted”), and His decisive, courageous intervention (“performs with valor”). The verse calls us to recognize, celebrate, and trust the Lord who has acted—most fully in Christ—and who still acts powerfully on behalf of His people today.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(16) Is exalted.--Here evidently the attitude of a warrior. The hand is lifted up to strike.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 16. - The right hand of the Lord is exalted (compare the parallel expression in Exodus 15:6, "Thy right hand, O God, is become glorious in power"). When God's right hand effects a deliverance, it gets, as it were, additional glory to itself. The right hand of the Lord doeth valiantly. So, already, in ver. 16. Repetition is a special characteristic of this psalm (see vers. 1, 2, 3, 4; vers. 8, 9; vers. 10-12, etc.).
