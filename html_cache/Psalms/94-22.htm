But the LORD has been my stronghold
This phrase emphasizes the personal relationship and trust the psalmist has in the LORD as a source of protection and security. In the historical context of ancient Israel, a stronghold was a fortified place, often a high tower or a walled city, providing safety from enemies. This imagery is used throughout the Psalms to describe God's protective nature (e.g., Psalm 18:2, Psalm 46:1). The use of "LORD" (YHWH) signifies the covenantal name of God, highlighting His faithfulness and unchanging nature. This reflects the belief that God is a reliable protector against both physical and spiritual adversaries.

and my God is my rock of refuge
The metaphor of God as a "rock" conveys strength, stability, and permanence. In the geographical context of Israel, rocks and cliffs were natural places of refuge and safety, often used by people to hide from danger. This imagery is prevalent in the Old Testament, symbolizing God's unchanging and dependable nature (e.g., Deuteronomy 32:4, 1 Samuel 2:2). The term "refuge" further underscores the idea of God as a safe haven, a place of shelter from life's storms and adversities. This phrase also points to the typology of Christ as the ultimate refuge for believers, fulfilling the role of protector and savior, as seen in the New Testament (e.g., Matthew 7:24-25, 1 Corinthians 10:4).

Teaching Points
God as Our Protector
In times of trouble, we can rely on God as our stronghold and refuge. He is our ultimate protector against life's adversities.

The Stability of God's Character
Just as a rock is unchanging, so is God's nature. We can trust in His steadfastness and faithfulness in every situation.

Personal Relationship with God
The psalmist's declaration of "my God" indicates a personal relationship. We are invited to cultivate a personal and intimate relationship with God, recognizing Him as our refuge.

Trust in God's Deliverance
Even when circumstances seem overwhelming, we are encouraged to trust in God's ability to deliver and sustain us.

Living with Confidence
Knowing that God is our stronghold allows us to live with confidence and peace, regardless of external challenges.

What Does Psalm 94:22 Mean
But

- The little connective word signals a decisive pivot. Up to this point (Psalm 94:1-21), the psalmist has cataloged injustice and pleaded for God to act. “But” turns the camera away from the turbulence and fixes it on God’s character. (Psalm 73:26; 2 Corinthians 4:8-9)

- For believers today, the word affirms that circumstances never have the last word; God does.

the LORD has been my stronghold

- “The LORD” (YHWH) highlights God’s covenant name—the One who keeps every promise. (Exodus 3:14-15; Malachi 3:6)

- “Has been” looks back over real history. The psalmist’s faith rests on a track record, not wishful thinking. Recall David’s testimony in Psalm 18:2: “The LORD is my rock, my fortress, and my deliverer.”

- “My stronghold” speaks of a fortified place:

• Impenetrable security (Psalm 27:1).

• Steady protection when enemies advance (Psalm 46:1-3).

• A shelter that frees the heart from fear so obedience can flourish.

- Personal pronouns matter: God is not merely “a” refuge but “my” one (Psalm 118:14). Saving faith is relational, not theoretical.

and my God is my rock of refuge

- “My God” re-emphasizes intimacy while confessing His supremacy (Deuteronomy 10:17).

- “Rock” conveys:

• Permanence—He does not erode with cultural tides (Isaiah 26:4).

• Strength—no foe can dislodge Him (Psalm 62:7).

• Elevation—He lifts His people above the flood of evil (Psalm 40:2).

- “Refuge” adds warmth to the imagery: the rock is not cold stone but a welcoming sanctuary. Picture the cities of refuge (Joshua 20) where the pursued found safety under God’s law.

- Together, the terms point to total security: external (stronghold) and internal (refuge for the soul).

summary

When evil seems unrestrained, Psalm 94:22 calls believers to pivot from what threatens to Who protects. The psalmist stakes everything on the covenant LORD: He has already proven Himself a fortress, and He remains a personal, immovable rock offering full refuge. Resting in Him, we stand secure, steadied for faithful living no matter the surrounding turmoil.
