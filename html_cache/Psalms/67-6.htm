The earth has yielded its harvest;
This phrase reflects the agricultural context of ancient Israel, where the success of crops was seen as a direct blessing from God. The harvest was a time of joy and thanksgiving, often associated with the Feast of Tabernacles, a festival celebrating God's provision (Leviticus 23:39-43). The imagery of the earth yielding its harvest can also be seen as a metaphor for spiritual fruitfulness, as in the parable of the sower (Matthew 13:23). The earth's productivity is a testament to God's faithfulness in creation, echoing the promise of Genesis 8:22 that seedtime and harvest will not cease.

God, our God, blesses us.
This phrase emphasizes the personal relationship between God and His people, highlighting the covenantal aspect of God's blessings. The repetition of "God, our God" underscores the intimacy and exclusivity of this relationship, reminiscent of the covenant language found in Exodus 6:7, where God declares, "I will take you as my own people, and I will be your God." The blessing here is not just material but also spiritual, pointing to the ultimate blessing found in Jesus Christ, who fulfills the promises of God (Ephesians 1:3). This assurance of blessing is a call to trust in God's provision and to live in gratitude and obedience.

Teaching Points
God as Provider
Recognize that all blessings, both physical and spiritual, come from God. We should cultivate a heart of gratitude for His provision.

Dependence on God
Just as the earth depends on God for its harvest, we must depend on Him for our needs and spiritual growth.

Blessings and Responsibility
With blessings come responsibilities. We are called to use what God has given us to bless others and further His kingdom.

Faith and Patience
Trust in God’s timing for the harvest in our lives, whether it be in personal growth, ministry, or other areas.

Worship and Praise
Our response to God’s blessings should be worship and praise, acknowledging His goodness and faithfulness.

What Does Psalm 67:6 Mean
The earth

- Scripture begins by naming creation itself: “The earth…” (Psalm 24:1).

- This reminds us that every field, forest, and backyard garden belongs to the Lord (Psalm 50:10-12).

- From Eden onward, God designed the planet to sustain life (Genesis 1:29-30) and to testify to His glory (Romans 1:20).

- The psalmist looks at the soil under his sandals and sees God’s faithful handiwork.

has yielded

- The phrase points to a completed action—seedtime has led to increase (Genesis 8:22).

- Harvest does not happen by chance; rainfall, sunshine, and seasons arrive on schedule because the Creator “opens His hand and satisfies the desire of every living thing” (Psalm 145:16).

- Israel knew this first-hand: obedience brought “rain in its season, and the land shall yield its produce” (Leviticus 26:4-5).

- Even in years of drought, believers trust the unchanging promise of provision (Philippians 4:19).

its harvest

- “Harvest” highlights abundance—grain piled high, fruit baskets overflowing (Psalm 65:9-13).

- Tangible crops picture spiritual fruit as well (John 15:5; Galatians 5:22-23).

- God intends His people to enjoy the yield and to share it: corners of the field for the poor (Leviticus 19:9-10) and firstfruits for His house (Proverbs 3:9-10).

- Every kernel and cluster whispers the same truth: the Lord is generous.

God, our God

- The double name—“God, our God”—moves from the universal Creator to the covenant Friend (Exodus 3:15; Deuteronomy 6:4-5).

- He is not a distant deity but the One who says, “I will be your God, and you will be My people” (Jeremiah 31:33).

- Personal pronouns matter: “our” signals relationship, security, and belonging (Psalm 118:28; Romans 8:15-17).

- The heart of worship is recognizing that the same God who paints sunsets also calls us His own.

blesses us

- The verse ends where every blessing begins—with God’s favor (James 1:17).

- Physical plenty and spiritual peace flow from His hand (Ephesians 1:3).

- Blessing is never merely inward-focused; God prospers His people so “all the ends of the earth will fear Him” (Psalm 67:7).

- Abraham heard it first: “I will bless you… and all the families of the earth will be blessed through you” (Genesis 12:2-3).

- The psalmist responds not with pride but with gratitude and a desire to make God’s name known among the nations (Psalm 67:2).

summary

Psalm 67:6 celebrates a literal, physical harvest that highlights God’s faithfulness. The soil has done its work under His sustaining care, and the grain now testifies that “God, our God, blesses us.” Every stalk and sheaf points to a deeper reality: the Lord provides generously, claims us personally, and blesses us purposefully so that His glory can spread to every people and place.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(6) Then shall the earth yield her increase.--It seems more in keeping with the expression of thanks to render here with the LXX. and Vulg., "The land hath yielded her increase."

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 6. - Then shall the earth yield her increase; literally, the land hath given her increase - a quotation from Leviticus 26:4, but probably in a metaphorical sense. An abundant spiritual harvest is seen by the psalmist as the result of the entrance of the Gentiles into the Church - an immense "increase" in the fruits of righteousness hitherto yielded - and this is spoken of as a result already obtained, through the perfect assurance of the writer that his prayers are granted and the result determined on in the Divine counsels. And God, even our own God, shall bless us. "Our own God" must certainly be Jehovah; but the writer, in his broad universalism, will not use the expression.
