Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
And the LORD made His people very fruitful
This phrase highlights God's covenant faithfulness to Israel, fulfilling His promise to Abraham to make his descendants as numerous as the stars (Genesis 15:5). The fruitfulness of the Israelites in Egypt is a testament to God's blessing, despite their oppression. This mirrors the creation mandate in Genesis 1:28, where God commands humanity to be fruitful and multiply. Theologically, it underscores God's sovereignty and providence, ensuring the growth and prosperity of His chosen people even in foreign lands.

more numerous than their foes
This part of the verse reflects the historical context of Israel's time in Egypt, where they grew in number despite being enslaved (Exodus 1:7-12). The increase in population was seen as a threat by the Egyptians, leading to harsher oppression. This multiplication is a fulfillment of God's promise and a demonstration of His power to protect and preserve His people. It also foreshadows the eventual Exodus, where God delivers His people from their enemies. In a broader biblical context, this can be seen as a type of Christ's victory over sin and death, where the Church, His body, grows and prevails against spiritual adversaries (Matthew 16:18).

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant-keeping God of Israel, who is faithful to His promises and actively involved in the lives of His people.

2. His People (Israelites)
The descendants of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, chosen by God to be His special possession and to fulfill His purposes on earth.

3. Foes (Egyptians)
The oppressors of the Israelites during their time in Egypt, representing the forces that stand against God's people.

4. Egypt
The land where the Israelites were enslaved, symbolizing a place of bondage and opposition to God's plans.

5. Fruitfulness
The blessing of multiplication and growth, both in numbers and in spiritual prosperity, as a sign of God's favor.
Teaching Points
God's Faithfulness
God is faithful to His promises, even in the face of opposition. Just as He made the Israelites fruitful in Egypt, He can bring growth and blessing in our lives despite challenges.

Divine Sovereignty
The increase of God's people is a result of His sovereign will. We can trust that God is in control and working for our good, even when circumstances seem unfavorable.

Spiritual Fruitfulness
While physical multiplication is a blessing, spiritual fruitfulness is equally important. We should seek to grow in the fruits of the Spirit and in our relationship with God.

Overcoming Opposition
The growth of the Israelites despite Egyptian oppression reminds us that God can help us overcome any opposition we face in our spiritual journey.

Community Growth
Just as God multiplied the Israelites, He desires for His church to grow in unity and number. We should actively participate in the mission of the church to reach others with the gospel.

What Does Psalm 105:24 Mean
And the LORD

• The verse begins by spotlighting the sole Initiator: “the LORD” (YHWH), covenant-keeping God.

• Israel’s story is driven by His promises, not by human ingenuity (Exodus 2:24-25; Psalm 44:1-3).

• Every blessing that follows is rooted in His sovereign grace—“Every good and perfect gift is from above” (James 1:17).

made His people very fruitful

• “Made” underscores divine action; Israel’s fruitfulness is engineered by God, not luck.

• “Very fruitful” recalls the creation mandate “Be fruitful and multiply” (Genesis 1:28) and God’s pledge to Abraham, “I will make you exceedingly fruitful” (Genesis 17:6).

• In Egypt “the Israelites were fruitful, increased greatly, multiplied, and became exceedingly numerous” (Exodus 1:7).

• Fruitfulness points to:

– Physical multiplication—families expanding rapidly (Deuteronomy 7:13-14).

– Covenant fulfillment—God keeping His word despite oppression (Hebrews 6:13-18).

– Missional purpose—an ever-growing people to display His glory (Isaiah 43:7, 10-11).

more numerous than their foes

• God tipped the demographic scales in favor of His people; He out-numbers their adversaries.

• Though Egypt wielded political power, Israel’s headcount kept rising (Exodus 12:37; Deuteronomy 26:5).

• This reversal highlights:

– Divine protection—oppressors could not stem the growth (Exodus 1:12).

– Strategic positioning—larger numbers readied Israel for the Exodus and conquest (Numbers 1:46; Deuteronomy 7:1).

– A pattern of victory—God’s people often stand strong “though they are greater and stronger than we” (2 Chronicles 14:11), because “If God is for us, who can be against us?” (Romans 8:31).

summary

Psalm 105:24 celebrates God’s faithfulness: He Himself multiplied Israel and made them outnumber their enemies, proving His covenant true and His power unbeatable. The verse calls believers to rest in the same faithful Lord who still grows, protects, and advances His people today.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 24. - And he increased his people greatly (comp. Exodus 1:7, 12, 20). And made them stronger than their enemies. So the Pharaoh who introduced the hard bondage, "The people of the children of Israel are more and mightier than we" (Exodus 1:9).
