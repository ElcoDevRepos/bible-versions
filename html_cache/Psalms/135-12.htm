He gave their land as an inheritance
This phrase refers to God's promise and fulfillment of giving the land of Canaan to the Israelites. The concept of land as an inheritance is deeply rooted in the Abrahamic covenant, where God promised Abraham that his descendants would inherit the land (Genesis 12:7, 15:18-21). This inheritance was not just a physical possession but a divine gift, symbolizing God's faithfulness and the fulfillment of His promises. The land was to be a place where Israel could live in obedience to God, serving as a light to the nations. The idea of inheritance also points to the spiritual inheritance believers receive through Christ, as seen in Ephesians 1:11.

as a heritage to His people Israel
The term "heritage" emphasizes the special relationship between God and Israel. It signifies a possession that is passed down through generations, highlighting the continuity of God's promises. This heritage was not earned by Israel's merit but was a result of God's sovereign choice and love (Deuteronomy 7:6-8). The land served as a tangible sign of God's covenant with Israel, setting them apart as His chosen people. This concept of heritage also foreshadows the spiritual heritage believers have in Christ, who is the fulfillment of the law and the prophets (Matthew 5:17). The land of Israel remains a significant symbol of God's enduring covenant with His people, and it plays a crucial role in eschatological prophecies concerning the end times (Ezekiel 37:21-28).

Persons / Places / Events
1. God (Yahweh)
The central figure in this verse, God is the one who gives the land as an inheritance. His sovereignty and faithfulness are highlighted.

2. Israel
The nation chosen by God to receive the inheritance. This refers to the descendants of Jacob, the twelve tribes of Israel.

3. The Promised Land
The land given to Israel, often referred to as Canaan, which God promised to Abraham and his descendants.

4. The Exodus
The event where God delivered the Israelites from slavery in Egypt, leading them to the Promised Land.

5. Conquest of Canaan
The historical event where the Israelites, under leaders like Joshua, took possession of the land God promised them.
Teaching Points
God's Faithfulness
God keeps His promises. Just as He gave Israel the land, He will fulfill His promises to us.

Inheritance in Christ
Believers have a spiritual inheritance in Christ, which is eternal and secure.

Trust in God's Timing
The Israelites waited many years to receive their inheritance. We must trust God's timing in our lives.

Obedience and Faith
The Israelites had to act in faith and obedience to possess the land. Similarly, we must live in obedience to receive God's blessings.

Gratitude for God's Gifts
Recognize and be thankful for the spiritual and material blessings God has given us.

What Does Psalm 135:12 Mean
He gave their land

- The initiative is entirely God’s. Israel did not conquer Canaan by sheer military genius; “it was not by their sword that they took the land… but by Your right hand” (Psalm 44:3; cf. Deuteronomy 7:1-2; Joshua 10:42).

- This underscores divine sovereignty—God rules history and carves out borders (Acts 17:26).

- Because the land grant rests on God’s promise, it is secure. Joshua could later say, “The LORD gave Israel all the land He had sworn to give their fathers” (Joshua 21:43).

as an inheritance

- Inheritance means something passed down by a benefactor to rightful heirs; it is not earned wages but a gift.

- Numbers 26:53-55 lays out how the land would be “divided as an inheritance,” showing orderly, covenant-based distribution.

- Moses longed to see this inheritance (Deuteronomy 4:21), and the prophets appealed to it whenever the nation drifted, reminding them of their God-given legacy (Jeremiah 3:19).

- New-covenant believers learn from this that our salvation is likewise an unearned inheritance (Ephesians 1:11; 1 Peter 1:4), secured by the same faithful God.

as a heritage

- Heritage carries the idea of something treasured, safeguarded, and celebrated across generations.

- Psalm 16:6 exults, “Indeed, I have a beautiful inheritance,” capturing the joy of living within God’s allotted portion.

- A heritage is preserved, not squandered. When Israel forgot this, exile followed (2 Chronicles 36:21), proving the seriousness of cherishing what God bequeaths.

- For families today, the concept encourages passing on faith, not mere possessions (2 Timothy 1:5), because God’s gifts are designed to bless descendants.

to His people Israel

- The recipients are specific: “His people,” the covenant family anchored in Abrahamic promises (Genesis 12:7; 15:18).

- Exodus 6:7 captures God’s heart: “I will take you as My own people, and I will be your God.” Land, law, and loving relationship converge here.

- Paul affirms that God has “not rejected His people, whom He foreknew” (Romans 11:1-2), showing the ongoing validity of the promise.

- Gentile believers are “grafted in” (Romans 11:17-18), sharing spiritual blessings while still honoring God’s unique dealings with ethnic Israel.

summary

Psalm 135:12 celebrates the Lord’s gracious gift of Canaan. He alone secured the land, designated it as an unearned inheritance, established it as a cherished heritage, and gave it specifically to Israel, His covenant people. The verse invites us to trust the same faithful God, treasure what He entrusts to us, and live gratefully within His promises.
