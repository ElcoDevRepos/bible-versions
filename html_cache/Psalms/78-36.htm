But they deceived Him with their mouths
This phrase highlights the insincerity of the Israelites in their relationship with God. Throughout their history, the Israelites often professed loyalty to God verbally, but their actions did not align with their words. This behavior is reminiscent of the warning in Isaiah 29:13, where God laments that the people honor Him with their lips while their hearts are far from Him. The act of deception here is not just a failure in communication but a deeper spiritual issue, reflecting a lack of true faith and commitment. This theme of hypocrisy is also echoed in the New Testament, where Jesus criticizes the Pharisees for similar behavior (Matthew 15:8).

and lied to Him with their tongues
The use of "lied" underscores the deliberate nature of their falsehoods. In the cultural context of ancient Israel, truthfulness was a covenantal obligation, and lying was a serious breach of trust. The Israelites' lies to God can be seen as a violation of the covenant established at Sinai, where they promised to obey God's commands (Exodus 24:7). This breach is not just a personal failing but a communal one, affecting their relationship with God and their standing as His chosen people. The phrase also foreshadows the ultimate truthfulness and faithfulness of Jesus Christ, who is described as "the way, the truth, and the life" (John 14:6), contrasting the deceitfulness of humanity with the perfect integrity of the Savior.

Teaching Points
The Danger of Lip Service
God desires genuine worship and obedience, not just empty words. Our relationship with Him should be marked by sincerity and truth.

The Consequences of Deception
Deceiving God with insincere words can lead to spiritual stagnation and distance from Him. True repentance and honesty are crucial.

The Importance of Heartfelt Worship
Worship should be an expression of our true devotion and love for God, not just a ritualistic practice.

Self-Examination and Repentance
Regularly examine your heart and motives in worship and service to ensure they align with God's desires.

The Role of the Tongue in Faith
Our words should reflect our faith and commitment to God. We must be mindful of how we use our tongues in our relationship with Him and others.

What Does Psalm 78:36 Mean
But they deceived Him with their mouths

Psalm 78 recounts Israel’s history of half–hearted religion. Just after the Lord struck them and “they turned back and sought God” (v. 34), we read that their pursuit was only skin–deep.

• They offered words of contrition, but their hearts stayed stubborn (v. 37).

• Isaiah 29:13 echoes the same pattern: “This people draw near with their mouths… yet their hearts are far from Me.” Jesus quotes it in Matthew 15:8–9 to expose empty worship.

• Ezekiel 33:31 pictures crowds who “sit before you to hear your words, but they will not do them.”

• God can never actually be fooled (Galatians 6:7), yet Israel went through the motions as if He could be.

Practical takeaway: verbal piety without inner loyalty is self–deception. James 1:26 warns that a person who “does not bridle his tongue deceives his heart, and his religion is worthless.”

and lied to Him with their tongues

Their false promises amounted to lies against the very covenant they claimed to honor.

• At Sinai they cried, “All that the LORD has spoken we will do” (Exodus 19:8), yet quickly violated those vows (Exodus 32).

• Joshua confronted the same duplicity in Joshua 24:19–22: “You are witnesses against yourselves… You have chosen to serve the LORD.”

• Psalm 51:6 reminds us that God “desires truth in the inmost being,” while Proverbs 12:22 states, “Lying lips are detestable to the LORD.”

• Ananias and Sapphira’s fate (Acts 5:1–11) shows the ongoing seriousness of lying to God.

Forms this lying can take:

– Making promises of obedience to gain relief in a crisis, then forgetting them.

– Singing praise while harboring rebellion (Amos 5:23–24).

– Confessing sin with no intent to forsake it (Proverbs 28:13).

Faithfulness, not flattery, is what the covenant requires (1 Samuel 15:22). Revelation 21:8 places “all liars” with the unrepentant, underscoring that God’s people must be people of truth.

summary

Psalm 78:36 exposes the tragedy of worship that stops at the lips. Israel mouthed devotion to escape discipline, but their hearts stayed false. God, who sees through every façade, calls His people to sincerity—words that match actions, tongues that tell the truth, and hearts that cling to Him in loyal love.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 36. - Nevertheless they did flatter him with their mouth. The Revised Version is simpler and better, But they flattered him with their mouth. All that they said or did when alarmed by some judgment of God's was a mere pretence - an attempt to "flatter" and cozen God, and so win his favour. And they lied unto him with their tongues. They offered him a lip service, which was a "lie," a mere semblance of real religion.
