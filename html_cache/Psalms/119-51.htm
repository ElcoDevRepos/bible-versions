The arrogant utterly deride me
This phrase highlights the experience of facing scorn and mockery from those who are proud and self-sufficient. In biblical context, the "arrogant" often refers to those who reject God's authority and wisdom, as seen in Proverbs 21:24, which describes the proud as "scoffer" and "haughty." Historically, the people of Israel frequently encountered derision from surrounding nations who worshipped other gods and viewed Israel's devotion to Yahweh as foolish. This derision can also be seen in the life of Jesus Christ, who was mocked by the religious leaders and others (Matthew 27:29-31). The psalmist's experience is a type of Christ's own suffering and rejection.

but I do not turn from Your law
Despite the mockery, the psalmist remains steadfast in adherence to God's law. This reflects a deep commitment to God's commandments, as seen throughout Psalm 119, which emphasizes the importance of God's word as a guide and source of strength. The law here is not merely a set of rules but represents the entire teaching and revelation of God. In the New Testament, Jesus affirms the enduring value of God's law, stating in Matthew 5:17 that He came not to abolish the law but to fulfill it. The psalmist's resolve serves as an example of faithfulness and perseverance, encouraging believers to remain true to God's word despite external pressures and ridicule.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Psalmist
Traditionally attributed to King David, the psalmist is a devout follower of God who seeks to live according to God's law despite facing opposition.

2. The Arrogant
These are individuals who mock or scorn the psalmist for his adherence to God's law. They represent those who are prideful and dismissive of divine instruction.

3. God's Law
Refers to the Torah or the teachings and commandments given by God, which the psalmist is committed to following.
Teaching Points
Endurance in Faith
The psalmist's commitment to God's law despite mockery teaches us the importance of steadfastness in our faith. We are called to remain faithful even when others ridicule our beliefs.

The Danger of Pride
The "arrogant" in this verse serve as a warning against pride and self-reliance. Pride can lead to a dismissive attitude toward God's commandments and those who follow them.

Value of God's Law
The psalmist's unwavering adherence to God's law underscores its value and importance. As believers, we should prioritize God's word as the guiding principle of our lives.

Responding to Mockery
When faced with derision, our response should be rooted in love and truth, reflecting Christ's example. We should not retaliate but continue to uphold God's standards.

What Does Psalm 119:51 Mean
The arrogant

- Scripture paints the “arrogant” as those who exalt themselves above God’s ways. “When pride comes, disgrace follows” (Proverbs 11:2), and “God opposes the proud” (1 Peter 5:5).

- Psalm 119 often mentions this group: “The arrogant have smeared me with lies” (Psalm 119:69). They refuse humility, setting themselves against the psalm-writer and, ultimately, against God.

utterly deride me

- Mockery is the weapon of the proud. “We have endured much contempt from the proud” (Psalm 123:3–4).

- Israel knew public scorn—“You make us a reproach to our neighbors, a derision and scorn” (Psalm 44:13)—and so did Christ, “Those who passed by hurled insults at Him” (Matthew 27:39).

- Believers should not be surprised: “All who desire to live godly in Christ Jesus will be persecuted” (2 Timothy 3:12).

but I do not turn

- The psalmist draws a line in the sand. Joshua heard a similar charge: “Do not turn from it to the right or to the left” (Joshua 1:7).

- Loyalty is non-negotiable: “All this has come upon us, yet we have not forgotten You; we have not strayed from Your path” (Psalm 44:17–18).

- When many disciples walked away, Peter answered, “Lord, to whom would we go? You have the words of eternal life” (John 6:68).

- True faith holds steady: “We are not of those who shrink back and are destroyed, but of those who believe and are saved” (Hebrews 10:39).

from Your law

- The psalmist’s anchor is God’s revealed Word. “I have hidden Your word in my heart that I might not sin against You” (Psalm 119:11).

- In hardship the Word sustains: “If Your law had not been my delight, then I would have perished in my affliction” (Psalm 119:92).

- The law is trustworthy and sufficient: “All Scripture is God-breathed…so that the man of God may be complete” (2 Timothy 3:16–17).

- The result is unshakable peace: “Abundant peace belongs to those who love Your law; nothing can make them stumble” (Psalm 119:165).

- James echoes the call to steadfast obedience: “The one who looks intently into the perfect law…and continues in it…will be blessed” (James 1:25).

summary

Arrogant scoffers may heap ridicule, yet the believer refuses to budge. Confidence rests not in self, but in the unchanging Word. When proud voices deride, Scripture steadies the heart, guides the path, and supplies the courage to stand firm without turning aside.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 51. - The proud have had me greatly in derision; rather, proud men. There is no article. (For the "derision" to which the righteous are always exposed, see Job 30:1, 9; Psalm 35:16; Psalm 44:13, 14; Lamentations 3:14; Jeremiah 20:7, etc.) Yet have I not declined (or, swerved) from thy Law (see the comment on ver. 31).
