Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
The LORD is gracious and righteous;
This phrase highlights two key attributes of God: His grace and righteousness. The concept of grace in the Old Testament is often associated with God's favor and kindness towards humanity, despite their shortcomings. This is evident in God's dealings with Israel, where He repeatedly shows mercy and forgiveness. Righteousness, on the other hand, refers to God's moral perfection and justice. These attributes are seen throughout the Bible, such as in Exodus 34:6-7, where God describes Himself as "compassionate and gracious, slow to anger, abounding in love and faithfulness." The balance of grace and righteousness is crucial, as it underscores God's ability to be just while also offering mercy. This duality is ultimately fulfilled in the New Testament through Jesus Christ, who embodies both grace and truth (John 1:14).

our God is full of compassion.
Compassion is a recurring theme in the Bible, reflecting God's deep love and care for His creation. In the Hebrew context, compassion is often linked to the idea of a mother's love for her child, indicating a profound, nurturing care. This is seen in passages like Isaiah 49:15, where God compares His compassion to that of a mother's for her nursing child. The phrase "full of compassion" suggests an overflowing abundance, indicating that God's compassion is limitless and ever-present. This attribute is also mirrored in the life of Jesus, who frequently showed compassion to the sick, the poor, and the marginalized (Matthew 9:36, Mark 1:41). The compassion of God is a source of comfort and hope, assuring believers of His constant presence and care in times of need.

Teaching Points
Understanding God's Nature
Recognize that God's graciousness and righteousness are foundational to His character. His actions are always aligned with His nature.

Experiencing God's Compassion
Reflect on personal experiences of God's compassion in your life. How has God shown His mercy and love to you in times of need?

Living in Response to God's Attributes
As recipients of God's grace and compassion, we are called to extend the same to others. Consider how you can be a vessel of God's love and righteousness in your community.

Trusting in God's Righteousness
Trust that God's righteousness ensures justice and fairness. In a world often marked by injustice, rely on God's righteous nature to guide your actions and decisions.

Prayer and Worship
Use Psalm 116:5 as a basis for prayer and worship, acknowledging God's graciousness, righteousness, and compassion in your life.

What Does Psalm 116:5 Mean
The LORD

- The verse begins with the covenant name “the LORD,” reminding us that the God who saved Israel is the same God who hears us today.

- This name signals His self-existent, eternal nature (see Exodus 3:14: “I AM WHO I AM”).

- Because He is the LORD, every attribute that follows—grace, righteousness, compassion—is entirely reliable (Malachi 3:6; James 1:17).

is gracious

- “Gracious” points to God’s undeserved favor toward helpless people.

• He rescues (Ephesians 2:8-9).

• He forgives (Psalm 86:5).

• He sustains daily life (Psalm 145:15-16).

- Old Testament believers knew this grace: “The LORD, the LORD God, compassionate and gracious” (Exodus 34:6).

- His grace is supremely displayed at the cross, “while we were still sinners” (Romans 5:8).

and righteous

- God’s grace never voids His righteousness; both stand together.

• He always acts justly (Deuteronomy 32:4).

• He judges sin fairly (Psalm 11:7).

• He keeps every promise (Psalm 119:137-138).

- The gospel satisfies this righteousness: “so that He might be just and the justifier” (Romans 3:26).

our God

- The psalmist shifts from “the LORD” to “our God,” highlighting personal relationship.

• “He is our God, and we are the people of His pasture” (Psalm 95:7).

• Covenant love makes Him approachable; prayer is possible because He belongs to us and we belong to Him (Hebrews 4:16).

- The plural “our” invites every believer into the same confidence.

is full of compassion

- Literally “abundant in mercy,” picturing a God whose heart overflows with tender care.

• He remembers our frailty (Psalm 103:13-14).

• His mercies are “new every morning” (Lamentations 3:22-23).

• Even discipline is tempered with compassion (Micah 7:18-19).

- Jesus embodies this compassion: “He was moved with compassion for them” (Matthew 14:14), revealing the Father’s heart.

summary

Psalm 116:5 holds together three majestic qualities—grace, righteousness, compassion—under one personal, covenant name. Because the LORD is simultaneously gracious, righteous, and overflowing with mercy, we can trust Him to forgive, to act justly, and to care tenderly for every need.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 5. - Gracious is the Lord, and righteous. God's answers to prayer show him to be both "gracious" and "righteous" - gracious, because it is of his mercy that he listens to men; righteous, because, having promised to hear prayer, he is bound to keep his promises. Yea, our God is merciful; or, "compassionate."
