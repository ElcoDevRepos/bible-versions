All day long they twist my words;
This phrase reflects the constant and relentless nature of the psalmist's adversaries. The twisting of words suggests deceit and manipulation, a common tactic used by enemies to undermine and discredit. In biblical context, this can be seen in the way the Pharisees and Sadducees often tried to trap Jesus with His own words (Matthew 22:15-22). The psalmist, traditionally understood to be David, faced similar challenges from those who sought to distort his intentions and statements. This reflects the broader theme of the righteous being persecuted by the wicked, a recurring motif throughout the Psalms.

all their thoughts are on my demise.
The focus on the psalmist's demise indicates a deep-seated malice and intent to harm. This reflects the historical context of David's life, where he was frequently pursued by enemies, including King Saul, who sought his life (1 Samuel 19:1-2). The cultural context of ancient Israel was one where personal vendettas and political rivalries often led to life-threatening situations. This phrase also connects to the broader biblical theme of the righteous suffering at the hands of the wicked, as seen in the life of Jesus Christ, who faced constant plots against Him (John 11:53). The psalmist's experience can be seen as a type of Christ, prefiguring the ultimate suffering and vindication of Jesus.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of Psalm 56, David is expressing his trust in God amidst persecution and fear. This psalm is attributed to the time when the Philistines seized him in Gath.

2. Philistines
A group of people who were often in conflict with Israel. In this context, they captured David, leading to his plea for God's deliverance.

3. Gath
A Philistine city where David sought refuge from King Saul, only to find himself in danger again.
Teaching Points
Trust in God Amidst Adversity
David's situation in Gath teaches us to place our trust in God even when we are surrounded by enemies or difficult circumstances. His reliance on God serves as a model for us to follow.

The Power of Prayer
David's response to his fear and persecution was to turn to God in prayer. This emphasizes the importance of prayer as a means of seeking God's help and comfort.

God's Sovereignty Over Our Enemies
Despite the constant plotting of his enemies, David acknowledges God's control over his life. This reminds us that God is sovereign and can protect us from those who seek to harm us.

Faith Over Fear
David's faith in God allowed him to overcome his fear. This encourages us to strengthen our faith, knowing that God is greater than any fear we may face.

The Importance of Integrity
David's life, even in distress, reflects a commitment to integrity and righteousness. We are called to maintain our integrity, trusting that God sees and honors our faithfulness.

What Does Psalm 56:5 Mean
All day long they twist my words

• David is lamenting a nonstop campaign of verbal distortion. The opposition does not merely misunderstand; they purposely “twist” what he says, turning truth into a weapon (cf. Psalm 52:2, “Your tongue devises destruction, like a sharpened razor, you who practice deceit,”).

• This is a picture of the righteous suffering under slander, a theme echoed later when false witnesses rise against Christ (Mark 14:56).

• Continuous pressure—“all day long”—reminds us that trials often come in relentless waves (Psalm 41:5; Jeremiah 20:10).

• For believers today:

– Expect misrepresentation when you stand for truth (John 15:20).

– Guard your own speech so that you never mirror the tactics of the wicked (Ephesians 4:29).

All their thoughts are on my demise

• The same people who mangle David’s words also meditate on his ruin. Their minds are set on harm, not reconciliation (Psalm 35:4, “May those who seek my life be disgraced and put to shame,”).

• The phrase underscores intent: destruction is premeditated, not accidental (Psalm 140:2).

• Though human enemies plot, the greater adversary is spiritual (1 Peter 5:8). David’s confidence later in the psalm—“This I know: God is for me” (v. 9)—answers their plots with faith.

• For the believer:

– Recognize that hostility toward God’s people is ultimately hostility toward God (Acts 9:4).

– Rest in divine protection; no scheme can succeed outside His sovereign will (Romans 8:31).

summary

Psalm 56:5 exposes a two-fold assault: words twisted and destruction plotted. David’s experience foreshadows the Suffering Servant and instructs every follower of Christ. Expect slander, discern malicious intent, yet remain steadfast, confident that God hears, keeps record, and will vindicate His own.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(5) Wrest.--Properly, afflict; and so some, "injure my cause." But "torture my words" is intelligible.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 5. - Every day they wrest my words; rather, all the day long. they wrest (or, torture) my words. They seek to give my words an evil meaning, and so to misrepresent me to Achish, their king. As Canon Cook says, "This description is singularly applicable to David's position among the envious nobles at the court of Achish Still, it does not speak of his having been actually arrested, and does not, therefore, seem to have suggested the inscription." All their thoughts are against his for evil. They are entirely bent on doing the psalmist some hurt. What they really seek is his life (ver. 6); but, short of that, they would gladly do him some mischief.
