But from everlasting to everlasting
This phrase emphasizes the eternal nature of God's attributes, particularly His loving devotion. The concept of "everlasting" is a recurring theme in Scripture, highlighting God's unchanging and infinite nature (Psalm 90:2). It contrasts the temporal nature of human life with the eternal nature of God, as seen in Isaiah 40:8, where the Word of God stands forever. This eternal perspective is foundational to understanding God's covenantal faithfulness throughout biblical history.

the loving devotion of the LORD
"Loving devotion" translates the Hebrew word "chesed," which encompasses steadfast love, mercy, and covenantal loyalty. This term is central to God's relationship with Israel, as seen in His covenant with Abraham (Genesis 12:1-3) and His promises to David (2 Samuel 7:12-16). It reflects God's commitment to His people despite their unfaithfulness, as illustrated in the prophetic writings, such as Hosea, where God's love persists despite Israel's infidelity.

extends to those who fear Him
The fear of the LORD is a foundational concept in wisdom literature, such as Proverbs 1:7, where it is described as the beginning of knowledge. This fear is not terror but a reverent awe and respect for God's holiness and authority. It denotes a relationship of obedience and trust, as seen in the lives of biblical figures like Job (Job 1:1) and Daniel (Daniel 6:10). This reverence is a prerequisite for experiencing God's loving devotion.

and His righteousness to their children’s children—
This phrase underscores the generational impact of God's righteousness, which is His moral perfection and justice. The promise of God's righteousness extending to descendants is reminiscent of the blessings promised to Abraham's offspring (Genesis 17:7). It highlights the biblical principle of generational blessings and responsibilities, as seen in Deuteronomy 6:6-7, where parents are instructed to teach God's commandments to their children. This continuity reflects God's faithfulness to His covenant and His desire for His people to walk in His ways across generations.

Teaching Points
Eternal Nature of God's Love
God's loving devotion is not limited by time; it spans from everlasting to everlasting, offering believers a foundation of security and hope.

Fear of the LORD as a Key to Blessing
Reverence and awe for God are crucial for experiencing His loving devotion and righteousness, impacting not only individuals but also their descendants.

Generational Impact of Faithfulness
The faithfulness of one generation can lead to blessings for future generations, encouraging believers to live in a way that honors God and benefits their descendants.

God's Righteousness as a Legacy
The righteousness of God is a gift that can be passed down, encouraging believers to cultivate a life of integrity and faithfulness.

Trust in God's Promises
Believers are encouraged to trust in the enduring promises of God, knowing that His love and righteousness are unwavering and extend beyond their lifetime.

What Does Psalm 103:17 Mean
But from everlasting to everlasting

Psalm 103:17 begins with a breathtaking timeframe—“from everlasting to everlasting.”

• God’s love is not confined to our brief years; it stretches back before Genesis 1:1 and forward beyond Revelation 22:21.

• Psalm 90:2 testifies, “Before the mountains were born… from everlasting to everlasting You are God”.

• Isaiah 40:28 reminds us that the LORD is “the everlasting God,” so His character cannot falter or fade.

Because His nature is eternal, the promises in this verse cannot expire.

the loving devotion of the LORD extends

• “Loving devotion” (often rendered steadfast love) speaks of loyal, covenant commitment. Exodus 34:6 calls Him “abounding in loving devotion and faithfulness”.

• Lamentations 3:22-23 underscores the same thought: “Because of the loving devotion of the LORD we are not consumed… great is Your faithfulness”.

• Psalm 136 repeats “His loving devotion endures forever” 26 times, emphasizing that His love is active and ongoing, not passive sentiment.

to those who fear Him

• “Fear” here is reverential awe mixed with wholehearted trust and obedience. Proverbs 9:10 says, “The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom”.

• Psalm 25:14 promises, “The LORD confides in those who fear Him, and reveals His covenant to them”.

• Luke 1:50 echoes our verse: “His mercy extends to those who fear Him, from generation to generation”.

Those who honor God as holy experience the fullness of His devoted love.

and His righteousness

• God’s righteousness is His perfect justice and moral purity, always expressed in right actions. Psalm 36:6 states, “Your righteousness is like the highest mountains”.

• For believers, that righteousness is also a gift. 2 Corinthians 5:21 affirms, “God made Him who knew no sin to be sin for us, so that in Him we might become the righteousness of God”.

• Isaiah 54:17 assures, “This is the heritage of the servants of the LORD, and their vindication is from Me”. His righteousness defends, covers, and transforms His people.

to their children’s children

• The reach of God’s covenant faithfulness is multi-generational. Deuteronomy 7:9 declares, “He keeps His covenant of loving devotion for a thousand generations with those who love Him and keep His commandments”.

• Psalm 78:6-7 urges parents to pass on God’s works “so that the next generation would know them… and they in turn would tell their children”.

• Timothy’s sincere faith was nurtured by his grandmother Lois and mother Eunice (2 Timothy 1:5), illustrating how one generation’s reverence plants seeds of blessing in the next.

Practical take-away: the choices we make today in honoring God ripple into our grandchildren’s tomorrows.

summary

Psalm 103:17 assures us that God’s steadfast love is eternal, firmly rooted in His unchanging nature. It reaches all who reverently trust Him, clothes them in His own righteousness, and overflows to upcoming generations. Our call is simple: fear the LORD, receive His faithful love, and pass the torch of faith so that our children’s children may rejoice in the same unending covenant devotion.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 17. - But the mercy of the Lord is from everlasting to everlasting upon them that fear him (comp. vers. 11, 13). Through this "everlasting mercy" of God, man, though so feeble and fragile, does not wholly pass away, but continues to be the recipient of God's bounty. And his righteousness unto children's children. God's "righteousness" is his everlasting justice, by which he gives to men according to their deserts.
