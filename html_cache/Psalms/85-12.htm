Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
The LORD will indeed provide what is good
This phrase emphasizes God's faithfulness and His role as the ultimate provider. Throughout the Old Testament, God is depicted as the source of all blessings and goodness (Psalm 34:8, James 1:17). The assurance that God will provide what is good reflects His covenant relationship with Israel, where He promises to care for His people if they remain faithful (Deuteronomy 28:1-14). This promise is not only material but also spiritual, pointing to the ultimate good found in a relationship with God through Jesus Christ, who is the embodiment of God's goodness (John 10:11).

and our land will yield its increase
This phrase speaks to the agricultural context of ancient Israel, where the land's productivity was directly linked to God's blessing (Leviticus 26:4). The land yielding its increase is a sign of divine favor and a fulfillment of God's promises to His people (Genesis 26:12). In a broader sense, this can be seen as a metaphor for spiritual fruitfulness, where the land represents the hearts of believers, and the increase signifies the fruits of the Spirit (Galatians 5:22-23). This also foreshadows the ultimate restoration and abundance in the new creation, where God's people will experience eternal prosperity and peace (Revelation 21:1-4).

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant-keeping God of Israel, who is faithful to His promises and provides for His people.

2. The Land of Israel
The physical land promised to the descendants of Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob, which is often seen as a symbol of God's blessing and provision.

3. The Psalmist
Likely a member of the sons of Korah, who authored this psalm as a prayer for restoration and blessing upon the land and people of Israel.
Teaching Points
God's Provision is Certain
Trust in the LORD's promise to provide what is good. His faithfulness ensures that He will meet the needs of His people.

Blessings Tied to Obedience
While God's grace is abundant, the fullness of His blessings often comes through our obedience and alignment with His will.

Spiritual and Physical Increase
The increase of the land can be seen as both a physical and spiritual blessing. Seek growth in both areas by nurturing your relationship with God.

Gratitude for God's Gifts
Recognize and give thanks for the good things God provides, understanding that they are expressions of His love and care.

Stewardship of God's Blessings
As recipients of God's provision, we are called to be good stewards of what He gives, using it to glorify Him and bless others.

What Does Psalm 85:12 Mean
The LORD

– The verse begins by centering on “The LORD,” the covenant name for God, reminding us that the promise rests on His faithful character.

– Scripture paints Him as a shepherd who never leaves His flock in need: “The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want” (Psalm 23:1).

– His steadfast nature is the anchor of every blessing (Exodus 34:6) and guarantees that what follows is not wishful thinking but divine certainty.

will indeed provide

– The word “indeed” underscores absolute assurance. God’s provision is not hypothetical; it is promised.

– Throughout Scripture He delights in meeting His people’s needs:

• “For I know the plans I have for you… plans to prosper you” (Jeremiah 29:11).

• “He who did not spare His own Son… how will He not also… freely give us all things?” (Romans 8:32).

– We can rest in the certainty that the God who provided salvation will also supply every lesser need.

what is good

– God’s gifts align with His perfect wisdom. He never withholds anything truly beneficial: “He withholds no good thing from those who walk with integrity” (Psalm 84:11).

– Goodness in God’s economy includes material, spiritual, and relational blessings. Even what seems hard can work for ultimate good (Romans 8:28).

– “Those who seek the LORD lack no good thing” (Psalm 34:10); therefore, seeking Him is the pathway to experiencing His goodness.

and our land

– The promise moves from personal to corporate, showing God’s concern for communities and nations.

– Under the old covenant, the land of Israel was the sign of God’s favor. Obedience brought fruitfulness (Deuteronomy 28:11).

– The principle remains: when God’s people humble themselves and seek His face, He “will… heal their land” (2 Chronicles 7:14).

– Today, the “land” can represent both the physical places we inhabit and the spheres of influence He entrusts to us—families, churches, workplaces.

will yield its increase

– The phrase pictures overflowing abundance:

• “I will send you rain in its season, and the land will yield its produce” (Leviticus 26:4).

• “The mountains will drip with sweet wine” (Amos 9:13).

– Physical harvests remind us of spiritual fruit. Jesus said, “The one who remains in Me… will bear much fruit” (John 15:5).

– As the Spirit works, He produces “love, joy, peace…” (Galatians 5:22-23). The land images an outward, visible harvest; the Spirit brings an inward, eternal one.

summary

Psalm 85:12 assures us that the covenant-keeping LORD is committed to actively supplying every good thing. His promise extends from individual hearts to the very ground beneath our feet, resulting in abundant fruitfulness that brings Him glory and blesses His people.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 12. - Yea, the Lord shall give that which is good; i.e. shower blessings on his laud, both spiritual and temporal. And our land shall yield her increase. Other crops may be included, but the special reference is to a large increase of good works.
