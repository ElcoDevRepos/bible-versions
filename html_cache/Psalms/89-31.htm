Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
if they violate My statutes
This phrase refers to the potential disobedience of God's people to His decrees. In the context of the Old Testament, statutes were specific laws given by God to Israel, often found in the Torah, particularly in books like Leviticus and Deuteronomy. These statutes were part of the covenant relationship between God and Israel, where obedience would lead to blessings, and disobedience to curses (Deuteronomy 28). The historical context includes the repeated cycle of Israel's disobedience, leading to consequences such as exile. This phrase also echoes the broader biblical theme of human failure to uphold God's law, pointing to the need for a savior, fulfilled in Jesus Christ, who perfectly kept God's statutes.

and fail to keep My commandments,
Commandments refer to the broader moral and ethical directives given by God, including the Ten Commandments found in Exodus 20. The failure to keep these commandments is a recurring theme in the history of Israel, as seen in the narratives of the Judges and Kings, where the people often turned to idolatry and injustice. This failure highlights the human condition of sin and the need for repentance and redemption. In the New Testament, Jesus summarizes the commandments as loving God and loving one's neighbor (Matthew 22:37-40), and He fulfills the law through His life and teachings. The phrase underscores the importance of obedience to God's will as a reflection of a faithful relationship with Him.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Ethan the Ezrahite
The author of Psalm 89, who is traditionally believed to be a wise man during the time of King Solomon. He is credited with writing this psalm, which reflects on God's covenant with David and the consequences of disobedience.

2. Davidic Covenant
The covenant God made with King David, promising that his descendants would rule Israel forever. This psalm reflects on the faithfulness of God to His promises, even when His people are unfaithful.

3. Israel
The nation chosen by God, often depicted in the Psalms as struggling with obedience to God's laws and commandments.

4. God's Statutes and Commandments
The laws given by God to His people, which they are expected to follow. These are central to the covenant relationship between God and Israel.

5. Disobedience and Consequences
The theme of disobedience to God's laws and the resulting consequences is a key focus of this verse and the surrounding passage.
Teaching Points
The Importance of Obedience
Obedience to God's statutes and commandments is crucial for maintaining a right relationship with Him. Disobedience leads to consequences, as outlined in the covenant with David.

God's Faithfulness Despite Human Failure
Even when we fail to keep God's commandments, He remains faithful to His promises. This should encourage us to repent and return to Him.

The Role of Discipline in Spiritual Growth
God's discipline is a form of His love and is meant to guide us back to the right path. Understanding this can help us accept and learn from the consequences of our actions.

Covenant Relationship with God
Our relationship with God is based on a covenant that requires our commitment to His laws. Reflecting on this can deepen our understanding of our responsibilities as believers.

Reflecting on Personal Obedience
Regularly assess your own life for areas where you may be violating God's statutes. Seek His guidance and strength to align your actions with His will.

What Does Psalm 89:31 Mean
If they violate My statutes

“if they violate My statutes …” (Psalm 89:31a)

The verse pictures the sons of David stepping outside God-given boundaries. A “statute” is a fixed decree; to violate it is to treat God’s clear lines as if they were optional.

• Covenant context: God had promised David an everlasting throne (2 Samuel 7:12-16), yet warned, “When he does wrong, I will discipline him with the rod of men” (2 Samuel 7:14). Psalm 89 echoes that same father-child dynamic.

• Historical precedent: Solomon himself was told, “But if you or your sons turn away from following Me … then I will cut off Israel” (1 Kings 9:6-7). God’s love never wavered, but willful rebellion still invited real consequences.

• Principle of blessing and discipline: “Keep His statutes and commandments … so that you may live” (Deuteronomy 4:40). Obedience safeguards life; violation triggers correction, not covenant cancellation (Psalm 89:33-34).

• Personal take-away: Crossing God’s fixed lines remains serious. His standards are not suggestions for any believer whose life is linked to the promised Son of David, Jesus Christ (Matthew 5:17-19).

and fail to keep My commandments

“… and fail to keep My commandments” (Psalm 89:31b)

The phrase moves from a single act of crossing the line to an ongoing refusal to obey. “Fail” suggests neglect, apathy, or open defiance toward the commands God lovingly supplies.

• Heart issue: “If you love Me, you will keep My commandments” (John 14:15). Persistent failure exposes a heart drifting from love.

• Consequence stated: “If you do not obey the LORD your God … all these curses will come upon you” (Deuteronomy 28:15). Discipline is a divine mercy meant to turn hearts back.

• Assurance of relationship: Even when failure occurs, the covenant holds. “I will punish their transgression with the rod … but I will not withdraw My loving devotion” (Psalm 89:32-33). God distinguishes between the person He loves and the sin He must correct.

• New-covenant mirror: “By this we can be sure that we have come to know Him: if we keep His commandments” (1 John 2:3-4). True faith still shows itself in obedience, yet lapses are met with the Father’s corrective grace (Hebrews 12:6-7).

summary

Psalm 89:31 warns that when David’s descendants—and by extension all God’s people—step outside His statutes and ignore His commandments, divine discipline follows. The verse underscores both God’s unwavering standards and His unwavering covenant love. Disobedience brings the rod, not rejection; correction, not cancellation. For today’s believer, the passage calls for wholehearted obedience while resting in the security of God’s faithful, covenant-keeping grace.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 31. - If they break my statutes, and keep not my commandments; rather, if they profane my statutes; i.e. make light of them, either in their words or in their lives.
