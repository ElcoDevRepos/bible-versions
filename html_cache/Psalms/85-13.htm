Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
Righteousness will go before Him
This phrase suggests the idea of righteousness as a forerunner or herald, preparing the way for the Lord. In biblical context, righteousness often refers to God's moral perfection and justice. This imagery is reminiscent of John the Baptist's role in preparing the way for Jesus Christ, as seen in Isaiah 40:3 and Matthew 3:3. The concept of righteousness going before God implies that His actions and presence are always preceded by His just and holy nature. This aligns with the biblical theme that God’s righteousness is foundational to His covenant with His people, as seen in passages like Psalm 89:14.

to prepare the way for His steps.
The preparation of the way for His steps indicates a path being made ready for the Lord's coming or intervention. This echoes the ancient practice of preparing roads for the arrival of a king or dignitary, ensuring a smooth and honorable passage. In a spiritual sense, it suggests that God’s righteous acts pave the way for His presence and blessings among His people. This can be connected to the prophetic imagery in Malachi 3:1, where a messenger prepares the way for the Lord. It also points to the ultimate fulfillment in Jesus Christ, whose life and ministry were marked by righteousness, preparing the way for humanity's reconciliation with God. The phrase underscores the importance of living righteously as a means of welcoming God's presence and guidance in one's life.

Teaching Points
Righteousness as a Pathway
God's righteousness is not just a characteristic but a guiding force that leads and prepares the way for His presence. As believers, we are called to walk in righteousness, aligning our lives with God's standards.

Preparation for God's Presence
Just as righteousness prepares the way for God's steps, we are to prepare our hearts and lives for His presence. This involves repentance, faith, and obedience.

The Role of Righteousness in Restoration
The Psalm speaks to a time of restoration for Israel. In our lives, embracing God's righteousness can lead to personal and communal restoration and peace.

Anticipation of Christ's Return
The imagery of preparing the way can also be seen as a call to readiness for Christ's second coming. We are to live in a manner that reflects His righteousness and anticipates His return.

What Does Psalm 85:13 Mean
Righteousness Will Go

• The psalmist personifies righteousness as if it were a herald marching at the front of God’s procession. Because the Lord is intrinsically righteous (Psalm 145:17), any movement He makes is characterized by perfect moral integrity.

• Psalm 89:14 declares, “Righteousness and justice are the foundation of Your throne,” emphasizing that righteousness is not an accessory but the very ground He stands on.

• This truth reassures believers that God never acts arbitrarily; every promise of restoration in Psalm 85 rests on His unwavering righteousness (Isaiah 45:19).

Before Him

• “Before Him” pictures righteousness clearing the path, much like an advance guard. Isaiah 58:8 uses similar language: “Your righteousness will go before you, and the glory of the LORD will be your rear guard.”

• God’s own righteousness is out front, guaranteeing that nothing opposing His holy nature can stand in His way (Habakkuk 1:13).

• For His people, this means any deliverance He brings is fully compatible with His justice (Romans 3:26).

To Prepare the Way

• The phrase echoes Isaiah 40:3 and Malachi 3:1, passages later applied to John the Baptist (Luke 1:76), showing how God’s advance work paves the road for His redemptive actions.

• Here, righteousness is the “road grader,” leveling obstacles so God’s blessings in Psalm 85—revival, forgiveness, peace—can arrive unhindered.

• It reminds believers that when God prepares to move, He first aligns circumstances with His character; nothing proceeds until the way is morally ready (Psalm 25:10).

For His Steps

• The verse ends with movement: God is not static. “Your procession, O God, has come into view” (Psalm 68:24).

• As righteousness lays down the path, God takes tangible steps—answering prayer, restoring the land (Psalm 85:1), granting peace (Psalm 85:8).

• Believers can trust that every divine footfall is deliberate and purposeful (Psalm 37:23). His steps always arrive exactly where righteousness has already marked the trail.

summary

Psalm 85:13 teaches that God’s own righteousness precedes Him like a herald, ensuring the way is morally cleared before He advances. Because His steps never deviate from perfect justice, His people can confidently expect restoration, blessing, and peace whenever He moves.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(13) Righteousness shall . . .--Better, Righteousness shall walk in front of Him, and follow in His steps.

Nothing is more instructive than the blending in Psalm 85:12-13 of material and moral blessings. They do go together, as experience, especially national testifies. In the same spirit is Wordsworth's well-known Ode to Duty:

"Stern Law-giver! Yet thou dost wear

The Godhead's most benignant grace,

Nor know we anything so fair

As is the smile upon thy face.

Flowers laugh before thee on their beds,

And fragrance in thy footing treads:

Thou dost preserve the stars from wrong,

And the most ancient heavens through Thee are . . .

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 13. - Righteousness shall go before him. Prepare the way, i.e., for the restoration of the people to God's favour (compare the first clause of ver. 11, and the second of ver. 12). And shall set us in the way of his steps; i.e. cause his people to walk in the way marked out by his footsteps - i.e. by the indications of his will either in nature or in the written Word.
