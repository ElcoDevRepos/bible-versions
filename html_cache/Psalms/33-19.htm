Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
to deliver them from death
This phrase emphasizes God's power and willingness to save His people from peril. In the biblical context, deliverance from death often signifies both physical and spiritual salvation. The Israelites experienced God's deliverance numerous times, such as during the Exodus from Egypt (Exodus 14:30) and in battles against their enemies (1 Samuel 17:37). This deliverance is a recurring theme throughout the Psalms, highlighting God's role as a protector and savior. Theologically, this can also be seen as a foreshadowing of Jesus Christ's ultimate deliverance of humanity from spiritual death through His sacrifice and resurrection (John 11:25-26).

and keep them alive in famine
Famine was a common and severe threat in the ancient Near East, often resulting from drought, war, or locust plagues. God's provision during such times is a testament to His faithfulness and care for His people. The story of Joseph in Genesis 41-47 illustrates God's providence, as He used Joseph to prepare Egypt and surrounding nations for a coming famine, ultimately preserving the lives of many, including the Israelites. This phrase also reflects God's promise to sustain His people, as seen in Elijah's time when God provided for the widow of Zarephath during a drought (1 Kings 17:8-16). Spiritually, it points to God's ability to sustain believers through times of spiritual drought, providing the "bread of life" through Jesus Christ (John 6:35).

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty and Protection
Trust in God's ability to deliver from both physical and spiritual death. His sovereignty assures us that He is in control, even in dire circumstances.

Faith in God's Provision
In times of scarcity or need, remember that God is our provider. Just as He promises to keep us alive in famine, He will meet our needs according to His riches in glory.

Hope in God's Unfailing Love
Our hope should be anchored in God's steadfast love. This hope is not wishful thinking but a confident expectation based on His character and promises.

The Importance of Fear and Reverence for God
Cultivate a healthy fear of the Lord, which is the beginning of wisdom and a key to experiencing His deliverance and provision.

Spiritual Deliverance and Eternal Life
Reflect on the ultimate deliverance from spiritual death through Jesus Christ, who offers eternal life to those who believe.

What Does Psalm 33:19 Mean
To deliver them from death

Psalm 33:19 declares that the LORD’s eye is “on those who fear Him, … to deliver them from death”. The promise is straightforward, not poetic exaggeration.

• Physical rescue: God literally intervenes in history to spare His people. Think of Noah (Genesis 7–8), Israel at the Red Sea (Exodus 14:13-31), and Hezekiah’s deliverance from the Assyrians (2 Kings 19:32-37). Each event shows Psalm 33:19 in action—death was imminent, yet God stepped in.

• Ongoing preservation: The same hand that once rescued continues to guard. Psalm 91:10-11 affirms, “no evil will befall you… He will command His angels concerning you.”

• Ultimate salvation: Even when believers face martyrdom, God still “delivers from death” by receiving them into eternal life (John 11:25-26; Philippians 1:21-23). The verse therefore embraces both temporal and eternal safety, never compromising either.

And keep them alive in famine

The sentence flows naturally: after rescue from sudden death, God sustains through prolonged crisis.

• Historical proof: During Joseph’s famine, “God sent a man before them… to preserve life” (Genesis 45:5-7). Elijah received bread from ravens and a widow’s jar that “was not exhausted” (1 Kings 17:4-16). These accounts mirror Psalm 33:19 in real time.

• Daily confidence: Jesus teaches us to pray, “Give us this day our daily bread” (Matthew 6:11), assuring provision even when cupboards look bare. Psalm 37:18-19 repeats the pledge: “In the days of famine they will have plenty.”

• Spiritual nourishment: God feeds souls as surely as bodies. Amos 8:11 warns of a “famine of hearing the words of the LORD.” In contrast, those who fear Him “taste and see that the LORD is good” (Psalm 34:8), never starving for truth or hope.

summary

Psalm 33:19 underscores God’s practical, hands-on care. He breaks in to save from sudden death and stays close to supply every need when resources dry up. The verse moves from emergency rescue to ongoing sustenance, painting a complete picture of covenant faithfulness. Trusting Him means resting in both aspects: He is the Deliverer who snatches from danger and the Provider who keeps His people flourishing when the world runs empty.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 19. - To deliver their soul from death. The protection and deliverance, which a man's own strength cannot give, which no host, however numerous, can afford (ver. 16), which are not to be obtained from the largest chariot or cavalry force (ver. 17), can he and will be furnished freely by God, who alone keeps souls from death, and "delivers" those who are in peril. And to keep them alive in famine. Famine was a calamity from which Palestine often suffered (see Genesis 12:10; Genesis 26:1; Genesis 42:5; Ruth 1:1; 2 Samuel 21:1; 1 Kings 18:5; 2 Kings 8:1, etc.). The righteous were sometimes "kept alive" through a time of famine by miraculous means (1 Kings 17:6, 16).
