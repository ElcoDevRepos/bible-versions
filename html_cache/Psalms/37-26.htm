They are ever generous
This phrase highlights the consistent and ongoing nature of generosity. In biblical context, generosity is a virtue highly esteemed throughout Scripture. Proverbs 11:25 states, "A generous person will prosper; whoever refreshes others will be refreshed." This reflects the biblical principle of sowing and reaping, where acts of kindness and generosity lead to blessings. The cultural context of ancient Israel placed a strong emphasis on community and mutual support, where generosity was not just a personal virtue but a communal expectation. The phrase also echoes the character of God, who is described as generous and giving, as seen in James 1:5, where God gives wisdom generously to all without finding fault.

and quick to lend
In ancient Israel, lending was often a necessity for those in need, and the Law provided guidelines to ensure fairness and compassion in these transactions (Exodus 22:25-27). The willingness to lend without hesitation reflects a heart aligned with God's commands, as seen in Deuteronomy 15:7-8, which instructs the Israelites to open their hands to the poor and needy. This phrase also connects to the New Testament teaching in Luke 6:34-35, where Jesus encourages lending without expecting anything in return, thus reflecting the selfless love of Christ. The act of lending in this context is not merely financial but also encompasses offering time, resources, and support to others.

and their children are a blessing
The blessing of children is a recurring theme in Scripture, seen as a sign of God's favor and a reward for righteousness (Psalm 127:3-5). In the cultural and historical context of the Bible, children were considered a vital part of the family unit, contributing to the family's labor and legacy. The phrase suggests that the generosity and righteousness of the parents have a positive impact on their offspring, aligning with Proverbs 20:7, which states, "The righteous lead blameless lives; blessed are their children after them." This reflects the biblical principle that the actions and character of one generation can influence the blessings and prosperity of the next. The idea of children as a blessing also points to the future hope and continuation of God's promises through generations, ultimately fulfilled in the lineage of Jesus Christ.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of Psalm 37, King David, is traditionally believed to have written this psalm. He was a man after God's own heart and often wrote about the righteous and their relationship with God.

2. The Righteous
This psalm speaks about the characteristics of the righteous, those who follow God's ways and live according to His commandments.

3. The Wicked
In contrast to the righteous, the wicked are also mentioned throughout Psalm 37, highlighting the differences in their behaviors and ultimate outcomes.

4. Children of the Righteous
The verse specifically mentions the children of the righteous, indicating the generational blessings that come from living a life pleasing to God.

5. The Land of Israel
While not directly mentioned in this verse, the context of Psalm 37 often reflects the promises and blessings associated with the land given to the Israelites.
Teaching Points
Generosity as a Mark of the Righteous
The righteous are characterized by their generosity. This is not just a one-time act but a continual lifestyle of giving and helping others.

The Blessing of Lending
Lending to others is seen as a positive action, reflecting trust in God's provision and a willingness to support those in need.

Generational Blessings
The children of the righteous are described as a blessing, indicating that the actions and faithfulness of the parents have a lasting impact on future generations.

Living Out Faith in Practical Ways
Our faith should be evident in our actions, particularly in how we manage our resources and treat others.

Trusting in God's Provision
By being generous and lending, we demonstrate our trust in God's ability to provide for our needs, even as we meet the needs of others.

What Does Psalm 37:26 Mean
They are ever generous

“They are ever generous…” (Psalm 37:26) paints a portrait of believers who consistently hold their resources with an open hand.

• This echoes Psalm 112:5, where “Good will come to the man who is generous and lends freely,” affirming that God delights in continual benevolence, not sporadic acts.

• Proverbs 11:24-25 reminds us that “the one who gives freely… will be enriched,” showing God’s built-in principle of blessing generosity.

• In 2 Corinthians 9:6-8 Paul confirms the same pattern: “God loves a cheerful giver,” and He supplies “all grace” so we can “abound in every good work.”

Living generously reflects the Lord’s own character in giving us salvation (John 3:16) and daily provision (James 1:17).

and quick to lend

“…and quick to lend…” (Psalm 37:26) stresses readiness, not reluctance, to meet needs.

• Deuteronomy 15:7-8 commands Israel to open wide their hand to a poor brother; the psalmist shows that righteous people still practice that mindset.

• Jesus amplifies it: “Lend, expecting nothing in return” (Luke 6:34-35), tying generous lending to our Father’s kindness.

• Proverbs 3:27 urges, “Do not withhold good from those to whom it is due, when it is in your power to act,” highlighting promptness.

This attitude trusts God’s promise in Psalm 37:25 that the righteous will not be forsaken; therefore, they can lend without fear of lack.

and their children are a blessing

“…and their children are a blessing.” (Psalm 37:26) links parental generosity with multigenerational favor.

• Proverbs 20:7 notes, “The righteous who walks in integrity—blessed are his children after him,” demonstrating how godly living leaves a tangible legacy.

• Psalm 112:1-2 promises that the offspring of those who fear the LORD “will be mighty in the land.” God’s covenant faithfulness embraces the family line (Genesis 17:7).

• Isaiah 54:13 assures, “All your children will be taught by the LORD, and great will be their peace,” showing that divine blessing flows beyond material prosperity into spiritual well-being.

The verse assures parents that faithful stewardship today shapes a heritage of favor tomorrow (3 John 4).

summary

Psalm 37:26 celebrates the righteous as people who continually give, eagerly lend, and see their children flourish. God’s dependable care frees believers to live open-handedly, and He responds by extending blessing to the next generation. This verse invites us to trust God’s provision, practice active generosity, and anticipate a ripple effect of grace that touches our families and our communities.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 26. - He is ever merciful, and lendeth (comp. ver. 21). This psalm contains a good deal of repetition, perhaps intended to emphasize certain portions of its teaching (see vers. 1, 7, 8; 3, 27; 11, 22, 29; 7, 34, etc.). And his seed is blessed (comp. Psalm 25:13; Psalm 102:28; Psalm 112:2).
