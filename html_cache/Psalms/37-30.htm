The mouth of the righteous man utters wisdom
This phrase emphasizes the importance of speech that aligns with God's truth. In biblical context, wisdom is often associated with the fear of the Lord (Proverbs 9:10). The righteous man, therefore, is one who lives in reverence to God and reflects His character through wise speech. Wisdom in the Bible is not merely intellectual but practical, guiding one's actions and decisions. The righteous man's words are a reflection of his heart, which is aligned with God's will (Matthew 12:34). This aligns with the wisdom literature of the Old Testament, such as Proverbs and Ecclesiastes, which frequently highlight the value of wise speech.

and his tongue speaks justice
Justice in the biblical sense is rooted in God's character and His laws. The righteous man's speech is not only wise but also just, reflecting God's concern for fairness and righteousness. This phrase suggests that the righteous man advocates for what is right and fair, echoing the prophetic calls for justice found throughout the Old Testament (Isaiah 1:17, Micah 6:8). In a cultural context, justice was a key aspect of Israelite society, where leaders and judges were expected to uphold God's standards. The righteous man's speech serves as a type of Christ, who is the ultimate embodiment of justice and righteousness (Isaiah 42:1-4). Jesus' teachings and actions consistently demonstrated a commitment to justice, providing a model for believers to follow.

Persons / Places / Events
1. David
The author of Psalm 37, King David, is traditionally understood to have written this psalm. He was the second king of Israel and a man after God's own heart, known for his wisdom and justice.

2. The Righteous Man
This refers to individuals who live in accordance with God's laws and principles, embodying wisdom and justice in their speech and actions.

3. Wisdom and Justice
These are key themes in the psalm, representing the qualities that the righteous are expected to exhibit. Wisdom is the application of knowledge and understanding, while justice involves fairness and righteousness in dealings with others.
Teaching Points
The Power of Words
Our words have the power to build up or tear down. As believers, we are called to use our speech to impart wisdom and justice, reflecting the character of Christ.

Cultivating Righteous Speech
Righteous speech is a result of a heart aligned with God. Regular study of Scripture and prayer can help cultivate a heart that naturally speaks wisdom and justice.

Wisdom and Justice in Action
Speaking wisdom and justice is not just about words but also about actions. Our lives should be a testament to the wisdom and justice we profess.

Guarding the Tongue
The tongue can be a source of great good or harm. We must be vigilant in guarding our speech, ensuring it aligns with God's truth and righteousness.

Reflecting Christ in Our Speech
As followers of Christ, our speech should reflect His character. This means speaking truth in love, offering encouragement, and standing for justice.

What Does Psalm 37:30 Mean
The mouth of the righteous man

• Psalm 37 contrasts the righteous with evildoers; here the focus narrows to what comes out of a godly person’s mouth.

• Righteousness is not self-achieved but received by faith (see Romans 3:22), then displayed in daily living (Psalm 1:1-3).

• Because “the mouth of the righteous brings forth wisdom” (Proverbs 10:31), his words consistently reflect God’s character rather than the culture’s shifting opinions.

• Jesus said, “The good man brings good things out of the good treasure stored in him” (Matthew 12:35), reminding us that speech reveals the heart’s condition.

utters wisdom

• Wisdom, in biblical terms, is skill for living in the fear of the Lord (Proverbs 9:10).

• The righteous person’s speech is measured, timely, and practical—guiding others toward godly choices (Proverbs 15:28; Colossians 3:16).

• James links wise words to gentleness and peace (James 3:13-18), showing that wisdom is not merely intellectual but relational.

• Such utterances build up rather than tear down (Ephesians 4:29), reflecting God’s own gracious counsel to His people (Proverbs 2:6).

and his tongue

• David singles out the tongue to emphasize intentional, controlled speech. While the mouth speaks, the tongue shapes each word.

• James warns that an uncontrolled tongue can “set the course of one’s life on fire” (James 3:2-6), yet here we see the opposite: a sanctified tongue restrained by righteousness.

• “May the words of my mouth and the meditation of my heart be pleasing in Your sight” (Psalm 19:14) captures the prayerful vigilance required to keep the tongue honoring God.

• Guarding the tongue protects the whole life (Proverbs 21:23) and models sanctified speech for those listening.

speaks justice

• The righteous do more than avoid corrupt talk; they actively champion what is right. Their words “speak justice,” aligning with God’s righteous standards.

• Speaking justice includes truthful testimony (Zechariah 8:16; Ephesians 4:25), defense of the vulnerable (Isaiah 1:17), and fair judgment in daily matters (Micah 6:8).

• Psalm 15:2-3 describes the person who “speaks truth from the heart” and “does no wrong to a neighbor,” showing how justice and righteousness converge in speech.

• By verbalizing justice, believers mirror the God who “loves righteousness and justice” (Psalm 33:5) and invite others to trust His righteous rule.

summary

Psalm 37:30 sketches a portrait of speech transformed by righteousness. The truly godly person uses his mouth to share heaven-sent wisdom and his tongue to advance justice. Such words stem from a heart aligned with God, build up those who hear, and display the character of the Lord who is both wise and just.
