Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
But You, O LORD,
This phrase acknowledges the personal and covenantal name of God, Yahweh, which signifies His eternal presence and faithfulness. The use of "O LORD" emphasizes a direct address to God, highlighting a personal relationship. In the context of the Psalms, this reflects the psalmist's reliance on God's sovereignty and power. The name Yahweh is first revealed to Moses in Exodus 3:14, establishing God's self-existence and eternal nature. This phrase sets the tone for worship and reverence, recognizing God's supreme authority over all creation.

are exalted
The term "exalted" signifies being lifted high or placed in a position of honor and authority. In biblical terms, God's exaltation is a recognition of His supreme power and majesty. This is a common theme throughout the Psalms, where God's greatness is celebrated (Psalm 97:9). The exaltation of God contrasts with the temporary nature of human power and the futility of idol worship. In the New Testament, Jesus Christ is also described as being exalted by God (Philippians 2:9), drawing a parallel between the divine nature of the Father and the Son.

forever!
The concept of "forever" underscores God's eternal nature, a central tenet of biblical theology. Unlike human rulers or idols, God's reign is everlasting, as affirmed in Psalm 90:2. This eternal aspect of God provides assurance and hope to believers, knowing that His promises and His kingdom are unchanging and enduring. The eternal nature of God is also reflected in the promise of eternal life through Jesus Christ (John 3:16), linking the Old Testament understanding of God's eternity with the New Testament revelation of salvation.

Teaching Points
God's Eternal Sovereignty
The LORD's exaltation is not temporary but eternal, reminding us of His unchanging nature and supreme authority over all creation.

Worship and Praise
Recognizing God's exaltation should lead us to a life of worship and praise, acknowledging His greatness in our daily lives.

Trust in God's Authority
Understanding that God is exalted forever encourages us to trust in His plans and purposes, even when circumstances seem uncertain.

Humility Before God
The exaltation of the LORD calls us to humility, recognizing our place before an almighty and eternal God.

Hope in God's Eternal Reign
The assurance of God's eternal reign provides hope and comfort, knowing that His kingdom will never end.

What Does Psalm 92:8 Mean
But You

• The psalmist pivots from describing the fleeting success of the wicked (Psalm 92:7) to gaze directly at God.

• That tiny word “But” signals a total change of focus—trading earthly chaos for heavenly certainty (see Psalm 73:17; Isaiah 40:6-8).

• “You” draws us into a personal encounter with the Living God, echoing the intimacy found in Psalm 23:1 (“The LORD is my Shepherd”).

• When life feels unpredictable, this phrase reminds us that God Himself is the fixed reference point (James 1:17; Hebrews 13:8).

O LORD

• Addressing Him as “LORD” (Yahweh) highlights His covenant faithfulness first revealed to Moses (Exodus 3:14; Psalm 100:3).

• Unlike idols that cannot act or speak (Psalm 115:4-8), the LORD is the personal, promise-keeping God who hears and responds (1 Kings 18:36-39).

• Calling on His name invites us to trust His character: holy (Isaiah 6:3), righteous (Psalm 145:17), and compassionate (Psalm 86:15).

are exalted

• “Exalted” proclaims God’s supremacy over every power and throne (Psalm 97:9; Isaiah 6:1).

• Nothing in creation rivals His authority—He commands oceans (Mark 4:39), kingdoms (Daniel 4:34-35), and even death itself (Revelation 1:18).

• For believers, His exaltation is comforting: our lives rest beneath an unshakable throne (Colossians 1:17; Philippians 2:9-11).

• Practically, acknowledging His exaltation fuels worship and silences worry (Matthew 6:25-33).

forever!

• God’s exalted reign is not seasonal or temporary; it stretches from eternity past to eternity future (Psalm 90:2; Revelation 4:8-11).

• Human rulers rise and fall (Daniel 2:21), but the LORD’s kingdom endures endlessly (Psalm 145:13).

• This eternal perspective steadies our hearts in trials, knowing every injustice will meet His perfect judgment (Revelation 20:11-15).

• Because His reign has no expiration date, our hope in Him is never misplaced (1 Peter 1:3-5).

summary

Psalm 92:8 lifts our eyes from the momentary turbulence of earth to the eternal throne of God. The psalmist counters every earthly uncertainty with one unchanging reality: “But You, O LORD, are exalted forever!” That single sentence re-centers us on God’s personal presence, covenant faithfulness, supreme authority, and everlasting rule—truths that invite confident worship and unwavering trust today and for all eternity.

Verse 8. - But thou, Lord, art most high forevermore; rather, art on high; i.e. remainest seated upon thy throne, unaffected either by their efforts or by their fall.
