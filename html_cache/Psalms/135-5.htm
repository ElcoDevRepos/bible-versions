Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
For I know that the LORD is great;
This phrase emphasizes personal conviction and acknowledgment of God's greatness. The psalmist's declaration reflects a deep, experiential knowledge of God's power and majesty. In the biblical context, the greatness of the LORD is often demonstrated through His creation, providence, and acts of deliverance, such as the Exodus (Exodus 15:11). The Hebrew word for "great" (gadol) conveys not only size but also importance and significance. This greatness is a recurring theme in the Psalms (Psalm 145:3) and is foundational to understanding God's sovereignty and authority over all creation.

our Lord is above all gods.
This statement asserts the supremacy of the LORD over any other deities, which were often worshiped by surrounding nations. In the ancient Near Eastern context, polytheism was prevalent, with various gods associated with different aspects of life and nature. The psalmist's declaration aligns with the monotheistic belief central to Israel's faith, as seen in the Shema (Deuteronomy 6:4). The phrase "above all gods" underscores the LORD's unique position as the one true God, superior to any idols or false gods (Isaiah 44:6-8). This superiority is also a foreshadowing of Christ's ultimate victory over all spiritual powers and authorities (Colossians 2:15), affirming His divine nature and authority.

Refers to the false gods or idols worshiped by surrounding nations, highlighting the contrast between them and the one true God.

The context of the Psalm, which is often associated with temple worship, where the greatness of God is celebrated.

What Does Psalm 135:5 Mean
For I know

• The psalmist’s declaration is personal and certain—no hesitation, no speculation.

• “I know whom I have believed” (2 Timothy 1:12) mirrors this assurance.

• Job stands in similar confidence: “I know that my Redeemer lives” (Job 19:25).

• Scripture calls believers to move from hearsay to firsthand conviction (1 John 5:13; John 4:42).

that the LORD is great

• “Great is the LORD and greatly to be praised” (Psalm 145:3). His greatness is both qualitative (His character) and quantitative (His works).

• Creation showcases that greatness: “He determines the number of the stars; He calls them each by name” (Psalm 147:4-5).

• Israel saw it in redemption—“The LORD has done great things for us” (Psalm 126:3).

• Jeremiah joined the chorus: “There is none like You, O LORD; You are great, and Your name is mighty in power” (Jeremiah 10:6).

• God’s greatness evokes worship, humility, and confidence for present trials.

our Lord is above all gods

• The statement is not a concession that other deities actually rival Him; rather, it declares His absolute supremacy. “For the LORD is a great God, a great King above all gods” (Psalm 95:3).

• Exodus 15:11 asks, “Who among the gods is like You, O LORD?”—answering its own question: none.

• Isaiah 44:6 boldly asserts, “I am the first and I am the last; apart from Me there is no God.”

• In the New Testament, Christ’s victory parades the same truth: “He disarmed the rulers and authorities and made a public spectacle of them” (Colossians 2:15).

• Every knee will bow and every tongue confess that Jesus Christ is Lord (Philippians 2:9-11), confirming that He stands alone, unrivaled, and uncontested.

summary

Psalm 135:5 moves from personal certainty to cosmic supremacy. The believer confidently knows, not guesses, that the covenant LORD is immeasurably great and exalted above every so-called god, idol, or power. That settled assurance fuels wholehearted worship and unwavering trust in every circumstance.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 5. - For I know that the Lord is great, and that our Lord is above all gods. Here is the second reason why God should be praised - he is great, greater far than any other being - "above all gods" - "more to be feared than all gods" (Psalm 96:4). This greatness is shown, firstly, in his power over nature, which is the subject of vers. 7, 8; and secondly, in his dealings with mankind, which form the subject of vers. 8-12.
