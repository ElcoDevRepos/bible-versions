This is my practice
The psalmist refers to a habitual action or lifestyle. In the context of Psalm 119, this practice involves a deep commitment to God's Word. The Hebrew culture emphasized the importance of daily meditation and adherence to the Torah, which was central to Jewish life and worship. This practice is not merely ritualistic but is a reflection of a heart devoted to God. The idea of practice here can be linked to the concept of spiritual discipline, which is echoed in the New Testament, such as in 1 Timothy 4:7-8, where believers are encouraged to train themselves in godliness.

for I obey Your precepts
The psalmist's practice is rooted in obedience to God's commands. The term "precepts" refers to specific instructions or guidelines given by God, often seen as part of the covenant relationship between God and His people. Obedience to these precepts is a recurring theme throughout Psalm 119, highlighting the psalmist's desire to live in accordance with God's will. This obedience is not out of compulsion but out of love and reverence for God. In the broader biblical narrative, obedience is a key aspect of faith, as seen in the lives of figures like Abraham (Genesis 22) and Jesus Christ, who perfectly fulfilled the law (Matthew 5:17). The psalmist's commitment to obeying God's precepts can be seen as a type of Christ, who exemplified perfect obedience to the Father.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Psalmist
The author of Psalm 119, traditionally believed to be King David, though the exact authorship is uncertain. The psalmist is expressing a deep commitment to God's laws.

2. God
The central figure to whom the psalmist is speaking, emphasizing a relationship based on obedience and reverence.

3. Israel
The nation to which the psalmist belongs, often seen as the recipient of God's laws and precepts.

4. Precepts
The commandments or instructions given by God, which the psalmist is committed to following.

5. Psalm 119
The longest chapter in the Bible, focusing on the beauty and importance of God's law.
Teaching Points
Commitment to God's Word
The psalmist's practice of obeying God's precepts serves as a model for believers to prioritize God's Word in their daily lives.

Consistency in Obedience
The phrase "This is my practice" suggests a habitual and consistent approach to following God's commands, encouraging believers to develop regular spiritual disciplines.

Joy in Obedience
Obedience to God's precepts is not portrayed as a burden but as a source of joy and fulfillment, inviting believers to find delight in God's commandments.

Relationship with God
The psalmist's obedience is rooted in a personal relationship with God, reminding believers that following God's laws is an expression of love and devotion.

Reflection and Meditation
Encourages believers to reflect on their own practices and consider how they can align more closely with God's precepts.

What Does Psalm 119:56 Mean
This

• “This” points back to the pattern just described in verses 49–55: remembering God’s promises (v. 49), finding comfort in affliction through His word (v. 50), refusing to turn from His law despite derision (v. 51), recalling His past judgments (v. 52), grieving over the wicked who reject His law (v. 53), singing His statutes as a pilgrim on earth (v. 54), and calling on His name even in the night (v. 55).

• Scripture ties the blessing of steadfast remembrance to life and stability (Joshua 1:8; Psalm 1:2–3).

• The psalmist’s entire outlook—hope, comfort, song, vigilance—flows from the convictions formed by God’s word.

is my practice

• “Practice” highlights a settled habit, not a sporadic impulse. Obedience is woven into daily routines (Deuteronomy 6:6–9).

• Faithful disciplines include:

– Regular meditation (Psalm 63:6).

– Night-time reflection and prayer (Psalm 16:7; 42:8).

– Public and private praise (Psalm 34:1; Hebrews 13:15).

• These rhythms cultivate resilience when mockery or hardship arises (James 1:25).

for I obey

• Obedience is the reason such practices exist; devotion without submission would be empty. Jesus linked love to obedience: “If you love Me, you will keep My commandments” (John 14:15).

• True obedience involves the heart and the will (1 Samuel 15:22; Romans 6:17).

• The psalmist’s obedience springs from trust in God’s character and promises, not mere duty (Psalm 119:32, 45).

Your precepts

• “Precepts” emphasize detailed, practical directives of God’s law—clear guidance for everyday choices (Psalm 19:8).

• They are personal: “Your” precepts come from a covenant-keeping Lord who expects to be heard and heeded (Exodus 24:7).

• By submitting to these precepts, the believer walks securely (Proverbs 3:5–6) and shines as a light in a dark world (Philippians 2:15–16).

summary

Psalm 119:56 captures the psalmist’s lifestyle in one concise statement: his established habit of remembering, singing, meditating, and praying flows directly from a heart committed to obeying God’s specific commands. Because the Lord’s word is sure, the believer’s daily routine is deliberately shaped by it, yielding comfort, perseverance, and joyful worship no matter the circumstances.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(56) This I had, because . . .--Literally, This was to me, &c, i.e., this consoling recollection of the mercies of God, of His covenant grace, was to him, happened, or came to him, in consequence of his habitual obedience. Virtue is indeed then most its own reward, in times of quiet reflection, like the night, when to the guilty come remorse and apprehension, but to the good man "calm thoughts regular as infant's breath."

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 56. - This I had, because I kept thy precepts; rather, this I have, that I keep thy precepts; i.e. this one thing I have, and it is my best and dearest possession, that I keep thy commandments.
