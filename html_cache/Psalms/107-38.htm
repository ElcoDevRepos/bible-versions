He blesses them
This phrase highlights God's active role in bestowing favor and prosperity upon His people. In the Old Testament, blessings often signify God's covenantal faithfulness, as seen in His promises to Abraham (Genesis 12:2-3). The concept of blessing is deeply rooted in the Hebrew understanding of God's provision and protection. It reflects the idea that all good things come from God, aligning with James 1:17, which states that every good and perfect gift is from above.

and they multiply greatly;
Multiplication here refers to both population growth and prosperity. This echoes the blessings given to the patriarchs, such as God's promise to Abraham that his descendants would be as numerous as the stars (Genesis 15:5). In a historical context, population growth was a sign of divine favor and strength, crucial for survival and influence in the ancient Near East. This multiplication can also be seen as a type of spiritual growth and expansion of God's kingdom, as seen in the New Testament with the spread of the Gospel (Acts 6:7).

He does not let their herds diminish.
In an agrarian society, herds were a primary measure of wealth and sustenance. The protection and increase of livestock signify God's provision and care for the material needs of His people. This assurance of not letting herds diminish reflects God's promise of sustenance and abundance, similar to His provision for Israel in the wilderness (Exodus 16). It also connects to the idea of God as a shepherd, a theme prevalent in Scripture, notably in Psalm 23, where God provides and cares for His flock.

Teaching Points
God's Blessing and Multiplication
God's blessings lead to growth and abundance. Just as He multiplied the Israelites' herds, He desires to multiply our spiritual and material blessings when we walk in obedience.

Divine Provision
Trust in God's provision. He is faithful to sustain and increase what we have, ensuring that our needs are met.

Stewardship of God's Gifts
With blessing comes responsibility. We are called to be good stewards of the resources and blessings God provides, using them for His glory and the benefit of others.

Faith in God's Promises
Stand firm in faith, believing that God will fulfill His promises of abundance and care, even in times of scarcity or challenge.

Gratitude and Praise
Cultivate a heart of gratitude for God's blessings. Recognize His hand in the multiplication and preservation of what we have.

What Does Psalm 107:38 Mean
He blesses them

“ He blesses them ” (Psalm 107:38) highlights the initiating work of God. Fruitfulness does not begin with human effort but with divine favor.

• The pattern stretches back to Genesis 1:28, where God blessed the first couple before commanding them to be fruitful.

• Israel heard the same assurance in Numbers 6:24 – “ The LORD bless you and keep you ”—confirming that every good gift flows from Him (James 1:17).

• Psalm 67:6-7 echoes, “ The earth has yielded its increase; God, our God, blesses us. ” Blessing is not vague optimism; it is the covenant God actively providing.

and they multiply greatly

Fruitfulness is the immediate result of God’s blessing.

• Genesis 17:2 shows this link when God promises Abraham, “ I will multiply you exceedingly. ”

• Centuries later, Exodus 1:7 notes that the Israelites “ multiplied greatly, so that the land was filled with them, ” confirming the promise despite hardship.

• Deuteronomy 30:9 assures a repentant people, “ The LORD your God will make you prosper abundantly in all the work of your hands, in the offspring of your womb… ” Abundance is God’s normal intention for His people when they walk with Him.

He does not let their herds diminish

God’s care extends to everyday necessities. Livestock meant wealth, food, and future security in an agrarian society.

• Deuteronomy 7:13-14 vows that God “ will bless the fruit of your womb and the fruit of your land… the calves of your herds and the lambs of your flocks. ”

• Job 1:10 reveals Satan’s frustration that the LORD had “ blessed the work of his hands, and his livestock have increased in the land. ”

• Psalm 144:13-14 prays for “ our barns filled with every kind of provision; our sheep increasing by thousands… our oxen well laden, ” picturing a community whose animals thrive under God’s watch.

summary

Psalm 107:38 celebrates the full circle of divine favor: God initiates blessing, His people experience exponential growth, and even their livestock flourish under His protection. It assures believers that the LORD’s covenant care touches every sphere—family, resources, future—inviting grateful trust in His unfailing provision.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 38. - He blesseth them also, so that they are multiplied greatly. With in creasing prosperity comes increase of population, naturally - i.e. by God's ordinary providence. This increase is, however, only a blessing within certain limits. And suffereth not their cattle to decrease. This modest under-statement suggests an enormous increase (comp. Job 42:12).
