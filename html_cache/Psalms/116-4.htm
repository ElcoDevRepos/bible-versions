Then I called on the name of the LORD:
This phrase reflects a common biblical practice of invoking God's name in times of distress. Calling on the name of the LORD signifies a personal relationship and trust in God's power and character. In the Old Testament, the name of the LORD is often associated with His covenant faithfulness and deliverance (Exodus 3:14-15). The act of calling on God's name is seen throughout the Psalms and is a demonstration of faith and reliance on divine intervention (Psalm 18:6, Psalm 50:15). This practice is rooted in the understanding that God is both transcendent and immanent, capable of intervening in human affairs.

“O LORD, deliver my soul!”
The plea for deliverance is a central theme in the Psalms, reflecting the human condition of vulnerability and the need for divine rescue. The term "soul" (Hebrew: nephesh) encompasses the whole being, indicating a plea for comprehensive salvation, not just physical but also spiritual and emotional. This cry for deliverance can be seen as a type of Christ, who in the New Testament is the ultimate deliverer of souls (Matthew 1:21, John 3:16). The historical context of the psalmist's plea may reflect personal or national crises, such as illness, persecution, or warfare, common in the ancient Near East. This cry echoes the broader biblical narrative of God's people seeking His salvation, culminating in the redemptive work of Jesus Christ.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Psalmist
The author of Psalm 116, traditionally believed to be King David, who often wrote about his personal experiences with God, expressing both distress and deliverance.

2. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal presence and faithfulness to His people. The Psalmist calls upon Yahweh for deliverance.

3. The Event of Distress
The Psalmist is in a situation of deep trouble or danger, prompting a heartfelt plea for divine intervention.
Teaching Points
The Power of Calling on God's Name
The act of calling on the LORD's name is a powerful expression of faith and trust in His ability to save and deliver.

God's Readiness to Deliver
God is always ready to respond to the cries of His people. The Psalmist's plea demonstrates that no situation is beyond God's reach.

The Importance of Personal Prayer
Personal prayer is a vital practice for believers. It is a direct line to God, allowing us to express our deepest needs and desires.

Faith in Times of Trouble
In moments of distress, turning to God should be our first response. This reflects a heart that trusts in God's sovereignty and goodness.

The Assurance of God's Presence
Calling on the LORD assures us of His presence and willingness to intervene in our lives, providing comfort and hope.

What Does Psalm 116:4 Mean
Then I called on the name of the LORD

- “Then” points back to verse 3, where “the cords of death encompassed me.” The sequence is clear: danger first, prayer second (cf. Psalm 18:6; Jonah 2:2).

- Calling “on the name of the LORD” means invoking His revealed, covenant name—turning to the only One who can help (Genesis 4:26; Psalm 99:6; Romans 10:13).

- This is a conscious act of faith, not a last-ditch superstition. The psalmist trusts that the Lord is listening and able (Isaiah 55:6; Hebrews 11:6).

- Practical takeaway: when trouble strikes, the believer’s first instinct is to speak God’s name aloud, anchoring hope in His unchanging character (Malachi 3:6; James 1:17).

“O LORD, deliver my soul!”

- The prayer itself is short, direct, and urgent—no embellishment, just the essential plea (cf. 2 Kings 20:3; Luke 18:13).

- “Deliver” highlights rescue from both physical peril and spiritual threat. God is seen as Savior of life now and forever (Psalm 34:6; 2 Timothy 4:18).

- “My soul” personalizes the request; faith is never abstract. The psalmist believes God values and preserves the individual life (Psalm 103:4; John 10:28).

- This cry echoes throughout Scripture: “This poor man cried, and the LORD heard him” (Psalm 34:6); “Whoever calls on the name of the Lord will be saved” (Acts 2:21).

summary

Psalm 116:4 captures the believer’s reflex in crisis: turn immediately to the Lord, speak His name, and plead for rescue. God’s readiness to hear confirms His covenant faithfulness, and the psalmist’s simple cry models the trusting heart that Scripture repeatedly assures will never be put to shame.
