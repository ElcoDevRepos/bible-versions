Yet I am always with You;
This phrase reflects the psalmist's acknowledgment of God's constant presence. Despite the struggles and doubts expressed earlier in the psalm, there is a realization of an unbroken relationship with God. This echoes the promise found in Deuteronomy 31:6, where God assures His people that He will never leave nor forsake them. The use of "always" emphasizes the perpetual nature of God's presence, which is a recurring theme throughout the Psalms and is foundational to the believer's faith journey. This assurance is also seen in the New Testament, where Jesus promises His disciples in Matthew 28:20, "I am with you always, to the very end of the age."

You hold my right hand.
The imagery of God holding the psalmist's right hand signifies guidance, support, and protection. In ancient Near Eastern culture, the right hand was often associated with power and authority. By holding the psalmist's right hand, God is depicted as a personal guide and protector, leading the believer through life's challenges. This image is reminiscent of Isaiah 41:13, where God says, "For I am the LORD your God who takes hold of your right hand and says to you, Do not fear; I will help you." It also prefigures the role of Jesus as the Good Shepherd in John 10:27-28, who leads and secures His followers. The act of holding hands is intimate and personal, underscoring the closeness of the relationship between God and the believer.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Asaph
The author of Psalm 73, Asaph was a prominent Levite singer and seer in David's court. He is known for his deep reflections on the prosperity of the wicked and the struggles of the righteous.

2. God
The central figure in this verse, God is depicted as a constant presence and support for the believer, holding their right hand.

3. The Sanctuary of God
While not directly mentioned in this verse, the sanctuary is a place of revelation and understanding for Asaph, as seen earlier in the psalm.
Teaching Points
God's Constant Presence
Despite life's challenges, believers can find comfort in knowing that God is always with them. This assurance should encourage us to trust Him more deeply.

Divine Guidance and Support
The imagery of God holding our right hand signifies His guidance and support. We should seek His direction in our daily decisions and rely on His strength.

Faith in Times of Doubt
Asaph's journey from doubt to faith reminds us that questioning and struggles are part of the faith journey. We should bring our doubts to God and seek His wisdom.

The Importance of Perspective
Asaph's realization in the sanctuary teaches us the importance of seeking God's perspective, especially when we are troubled by the apparent success of the wicked.

Living in God's Presence
Practically, living with the awareness of God's presence involves regular prayer, meditation on Scripture, and a heart open to His leading.

What Does Psalm 73:23 Mean
Yet

- The psalmist has just confessed that envy and doubt had made him “senseless and ignorant” (Psalm 73:22). That raw honesty sets the stage for the single word “Yet”—a pivot from confusion to confidence.

- Throughout Scripture, “yet” often signals faith overriding circumstances: “But I have trusted in Your loving devotion” (Psalm 13:5); “yet I will exult in the LORD” (Habakkuk 3:18).

- The term anchors the heart to what is unchanging in God when everything around seems unjust or unstable.

I am always with You

- This is not merely God with us; it is we with Him—an unbroken fellowship secured by His covenant love.

- David celebrated the same reality: “Where can I go from Your Spirit? … even there Your hand will guide me” (Psalm 139:7-10).

- Jesus echoes it: “And surely I am with you always, even to the end of the age” (Matthew 28:20).

- Because the promise is literal and true, we can rest in it moment by moment:

• God’s nearness is constant (Hebrews 13:5, “Never will I leave you”).

• Our role is to remain in that nearness (“Remain in Me, and I will remain in you,” John 15:4).

You hold my right hand

- The right hand in Scripture pictures strength, honor, and action. For God to take that hand means:

• Guidance—He leads each step (Isaiah 41:13, “I am the LORD … who takes hold of your right hand”).

• Support—“Your right hand upholds me” (Psalm 18:35).

• Security—“No one can snatch them out of My hand” (John 10:28).

- The image is tender as well as powerful: like a father steadying a child, the Lord personally steadies those who trust Him.

- Because He literally holds on, the believer need not fear slipping, even when walking through doubts, valleys, or temptations.

summary

Psalm 73:23 affirms that, in spite of wavering emotions or perplexing circumstances, the believer enjoys uninterrupted closeness with God and steadfast support from Him. The pivot word “Yet” declares a choice to trust; “I am always with You” celebrates an unbreakable relationship; “You hold my right hand” assures daily guidance and safety. Taken together, the verse invites us to live confidently, knowing our faithful God is both present and actively holding us fast.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 23. - Nevertheless I am continually with thee; i.e. "nevertheless, I have not fallen away, but have kept always my hold upon thee;" and, on thy part, thou hast holden me by my right hand; i.e. thou hast upheld me and prevented me from slipping (comp. Psalm 18:35; Psalm 89:21; Psalm 119:117).
