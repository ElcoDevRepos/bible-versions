Let them exalt Him in the assembly of the people
This phrase calls for public worship and acknowledgment of God's greatness. The "assembly of the people" refers to the gathering of the Israelites, which was a central aspect of their communal life. In ancient Israel, assemblies were often held at the temple in Jerusalem, a place of significant religious and cultural importance. This setting underscores the communal nature of worship, where individuals come together to honor God collectively. The act of exalting God in such a setting emphasizes the importance of corporate worship, a theme echoed in other scriptures such as Psalm 22:22 and Hebrews 2:12, where the faithful are encouraged to declare God's name among the congregation. This public declaration of faith serves as a testimony to others and strengthens the community's spiritual bond.

and praise Him in the council of the elders.
The "council of the elders" refers to the leaders and decision-makers within the community. In ancient Israel, elders were respected figures who held authority and were responsible for maintaining justice and order. Praising God in their presence highlights the importance of integrating faith into leadership and governance. This phrase suggests that those in positions of authority should also be involved in worship and set an example for others. It reflects the biblical principle that leadership should be grounded in reverence for God, as seen in passages like Proverbs 11:14 and 1 Timothy 5:17. The inclusion of elders in worship underscores the idea that all levels of society, from the general populace to its leaders, are called to honor God, ensuring that His principles guide both personal and communal life.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The Assembly of the People
This refers to the gathering of the Israelites, often for worship or important communal decisions. It signifies a collective body united in purpose and faith.

2. The Council of the Elders
A group of respected leaders within the community who provide wisdom and guidance. In ancient Israel, elders held significant authority and were often involved in decision-making processes.

Teaching Points
The Importance of Corporate Worship
Gathering together as a community to worship God is vital for spiritual growth and unity. It reflects the biblical pattern of communal praise and accountability.

Role of Leadership in Worship
Elders and leaders play a crucial role in guiding worship and maintaining doctrinal integrity. Their wisdom and experience are invaluable to the spiritual health of the community.

Exalting God in All Circumstances
Regardless of personal or communal challenges, believers are called to exalt God. This act of worship acknowledges His sovereignty and faithfulness.

The Power of Testimony
Sharing testimonies of God's goodness in the assembly encourages others and strengthens faith. It is a powerful way to exalt God and inspire collective worship.

Unity in Diversity
The assembly and council represent diverse groups coming together for a common purpose. This unity in diversity is a testament to the power of God's love and grace.

What Does Psalm 107:32 Mean
Let them exalt Him

“Let them exalt Him” calls every redeemed person to lift God high in honor. This is not a suggestion but a command flowing from grateful hearts, echoing Psalm 107:31, “Let them give thanks to the Lord for His loving devotion.”

• Exalting means openly magnifying God’s character—His steadfast love, wisdom, and saving power (Psalm 34:3; Revelation 5:12).

• It recognizes His rightful supremacy over all creation (Psalm 97:9).

• It testifies to His intervening grace in personal stories of rescue described throughout Psalm 107.

In the assembly of the people

The praise is to take place “in the assembly of the people,” underscoring public worship, not private isolation.

• Israel gathered at appointed feasts to recount God’s mighty acts (Deuteronomy 16:16).

• The early church continued this pattern, meeting regularly for teaching, breaking bread, and praise (Acts 2:46-47; Hebrews 10:24-25).

• Corporate worship unites diverse believers under one confession, fulfilling Jesus’ prayer for oneness (John 17:20-23).

• When God’s people unite in praise, His presence is uniquely manifest (Psalm 22:22; Matthew 18:20).

And praise Him

“Praise” is the natural overflow of exalting hearts, expressed in words, song, and joyful testimony.

• The psalmist models this by recounting specific deliverances—storm-tossed sailors saved, prisoners set free, wanderers led home (Psalm 107:4-30).

• Praise seats God on the throne of our affections (Psalm 22:3).

• New-covenant believers are urged to “continually offer to God a sacrifice of praise—the fruit of lips that confess His name” (Hebrews 13:15; Ephesians 5:19).

In the council of the elders

The spotlight now shifts to leadership circles—“the council of the elders.”

• Israel’s elders judged, guided, and taught (Exodus 18:21; Deuteronomy 27:1).

• God expects leaders to model worship, not merely direct it (2 Chronicles 29:20-28).

• Elders who praise cultivate congregations that praise; their example guards against dry formality (1 Peter 5:3).

• Heaven’s “twenty-four elders” fall down before the Lamb, illustrating that no rank exempts anyone from wholehearted adoration (Revelation 4:10-11).

summary

Psalm 107:32 calls rescued people to exalt and praise the Lord publicly and passionately, both in the wide congregation and within leadership circles. True gratitude refuses to stay silent; it gathers with the faithful, follows the lead of godly elders, and lifts God high so that all may see and join in celebrating His steadfast love.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(32) Let them exalt.--The addition of this to the refrain, as of 22 to that of the last stanza, clearly points to a liturgical use in the psalm.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 32. - Let them exalt him also in the congregation of the people. The psalmist holds it to be not enough for men who have received deliverances to thank God in their hearts, or secretly in their chambers. He requires them to make public profession of their thankfulness "in the congregation of the people." The Christian Church maintains the same attitude. And praise him in the assembly of the elders. The elders led the congregation and presided in it (Ezra 3:9-11; Ezra 6:16-22; Nehemiah 8:4-9; Nehemiah 9:4, 5; Nehemiah 12:27-43, etc.).
