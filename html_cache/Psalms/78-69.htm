He built His sanctuary like the heights
This phrase refers to God's construction of His holy dwelling place, the sanctuary, which is often understood as the temple in Jerusalem. The "heights" symbolize majesty, grandeur, and divine presence, suggesting that the sanctuary is exalted and set apart. In biblical context, mountains and high places are frequently associated with divine encounters, such as Mount Sinai where Moses received the Law (Exodus 19) and Mount Zion, the location of the temple (Psalm 48:1-2). The temple's design and construction, as described in 1 Kings 6, reflect the heavenly pattern shown to David and Solomon, indicating its divine origin and purpose. The sanctuary serves as a type of Christ, who is the ultimate dwelling place of God among men (John 1:14, Colossians 2:9).

like the earth He has established forever
This phrase emphasizes the permanence and stability of God's creation, drawing a parallel between the enduring nature of the earth and the sanctuary. The earth's establishment is a testament to God's sovereign power and faithfulness, as seen in Genesis 1:1 and Psalm 104:5. The sanctuary, like the earth, is meant to be a lasting testament to God's presence and covenant with His people. This permanence is echoed in the New Testament, where the church is described as a spiritual house and temple (1 Peter 2:5, Ephesians 2:19-22), signifying the eternal nature of God's kingdom. The imagery of the earth also points to the new creation promised in Revelation 21:1-3, where God's dwelling will be with humanity forever, fulfilled in Christ.

Persons / Places / Events
1. God
The primary actor in this verse, God is depicted as the builder of His sanctuary, emphasizing His role as Creator and Sustainer.

2. Sanctuary
Refers to the holy place where God dwells among His people, symbolizing His presence and holiness.

3. Heights
This term suggests the grandeur and majesty of God's creation, often associated with mountains or high places that signify strength and permanence.

4. Earth
Represents the physical world that God has established, highlighting His power and the enduring nature of His creation.

5. Israel
While not directly mentioned in this verse, Israel is the nation for whom the sanctuary was built, serving as a central place of worship and covenant relationship with God.

What Does Psalm 78:69 Mean
He built His sanctuary

Psalm 78:69 opens with, “He built His sanctuary.” God Himself is the architect and builder.

• This points first to the tabernacle (Exodus 25:8 – 9: “And they are to make a sanctuary for Me, so that I may dwell among them.”) and then to the temple Solomon raised (1 Kings 8:13).

• It is the place of His dwelling and revelation (Psalm 132:13–14).

• The passage reminds us God initiates worship; we do not design access to Him—He does.

• By calling it “His sanctuary,” ownership and holiness are stressed; it belongs to Him, is filled by Him, and is set apart for Him alone (Leviticus 19:30).

like the heights

The comparison “like the heights” (sometimes rendered “like the lofty heavens”) draws our gaze upward.

• Mountains and skies in Scripture symbolize permanence, majesty, and transcendence (Psalm 36:5–6; Isaiah 57:15).

• God positions His dwelling “high,” not to distance Himself, but to proclaim His supremacy (Psalm 113:4–6).

• Because His sanctuary is “like the heights,” worship is meant to lift hearts beyond earthly limits to the greatness of the Lord (Colossians 3:1–2).

• The imagery also foreshadows the heavenly sanctuary where Christ ministers on our behalf (Hebrews 8:1–2).

like the earth

Next we read, “like the earth.” The sanctuary is compared not only to what is lofty but also to what is wide and solid.

• The earth’s foundation speaks of stability and reach (Psalm 104:5).

• God’s dwelling is intended to be accessible on the ground where His people live and walk (Exodus 40:34–38).

• This pairing—“heights” and “earth”—shows God’s presence spanning heaven and earth, fulfilling the promise of Isaiah 66:1: “Heaven is My throne, and the earth is My footstool.”

• The temple in Jerusalem thus became a visible testimony that the Creator of all shares life with His covenant people in their everyday world (Psalm 46:4–5).

He has established forever

The verse closes with lasting certainty: “He has established forever.”

• God’s dwelling place is not a temporary shelter; His commitment to be among His people is eternal (Psalm 125:1; Revelation 21:3).

• Even though the physical temple was later destroyed (2 Chronicles 36:19), God’s promise endures in the greater, unshakable sanctuary fulfilled in Christ and His body, the Church (Ephesians 2:19–22).

• The permanence of His sanctuary guarantees the permanence of His covenant love (Psalm 103:17).

• For believers, this means our future with Him is secure and unending (John 14:2–3).

summary

Psalm 78:69 layers grand imagery to declare that God personally erected His holy dwelling, breathtaking as the heights, solid as the earth, and enduring forever. His sanctuary calls us upward in worship, grounds us in daily life, and assures us of His unbreakable, eternal presence.

Ellicott's Commentary for English Readers
(69) He built.--The first clause is vague, but evidently the poet is drawing attention to the grandeur and solidity of the Temple. Perhaps, high as heaven--firm as earth.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 69. - And he built his sanctuary like high palaces; rather, like the heights. The "heights of heaven" (Job 11:8; Job 22:12) are probably meant. Like the earth which he hath established forever; i.e. lofty as heaven, stable and firmly fixed as earth. The ultimate fate of the sanctuary is mercifully hidden from the psalmist.
