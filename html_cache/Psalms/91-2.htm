I will say to the LORD
This phrase indicates a personal declaration of faith and trust in God. The use of "LORD" refers to Yahweh, the covenant name of God, emphasizing a personal relationship. This declaration is an act of worship and acknowledgment of God's sovereignty and faithfulness. In biblical context, speaking to the LORD is an act of prayer and communication, reflecting the psalmist's confidence in God's presence and protection.

You are my refuge and my fortress
The imagery of "refuge" and "fortress" conveys a sense of safety and protection. In ancient times, a refuge was a place of shelter from danger, while a fortress was a stronghold that provided defense against enemies. This metaphor highlights God's role as a protector and defender. The use of these terms suggests a deep trust in God's ability to provide security and safety, much like a fortified city or a safe haven. This imagery is consistent with other biblical passages that describe God as a shield and protector, such as in Psalm 18:2.

my God
The phrase "my God" signifies a personal relationship and ownership, indicating that the psalmist has a personal connection with the divine. This expression of faith is rooted in the covenant relationship between God and His people, as seen throughout the Old Testament. It reflects the psalmist's acknowledgment of God's authority and power, as well as a personal commitment to Him.

in whom I trust
Trust in God is a central theme throughout the Bible, and this phrase underscores the psalmist's reliance on God's faithfulness and promises. Trust implies confidence and reliance, suggesting that the psalmist has experienced God's protection and provision in the past. This trust is not blind but is based on the knowledge of God's character and past acts of deliverance. The concept of trust is echoed in other scriptures, such as Proverbs 3:5-6, which encourages believers to trust in the LORD with all their heart.

Persons / Places / Events
1. The LORD (Yahweh)
The covenant name of God, emphasizing His eternal, self-existent nature and His faithfulness to His promises.

2. The Psalmist
Traditionally attributed to Moses or David, the psalmist is expressing personal trust and confidence in God.

3. Refuge and Fortress

4. Trust

What Does Psalm 91:2 Mean
I will say to the LORD

- The psalmist begins with a deliberate, spoken confession. Faith is voiced, not just felt (Psalm 107:2: “Let the redeemed of the LORD say so,”).

- Speaking to “the LORD” (Yahweh) centers the focus on the covenant-keeping God who revealed Himself to Israel (Exodus 3:15).

- Declaring truth out loud reinforces confidence, much like Romans 10:10: “For with your heart you believe and are justified, and with your mouth you confess and are saved”.

You are my refuge

- A refuge is a safe shelter when storms rage. Psalm 46:1 echoes this: “God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble”.

- This is not a distant hiding place but a Person who welcomes us (Psalm 34:8).

- Choosing God as refuge means rejecting lesser shelters—self-reliance, wealth, popularity (Proverbs 18:10; Matthew 6:19-21).

and my fortress

- A fortress pictures strong, fortified walls—security against armed attack. Psalm 18:2 agrees: “The LORD is my rock, my fortress, and my deliverer”.

- Fortresses are built for prolonged siege; God sustains us not only in sudden crises but through long battles (2 Corinthians 12:9).

- This image reassures believers that no enemy—spiritual or physical—can breach God’s defense (Ephesians 6:10-11).

my God

- Personal pronoun “my” shows intimacy. The psalmist claims covenant relationship, echoing Exodus 20:2: “I am the LORD your God”.

- New Testament faith echoes the same ownership: Thomas exclaimed to Jesus, “My Lord and my God!” (John 20:28).

- Calling Him “my God” aligns the heart with His supremacy, rejecting all idols (1 John 5:21).

in whom I trust

- Trust is more than belief; it is reliance. Proverbs 3:5 urges, “Trust in the LORD with all your heart and lean not on your own understanding”.

- Trust grows as we remember past deliverances (Psalm 77:11-12) and rehearse His promises (Hebrews 13:5-6).

- The phrase completes a progression: declare (“I will say”), identify (“You are”), personalize (“my”), then rest (“in whom I trust”).

summary

Psalm 91:2 is a spoken act of faith that rests in a personal, covenant God. By naming the LORD as refuge and fortress, the believer claims Him as the only secure shelter. Calling Him “my God” seals the relationship, and trusting in Him transforms fear into confidence. The verse invites every follower of Christ to voice the same assurance: God Himself is our safe place, our strong defense, and the One we confidently rely on.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 2. - I will say of the Lord. The general sentiment is followed by a personal application. "I, at any rate," says the first speaker, "will place myself under this powerful protection." He is my Refuge and my Fortress (comp. Psalm 18:2; Psalm 144:2). My God; in him will I trust (comp. Psalm 29:2; Psalm 31:6; Psalm 55:23; Psalm 56:3; Psalm 61:4, etc.).
