then the raging waters
In biblical literature, water often symbolizes chaos and danger. The imagery of "raging waters" evokes the idea of overwhelming trials and adversities. In the ancient Near East, floods were a common threat, and the Israelites would have been familiar with the destructive power of water. This phrase can be connected to the Red Sea crossing (Exodus 14), where God delivered Israel from the Egyptian army, symbolizing His power over chaos. The "raging waters" can also be seen as a metaphor for the trials and tribulations faced by believers, which God can deliver them from.

would have swept us away
This phrase emphasizes the totality of the threat and the helplessness of the people without divine intervention. The idea of being "swept away" suggests complete destruction and loss, akin to the fate of those who perished in the flood during Noah's time (Genesis 7). It underscores the necessity of God's protection and deliverance. In a broader theological context, this can be seen as a type of Christ's saving work, where He rescues humanity from the overwhelming tide of sin and death. The imagery of being "swept away" also resonates with the New Testament, where Jesus calms the storm (Mark 4:39), demonstrating His authority over nature and chaos.

Teaching Points
God's Sovereignty Over Chaos
Just as God delivered Israel from overwhelming threats, He remains sovereign over the chaos in our lives. Trust in His power to save and protect.

The Symbolism of Water in Scripture
Water often represents both life and destruction in the Bible. Recognize the dual nature of water as a symbol and how God uses it to demonstrate His control and provision.

The Importance of Remembering Past Deliverance
Reflect on past instances of God's deliverance in your life as a source of encouragement and faith for current and future challenges.

Community in Facing Trials
Like Israel, we are part of a community of believers. Support and pray for one another, recognizing that we face trials together with God's help.

Faith in God's Deliverance
Cultivate a faith that trusts in God's deliverance, even when circumstances seem insurmountable. Remember that God is faithful to His promises.

What Does Psalm 124:5 Mean
setting the scene

Psalm 124 opens with David inviting Israel to ponder where they would be “if the LORD had not been on our side.” The verse in focus—“then the raging waters would have swept us away” (v. 5)—caps a dramatic description of danger. Throughout Scripture, water often pictures overwhelming chaos (Genesis 7:17; Exodus 14:27-28). Here it represents hostile forces that, apart from God’s intervention, would have drowned His people utterly.

the threat described

• “Raging waters” evokes a torrent in flood stage, unstoppable and deadly (Psalm 69:1-2; Isaiah 59:19).

• David couples it with earlier images in the psalm—“men rose up against us…their anger flared against us” (vv. 2-3)—to show that human opposition can be as lethal as a flash flood.

• Without the LORD’s help, David insists Israel would have shared the fate of those engulfed at the Red Sea (Exodus 14:27). This is no exaggeration; it is a sober acknowledgment that the covenant people survive only by divine mercy.

the rescue declared

The verse’s force lies in its hypothetical: “then…would have.” Because God stepped in, the catastrophe never occurred. Similar deliverances fill the biblical record:

• The Jordan’s floodwaters stopped for Israel (Joshua 3:15-17).

• Hezekiah was spared when Assyria “could not shoot an arrow there” (2 Kings 19:32-34).

• The disciples cried out in the storm, and “He rebuked the wind” (Mark 4:37-39).

Each instance underscores that God’s people repeatedly face circumstances that should end them—yet do not, because the LORD acts.

the ongoing relevance

• For believers today, raging waters may appear as persecution, cultural hostility, or personal trials (2 Timothy 3:12; 1 Peter 4:12-13).

• The verse invites habitual remembrance: if God had not intervened in our conversion, our family, or specific crises, disaster would have swept us away (Ephesians 2:4-6).

• It fuels gratitude and confidence; the God who preserved Israel “from age to age” (Psalm 90:1) still commands the storms we face (Matthew 14:30-32).

summary

Psalm 124:5 pictures utter ruin—“the raging waters would have swept us away”—to magnify the LORD’s saving power. The line reminds us that enemies and trials can surge like a flood, yet they stop short of victory because God stands with His people. Remembering this truth fosters worship, humility, and unshakeable trust in the One who forever keeps the waters in check.
