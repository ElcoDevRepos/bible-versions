Study BibleBook ◦ Chapter 
For behold, I am on your side;
This phrase emphasizes God's commitment and favor towards Israel. Historically, Israel had experienced God's judgment due to their disobedience, but this statement marks a shift towards restoration. The assurance of divine support echoes God's covenant promises, reminiscent of His declarations in Genesis 12:3 to bless those who bless Abraham's descendants. It also reflects the theme of divine favor found in Romans 8:31, "If God is for us, who can be against us?" This phrase reassures the Israelites of God's protective and restorative presence.

I will turn toward you,
This indicates a change in God's disposition from judgment to blessing. In the context of Ezekiel, this turning signifies a reversal of the exile's consequences. Theologically, it represents repentance and restoration, aligning with the broader biblical narrative of redemption. The turning of God towards His people is a recurring theme, seen in passages like 2 Chronicles 7:14, where God promises healing and restoration if His people turn back to Him. This phrase underscores the hope of renewed relationship and favor.

and you will be tilled and sown.
This agricultural imagery symbolizes productivity and fruitfulness, contrasting the desolation experienced during the Babylonian exile. The land, once barren due to judgment, will be cultivated and yield crops, signifying economic and spiritual renewal. This promise connects to the covenant blessings in Deuteronomy 28:11, where obedience leads to prosperity in the land. The imagery also foreshadows the spiritual sowing and reaping found in the New Testament, such as in Galatians 6:7-9, where sowing to the Spirit results in eternal life. This phrase highlights the transformation from desolation to abundance through divine intervention.

Teaching Points
God's Favor and Support
God declares His support for His people, emphasizing His commitment to their well-being and restoration.

Restoration and Renewal
The promise of being "tilled and sown" symbolizes a process of preparation and growth, applicable to both physical and spiritual renewal.

Divine Sovereignty and Faithfulness
God's ability to turn towards His people and change their circumstances highlights His sovereignty and faithfulness to His promises.

Hope in Desolation
Even in times of desolation or exile, God’s promises provide hope and assurance of future restoration and blessing.

Spiritual Growth and Fruitfulness
Just as the land is prepared for sowing, believers are called to be receptive to God’s work in their lives, leading to spiritual growth and fruitfulness.

What Does Ezekiel 36:9 Mean
For behold

The single word “behold” calls God’s people to stop, look, and listen. Whenever Scripture uses it, the Lord is drawing attention to something important and certain. Here the certainty is His coming intervention. ➔ Isaiah 43:19 speaks the same way: “Behold, I am doing a new thing; now it springs forth.” Like Ezekiel, Isaiah uses “behold” to announce God’s fresh, undeniable action on behalf of His covenant people.

I am on your side

God openly declares allegiance to Israel. After seasons of discipline, He now promises favor, not wrath.

• Psalm 118:6 affirms, “The LORD is on my side; I will not fear.”

• Romans 8:31 echoes the principle for all believers: “If God is for us, who can be against us?”

The verse leaves no room for doubt. God’s posture is protective and supportive, reversing former judgments (see Ezekiel 36:17–19). He is not merely sympathetic; He is actively committed.

I will turn toward you

This is the relenting, relational face of God. When He turns toward His people, blessing flows.

• Numbers 6:25–26 records the priestly blessing: “The LORD make His face shine upon you… the LORD lift up His countenance upon you and give you peace.”

• Malachi 3:7 captures the reciprocal call: “Return to Me, and I will return to you.”

The Creator’s attention guarantees restoration. His turning is personal, conveying renewed fellowship and the end of estrangement.

You will be tilled and sown

The promise moves from relationship to result: a land once desolate will become productive again.

• Jeremiah 31:27 foretells, “I will sow the house of Israel and the house of Judah with the seed of man and of beast.”

• Hosea 10:12 urges, “Break up your fallow ground, for it is time to seek the LORD, until He comes and rains righteousness upon you.”

• Psalm 126:5–6 reinforces, “Those who sow in tears will reap with shouts of joy.”

Tilling speaks of preparation; sowing anticipates future harvest. God not only comforts His people but restores their environment, ensuring physical abundance that mirrors their spiritual renewal.

summary

Ezekiel 36:9 unfolds like a gentle sunrise: God commands attention (“behold”), pledges loyalty (“I am on your side”), draws near (“I will turn toward you”), and promises tangible renewal (“you will be tilled and sown”). The verse guarantees that the same God who disciplines also restores, turning wastelands into fertile fields and despair into hope.

Pulpit Commentary
Verse 9. - I am for you. He had previously been against (Ezekiel 5:8; Ezekiel 13:8), but was now for Israel and against Seir (Ezekiel 35:3). This change of dispensation implied no mutation in God, but merely that, as God had previously visited Israel with judgment on account of sin, so henceforth would he visit her with grace on condition of repentance. I will turn unto you. Always it is presupposed that Israel turns unto Jehovah.
