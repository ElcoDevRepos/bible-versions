You boasted against Me with your mouth
This phrase highlights the arrogance and pride of Edom, a nation that often opposed Israel. In biblical context, boasting against God signifies a deep-seated rebellion and lack of reverence. Historically, Edom, descended from Esau, had a long-standing enmity with Israel, the descendants of Jacob. This enmity is rooted in the Genesis account of Jacob and Esau, where Esau sold his birthright to Jacob. The boasting here can be seen as Edom's prideful attitude and their belief in their own strength and security, despite God's sovereignty. This is reminiscent of other biblical instances where nations or individuals boasted against God, such as in Isaiah 10:15, where Assyria is rebuked for its arrogance.

and multiplied your words against Me.
The multiplication of words against God suggests persistent and repeated blasphemy or slander. This indicates not just a single act of defiance but a continual pattern of disrespect and opposition. In the cultural context of the ancient Near East, words held significant power, and to speak against a deity was a serious offense. This phrase underscores the gravity of Edom's sin, as they not only opposed God but did so repeatedly and with increasing intensity. This can be paralleled with the behavior of other nations in the Bible, such as Babylon, which also spoke arrogantly against God (Isaiah 47:8-10).

I heard it Myself!
This declaration emphasizes God's omniscience and His personal involvement in the affairs of nations. It serves as a reminder that no action or word escapes God's notice. Theologically, this reflects the belief in a God who is both transcendent and immanent, aware of and responsive to human actions. This assurance of divine awareness is echoed in other scriptures, such as Psalm 94:9, which affirms that God, who created the ear, surely hears. It also serves as a warning that God will hold nations and individuals accountable for their words and actions, as seen in the judgment pronounced on Edom in the surrounding passages of Ezekiel 35.

Persons / Places / Events
1. Ezekiel
A prophet of God who ministered to the Israelites during their Babylonian exile. He conveyed God's messages of judgment and restoration.

2. Mount Seir
A mountainous region associated with Edom, the descendants of Esau. In this chapter, it symbolizes the hostility and enmity of Edom against Israel.

3. Edomites
The descendants of Esau, Jacob's brother. Historically, they were often in conflict with the Israelites and are the primary focus of God's judgment in this passage.

4. God
The sovereign Lord who hears and judges the words and actions of nations and individuals.

5. Judgment
The event of divine retribution that God pronounces against Edom for their prideful and hostile words against Him and His people.
Teaching Points
The Power of Words
Our words have significant power and can reflect the condition of our hearts. We must be mindful of how we speak about God and others.

God Hears and Judges
God is attentive to the words we speak. He hears both our praises and our boasts, and He will hold us accountable for them.

Pride Leads to Downfall
The pride of Edom led to their judgment. We must guard against pride and arrogance, especially in how we relate to God and His people.

Interpersonal Relationships
Just as Edom's hostility towards Israel was condemned, we should strive for peace and reconciliation in our relationships, avoiding enmity and strife.

Repentance and Humility
Recognizing our faults and turning away from prideful speech is crucial. Humility before God and others is a key aspect of Christian living.

What Does Ezekiel 35:13 Mean
You boasted against Me with your mouth

• In Ezekiel 35 the LORD addresses Mount Seir (Edom), calling out a long-standing, pride-filled hostility toward Israel. Here He pinpoints the root: arrogant speech directed at God Himself.

• Boasting is not mere trash talk; it is a heart posture of self-exaltation (Psalm 94:4, Obadiah 1:3). When Edom ridiculed Israel in her distress, Edom effectively mocked Israel’s covenant God (Genesis 12:3).

• Scripture links sinful speech to the overflow of the heart (Matthew 12:34-37). What Edom said exposed entrenched rebellion.

• Practical takeaway: words reveal allegiance. Consistent contempt for God or His people signals spiritual opposition (James 3:9-10).

and multiplied your words against Me

• The charge intensifies: not a one-time slip, but repeated, calculated defiance (Malachi 3:13).

• “Multiplied” underscores persistence. Edom’s insults kept pace with Israel’s suffering—most likely during Jerusalem’s fall (Psalm 137:7, Obadiah 1:10-12).

• Scripture highlights the cumulative weight of sinful speech: “The tongue is a small part of the body, but it makes great boasts” (James 3:5). Continuous verbal sin stores up judgment (Romans 2:5).

• God takes note when antagonism becomes habitual. Every added word enlarges guilt (Proverbs 10:19).

I heard it Myself!

• The LORD’s declaration is personal and emphatic: divine omniscience cancels any hope of secrecy (Psalm 139:4, Hebrews 4:13).

• Hearing implies impending action. Just as He “heard” Israel’s groaning in Egypt (Exodus 2:24), He now “hears” Edom’s arrogance, guaranteeing retribution (Obadiah 1:15).

• This assurance comforts God’s people: mockery against them never escapes His notice (Psalm 56:8).

• For the unrepentant, the same truth warns that no word is lost in the wind (Matthew 12:36). Accountability is certain.

summary

Ezekiel 35:13 exposes Edom’s sustained, arrogant speech against the LORD. Their boasts flowed from proud hearts, multiplied without restraint, and reached the ears of the all-knowing God, sealing their judgment. The verse underscores the seriousness of our words, the certainty that God hears every one of them, and His commitment to defend His covenant and His people.
